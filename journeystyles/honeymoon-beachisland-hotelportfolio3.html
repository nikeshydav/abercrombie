  • BANYAN TREE SEYCHELLES

    Nestling amongst the swaying palm trees and tropical forest of Intendance Bay, on the main island of Mahé, the Banyan Tree is a romantic hideaway providing a rare taste of paradise. After a day of exploring or lying by the pool, a visit to the Banyan Tree Spa is the ultimate indulgence. Awaken your senses with an invigorating stroll along the beach before breakfast. Once you have worked up an appetite, a delicious spread of fresh fruits, pastries and hot à la carte dishes awaits.

    On the Banyan Lagoon I in the company of your personal host and skipper, you can explore the islands of the Seychelles in complete style, stopping whenever you choose for ad hoc lunches, moonlit picnics or a spot of snorkelling. Back on land, a visit to the fabulous spa is an absolute must. All sessions (except beauty & hair treatments) consist of 30 minutes calm time, which includes a footbath before the treatment and a herbal drink served during refreshments & relaxation.

    22 Hillside Pool Villas have large bedrooms, a sunken bath and an outside pavilion with living and dining area that leads onto a timber deck with private pool. Four Pool Villas by the Rocks and five Intendance Pool Villas also feature a jet pool, the latter with amazing views of the ocean below. Our favourite for romantic couples are the Beachfront Spa Pool Villas. Each of these has a shower/steam room, larger swimming pool, spa pavilion and outdoor jet pool and shower. But best of all, they also have direct access onto the beach. For families we would recommend the six new Two-Bedroom Double Pool Villas. Just steps from the sand, they come with their own butler, two sprawling master bedrooms, private open-air dining area, two swimming pools and an outdoor jet pool.

    BIRD ISLAND LODGE

    Bird Island Lodge is all about simplicity and the environment that you are in, there are no telephones and no phones immerse yourself in the beauty of this unspoilt unique island. Conservation plays an important role on the island and the conservation workers like Robbie will guide you through the island enlightening your mind on the different species of birds, turtle and the fauna of the island. If you would like to help out while staying on the island with any of the conservation projects this is also possible.

    The cuisine on the island is simple and fresh combining the fresh fish caught daily, with the organic fruit and vegetables as well as hand reared pork from the small farm, fresh tasty food at its best. The turtle laying season is from mid-October to mid-January and the laying season from mid-December to mid-March. Bird Island can claim the highest number of first time records for any single island of the Seychelles. With 112 species recorded in total, they also became the first island to record 100 non-breeding migratory birds. Sooty Terns hover, in their tens of thousands, above their island sanctuary in March and April, before beginning to land in the colony at the northern end of the island from the end of April. Bird Island offers a truly unique experience that is not for everyone, but a very special place that is waiting to be discovered.

    CONSTANCE LEMURIA RESORT

    Constance Lemuria is surrounded by rare and luxuriant vegetation and washed by the warm waters of a turquoise lagoon. The architecture and design have been created with natural materials and work in harmony with the environment. Even the lighting has been ingeniously designed, so as not to disturb the turtles as they come ashore at night to lay their eggs on the secluded white sand beaches.

    Pack your camera in your beach-bag and ask the chef to prepare you a special picnic, to spend a lazy afternoon in picture-perfect surroundings. 88 palm-thatched Junior Suites and eight Senior Suites are scattered behind the two beaches of Petite and Grande Anse Kerlan, peeping between the trees to the ocean beyond.

    Each room, decorated in a minimalist yet luxurious style, has an LCD TV, mac mini entertainment system, large bathroom, a terrace and beach-loungers in the shade of overhanging palm trees. The Senior Suites are exceptionally spacious and have a separate lounge and double-size terrace. If you are looking for more privacy, eight Two-Bedroom Pool Villas face directly onto the beach of Grand Anse Kerlan, and come complete with a private pool, relaxation pavilion and personal Villa Master to look after your every need. Even more lavish is the three-bedroom Presidential Villa, majestically positioned on granite boulders above its own private beach.

    DENIS PRIVATE ISLAND

    Make sure you find time to explore the island which includes a 100-year old lighthouse and densely forested interior. Look out for Toby the tortoise (about the same age as the lighthouse) and the tiny chapel. Tour the gardens and the farm, which ensures the island is more or less self-sufficient.

    Meal times are a feast of locally grown produce and freshly caught seafood cooked on the open-grill. Or, for a treat, spend an evening dining in the wine cellar. Relaxing days on Denis can be spent sea-fishing, diving, shell-collecting or simply walking on the pristine beach.

    25 island-style cottages sit amongst casuarina and coconut trees, bordering the beach. Each has a high woven coconut-grass ceiling and a comfy double bed, romantically draped with mosquito nets and the softest Indian cotton sheets. The private bathroom opens onto a private courtyard. A sitting area is equipped with a mini-fridge, whilst private verandas face the sea. The newly renovated Beach Villa (325 m² in size) has an extended sea facing private deck with an outdoor sitting and dining area. The villa is also has a plunge pool and a sala day bed. If you are travelling with children, the Family Villa has a master bedroom and two additional twin beds.

    DESROCHES

    Located on Desroches Island, this property is the perfect island getaway offering a selection of accommodation; 20 Beach suites, 23 Beach villas, 8 Beach Retreats.

    There are lots of different dining options on the island, from in villa dinning, to a private picnic on the beach, the main restaurant Le Ve Loutier offers an eclectic mix of creole and international cuisine while the Ghost Crab Grill & Pizzeria offers the perfect light lunch. Desroches grow the majority of their vegetables, fruit and herbs on the island and will happily give a guided tour of the garden as well as delight your taste buds at meal times.

    There is a vast array of activities on offer with 14kms of beach to explore, there are a team of conservationists on hand to give tours of the island, or take a bicycle and explore by yourself, enjoy a game of tennis on the floodlit courts, try your hand at big game fishing or have a relaxing day trip to one of the neighbouring islands as well as a selection of childrens activities available.

    FOUR SEASONS RESORT SEYCHELLES

    The Four Seasons Resort Seychelles spills dramatically down a lushly forested incline, to the white powder sands below. Villas and resort buildings have been designed to blend with the natural landscape, many made from hand-split local granite. Secreted amongst the idyllic beauty of the south-west shores of Mahé, this exclusive retreat is one of the most sought-after hideaways in the Seychelles. Spend a relaxing day on the beach, or pop into the Beach Club to book on a dive boat or snorkelling tour. Children aged 4-12 years have their own space at the Kids For All Seasons Club offering a range of indoor and outdoor activities.

    62 Villas and five Suites are hidden amongst the lush vegetation of the hillside. A spacious bedroom, living area and bathroom offer you a special place to retreat. Each Villa offers a generous outdoor living space comprising a large sun deck, infinity plunge pool, relaxation pavilion with day bed, outdoor shower and dining area. The Garden Villas have quick access to the beach, making them ideal for families. If you are looking for more privacy, the Ocean View Villas, inspired by tree-houses, peek out from a canopy of cinnamon trees and have breath-taking views of the turquoise ocean and beach below. Right at the top of the hill you will find the Hilltop Ocean Villas featuring magnificent panoramic ocean views. Five Suites include between two and three bedrooms and represent the ultimate in expansive indulgence.

    There is complimentary high-speed Internet access at two workstations with desktop personal computers and colour-printing access in the library. A wide selection of complimentary DVDs, CDs and books are available for guests to enjoy in the library or borrow to use in their villa.

    There are four different categories of villas each have WIFI, a ceiling fan and a private terrace, the 10 Garden villas also have a kitchenette, the 31 Villa de Chambre have an outdoor bathroom, the 3 Villa de Chambre Elegance have a deck extension with whirlpool and the beautiful Villa Presidentielle is 485sq metres of true indulgence with 3 bedrooms, air condition, a private terrace with swimming pool, butler service and a deck extension with whirlpool.

    Meals at La Domaine de L'Orangerie offer a total leisure approach with an all-day dining experience with local selected flavours that will indulge every palate and senses. Let your desires be fulfilled in a complete relaxing atmosphere, overlooking the Indian Ocean and the tropical sunset. All guests staying at Le Domaine de L'Orangeraie have complimentary use of snorkelling equipment and bicycles for their entire stay. La Digue is a 15 minute flight from Mahe and a 20 minute flight from Praslin.

    L'ARCHIPEL

    L'Archipel is set above one of the most beautiful beaches on Praslin, a small boutique property, charming with great character and a great place to be based to discover some of the other islands of the Seychelles. Due to its size L'Archipel makes a perfect honeymoon retreat.

    The dining options at the hotel offer stunning views as well as fantastic cuisine as the head chef has learnt his trade from a famous French chef and has used what he has learnt to add the creole flavours to the food. There is also an authentic Creole menu once a week.

    L'Archipel is an excellent base, hire a bicycle or car and discover the rest of the island, visit the prehistoric vegetation of 'Vallée de Mai' National Park where you shall discover The Coco de Mer, with its distinctive and unusually shaped nut and La Digue is 30 minutes away by boat from Praslin, discover this island at your own pace with either an ox-cart or a bicycle a great way to spend a day as well as the opportunity to see the Giant Tortoise. Water temperature is around 28°C all year round
