CROATIA BEACH

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 Days

    A luxurious and relaxing 4 day visit to the stunning Croatian coastal town of Split.

    Highlights: Explore the Ancient city of Split and enjoy plenty of time at leisure to make the most of your wonderful hotel facilities.

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE IN SPLIT

    Meet your Chauffeur at Split airport and transfer to your hotel which overlooks the bay of Split, the Adriatic sea and the iconic island of Brac.

    Le Meridien Lav, Split is located at Podstrana, five miles south of Split. The beautiful setting features 800 metres of beach frontage, gardens landscaped by award-winning designer Jim Nicolay of Hawaii, and fantastic sea views overlooking Split and the surrounding islands.

    Overnight: Le Meridien Lav

  • Day 2

    SPILT

    Today is at your leisure to enjoy the facilities of your beachfront hotel. The hotel facilities include an outdoor and an indoor pool, tennis courts and a Water Sports Centre which offers scuba diving, waterskiing, sailing, and more. The exclusive spa and wellness centre is a magnificent space offering treatment rooms, relaxation areas and a fitness centre, the perfect place to unwind and feel pampered.

    Overnight: Le Meridien Lav
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    SPLIT

    Today you may wish to explore the ancient city of Split, including the palace which was the 'retirement home' of the roman emperor Hadrian. Alternatively why not enjoy a romantic dinner of fresh oysters overlooking the marina.

    Overnight: Le Meridien Lav
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    SPLIT

    Your chauffeur will bring you back in style to Split Airport today.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Urubamba/Machu Picchu

    Board the deluxe Hiram Bingham train, the most luxurious way to travel the breathtaking landscape to Machu Picchu. Step on board, and you are immersed in an elegant world of polished wood, gleaming cutlery and glittering glass that evokes a romantic ambience. After a light lunch on board, arrive at the breathtaking Incan citadel and begin exploring the ruins in the company of your expert guide. Feel the pulse of this magical place as you visualize the lives of the priests, craftsmen, and servants who inhabited this seemingly uninhabitable citadel and then left, leaving no written records about its destruction. After a busy day of sightseeing, return to your beautiful mountainside retreat, high in the lush Andean clouds forest. It features the look and feel of a rural Andean village set in spectacular grounds with paths that wind past waterfalls and gardens, and trails that wander through wild forest. Snuggle under a thick alpaca blanket in your suite, which offers incredible views of the exotic gardens or the dramatic Vilcanota River.

    Overnight: Inkaterra Machu Picchu Pueblo Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Machu Picchu/Cusco

    Return to Machu Picchu with your guide this morning for a final look at the ruins - and for an even more meaningful event for you. A shaman joins you to perform a vow renewal ceremony for you. Stand before the spirits of the Incas and reaffirm your love in this magical place. Afterward, return to the hotel, renewed and refreshed, for lunch. This afternoon, return to Cusco by Vista dome train and settle in at your hotel, where you receive VIP amenities.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Cusco

    A morning city tour takes you to the Cathedral on the Plaza de Armas and the Santo Domingo Temple, a Dominican church and convent built on the foundations of the Koricancha (place of gold), where the Incas worshipped the sun. Continue just outside Cusco to the ruins of Sacsayhuaman, a testimonial to the Incan architectural skills. Try to slip a piece of paper between the cracks of the massive stone blocks, cut and fitted together with incredible precision without a single drop of mortar to hold them in place. Your afternoon is at leisure. Make sure to visit the hotel spa for a relaxing couple's massage. This evening, enjoy a romantic dinner at Limo restaurant, overlooking the main plaza.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 8

    Cusco/Lima

    Fly to Lima and transfer to your hotel. The remainder of the day is at leisure to do some last-minute shopping, take a walk along the ocean or enjoy the views from your suite.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Lima/India

    Transfer to the airport &Board your international departure flight.

    Overnight: Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

Morocco: Land of the Kasbahs (2012)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 Nights

    The land of the Kasbah's, busy ports, nomadic Berbers, high mountains and expansive desert offers a romantic escape from the world as you know it. Visit ancient mosques and walk hand in hand through lively city squares, follow mountain slopes and feel the breeze coming from picturesque lakes. Then share your nights in amazing kasbahs, palace hotels and intimate desert tents that all reflect the ambience of a bygone era.

  • Day 1

    India/Casablanca, Morocco

    You are met on arrival and given VIP assistance in clearing customs formalities and collecting your luggage; then travel by private vehicle to your unique vintage townhouse hotel, located in the heart of the city. Originally settled by Berbers in the seventh century, Casablanca today is a bustling, French-influenced center. This afternoon, join your guide for a tour of this port city, stopping at the formidable Hassan II Grand Mosque. Its minaret is the tallest religious structure in the world, and part of the mosque's floor is glass, enabling worshippers to kneel directly over the sea. Enjoy dinner at Rick's CafÃ©, housed in a 1930's mansion and based on the cafÃ© everybody came to in the famous film Casablanca.

    Overnight: Le Doge
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 2

    Casablanca/Er-Rachida/Merzouga

    Meet your private A&K guide, an affable local resident and an expert on Morocco's culture and traditions, who accompanies you throughout your journey in Morocco. Board a charter flight to Er-Rachidia, and on arrival have lunch in Erfoud. Hop into a four-wheel-drive vehicle for a drive across the stunning Erg Chebbi Dunes to your exclusive A&K Desert Tented Camp in a remote area of the Sahara Desert. You are welcomed with mint tea and pastries and led to your private Moroccan caidal-styled tent that combines elements of Berber and Bedouin styles. Decorated with colorful carpets, it comfortably sleeps two, and has its own private bathroom with a shower and toilet next door. Set off on camelback in the evening and begin exploring the sand dunes. Return to your nomadic accommodations before sitting down to a Bedouin dinner served around the campfire.

    Overnight: Desert Tented Camp
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Merzouga/Erfoud/Skoura

    The drive today takes you across the southern slopes of the High Atlas range and through the Dades Valley. Along the way, stop at some of the many date palmeries and a small, private museum that houses exhibits showcasing local art, culture and history. Travel the "Road of a Thousand Kasbahs" and view the amazing 17th-century Amerhidl Kasbah; its faÃ§ade appears on the Moroccan 50-dirham note. After lunch, continue to your home for the next two nights - a luxurious 19th-century Kasbah that was part of a sultan's private hunting ground in the Skoura Oasis. Dar Ahlam means "house of dreams," and the name suits it well. Enter what seems like a dreamscape, where colorful veils, lanterns and candles - and only 12 rooms - ensure complete privacy, setting the mood for romance. Sit by the fireplace, surrounded by a kaleidoscope of colors; have lunch in the garden or olive grove; or dine in a secluded corner of the Kasbah or even on a hidden terrace.

    Overnight: Dar Ahlam
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Skoura

    Not far from the desert oasis of Skoura is Ouarzazate Lake, where you take a walk and then sit down to a delightful picnic lunch on its shores. If you look across the lake you can see the mountains and desert; on a windy day, the waters toss and swell like the ocean. Afterward, return to your Kasbah for a refreshing swim and maybe a massage. This evening, savor a romantic dinner in an equally romantic setting: in the gardens under the almond trees, in the palm grove or on the riverbank.

    Overnight: Dar Ahlam
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Skoura/Ouarzazate/Ait Benhaddou/Marrakech

    Spend some time wandering around Ouarzazate, a city originally built as a French garrison in the 1920s and now known for its pottery and carpets. Afterward, visit the Glaoui Kasbah of Taourit. One of Morocco's most beautiful Kasbahs, it consists of a network of luxury apartments, simple clay houses and crenellated towers decorated with geometric motifs. Continue to the fortified village of Ait Benhaddou, where some of the world's most elaborate and decorative kasbahs are found. See how they seem to defy the laws of gravity as they perch upon the sleep slope. Ait Benhaddou's charms have also reached all the way to Hollywood. Many popular movies, including Lawrence of Arabia, Jesus of Nazareth, Gladiator and The Mummy have been filmed here. After lunch in a local restaurant, continue to exotic Marrakech, the "Pearl of the South," and your Moorish-style palace hotel.

    Overnight: Ksar Char Bagh
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 6

    Marrakech

    Morocco's most famous city, Marrakech is veiled in mystery and magic. Visit the medina, the old walled section of the city, where most of the architectural attractions are located. Begin with a stop at Koutoubia Mosque and its 12th-century minaret, which dominates the local skyline. Make your way to vibrant Djemaa el-Fna, Marrakech's main square. Watch as fire-eaters, mime artists, snake charmers and street musicians perform at every turn. Continue walking and look for the Saadian Tombs, decorated in Granada-style carving and hidden away behind high walls. Discover how the privileged lived in the 19th century when you wander Bahia Palace to see the beautiful mosaics, fountains and courtyards. At the Museum of Moroccan Arts and Crafts, housed in the beautiful Dar Si Said palace, view stunning traditional woven carpets and Berber silver jewelry. Afterward, return to your hotel to experience a very important part of Moroccan culture and life: a hammam (steam bath). More than a steam bath, the hammam is a ritual. Soak in hot, soft water, lather up with a special penetrating black soap and exfoliate for revived skin. Follow up with a cold shower and an incredibly relaxing massage in a warm, darkened room - total bliss.

    Overnight: Ksar Char Bagh
    Meal Plan: Meals: B,L

  • Day 7

    Marrakech/Atlas Mountains/Marrakech

    High up in the Atlas Mountains, Morocco's tallest and most majestic range, see traditional villages clinging to the almost vertical sides of the valley. Spend your day exploring the Atlas Mountains and its flora and fauna with your private guide, and gain insight into the daily lives of the villagers. While you have lunch at Sir Richard Branson's Kasbah Tamadot, admire the fabulous views of the mountains. Later this afternoon, return to your hotel for a relaxing massage. Conclude your romantic holiday with dinner for two served in a private room or under the stars in the hotel's garden

    Overnight: Ksar Char Bagh
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    Marrakech/India

    Privately transfer to the airport to board your departure flight.

    Meal Plan: B

French Honeymoon

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 20 Days

    Highlights: • Sightseeing of Paris city including major highlights and evening enjoy romantic 4 Course menu dinner including live musical animation.
    • Sightseeing tour to Versailles with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. Situated to the west of Paris, Versailles is the location of the huge palace that was constructed in the 17th century for Louis XIV, the 'Sun King'.
    • Full day (8 hour) sightseeing tour including Beaune and Nuits Saint Georges, well known as the wine capital of Burgundy.

    India/Paris

    Arrival Paris
    Upon arrival at the Paris airport, you will be met and transferred by your Guardian Angel and private vehicle with English-speaking driver to your hotel in Paris, where you will be assisted with check-in.

    Day at leisure.
    This evening discover Paris, the city of lights and enjoy a dinner cruise on the Seine.
    8:30PM board an entirely glass-encased boat for your romantic dinner including live musical animation. Your 4-course menu will include some wine (1 bottle for 4 guests) and a glass of champagne with dessert.

    Overnight: Sofitel Paris Le Faubourg
    Luxury Double/Twin
    Meal Plan: D

    Paris

    Today you will enjoy a full day (8 hours) sightseeing tour in Paris with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. This morning an overview tour will include famous Parisian sights such as the Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Champs Elysées and the Ile de la Cité, where you will visit the beautiful Notre Dame Cathedral and the Sainte Chapelle, a pearl of Gothic architecture. This afternoon visit the world-famous Louvre Museum. Originally the Royal residence of the Kings of France, the sprawling Louvre palace contains one of the most important art collections in the Western world, spanning from remotest antiquity to the mid 19th cent, with the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo as perhaps its two most famous exhibits, and the upturned glass pyramid by I.M. Pei at the entrance adding a contemporary touch.

You will work with a selection of high quality ingredients and design your own personal blend of notes. You will name and label your 17.5 fl oz bottle of personal fragrance and take it away with you as a souvenir of this olfactory experience.

    9.00 PM - enjoy the cabaret theatre show at the Moulin Rouge (1/2 bottle of Champagne included). See the world-renowned showgirls and French Cancan dancers on the Moulin Rouge's historic stage.

    Make your own way back to the hotel after the show.

    Meal Plan: B

It became the model for grand houses of royalty all over Europe. Take a tour of the opulent State Apartments that culminate in the magnificent Gallery of Mirrors. You may like to take a stroll in the formal ornamental gardens that are embellished with many fountains, and see the Grand Canal that stretches into the distance across the park. Return to Paris following your visit.

    Morning after breakfast, check out and you will be transferred to the station to board train to Dijon.

    Concierge Rail service from Paris to Dijon.

    After lunch at leisure, meet your local guide for a 3-hr walking tour of the city. Once the capital city of the mighty Dukes of Burgundy before coming under the control of the French crown, Dijon is still the capital of the Burgundy region and its wonderful old quarters are among the most interesting in France. Stroll through a network of narrow streets and small squares with fine 15th-16th century mansions and the 13th century gothic church of Notre-Dame before coming up behind the Ducal Palace, which houses a huge museum of French and Flemish art. Full of fascinating shops, Dijon's famous mustard is sold everywhere as well as the blackcurrant liqueur.

Today Beaune is famous for its tiled roofs and Flemish architecture which have become icons of Burgundy. Continue on to the wine country between Beaune and Dijon and visit the Clos de Vougeot famous for its wine-making barn. Also see the famous villages such as Gevrey-Chambertin or Marsannay-la-Côte before returning to Dijon.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel La Cloche
    Meal Plan: B

    Place Bellecour and Place des Terreaux are linked by fashionable shopping streets and surrounded by fine Renaissance buildings. Across the Saône, Le Vieux Lyon, is a picturesque and well-restored district with numerous impressive Gothic and Renaissance buildings. Visit the The Maison des Canuts (silk workers of the 19th century) is a living museum that reveals the richness and quality of the silk industry in Lyon and it is the only place where manual looms, invented by Jacquard, are in use. Your tour will give you the opportunity to learn about the history of silk, the life of the silk workers.

This exceptional city-state is just east of Nice, a land steeped in history and also a Mediterranean mini Las Vegas. The capital, Monaco, is a fairy tale city standing on a rocky headland almost surrounded by sea, and contains the palace complete with guards, the tropical gardens offering excellent views, and the museum containing Roman antiquities and prehistory collections.

    Look around the Place du Casino, possibly the most famous casino in the world, in the center of Monte Carlo.     Bordeaux

    Today you experience historic Bordeaux with your guide on a walking tour (3hrs) to see some of the citys famous monuments and buildings. See the 18th-century Place de la Bourse and the 13th-century Cathedral of St. Andre, now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Admire the neoclassical facades of the Bordeaux Law Courts, St. Andre Hospital and the 18th-century Palais Rohan, now the City Hall. When you explore Old Bordeaux, walk past the Cailhau Gate and the Grosse Cloche, the former belfry of the town hall in the Middle Ages.

    Afternoon at leisure to do some shopping or enjoy the hotels spa.

    Optional - 6hr excursion to St-Emilion with English Speaking driver-guide

    Only a short distance from Bordeaux, the village of St. Emilion was built into a limestone hill. The cobbled streets of this medieval town are lined with stores selling the wines the region is known for. In the eighth century, Emilion, a hermit, lived in a cave with an underground spring. Over the next 500 years, his cave was enlarged to create Eglise Monolithe, one of Frances largest underground rock churches. Visit the cave and then take time to stop at some of the local vineyards, like the famous Château Franc Mayne or Chateau La Tour du Pin Figeac

    Overnight: Grand Hotel de Bordeaux & Spa
    Superior Double

Rich merchants built its Renaissance and neo-classic mansions and it is home to the Musée des Beaux Arts, a remarkable series of salons decorated in silk and wood panelling. See the Cathedral of St. Gatien with its Renaissance lanterns and 13th century stained glass as well as the Place Plumereau and Rue Briconnet, center of the old medieval quarter.
