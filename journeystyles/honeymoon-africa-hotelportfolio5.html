Suggested Botswana Accommodation

  • ABU CAMP

    Abu Camp is a stunning camp, every detail has been thought of and it is a place of true luxury and discovery. The elephant experience at Abu camp involves being part of an elephant herd, meeting each elephant and beginning to understand what makes the largest land mammal tick. The elephant back safari is most notable for its natural stately pace which does not disturb other game in the wilderness, ensuring intimate close-ups of game which can be achieved no other way.

    The accommodation at Abu Camp is six airy tents, each individually designed with a private deck overlooking the lagoon. Each unit is en-suite and includes an outdoor copper bath. The main area of the camp consists of a lounge, dining area, plunge pool, library and gym, the areas are split over different levels creating space and comfort. Meals are a sumptuous affair and there is an excellent choice of award winning wines on offer.

    The habitats around Abu Camp are varied and different to the typical Okavango Delta scenery with open grasslands to secluded island sanctuaries and papyrus-fringed channels, allowing for an abundance of general game viewing: from giraffe, zebra, elephant, impala, tsessebe and lechwe to wildebeest, buffalo and warthog.

    JACK'S CAMP

    Sited on the original spot chosen by Jack Bousfield in the 1960's, Jack's Camp is set on the Pans of the Makgadikgadi. A relic of one of the world's largest super-lakes, formed some five to seven million years ago, the lake dried thousands of years ago to leave a vast salt pan. Jacks camp has 2 distinctive seasons dry Season (winter) 16th April - 31st October and Wet season (summer) 01 November - 15th April. During the wet season the landscape transforms, clouds of flamingo and other migratory birds along with herds of zebra and wildebeest who are drawn to the lush long grass ultimately the predators head this way too.

    It's not uncommon for guides to team up with local bushmen to escort you on morning walks or game drives. Four wheel drive quad bikes will allow you to venture far into the centre of the Makgadikgadi, explore remote archaeological sites and periodically discover never before documented fossil beds of extinct giant zebra and hippo. Jack's Camp is the only place where you are virtually guaranteed to see the rare and elusive brown hyena. Close by is the famous Chapman's Baobab (Also known as the Seven Sisters) which is acknowledged to be the largest tree in Africa, and was the campsite of early explorers like Livingstone and Selous when they pioneered the area.

    At the camp there is a beautiful swimming pool where you can escape the heat and relax in the cool waters. Afternoon tea at Jacks camp is a sumptuous affair and the chef goes to great trouble to create mouth-watering sweet and savoury delicacies. The meals at the camp are served in the dinning tent surrounded by interesting artefacts.

    NXABEGA OKAVANGO TENTED

    Nxabega Okavango tented Camp is located on the edge of the Okavango Delta in a 7 000 hectare exclusive wildlife concession bordering the Moremi Game Reserve. The camp is known for its elegant tented accommodation with breathtaking views of the Okavango Delta.

    It's the perfect combination of modern luxury and nature. Nine tented suites on raised wooden platforms are tucked under a canopy of African ebony trees.

    The reed-and-thatch guest areas include indoor and outdoor dining, a safari shop and a swimming pool with a grand deck overlooking the bush. From this cove of comfort, guests are able to appreciate the exquisite beauty of the Okavango Delta. Private bush dinners, cocktails on the river island are provided by Nxabega's friendly staff.

    SANDIBE OKAVANGO

    Sandibe Safari Lodge is set on a permanent channel of the magnificent Okavango Delta. It's adjacent to the game-rich Moremi Wildlife Reserve. The beautiful thatched lodge sits between calming waters and grass-swept plains and was built with a commitment to nature conservation.

    The result is organic architecture that blends seamlessly with one of the most breathtaking landscapes in Africa. Gaze out onto the banks of the Santanadibe River from under the canopy of your king-size bed or spend a restful hour watching the birdlife from your private raised deck. Sandibe is surrounded by an abundant array of wildlife and throughout the year, guests are enchanted by the changing moods of the Delta.

    SAVUTI CAMP

    Savuti Camp has witnessed the most dramatic change over the past few years; up to 2008 the Savuti Channel was a dry river bed and had been for over 30 years. In 2008 the waters returned and now the Savuti Channel is in full flood which has meant a dramatic turnaround for the wildlife. There are now larger concentrations of birdlife here, a large amount of plains game have seen the channel as a refuge and a source of food, which then attracts the predators and for the wildlife spectator this creates a fantastic platform for exciting wildlife events to unfold.

    Savuti camp is a beautiful and intimate camp, each room is light and airy with very comfortable furniture and has a private deck that overlooks the Savuti Channel. The main area of the camp consists of a lounge and dining area, with the lounge area overlooking the channel. There is also a plunge pool and a camp fire pit essential for those cooler evenings in the bush.

    Game concentrations here are high, particularly in the winter dry season, and apart from abundant plains game, species such as roan and sable antelope, southern giraffe, lion, leopard, cheetah, spotted hyaena, black-backed jackal and wild dog may also be encountered. In winter, this area can also be excellent for the sought-after aardvark and aardwolf.
