Honeymoon in Vietnam – Beaches & More

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 14 Days

    Highlights: Hanoi- Ho Chi Minh-Con Dao, itinerary covers Bustling commercial city & Southeast Asia nicest beach & offer UNESCO World Heritage site Halong Bay & Hoi An ancient town, Colonial city Hanoi,Southeast Asia nicest beach Hoi An & Con Dao & Bustling commercial city Ho Chi Minh .

  • Day 1

    Arrival Hanoi

    ***Note: Early check-in at hotel is NOT included.

    Arrive in Hanoi by Flight # TBA (Own ticket).

    Meet & transfer to hotel. Balance of the day is free at leisure.

    Lunch & Dinner at your own account

    Overnight Stay: Intercontinental Hanoi Westlake

  • Day 2

    Hanoi//Visit to Duong Lam Village, Temple of Literature

    After breakfast, start your day to visit Duong Lam Village. The ancient village has a history of about 1,200 years with many houses dating back up to 400 years. One special thing about the village is that most of the buildings here are made of laterite and mud, two materials that are abundant in the area. Laterite is used on house walls, gates, wells, temple walls, and so forth. The mud is taken from ponds. Apart from its historical and tourism values, Duong Lam ancient village is an important place for scientists to study resident communities in ancient agriculture. The village gate, banyan, well, communal house are important factors in classifying Duong Lam ancient village. Enjoy Lunch in the village before heading back to Hanoi.

    Next, you will visit the Temple of Literature, founded in 1070 and dedicated to Confucius, honors scholars and men of literary accomplishment.

    Later in the afternoon, you will take an hour cyclo ride into the Old Quarter area. The Old Quarter is characterized by numerous narrow streets, which date from the 15th century, each bearing a name that reflects the business once conducted there.

    Drop by Thang Long theatre to enjoy famous Water Puppet Show, the unique Vietnamese traditional art. This art originates from rural festivals in Vietnam. The artists-men and women-stand waist-deep in the water to manipulate the puppets, making them move about, talk, laugh and even dance on water surface.

    Dinner at your own account

    Overnight Stay: Intercontinental Hanoi Westlake

  • Day 3

    Hanoi/Halong (Overnight Cruise)

    After breakfast, departure transfer to Halong bay (around 3.5 – 4 hours driving).

    Noon: Check-in at Bhaya Café, Tuan Chau Island.

    Embark on Bhaya Legend and enjoy a welcome cocktail while slowly sailing to Bai Tu Long, passing the setting for the movie Indochina, Mat Quy and Am Tich Islets. Savor a delicious fresh seafood lunch on-board.

    Bhaya Legend moves on into Vung Vieng fishing village, nestled among the limestone peaks, unaffected by ocean waves or strong winds. Visit the village by kayak or small boat to see the daily lives of locals up close.

    Swim or kayak the turquoise waters off the pristine white sandy beaches. Watch the sunset over enchanting Halong Bay while cruising to the overnight anchorage.

    Candlelit set-dinner on board

    Retire to your cabin or join the fishing program.

    Overnight: Bhaya Legend I Cruise

  • Day 4

    Halong/Hanoi/Danang/Hoi An

    Enjoy the sunrise, the lapping waves, the fresh sea air, the gentle breeze or get up at your leisure. Complimentary tea & coffee are available on request.

    The crew will ready the vessel to sail to a destination of your choice. Choices include: sightseeing at Sung Sot Grotto; hiking up Titop Mountain; lagoon kayaking or swimming on a deserted beach.

    Brunch is served onboard. Bhaya Legend returns to Tuan Chau Island at 11 am. End of services.

    Transfer back to Hanoi for free shopping time & dinner (own arrangement) before transfer to Noi Bai Airport for flight to Da Nang (Own ticket)

    Upon arrival, private pick up and transfer to check in hotel for night.

    Overnight Stay: Victoria Hoi An Resort

  • Day 5

    Hoi An//Walking tour Hoi An

    After breakfast, free at leisure for your own activities.

    In the afternoon, take a walking tour of Hoi An, discover the historic town which used to be a prosperous seaport city during the 16th to 18th centuries. Visit Hoi An colorful local market, Tan Ky old house, Fukien Assembly Hall, and the 400-year-old Japanese covered bridge.
    Travelers can also see local residents raise silkworms and produce silk for Hoi An's burgeoning textile industry.

    Lunch & Dinner at your own account

    Overnight Stay: Victoria Hoi An Resort

  • Day 6

    Hoi An//Visit to Tra Que Village

    Pick guest up at hotel and transfer to Tra Que Village (bike can be arrange somewhere in the middle route with additional charge), At the home of your host, Mr Tuan , a welcome drink of freshly picked fruits is waiting, before we take a walk around the village and learn about the history and customs of this charming village. We walk through the market gardens where hundreds of local farmers work their fields using the traditional methods of hundreds of years ago - no electrical machinery here.

    We return to the home of Mr Tuan, and this is where the real fun begins. Everyone is given farming instruments, and we set about joining the farmers in preparing the land for sewing. Our guide describes the traditional farming methods while we help fertilize the garden using seaweed from the local river, and then water it using huge tin cans strapped to our back. Time is then spent picking vegetables and herbs, and learning how to wrap them to prevent damage.
    Many of the local herbs are used by the farmers for traditional village medicines.

    It's now time to try are hands at making the village speciality named “Tam Huu” spring rolls - using the local herbs and vegetables we have just picked or Xeo Cake ( Banh Xeo). We then sit down and enjoy a lovely local meal.

    Rest of the day is free at leisure.

    Dinner at your own account

    Overnight Stay: Victoria Hoi An Resort

  • Day 7

    Hoi An//Danang//Ho Chi Minh City visit Cu Chi

    After late breakfast, transfer to the airport for your noon flight to Ho Chi Minh City (Own ticket)

    Arrive in Ho Chi Minh City, meet your guide and start to visit Cu Chi, a small community located in the northwest of Ho Chi Minh City, which gained notoriety for its network of deep tunnels that provided shelter and an effective source of infiltration for the Viet Cong during the later years of the Vietnam War. The tunnels, which reached hundreds of miles in total length, housed storage rooms, make-shift hospitals and sleeping quarters that accommodated rebel fighters for up to two weeks at a time. Heavy bombing by U.S. forces did not halt movement through the tunnels, making this an unusually successful defense against the superior weaponry of the American troops.

    Back to Saigon

    Overnight Stay: Intercontinental Asiana Saigon

  • Day 8

    Ho Chi Minh City// Insider Saigon by Vintage Vespa

    Breakfast at hotel

    This is the best way to get around and explore real Saigon bustle local life, drivers to take you on a tour: past the historic Reunification Hall, down Dong Khoi Street – passing the red-brick Notre Dame Cathedral, elegant Post Office and Opera House – before cruising down central Le Loi and out to Ben Thanh Market, squeezing through backpacker Pham Ngu Lao, out to the Chinese Cholon district and back to the city. Our Vespa tour of modern-day Ho Chi Minh City will give you a view of greater Saigon unlike anything you'll see from the hot, crowded interior of a tour bus, and much more of the city than you could see on your own. (Note: tour will not take guest inside the buildings).
    Tour includes: Bird club (morning tour), Thich Quang Duc Monument, Flower market, Funeral house, Ong Bon Pagoda, Tailor’s accessories market, Chinese medicine market in Chinatown, driving through tunnel to District 2, visit a local market, People’s Committee building, Rex Reunification Palace, Notre Dame Cathedral and the Old Central Post Office, CIA building on Dong Khoi, Opera House. Guests ride pillion behind our drivers.
    Return to hotel.

    Lunch & Dinner at your own account

    Overnight Stay: Intercontinental Asiana Saigon

  • Day 9

    Ho Chi Minh City//Con Dao

    After breakfast, transfer the airport for the airport for the noon flight to Con Dao (Own ticket)

    Arrive in Con Dao, PRIVATE transfer to Six Senses Con Dao (arranged by hotel).

    Free at leisure rest of the day.

    Overnight Stay: Six Senses Con Dao Resort

  • Day 10-13

    Con Dao// Enjoy the Resort at Leisure

    Breakfast at hotel & Full day at leisure

    Overnight Stay: Six Senses Con Dao Resort

  • Day 14

    Con Dao// Ho Chi Minh City// Departure

    Breakfast at hotel

    Transfer to airport for the flight to Ho Chi Minh to connect with your departure.(Own ticket)

SEVENTH HEAVEN: HONEYMOON IN MYANMAR

Programme Overview

Yangon, Bagan and Inle Lake

  • Duration: 7 Days

    Celebrate your new life together on this luxurious and romantic journey into one of Asia's most fascinatingly mysterious country

    Highlights : Short sightseeing and foot massage at one of the best spa of Yangon
    A bottle of champagne and special welcome present at the hotel in Yangon
    Sightseeing in Bagan of an Elevated Temple, Myinkaba village & Gubyaukgyi Temple
    Hot air ballooning, visit to the Colorful Nyaung Oo Market
    Sightseeing in Heho
    Excursion of village of Inpawkhone to observe the traditional silk weaving techniques of Inthar people
    Explore a cheroot factory to see hand rolled Burmese cigars
    Wine tasting and private dinner on the terrace at the Inle Princess Resort
    A private boat trip sightseeing along a small canal to the Pa-oh village of Indein
    Session of wine tasting at Red Mountain Estate Vineyard

    A&K Advantage
    An unforgettable fine dining experience with Felix Eppisser, one of Switzerland’s top Michelin star chefs restaurant Le Planteur, transit in a vintage British car. A truly romantic experience
    Sunset cruise & dinner on Ayeyarwaddy River and cocktail and canapés on the sand bank
    Participate in a special traditional Buddhist ceremony and receive special blessings from the monks
    Lunch on a rice carrier boat in the middle of Inle Lake
    Private Candlelit dinner organised in the Wine Cave at Inle Princess Resort
    Private shopping experience at Bogyoke Market (also known as Scott Market)

    Private arrival and departure airport transfers
    The services of an A&K Representative

    * On arrival in each country, you are welcomed by your A&K Representative who conducts a briefing on your time in the country. An A&K Representative is always at hand to provide unparalleled 24/7 support, be it local travel tips or reconfirming arrangement details, to providing recommendations for restaurants and shopping. Should you ever need help or advice, dial your A&K Representative’s mobile phone for prompt and courteous service at any time.

  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE YANGON–Sightseeing and special dinner at Le Planteur, a beautiful French restaurant

    On arrival at the Yangon airport, you will be welcomed by an A&K Representative, who will assist you through all the immigration procedures. You will be transferred to the hotel in a private vehicle and assisted with check-in and a special Burmese wedding present awaits you.

    En-route to your hotel, halt in town for a foot massage at Yangon’s best spa. This is the perfect way to relax and unwind after a long international flight.

    Depending on your arrival time, we recommend having lunch at Monsoon restaurant to relish freshly prepared authentic Myanmar cuisine.

    This afternoon, enjoy a private tour of Yangon's colonial city centre, with its many cultural delights along with an English speaking private guide. Yangon, formerly known as Rangoon, is a fairly quiet and appealing city with an impressive colonial and spiritual heritage. Travel past faded colonial buildings, busy street markets, and scintillating temples. Visit the Reclining Buddha, one of Myanmar’s largest icons at 230 feet long.

    The tour ends at the magnificent Shwedagon Pagoda the most revered Buddhist temple in Myanmar. Although the origins of the pagoda are vague, the local legend states that the original structure was built 25 centuries ago and renovated several times before taking its current structure as we see it today in the 15th century. The exterior of the shrine is plated with 8,688 solid gold slabs and a giant emerald sits in the middle to catch the first and last rays of the sun. The tip of the stupa, far too high for the human eye to discern in any detail, is set with 5448 diamonds, 2317 rubies, sapphires, and other gems, 1065 golden bells and, at the very top, a single 76-carat diamond. Your private guide will show you around the massive complex and enlighten you on its varied facts. Enjoy breathtaking views of the golden stupa from this vantage point and observe the devout praying, the evocative fragrance of incense enveloping you.

    Enjoy an unforgettable fine dining experience with Felix Eppisser, one of Switzerland’s top Michelin star chefs. Relish delicately prepared French food in the gardens of a stately colonial house, which appeals romance seekers to Le Planteur. Begin your dinner with a glass of Champagne and a selection of creative canapés, hot and cold. Continue with an elegant four course menu. Your dinner will be accompanied by a group of traditional musicians and dancers and a lovely and elaborate illumination by 1000 candles (not available during the rainy season). You will be transferred a vintage British car (Vauxhall 1947, Austin Somerset 1953 or Morris Oxford 1954). Pair your delicious meal with a wine from their well-stocked wine cellar having a storage capacity of 25,000 bottles with enormous collection that encompasses the world of wines, where affordable “trouvailles” meet the rarest of bottles and vintages.

    Return to your hotel room, where a bottle of champagne and special welcome present awaits you to toast your Honeymoon in Myanmar.

    Overnight: The Strand
    Meal Plan : D

  • Day 2

    YANGON–BAGAN–Visit to an Elevated Temple, Myinkaba village & Gubyaukgyi Temple, sunset cruise & dinner on Ayeyarwaddy River and sand bank

    This morning, after breakfast you will be transferred to the airport for your private charter flight to Nyaung Oo in a private vehicle. Upon arrival, you will be welcomed by a A&K Representative and transferred to your hotel by a private vehicle in style where a special wedding present will be waiting for you in your room.

    After freshening up, the excursion with an English speaking private guide begins on a horse carriage with a visit to an elevated temple with stunning views over the surrounding plains. The excursion is a picture-perfect overview of the grandeur of Bagan’s architecture. Next visit the Ananda Temple, one of the surviving masterpieces of the Mon architecture. Also famous as the finest, largest, best conserved and most regarded of the Bagan temples.

    On reaching Myinkaba Village, visit Gubyaukgyi Temple well noted for its gorgeous mural paintings on its inner walls and well-preserved plaster carvings on its exterior. The other temples close by are two smaller temples with nice Buddha statues and stone work, Manuha Temple and Nan Paya Temple.

    Enjoy a sunset cruise on the Ayeyarwaddy River and enjoy a cocktail and canapés on a sand bank. For many centuries, the mighty Ayeyarwaddy River served as Myanmar’s main artery and crucial highway. Celebrated in legend, myth, song and history, it is still vital to the country, sustaining life of every kind. The river-scape offers teak dwellings, cheerful waving youngsters, and local women balancing water jugs on their heads. Watch the sun set at a distance in the company of your loved one in these serene surroundings.

    The evening will be an exclusive and memorable experience of dining under the stars, on the sand bank, surrounded by torches and dinner will be served accompanied by traditional Burmese music and dancing.

    Overnight: Aureum Palace
    Meal Plan :B, D

  • Day 3

    BAGAN–Hot air ballooning, visit to the Colorful Nyaung Oo Market

    Enjoy an early morning sunrise over Bagan from aloft a hot air balloon. You will have a magnificent "bird's-eye" view of the thousand year-old pagodas, temples and the mighty Ayeyarwaddy River, as you float serenely across the “Golden Land”, as the rising sun illuminates the gold and sand-colored stupas.

    Begin today with a visit to the Taungbi Monastery where you will participate in a special traditional Buddhist ceremony. Afterwards you will receive a special blessing from the monks to ensure good luck and happiness. This auspicious occasion is sure to be an unforgettable part of your honeymoon in Myanmar.

    You may choose to explore the colourful local markets of Nyaung Oo at your pace.

    Afternoon is at leisure.

    You may choose to indulge in a special private couple spa treatment. The spa is a majestic sanctuary that uniquely resonates with a timeless echo of ancient philosophies with remedies for body, mind and soul.

    Dinner at the hotel will be organised in an elegant, romantic private setting especially for you. Enjoy the beautiful ambience with your loved one sipping wine or a cocktail and romancing under the moon light.

    Overnight: Aureum Palace
    Meal Plan :B, D

  • Day 4

    BAGAN–HEHO–INLE LAKE–Sightseeing, tour of silk weaving and cheroot factory

    Enjoy a leisurely breakfast in bed or at the poolside of your private island villa.

    After breakfast you will be privately transferred to Nyaung Oo Airport for your private charter flight to Heho. Here you will enjoy a one-hour scenic drive to Nyaung Shwe, the gateway village to the lovely Inle Lake.

    Venture on the lake in a private boat. Explore the lake's main sanctuary, Phaung Daw Oo Pagoda, and the Nga Phe Chaung Monastery, well known as Jumping Cat's Monastery which has the five sacred Buddha statues.

    Continue to your hotel for check-in. A fruit basket and welcome gift will be waiting in your room.

    Your lunch will be served on a rice carrier boat in the middle of Inle Lake. The boat has been transformed into a fully equipped ephemeral restaurant. This is an exclusive experience for you to amaze and surprise your loved one. The restaurant boat is catered by service and kitchen boats. The menu includes local appetizers, a choice of several main dishes and special dessert of the day.

    Visit the village of Inpawkhone to observe the traditional silk weaving techniques of Inthar people. Inpawkhone is also famous for weaving from lotus flower stems, a laborious process that results in extraordinary quality materials famed throughout the country.

    You will also explore a cheroot factory where local Burmese cigars are rolled by hand.

    Your romantic experience continues with a wine tasting at the Inle Princess Resort, before enjoying a relaxed quixotic private dinner on the terrace.

    Overnight: Inle Princess Resort
    Meal Plan :B,L ,D

  • Day 5

    INLE LAKE–IN DEIN–Explore area on foot, visit to Red Mountain Estate Vineyard

    Breakfast will be in the privacy of your chalet’s terrace overlooking the lake.

    This morning, enjoy a private boat trip along a small canal to the Pa-oh village of Indein. Explore the area on foot at your pace, strolling around the village, wandering through the beautiful Alaung Sitthou area. Alaung Sitthou is filled with picturesque ancient stupas and has magnificent views of the lake below.

    Located along the beautiful Inle Lake, in the high country Shan State of Myanmar, sits the Red Mountain Estate Vineyards & Winery, which produces the best wines of the country. Since the establishment, the wines have been produced with locally grown grapes. All of the original 400,000 plants were imported from France, Spain and Israel and were specially chosen after experimentations from the French winemaker. The wines are made in the same tradition as the western wines, utilizing all new technologies and modern equipment imported from Italy. Enjoy a session of wine tasting.

    • A private & romantic candlelit dinner is organised in the Wine Cave at the resort especially for you and your loved one.

    Overnight: Inle Princess Resort
    Meal Plan :B,D

  • Day 6

    INLE–HEHO–YANGON–Visit to Musmeah Yeshua, private shopping experience at Bogyoke market

    After breakfast you will be transferred by a private charter flight to Yangon. Venture off the beaten path on a tour of Musmeah Yeshua, the last remaining Jewish synagogue in Myanmar. Built in 1896 to replace an earlier building erected in 1854, the synagogue is a remnant from Myanmar's colonial period. Explore the Bogyoke Market (also known as Scott Market), known for its colonial architecture and inner cobblestone streets with a private shopper for a special shopping experience. Browse the aisles of handmade goods, looking for the perfect souvenir of your Myanmar holiday. Before leaving the market, don’t miss the opportunity to know what the future holds for you by visiting a famous Myanmar fortune teller, San Zar Ni Bo. He will read your palm and discuss your future. Enjoy a romantic candle lit dinner at The Strand Grill. A classically trained guitarist in this timeless elegance accompanies delectable dishes for fining dining.

    Overnight: The Strand
    Meal Plan :B,D

  • Day 7

    DEPART YANGON

    Your private car and guide will be at your disposal until it's time to be transferred to the airport.

    ***************************************End of A&K services**************************************

  • Day 1

    India/Bangkok, Thailand

    ARRIVE BANGKOK–At leisure
    On Arrival at Bangkok, Thailand's capital city, where you are met by your A&K representative and provided with VIP assistance through customs and immigration procedures. You will then be transferred in a private vehicle to your hotel and assisted with check-in. The legendary Mandarin Oriental is set on the banks of the Chao Phraya River.

    You can cool off in one of the lovely pools surrounded by tropical gardens or visit the renowned spa.

    After having high tea in the hotel lobby lounge, the rest of the day is at your leisure.

    Overnight: Mandarin Oriental
    Meal Plan: High Tea

    Overnight: Mandarin Oriental, Bangkok
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    Bangkok

    BANGKOK–Attend a private traditional food offering ceremony to the monks, River cruise in a private long tailed boat, tuk-tuk ride, Ladyboy Cabaret show with a romantic dinner
    As the sun rises across the river, you will attend a private traditional ceremony, where food offering will be made to the monks and blessing of good karma received.

    Cruise along the river in a private long-tailed boat through the busy khlongs (canals) for a glimpse of the waterborne life in "the Venice of the East". Keep your eyes peeled and your shutter bug ready as you pass by the Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn). Made of many pieces of cracked porcelain ornately carved out of sandstone, the temple seems to glisten in the sunlight, while its rich images of demons, monkeys and guardians cast an eerie picture across the city.

    Disembark and take a tuk-tuk (motorized tricycle) ride to the Grand Palace, which reveals the splendour of early Rattanakosin architecture and is one of the most beautiful examples of an ancient Siamese court. So small and distant that it can hardly be seen, little is known about the famed Emerald Buddha in sacred Wat Phra Kaew Temple, except that it is made of green jade or jasper and denotes independence, strength and good fortune for the country. Within the temple, interior walls are painted with superb frescoes.

    Afternoon is at leisure. You may choose to take a sunset cocktail cruise on a local rice barge.

    Be entertained at a famous Ladyboy Cabaret show. An explosion of lights sounds and pouts a breathless amalgamation of splendid sets, glittering costumes and electrifying musical acts. Be dazzled by the show and get smitten by the gender-straddling performers.

    After a fantabulous show, enjoy one of the most romantic dinners in Bangkok together with an excellent and an authentic Thai cuisine at Issaya Siamese Club Restaurant. A table would have been reserved for you at this charming restaurant on a small street near Rama IV Road. Planned in a century old house with a wooden verandah and an old fashioned staircase it instantly sets a romantic mood, making it a perfect place for duo dining.

    Overnight: Mandarin Oriental
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    BANGKOK–Shopping with a personal shopper

    Shopping experience in Bangkok goes far beyond a stroll in a mall or exploring the local markets. Shopping in Bangkok can be a day-and-night activity giving a flavour of the cultural uniqueness of the East. Explore these entirely different experiences with a personal shopper, and you'll soon come to realize why Bangkok is such a famous shopping hub.

    You may explore the hotel's restaurant for meals as they offer delectable cuisines with unimaginably gorgeous settings.

    Overnight: Mandarin Oriental
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    BANGKOK–CHIANG RAI–At leisure enjoy a nature walk or a sundowner & dinner at the wine cellar

    Today you will take a flight to remote Chiang Rai, where you are transferred in style in a private river boat to your luxurious tented camp set deep in the heart of the Golden Triangle, where Thailand, Myanmar and Laos meet.

    Day is at leisure.

    You may take a nature walk in the jungle and pause for a glimpse of the remote villages.

    Settle down with a cocktail in hand as you watch the sun dip over the horizon in the company of your loved one. The view is stunningly beautiful and will leave you with unforgettable memories.

    Enjoy a private, romantic meal in the wine cellar set especially for you. .

    Overnight: Four Seasons Tented Camp Golden Triangle
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    CHIANG RAI–Mahout training, river safari and spa

    After a romantic breakfast in bed, you will learn the basic skills of a mahout (trainer) at the on-site elephant camp. You will receive traditional mahout outfits and have an opportunity to practice on an exploration of the Thai jungle.

    After lunch, you will go on a short river safari. Explore the life along the banks of the river and appreciate breathtaking views of the Mekong River.

    Rediscover inner peace by indulging in a rejuvenating private couple spa nestled amidst the bamboo forests in open-air salas.

    Dinner at the camp is an event each night. Enjoy your last night at Chiang Rai.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Tented Camp Golden Triangle
    Meal Plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    CHIANG RAI–CHIANG MAI–Paddy field excursion & night market exploration

    After breakfast, depart by private helicopter for Chiang Mai, where you will be welcomed by a A&K Representative and assisted with check-in to your resort hotel. Chiang Mai, capital of the Lana Kingdom, this ancient destination is known as the cultural capital of Thailand. Set in the breathtakingly beautiful Mae Rim Valley against the backdrop of the mountains, your resort is surrounded by 20 acres of landscaped tropical gardens and rice paddies. Your luxurious room has its own private outdoor sala that is ideal for absorbing the ambience and beauty of the area.

    This afternoon, you will leave for a short excursion to the paddy fields. Learn how rice is grown when you meet farmers and lend a hand in transplanting rice sprouts from the seedbed to the rice fields (season permitting).

    Later in the evening, head to Chiang Mai's famous Night Market, a shopper's paradise. Your private shopper will assist you explore the largest and most colorful bazaar of Southeast Asia, and see a swirling kaleidoscope of merchants, entertainment and food carts.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Resort
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    CHIANG MAI–Waroros market, thrilling rickshaw ride exploration of the city an exclusive candlelit dinner

    After breakfast, your day begins with a city tour of Chiang Mai. Visit the Waroros Market, a massive complex of stalls where you can find almost anything. Take a thrilling rickshaw ride through Chiang Mai's bustling streets to see the monastery of Wat Phra Singh, passing the old city wall on the way. It is home to one of Thailand's most famous images: the mysterious and powerful Pra Buddha Sing (Lion Buddha). After an uphill ride along a hairpin switchback, you arrive at Wat Prathat (Doi Suthep), tucked away in the mountains.
