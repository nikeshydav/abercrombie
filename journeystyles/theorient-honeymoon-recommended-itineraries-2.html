HONEYMOON IN VIETNAM: BEACHES AND MORE

Programme Overview

Hanoi, Halong, Hoi An, hoi Chi Minh City and Con Dao

  • Duration: 12 Days/11 Nights

    Highlights
    Explore Duong Lam Village & cycle tour
    Temple of Literature
    Water puppet show
    A visit to Vung Vieng floating fishing village by Kayak
    A walking tour of Hoi An
    Visit to Tra Que Village
    Look into the notorious small town of Cu Chi
    Con Dao Sightseeing – Monuments, museum and Prison

    A&K Advantage
    Bhaya Legend I Cruise-Private luxury cruise on Halong Bay
    Learning the art of making colourful lanterns
    Saigon sightseeing on a Vintage Vespa along with a private guide
    A gourmet picnic lunch on the beach of Con Dao
    A private Hobie catamaran with a champagne sundowner
    An intimate candlelit dinner on a secluded spot on the white sandy beach of Con Dao

    Private arrival and departure airport transfers
    The services of an A&K Representative

    * On arrival in each country, you are welcomed by your A&K Representative who conducts a briefing on your time in the country. An A&K Representative is always at hand to provide unparalleled 24/7 support, be it local travel tips or reconfirming arrangement details, to providing recommendations for restaurants and shopping. Should you ever need help or advice, dial your A&K Representative’s mobile phone for prompt and courteous service at any time.

  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE HANOI

    On arrival you will be met by an A&K Representative and transferred to your hotel by a private vehicle and assisted with check-in. The hotel is exclusively placed over the tranquil waters of West Lake only minutes from Hanoi’s well-known Old Quarter.

    Day is at leisure.

    Enjoy a drink at the hotel’s Sunset Bar, and watch the sun set across the lake. A beautiful view of the skyline creates a romantic ambience.

    For dinner relish the freshest blend of Vietnamese cuisine in a restaurant featuring modern-day Asian design, vivid glass fronted kitchens and stimulating sights of the city's transforming skyline.

    Overnight: Intercontinental Hanoi Westlake
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    HANOI–Visit to Duong Lam Village, Temple of Literature & Water puppet show

    After breakfast, you make a visit to Duong Lam Village. The ancient village has a history of about 1,200 years with many houses dating back up to 400 years. A special thing about the village is that most of the structures here are made of laterite and mud, abundant in the area. Apart from its historical and tourism values, it is a significant place for scientists to study resident communities in ancient agriculture. You may enjoy lunch in the village before heading back to Hanoi.

    After lunch visit the Temple of Literature, founded in 1070 and dedicated to Confucius. It honors scholars and men of literary accomplishment.

    Later take a, exploratory cycle ride into the Old Quarter area, characterized by numerous narrow streets, dating back to the 15th century, and each bearing a name that reflects the business once conducted there.

    Drop by Thang Long theatre to enjoy famous Water Puppet Show, unique Vietnamese traditional art. This ancient art originated in the countryside by the farmers who worked on the paddy fields. The farmers amused themselves during times of flooding, by performing water puppet shows out in the paddy fields. The artists-men and women-stand waist-deep in the water and manipulate the puppets from behind the curtains, making them move about, talk, laugh and even dance on water surface.

    Overnight: Intercontinental Hanoi Westlake
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    HANOI–HALONG (Overnight Cruise)–Visit Vung Vieng floating fishing village by Kayak

    After breakfast, you will be transferred by a private vehicle to Halong bay to board Bhaya Legend I Cruise, a private luxury cruise.

    Embark on Bhaya Legend and enjoy you will be welcomed with a cocktail slowly sailing to Bai Tu Long, passing the setting for the movie Indochina, Mat Quay and Am Tich Islets.

    Relish a sumptuous fresh seafood lunch on-board.

    Bhaya Legend moves on into Vung Vieng floating fishing village, nestled among the limestone peaks, unaffected by ocean waves or strong winds. Visit the village by kayak or a small boat to see the daily lives of locals up close.

    Swim or kayak the turquoise waters off the pristine white sandy beaches. Watch the sunset over enchanting Halong Bay while cruising to the overnight anchorage with wine in hand in the company of your loved one, on the sundeck of your luxury vessel.

    Enjoy a romantic candlelit set-dinner on board.

    Retire to your cabin or join the fishing program.

    Overnight: Bhaya Legend I Cruise
    Meal Plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    HALONG–HANOI–DANANG–HOI AN–Activity on board

    Appreciate the beauty of the sunrise, lapping waves, fresh sea air, and gentle breeze over the bay with cup of tea or coffee on the sundeck. Or wake up at your leisure.

    The crew will prepare the vessel to sail to a destination of your choice. You could decide between sightseeing at Sung Sot Grotto, or hiking up Titop Mountain or being adventurous for lagoon kayaking or swimming on a deserted beach.

    Brunch is served on board. Bhaya Legend returns to Tuan Chau Island at 11 am.

    On return to Hanoi, you will be received by your private shopper and will be free to explore and exploit the exclusive shopping areas.

    Later you will take a private charter flight to Danang.

    Upon arrival, you will be received by a A&K Representative and privately transferred to check-in to the hotel at Hoi An, a town by Thu Bon River and has been declared as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. It was once the chief trading center for silk, porcelain, pepper, cinnamon and medicinal plants. In spite of the wars of the 19th and 20th centuries the town's characteristic architecture remains intact.

    Romance under the open sky and enjoy a romantic candlelit dinner sophistically setup on the private beach area right outside your suite.

    Overnight: Victoria Hoi An Resort
    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 5

    HOI AN–Walking tour Hoi An

    A steamy aromatic rose petal bath for you and your partner would be awaiting you–a perfect way to pamper yourselves and begin your day.

    Enjoy leisurely breakfast in bed.

    Take a walking tour of Hoi An, sightsee the historic town which used to be a flourishing seaport city during the 16th to 18th centuries. Explore the colorful local market, Tan Ky old house, Fukien Assembly Hall, and the 400-year-old Japanese covered bridge.

    After lunch, you can also watch local residents raise silkworms and produce silk for Hoi An's flourishing textile industry.

    Evening is at leisure.

    Overnight: Victoria Hoi An Resort
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    HOI AN–Visit to Tra Que Village and Lantern making

    After breakfast you will be visiting Tra Que Village. A drink of freshly picked fruits will await you at the home of your host, Mr. Tuan, before you take a walk around the charming village and learn about its history and customs. On walking a short distance you will reach the vegetable gardens where hundreds of local farmers work their fields using century old traditional methods - no electrical machinery used till date.

    The real fun begins once you return to Mr. Tuan’s residence. You will be given farming instruments, and set to join the farmers in preparing the land for sowing. Your private guide describes the traditional farming methods while you help fertilize the garden using seaweed from the local lake, and then water it using huge tin cans strapped to your back. Time is then spent picking vegetables and herbs, and learning how to wrap them to prevent damage. Many of the local herbs are used by the farmers for traditional village medicines.

    It's now time to try your hands at making the village specialty named “Tam Huu” spring rolls - using the local herbs and vegetables you have just picked or Xeo Cake (Banh Xeo). You may then enjoy a lovely local meal.

    Colourful lanterns are not only the symbol of Hoi An’s but also a part of its spirit. Flickering light of these beautiful lanterns at night is alluring to all. You will have the opportunity to learn the art of making these beautiful lanterns by visiting the home of a craftsman in the old streets. A brief introduction to the history, shape, and color of the lanterns as well as the many stages of making them will be provided. You may make your own lantern as a symbolism of your honeymoon and keep it as a souvenir.

    Overnigh: Victoria Hoi An Resort
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    HOI AN–DANANG–HO CHI MINH CITY–Visit Cu Chi

    After a leisurely breakfast, you will be transferred by a private vehicle to the airport for your flight to Ho Chi Minh City.

    On arrival, you will be met by your private guide and visit Cu Chi, a small community located in the northwest of Ho Chi Minh City. It gained notoriety for its network of deep tunnels that provided shelter and an effective source of infiltration for the Viet Cong during the later years of the Vietnam War. The tunnels, which stretched hundreds of miles in total length, housed storage rooms, make-shift hospitals and sleeping quarters that accommodated rebel fighters for up to two weeks at a time.

    On returning to Ho Chi Minh City you will be assisted with check-in and welcomed with a glass of Champagne.

    Evening is at leisure for you to relax. Enjoy innovative cocktails at the hotel’s Jade Bar or other tempting drinks.

    Overnight: Intercontinental Asiana Saigon
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    HO CHI MINH CITY–Saigon sightseeing by Vintage Vespa

    Enjoy a relaxed romantic breakfast in bed.

    After breakfast sightsee the city romantically on a Vintage Vespa. One of the best way to get around and explore real Saigon local life bustle, go on a guided tour with a private guide on a ride beside you. Explore the historic Reunification Hall, down Dong Khoi Street – passing the red-brick Notre Dame Cathedral, elegant Post Office and Opera House – before cruising down central Le Loi and out to Ben Thanh Market, squeezing through backpacker Pham Ngu Lao, out to the Chinese Cholon district and back to the city. Vespa tour of modern-day Ho Chi Minh City will give you a view of greater Saigon unlike anything you'll see from the window of your private car.

    Evening is at leisure. You may choose to unwind and relax with a private romantic couple spa treatment at the hotels spa.

    Overnight: Intercontinental Asiana Saigon
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    HO CHI MINH–CON DAO–At leisure

    After breakfast, you will be transferred to the airport in style for your flight to Con Dao. On arrival at Con Dao you will met by a A&K Representative and be transferred in a private vehicle to Six Senses Hotel where you will be assisted with check-in.

    Con Dao is a spectacular location, which has been sheltered for decades as a national and marine park.

    Six Senses Con Dao was the first five-star resort in the archipelago islands, modern in design and reflects the true spirit of a traditional fishing village. Recognised as one of world’s best eco lodges it is zealous about supporting and protecting the environment.

    Afternoon is at leisure to relax in your private infinity pool or walk hand in hand on the beach which can be directly accessed from your villa.

    Enjoy a panoramic view of the horizon and get fascinated seeing the sky change colours as the sun sets. Wine will be on offer to add to the romance and beauty of the sunset in the privacy of the beachfront of your villa. As the sun dissolves into a starlit night, you can share a romantic kiss or two as you enjoy on the sandy beach.

    You may choose to dine in the privacy of your villa with a private butler service.

    Overnight: Six Senses Resort
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    CON DAO–Snorkeling, picnic lunch and catamaran ride sundowner

    Enjoy your aroma bath with rose petals, candles and a romantic breakfast in bed.

    Since Can Dao is considered as the best for marine conservation in the country, supporting a high level of bio diversity, over 340 species of coral of varied colours and hues, snorkeling in the turquoise waters will surely be justified. Explore the colourful and exotic marine life.

    To add to the fun, enjoy a gourmet picnic lunch on the beach. Elegantly set with a private butler, it will be nothing less than a luxurious experience.

    Hopping aboard a private Hobie catamaran is a little bit of heaven. Feel the soft breeze brush your face, the beauty and charm of the view mesmerise you. Sail in the fisherman boats to that point in the horizon where the sky seems to blend with the sea. Enjoy some champagne to celebrate your honeymoon on this sundowner ride, as you watch the sun setting at a far end over the horizon with your loved one.

    Overnight: Six Senses Resort
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    CON DAO–Sightseeing and special farewell dinner

    After breakfast, take a short exploratory trip of Con Dao. Explore the monuments, museum & the prison to know about the history and mystery of Con Dao Islands.

    Day is at leisure.

    You may choose to explore the local market and town of Con Dao at your pace.

    Or you may simply stimulate and rejuvenate you senses in enjoy each other’s company during a private couple spa at an outdoor treatment salas.

    Evening is at leisure.

    A special farewell dinner will be organized. An intimate candlelit dinner on a secluded spot on the white sandy beach, with elegant setting under the moonlit sky and torches lit around will set the mood for the perfect ambience of a romantic dinner. A private butler will serve you.

    Overnight: Six Senses Resort
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 12

    CON DAO–Enjoy the Resort at Leisure

    Post a lavish breakfast on the beach; you will be transferred to the airport in a private vehicle for your private charter flight to Ho Chi Minh City to catch your flight ahead.

    Meal Plan : B

SEVENTH HEAVEN: HONEYMOON IN MYANMAR

Programme Overview

Yangon, Bagan and Inle Lake

  • Duration: 7 Days/6 Nights

    Highlights
    Short sightseeing and foot massage at one of the best spa of Yangon
    A bottle of champagne and special welcome present at the hotel in Yangon
    Sightseeing in Bagan of an Elevated Temple, Myinkaba village & Gubyaukgyi Temple
    Hot air ballooning, visit to the Colorful Nyaung Oo Market
    Sightseeing in Heho
    Excursion of village of Inpawkhone to observe the traditional silk weaving techniques of Inthar people
    Explore a cheroot factory to see hand rolled Burmese cigars
    Wine tasting and private dinner on the terrace at the Inle Princess Resort
    A private boat trip sightseeing along a small canal to the Pa-oh village of Indein
    Session of wine tasting at Red Mountain Estate Vineyard

    A&K Advantage
    An unforgettable fine dining experience with Felix Eppisser, one of Switzerland’s top Michelin star chefs restaurant Le Planteur, transit in a vintage British car. A truly romantic experience
    Sunset cruise & dinner on Ayeyarwaddy River and cocktail and canapés on the sand bank
    Participate in a special traditional Buddhist ceremony and receive special blessings from the monks
    Lunch on a rice carrier boat in the middle of Inle Lake
    Private Candlelit dinner organised in the Wine Cave at Inle Princess Resort
    Private shopping experience at Bogyoke Market (also known as Scott Market)

    Private arrival and departure airport transfers
    The services of an A&K Representative

    * On arrival in each country, you are welcomed by your A&K Representative who conducts a briefing on your time in the country. An A&K Representative is always at hand to provide unparalleled 24/7 support, be it local travel tips or reconfirming arrangement details, to providing recommendations for restaurants and shopping. Should you ever need help or advice, dial your A&K Representative’s mobile phone for prompt and courteous service at any time.

  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE YANGON–Sightseeing and special dinner at Le Planteur, a beautiful French restaurant

    On arrival at the Yangon airport, you will be welcomed by an A&K Representative, who will assist you through all the immigration procedures. You will be transferred to the hotel in a private vehicle and assisted with check-in and a special Burmese wedding present awaits you.

    En-route to your hotel, halt in town for a foot massage at Yangon’s best spa. A perfect way to relax and unwind after a long international flight.

    Depending on your arrival time, we recommend having lunch at Monsoon restaurant to relish freshly prepared authentic Myanmar cuisine.

    This afternoon, enjoy a private tour of Yangon's colonial city centre, with its many cultural delights along with an English speaking private guide. Yangon, formerly known as Rangoon, is a fairly quiet and appealing city with an impressive colonial and spiritual heritage. Travel past faded colonial buildings, busy street markets, and scintillating temples. Visit the Reclining Buddha, one of Myanmar’s largest icons at 230 feet long.

    The tour ends at the magnificent Shwedagon Pagoda the most revered Buddhist temple in Myanmar. Although the origins of the pagoda are vague, the local legend states that the original structure was built 25 centuries ago and renovated several times before taking its current structure as we see it today in the 15th century. The exterior of the shrine is plated with 8,688 solid gold slabs and a giant emerald sits in the middle to catch the first and last rays of the sun. The tip of the stupa, far too high for the human eye to discern in any detail, is set with 5448 diamonds, 2317 rubies, sapphires, and other gems, 1065 golden bells and, at the very top, a single 76-carat diamond. Your private guide will show you around the massive complex and enlighten you on its varied facts. Enjoy breathtaking views of the golden stupa from this vantage point and observe the devout praying, the evocative fragrance of incense enveloping you.

    Enjoy an unforgettable fine dining experience with Felix Eppisser, one of Switzerland’s top Michelin star chefs. Relish delicately prepared French food in the gardens of a stately colonial house, which appeals romance seekers to Le Planteur. Begin your dinner with a glass of Champagne and a selection of creative canapés, hot and cold. Continue with an elegant four course menu. Your dinner will be accompanied by a group of traditional musicians and dancers and a lovely and elaborate illumination by 1000 candles (not available during the rainy season). You will be transferred by a vintage British car (Vauxhall 1947, Austin Somerset 1953 or Morris Oxford 1954). Pair your delicious meal with a wine from their well-stocked wine cellar having a storage capacity of 25,000 bottles, an enormous collection that encompasses the world of wines, where affordable “trouvailles” meet the rarest of bottles and vintages.

    Return to your hotel room, where a bottle of champagne and special welcome present awaits you to toast your Honeymoon in Myanmar.

    Overnight: The Strand
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    YANGON–BAGAN–Visit to an Elevated Temple, Myinkaba village & Gubyaukgyi Temple, sunset cruise & dinner on Ayeyarwaddy River and sand bank

    This morning, after breakfast you will be transferred to the airport for your private charter flight to Nyaung Oo in a private vehicle. Upon arrival, you will be welcomed by an A&K Representative and transferred to your hotel by a private vehicle in style where a special wedding present will be waiting for you in your room.

    After freshening up, the excursion with an English speaking private guide begins on a horse carriage with a visit to an elevated temple with stunning views over the surrounding plains. The excursion is a picture-perfect overview of the grandeur of Bagan’s architecture. Next, visit the Ananda Temple, one of the surviving masterpieces of the Mon architecture. Also famous as the finest, largest, best conserved and most regarded of the Bagan temples.

    On reaching Myinkaba Village, visit Gubyaukgyi Temple well noted for its gorgeous mural paintings on its inner walls and well-preserved plaster carvings on its exterior. The other temples close by are two smaller temples with nice Buddha statues and stone work, Manuha Temple and Nan Paya Temple.

    Enjoy a sunset cruise on the Ayeyarwaddy River and enjoy a cocktail and canapés on a sand bank. For many centuries, the mighty Ayeyarwaddy River served as Myanmar’s main artery and crucial highway. Celebrated in legend, myth, song and history, it is still vital to the country, sustaining life of every kind. The river-scape offers teak dwellings, cheerful waving youngsters, and local women balancing water jugs on their heads. Watch the sun set at a distance in the company of your loved one in these serene surroundings.

    The evening will be an exclusive and memorable experience of dining under the stars, on the sand bank, surrounded by torches and dinner will be served accompanied by traditional Burmese music and dancing.

    Overnight: Aureum Palace
    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 3

    BAGAN–Hot air ballooning, visit to the Colorful Nyaung Oo Market

    Enjoy an early morning sunrise over Bagan from aloft a hot air balloon. You will have a magnificent "bird's-eye" view of the thousand year-old pagodas, temples and the mighty Ayeyarwaddy River, as you float serenely across the “Golden Land”, as the rising sun illuminates the gold and sand-colored stupas.

    Begin today with a visit to the Taungbi Monastery where you will participate in a special traditional Buddhist ceremony. Afterwards you will receive a special blessing from the monks to ensure good luck and happiness. This auspicious occasion is sure to be an unforgettable part of your honeymoon in Myanmar.

    You may choose to explore the colourful local markets of Nyaung Oo at your pace.

    Afternoon is at leisure.

    You may choose to indulge in a special private couple spa treatment. The spa is a majestic sanctuary that uniquely resonates with a timeless echo of ancient philosophies with remedies for body, mind and soul.

    Dinner at the hotel will be organised in an elegant, romantic private setting especially for you. Enjoy the beautiful ambience with your loved one sipping wine or a cocktail and romancing under the moon light.

    Overnight: Aureum Palace
    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 4

    BAGAN–HEHO–INLE LAKE–Sightseeing, tour of silk weaving and cheroot factory

    Enjoy a leisurely breakfast in bed or at the poolside of your private island villa.

    After breakfast you will be privately transferred to Nyaung Oo Airport for your private charter flight to Heho. Here you will enjoy a one-hour scenic drive to Nyaung Shwe, the gateway village to the lovely Inle Lake.

    Venture on the lake in a private boat. Explore the lake's main sanctuary, Phaung Daw Oo Pagoda, and the Nga Phe Chaung Monastery, well known as Jumping Cat's Monastery which has the five sacred Buddha statues.

    Continue to your hotel for check-in. A fruit basket and welcome gift will be waiting in your room.

    Your lunch will be served on a rice carrier boat in the middle of Inle Lake. The boat has been transformed into a fully equipped ephemeral restaurant. This is an exclusive experience for you to amaze and surprise your loved one. The restaurant boat is catered by service and kitchen boats. The menu includes local appetizers, a choice of several main dishes and special dessert of the day.

    Visit the village of Inpawkhone to observe the traditional silk weaving techniques of Inthar people. Inpawkhone is also famous for weaving from lotus flower stems, a laborious process that results in extraordinary quality materials famed throughout the country.

    You will also explore a cheroot factory where local Burmese cigars are rolled by hand.

    Your romantic experience continues with wine tasting at the Inle Princess Resort, before enjoying a relaxed quixotic private dinner on the terrace.

    Overnight: Inle Princess Resort
    Meal Plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    INLE LAKE–IN DEIN–Explore area on foot, visit to Red Mountain Estate Vineyard

    Breakfast will be in the privacy of your chalet’s terrace overlooking the lake.

    This morning, enjoy a private boat trip along a small canal to the Pa-oh village of Indein. Explore the area on foot at your pace, strolling around the village, wandering through the beautiful Alaung Sitthou area. Alaung Sitthou is filled with picturesque ancient stupas and has magnificent views of the lake below.

    Located along the beautiful Inle Lake, in the high country Shan State of Myanmar, sits the Red Mountain Estate Vineyards & Winery, which produces the best wines of the country. Since the establishment, the wines have been produced with locally grown grapes. All of the original 400,000 plants were imported from France, Spain and Israel and were specially chosen after experimentations from the French winemaker. The wines are made in the same tradition as the western wines, utilizing all new technologies and modern equipment imported from Italy. Enjoy a session of wine tasting.

    A private & romantic candlelit dinner is organised in the Wine Cave at the resort especially for you and your loved one.

    Overnight: Inle Princess Resort
    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 6

    INLE–HEHO–YANGON–Visit to Musmeah Yeshua, private shopping experience at Bogyoke market

    After breakfast you will be transferred by a private charter flight to Yangon.

    Venture off the beaten path on a tour of Musmeah Yeshua, the last remaining Jewish synagogue in Myanmar. Built in 1896 to replace an earlier building erected in 1854, the synagogue is a remnant from Myanmar's colonial period.

    Explore the Bogyoke Market (also known as Scott Market), known for its colonial architecture and inner cobblestone streets with a private shopper for a special shopping experience. Browse the aisles of handmade goods, looking for the perfect souvenir of your Myanmar holiday. Before leaving the market, don’t miss the opportunity to know what the future holds for you by visiting a famous Myanmar fortune teller, San Zar Ni Bo. He will read your palm and discuss your future.

    Enjoy a romantic candle lit dinner at The Strand Grill. A classically trained guitarist in this timeless elegance accompanies delectable dishes for fining dining.

    Overnight: The Strand
    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 7

    DEPART YANGON

    Your private car and guide will be at your disposal until it's time to be transferred to the airport.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE BANGKOK–At leisure

    On arrival at Bangkok, Thailand's capital city, you are met by your A&K representative and provided with VIP assistance through customs and immigration procedures. You will then be transferred in a private vehicle to your hotel and assisted with check-in. The legendary Mandarin Oriental is set on the banks of the Chao Phraya River.

    You can cool off in one of the lovely pools surrounded by tropical gardens or visit the renowned spa.

    After having high tea in the hotel lobby lounge, the rest of the day is at your leisure.

    Overnight: Mandarin Oriental, Bangkok
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    BANGKOK–Attend a private traditional food offering ceremony to the monks, River cruise in a private long tailed boat, tuk-tuk ride, Ladyboy Cabaret show with a romantic dinner

    As the sun rises across the river, you will attend a private traditional ceremony, where food offering will be made to the monks and blessing of good karma received.

    Cruise along the river in a private long-tailed boat through the busy khlongs (canals) for a glimpse of the waterborne life in "the Venice of the East". Keep your eyes peeled and your shutter bug ready as you pass by the Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn). Made of many pieces of cracked porcelain ornately carved out of sandstone, the temple seems to glisten in the sunlight, while its rich images of demons, monkeys and guardians cast an eerie picture across the city.

    Disembark and take a tuk-tuk (motorized tricycle) ride to the Grand Palace, which reveals the splendour of early Rattanakosin architecture and is one of the most beautiful examples of an ancient Siamese court. So small and distant that it can hardly be seen, little is known about the famed Emerald Buddha in sacred Wat Phra Kaew Temple, except that it is made of green jade or jasper and denotes independence, strength and good fortune for the country. Within the temple, interior walls are painted with superb frescoes.

A table would have been reserved for you at this charming restaurant on a small street near Rama IV Road.
