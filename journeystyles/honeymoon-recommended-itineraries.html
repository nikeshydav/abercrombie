PRAGUE FAIRYTALE CITY

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 Days

    A fantastic 4 day visit to the romantic, fairy-tale city of Prague. Meander through the cobbled streets flanked with beautiful architecture and take in the magic of Prague's history and culture.

    Highlights: Explore Prague's charming Old Town, stroll along the Charles Bridge and discover the wonderful Baroque architecture of the Little Quarter

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE IN PRAGUE

    Upon arrival at Prague airport you will be met in the arrival hall by your driver and privately transferred to your hotel. The hotel Augustine exudes history and elegance and has been created from 7 different buildings including the 13th century Augustinian monastery after which the hotel has been named.

    Within short walking distance of the character and enchanting history of Prague's old town, you may enjoy a romantic evening stroll, or a quiet drink in the hotel's intimate St Thomas brewery bar, housed in the cellar.

    For your honeymoon enjoy a complimentary rose petal bath and breakfast in bed on your first morning. You will also receive 50% off couples massage treatments to ensure you can fully relax and feel pampered during your stay.*

    *honeymoon promotion valid throughout 2013

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel

  • Day 2

    PRAGUE

    Meet your English-speaking local guide and enjoy a half-day walking tour (4 hours) of the Old Town with its winding cobbled streets and fairy-tale charm and the fascinating Jewish Quarter, birthplace of Franz Kafka. See the striking Gothic Town Hall in Old Town Square, famous for its unique astronomical clock, and visit the Jewish Museum which houses the largest collection of Judaica in Europe, the Old-New Synagogue and the very moving Jewish Cemetery. You may also enjoy a walk across the romantically picturesque 14th century Charles Bridge. This afternoon is at your leisure for you to relax and enjoy the facilities of your hotel. Or, for those keen to explore this delightful city further, why not venture to Prague's Castle District. Hradcany Castle was the seat of Bohemian kings and Holy Roman emperors. See the Cathedral of St. Vitus, one of the finest and most richly endowed in central Europe, and the Basilica of St. George which was founded in 905. Stroll along the Golden Lane, a row of tiny and colourful houses dating from the 16th century.

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    PRAGUE

    Meet your local guide and enjoy a walking tour (3 hours) of the picturesque Lesser Town (Mala Strana). Also known as The Pearl of Baroque Architecture, the Lesser Town is one of Prague's oldest districts and full of captivating history and hidden gems which can't fail to enchant you. The most extensive and unforgettable building of this era is Albrecht von Wallenstein's Palace with five courtyards and a magnificent garden set as a French Park (please note that the palace is only open on Saturdays & Sundays and garden is opened daily from April till October).

    You may also like to see the Lesser Town square with the beautiful baroque St Nicholas Church and perhaps visit the Carmelitan church, The Church of Our Lady Victorious, with the famous statue of the Holy Infant Jesus of Prague. Or take a visit to the lovely Vrtba's Gardens, near the Church of Our Lady Victorious (open daily from April till October).

    If music is the food of love, what better way to spend the afternoon that with a private musical tour of Prague. The location of the Czech Museum of Music is within the former Baroque church of St. Mary Magdalene in the Little Quarter, the National Museum has taken the unique opportunity to update this space - once decorated with Baroque masterpieces and resounding with supposedly one of the biggest organs of 17th-century Prague. Also enjoy a visit to the Museum of Antonin Dvorak, located in a baroque Vila 'Amerika', since 1932 in an important early 18th century building of architect Kilián Ignác Dienzenhofer. End your Musical tour of Prague by a private walk-through the Estate Theatre, lasting about 60 minutes with explanation about the theatre renovation, history and work of W.A. Mozart in this theatre.

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    DEPART PRAGUE

    Today you will be transferred to Prague airport by private vehicle to board your flight.

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

BOHEMIAN PARIS

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 Days

    A special 4 day tour of the French capital, exploring all the main sights of one of the most romantic cities in the world.

    Overnight: Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Champs Elysées, Notre Dame Cathedral, Versailles

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE PARIS

    Upon arrival at Paris airport you will be met in the arrival hall by your driver and privately transferred to your hotel. You will find a complimentary bottle of champagne in your room upon your arrival to celebrate the beginning of your mini-moon.

    On the Rue de Rivoli overlooking the beautiful Tuileries Gardens, the Hotel Meurice boasts an excellent location in the heart of Paris as it is nestled between the Place de la Concorde and the Grand Louvre. Also nearby are the Opéra Garnier, the Jewellery stores on the rue de la Paix, and the Place Vendôme. Original antiques and paintings are featured throughout the hotel and every floor is decorated in a distinctive style.

    Overnight: Hotel Le Meurice

  • Day 2

    PARIS

    This morning you will enjoy a half day (4 hours) sightseeing tour in Paris with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. This overview tour will include famous Parisian sights such as the Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Champs Elysées and the Ile de la Cité, where you will visit the beautiful Notre Dame Cathedral.

    This afternoon is at your leisure - you may like to explore Montmartre. This district is forever associated with the idealistic Bohemian lifestyles of the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Parisians call it the 'Butte' (knoll) as it is the highest point in the city. Discover Montmartre's romantic charms along the winding back streets, small squares and terraces. Many artists paint quick-fire portraits in the 'Place du Tertre'. Walk up to the Sacré-Coeur Basilica for spectacular views over the city. Other famous landmarks of Montmartre include the 'Moulin Rouge', a 19-century windmill transformed into a world-famous cabaret and immortalized by the painter Toulouse-Lautrec, the 'Place des Abbesses', a picturesque square which is now a hub of the fashion world, or the Dalí Centre, a unique exhibition entirely devoted to the master of surrealism, and more particularly to his sculptures and engravings.

    Overnight: Hotel Le Meurice
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    PARIS - VERSAILLES - PARIS

    Enjoy a full-day (up to 8 hours) excursion tour to Versailles with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. Situated to the west of Paris, Versailles is the location of the huge palace that was constructed in the 17th century for Louis XIV, the 'Sun King'. It became the model for grand houses of royalty all over Europe. Take a tour of the opulent State Apartments that culminate in the magnificent Gallery of Mirrors.

    Enjoy a stroll in the formal ornamental gardens that are embellished with many fountains, and see the Grand Canal that stretches into the distance across the park; a beautiful spot to indulge in an intimate picnic for two which has been especially prepared for you. Return to Paris following your visit.

    Overnight: Hotel Le Meurice
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    DEPART PARIS

    Today you will be transferred to the Paris airport by private vehicle to board your flight.

    Meal Plan: B

CROATIA BEACH

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 Days

    A luxurious and relaxing 4 day visit to the stunning Croatian coastal town of Split.

    Highlights: Explore the Ancient city of Split and enjoy plenty of time at leisure to make the most of your wonderful hotel facilities.

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE IN SPLIT

    Meet your Chauffeur at Split airport and transfer to your hotel which overlooks the bay of Split, the Adriatic sea and the iconic island of Brac.

    Le Meridien Lav, Split is located at Podstrana, five miles south of Split. The beautiful setting features 800 metres of beach frontage, gardens landscaped by award-winning designer Jim Nicolay of Hawaii, and fantastic sea views overlooking Split and the surrounding islands.

    Overnight: Le Meridien Lav

  • Day 2

    SPILT

    Today is at your leisure to enjoy the facilities of your beachfront hotel. The hotel facilities include an outdoor and an indoor pool, tennis courts and a Water Sports Centre which offers scuba diving, waterskiing, sailing, and more. The exclusive spa and wellness centre is a magnificent space offering treatment rooms, relaxation areas and a fitness centre, the perfect place to unwind and feel pampered.

    Overnight: Le Meridien Lav
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    SPLIT

    Today you may wish to explore the ancient city of Split, including the palace which was the 'retirement home' of the roman emperor Hadrian. Alternatively why not enjoy a romantic dinner of fresh oysters overlooking the marina.

    Overnight: Le Meridien Lav
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    SPLIT

    Your chauffeur will bring you back in style to Split Airport today.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Urubamba/Machu Picchu

    Board the deluxe Hiram Bingham train, the most luxurious way to travel the breathtaking landscape to Machu Picchu. Step on board, and you are immersed in an elegant world of polished wood, gleaming cutlery and glittering glass that evokes a romantic ambience. After a light lunch on board, arrive at the breathtaking Incan citadel and begin exploring the ruins in the company of your expert guide. Feel the pulse of this magical place as you visualize the lives of the priests, craftsmen, and servants who inhabited this seemingly uninhabitable citadel and then left, leaving no written records about its destruction. After a busy day of sightseeing, return to your beautiful mountainside retreat, high in the lush Andean clouds forest. It features the look and feel of a rural Andean village set in spectacular grounds with paths that wind past waterfalls and gardens, and trails that wander through wild forest. Snuggle under a thick alpaca blanket in your suite, which offers incredible views of the exotic gardens or the dramatic Vilcanota River.

    Overnight: Inkaterra Machu Picchu Pueblo Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Machu Picchu/Cusco

    Return to Machu Picchu with your guide this morning for a final look at the ruins - and for an even more meaningful event for you. A shaman joins you to perform a vow renewal ceremony for you. Stand before the spirits of the Incas and reaffirm your love in this magical place. Afterward, return to the hotel, renewed and refreshed, for lunch. This afternoon, return to Cusco by Vista dome train and settle in at your hotel, where you receive VIP amenities.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Cusco

    A morning city tour takes you to the Cathedral on the Plaza de Armas and the Santo Domingo Temple, a Dominican church and convent built on the foundations of the Koricancha (place of gold), where the Incas worshipped the sun. Continue just outside Cusco to the ruins of Sacsayhuaman, a testimonial to the Incan architectural skills. Try to slip a piece of paper between the cracks of the massive stone blocks, cut and fitted together with incredible precision without a single drop of mortar to hold them in place. Your afternoon is at leisure. Make sure to visit the hotel spa for a relaxing couple's massage. This evening, enjoy a romantic dinner at Limo restaurant, overlooking the main plaza.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 8

    Cusco/Lima

    Fly to Lima and transfer to your hotel. The remainder of the day is at leisure to do some last-minute shopping, take a walk along the ocean or enjoy the views from your suite.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Lima/India

    Transfer to the airport &Board your international departure flight.

    Overnight: Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

Morocco: Land of the Kasbahs (2012)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 Nights

    The land of the Kasbah's, busy ports, nomadic Berbers, high mountains and expansive desert offers a romantic escape from the world as you know it. Visit ancient mosques and walk hand in hand through lively city squares, follow mountain slopes and feel the breeze coming from picturesque lakes. Then share your nights in amazing kasbahs, palace hotels and intimate desert tents that all reflect the ambience of a bygone era.

  • Day 1

    India/Casablanca, Morocco

    You are met on arrival and given VIP assistance in clearing customs formalities and collecting your luggage; then travel by private vehicle to your unique vintage townhouse hotel, located in the heart of the city. Originally settled by Berbers in the seventh century, Casablanca today is a bustling, French-influenced center. This afternoon, join your guide for a tour of this port city, stopping at the formidable Hassan II Grand Mosque. Its minaret is the tallest religious structure in the world, and part of the mosque's floor is glass, enabling worshippers to kneel directly over the sea. Enjoy dinner at Rick's Café, housed in a 1930's mansion and based on the café everybody came to in the famous film Casablanca.

    Overnight: Le Doge
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 2

    Casablanca/Er-Rachida/Merzouga

    Meet your private A&K guide, an affable local resident and an expert on Morocco's culture and traditions, who accompanies you throughout your journey in Morocco. Board a charter flight to Er-Rachidia, and on arrival have lunch in Erfoud. Hop into a four-wheel-drive vehicle for a drive across the stunning Erg Chebbi Dunes to your exclusive A&K Desert Tented Camp in a remote area of the Sahara Desert. You are welcomed with mint tea and pastries and led to your private Moroccan caidal-styled tent that combines elements of Berber and Bedouin styles. Decorated with colorful carpets, it comfortably sleeps two, and has its own private bathroom with a shower and toilet next door. Set off on camelback in the evening and begin exploring the sand dunes. Return to your nomadic accommodations before sitting down to a Bedouin dinner served around the campfire.

    Overnight: Desert Tented Camp
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Merzouga/Erfoud/Skoura

    The drive today takes you across the southern slopes of the High Atlas range and through the Dades Valley. Along the way, stop at some of the many date palmeries and a small, private museum that houses exhibits showcasing local art, culture and history. Travel the "Road of a Thousand Kasbahs" and view the amazing 17th-century Amerhidl Kasbah; its façade appears on the Moroccan 50-dirham note. After lunch, continue to your home for the next two nights - a luxurious 19th-century Kasbah that was part of a sultan's private hunting ground in the Skoura Oasis. Dar Ahlam means "house of dreams," and the name suits it well. Enter what seems like a dreamscape, where colorful veils, lanterns and candles - and only 12 rooms - ensure complete privacy, setting the mood for romance. Sit by the fireplace, surrounded by a kaleidoscope of colors; have lunch in the garden or olive grove; or dine in a secluded corner of the Kasbah or even on a hidden terrace.

    Overnight: Dar Ahlam
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Skoura

    Not far from the desert oasis of Skoura is Ouarzazate Lake, where you take a walk and then sit down to a delightful picnic lunch on its shores. If you look across the lake you can see the mountains and desert; on a windy day, the waters toss and swell like the ocean. Afterward, return to your Kasbah for a refreshing swim and maybe a massage. This evening, savor a romantic dinner in an equally romantic setting: in the gardens under the almond trees, in the palm grove or on the riverbank.

    Overnight: Dar Ahlam
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Skoura/Ouarzazate/Ait Benhaddou/Marrakech

    Spend some time wandering around Ouarzazate, a city originally built as a French garrison in the 1920s and now known for its pottery and carpets. Afterward, visit the Glaoui Kasbah of Taourit. One of Morocco's most beautiful Kasbahs, it consists of a network of luxury apartments, simple clay houses and crenellated towers decorated with geometric motifs. Continue to the fortified village of Ait Benhaddou, where some of the world's most elaborate and decorative kasbahs are found. See how they seem to defy the laws of gravity as they perch upon the sleep slope. Ait Benhaddou's charms have also reached all the way to Hollywood. Many popular movies, including Lawrence of Arabia, Jesus of Nazareth, Gladiator and The Mummy have been filmed here. After lunch in a local restaurant, continue to exotic Marrakech, the "Pearl of the South," and your Moorish-style palace hotel.

    Overnight: Ksar Char Bagh
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 6

    Marrakech

    Morocco's most famous city, Marrakech is veiled in mystery and magic. Visit the medina, the old walled section of the city, where most of the architectural attractions are located. Begin with a stop at Koutoubia Mosque and its 12th-century minaret, which dominates the local skyline. Make your way to vibrant Djemaa el-Fna, Marrakech's main square. Watch as fire-eaters, mime artists, snake charmers and street musicians perform at every turn. Continue walking and look for the Saadian Tombs, decorated in Granada-style carving and hidden away behind high walls. Discover how the privileged lived in the 19th century when you wander Bahia Palace to see the beautiful mosaics, fountains and courtyards. At the Museum of Moroccan Arts and Crafts, housed in the beautiful Dar Si Said palace, view stunning traditional woven carpets and Berber silver jewelry. Afterward, return to your hotel to experience a very important part of Moroccan culture and life: a hammam (steam bath). More than a steam bath, the hammam is a ritual. Soak in hot, soft water, lather up with a special penetrating black soap and exfoliate for revived skin. Follow up with a cold shower and an incredibly relaxing massage in a warm, darkened room - total bliss.

    Overnight: Ksar Char Bagh
    Meal Plan: Meals: B,L

  • Day 7

    Marrakech/Atlas Mountains/Marrakech

    High up in the Atlas Mountains, Morocco's tallest and most majestic range, see traditional villages clinging to the almost vertical sides of the valley. Spend your day exploring the Atlas Mountains and its flora and fauna with your private guide, and gain insight into the daily lives of the villagers. While you have lunch at Sir Richard Branson's Kasbah Tamadot, admire the fabulous views of the mountains. Later this afternoon, return to your hotel for a relaxing massage. Conclude your romantic holiday with dinner for two served in a private room or under the stars in the hotel's garden

    Overnight: Ksar Char Bagh
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    Marrakech/India

    Privately transfer to the airport to board your departure flight.

    Meal Plan: B

Europe Honeymoon Exclusive

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 23 Nights

    Tour at Patek Philippe museum, Excursion to Lausanne and Montreux, Private chocolate tasting at Max Chocolatier, Lunch cruise on Lake Lucerne, Excursion in to the Tuscany countryside, INSIDER ACCESS: Rome by Night, INSIDER ACCESS: Learn to the secrets of authentic pizza making, Dinner at Solo per due Restaurant(only for two), Paris: Exclusive Artists Jewellery Tour, Paris: Invitation to the World Of Fashion, Paris: Versailles with Helicopter, Paris: Create Your Own Fragrance, Paris: Exclusive Private Yacht hire with dinner.

  • Day 1

    Arrival Geneva

    Arrival at Geneva airport, flight tba
    Private transfer from Geneva airport to Geneva hotel

    Geneva : Capital of Peace is one of its names - the European headquarters of the United Nations is located here. Another well-deserved name for this city with a humanitarian tradition, international organisations and cosmopolitan flair, is The World's Smallest Metropolis. Quays, lakeside promenades, parks, elegant stores and lively streets and alleyways in the old part of town wait to be discovered. Boat trips beckon on the lake and the Rhone River. The Alps seem close enough to touch. Geneva - small metropolis surrounded by unspoiled nature and breathtaking scenery.

    Overnight: otel Mandarin Oriental
    Meal Plan:

  • Day 2

    Geneva

    Private transfer from Geneva hotel to Patek Philippe museum
    Private guided tour at Patek Philippe museum (2 hours)
    Private transfer from Geneva Patek Philippe museum to hotel

    The Patek Philippe Museum hosts an extraordinary collection of watchmaking masterpieces dating from the 16th to the 20th century. This private tour highlights the historical significance of the art of watchmaking and enamelling.

    Overnight: Hotel Mandarin Oriental
    Meal Plan:

  • Day 3

    Full Day to Lausanne

    Full day excursion to Lausanne and Montreux by private transfer (max. 8 hours)
    Private guided tour at Chillon Castle (1 hour)
    Private guide for walking tour of Lausanne (2 hours)

    Overnight: Hotel Mandarin Oriental
    Meal Plan:

  • Day 4

    Geneva to Interlaken

    Today you will pick up your self-drive car MERCEDES ML 63 AMG from hotel and you will have this car from 14th till 19 Dec 13.
    Self-drive from Geneva to Interlaken via scenic Gruyeres, travel time is approx.. 2 hours and 30 minutes.

    This scenic transfer takes you to the Swiss Cheese & Chocolate country. Drive along picturesque vineyards to Chexbres and Chatel St. Denis, typical wine growing villages. Visit the Cheese Factory where you can watch the cheese production and even taste some of it. Next highlight is the visit of the famous Chocolate Factory Cailler in Broc. Perched on a hill in a landscape full of grace and harmony is the picture-book, medieval town of Gruyeres with its quaint houses dating from the 16th and 17th centuries, a real paradise for photographers. Continue to Interlaken with you rental car.

    Interlaken is one of the best known and most popular summer holiday resorts in Switzerland, outstandingly well-equipped to cater to the needs of visitors. The Hohematte, 700 yards long, is a pedestrian paradise of shops, hotels and sidewalk cafes, interrupted only by inviting gardens. For a comfortable excursion through this quaint town, take a horse-carriage ride. You will view the mountains, the lakes, the endless beauty of Switzerland; all are at your fingertips in this comfortable, inviting, and elegant town.

    Overnight:Hotel Victoria Jungfrau
    Meal Plan:

  • Day 5

    Visit of Mount Jungfraujoch

    Full day excursion to Mount Jungfraujoch (unescorted, without guide & vehicle),

    This excursion takes you to the very heart of the Bernese Alps - the mighty "Eiger" (13'026 ft/ 3970m), "Monch" (13'449 ft/ 4099m) and "Jungfrau" (13'643 ft/ 4158m), which form an imposing nucleus of the Alpine Region. Walk to Interlaken Ost station and take the train into the Waterfall Valley of Lauterbrunnen where you change and board the train to Jungfraujoch. The travelling time from Interlaken to the Jungfraujoch is approx. 2 1/2 hours each way. At Jungfraujoch, the highest railway station in Europe, you can visit the Ice Palace and the Sphinx Terraces - reached by lift affording breathtaking view of the Aletsch Glacier, the largest river of ice in Europe. Refreshment or a meal can be obtained at the Jungfraujoch Restaurant "Top of Europe". After approx. 2 ½ hours you will return to the mountaineering village of Grindelwald, the winter sports and mountaineering centre. Enjoy the view on the mighty mountains, before returning to Interlaken.

    Overnight: Hotel Victoria Jungfrau

    Meal Plan:

  • Day 6

    Interlaken to Lucerne

    Self-drive from Interlaken to Lucerne via scenic Brünig pass
    Travel time is approx.1 hour.
    Private chocolate tasting at Max Chocolatier (1 hour)

    After a scenic drive along the Lake of Brienz have a short stop at the quaint typical Swiss Village of Brienz, the center of Switzerland's wood-carving industry. Before leaving you should not forget to take one or two pictures of these beautiful wood chalets with bright splashes of the geraniums in the window sills. Cross the Brünig Pass and drive along the Lakes of Lungern, Sarnen and Lucerne.

    The world-famous, historical city of Lucerne lies in Switzerland in the heart of Europe. Because of its central location right on Lake Lucerne, which is surrounded by grand mountains like Mount Titlis at 3000 meters high or the impressive Mount Pilatus, Lucerne is considered to be the "Switzerland in Switzerland". You will find everything here: the lake, the mountains, the city – a distinctive consonance of nature and work of man, which has been preserved and carefully developed for centuries.

    What do you want to see while in Lucerne?
    The Chapel Bridge with its gabled paintings of old battles and the adjacent quaint quarters with little streets ranging and changing from silence to abundant liveliness? The curiosities of the museums, the treasures of the many churches? Take a leisurely walk through a maze of small streets, bridges, promenades and plazas dominated by countless historical towers, fountains and frescoed buildings.

    Overnight: Palace Luzern
    Meal Plan:

  • Day 7

    Excursion to Mount Pilatus

    Excursion to Mount Pilatus (Self-drive) unescorted, without guide & vehicle

    To see Central Switzerland at its best, you need only step aboard any one of the five "old-timer" paddle steamers for a journey into time.
