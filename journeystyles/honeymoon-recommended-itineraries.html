Europe Honeymoon Exclusive

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 23 Days

    Highlights :Tour at Patek Philippe museum, Excursion to Lausanne and Montreux, Private chocolate tasting at Max Chocolatier, Lunch cruise on Lake Lucerne, Excursion in to the Tuscany countryside, INSIDER ACCESS: Rome by Night, INSIDER ACCESS: Learn to the secrets of authentic pizza making, Dinner at Solo per due Restaurant(only for two), Paris: Exclusive Artists Jewellery Tour, Paris: Invitation to the World Of Fashion, Paris: Versailles with Helicopter, Paris: Create Your Own Fragrance, Paris: Exclusive Private Yacht hire with dinner.

  • ITINERARY

    Day 1

    Arrival Geneva

    Arrival at Geneva airport, flight tba
    Private transfer from Geneva airport to Geneva hotel

    Geneva : Capital of Peace is one of its names - the European headquarters of the United Nations is located here. Another well-deserved name for this city with a humanitarian tradition, international organisations and cosmopolitan flair, is The World's Smallest Metropolis. Quays, lakeside promenades, parks, elegant stores and lively streets and alleyways in the old part of town wait to be discovered. Boat trips beckon on the lake and the Rhone River. The Alps seem close enough to touch. Geneva - small metropolis surrounded by unspoiled nature and breathtaking scenery.

    Overnight: otel Mandarin Oriental

  • Day 2

    Geneva

    Private transfer from Geneva hotel to Patek Philippe museum
    Private guided tour at Patek Philippe museum (2 hours)
    Private transfer from Geneva Patek Philippe museum to hotel

    The Patek Philippe Museum hosts an extraordinary collection of watchmaking masterpieces dating from the 16th to the 20th century. This private tour highlights the historical significance of the art of watchmaking and enamelling.

    Overnight: Hotel Mandarin Oriental

  • Day 3

    Full Day to Lausanne

    Full day excursion to Lausanne and Montreux by private transfer (max. 8 hours)
    Private guided tour at Chillon Castle (1 hour)
    Private guide for walking tour of Lausanne (2 hours)

    Overnight: Hotel Mandarin Oriental

  • Day 4

    Geneva to Interlaken

    Today you will pick up your self-drive car MERCEDES ML 63 AMG from hotel and you will have this car from 14th till 19 Dec 13.
    Self-drive from Geneva to Interlaken via scenic Gruyeres, travel time is approx.. 2 hours and 30 minutes.

    This scenic transfer takes you to the Swiss Cheese & Chocolate country. Drive along picturesque vineyards to Chexbres and Chatel St. Denis, typical wine growing villages. Visit the Cheese Factory where you can watch the cheese production and even taste some of it. Next highlight is the visit of the famous Chocolate Factory Cailler in Broc. Perched on a hill in a landscape full of grace and harmony is the picture-book, medieval town of Gruyeres with its quaint houses dating from the 16th and 17th centuries, a real paradise for photographers. Continue to Interlaken with you rental car.

    Interlaken is one of the best known and most popular summer holiday resorts in Switzerland, outstandingly well-equipped to cater to the needs of visitors. The Hohematte, 700 yards long, is a pedestrian paradise of shops, hotels and sidewalk cafes, interrupted only by inviting gardens. For a comfortable excursion through this quaint town, take a horse-carriage ride. You will view the mountains, the lakes, the endless beauty of Switzerland; all are at your fingertips in this comfortable, inviting, and elegant town.

    Overnight:Hotel Victoria Jungfrau

  • Day 5

    Visit of Mount Jungfraujoch

    Full day excursion to Mount Jungfraujoch (unescorted, without guide & vehicle),

    This excursion takes you to the very heart of the Bernese Alps - the mighty "Eiger" (13'026 ft/ 3970m), "Monch" (13'449 ft/ 4099m) and "Jungfrau" (13'643 ft/ 4158m), which form an imposing nucleus of the Alpine Region. Walk to Interlaken Ost station and take the train into the Waterfall Valley of Lauterbrunnen where you change and board the train to Jungfraujoch. The travelling time from Interlaken to the Jungfraujoch is approx. 2 1/2 hours each way. At Jungfraujoch, the highest railway station in Europe, you can visit the Ice Palace and the Sphinx Terraces - reached by lift affording breathtaking view of the Aletsch Glacier, the largest river of ice in Europe. Refreshment or a meal can be obtained at the Jungfraujoch Restaurant "Top of Europe". After approx. 2 ½ hours you will return to the mountaineering village of Grindelwald, the winter sports and mountaineering centre. Enjoy the view on the mighty mountains, before returning to Interlaken.

    Overnight: Hotel Victoria Jungfrau

  • Day 6

    Interlaken to Lucerne

    Self-drive from Interlaken to Lucerne via scenic Brunig pass
    Travel time is approx.1 hour.
    Private chocolate tasting at Max Chocolatier (1 hour)

    After a scenic drive along the Lake of Brienz have a short stop at the quaint typical Swiss Village of Brienz, the center of Switzerland's wood-carving industry. Before leaving you should not forget to take one or two pictures of these beautiful wood chalets with bright splashes of the geraniums in the window sills. Cross the Brunig Pass and drive along the Lakes of Lungern, Sarnen and Lucerne.

    The world-famous, historical city of Lucerne lies in Switzerland in the heart of Europe. Because of its central location right on Lake Lucerne, which is surrounded by grand mountains like Mount Titlis at 3000 meters high or the impressive Mount Pilatus, Lucerne is considered to be the "Switzerland in Switzerland". You will find everything here: the lake, the mountains, the city a distinctive consonance of nature and work of man, which has been preserved and carefully developed for centuries.

    What do you want to see while in Lucerne?
    The Chapel Bridge with its gabled paintings of old battles and the adjacent quaint quarters with little streets ranging and changing from silence to abundant liveliness? The curiosities of the museums, the treasures of the many churches? Take a leisurely walk through a maze of small streets, bridges, promenades and plazas dominated by countless historical towers, fountains and frescoed buildings.

    Overnight: Palace Luzern

  • Day 7

    Excursion to Mount Pilatus

    Excursion to Mount Pilatus (Self-drive) unescorted, without guide & vehicle
    After a drive through Lucerne and Kriens by rental car ascend to Fräkmüntegg by gondolas which sweep you over pine forests and mountain meadows and change to aerial cable car to Pilatus Kulm. On top you have time to walk up to the vista point. There will be time to admire the breathing panorama over the Swiss Alps and Lucerne and also to enjoy a delicious meal in the restaurant. Descend to Kriens by cable car and gondola again and drive back to Lucerne.

    Overnight: Palace Luzern

  • Day 8

    Cruise on Lake Lucerne

    Lunch cruise on Lake Lucerne (2 hours)
    Note:Lunch will be chosen and paid on spot
    To see Central Switzerland at its best, you need only step aboard any one of the five "old-timer" paddle steamers for a journey into time. If you prefer however, there are 15 elegant saloon cruisers also available for a tour of the lake that encompasses 38 kilometres of typically Swiss scenic splendour. You will drift by peaceful meadows and idyllic bays, see dramatic inland fjords stretching to the bottom of vertical cliffs that rise majestically to the first Alpine summits. Enjoy your lunch break on the lake. The chef on board has prepared a number of delightful culinary surprises for you.

    Overnight: Palace Luzern

  • Day 9

    Lucerne to Zurich Airport

    You will drop your self- drive car at hotel and you will be privately transferred to Zurich Airport.
    Departure Zurich airport.
    Upon arrival at Florence airport you will be met and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel in Florence. The remainder of the day is at leisure to relax after your flight.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Florence

  • Day 10

    Florence

    Today you will pick-up your self-drive car MASERATI GRAN CABRIO from hotel which will be with you from 20th till 23rd Dec.

    This morning you will be met in the lobby of your hotel by your Guardian Angel who will accompany you on foot to your cooking school in the centre of Florence. Experience the vibrant sights and sounds of a local food market during a guided tour (1hour) with your English-speaking tutor. Then enjoy a private three-hour hands-on lesson which can be tailored to your level of ability, your taste and interests, and will teach you some of the secrets of the local and national cuisine.

    This afternoon you will enjoy a half-day walking tour (4 hours) of Florence with your local English-speaking guide. Your tour will include visits to the Uffizi Gallery, housing some of the world's most important art treasures, and the Church of Santa Croce, where many famous Florentines such as Michelangelo and Galileo are buried.

    Vasari Corridor: Your tour will include a visit to the Uffizi Gallery, housing some of the world's most important art treasures. The Uffizi was built in 1560-80 as a suite of offices (uffici) for Duke Cosimo I’s new Tuscan administration. Here you will enjoy a very special private entry to the Vasari Corridor*, the private corridor built in 1565 by Giorgio Vasari, court architect to the Medici family. Having walked through this private ‘Princes’ route’ to the Pitti palace on the south side of the River, enjoy some time taking in the Boboli Gardens, built as a private garden to the impressive Medici residence.

    Inclusions: Inclusions: Services of A&K Guardian Angel to accompany by foot to cooking school;
    Cooking lesson (approx. 3 hours); Services of a local English-speaking Guide up to 4 hrs; Entrance to the Santa Croce Church and Leather school; Entrance to the Uffizi Gallery; Entrance fund
    Exclusions: Any meals not specified; Tips

    Overnight:Four Seasons Hotel Florence

  • Day 11

    Day at Leisure

    Today if you want you can visit chianti region on your own using self drive (unescorted, no guide & vehicle)

    Renowned for its art, wine, history and evocative landscapes, Tuscany is a region where the past and present merge in pleasant harmony. Today's full-day tour takes in the Chianti wine region; often considered the most beautiful area in Tuscany. Hill-towns gaze across the countryside from high, many encircled by Etruscan walls and slender cypress trees. As you travel along winding roads, soak up the rich panorama comprising beautiful rolling hills, vineyards and olive groves. Admire the charming hamlets and farmhouses juxtaposed with fortified villas and castles, architectural symbols of the violence and inter-communal strife that tore Tuscany apart in the Middle Ages. Heading south of Florence towards Siena, enjoy a leisurely espresso or gelato in the historic main square of Greve, considered the principal town in Chianti. This afternoon, sit back and enjoy a stunning drive through the Chianti countryside until you reach the hilltop town of Castellina, whose noble origins date back to the thirteen century. Here you will have time to soak up the lively atmosphere as you wander through its charming streets, prior to visiting two local vineyards to sample the world-famous Chianti Classico.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Florence

  • Day 12

    Day at Leisure

    Today if you want you can visit Tuscany for shopping using self-drive. unescorted, no guide & vehicle)

    Excursion in to the Tuscany countryside to visit the various outlet stores, the ideal opportunity to be immersed into Italian haute couture. Visit the best outlets, where you can shop with savings of up to 70%. At The Mall, you can find famous names such as Gucci, Bottega Veneta, Emanuel Ungaro, Ermenegildo Zegna, Giorgio Armani, Hogan, I Pinco Pallino, La Perla, Loro Piana, Marni, Salvatore Ferragamo, Sergio Rossi, Tod's Valentino and Yves Saint Laurent. Only 5km's from The Mall is located the Fendi and Dolce & Gabbana outlets’ then on to the Prada village ‘SPACE’, where you'll find designers Helmut Lang, Miu Miu and Jil Sander. Lunch can be enjoyed in a local restaurant (payable directly).

    Overnight:Four Seasons Hotel Florence

  • Day 13

    Florence to Rome

    Car pick up from Rome hotel (you will drive from Florence hotel to Rome hotel on today).
    Check-in at hotel.

    INSIDER ACCESS: Rome by Night
    This evening you will be met at your hotel by your English-speaking guide and private vehicle for a 2-hour drive around the eternal city at dusk. As the evening progresses and the sun goes down Rome’s city lights superbly highlight the monuments and sights of the city. Explore the most famous piazzas and fountains of Rome aglow, each of which takes on a new perspective when lit. Proceed to the Gianicolo, the eighth hill of Rome for a breathtaking view of the entire illuminated city.

    Overnight: St. Regis, Rome

  • Day 14

    Rome

    Enjoy a half-day tour (4 hours) which will no doubt leave you in awe as your guide shows you the heart of the Roman Empire. Visit the imposing Colosseum, commissioned by Emperor Vespasian, the stadium could accommodate up to 50,000 spectators who came to view the gladiatorial battles that lasted up to 100 days! Adjacent is the Arc of Constantine, dating from AD 315 it is a forerunner to many other Triumphal Arches around the world. Possibly even more impressive are the excavations of the vast Roman Forum, where the remains of temples, streetscapes, palaces and homes have been uncovered and can now be visited up close. Your guide will bring to life the atmosphere of Rome's pulsating heart, crowded with people from across the Roman Empire. Your guide will also explain the other nearby Emperor's forums; Traiana, Augusto and Cesare. Your visit today also includes the Palatine Hill, inhabited since the 9th century BC, archaeologists believe this to be the place where Romulus, founder of Rome, lived.

    Inclusions: Transport in private, air-conditioned vehicle with English speaking driver (1-2 pax = Mercedes E Class/ 3-4 pax = Mercedes Viano or similar/ 5-6 pax= Mercedes 8 seater minibus); Services of an English-speaking local Guide; Entrance to the Colosseum and Roman Forum & Palatine; Entrance fund;
    Exclusions: Any meals not specified; Tips;

    Overnight: St. Regis, Rome

  • Day 15

    Dinner at SOLO PER DUE

    INSIDER ACCESS: Learn to the secrets of authentic pizza making
    This morning, transfer to a popular Roman Pizzeria for a private cooking class, starting at 10:00am. You will prepare the dough, learning of the various aspects as you go from recommend flour and dough rising times, to the toppings and cooking process required for the perfect pizza! Your pizzaiolo speaks English and can give you some further insight into what it is like running a restaurant business in Rome. After your lesson (up to 3 hours), enjoy the fruits of your labour for lunch.
    Later in the evening we have arranged a special dinner for you.

    Solo per due Restaurant
    Today you will be transferred by private vehicle to the smallest restaurant in the world with a table just for you spend a special moment in superb atmosphere enjoying a divine dinner. SOLO PER DUE - or "Just for Two" - is the smallest restaurant in the world: it has only one table and it takes just two people at a time. So there are no queues, no turns, and no waiting; all the attention is dedicated to the two people who have booked. The menu is entirely based on the use of fresh ingredients, chosen each day according to the season and to the clients wishes (the menu can be based on meat or fish accordingly).
    After your relaxed Dinner you will be returned to your hotel in Rome.
    Transfer for dinner is by Mercedes E class and driver at disposal until the end of thedinner aperitivo, 4 courses dinner, exclusively Italian cuisine, accompanied by wines from only the best cellars.

    Overnight: St. Regis, Rome

  • Day 16

    Half day Driver At Disposal

    Today an English- Speaking Driver will be at your disposal to travel as you wish within Rome. Available for a half day up to 4 hours.

    Overnight: St. Regis, Rome

  • Day 17

    Half day Vatican sightseeing

    With your guide and driver, cross the Tiber River into the awe inspiring Vatican City (4 hours). The Vatican was created by the Lateran Treaty in 1929 and is the World's Smallest Independent State. Visit the Vatican Museums, part of the Papal Residence which is estimated to contain 1400 rooms, chapels and galleries. The Museums contain an extraordinarily rich collection of precious arts, maps, sculptures and antiquities however the most breath-taking masterpiece is Michelangelo's frescoed ceiling in the Sistine Chapel. At the centre of the Vatican lies the vast Basilica of St. Peter, you pass by the colourfully dressed Swiss Guard into the world's largest church. The present Cathedral is a beautiful mix of Renaissance and Baroque architecture, formed under the hands of masters such as Michelangelo. The stunning interior is an elaborate and detailed display of marble, mosaic, gilt and bronze. Your tour includes express entry to the Vatican Museums, past the regular lines. The Vatican requires visitors to cover upper arms and legs to below the knee
    Inclusions: Transport in private, air-conditioned vehicle with English speaking driver (1-2 pax = Mercedes E Class/ 3-4 pax = Mercedes Viano or similar/ 5-6 pax= Mercedes 8 seater minibus); Services of an English-speaking local Guide; Entrance to Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel, and St Peters Basilica; Entrance fund;
    Exclusions: Any meals not specified; Tips;

    Overnight: St. Regis, Rome

  • Day 18

    Rome to Paris (Flights Not Included)

    Upon arrival at the Paris airport, you will be met by your Guardian Angel and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel in Paris, where you will be assisted with check-in.

    Inclusions: Transfer in a private, air-conditioned vehicle with English-speaking driver (1-2 pax = Mercedes E class or similar / 3-4 pax Mercedes Viano or similar / 5-6 pax = Mercedes Sprinter 15-seater or similar);
    Services of an A&K Guardian Angel including airport
    meeting; Exclusions: Any stops en route; Porterage; Tips;

    Overnight: Mandarin Oriental Hotel, Paris

  • Day 19

    Full day Driver at Disposal

    Today an English- Speaking Driver will be at your disposal to travel as you wish within Paris. Available for a full day up to 8 hours.
    OR You can do following Insider access tour at optional cost.

    Paris: Exclusive Artists Jewellery Tour
    In the company of your private guide, this afternoons excursion gives you the unique opportunity to meet some of the best French specialists in artists jewellery. Begin with an exclusive visit to a private Parisian apartment to admire stunning pieces, some of which have been exhibited in the best museums in the world. Continue to Les Arts Decoratifs Museum, where you can marvel at the intimate collection of jewellery created by Georges Braque, Alexandre Calder, Henri Laurens and Jean Lurçat. The excitement of your tour culminates at the MiniMasterpiece Gallery, which has been hired exclusively for you. The gallery is entirely dedicated to modern and contemporary artists' jewelry, notably that of Pablo Picasso and Anish Kapoor. You will be greeted by owner Esther de Beauce, who will share her most surprising pieces of art with you over a glass of champagne.

    OR

    Paris: Invitation to the World Of Fashion
    This morning your private fashion guide will open the doors for you of one the legendary Parisian Fashion Houses, allowing you a privileged look inside the world of Haute Couture. On your VIP visit you will be greeted by the boutique director and given a personal behind-the-scenes tour. Throughout the day your expert guide will share the history behind the famous names of the fashion industry with you. In the afternoon your exclusive introduction to the Temples of Parisian elegance will continue with a visit to the archives of the Yves Saint Laurent Foundation (closed to the public). Following afternoon tea at the fashionable Plaza Athenee you will conclude the day with a stroll along Avenue Montaigne that best exemplifies the refined elegance of French luxury design to shop or simply to admire the fabled shop windows.

    Overnight: Mandarin Oriental Hotel, Paris

    Overnight: Mandarin Oriental Hotel, Paris

    OR You can do following Insider access tour at optional cost.

    Paris: Versailles with Helicopter
    Today you will enjoy a half day (5 hours) sightseeing tour to Versailles with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. You will drive out to Versailles to admire the huge palace of the great Sun King Louis XIV. Enjoy a two hour private tour of this Palace and later you may also like to stroll through the decorative gardens. After your tour you will be transferred to a local airport for you to enjoy a private 45 minute helicopter flight back to Paris taking in an unspoiled view of Paris and its most beautiful monuments from a VIP helicopter (Arc de Triomphe, Eiffel Tour, Montparnasse Tower, Invalides, the National Library, Tuileries, Versailles Palace and its gardens).

    OR

    Paris: Versailles Private Tour Today you will be met by your driver at your hotel and transferred to Versailles for a sightseeing tour. The awe-inspiring residence of the great Sun King Louis XIV, with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. Housing up to 20,000 people, the palace was the glory of the Sun King’s reign and was the epicentre of political power in France at the time.
    Enjoy a private tour of the of private parts of the castle, that are not open to the general public for example the apartments of Mrs Pompadour or the famous Mrs DuBarry. Furthermore, enter the private quarters of Louis XV and Louis XVI behind the walls of the
    Grand Apartments and the court’s pomp. Accompanied by a qualified guide, explore the rooms where they lived with their families: the dining room, the Cabinet de la Pendule (Clock room), the corner room with the cylinder desk of Louis XV regarded as one of the most outstanding pieces of furniture in the world, the library of Louis XVI, etc. End your tour by discovering the Opera House or the Chapel.

Honeymoon in Morocco

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 Nights

    Highlights:Sahara Experience, Discover Sahara Desert and Marrakech the Red City, Candlelit dinner in a traditional Moroccan restaurant, Ourzazate, enjoy a private lunch at Ait Benhaddou, Private camp set up just for you in the Desert for a unique overnight under the stars, 1001 night dinner with a romantic atmosphere. A calligrapher will write your names in Arabic to offer you a most beautiful souvenir, Private visit of the Photography House.

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE TO MARRAKECH
    MARHABA!!!

    This is the traditional greeting used in Morocco which conveys the meaning of a warm Hello & Welcome.

    Marhaba to Marrakech!!!

    The red city, magical and coulourful is charming us by its beauty.

    Architectural attractions, historical monuments, mystical medina, thrilling souk and welcoming people are some of the wonders that you will discover.

    Ideal location nears to Atlas Mountains, Sahara Desert and Atlantic Ocean, makes Marrakech the hub of Morocco.

    On arrival into Marrakech airport, you will be greeted and invited to the VIP lounge, while special arrangements are made for you to facilitate and expedite immigration formalities, and collection of your luggage. You are then met by our A&K representative.

    Afterwards you will be transferred to you hotel or riad for check in and relax.

    Special honeymoon:

    Depending on your arrival time, enjoy a little tour in horse carriage to have an overview of the Pearl of the South
    Candlelit dinner in a traditional Moroccan restaurant
    Then get back to your room, candles and roses petals will be displayed for a romantic atmosphere

  • Day 2

    MARRAKECH - OUAZAZATE

    Today depart for Ouarzazate the door of the Desert! The main gateways for caravanners and a great place to discover the Berber culture by visiting beautiful Kasbah.

    Visit Ait Ben Haddou The most exotic and best-preserved Kasbah in the whole of the Atlas region. The village has starred in many masterpieces of the cinema such as Lawrence of Arabia, Jesus of Nazareth, Gladiator and The Mummy. Some of the world’s most elaborate and decorative Kasbahs can be found here, with houses piled up around them, all seeming to defy the laws of gravity as they perch upon the sleep slope.

    In the afternoon enjoy a visit of Kasbahs situated in the surroundings of Ouarzazate.

    Your sightseeing includes a visit to the Glaoui Kasbah of Taourirt. The Glaoui brothers were the most powerful tribal leaders at the turn of the century and the Kasbah of Taourirt is considered to be one of the most beautiful Kasbahs in all of Morocco. It consists of a network of luxury apartments, simple clay houses and crenellated towers which are beautifully decorated with geometric motifs.

    This gorgeous city is located on the caravan route between the Sahara Desert and Marrakech.

    Afterwards you will be transferred to you hotel for check in and relax.

    Special honeymoon:

    At your arrival near to Ourzazate, enjoy a private lunch at Ait Benhaddou. A tent rose from the sand especially for you in the middle of nowhere.

  • Day 3

    OURZAZATE SIGHTSEEING DESERT

    This morning discover Ouarzazate and its region, the land of a 101 Kasbahs.

    Ouarzazate was originaly built as a Frech garrison in the 1920s. Today the city is a regional trade center, known for its pottery and carpets. Your visit will include the discovery of the famous Kasbah of Taourirt.

    Then depart by helicopter or aircraft for your Sahara experience discovering the unrevealed Kasbah and Oasis from the sky.

    You will arrive to your private camp set up just for you in the Desert for a unique overnight under the stars. Climb the highest dunes or enjoy a camel ride to admire the colours of a Sahara sunset.

    To end this incredible day, a wonderful dinner is enjoyed by the fire under the starry sky.

    Then sweet dreams in your luxury tent. Just you and nobody else in the whole silent desert.

    Special honeymoon:

    This night under stars in the Sahara Desert is just so romantic.

  • Day 4

    SAHARA DESERT SKOURA

    Today you will discover the Tafilalet region and Kasbah before heading to Skoura. On the way, visit in the Oasis Museum near Tinghir and continue your journey along the Dades Valley, known as the Valley of the Kasbahs.

    In the afternoon, arrive to Skoura where you will check in. A 19th century Kasbah welcomes you with its exceptional atmosphere. Once part of a medieval sultan's private hunting ground in the Skoura Oasis it offers refined decoration and a meeting of styles.

    Dar Ahlam means house of dreams. It is a truly unique location where everything is designed to ensure that you are in a true oasis of dreams. This 19th-century Kasbah will leave even the most committed sybarite overwhelmed. Dar Ahlams luxury is all the more striking against the backdrop of the Sahara. It’s a universe whose refined decoration is a subtle mixture of tastes and styles, in which light gives the aura of a museum, where space and quietude are the most dominant characteristics. The property stands in an oasis that once formed part of a medieval sultan’s private hunting ground. Within the walls of the kasbah are eight suites, and three private villas stand in the grounds.

    Special honeymoon:

    Traditional hammam and massage with enchanting smell of orange flowers and roses petals displayed everywhere

  • Day 5

    SKOURA

    Day at leisure to enjoy all the benefits of Dar Ahlam. Swim in the beautiful heated swimming pool, sit by the fire place, experience a desert jaunt on a camel or a horse.

    Special honeymoon:

Luxury Honeymoon Brazil

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 Nights

    Highlights:Enjoy Rio de Janeiro sightseeing & cable car ride up Sugar Loaf Mountain & train to Corcovado summit. Top properties in each city: The best boutique hotel in Rio, the best beach resort in Brazil.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Rio de Janeiro / Sugar Loaf

    Welcome to Rio de Janeiro, the city of mountains and sea, samba and carnival, and a relaxed way of life. You are transferred to your hotel along Copacabana Beach. This afternoon, your orientation begins with a cable car ascent of Sugar Loaf Mountain, a tour of city sights, and a ride along the world-famous beaches. Bottle of champagne in the room to celebrate your first night in Rio.

    Overnight: Fasano, Rio De Janeiro

  • Day 2

    Rio de Janeiro / Corcovado & Lunch at Confeitaria Colombo

    This morning, the breakfast will be served in the room. You will be taken to one of Wonders of the World: The statue of Christ the Redeemer, which crowns the top of the Corcovado mountain with stunning views of Guanabara Bay, Sugar Loaf and the beaches of Copacabana and Ipanema below. The perfect way to reach the summit is on the toy train which zig zags its way up through the lush rainforest of the Tijuca Forest where it is possible see monkeys and parrots in the trees. Once on top you will have ample time to admire the views. You will have lunch at Confeitaria Colombo, a 100 year old Tea Room / Restaurant (closed on Saturdays afternoon, Sundays and public holidays) that has been kept in pristine conditions and it is a pleasure to enjoy. It is one of the most traditional restaurants in Rio and it is part of the city's cultural heritage.

    Overnight: Fasano, Rio De Janeiro

  • Day 3

    Rio de Janeiro / Samba Class & Shall we dance

    Samba Dance Class at the prestigious dancing school of Carlinhos de Jesus. Rio de Janeiro is the birthplace of samba. It's impossible to resist the most Brazilian of all beats. You listen to it, and before you notice your feet are tapping, your fingers snapping! If only you knew how to dance it the way locals do... Well, you can. With a 2-hour lesson you’ll be ready to do the basic samba steps and body movements, and before you know it you will be dancing really well! Tonight dinner will be at Rio Scenarium which if there was an award for most beautiful bar, it would certainly deserve it. Located in a renovated warehouse on the edge of Lapa, it is an antique store-turned-bar which serves a wide range of seafood and traditional Brazilian dishes. There is also a stage on the main floor where live Brazilian samba and choro bands will be playing as well as dance floor in case you want to samba the night away. This is the perfect place to practice the moves you learnt earlier today.

    Overnight: Fasano, Rio De Janeiro

  • Day 4

    Rio de Janeiro Florianopolis

    Morning at leisure to relax on the beach or linger at sidewalk cafes or take in an optional tour. In the afternoon, fly to Florianopolis. Upon arrival at Florianopolis airport, you will be met by your guide who will take you to paradise. The destination is Ganchos. Thats how it was baptized by its founders: Azorean immigrants who settled on this lush, mountainous, cove-studded peninsula, more than two hundred years ago at the height of the whaling boom. Some decades ago, the small city was renamed as Governador Celso Ramos. Protected from people’s curiosity as by its topography, Ganchos has to this day remained one of the last surviving secrets of the Brazilian coast.

    More than just natural beauty, a whole way of life has been preserved here. The colonists descendants still make their living from the sea – nowadays cultivating oysters, scallops and other shellfish.

    You leave the main road behind and eight miles further on you find yourself winding along through a succession of villages strung along the north-facing peninsula as though frozen in time. Slow down. The coast road passes hard by the picturesque colonial style houses, as calm little bays come into view one by one, the water dotted with the simple boats and mollusk farmers colored buoys.

    At the end of an unassuming lane, a gate will open to reveal the most amazing spot on the whole peninsula.

    Welcome to Ponta dos Ganchos.

    Be warned in advance: it’s not easy to leave the bungalow. Well, of course, the instant you call for one, an electric car will appear at your door in a flash. Nevertheless, sometimes it is going to be difficult to want to leave.
    It might be because of the therapeutic mattress or perhaps, it is because there are just too many threads in the Egyptian cotton sheets. Sometimes the espresso machine or the organic snacks next to it in the mini-bar are to blame.
    Other times it will be a combination of things – your private wine cellar will join forces with the view of the sunset over the bay to prevent you from venturing beyond the terrace. This is the reason why breakfast is like a miniature gourmet festival – a parade of delicacies served in dainty portions. Providing a little competition for the terrace of your bungalow is a little stone island just in front of the beach that you can reach swimming or along a catwalk.

    Overnight:Ponta dos Ganchos, Florianopolis

  • Day 5

    Ponta Dos Ganchos Governador Celso Ramos

    Once you’ve become accustomed to the idea that the bungalows, with its creature comforts and its view, is always going to be there waiting for you, you will become more enthusiastic about venturing out. There is a wide selection of short trips, lasting from a couple of hours to a whole afternoon and that don’t involve leaving the peninsula. or going too far away from your whirlpool bath.
    A quick chat with the concierge will provide you with enough activity choices to keep you busy for this visit such as: visit to oyster farm, a walk along deserted beaches, a visit to the dolphins, beaches and forest on horseback, trip to waterfall by bike, diving with biodiversity, sport fishing and more.
    All activities are optional and not included in the program.

    Overnight:Ponta dos Ganchos, Florianopolis

  • Day 6

    Ponta Dos Ganchos Governador Celso Ramos

    Day at leisure.

    Overnight:Ponta dos Ganchos, Florianopolis

  • Day 7

    Governador Celso Ramos

    Around noon you will be taken back to Florianopolis airport for your flights back home.
