PRAGUE FAIRYTALE CITY

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 Days

    A fantastic 4 day visit to the romantic, fairy-tale city of Prague. Meander through the cobbled streets flanked with beautiful architecture and take in the magic of Prague's history and culture.

    Highlights: Explore Prague's charming Old Town, stroll along the Charles Bridge and discover the wonderful Baroque architecture of the Little Quarter

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE IN PRAGUE

    Upon arrival at Prague airport you will be met in the arrival hall by your driver and privately transferred to your hotel. The hotel Augustine exudes history and elegance and has been created from 7 different buildings including the 13th century Augustinian monastery after which the hotel has been named.

    Within short walking distance of the character and enchanting history of Prague's old town, you may enjoy a romantic evening stroll, or a quiet drink in the hotel's intimate St Thomas brewery bar, housed in the cellar.

    For your honeymoon enjoy a complimentary rose petal bath and breakfast in bed on your first morning. You will also receive 50% off couples massage treatments to ensure you can fully relax and feel pampered during your stay.*

    *honeymoon promotion valid throughout 2013

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel

  • Day 2

    PRAGUE

    Meet your English-speaking local guide and enjoy a half-day walking tour (4 hours) of the Old Town with its winding cobbled streets and fairy-tale charm and the fascinating Jewish Quarter, birthplace of Franz Kafka. See the striking Gothic Town Hall in Old Town Square, famous for its unique astronomical clock, and visit the Jewish Museum which houses the largest collection of Judaica in Europe, the Old-New Synagogue and the very moving Jewish Cemetery. You may also enjoy a walk across the romantically picturesque 14th century Charles Bridge. This afternoon is at your leisure for you to relax and enjoy the facilities of your hotel. Or, for those keen to explore this delightful city further, why not venture to Prague's Castle District. Hradcany Castle was the seat of Bohemian kings and Holy Roman emperors. See the Cathedral of St. Vitus, one of the finest and most richly endowed in central Europe, and the Basilica of St. George which was founded in 905. Stroll along the Golden Lane, a row of tiny and colourful houses dating from the 16th century.

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    PRAGUE

    Meet your local guide and enjoy a walking tour (3 hours) of the picturesque Lesser Town (Mala Strana). Also known as The Pearl of Baroque Architecture, the Lesser Town is one of Prague's oldest districts and full of captivating history and hidden gems which can't fail to enchant you. The most extensive and unforgettable building of this era is Albrecht von Wallenstein's Palace with five courtyards and a magnificent garden set as a French Park (please note that the palace is only open on Saturdays & Sundays and garden is opened daily from April till October).

    You may also like to see the Lesser Town square with the beautiful baroque St Nicholas Church and perhaps visit the Carmelitan church, The Church of Our Lady Victorious, with the famous statue of the Holy Infant Jesus of Prague. Or take a visit to the lovely Vrtba's Gardens, near the Church of Our Lady Victorious (open daily from April till October).

    If music is the food of love, what better way to spend the afternoon that with a private musical tour of Prague. The location of the Czech Museum of Music is within the former Baroque church of St. Mary Magdalene in the Little Quarter, the National Museum has taken the unique opportunity to update this space - once decorated with Baroque masterpieces and resounding with supposedly one of the biggest organs of 17th-century Prague. Also enjoy a visit to the Museum of Antonin Dvorak, located in a baroque Vila 'Amerika', since 1932 in an important early 18th century building of architect Kilián Ignác Dienzenhofer. End your Musical tour of Prague by a private walk-through the Estate Theatre, lasting about 60 minutes with explanation about the theatre renovation, history and work of W.A. Mozart in this theatre.

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    DEPART PRAGUE

    Today you will be transferred to Prague airport by private vehicle to board your flight.

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

BOHEMIAN PARIS

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 Days

    A special 4 day tour of the French capital, exploring all the main sights of one of the most romantic cities in the world.

    Overnight: Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Champs Elysées, Notre Dame Cathedral, Versailles

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE PARIS

    Upon arrival at Paris airport you will be met in the arrival hall by your driver and privately transferred to your hotel. You will find a complimentary bottle of champagne in your room upon your arrival to celebrate the beginning of your mini-moon.

    On the Rue de Rivoli overlooking the beautiful Tuileries Gardens, the Hotel Meurice boasts an excellent location in the heart of Paris as it is nestled between the Place de la Concorde and the Grand Louvre. Also nearby are the Opéra Garnier, the Jewellery stores on the rue de la Paix, and the Place Vendôme. Original antiques and paintings are featured throughout the hotel and every floor is decorated in a distinctive style.

    Overnight: Hotel Le Meurice

  • Day 2

    PARIS

    This morning you will enjoy a half day (4 hours) sightseeing tour in Paris with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. This overview tour will include famous Parisian sights such as the Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Champs Elysées and the Ile de la Cité, where you will visit the beautiful Notre Dame Cathedral.

    This afternoon is at your leisure - you may like to explore Montmartre. This district is forever associated with the idealistic Bohemian lifestyles of the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Parisians call it the 'Butte' (knoll) as it is the highest point in the city. Discover Montmartre's romantic charms along the winding back streets, small squares and terraces. Many artists paint quick-fire portraits in the 'Place du Tertre'. Walk up to the Sacré-Coeur Basilica for spectacular views over the city. Other famous landmarks of Montmartre include the 'Moulin Rouge', a 19-century windmill transformed into a world-famous cabaret and immortalized by the painter Toulouse-Lautrec, the 'Place des Abbesses', a picturesque square which is now a hub of the fashion world, or the Dalí Centre, a unique exhibition entirely devoted to the master of surrealism, and more particularly to his sculptures and engravings.

    Overnight: Hotel Le Meurice
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    PARIS - VERSAILLES - PARIS

    Enjoy a full-day (up to 8 hours) excursion tour to Versailles with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. Situated to the west of Paris, Versailles is the location of the huge palace that was constructed in the 17th century for Louis XIV, the 'Sun King'. It became the model for grand houses of royalty all over Europe. Take a tour of the opulent State Apartments that culminate in the magnificent Gallery of Mirrors.

    Enjoy a stroll in the formal ornamental gardens that are embellished with many fountains, and see the Grand Canal that stretches into the distance across the park; a beautiful spot to indulge in an intimate picnic for two which has been especially prepared for you. Return to Paris following your visit.

    Overnight: Hotel Le Meurice
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    DEPART PARIS

    Today you will be transferred to the Paris airport by private vehicle to board your flight.

    Meal Plan: B

CROATIA BEACH

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 Days

    A luxurious and relaxing 4 day visit to the stunning Croatian coastal town of Split.

    Highlights: Explore the Ancient city of Split and enjoy plenty of time at leisure to make the most of your wonderful hotel facilities.

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE IN SPLIT

    Meet your Chauffeur at Split airport and transfer to your hotel which overlooks the bay of Split, the Adriatic sea and the iconic island of Brac.

    Le Meridien Lav, Split is located at Podstrana, five miles south of Split. The beautiful setting features 800 metres of beach frontage, gardens landscaped by award-winning designer Jim Nicolay of Hawaii, and fantastic sea views overlooking Split and the surrounding islands.

    Overnight: Le Meridien Lav

  • Day 2

    SPILT

    Today is at your leisure to enjoy the facilities of your beachfront hotel. The hotel facilities include an outdoor and an indoor pool, tennis courts and a Water Sports Centre which offers scuba diving, waterskiing, sailing, and more. The exclusive spa and wellness centre is a magnificent space offering treatment rooms, relaxation areas and a fitness centre, the perfect place to unwind and feel pampered.

    Overnight: Le Meridien Lav
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    SPLIT

    Today you may wish to explore the ancient city of Split, including the palace which was the 'retirement home' of the roman emperor Hadrian. Alternatively why not enjoy a romantic dinner of fresh oysters overlooking the marina.

    Overnight: Le Meridien Lav
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    SPLIT

    Your chauffeur will bring you back in style to Split Airport today.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Urubamba/Machu Picchu

    Board the deluxe Hiram Bingham train, the most luxurious way to travel the breathtaking landscape to Machu Picchu. Step on board, and you are immersed in an elegant world of polished wood, gleaming cutlery and glittering glass that evokes a romantic ambience. After a light lunch on board, arrive at the breathtaking Incan citadel and begin exploring the ruins in the company of your expert guide. Feel the pulse of this magical place as you visualize the lives of the priests, craftsmen, and servants who inhabited this seemingly uninhabitable citadel and then left, leaving no written records about its destruction. After a busy day of sightseeing, return to your beautiful mountainside retreat, high in the lush Andean clouds forest. It features the look and feel of a rural Andean village set in spectacular grounds with paths that wind past waterfalls and gardens, and trails that wander through wild forest. Snuggle under a thick alpaca blanket in your suite, which offers incredible views of the exotic gardens or the dramatic Vilcanota River.

    Overnight: Inkaterra Machu Picchu Pueblo Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Machu Picchu/Cusco

    Return to Machu Picchu with your guide this morning for a final look at the ruins - and for an even more meaningful event for you. A shaman joins you to perform a vow renewal ceremony for you. Stand before the spirits of the Incas and reaffirm your love in this magical place. Afterward, return to the hotel, renewed and refreshed, for lunch. This afternoon, return to Cusco by Vista dome train and settle in at your hotel, where you receive VIP amenities.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Cusco

    A morning city tour takes you to the Cathedral on the Plaza de Armas and the Santo Domingo Temple, a Dominican church and convent built on the foundations of the Koricancha (place of gold), where the Incas worshipped the sun. Continue just outside Cusco to the ruins of Sacsayhuaman, a testimonial to the Incan architectural skills. Try to slip a piece of paper between the cracks of the massive stone blocks, cut and fitted together with incredible precision without a single drop of mortar to hold them in place. Your afternoon is at leisure. Make sure to visit the hotel spa for a relaxing couple's massage. This evening, enjoy a romantic dinner at Limo restaurant, overlooking the main plaza.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 8

    Cusco/Lima

    Fly to Lima and transfer to your hotel. The remainder of the day is at leisure to do some last-minute shopping, take a walk along the ocean or enjoy the views from your suite.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Lima/India

    Transfer to the airport &Board your international departure flight.

    Overnight: Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

Morocco: Land of the Kasbahs (2012)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 Nights

    The land of the Kasbah's, busy ports, nomadic Berbers, high mountains and expansive desert offers a romantic escape from the world as you know it. Visit ancient mosques and walk hand in hand through lively city squares, follow mountain slopes and feel the breeze coming from picturesque lakes. Then share your nights in amazing kasbahs, palace hotels and intimate desert tents that all reflect the ambience of a bygone era.

  • Day 1

    India/Casablanca, Morocco

    You are met on arrival and given VIP assistance in clearing customs formalities and collecting your luggage; then travel by private vehicle to your unique vintage townhouse hotel, located in the heart of the city. Originally settled by Berbers in the seventh century, Casablanca today is a bustling, French-influenced center. This afternoon, join your guide for a tour of this port city, stopping at the formidable Hassan II Grand Mosque. Its minaret is the tallest religious structure in the world, and part of the mosque's floor is glass, enabling worshippers to kneel directly over the sea. Enjoy dinner at Rick's Café, housed in a 1930's mansion and based on the café everybody came to in the famous film Casablanca.

    Overnight: Le Doge
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 2

    Casablanca/Er-Rachida/Merzouga

    Meet your private A&K guide, an affable local resident and an expert on Morocco's culture and traditions, who accompanies you throughout your journey in Morocco. Board a charter flight to Er-Rachidia, and on arrival have lunch in Erfoud. Hop into a four-wheel-drive vehicle for a drive across the stunning Erg Chebbi Dunes to your exclusive A&K Desert Tented Camp in a remote area of the Sahara Desert. You are welcomed with mint tea and pastries and led to your private Moroccan caidal-styled tent that combines elements of Berber and Bedouin styles. Decorated with colorful carpets, it comfortably sleeps two, and has its own private bathroom with a shower and toilet next door. Set off on camelback in the evening and begin exploring the sand dunes. Return to your nomadic accommodations before sitting down to a Bedouin dinner served around the campfire.

    Overnight: Desert Tented Camp
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Merzouga/Erfoud/Skoura

    The drive today takes you across the southern slopes of the High Atlas range and through the Dades Valley. Along the way, stop at some of the many date palmeries and a small, private museum that houses exhibits showcasing local art, culture and history. Travel the "Road of a Thousand Kasbahs" and view the amazing 17th-century Amerhidl Kasbah; its façade appears on the Moroccan 50-dirham note. After lunch, continue to your home for the next two nights - a luxurious 19th-century Kasbah that was part of a sultan's private hunting ground in the Skoura Oasis. Dar Ahlam means "house of dreams," and the name suits it well. Enter what seems like a dreamscape, where colorful veils, lanterns and candles - and only 12 rooms - ensure complete privacy, setting the mood for romance. Sit by the fireplace, surrounded by a kaleidoscope of colors; have lunch in the garden or olive grove; or dine in a secluded corner of the Kasbah or even on a hidden terrace.

    Overnight: Dar Ahlam
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Skoura

    Not far from the desert oasis of Skoura is Ouarzazate Lake, where you take a walk and then sit down to a delightful picnic lunch on its shores. If you look across the lake you can see the mountains and desert; on a windy day, the waters toss and swell like the ocean. Afterward, return to your Kasbah for a refreshing swim and maybe a massage. This evening, savor a romantic dinner in an equally romantic setting: in the gardens under the almond trees, in the palm grove or on the riverbank.

    Overnight: Dar Ahlam
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Skoura/Ouarzazate/Ait Benhaddou/Marrakech

    Spend some time wandering around Ouarzazate, a city originally built as a French garrison in the 1920s and now known for its pottery and carpets. Afterward, visit the Glaoui Kasbah of Taourit. One of Morocco's most beautiful Kasbahs, it consists of a network of luxury apartments, simple clay houses and crenellated towers decorated with geometric motifs. Continue to the fortified village of Ait Benhaddou, where some of the world's most elaborate and decorative kasbahs are found. See how they seem to defy the laws of gravity as they perch upon the sleep slope. Ait Benhaddou's charms have also reached all the way to Hollywood. Many popular movies, including Lawrence of Arabia, Jesus of Nazareth, Gladiator and The Mummy have been filmed here. After lunch in a local restaurant, continue to exotic Marrakech, the "Pearl of the South," and your Moorish-style palace hotel.

    Overnight: Ksar Char Bagh
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 6

    Marrakech

    Morocco's most famous city, Marrakech is veiled in mystery and magic. Visit the medina, the old walled section of the city, where most of the architectural attractions are located. Begin with a stop at Koutoubia Mosque and its 12th-century minaret, which dominates the local skyline. Make your way to vibrant Djemaa el-Fna, Marrakech's main square. Watch as fire-eaters, mime artists, snake charmers and street musicians perform at every turn. Continue walking and look for the Saadian Tombs, decorated in Granada-style carving and hidden away behind high walls. Discover how the privileged lived in the 19th century when you wander Bahia Palace to see the beautiful mosaics, fountains and courtyards. At the Museum of Moroccan Arts and Crafts, housed in the beautiful Dar Si Said palace, view stunning traditional woven carpets and Berber silver jewelry. Afterward, return to your hotel to experience a very important part of Moroccan culture and life: a hammam (steam bath). More than a steam bath, the hammam is a ritual. Soak in hot, soft water, lather up with a special penetrating black soap and exfoliate for revived skin. Follow up with a cold shower and an incredibly relaxing massage in a warm, darkened room - total bliss.

    Overnight: Ksar Char Bagh
    Meal Plan: Meals: B,L

  • Day 7

    Marrakech/Atlas Mountains/Marrakech

    High up in the Atlas Mountains, Morocco's tallest and most majestic range, see traditional villages clinging to the almost vertical sides of the valley. Spend your day exploring the Atlas Mountains and its flora and fauna with your private guide, and gain insight into the daily lives of the villagers. While you have lunch at Sir Richard Branson's Kasbah Tamadot, admire the fabulous views of the mountains. Later this afternoon, return to your hotel for a relaxing massage. Conclude your romantic holiday with dinner for two served in a private room or under the stars in the hotel's garden

    Overnight: Ksar Char Bagh
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    Marrakech/India

    Privately transfer to the airport to board your departure flight.

    Meal Plan: B

Quays, lakeside promenades, parks, elegant stores and lively streets and alleyways in the old part of town wait to be discovered. Boat trips beckon on the lake and the Rhone River.     Full day excursion to Lausanne and Montreux by private transfer (max. 8 hours)
    Private guided tour at Chillon Castle (1 hour)
    Private guide for walking tour of Lausanne (2 hours)

  • Day 4

    Geneva to Interlaken

    Today you will pick up your self-drive car MERCEDES ML 63 AMG from hotel and you will have this car from 14th till 19 Dec 13.
    Self-drive from Geneva to Interlaken via scenic Gruyeres, travel time is approx.. 2 hours and 30 minutes.

    This scenic transfer takes you to the Swiss Cheese & Chocolate country. Drive along picturesque vineyards to Chexbres and Chatel St. Denis, typical wine growing villages. Visit the Cheese Factory where you can watch the cheese production and even taste some of it. Next highlight is the visit of the famous Chocolate Factory Cailler in Broc. Perched on a hill in a landscape full of grace and harmony is the picture-book, medieval town of Gruyeres with its quaint houses dating from the 16th and 17th centuries, a real paradise for photographers. Continue to Interlaken with you rental car.

    Interlaken is one of the best known and most popular summer holiday resorts in Switzerland, outstandingly well-equipped to cater to the needs of visitors. The Hohematte, 700 yards long, is a pedestrian paradise of shops, hotels and sidewalk cafes, interrupted only by inviting gardens. For a comfortable excursion through this quaint town, take a horse-carriage ride. You will view the mountains, the lakes, the endless beauty of Switzerland; all are at your fingertips in this comfortable, inviting, and elegant town.

    Overnight:Hotel Victoria Jungfrau
    Meal Plan:

  • Day 5

    Visit of Mount Jungfraujoch

    Full day excursion to Mount Jungfraujoch (unescorted, without guide & vehicle),

    This excursion takes you to the very heart of the Bernese Alps - the mighty "Eiger" (13'026 ft/ 3970m), "Monch" (13'449 ft/ 4099m) and "Jungfrau" (13'643 ft/ 4158m), which form an imposing nucleus of the Alpine Region. Walk to Interlaken Ost station and take the train into the Waterfall Valley of Lauterbrunnen where you change and board the train to Jungfraujoch. The travelling time from Interlaken to the Jungfraujoch is approx. 2 1/2 hours each way. At Jungfraujoch, the highest railway station in Europe, you can visit the Ice Palace and the Sphinx Terraces - reached by lift affording breathtaking view of the Aletsch Glacier, the largest river of ice in Europe. Refreshment or a meal can be obtained at the Jungfraujoch Restaurant "Top of Europe". After approx. 2 ½ hours you will return to the mountaineering village of Grindelwald, the winter sports and mountaineering centre. Enjoy the view on the mighty mountains, before returning to Interlaken.

    Overnight: Hotel Victoria Jungfrau

    Meal Plan:

  • Day 6

    Interlaken to Lucerne

    Self-drive from Interlaken to Lucerne via scenic Brünig pass
    Travel time is approx.1 hour.
    Private chocolate tasting at Max Chocolatier (1 hour)

    After a scenic drive along the Lake of Brienz have a short stop at the quaint typical Swiss Village of Brienz, the center of Switzerland's wood-carving industry. Before leaving you should not forget to take one or two pictures of these beautiful wood chalets with bright splashes of the geraniums in the window sills. Cross the Brünig Pass and drive along the Lakes of Lungern, Sarnen and Lucerne.

    The world-famous, historical city of Lucerne lies in Switzerland in the heart of Europe. Because of its central location right on Lake Lucerne, which is surrounded by grand mountains like Mount Titlis at 3000 meters high or the impressive Mount Pilatus, Lucerne is considered to be the "Switzerland in Switzerland". You will find everything here: the lake, the mountains, the city – a distinctive consonance of nature and work of man, which has been preserved and carefully developed for centuries.

    What do you want to see while in Lucerne?
    The Chapel Bridge with its gabled paintings of old battles and the adjacent quaint quarters with little streets ranging and changing from silence to abundant liveliness? The curiosities of the museums, the treasures of the many churches? Take a leisurely walk through a maze of small streets, bridges, promenades and plazas dominated by countless historical towers, fountains and frescoed buildings.

    Overnight: Palace Luzern
    Meal Plan:

  • Day 7

    Highlights:Enjoy Rio de Janeiro sightseeing & cable car ride up Sugar Loaf Mountain & train to Corcovado summit. Top properties in each city: The best boutique hotel in Rio, the best beach resort in Brazil.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Rio de Janeiro / Sugar Loaf

    Welcome to Rio de Janeiro, the city of mountains and sea, samba and carnival, and a relaxed way of life. You are transferred to your hotel along Copacabana Beach. This afternoon, your orientation begins with a cable car ascent of Sugar Loaf Mountain, a tour of city sights, and a ride along the world-famous beaches. Bottle of champagne in the room to celebrate your first night in Rio.

    Overnight: Fasano, Rio De Janeiro

  • Day 2

    Rio de Janeiro / Corcovado & Lunch at Confeitaria Colombo

    This morning, the breakfast will be served in the room. You will be taken to one of Wonders of the World: The statue of Christ the Redeemer, which crowns the top of the Corcovado mountain with stunning views of Guanabara Bay, Sugar Loaf and the beaches of Copacabana and Ipanema below. The perfect way to reach the summit is on the toy train which zig zags its way up through the lush rainforest of the Tijuca Forest where it is possible see monkeys and parrots in the trees. Once on top you will have ample time to admire the views. You will have lunch at Confeitaria Colombo, a 100 year old Tea Room / Restaurant (closed on Saturdays afternoon, Sundays and public holidays) that has been kept in pristine conditions and it is a pleasure to enjoy. It is one of the most traditional restaurants in Rio and it is part of the city's cultural heritage.

    Overnight: Fasano, Rio De Janeiro

  • Day 3

    Rio de Janeiro / Samba Class & Shall we dance

    Samba Dance Class at the prestigious dancing school of Carlinhos de Jesus. Rio de Janeiro is the birthplace of samba. It's impossible to resist the most Brazilian of all beats. You listen to it, and before you notice your feet are tapping, your fingers snapping! If only you knew how to dance it the way locals do... Well, you can. With a 2-hour lesson you’ll be ready to do the basic samba steps and body movements, and before you know it you will be dancing really well! Tonight dinner will be at Rio Scenarium which if there was an award for most beautiful bar, it would certainly deserve it. Located in a renovated warehouse on the edge of Lapa, it is an antique store-turned-bar which serves a wide range of seafood and traditional Brazilian dishes. There is also a stage on the main floor where live Brazilian samba and choro bands will be playing as well as dance floor in case you want to samba the night away. This is the perfect place to practice the moves you learnt earlier today.

    Overnight: Fasano, Rio De Janeiro

  • Day 4

    Rio de Janeiro Florianopolis

    Morning at leisure to relax on the beach or linger at sidewalk cafes or take in an optional tour. In the afternoon, fly to Florianopolis. Upon arrival at Florianopolis airport, you will be met by your guide who will take you to paradise. The destination is Ganchos. Thats how it was baptized by its founders: Azorean immigrants who settled on this lush, mountainous, cove-studded peninsula, more than two hundred years ago at the height of the whaling boom. Some decades ago, the small city was renamed as Governador Celso Ramos. Protected from people’s curiosity as by its topography, Ganchos has to this day remained one of the last surviving secrets of the Brazilian coast.

    More than just natural beauty, a whole way of life has been preserved here. The colonists descendants still make their living from the sea – nowadays cultivating oysters, scallops and other shellfish.

    You leave the main road behind and eight miles further on you find yourself winding along through a succession of villages strung along the north-facing peninsula as though frozen in time. Slow down. The coast road passes hard by the picturesque colonial style houses, as calm little bays come into view one by one, the water dotted with the simple boats and mollusk farmers colored buoys.

    At the end of an unassuming lane, a gate will open to reveal the most amazing spot on the whole peninsula.

    Welcome to Ponta dos Ganchos.

    Be warned in advance: it’s not easy to leave the bungalow. Well, of course, the instant you call for one, an electric car will appear at your door in a flash. Nevertheless, sometimes it is going to be difficult to want to leave.
    It might be because of the therapeutic mattress or perhaps, it is because there are just too many threads in the Egyptian cotton sheets. Sometimes the espresso machine or the organic snacks next to it in the mini-bar are to blame.
    Other times it will be a combination of things – your private wine cellar will join forces with the view of the sunset over the bay to prevent you from venturing beyond the terrace. This is the reason why breakfast is like a miniature gourmet festival – a parade of delicacies served in dainty portions. Providing a little competition for the terrace of your bungalow is a little stone island just in front of the beach that you can reach swimming or along a catwalk.

    Overnight:Ponta dos Ganchos, Florianopolis

  • Day 5

    Ponta Dos Ganchos Governador Celso Ramos

    Once you’ve become accustomed to the idea that the bungalows, with its creature comforts and its view, is always going to be there waiting for you, you will become more enthusiastic about venturing out. There is a wide selection of short trips, lasting from a couple of hours to a whole afternoon and that don’t involve leaving the peninsula. or going too far away from your whirlpool bath.
    A quick chat with the concierge will provide you with enough activity choices to keep you busy for this visit such as: visit to oyster farm, a walk along deserted beaches, a visit to the dolphins, beaches and forest on horseback, trip to waterfall by bike, diving with biodiversity, sport fishing and more.
    All activities are optional and not included in the program.

    Overnight:Ponta dos Ganchos, Florianopolis

  • Day 6

    Ponta Dos Ganchos Governador Celso Ramos

    Day at leisure.

    Overnight:Ponta dos Ganchos, Florianopolis

  • Day 7

    Governador Celso Ramos

    Around noon you will be taken back to Florianopolis airport for your flights back home.

Luxury Honeymoon in New Zealand

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 14 Nights

    Highlights:Auckland, Rotorua, Christchurch, Franz Joseph, Wanaka, Queenstown

    Unveil the secrets of both North and South Islands of New Zealand with a chauffeur driven vehicle and customized guide services during sightseeing. Best for those who adore handholding and guidance during their holidays to enjoy every aspect/ offerings of the country visiting

  • Day 1

    Arrive Auckland

    Welcome to New Zealand!

    On arrival into Auckland meet your A&K guide and transfer by private vehicle to your accommodation.

    Hilton Auckland

    This contemporary hotel is located 300 metres out to sea on Princes Wharf offering superb views across Auckland?s harbour and city. Built in the concept of a luxury ship, the hotel is the focal point of the wharf development and boasts an upbeat mix of apartments, retail outlets, restaurants, and cafes and bars. The hotel is also within easy walking distance of Auckland?s CBD and Viaduct Basin, the hub of the city?s new restaurant and nightlife scene.

    Overnight:Sofitel Auckland Viaduct Harbour

  • Day 2

    Auckland

    Morning at leisure

    This afternoon meet your A&K guide in the lobby and depart on your half day private sightseeing tour of Auckland. Today?s sightseeing tour of Auckland includes a scenic drive along the waterfront, the quaint Victorian village of Parnell and panoramic views from the extinct volcano - Mount Eden. At the Auckland Museum see a world-renowned collection of Polynesian arte facts including a 25-metre long war canoe built in 1836. On conclusion you will return to your accommodation.

    Overnight:Sofitel Auckland Viaduct Harbour

  • Day 3

    Auckland / Rotorua

    Depart by private vehicle to your accommodation in Rotorua. A journey of approximately three hours, travelling south through the lush pasturelands of the Waikato, stopping enroute for lunch (own expense) and a bush walk (optional) at the settlement of Te Aroha. Discover Te Aroha?s rich history when "taking the waters? was a fashionable pastime and a cure for everything. Be transported back in time to the gold mining days of Waiorongomai Valley where fortunes were made and lost, where you can still follow old miners? trails and discover relics of the gold rush days. On completion continue to your accommodation in Rotorua.
    Treetops Lodge and Estate, Rotorua

    Nestled in 2,500 acres of 800-year old native forest, award-winning Treetops Lodge & Wilderness Experience is a life-long dream realised by owner and passionate naturalist, John Sax. This uniquely New Zealand experience is a sanctuary and retreat of breathtaking terrain, offering the ultimate in world-class accommodation and adventure. Located in Rotorua the Maori heartland and trout fishing capital of the world the Lodge?s elegant, ecology-inspired architectural style was derived from the country?s pioneering past, with timber and stone finishes echoing the natural beauty and simplicity of the great outdoors. Treetops offer seven streams, on-site trout fishing, four lakes, and over 70 kilometers of adventure trails.

    Overnight:Treetops Lodge & Estate

  • Day 4

    Rotorua

    Today's full day tour begins with a visit to the beautiful Government Gardens and the Rotorua Museum housing exhibits devoted to the 1886 eruption of Mount Tarawera, a nearby dormant volcanic mountain. You will then visit Rainbow Springs where you will take a tour through Kiwi Encounter, a hatchery and nursery for the unique and treasured "Kiwi? bird. Enjoy lunch (own expense) at a local restaurant. After lunch enjoy a stroll through the magnificent Redwood Forest before returning to your accommodation.

    Insider Access Kiwi Encounter

    This Insider Access tour is all about becoming better acquainted with New Zealand's elusive kiwi. Your tour will begin with a brief introduction at the incubation room and hatchery where you are able to see the equipment working. You will then continue to the Kitchen where the artificial diet for the young kiwi is prepared and you can see a variety of interesting food including worms and insects! Visit the nursery room filled with "brooders" - breeding units where the kiwi chicks stay in the nursery until they are approx 400 grams in body size then they are moved to outdoor enclosures where they grow until they are 1.2kg. From here you will enter the indoor nocturnal area where kiwi enclosures are completely open with no partitions between the kiwi and visitors! Your tour concludes with a static display of Kiwi Encounter that provides a wealth of information and audio visual of kiwi.

    Overnight:Treetops Lodge & Estate

  • Day 5

    Rotorua

    This morning meet your pilot in the lobby and make your way to your waiting helicopter to depart on your White Island / Mount Tarawera Active Volcano Tour.

    Have you ever wondered what it is like standing on the edge of a live volcanic crater? Fly to White Island and land for a guided walk in the crater of New Zealand?s most active Volcano, once home to factory workers who mined the island for Sulphur. Ruined remains of this hard life still exist today. During your flight be on the lookout for dolphins, a common sight in the Pacific Ocean. Your return flight includes an exclusive Mount Tarawera landing. On conclusion you will be returned to your accommodation.

    You will have time at leisure until late afternoon when you are collected from your accommodation by private vehicle and transferred to connect with the scheduled evening cultural experience.

    Te Po Combo is an Indigenous Evening Experience plus the Te Puia Daytime Experience - a feast of storytelling, entertainment and Maori cuisine, with a daytime guided tour included.Enjoy an exclusive guided tour at 4:30pm with one of Te Puia's world renowned guides. It includes a visit to the New Zealand national carving and weaving schools where you can watch these disciplines in practice under the guidance of our master carvers and weavers. The tour will include a visit to the award winning interactive galleries and Kiwi House, where you can see New Zealand's flightless bird up-close, followed by a visit to the mud pools and geysers.

    Po evening experience begins at Te Keketanga-a-Rangi (Heavenly Origins), a space comprising twelve monumental carvings reaching skyward. Hear stories of the past prior to assembling at the waharoa (gateway) of our marae. Experience an authentic Maori welcome ceremony, warrior's challenge and kappa haka (performing arts) concert.

    The Maori Feast a culinary opportunity like no other. The range of traditional and indigenous inspired dishes has been designed to create a memorable cultural experience. On conclusion you will be met and transferred by private vehicle back to your accommodation.

    Overnight:Treetops Lodge & Estate

  • Day 6

    Rotorua/ Christchurch

    Meet your A&K chauffeur in the lobby and transfer by private vehicle to the airport to connect with your domestic flight to Christchurch.

    On arrival in Christchurch you will be met by your A&K guide and taken on a private afternoon sightseeing tour. Today?s sightseeing tour includes a scenic drive along the River Avon the Botanical Gardens. Drive to the top of the Port Hills for a wonderful vista of Christchurch and the surrounding countryside including quaint Lyttleton Harbour. Visit the International Antarctic Centre that focuses on the great southern continent and join an exclusive tour. Enjoy a leisurely punt on the Avon River before being returned to your accommodation.

    Insider Access Old Antarctic Explorer Tour
    Join an Old Antarctic Explorer on an exclusive and personalized tour of Christchurch?s International Antarctic Centre discovering New Zealand?s Scott Base, The Snow & Ice Experience and the Antarctic Storm where you will experience extreme wind chill Antarctic conditions. Enjoy a cruise to Antarctica on board an Antarctic Cruise Ship in the brand new 4D Extreme Theatre or enjoy watching the penguins being fed in the New Zealand Penguin Encounter.

    Rations are available in the Antarctic Cafe & Bar and a signed copy of an Antarctic book is yours to take away at the end of your 3-hour personalized Antarctic experience.

    Includes: * All day pass to Antarctic Centre including penguin feeding & all activities. * Light lunch in cafe at reserved table with old Antarctic explorer. * Signed Antarctic book on departure with personal note.

    The George Christchurch The George, a luxury boutique hotel, is the essence of supreme comfort and is renowned for offering a personalized stay and attentive staff as well as two award winning restaurants. Located in the heart of Christchurch overlooking the picturesque Hagley Park and river Avon you will find a peaceful retreat. Impeccable service in an intimate ambience is guaranteed at The George, the only Christchurch Hotel with a one-to-one staff to guest ratio.

    Overnight:The George Christchurch

  • Day 7

    Christchurch/Greymouth/Franz Joseph

    Meet your A&K chauffeur in the lobby and transfer by private vehicle to the Christchurch train station to connect with the Tranz Alpine Train to Greymouth.

    The TranzAlpine scenic train journey travels between Christchurch and Greymouth, from one coast of New Zealand to the other. Climb into the Southern Alps and descend through lush beech rain forest to the West Coast town of Greymouth.

    On arrival in Greymouth meet your A&K driver / guide who will escort you down the West Coast of New Zealand over the coming days. You will depart Greymouth for Franz Josef, a journey of approximately two and a half hours. The road to Franz Josef is rural, but with a wild streak. The farmland is trying to turn back into forest, and the beauty of the rivers is raw and primeval. On arrival in Franz Josef you will be set down at your accommodation.

    Westwood Lodge
    Westwood Lodge has a warm relaxed atmosphere in a quiet rural setting and is only minutes from the world-renowned Franz Josef Glacier. The lodge is set in bush and farmlands with views of the mountains. It is just a short stroll to the township of Franz Josef and to all activities. It is the perfect place to relax and unwind, with just the right level of personal attention and unobtrusive service.

    Overnight:Te Waonui Forest Retreat

  • Day 8

    Franz Joseph

    Meet your A&K driver / guide in the lobby and transfer by private vehicle to connect with your private scenic helicopter tour.

    This premier flight lets you experience two of New Zealand's finest National Parks. Discover the gigantic expanse of the Franz Josef Glacier and Fox Glacier snowfields with a landing at high altitude. Step out of your helicopter and experience how it feels to stand in snow. The flight includes fantastic views of the Tasman Glacier and Mount Cook.

    • Flight duration 40 minutes (including snow landing)
    • Flights operate: on demand
    • Clients are required to reconfirm their bookings 24 hours prior

    After your helicopter tour meet your driver / guide and enjoy an afternoon touring this area. Franz Josef Glacier Village?s natural credentials are impeccable and there is a huge variety of natural attractions just a short distance from the Franz Josef Glacier Village. Lakes, rainforests, surging waterfalls and the glacial rivers of ice mean that the area is an adventure and leisure destination that is ready to be explored. The Village itself is the hub of the region with restaurants, nightlife and shopping.

    This afternoon / evening a private hot pool has been reserved for you (45 minutes) at the Glacier Hot Pools. Your driver / guide will transfer you and from the pools this evening. Nestled in lush rainforest, the Glacier Hot Pools is a haven of natural tranquillity and the perfect way to unwind and experience a blissful state of pure relaxation after travelling along Te Tai o Poutini (The West Coast) or enjoying one of its many attractions. The pools are conveniently located in the heart of Franz Josef township on Cron Street, and can be enjoyed all year round from 12 noon to 10pm (Last entry 9pm).

    Overnight:Te Waonui Forest Retreat

  • Day 9

    Franz Joseph / Lake Wanaka

    Today make your way to your accommodation in Wanaka, a journey of approximately four hours. Discover the rugged scenery along one of New Zealand's most awe-inspiring highways. Your journey will take you via the Haast Pass, the southernmost pass that connects the communities of the East and West, is humbling. Marvel at the vast rainforests to the clearest waterfalls that eclipse this untouched landscape. Enjoy breathtaking sights, from high mountain peaks to blue lakes before arriving into Wanaka.

    Whare Kea LodgeSituated amongst the mountains of New Zealand's spectacular Southern Alps on the shores of glacial Lake Wanaka lays a private alpine retreat. Whare Kea Lodge is designed to capture the setting's unique feeling appearing to float above the water giving magnificent vistas in an intimate atmosphere. All of the six suites have a separate bath and shower and have direct access to the sun deck.

    After your helicopter tour meet your driver / guide and enjoy an afternoon touring this area. Franz Josef Glacier Village?s natural credentials are impeccable and there is a huge variety of natural attractions just a short distance from the Franz Josef Glacier Village. Lakes, rainforests, surging waterfalls and the glacial rivers of ice mean that the area is an adventure and leisure destination that is ready to be explored. The Village itself is the hub of the region with restaurants, nightlife and shopping.

    Overnight:Whare Kea Lodge

  • Day 10

    Lake Wanaka

    Today you will enjoy a Wanaka Nature Encounter Tour! You will be collected from your accommodation and transferred to connect with the scheduled boat cruise on the pristine waters of Lake Wanaka to Mou Waho Island Nature Reserve. Enjoy a guided walk to the spectacular summit that reveals the island's secret – A Lake on top of an Island! - Enjoy a serene "High Tea" here, up close and personal with rare native birds, and plant a tree as part of this unique eco experience. On conclusion you will return to Wanaka where you will meet your guide and enjoy lunch before continuing on to the 4WD part of your day. After lunch (own expense) experience the stark beauty and solitude of the spectacular high country, bordering a World Heritage area by 4WD. You will explore the private farm tracks on an historic Wanaka sheep, cattle and deer station. From the site of Nehenehe, an ancient Maori settlement abandoned in the 1830‟s, to the ruins of Wanaka‟s first European settler‟s farmhouses, this journey offers a unique insight into Wanaka‟s rich history, heritage and modern-day farming life. On conclusion of your tour you will be returned to your accommodation.

    Lake Wanaka Cruise and Island Nature Walk Information
    • Total walking time 1.5 hours
    • Requirements: average health and fitness, strong treaded sports shoes or hiking boots, suitable warm clothing, good sweater or jacket

    Overnight:Whare Kea Lodge

  • Day 11

    Lake Wanaka/ Queenstown

    Meet your A&K driver / guide in the lobby and transfer by private vehicle to Queenstown, a journey of approximately one hour. Your journey today is alongside the Clutha River, Lake Dunstan and the Shotover River, through to Queenstown. The vineyards of the Gibbston area and bungy jumpers at the Kawarau River Bridge are interesting distractions along the way. The remainder of today is at your leisure.

    Matakauri Lodge
    Matakauri Lodge offers absolute comfort as well as stunning panoramic views. The Lodge and its adjoining suites are luxuriously appointed and have been designed to be in harmony with the lakefront environment and surrounding bush. It is one of New Zealand's most private and serene vacation retreats, nestled among bush on the edge of Lake Wakatipu, just 10 minutes‟ drive from Queenstown. Lodge facilities included a business centre, full equipped gymnasium, heated infinity pool and Jacuzzi.
    Note: During cocktails and dinner, gentlemen are required to wear a jacket (the Lodge has jackets available for guest use at Reception).

Overnight:Matakauri Lodge

  • Day 12

    Queenstown

    Today you will be whisked by helicopter to the World Heritage listed Milford Sound flying over secluded alpine lakes, lush rainforest, through deep bush-fringed valleys and past rugged mountain peaks. Take in the beauty of the Southern Alps on your way into Milford Sound, experiencing this amazing glacial creation. View Lake Quill, the source of the spectacular Sutherland Falls – the second highest waterfall in the Southern Hemisphere. Sweep the full length of the fiord, flying to a remote West Coast beach to view seal colonies. Before heading for home, weather permitting, you have a chance to land „on top of the world‟ where you can stand among the ancient ice flows of the Fiord land Glaciers. Enjoy a picnic lunch before flying back to Queenstown and transfer to the hotel.

    Overnight: Matakauri Lodge

  • Day 13

    Queenstown

    Today‟s tour of the Wakatipu basin includes a visit to the historic gold-mining settlement of Arrow town, local artist studios and the Lakes and District Museum, followed by the Kawarau Bridge made famous by A.J. Hackett and over one million bungy jumpers. After private winery experience and lunch you will return to Queenstown to take the Skyline Gondola cable car ride to Bob‟s Peak for spectacular panoramic views over Lake Wakatipu. On conclusion you will be returned to your accommodation.

    Private Mount Edward Winery Experience
    Mount Edward winery is situated in the Gibbston sub region of Central Otago, some 25km east of Queenstown. Founded in 1997 by the godfather of Central Otago Pinot Noir, Alan Brady, the winery grows limited quantities of premium pinot noir, single vineyard designates, and small batch classic white varieties. The first Mount Edward vineyard, “The Drumlin”, is situated on the steep hillside slope next to the winery and is testament to both hard work and vision, the 30 deg slope a reminder that nothing great can come easily.

    Tour includes:
    * 1.5 hours‟ time with winemaker. * Wine tasting (minimum 5 wines). * Barrel tasting. * One on one private appointment. * Lunch.

    Overnight: Matakauri Lodge

  • Day 14

    Queenstown

    Meet your A&K chauffeur in the lobby and transfer by private vehicle to the airport to connect with your onward flight.

    Overnight: Matakauri Lodge

    Tahiti Bora Bora

    Programme Overview

    • Duration: 8 Nights

      Highlights:Tahiti, Bora Bora, Lagoon cruise with shark & ray discovery, Jet Ski around Bora Bora lagoon, Discovery of Bora Bora Island in a 4x4, Sunset & Romantic dinner.

    • Day 1

      ARRIVAL TAHITI

      Arrival to Tahiti. Meet & Greet with flower lei, document remittance and transfer to the Radisson Plaza Resort in a private car. Check in to Ocean view Jacuzzi room, 1 night with American breakfast included.
      If your arrival is on a morning flight, it is also possible to proceed directly to Bora Bora.

      Overnight: Radisson Plaza Resort

    • Day 2

      TAHITI BORA BORA

      Transfer to Tahiti airport and take off on a 50 min flight to Bora Bora. Upon arrival, the St Regis Bora Bora staff will meet you directly at the airport and take you by boat to the resort.

      Check in to your Deluxe Overwater bungalow, 6 nights, American breakfast included for two.

      Facing the main island of Bora Bora and at 1,550 square feet, the Deluxe Overwater One Bedroom Villas are the largest fleet in the Pacific. Tastefully designed in exotic woods and marble; and featuring a private terrace and gazebo they easily translate to islands of tranquility.

      Overnight: St Regis Bora Bora

    • Day 3

      BORA BORA

      Suggested excursion: Lagoon cruise with shark & ray discovery
      A must on Bora Bora is to cruise around the lagoon, snorkel in coral gardens and admire the sting rays & sharks. The boat will take you to the most beautiful spots of the lagoon with the opportunity to feed rays, watch sharks & swim in a coral garden. It will also be a fantastic photo opportunity. Your morning will end with a stop on an islet for some fresh fruits and juice.

      Available by private boat or as a regular tour.

      Overnight: St Regis Bora Bora

    • Day 4

      BORA BORA

      Not to miss, The St. Regis Bora Bora 13,000 square foot world-class spa and fitness center is located on its own private island in the lagoon. The facility houses Tahitian and Pacific Rim treatments in seven luxurious spa treatment rooms and a 24-hour Techno gym fitness center. The use of natural herbs, extracts from the local beauty products as monoi oil, pure mother of pearl, and luminescent pearl powder, will create an unforgettable revitalizing experience.

      Overnight:St Regis Bora Bora

    • Day 5

      BORA BORA

      Suggested excursion: Guided tour by Jet Ski around Bora Bora lagoon
      This 2 hour circle island tour by Jet Ski is an opportunity to really enjoy yourself while discovering the most beautiful lagoon of the world. You will be amazed of the various shades of blue & turquoise of the lagoon while you follow your guide on your own Jet Ski (one or two persons per Jet Ski). A stop is included on a white sandy beach where some fresh fruits will be served. During a second stop on a ―motu‖ (small islet), enjoy a ―coconut show‖ – your guide demonstrates how to climb the coconut tree and how to open a coconut.

      Overnight:St Regis Bora Bora

    • Day 6

      BORA BORA

      Suggested excursion: Discovery of Bora Bora Island in a 4x4
      Discover the culture, history, fauna and flora of the island. Learn about the ancient temples of the great Tahitian seafaring society built around 1000AD & learn about the local legends visiting ancient archeological sites such as the Marae "temple", and the ―Turtle stone‖, located in Bora Bora's very own rain forest.
      In Faanui valley you'll spot small fruit plantations and learn about some of the local flora including medicine plants used by the ancient Medicine man, such as the Nono and the Hotu tree. Explore also the World War 2 sites of the Cannons and the bunkers and find out why Americans used Bora Bora as a staging area during WWII and their influence on the locals of Bora Bora. Other stops include various look out points around the island, such as "Top of the World " where you'll get to see Bora Bora on the West and East side and the Matira look out from where you'll see all various colors of the lagoon.

      Overnight:St Regis Bora Bora

    • Day 7

      BORA BORA

      Enjoy another relaxing day at the resort. In the evening we suggest another unforgettable tour :

      Sunset & Romantic dinner

      To end this very special holiday on Bora Bora, enjoy a sunset & a romantic dinner on an islet. As the day fades, a transfer by boat will take you directly from the pontoon of your hotel to a private islet. You will be comfortably seated in your lounge chair, facing the sunset, with a glass of punch. After this magical moment it’s time for a romantic dinner on a nicely dressed table. Maximum 6 persons (it is also possible to book a private evening).

      Menu:

      Appetizer : Cream of Uru (breadfruit)
      First course: Tahaa Vanilla Foie Gras with its fruit chutney
      Main Course: Plancha of fish, chicken, shripms (& lobster upon request - supplement)
      Dessert: Vanilla Creme Brulee
      One bottle of Champagne, wine, soft drinks, water, tea/coffee

      Overnight:St Regis Bora Bora

    • Day 8

      BORA BORA / TAHITI

      Take off to Papeete and connect to your international flight.
