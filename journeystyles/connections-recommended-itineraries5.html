South Africa: Cape Town, Kruger & Victoria Falls

Programme Overview

Explore Southern Africa's remarkable cities and landscapes, including Cape Town, Johannesburg, Victoria Falls, and Ngala Private Game Reserve, which you canvass on thrilling game drives.

  • Connections at a Glance

    • Visit legendary Table Mountain and spend a full day exploring Cape Point, with a visit to the Boulders African penguin colony
    • Make Culinary Connections when you explore the timeless vineyards of Constantia Valley and partake in preparing local dishes alongside a chef while in the African bush
    • Venture out on twice-daily game activities in Ngala Private Game Reserve, known for its elephants, buffalo, and rhinos
    • Make a Neighborhood Connection when you visit the rural community of Welverdiend
    • Absorb South Africa's deep history when you tour the capital of Pretoria and bustling Soweto, where you find the former homes of Nelson Mandela and Archbishop Desmond Tutu
    • Explore incredible Victoria Falls on a private walking tour
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE IN CAPE TOWN, SOUTH AFRICA

    After your arrival in the seaside city of Cape Town, you are met by an A&K representative and transferred to your boutique hotel.

    Spend the remainder of the day at leisure

    Overnight: Vineyard Hotel & Spa

  • Day 2

    CAPE TOWN / Rise of the Rainbow Nation

    Today, drive through the slopes of Table Mountain, and later board on a cable car (weather permitting) to reach the peak. On arrival, you enjoy the stunning panoramic views of the city and sea.

    Continue to journey downwards to arrive at the historic Cape Town, where you take a walking tour of the serene Company Gardens, en-route visiting the National Art Gallery and National Library.

    Further visit the Castle of Good Hope, South Africa's oldest surviving colonial building, where you appreciate the castle’s notorious dungeon.

    This afternoon, tour Constantia Valley, the oldest and famed wine making region in the southern hemisphere. Relish lunch at Jonkershuis, located inside the historic heart of the Groot Constantia Wine Estate, with over 327 years of uninterrupted wine production.

    Further, visit the Manor House Museum to appreciate the extra ordinary collection of Cape furniture and maritime art at the estate’s Manor House Museum. Continue for a visit to Constantia Glen Wine Estate, a boutique wine estate located in the magnificence of the Constantia Valley ,known for its pleasant climate, Bordeaux-style reds and Sauvignon Blancs.

    This evening, Join your fellow travelers at the hotel, where you enjoy the welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Vineyard Hotel & Spa
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 3

    CAPE TOWN / Scenic Cape Point

    Today, enjoy a beautiful drive along the Atlantic coast to Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve, a nature lover's paradise. En-route visit the bucolic fishing harbors and the Clifton and Camps Bay “bikini beaches,” at the foot of the Twelve Apostles Mountains. After arriving at the Cape of Good Hope, the rocky tip of the Cape Peninsula, you may choose to ride the Flying Dutchman funicular up to the lighthouse for panoramic aerial views of the rocky coastline. Admire the southern right whales breaching the water in spring and early summer.

    Continue to the Boulders Beach, a sheltered beach made up of inlets between granite boulders, famous for its charming colony of African penguins. Later, unwind at the quaint fishing hamlet of Kalk Bay. Proceed to explore the indigenous Kirstenbosch National Botanical Garden at the slopes of Table Mountain.

    Overnight: Vineyard Hotel & Spa
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 4

    GREATER KRUGER / Ngala Private Game Reserve

    This day, depart for Hoedspruit and then continue to drive to the Ngala Private Game Reserve, a luxury Safari Lodge located in South Africa's Kruger Park. Settle in your colonial-style lodge set on the reserve under a dense canopy of tamboti trees.

    This afternoon, enjoy the game viewing activities on offer at leisure. Or, simply relax on the veranda with views over a popular watering hole.

    Overnight: Ngala Safari Lodge
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 5-6

    GREATER KRUGER / The Vast African Veldt

    The next two days at Ngala Private Game Reserve, enjoying a relaxed serene atmosphere set in the wilds of the African bush. Each day you may choose your game viewing activities through Ngala, home to all of Africa’s “Big Five,” including: the elusive leopard, several prides of lion and notable packs of the rare and endangered African wild dog.

    Enjoy an exciting culinary experience by indulging in an interactive chef experience to learn and prepare local dishes. Next, explore the Hoedspruit Endangered Species Centre, with a large bio-diversity. Get an opportunity to closely observe cheetahs, wild dogs, caracals and various other rehabilitated species. Experience the local culture by meeting and interacting with the local Welverdiend community, also visiting its schools and crèches (nurseries) when in session.

    Overnight: Ngala Safari Lodge
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 7

    JOHANNESBURG / Thrilling Metropolis

    Depart for Johannesburg, on arrival at the airport, you are met and transferred to your boutique hotel with spectacular views of the city from a countryside setting.

    This afternoon, explore the place at your own pace or relax in the serene environment. Later, join your fellow guests for dinner.

    Overnight: Tintswalo at Waterfall
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 8

    JOHANNESBURG / Beginnings of Great Men

    Begin the day with a morning travel to historic Pretoria, famously known as the “Jacaranda City” for its tree-lined streets. After your arrival, explore the place starting with the Voortrekker Monument, massive granite structure prominently located on a hilltop. Continue, to the Church Square and the imposing Union Building, where you admire the spectacular city views.

    Enjoy lunch in Soweto, Infused with the history of the struggle against apartheid and abuzz with the energy of the city of gold. Continue for a guided tour of the former home of Nelson Mandela, now a museum and Archbishop Desmond Tutu, still occupied by family.

    Later, visit the Hector Pieterson Museum, where Hector Pieterson was shot and killed.

    Also view the Regina Mundi Catholic Church, the largest Roman Catholic Church in South Africa and take sight of the Chris Hani Baragwanath Hospital. The rest of the evening is at leisure.

    Overnight: Tintswalo at Waterfall
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 9

    VICTORIA FALLS / Cruising the Zambezi River

    Take a morning flight to Victoria Falls. Upon arrival you are met and escorted to your hotel set on a plateau adjacent to Zambezi National Park. After settling down, board on a sundowner cruise on the charming Zambezi River. Enjoy the cruise with spectacular views of hippos, crocodiles and antelope at the water’s edge. Also catch sight of Victoria Falls rising at a distance.

    Overnight: Victoria Falls Safari Lodge
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 10

    VICTORIA FALLS / The Smoke That Thunders

    Begin your day to admire the spectacular sight of awe-inspiring beauty and grandeur on the Zambezi River at the magnificent Victoria Falls. Take a guided walking tour of Victoria Falls to explore the falls, dropping at a massive 1.2-mile-wide curtain of water over the edge of the falls, with plumes of mist, rainbows and a thrilling roar.

    Go on to shop for locally made crafts and wares at the Falls Market before you join your fellow guests in the evening for a special farewell dinner.

    Overnight: Victoria Falls Safari Lodge
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 11

    DEPART JOHANNESBURG

    After breakfast, depart for Johannesburg, where you check in and relax at the comfortable Premier Lounge before boarding for your onward flight.

    Meal Plan: B

Spain: Seven Cities

Programme Overview

  • Explore Spain's cultural and culinary delights. Enjoy dinner at a private palace followed by a flamenco performance. You also get an opportunity to sample savory Spanish olive oil at a private tasting in Granada, sherry in Jerez and tapas in Seville.

    Duration: 10 Days/ 9 Nights

    Highlights: Barcelona | Granada| Marbella | Gibraltar | Seville | CÓrdoba | Madrid

    A&K advantages


    Our connections at a glance:

    • Explore the Boquería in Barcelona on our Market Connection
    • Sample savory Spanish olive oil at a private tasting in Granada
    • Visit an authentic bodega to privately sample sherry in the famed region of Jerez
    • Make a Culinary Connection in Seville on an evening tapas tour
    • Travel via horse-drawn carriage to a private palace in Seville for dinner with the owners
    • Enjoy an unforgettable private performance by local flamenco dancers and singers on an exuberant Musical Connection
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Barcelona

    On Arrival at the Barcelona Airport, you will be met by an A&K representative and assisted to your hotel. Enjoy a welcome dinner along with your fellow guests.

    Overnight: El Palace Hotel Barcelona
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    Barcelona

    Experience a culinary pleasure in one of the finest food market in all Europe at the exuberant city of Boquería.

    Further, visit the Sant Jaume Square, the cathedral and then down into the elegant Eixample district. Conclude your tour of this vibrant city with a visit to Gaudi's sensational Sagrada Familia Basilica.

    Overnight: El Palace Hotel Barcelona
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Granada

    Catch your flight to Granada and check in to your historic hotel. In the afternoon, visit the Alhambra Palace, a splendid example of the Islamic architecture from around the world. Next, travel to visit the magnificent district of the Albaizín, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in its own right. Unwind yourself with a unique Spanish experience: a tasting of local olive oils. Following dinner, enjoy a remarkable recital by an ensemble of world-class Andalucian musicians.

    Overnight: Hotel Alhambra Palace
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 4

    Marbella

    Take a departure from Granada and savor the journey to Marbella by stopping at the beautifully restored historic center in Málaga to visit the the Picasso and Carmen Thyssen Museums.

    Move ahead with your journey to explore Marbella, the finest resort on the south coast of Spain. Spend the afternoon at your leisure.

    Overnight: Hotel Fuerte Marbella
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Gibraltar

    Follow the coast westward to the Rock of Gibraltar and make a visit to the spectacular St. Michael's Cave .Explore Gibraltar's Barbary macaques and sight the only wild monkeys in all of Europe. Enjoy your return journey to Marbella with the remaining day at your leisure to rove around the city’s shops and restaurants.

    Overnight: Hotel Fuerte Marbella
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 6

    Seville

    Journey into the heart of Andalucía, starting with the winding hills to Ronda, one the most beautiful "white villages" in all of Spain. Continue through the mountains to Jerez, home of golden sherry. Get the opportunity to get a raw experience of the culture and culinary of the place, visit a bodega to learn about sherry and taste its different varieties.

    After enjoying the rest of your day at leisure take the roadway to reach Seville. The evening gives you a tour through Seville's finest taverns in the company your fellow guests.

    Overnight: Hotel Alfonso XIII
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 7

    Seville

    Begin your day by discovering this charming city, visiting the Royal Palace of the Alcázar, the enormous Gothic cathedral and of course, the flower-filled Santa Cruz district.

    This afternoon, explore the city at your leisure. Further, Take pleasure in a unique and exhilarating ride on a horse-drawn carriage to a ravishing palace, where the owners host you an exclusive private dinner. End your day with an enjoyable an unforgettable performance of flamenco dancing and singing on the patio of this historic home.

    Overnight: Hotel Alfonso XIII
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 8

    Córdoba

    Head your journey northwards today to Cordoba, where you visit the extraordinary Mezquita, the cathedral and former great mosque of Cordoba. Take a stroll along its flower-lined streets. By the late afternoon, board your high-speed AVE train to Madrid.

    Overnight: Hotel Ritz Madrid
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Madrid

    This morning, make time to witness and appreciate the exemplary works by the great Spanish masters El Greco, Velázquez and Goya in the Prado Museum. Further, visit the Royal Palace, followed by a short walk to the Plaza Mayor, and the marvelous San Miguel market, where you arrive in time to relish its mouth-watering ensemble of tapas bars for lunch at leisure. Join you’re A&K group leader and fellow guests for a farewell dinner.

    Overnight: Hotel Ritz Madrid
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 10

    Depart Madrid

    Take a transfer to the airport for your flight home.

    Meal Plan: B

SOUTHEAST ASIA: THAILAND, CAMBODIA & VIETNAM

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 Days/ 9 Nights

    A journey to the south eastern lands, exploring their culture and traditions with stunning panoramic views and experiences.

    Highlights:Bangkok | Chiang Mai | Siam Reap | Hoi Chi Minh City | Hoi An | Hanoi

    A&K Advantages

    • Enter the Grand Palace via a secret doorway.
    • Encounter one of the last masters of Thai traditional puppetry.
    • Explore Bangkok through a unique rickshaw and walking tour.
    • Witness a stunning sunrise at Angkor Wat.
    • Meet a war veteran who shares his eye-opening experience during your visit to the Cu Chi tunnels in Vietnam.
    • Travel by traditional cyclo through Hanoi's old quarter.
    • Experience a private elephant ride at Maesa Elephant Camp.
    • Chat with a Buddhist monk at Wat Umong.
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE BANGKOK

    On arrival at this vibrant capital city of Thailand, you will be met by an A&K representative and assisted to your hotel.

    Overnight: Anantara Bangkok Riverside Resort & Spa or similar
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 2

    Bangkok

    This day you begin with a visit to the Grand Palace, entering via a secret doorway that reveals oft-missed treasures in the exhibit of royal regalia. Stroll around its sprawling grounds populated with over 100 ornate buildings, gardens and statues of mythological creatures. Continue to the Wat Phra Kaew, where you will be mesmerized with the famed Emerald Buddha. Later, you proceed to Wat Pho, home to the 150-foot-long Reclining Buddha. Cruise over the Chao Phraya River as you move ahead to visit an artists' collective for a traditional performance by one of the last masters of Thai puppetry.

    A welcome dinner with fellow guests is on schedule tonight along with traditional Thai dance performances.

    Overnight: Anantara Bangkok Riverside Resort & Spa or similar
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 3

    Bangkok

    Explore the beaten path with a rickshaw ride and a walking tour along the other side of the river. Visit the local temple to experience the Buddhists worship style.

    Later during the day enjoy a long-tail boat ride to a lush, secluded area of the city. Take a ride on a samlor (bicycle taxi) through the luxuriant green parts of Bangkok before returning to the other side of the river.

    Rest of the day is at your leisure.

    Overnight: Anantara Bangkok Riverside Resort & Spa or similar
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 4

    Chiang Mai

    Take the Flight to Chiang Mai, the largest city in northern Thailand. Experience the Thai culture through its streets via a rickshaw ride with a stop at Wat Phra Singh, home to the revered Phra Singh (Lion Buddha), one of the most revered images of Buddha.

    Later, stroll through Chiang Mai's famous Night Market, a kaleidoscope of merchants and food carts.

    Later, experience the beauty of the place with a cruise of the rivers and canals of St. Petersburg. Pass by the Church of the Resurrection of Christ, a fine example of late 19th-century Russian architecture, and marvel its extraordinary exteriors.

    Overnight: DusitD2 Chiang Mai
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 5

    Chiang Mai

    Start your day with an engaging conversation with a Buddhist monk at Wat Umong, a famous centre of meditation. Afterward, visit Wat Doi Suthep.

    Enjoy your lunch in a hill tribe village, followed by an opportunity to experience a private elephant ride to Maesa Elephant Camp.

    Overnight: DusitD2 Chiang Mai
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 6

    Siem Reap

    Take the Flight from Chiang Mai via Bangkok to Siem Reap, near the ruins of Angkor Wat.

    Enjoy the romantic Ta Prohm, entangled in the surrounding jungle foliage. Climb over its crumbling stonework and marvel at the tree roots dripping down the temple's walls.

    Overnight: Sofitel Angkor Phokeethra Golf & Spa Resort
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 7

    Siem Reap

    Begin your day early today to break the dawn with a sunrise view at Angkor Wat. Go on to discover the masterpiece of the Khmer empire. Explore the place by walking through the moss-covered cobbled pathways and marveling at the intricately carved bas-reliefs.

    Continue your journey to visit Banteay Srei, a temple constructed with red sandstone. After lunch, Set out on a wooden boat and cruise through the floating villages on Tonle Sap Lake, visiting a local school en route.

    Overnight: Sofitel Angkor Phokeethra Golf & Spa Resort
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 8

    Siem Reap

    Set forth your journey to explore the fortified city of Angkor Thom and Bayon Temple, with its beguiling 172 large-scale faces.

    Conclude your day at the Elephant Terrace and the Terrace of the Leper King. The rest of the afternoon is at your leisure to explore Siem Reap.

    Overnight: Sofitel Angkor Phokeethra Golf & Spa Resort
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 9

    Ho Chi Minh City

    Set off on a flight to Ho Chi Minh City. Begin your exploration of the city formerly known as Saigon in Cholon, its Chinatown, home to delis, ancient quarters, old temples and beautiful architecture.

    Go forward with your exploration to Thien Hau Pagoda and Le Cong Kieu, one of the city's oldest commercial streets.

    Overnight: Caravelle Hotel
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 10

    Ho Chi Minh City

    Drive into the countryside to discover the expansive Cu Chi Tunnel network, a key Vietcong resource during the Vietnam War, and meet a war veteran who recounts his experiences in the tunnels.

    After enjoying your lunch, visit the Notre Dame Cathedral, with its neo-Romanesque façade and bell towers. Following that you visit the 19th-century French-colonial Post Office, the largest in Vietnam.

    Overnight: Caravelle Hotel
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 11

    Hoi An

    Take a departure from Ho Chi Minh City with a flight to Danang and continue to Hoi An. Experience this traditional town by visiting the colorful local market and the 400-year-old Japanese covered bridge.

    Stop by at a local home to witness how silkworms are raised. Moving further, visit an artisan's house for a demonstration on how to make Hoi An's famous silk lanterns.

    Overnight: Hyatt Regency Danang Resort and Spa
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 12

    My Son

    Begin your day with a visit to My Son, the former capital of the ancient Cham civilization and considered to be the Champa Kingdom's counterpart to Angkor in Cambodia.

    Enjoy your lunch near My Son. Later, return to Hoi An for an evening at leisure.

    Overnight: Hyatt Regency Danang Resort and Spa
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 13

    Hanoi

    Take a flight to the next destination, Hanoi. Start exploring Vietnam's capital by visiting Ho Chi Minh's House, and continue to the lotus-shaped One Pillar Pagoda, the Temple of Literature and the Quan Thanh Temple.

    Wrap up your city tour by visiting the famous "Hanoi Hilton" Prison, used by French colonists in Vietnam for political prisoners and later by the North Vietnamese for prisoners of war.

    Overnight: Hanoi Westlake Hotel
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 14

    Hanoi

    Make a Visit to the Fine Arts Museum, home to ancient antiquities and contemporary art. Following the visit set off on an exciting cyclo ride into the Old Quarter, where narrow streets dating back to the 15th century bear names reflecting the business once conducted there: Silk Street, Rice Street, Paper Street.

    Enjoy the lunch and an afternoon at your leisure. In the evening enjoy a farewell dinner with your fellow guests.

    Overnight: Hanoi Westlake Hotel
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 15

    Depart Hanoi

    Take a Transfer to the airport for your flight home.

    Meal Plan: B

Scotland & Ireland: Stories & Legends

Programme Overview

Visit Scotland's smallest malt whisky distillery and tour historic Glasgow. In Ireland, listen to a traditional storyteller, view the Book of Kells and watch as sheepdogs prove their amazing skills.

  • Duration: Days13/ 12 nights

    Connections at a Glance

    • Discover fascinating aspects of city and country life in each of these culturally vibrant countries
    • An Artisan Connection takes you to Scotland's smallest malt whisky distillery to meet its craftsmen and taste a sample
    • Enjoy rousing Scottish dancing and bagpipe music on a lively Musical Connection
    • Spend a heartwarming evening in the company of an authentic seancaithe (Irish storyteller)
    • Experience an amazing sheepdog demonstration as you travel around the scenic Ring of Kerry
    • Learn the fascinating culture and heritage of the Gaelic-speaking Blasket Islands region
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE EDINBURGH

    After your arrival in Edinburgh, you will be greeted and escorted to your hotel by an A&K representative. Tonight, enjoy welcome dinner with your fellow guests.

    Overnight: Waldorf Astoria Edinburgh - The Caledonian
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    EDINBURGH

    Begin to explore with a tour of the famous Edinburgh Castle, where you view the “Honors of Scotland” (the Scottish Crown Jewels).

    Later drive through the Royal Mile, en-route view St. Giles High Kirk, an architectural landmark.

    Arrive at Holyrood Palace, Queen Elizabeth’s official residence in Scotland (barring a Royal family visit). This afternoon, Savor a lunch of Scottish specialties.

    Overnight: Waldorf Astoria Edinburgh - The Caledonian
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 3

    SCOTLAND

    This day, explore the charming and historic town of St. Andrews, birthplace of golf and home to Scotland’s oldest university. Continue to visit the ruins of the castle and cathedral named for St. Andrew, the patron saint of Scotland.

    This evening, get the opportunity to form a musical connection with a rousing Scottish dancing and bagpiping performance.

    Overnight: Atholl Palace
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 4

    SCOTLAND

    Start the day to explore the Grampian Mountains, Inhale the air up there and be dazzled by the natural beauty. Continue for a tour of white-turreted Blair Castle, a picturesque stone village. Here, learn about the history of the major Highland clans and you may even see members of the Athol Highlanders, the Duke’s private army, making their rounds.

    Next, explore a malt whisky distillery, closely observe the craftsmen at work while you taste a sample. Later, travel back to Pitlochry, in the heart of Scotland for time at leisure.

    Overnight: Atholl Palace
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    GLASGOW

    Today, travel through the scenic Highland countryside, en-route viewing the spectacular Loch Laggan and Ardverikie House, location for the BBC’s Monarch of the Glen.

    Continue through the Great Glen to the famed Trossachs National Park. Pass through Loch Fyne to the beautifully conserved village of Luss.

    End the day at your hotel in Glasgow.

    Overnight: Hotel du Vin
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 6

    GLASGOW

    Begin the day with a city tour, visiting the largest medieval cathedral in Scotland and the many fine buildings of the university district.

    Continue to visit the Hunterian Art Gallery, the oldest public museum in Scotland. Later, view the reconstruction of the house of Charles Rennie Mackintosh, the great art nouveau designer.

    Next, explore the Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum to view its extensive and wide-ranging collections, including a superb selection of French Impressionists.

    Overnight: Hotel du Vin
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    DUBLIN

    Today, depart for Dublin, Ireland, where you will be met by an A&K representative and escorted to your hotel.

    This evening, form a connection with the age old story telling art of Ireland; indulge in a heartwarming exclusive story telling session with an authentic seancaithe (Irish storyteller).

    Overnight: The Merrion
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 8

    DUBLIN

    This morning, explore the capital city of Dublin. Begin with a visit to the esteemed Trinity College, Ireland’s oldest university. Explore the college with a private guided tour of its library, viewing the Book of Kells, a fabulous early Irish illuminated manuscript, ornate with complex insular art.

    Later, visit Ireland’s largest majestic church, St. Patrick’s Cathedral, named for the nation’s patron saint.

    Overnight: The Merrion
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    IRELAND

    This day, travel westward to Kilkenny and visit the iconic and spectacular Rock of Cashel, with its stone towers, walls and monuments, one of Europe’s most impressive collections of Celtic art and medieval architecture.

    Continue to view the stunning Blarney Stone, which is built into the steep battlements of Blarney Castle, and said to bestow the “gift of the gab” upon all who kiss it.

    End the day in your beautiful Victorian hotel in Killarney.

    Overnight: The Malton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    IRELAND

    Today, enjoy a beautiful drive through the scenic Ring of Kerry, passing gorgeous mountains and moorlands. Visit the Derrynane House, ancestral home of one of Ireland's most famous historical figures.

    Continue the journey into Killarney National Park to the world renowned Muckross House, a historic vine-covered stone manor in the shadows of Macgillycuddy’s Reeks, Ireland’s highest peaks. Explore its magnificent gardens and traditional farms before you witness an intriguing sheepdog demonstration at the Kissane Sheep Farm.

    Overnight: The Malton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    CLARE

    Travel along the south shore of the Dingle Peninsula to the beautiful and colorful fishing town of Dingle. Continue to take the Slea Head Drive, a road that hugs the cliff tops. Arrive at the westernmost tip of mainland Ireland for panoramic views of the Blasket Islands, one of the last entirely Irish-speaking regions of the country, also center of anthropological study. Visit the heritage center before you check into your accommodation in an actual Irish castle.

    Overnight: Dromoland Castle Estate
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 12

    CLARE

    Start your day with a travel to the towering Cliffs of Moher. Marvel at the tallest cliffs in Ireland, located in a rugged landscape that regularly endures the fiercest of Atlantic storms to spot the large colonies of birds precariously living there, including Atlantic puffins.

    Continue the travel to Burren, a great rocky expanse in Ireland's County Clare and one of the world's truly unique places, with its ancient awe inspiring stone structures.

    This evening, bid farewell to Ireland with a festive dinner along with your fellow travelers.

    Overnight: Dromoland Castle Estate
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 13

    DEPART IRELAND

    After Breakfast, transfer to the airport in Shannon for your onward flight.

    Meal Plan: B
