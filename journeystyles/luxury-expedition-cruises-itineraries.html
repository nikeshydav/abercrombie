ANTARCTICA, SOUTH GEORGIA & THE FALKLAND ISLANDS

Programme Overview

Embark on the luxurious 'Le Boreal' for an adventurous expedition across the Southern Ocean, stopping at wildlife-rich South Georgia, the unspoiled Falklands and the vast, wild shores of Antarctica.

  • Duration: 17 Days / 16 Nights

    Highlights: South Georgia | Falkland Islands | Antarctica

  • A&K advantages

    • A 15 night cruise aboard the luxury all-balcony expedition ship 'Le Boreal' on an exclusive A&K charter
    • Deep understanding of the region with enriching lectures by award-winning experts, including scientists, authors, historians and professional photographers
    • Visit to the gravesite of Ernest Shackleton, Antarctica’s greatest explorer, in Grytviken.
    • Trip toan active research station, and observe preservation efforts to protect this one-of-a-kind habitat
    • Exciting shore excursions in swift Zodiacs (sturdy inflatable boats)
    • A&K charter flights to and from Ushuaia

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE BUENOS AIRES, ARGENTINA

    On arrival in Argentina’s capital city, Buenos Aires, you will be welcomed by an A&K representative who will help you with an immediate check-in at your lavish hotel. With its wide boulevards and classic buildings, romantic Buenos Aires boasts energy of its own.

    Relax before this afternoon’s optional tour of the city’s highlights, including Plaza de Mayo, the stately Casa Rosada, the Cabildo and the Metropolitan Cathedral. You may drive past the colorful La Boca District or San Telmo, the birthplace of tango

    This evening awaits a special cocktail party exclusively organized for our A&K Guests where you may interact with the fellow guests.

    Overnight: Sofitel Buenos Aires or Four Seasons Buenos Aires (Categories 5-8)
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 2

    USHUAIA

    This morning, you will be assisted with your flight to the port city of Ushuaia, located at the southern tip of South America.

    On arrival, enjoy lunch at anindigenous restaurant. Time and weather permitting, take a guided trek in the Cerro Alarken Nature Reserve or simply stroll through the lively streets of Ushuaia.

    Board ‘Le Boreal’ and settle in to your cabin and appreciate the panoramic sea view right from your own private balcony. Walk the decks and explore the common spaces as you familiarize yourself with the luxury cruiser, your home for the next 15 nights at sea.

    Spend a comfortable and stress free evening at the bar with the fellow guests followed by a scrummy multi cuisine dinner.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    AT SEA

    As you sail east, towards the Falkland Islands, the expert lecturers begin their informal series of enlightening, daily discussions on the flora, fauna, geology and history of the region. In the past, lectures have covered diverse topics such as seabirds and penguins of the Southern Ocean, the formation of sea ice over thousands of years, and personal accounts of living and working in Antarctica.

    Although a professional photography enrichment coach is on hand to assist you with specific camera and composition questions, and lead lectures on various skills, the January 13 departure in particular focuses on photography.

    The cool waters in these parts are rich in krill, making them a favorite with local wildlife searching for food.Alongside the naturalist guides, you maycome across various species of whales and seabirds from ‘Le Boreal’s’ deck, experience of a lifetime.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    FALKLAND ISLANDS

    Spend the day exploring the Falklands. You may either visit Stanley, Falklands’ capital, and take a stroll along its charming streets, or explore thebackwoods. Also, discover the southeast Falkland Islands of Sea Lion or Bleaker, home to wildlife habitats, unscathed by human civilization.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L,D

  • Day 5-6

    CRUISING THE SOUTH ATLANTIC

    As you approach South Georgia, deck-side sightings of whales and other fauna become more frequent. Time spent cruising is once again filled with informative orations and casual talks with your guides.

    Scan the seas like an expert on Day 6, as the ship cruises through the rich waters along massive rocks.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7-9

    SOUTH GEORGIA

    South Georgia is nearly a submerged continuation of the Andes mountain range, and your days here are spent exploring its glacier-carved coves and rugged shores. Visit Salisbury Plain, St. Andrews Bay or Gold Harbour, homes to vast colonies of King Penguins as well as Fur and Elephant Seals.

    Avail the opportunity to retrace part of the route taken by Ernest Shackleton during his historic crossing of South Georgia in 1916.Weather permitting, you may visit his gravesite in Grytviken alongside your onboard historian, an expert on the great explorer’s life.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 10-11

    CRUISING THE SCOTIA SEA

    During your time cruising the Scotia Sea — rich with marine mammals and fish — the expert lecturers will help yourecapitulate your adventures in South Georgia together with sharing more enriching lectures, revealing more of what is to come on the Seventh Continent, from the terrain to the hardy wildlife that dwells in this dramatic environment.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 12-14

    ANTARCTICA

    This day calls for landings on the white continent and a number of South Shetland Islands, which may include Half Moon, Penguin and Deception. Other possible island visits include Cuverville, Paulet and Goudier. You may also sail through Paradise Bay and the Lemaire Channel, the latter often referred to as “Kodak Alley” for its breathtaking, picture-perfect beauty.Additional opportunities for exploration include a scientific research station.

    While on board ‘Le Boreal,’ spot whales and birdlife from the deck or spend time reading and relaxing while you enjoy some hot chocolate in your own private balcony.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 15-16

    CRUISING THE DRAKE PASSAGE

    This day, bid farewell to Antarctica as you cruise back to Ushuaia through the Drake Passage, where you find more opportunities to observe marine and birdlife.

    The evening awaits a palatable dinner with some lip smacking freshly prepared delicacies. Relive your incredible journey with fellow guests and new friends.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 17

    Disembark ‘Le Boreal’ after a lovely continental breakfast. You will then be transferred to the airport in your private vehicle, where you board your flight back home.

    Meal plan: B


    *Please note:This itinerary is provisional and subject to change due to ice, weather, wildlife and other conditions beyond A&K's control. The final itinerary will be determined at the sole discretion of the Captain and Expedition Leader.

  • Iguazu Falls Pre-Tour Extension

    Day 1

    ARRIVE IGUAZU FALLS

    This day, you will be aided with your flight to Iguazu Falls by an A&K representative. Explore the Brazilian side of the park, with its hundreds of cascades along a gulf. Join your guide on a tour through the national park to a catwalk within arm’s reach of the falls.

    Overnight: Hotel Das Cataratas
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    IGUAZU FALLS

    This morning, explore the Argentine side of the park, and walk the trails above and along the falls. You may choose to venture out on a catwalk that extends 3,600 feet over the tropical basin of Devil’s Throat.

    The afternoon is at leisure.

    Overnight: Hotel Das Cataratas
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 3

    DEPART IGUAZU FALLS

    After a wonderful mouthwatering breakfast you will be assisted with your transfer for your flight back to Buenos Aires.

    Meal plan: B

    Available on the Dec 19 and Jan 13 departures

ARCTIC CRUISE ADVENTURE: NORWAY, GREENLAND & ICELAND

Programme Overview

Cruise on the all-balcony 'Le Boreal' to the pristine and remote Arctic islands which only a few have seen, including the polar bear haven of Svalbard, Greenland and its remote villages, and Iceland's West Fjords.

  • Duration: 15 Days / 14 Nights

    Highlights: Norway | Greenland | Iceland

  • A&K Advantages

    • A 13 night cruise aboard the luxury all-balcony expedition ship 'Le Boreal'
    • Travel in the company of A&K's captivating Expedition Team for a rich and memorable journey
    • Visit to the remote Arctic islands that are rarely seen by others, all during the summer season of the midnight sun
    • Explore the off-beaten-path areas of Kejser Franz Josef Fjords and Scoresby Sound on Greenland's east coast
    • A private behind-the-scenes tour of the world renowned Husavik Whale Museum in Iceland
    • Excursion to theextinct Snaefellsjokull volcano.
    • Convenience of an A&K charter flight to Longyearbyen

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE OSLO, NORWAY

    On arrival in Oslo, Norway’s modern capital city that also coexists in close harmony with nature, you will be received by an A&K representative who will assist you wthl your transfer to the hotel. Once you settle down, you will meet your local guide for an afternoon walking tour.

    Enjoy your eveningat a private graceful reception in your palatial hotel’s Speilen room, site of the Nobel Prize banquet.

    Overnight: Radisson Blu Scandinavia Hotel
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    LONGYEARBYEN

    This morning, you will be assisted to board a charter flight to Longyearbyen, the capital of Svalbard, also known as Spitsbergen, the sovereign Arctic archipelago of Norway. This city is one of the most secludedgroup of islands on the planet, with nearly two-thirds of its surface covered with glaciers. Take a guided tour of the tiny town and its Arctic museum.

    Take a brief stop for local lunch, after which you board your expedition cruiser.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3-6

    SVALBARD ISLANDS

    Very few people have the opportunity to view the remote wilderness of the Svalbard Islands. In the Svalbard archipelago, polar bears, walrus, Svalbard reindeer and an incredibly rich variety of birds are as abundant as are cathedrals of ice, glaciers and craggy mountain peaks. An amazing amount of diminutive flora surrounds this barren landscape, which comes alive with color under the August midnight sun.

    Svalbard’s wildlife is contrasted by the unexpected culture of its fishing villages. Your captain and expedition crew determine the day’s best sightseeing opportunities, which may include these extraordinary regions:


    Magdalenefjoren:

    A stunning fjord with rugged rock walls, Magdalenefjoren surmounts blue-hued glaciers crisscrossed by crevasses, with birds perched in the high cliffs. This “glacier coast” provides magnificent views of slow-moving glaciers travelling out to sea as well as beaches frequented by walrus.


    NyAlesund:

    History here stretches back to the early Arctic explorers. Today, a polar research station occupies the site where a Norwegian coal mine once operated, and scientists study meteorology, seismology, atmospheric sciences and marine biology.

    Conditions permitting, you visit the small community, including a museum, art gallery and the northernmost post office in the world which is sure to be a life-changing experience.

    Alkjefellet (Auk Mountains):

    Set off on Zodiacs (sturdy inflatable boats) to explore the 325-foot sheer cliffs which are home to 60,000 guillemots and kittiwakes. These nests attract “poaching” polar bears and Arctic fox, who come to feast on eggs and birds, and provide an opportunity to view them safely from the Zodiacs.


    Monaco Bay and Glacier:

    Located in Northwest Spitsbergen, Monaco Bay is frequented by a variety of wildlife population, including polar bears, walrus, harbor and bearded seals, and a multitude of seabirds.


    Liefdefjorden (Loves Bay):

    In far Northern Svalbard, Liefdefjorden is an excellent area for spotting polar bears and other Arctic wildlife.

    Throughout the landings and days of cruising in Spitsbergen,an objective will be to search for polar bears as they hunt in their true natural habitat — be it on land or among the ice floes, each landing under the close supervision of the guardians, who have the best knowledge about where to find the white giants.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7-8

    CRUISING THE GREENLAND SEA

    Comfort yourself as you cruise along the Arctic Ice Edge through the Greenland Sea, as the presenters recap their productions on the wildlife, history and geology of Svalbard, and prepare you for the world’s largest island: Greenland.

    Spend time on board with new friends and the Expedition Team, ever on the lookout for wildlife

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 9-11

    GREENLAND

    For the next three days, explore the rarely visited east coast of Greenland, Kejser Franz Josef Fjords and Scoresby Sound.

    Embark on twice-daily Zodiac excursions to cruise the intricate fjord network and watch out for the musk ox and reindeer. If the ice conditions permit,take a brief stop to visitIttoqqortoormiit, a small settlement founded in 1925 and one of the most isolated villages in Greenland. You may also visit the “warming island” of Uunartoq, which was once believed to be a point on the mainland of Greenland, and today provides further evidence of climate change.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 12

    CRUISING THE DENMARK STRAIT

    Luxuriate in a relaxing day in your vessel as you cruise the relatively narrow strip of water that separates Greenland from Iceland, and connects the Arctic Sea with the Atlantic Ocean.

    Spot exotic seabirds and whales from the deck in between fascinating lectures presented by A&K’s Expedition Team.

    This evening awaits an exhilarating gala dinner hosted by the captain for all the guests on board.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 13

    HUSAVIK

    This morning, arrive in Husavik, the first permanent village settled in Iceland.Visit the attractions of the excellent Husavik Whale Museum, actively supported by A&K Philanthropy, and one of the world’s foremost centers focused on whales and their habitat. Afterward, choose to embark on a scenic whale watching tour followed by free time in Husavik. Or, set off for Godafoss and Lake Myvatn, a thermally active region that provides a rich habitat for a variety of wildlife.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 14

    GRUNDARFJÖRDUR

    Take in the glacier-carved terrain of Iceland’s West Fjords, highlighted by the sparkling waterfalls and icebergs of Drangajokull Glacier. Visit Grundarfjördur, one of the first villages in Iceland. Then, choose from three excursions; You may visit the Snaefellsnes Peninsula with its fishing villages, stunning Snaaefelsnes Glacier and views of the majestic, extinct Snaaefelsjokull volcano — the site that inspired Jules Verne’s fictional ‘Journey to the Center of the Earth’. Alternatively, you may embark on a boat tour among the numerous islands of Breidafjörður, an archipelago teeming with exotic birds, followed by catch of a fresh shellfish which you can relish over dinner. Or, you may even hike well-known sites from the Icelandic Sagas, including the beautiful Berserkjahraun lava field; the 240-foot Mt. Helgafell (“The Holy Mountain”), with spectacular views over the Breidafjordur islands (weather permitting); and, the old town of Stykkisholmur and Bjarnarhofn (“Bear Harbor”), where you explore a shark museum and a wooden Dutch church.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L,

  • Day 15

    DEPART REYKJAVIK

    This morning, disembark ‘Le Boreal’ in Reykjavik from where you will be driven in a private vehicle to the airport for your flight back home, or continue your journey to Reykjavik for a post-tour extension.

    Meal plan: B,

    *Please note:This itinerary is provisional and subject to change due to ice, weather, wildlife and other conditions beyond A&K's control. The final itinerary will be determined at the sole discretion of the Captain and Expedition Leader.
  • Reykjavik Post-Tour Extension

    Day 1

    REYKJAVÍK

    After an appetizing breakfast, depart ‘Le Boreal’ accompanied by your A&K Expedition Team for a Reykjavík city tour in your custom built four-wheel-drive.

    Overnight: Radisson 1919
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 2

    REYKJAVÍK

    This day, choose from two full-day excursions: explore Iceland’s Golden Circle, including Thingvellir National Park, the geologically active Geyser region and the Gullfoss waterfall; or experience the magnificent Blue Lagoon.

    Overnight: Radisson 1919
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 3

    REYKJAVÍK

    Rise early this morning to visit the Blue Lagoon or for a journey to the south shores of Iceland, including Skogarfoss waterfall, the black sand beach of Reynisfjara and an ice walk.

    The evening is at leisure.

    Overnight: Radisson 1919
    Meal plan: B

  • Reykjavik Post-Tour Extension

    Day 4

    DEPART REYKJAVÍK

    After a fresh delicious breakfast, you will be assisted with your transfer to the airport for your flight back home.

    Meal plan: B

CLASSIC ANTARCTICA

Programme Overview

Join a world-renowned Expedition Team on a voyage to the southern edge of the globe, timed to feature the best weather as well as an ocean and shorelines teeming with magnificent wildlife.

  • Duration: 12 Days/ 11 Nights

    Highlights: Argentina | Antarctica

    A&K Advantages

    • A 10 night cruise aboard the luxury all-balcony expedition ship 'Le Boreal' • Discover some of the world's rarest wildlife and terrain on exciting excursions led by this region's most passionate and seasoned guides
    • Gain a deep understanding of the region with enriching lectures by A&K's own award-winning experts, including leading scientists, authors, historians and professional photographers
    • Choice of two unique departures: an A&K Philanthropy-supported journey on Fighting Climate Change, led by climate expert, Dr. James McClintock, or a Photographic Expedition, led by wildlife photographer Richard Harker and designed for all levels of interest and skill
    • A&K charter flights to and from Ushuaia

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE BUENOS AIRES, ARGENTINA

    Arrive this morning in Argentina’s capital where you will be received by an A&K representative who with assist you with transfer to your accommodation and an immediate check-in.

    Enjoy some time to relax before this afternoon’s city tour. This energetic and seductive port city has been the gateway to Argentina for centuries, bringing many porteños into its boundaries. Although Buenos Aires is the reminiscent of a European city, the spirit of this city is evident in its diverse and distinctly local neighborhoods.

    In the evening, meet your fellow adventurers over a welcome cocktail reception.

    Overnight: Sofitel Buenos Aires or Four Seasons Buenos Aires (Categories 5-8)
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 2

    USHUAIA

    In the morning, after a hearty breakfast, you will be escorted to your flight to the charming seaside town of Ushuaia, the southernmost city in the world.

    On arrival, enjoy lunch at an indigenousrestaurant. Time and weather permitting, take a guided trek in the Cerro Alarken Nature Reserve or simply enjoy downtown Ushuaia.

    This afternoon, you will be assisted for your arrival at your ship. The stylish and comfortable ‘Le Boreal’ features all-balcony staterooms and suites exclusively available for A&K journeys to Antarctica. Wander the decks to become acquainted with the ship, relax in the lounge and mingle with fellow guests in anticipation of your voyage.

    Cruise through the stunning Beagle Channel before heading out to sea.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3-4

    CRUISING THE DRAKE PASSAGE

    The journey towards Antarctica is an exciting one through the Drake Passage. An important trade route in the 19th and early 20th century, the Drake Passage is a 600-mile-wide swath of water where the Southern Ocean currents are squeezed between South America’s southern tip and Antarctica’s northernmost reaches. The stormy waters and icy conditions often make the journey a difficult one, though the stabilizer-equipped ‘Le Boreal’ handles it with ease. As you travel in the comfort of your luxury cruiser, indulge in enriching orations from members of the Expedition Team on topics ranging from bird life and geology, to photography and history.

    After a scrumptious lunch, join our naturalists on the deck from where you may spot various species of whales and abundant seabirds, which join other mammals on the krill-rich waters of the Drake Passage; it’s all just a taste of the wildlife you will encounter in the days to come.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5-9

    ANTARCTICA

    As the Antarctic Peninsula and its offshore islands appear on the horizon, a sense of excitement begins to build. While day-to-day itineraries depend on weather, water and wildlife conditions, two excursions are carefully planned for each day by the captain and expedition leader.

    Each cruise day begins with an early exquisite breakfast. Afterward, venture out to a number of sites in swift Zodiacs (sturdy inflatable boats) where you will be accompanied by expert drivers and lecturers. These sites may include the penguin-filled islands in the South Shetlands of Half Moon, Penguin or Deception; islands off the continental coastline such as Cuverville, Paulet or Vortex and, weather permitting, the fabled Antarctic Peninsula. You may also sail through Paradise Bay and the Lemaire Channel, referred to as “Kodak Alley” for its stunning, camera-ready vistas of towering icebergs and deep blue waters.

    After a morning exploring native species and striking terrain,return to ‘Le Boreal’ for some lip-smacking lunch and perhaps some time to relax and scan the wildlife-rich waters that surround you, where you may spot everything from fin whales, humpback whales and orcas, to imperial shags and the southern giant petrel.

    Later in the afternoon, set out for the day’s second excursion, which may include a visit to a working scientific research station, the remains of a turn-of-the-century whaling and sealing station, a sheltered beach teeming with basking elephant seals, or simply circumnavigating colossal icebergs that serve as floating homes to seals and penguins.

    After an exciting day, return to your cruiser to relax and chat over cocktails in the cozy, comfortable lounge and enjoy dinner in the dining room. You may also choose to recap the day’s discoveries with your naturalist guides on deck or seize the opportunity to capture photos in the stunning low light characteristic of Antarctica’s long, ethereal twilight.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 10-11

    CRUISING THE DRAKE PASSAGE

    Bid farewell to the wondrous Antarctica and begin your cruise back to Ushuaia. The ship’s return through the Drake Passage is an interlude for recap and reflection of the White Continent. Spend time on board with newfound friends in the comfortable common spaces of ‘Le Boreal,’ enjoy quiet relaxation on the private balcony of your cabin or absorb more fascinating talks by your captivating Expedition Team. While cruising through this historic passage, you may spot various species of whales and seabirds, including the elusive albatross and the giant petrel, an opportunistic predator and scavenger nicknamed the “stinker.” The albatross is an especially stunning sight to behold. These wandering birds boast a wingspan of 11.5 feet on average and use their powerful wings to glide through the air for hours at a time in search of food.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 12

    Depart Buenos Aires

    ‘Le Boreal’ docks at Ushuaia this morning, and after breakfast, say goodbye to the A&K staff and ship’s crew before disembarking. You will then be assisted with your transfer to the airport for your homebound flight via Buenos Aires.

    Meal plan: B *Please note:This itinerary is provisional and subject to change due to ice, weather, wildlife and other conditions beyond A&K's control. The final itinerary will be determined at the sole discretion of the Captain and Expedition Leader.

  • Iguazu Falls Pre-Tour Extension

    Day 1

    IGUAZU FALLS

    This day, you will be driven to the airport for your flight to Iguazu Falls. Explore the Brazilian side, with its hundreds of cascades along a gulf and join your guide on a tour through the national park for a catwalk within an arm’s reach from the falls.

    Overnight: Hotel Das Cataratas
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    IGUAZU FALLS

    Rise early this morning and explore the Argentine side of the park, and walk the trails above and along the falls. You may choose to venture out on a catwalk that extends 3,600 feet over the tropical basin of Devil’s Throat.

    Enjoy an afternoon at leisure.

    Overnight: Hotel Das Cataratas
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 3

    DEPART IGUAZU FALLS

    In the morning, you will be assisted with your transfer to the airport as you fly back to Buenos Aires for your onward connections.

    Meal plan: B

    Available on the Dec 9 departure

THE NORTHWEST PASSAGE: FROM GREENLAND TO THE BERING SEA

Programme Overview

Sail from western Greenland to Alaska and the Bering Sea on an extraordinary voyage that transits the entire Northwest Passage, aboard exclusively chartered 'Le Boreal.'

  • Duration: 24 Days / 23 Nights

    Highlights: Canada | Greenland | Bering Sea | Alaska

    A&K Advantages

    • A 21 night cruise aboard the luxury, all-balcony expedition ship 'Le Boreal'
    • Sail through the full extent of the Northwest Passage, from Greenland to the Canadian Arctic provinces, to the Bering Sea and ending in Nome, Alaska.
    • Discover historic and remote islands, fjords and harbors along the elusive Northwest Passage
    • Visit to a traditional hunting village
    • Learn the art of leather tanning
    • Excursion to geological wonders from the "Smoking Hills" of Franklin Bay to the dramatic IlulissatIcefjord, a UNESCO World Heritage Site teeming with icebergs
    • A trip to the rarely visited Yukon Territory, where whaling once thrived and an abundance of wildlife still reigns supreme
    • A&K charter flights to Kangerlussuaq and from Nome

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE MONTREAL, QUEBEC

    Arrive in Montreal, Quebec’s European-style city where you will be received by an A&K representative who will assist you with your transfer to your elegant and luxurious hotel.

    In the afternoon, meet your local guide for a walking tour.

    Tonight, gear up for an exclusive welcome cocktail reception and dinner to celebrate the start of your exhilarating adventure.

    Overnight: Fairmont Queen Elizabeth
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    KANGERLUSSUAQ

    Arrive by a charter flight this afternoon in Western Greenland at Kangerlussuaq (SøndreStrømfjord in Danish), located on the tip of its namesake fjord and once a strategic allied stronghold during World War II.

    Then, you will be driven in your private vehicle to the pier to board your luxurious expedition cruiser, ‘Le Boreal.’ Spend a pleasant evening interacting with your award-winning Expedition Team and crew over a welcome cocktail reception.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3-7

    WESTERN GREENLAND

    In 1906, polar explorer Roald Amundsen became the first person to successfully cross the entire Northwest Passage under sail. Start your voyage exactly where Amundsen did — along the stunning West Coast of Greenland and north into Baffin Bay.

    Begin this day with your enrichment and lecture program, offering the first of many opportunities to delve deeper into the story of this dramatic and remote region.

    Based on ice, weather and sea conditions, your captain and expedition crew determine the day’s best sightseeing opportunities, which may include:

    SISIMIUT

    Located just north of the Arctic Circle, Sisimiut is both the northernmost city in Greenland able to maintain a year-round, ice-free port as well as the southernmost town with sufficient snow for dog sledding through the winter and spring. Visit a local museum with its interactive exhibits on Inuit culture and Greenlandic colonial history, as well as a local dog musher and his dog-sled team.

    DISKO BAY & IlULISSAT

    Cruise into Disko Bay, a wide inlet off the Baffin Sea first explored by Erik the Red in AD 985, when he established the first Norse settlements in Western Greenland. Discover the IlulissatIcefjord, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, at the mouth of one of the fastest and most active glaciers in the world, SermeqKujalleq. The view is spectacular with giant icebergs, floating growlers, large chunks of glacial ice, and the sounds of the calving ice-stream.

    You may also take a walking tour of the village of Ilulissat, including a visit to the local history museum, and interact with the villagers in their multicolored homes to learn about their lifestyle in this often-harsh Arctic region. Indulge in a huskie dog-sled demonstration and absorb the centuries-old methods of leather tanning.

    KULLORSUAQ

    Located at the southern end of Melville Bay, Kullorsuaq is the northernmost village in the Upernavik Archipelago, and one of the northernmost settlements in Western Greenland. Founded in 1928 as a trading station, Kullorsuaq welcomed its first migrants some 4,000 years ago as well as the migration of all southbound inuits, which followed. It stands today as one of the most traditional hunting and fishing villages in Greenland.

    Grasp the full meaning when you spend time with local villagers who still maintain a traditional way of life, living off of the narwhal and seal populations common to the region.

    MELVILLE BAY/KAP YORK

    Enjoy excursions on Zodiacs (sturdy inflatable boats) through this remote area of far Northwestern Greenland, and among thousands of small icebergs and bergy bits.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8-16

    NUNAVUT & THE CANADIAN ARCTIC ARCHIPELAGO

    This day, cruise west across Baffin Bay, into the Canadian Arctic Archipelago of Nunavut, where your journey to the heart — and history — of the Northwest Passage begins. The A&K Expedition Team continues to share a captivating oration programs to enhance your discovery.

    As ‘Le Boreal’ winds its way through legendary channels and inlets, the crew and Expedition Team will determine the best route based on ice, weather and sea conditions.

    The coming days await innumerable exciting excursions which are likely to include:

    POND INTET

    Located on the Northern end of Baffin Island, Pond Inlet is a noted gateway to the fabled Northwest Passage. You will be assisted with customs formalities for Nunavut, after which you head out for a shore excursion to an area originally inhabited by the Thule. Stop by the Nattinnak Visitor’s Center or ToonoonikSahoonik Co-op, where you may shop artisan carvings made from local red and green soapstone, beautiful wall hangings and various other handicrafts.

    LANCASTER SOUND

    Situated between Devon Island and Baffin Island, this body of water forms the eastern entrance to the Parry Channel and the Northwest Passage. It’s also home to a rich abundance of Arctic cod, which in turn draws copious populations of sea birds and marine mammals. Beluga and endangered bowhead whales, the narwhal with its spiraling tusk, ringed and bearded seals, the enchanting polar bear, and mustached walrus, as well as northern fulmars, black guillemots and Arctic terns — all are among the fantastic wildlife that belong to this area.
    Some may even come into view on exciting Zodiac excursions and landings.

    BEECHEY ISLAND

    Historic moments in Arctic exploration define this island, best known for providing a safe haven to British explorer Sir John Franklin in 1845. Look east, towards Resolute Bay at the huge silhouette of Cape Riley and imagine what Franklin saw here in Erebus Harbour, where he took shelter for two years before his ill-fated attempt to conquer the Northwest Passage. Observe the wooden grave markers for three of Franklin’s men, now bleached by the sun, and visit the cenotaph memorial erected in memory of the lost explorer, a remarkable experience!

    GJOA HAVEN

    During his first attempt to transit the Northwest Passage on ‘Gjøa,’ a small fishing vessel, Roald Amundsen used this natural harbor as a respite while waiting for ice conditions to improve. For two years, he lived with the NetsilikInuit, learning their skills of survival and more efficient travel, which later proved invaluable in his successful South Pole expedition.

    Today, Gjoa Haven has a population of 1,200 and still bears the historic significance of playing a key role in polar exploration.

    VICTORIA ISLAND

    Cruise along the south coast of Victoria Island through Queen Maud Gulf, Dease Strait and Coronation Gulf. Expedition stops may include: the bird sanctuary of Jenny Lind Island, where you may also spy its populations of musk ox; the village of Cambridge Bay; and remote Holman Village (Ulukhaktok).

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 17

    FRANKLIN BAY & THE SMOKING HILLS

    This day, in the Northwest Territories at Franklin Bay, see the spectacular “Smoking Hills,” cliffs of bituminous shale that combust and endlessly burn. This rare geological phenomenon has likely been occurring for millennia, with layers of the relatively unstable mineral jarosite covering these hills. When the mineral comes into contact with cold air, it becomes red-hot and produces a thick, black smoke — a fantastic site not unlike the smoky fumaroles produced by volcanoes.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 18

    HERSHEL ISLAND & THE YUKON TERRITORY

    During a long mapping expedition in 1826, Captain Franklin was the first European to lay eyes on this unique island, at the northernmost point of the Yukon Territory. Named by Franklin, Herschel Island is a landmark in the West Arctic and has since served alternately as a whaling station, relay station and refuge for travelers.

    This morning, visit the island which teems with wildlife that includes the migrating bowhead whale, walrus, moose, musk ox, Arctic fox and 94 species of birds. It is also one of the only places on earth where you may see a grizzly bear, black bear and polar bear, the last of which live along the ice edge in summer. Evidence of the island’s whaling culture and Thule Inuit predecessors remains near the shoreline, though it may not be for much longer; the island is subject to extreme coastal erosion and scientists predict the shoreline will disappear under the waves within 50 years.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 19-20

    BEAUFORT SEA & POINT BARROW

    Typically dense with ice floes and fog, the Beaufort Sea opens up a 60-mile-wide coastal pass from August to September. Continue sailing in the comfort of your luxury expedition cruiser, participating in eye-opening lectures led by the Expedition Team.

    Be on the lookout across the sea for bowhead and beluga whales, the latter of which sustain one of the largest populations in the world here. Still hunted on a sustenance quota basis by local Inuit, the sociable creatures often travel in huge numbers and are said to be quite “chatty,” with their trills, clicks and squeals audible above the surface.‘Le Boreal’ cruises into the U.S. and clears at Point Barrow, Alaska. Your voyage continues through the narrow passage between North America and the ever-changing Arctic ice cap.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 21

    LITTLE DIOMEDE

    This day, continue cruising through the Bering Sea to Little Diomede, an island that sits between Alaska and Russia.

    Disembark for a Zodiac cruise of the island, where the Ingalikmiut people still maintain a traditional lifestyle of hunting, fishing and egg gathering. In line with customs and necessity, the Ingalikmiut also use seal, walrus and polar bear hides to make clothing, parkas, hats and mukluks, as well as trade currency for bartering.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 22

    GAMBELL & ST LAWRENCE ISLAND

    In the morning after a great continental breakfast, conditions permitting, visit St. Lawrence Island, where the distinct Yup’ik Eskimo culture and dialect, similar to that found in Siberia, is a reminder of the cultural transition from East to West their prehistoric ancestors made across the former “Land Bridge” which is now the Bering Strait.

    Whalebones, fish-drying racks and umiaks (traditional walrus-skin boats) are part of the landscape. Revel in traditional dances, which also bare similarities to those of their Siberian cousins.

    Overnight: Le Boreal
    Meal plan: B, L, D
