Sicily: From the Mountains to the Sea

Programme Overview

Embark on a comprehensive journey that spans this storied island and includes 12-century mosaics in Palermo, wine tasting in Marsala, ancient archaeological sites and a traditional puppet maker.

  • Duration: 10 Days/ 9 Nights

    Highlights: Palermo | Sciacca | Selinunte | Agrigento | Taormina | Catania

  • A&K advantages

    • Ride Like a Local when you take a cable car up to Mount Etna to see its fascinating, lunar-like landscape
    • Learn the history and elaborate procedure needed to create Marsala wine
    • Watch a demonstration by an artisan chocolate maker and enjoy a tasting at a Chef's Table culinary experience
    • Enjoy an Artisan Visit to a traditional Sicilian puppet maker

  • Day 1

    Arrive Palermo

    On your arrival in Palermo, you will be met by an A&K representative and assisted with transfer to your hotel located in the city’s historic center.

    This evening, be welcomed with drinks followed by a festive dinner.

    Overnight: Palazzo Sitano
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    Palermo

    Begin to explore Palermo, Sicily’s chief port. View its majestic Cathedral, famous Pretoria Fountain and medieval Norman Royal Palace, one of the most important architectural monuments of Palermo.

    Further, explore the 12th-century Cathedral of Monreale, lavishly adorned with mosaics illustrating Biblical events. Continue to the cloistered gardens decorated with mosaics.

    Overnight: Palazzo Sitano
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 3

    Sciacca

    This day, drive to Segesta, where you view its fifth-century-BC temple. Proceed to Erice, a mesmerizing medieval town above the bay of Castellammare del Golfo, for splendid panoramic views of the surrounding area.

    Continue to travel along the Via del Sale (“salt way”) towards Marsala, en-route catch sight of windmills and pause to learn about salt production.

    After arrival, visit a local wine cellar to learn the history of making Marsala fortified wine, followed by a wine tasting session. Then, proceed to Sciacca, where you check into your hotel.

    Overnight: Verdura Golf & Spa Resort
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Selinunte

    Begin the day with a travel to Selinunte. Stroll through this gigantic archaeological site exhibiting remains of eight temples, set against the blue Mediterranean Sea.

    Enjoy your lunch and the remaining afternoon is at leisure to unwind.

    Overnight: Verdura Golf & Spa Resort
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 5

    Agrigento

    Continue the journey with a drive to Agrigento. After arrival, discover the ancient Valley of the Temples, including the famous Temple of Concord, an exemplary of sacred classical architecture.

    Further, view Piazza Armerina, a charming old countryside town. Explore Villa Romana Del Casale and appreciate its unparalleled mosaics, beautifully illustrating hunting and the domestic life.

    Overnight: Donnafugata Golf Resort
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    Agrigento

    Start the day with a visit to the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Val di Noto. This region is seven towns influenced by the Greeks, Arabs, Byzantines, Romans, Normans and Spaniards.

    Further, appreciate the grandeur of Ragusa, an explosion of Sicilian Baroque architecture.

    Next is a visit to the Modica village. Participate in a private chocolate-making demonstration and savor the results.

    Overnight: Donnafugata Golf Resort
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 7

    Agrigento

    Today, travel to the Greek Theater in Syracuse, an important example of theater architecture. Continue to Dionysius’ Ear, a nearby carved-stone cave with astounding acoustic qualities.

    Further, travel to the charming neighborhood of Syracuse known as Ortygia, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Travel around the town and later, transfer back to your hotel for dinner and wine tasting.

    Overnight: Donnafugata Golf Resort
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 8

    Taormina

    As you travel to Taormina stop by in Catania to visit a traditional Sicilian puppets maker.

    Afterward, pause at Catania’s vibrant and bustling fish market for an authentic local Market Visit. Savor a lunch of fresh fish.

    Proceed with your travel to splendid Taormina. Walk through its colorful main street, Umberto Corso, and further visit the Greek Theater for remarkable views of Mount Etna.

    Overnight: San Domenico Palace
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 9

    Taormina

    Today, travel into the fertile lava flow country of Europe’s largest active volcano. Ascend to 6,000 feet and be engulfed by the mesmerizing views on your way.

    Further, board a cable car to ascend to charming lunar-like landscape. Later, return to Taormina and relax with a Scenic Sundowner overlooking Mount Etna.

    Tonight, join your fellow guests for a farewell dinner.

    Overnight: San Domenico Palace
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 10

    Depart Catania

    After breakfast, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

Botswana Safari in Style

Programme Overview

Stay at boutique camps and lodges on this A&K safari journey into three phenomenal game areas - the Okavango Delta, Moremi Game Reserve and Chobe National Park - as well as stunning Victoria Falls.

  • Duration: 10 days/ 9 nights

    Highlights: Botswana

  • A&K Advantages

    • A&K's flagship Southern Africa safari journey, backed by more than five decades of on-the-ground experience
    • Stay in intimate boutique-style camps and lodges, exclusive to small groups or individual guests
    • Get tips on capturing the best wildlife images during a private lecture with a professional photographer
    • View Africa from an elephant's perspective during a special interaction with a group of semi-habituated African elephants
    • Taste local dishes prepared by a resident chef as part of A&K's Chef's Table experiences
    • Take a guided tour of Victoria Falls, experiencing both the Zambian and Zimbabwean sides of the falls
    • Coast along the Zambezi River on a relaxing sunset cruise

  • Day 1

    Arrive Johannesburg

    Upon your arrival in Johannesburg, you will be assisted with transfer to your hotel.

    This evening, experience an A&K Chef's Table welcome featuring a locally sourced dinner. Later, you will be joined by a professional photographer, who offers you tips for your journey ahead.

    Overnight: Saxon Boutique Hotel & Spa
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    Okavango Delta | Wetlands Wildlife

    Today, depart for Maun, the "tourism capital" of Botswana. Proceed for your light aircraft transfer to a 260,000-acre private concession in the Okavango Delta, the largest inland delta in the world and home to some of the world's most endangered species of large mammal. After arrival, settle on your classic-style tented camp under a canopy of ebony and sausage trees.

    This afternoon, enjoy your choice of game viewing activities at leisure, including seasonal mokoro (dugout canoe) ride on the delta or a nighttime game viewing drive, made possible on this private concession.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Stanley's Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Okavango Delta | Living With Elephants

    This morning, enjoy your breakfast before you proceed for your choice of activities. Set off on a thrilling, guided game drive in a customized 4x4 vehicle or a bush walk in the company of an armed, expert guide. On you lookout for giraffes, hippos, lions and cheetahs cross unspoiled vast landscapes. Afterward, enjoy a picnic in the shade near the elephants.

    Further, you get a unique opportunity to interact up-close with the semi-habituated African elephants in their natural habitat at an A&K Philanthropy sponsored project. Observe their unique personalities and everything from their powerful trunks and tusks to their fanning ears and bristled tails.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Stanley's Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Moremi Game Reserve | Sanctuary Chief’s Camp

    Today, fly across the Okavango Delta to Sanctuary Chief's Camp, located in the heart of the Moremi Game Reserve amid the glassy waters of the seasonal flood waters.

    Settle in your uber luxurious bush lodge, set along natural pathways frequented by the local wildlife. Widely acknowledged as the "predator capital of the world," Moremi offers some of the most exciting game viewing in Africa and is one of the best places in Botswana to see the country’s “Big Five” — the lion, leopard, rhino, elephant and Cape buffalo. This is primarily because the lush delta draws a fantastic concentration of wildlife.

    Predators include cheetahs, spotted hyenas and, at the very top of the food chain, a large resident pride of lions. White and black rhinos are also found here, the latter of which is among Africa’s fastest-disappearing species. This afternoon, take advantage of game viewing activities led by experienced driver/guides or enjoy the views from your own private viewing deck.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Chief's Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Moremi Game Reserve | Remarkable Game Drives

    Begin your day early for a spectacular morning game drive activity. Beyond predators expect to sight large concentrations of elephants, buffalo, red lechwes, giraffes and impalas.

    Later, enjoy a seasonal mokoro ride, a rare safari opportunity across deceptively quiet marshlands alive with crocodiles, bathing hippos, birds and tiny red reed frogs.

    Further, you may opt for an afternoon game drive. Experience the natural habitat of the wildlife as you listen to roaring lions on hunt or spy nocturnal animals as they start out for the night.

    End your day at the camp to relax, dine and enjoy the hospitality.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Chief's Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    Chobe National Park | Magnificent Watering Hole

    This day, enjoy a hearty breakfast and depart for Chobe National Park, appearing as a swathe of brilliant, peacock blue ribbon, winding its way through the tiny town of Kasane, and ensuing wilderness.

    Arrive at your exquisite lodge located above the Chobe River, with stunning views across the islands. Bounding impalas and purposeful elephants — with a staggering concentration of some 120,000 in the park — may pass before your outdoor patio.

    This evening, relax with a dip in the split-level swimming pool or book a treatment at the exceptional spa.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Chobe Chilwero
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Chobe National Park | Photographic Safari Cruise

    This morning, embark on a sunrise game drive to observe the animals at the start of their day. You may see hippos, crocodiles, leopards and even a colony of carmine bee eaters.

    Further, explore the local culinary as you prepare and eat traditional fare alongside the resident chef. Next is a photographic safari cruise aboard a double-decker boat with 360-degree swivel seats.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Chobe Chilwero
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    Victoria Falls, Zimbabwe | Victoria Falls

    Start the day with a travel to Zimbabwe to visit awe-inspiring Victoria Falls, the world's greatest sheet of falling water.

    Later, proceed to Sanctuary Sussi & Chuma in Zambia, for your luxurious stay in a beautiful tree house, built into the riverside canopy.

    This evening, enjoy the sunset with a sundowner cruise, sipping cocktails against the African sky.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Sussi & Chuma
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 9

    Livingstone, Zambia | Victoria Falls & Village Visit

    This morning, explore the magnificent Victoria Falls with a guided walking tour from the Zambian side — a different perspective on an awe-inspiring natural wonder. Enjoy a closer view of the mighty Zambezi River, as it cascades over the edge of an impressive 1.2-mile wide basalt lip.

    Later, stroll through the market to sight local crafts and excellent wood carvings.

    This day, you may also visit Nakatindi Village and provide support for the community’s school and clinic, both operating with assistance from A&K Philanthropy.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Sussi & Chuma
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 10

    Depart Johannesburg

    Take a flight to Johannesburg and relax in the Premier Lounge before your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

South Africa & Victoria Falls

Programme Overview

Journey across South Africa's varied landscapes, experiencing vibrant Cape Town and scenic Cape Point. Embark on game drives in Sabi Sands and Lion Sands Game Reserves. Tour the magnificent Victoria Falls.

  • Duration: 9 days/ 8 nights

    Highlights: South Africa & Victoria Falls

    A&K Advantages

    • Combine contrasting - but consistently luxurious - stays in the city center, wild bush and picturesque Victoria Falls
    • Discover the Cape of Good Hope and the Boulders African penguin colony
    • Dive into local cuisine with Chef's Table experiences from a progressive dinner to cooking side-by-side with a chef
    • Experience game drives, bush walks, hippo walks, astronomy and wildlife lectures during a stay at Lion Sands, the only reserve in South Africa to offer safaris in both the Sabi and Sand rivers ecosystems
    • Cruise the Zambezi River at sunset with a sundowner cocktail in hand, all while taking in the fiery African sky
    • Explore Victoria Falls by private walking and cycling tours
    • Retreat to award-winning accommodations named on Conde Nast Traveler's 2014 Gold List and among Travel + Leisure's 2014 World's Best, including One&Only Cape Town and Lion Sands River Lodge

  • Day 1

    Arrive Cape Town

    After arrival in Cape Town, you will be met by an A&K representative, who will assist you with transfer to your luxurious hotel on the fashionable Victoria & Alfred Waterfront.

    Begin with your journey in Cape Town, a breathtaking harbor city where old and new converge in history, architecture, cultural pursuits, and the essence of its people. Warmed by the African sun, the city is dominated by the towering, flat-topped Table Mountain, set on a peninsula of rocky heights and lush valleys.

    Overnight: One&Only Cape Town
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Cape Town

    Today, travel through the scenic, shrub-dotted landscape to Table Mountain; ascend to its sky-high plateau by cable car (weather permitting) for dazzling views of city and sea. Continue to visit the old Cape Town with views of notable buildings, including Groote Kerk (South Africa’s oldest church), City Hall and the Grand Parade.

    Later, explore the pentagonal Castle of Good Hope, South Africa's oldest surviving colonial building, exhibiting a unique combination of cultural and military history. Experience the local art and culture with an Artisan Visit at Streetwires, where locals come together to craft unique handmade wire and bead art, successfully exported to over 15 countries worldwide.

    Spend the afternoon at leisure and later proceed for a welcome dinner. Enjoy a culinary experience with A&K Chef’s Table, where the resident chef or sommelier guides you through a progressive, contemporary feast.

    Overnight: One&Only Cape Town
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 3

    Cape Town

    This day, explore Cape Point, the mountainous and scenic promontory at the southwestern tip of the continent. Travel past the “bikini beaches” at the base of the towering Twelve Apostles Mountains, bucolic Hout Bay with its bustling fishing harbor and over the famous Chapman’s Peak Drive.

    Further, visit the picturesque Cape Peninsula and Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve, rich in cultural and natural heritage with a vast collection of rare and endemic plant species not found elsewhere.

    Later, ride the “Flying Dutchman” funicular to the great pinnacle at blustery Cape Point, which presides over treacherous reefs, sandy beaches and, during the winter months, breathtaking views of Southern right whales.

    Next, visit the Boulders Beach, famous for its colony of endangered African penguins. Enjoy endless photo opportunities at the beach before you take a guided tour of the spectacular Kirstenbosch Botanical Gardens.

    This evening, you may choose to relax at your hotel, explore the city’s harborfront and boutique shops, or dine at one of Cape Town’s world-class restaurants.

    Overnight: One&Only Cape Town
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    Sabi Sands Game Reserve

    Today, fly to Sabi Sands Game Reserve, the most prestigious game park in South Africa and renowned for some of the best Big Five game safaris in the country. After arrival, proceed to the privately owned Lion Sands Game Reserve.

    Settle in your beautiful lodge, located along the banks of the Sabie River surrounded by trees, around 800 years old. Facing due east, the lodge catches the sun as it rises over Kruger National Park. Hosted by the fourth generation of a family that makes it feel like home away from home, the lodge embodies spirit, warmth and nature.

    This afternoon, set off in search of the “Big Five” — elephants, lions, rhinos, buffalo and leopards — and other wildlife.

    Overnight: Lion Sands River Lodge
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5-6

    Lion Sands Game Reserve

    The next two days discover this private game reserve, the only reserve in South Africa to offer safari adventures in both the Sabi and Sand rivers ecosystem.

    Get the opportunity to explore through game drives in open Land Rovers for optimum viewing. Embark on early-morning game walks with your ranger. Lion Sands Game Reserve is known as one of the finest in Africa for its high densities of wildlife, which often appear just steps in front of you.

    Whether on a game drive or walk, you learn about the local wildlife, flora and fauna, as well as basic tracking skills. In between safari activities, partake in an A&K exclusive culinary experience at the Chef’s Table, where you prepare a traditional South African stew of potjiekos.

    Enjoy the stunning view of sunset with a sundowner; later, proceed with your search for nocturnal animals before returning to your lodge for dinner.

    Overnight: Lion Sands River Lodge
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Victoria Falls

    Begin the day with a morning flight to Livingstone, Zambia. After arrival, you will be met by an A&K representative, who will assist you with transfer to your hotel in Victoria Falls, Zimbabwe, located on a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This original Edwardian colonial hotel is designed with the elegance of old world travel in modern luxurious style.

    Further, you may explore the town market and nature trail through a private cycling tour.

    Overnight: The Victoria Falls Hotel
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 8

    Victoria Falls

    Begin the day with a guided walking tour of breathtaking Victoria Falls, one of the natural wonders of the world, dropping at a massive 1.2-mile-wide curtain of water over the edge of the falls, with plumes of mist, rainbows and a thrilling roar. Later, stroll through the Falls Market, where you may shop for locally made crafts and wares.

    Enjoy the sunset with a sundowner cruise along the Zambezi River, while sipping cocktails, view hippos across the water and elephants at the shore.

    This evening, gather with fellow guests for a farewell dinner.

    Overnight: The Victoria Falls Hotel
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 9

    Depart Johannesburg

    Today, fly to Johannesburg; relax in the Premier Lounge before boarding your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

    Also visit the 300-year-old Grand Provence Heritage Wine Estate, en-route viewing Haute Cabriere Wine Estate and the Huguenot Memorial Museum.

    Later, return to Cape Town to begin your main program.

Southern Africa Safari by River & Rail

Programme Overview

Stand atop Table Mountain and under the mist of Victoria Falls, experience Chobe River on a luxury houseboat, ride by train on the timeless Rovos Rail and set off on game drives across the Okavango

  • Duration: 13 Days/ 12 Nights

    Highlights: Cape Town | Rovos Rail | Johannesburg | Victoria Falls Chobe | Okavango Delta | Johannesburg

    A&K Advantages

    • From cosmopolitan Cape Town to thundering Victoria Falls to the extraordinary Okavango Delta, experience the best of Southern Africa on one spectacular itinerary
    • Embark on a three-night stay along the banks of the Chobe River aboard the graceful 'Zambezi Queen'
    • Ride over mountain ranges and grassy savannas on a two-day train journey by elegant, timeless Rovos Rail
    • Enjoy a guided tour of jaw-dropping Victoria Falls - "The Smoke That Thunders"
    • Set off on thrilling game drives from Sanctuary Chief's Camp, situated in the Moremi Game Reserve and offering incredible wildlife viewing, including the "Big Five"
    • At a Chef's Table experience, enjoy preparing and eating traditional local fare alongside the resident camp chef

  • Day 1

    Arrive Cape Town

    After your arrival in the seaside city of Cape Town, you are met by an A&K representative and escorted to one of the country’s most famous hotels.

    Overnight: Belmond Mount Nelson Hotel
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Cape Town

    Today, drive through the slopes of Table Mountain, and later board on a cable car to reach the peak. Take in the panorama of southern seas, sun-drenched city and the impressive cliffs of the Twelve Apostles, brooding high above the ragged shore.

    Continue to travel towards the historic Cape Town, en-route viewing the serene Company Gardens with views of the Houses of Parliament and National Art Gallery.

    Next, delight yourself with locally handcrafted wire and bead gifts from streetwires, followed by an afternoon at leisure. This evening, join your fellow travelers for a welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Belmond Mount Nelson Hotel
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 3

    Cape Town

    Today, enjoy a beautiful drive along the Atlantic coast to Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve, a nature lover's paradise. En-route view the fishing harbors of Hout Bay. After arriving at the rocky tip of the Cape Peninsula, you ride the Flying Dutchman funicular up to the lighthouse for panoramic aerial views of the rocky coastline. Admire the southern right whales breaching the water in spring and early summer.

    Spot Cape mountain zebra and possibly the rare bontebok en route to the Boulders Beach, a sheltered beach made up of inlets between granite boulders, famous for its charming colony of African penguins.

    Pass through Simon’s Town with its English seaside feel, the brightly painted boats on False Bay. Further, cross Constantia, South Africa’s legendary wine country on your way to the indigenous Kirstenbosch National Botanical Garden at the slopes of Table Mountain. Here, explore five of Africa’s six different biomes with a private, local guide. Later, return to your hotel for an evening at leisure.

    Overnight: Belmond Mount Nelson Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4-5

    Rovos Rail

    Recapture the romance of a bygone era as you board the wood-paneled coaches of the luxurious Rovos Rail. Settle into your suite featuring a sitting area, en suite bathroom, and perfect blend of period ambiance and modern amenities. Depart Cape Town Station and wind through mountain ranges, rolling green hills and the Cape Winelands, before stopping to visit Matijiesfontein, an authentically preserved Victorian village. Sip South African vintages in fine dining cars serving up artful menus as the stunning scenery scrolls by.

    The next day, unveil another perspective from the Observation Car at the rear of the train. In Kimberley, take a tour of the Diamond Mine Museum and the world’s largest man-made excavation site. Continue on through the impressively barren Great Karoo and the grasslands of gold-rich Highveld, marveling at the changing landscapes as day turns to night.

    Overnight: Rovos Rail
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    Johannesburg

    After breakfast and views of the gold fields of Witwatersrand, arrive at Pretoria Station.

    Later, proceed to settle in your modern, residential-style retreat in dynamic Johannesburg. Enjoy lunch and spend the remaining day at leisure.

    Overnight: Saxon Boutique Hotel & Spa
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 7

    Victoria Falls

    Take a morning flight to Victoria Falls. Upon arrival you are met and escorted to your beautiful, historic hotel overlooking the awe-inspiring falls.

    After settling down, set sail on a sunset cruise on the charming Zambezi River, watching for the rising mist of the falls in the far distance while you sip a refreshing sundowner cocktail. Enjoy the cruise with spectacular views of hippos, crocodiles and antelope at the water’s edge. Also catch sight of Victoria Falls rising at a distance.

    Overnight: The Victoria Falls Hotel
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 8

    'Zambezi Queen'

    Begin the day with a guided walking tour on the lush, mist-drenched Zimbabwe side of iconic Victoria Falls, also known as “The Smoke That Thunders.” See the cloud of spray where the mile-wide Upper Zambezi falls 300 feet into the Lower Zambezi; this same mist can be seen for miles, as well as rainbows that constantly form and fade away. Further, you may shop for locally made crafts and wares at the Falls Market.

    Continue with a leisurely drive to Kasane, where you board your five-star houseboat, the ‘Zambezi Queen.’ Enjoy the spectacular sunset views on this elegant, uniquely crafted African floating home while sipping cocktails and relishing canapés. Enjoy surrounding views of profuse habitats and abundant plant life.

    Tonight, enjoy a scenic dinner onboard amidst the majestic surroundings of the river.

    Overnight: 'Zambezi Queen'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 9-10

    'Zambezi Queen'

    Continue to explore the wildlife-rich Chobe plain which boasts lions, giraffes and hippos, as well as Africa’s highest concentration of elephants. Bespoke your experience of this wildlife-rich region, you may opt for a morning and afternoon activity. Choose from a tour of the local Namibian village; fishing on the Chobe River; or a river safari from the seat of a small boat.

    The next morning, explore Chobe National Park with a game drive. Followed by lunch and time to relax aboard ‘Zambezi Queen’, perhaps aboard a smaller boat to fish or embark on a river safari for more intimate views of the river.

    This evening, observe baboons, buffalo, and captivating familial herds of elephants from the decks of ‘Zambezi Queen, also view hippos emerge for their nonstop, nightly graze.

    Overnight: 'Zambezi Queen'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 11

    Okavango Delta

    Enjoy breakfast before you disembark the ‘Zambezi Queen’. Then, fly to the Okavango Delta via Kasane Airport.

    Arrive at Sanctuary Chief ’s Camp in the Mombo Concession of the vast Moremi Game Reserve. Known as the “Predator Capital of the World” and home to the “Big Five,” the reserve offers Botswana’s most exciting game viewing with a lush delta that draws a remarkable concentration of birds and wildlife.

    Spend the rest of the day at leisure, perhaps enjoying the activities on offer or exploring at your own.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Chief's Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 12

    Okavango Delta

    Rise early for a game drive on the Okavango Delta, the largest inland delta in the world and home to some of the world's most endangered species of large mammal. Expect to view magnificent large concentrations of elephants, buffalo and impalas; lions and other predators also call the Okavango home.

    Later, enjoy a seasonal mokoro ride, a rare safari opportunity across deceptively quiet marshlands alive with crocodiles, bathing hippos, birds and tiny red reed frogs. Participate in an exclusive Chef’s Table cooking demonstration with the resident chef.

    Tonight, conclude your journey with a special farewell dinner.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Chief's Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 13

    Depart Johannesburg

    After breakfast, depart for Johannesburg, where you check in and relax at the comfortable Premier Lounge before boarding for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B
