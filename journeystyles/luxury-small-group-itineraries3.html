Tanzania Safari in Style

Programme Overview

Embark on an authentic Tanzanian safari adventure, watching for wildlife in Tarangire, Ngorongoro Crater and the Serengeti, and slumbering at A&K's luxurious private tented camps.

  • Duration: 8 days/ 7 nights

    Highlights: ARUSHA | TARANGIRE NATIONAL PARK | NGORONGORO CRATER | SERENGETI NATIONAL PARK

  • A&K advantages

    • Experience A&K's best Tanzania itinerary on a flagship safari journey that features luxurious permanent tented camps and a private mobile tented site
    • In the atmospheric Ngorongoro Crater, attend a private, hosted barbecue lunch, watch a private Maasai dance performance and visit a Maasai village to learn about the tribe's customs and daily life
    • Discover humanity's ancient past on a privately guided visit to Olduvai Gorge
    • Prepare local dishes as part of a Chef's Table experience

  • Day 1

    Arrive Arusha

    After your arrival in Arusha, transfer to your lodge set on a working coffee plantation. Your private chalet near Mount Meru features a log fireplace and a balcony.

    Overnight: Arusha Coffee Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2-3

    Tarangire National Park

    Today, arrive at the Tarangire National Park. Begin your exploration of the wild landscape with open grasslands, acacia trees and the Tarangire River. This afternoon, discover the park with game drives. Embark on a search for eland, oryx, wildebeests, zebras and an abundant elephant population.

    Continue with your exploration on the next day. Set off on a game drive and lookout for hartebeests, gazelles and exotic birds like the bateleur and martial eagle.

    On your last day at the camp, take part in an A&K exclusive Chef’s Table experience, where you learn to prepare local dishes like ugali and kachumbari.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Swala or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Ngorongoro Crater

    This day, travel though the Great Rift Valley. En-route stroll through the markets in Mto wa Mbu, where stalls brim with vibrant local produce. Continue with a scenic drive to tranquil Gibb’s Farm. After arrival, enjoy your lunch made with organic, fresh ingredients.

    Further, drive across the Tanzanian border to the Ngorongoro Crater, a UNESCO World Heritage site, an extinct volcano, now a bucolic sanctuary supporting a wide array of wildlife. Climb the outer wall, passing through dense vegetation until a breathtaking view across the crater floor appears.

    Proceed to settle in your exclusive, private mobile camp, set on the rim of the crater and featuring luxury accommodations adorned with mosquito nets, hand-woven rugs, crisp linens and en suite bathrooms with flush toilets and hot showers.

    Relax in your balcony and enjoy the enchanting scenic beauty of the 14-mile-wide caldera, home to the world’s highest-density of lions, also a haven for elephants, zebras, gazelles and hyenas.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Ngorongoro Crater Camp or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Ngorongoro Crater

    Today, descend the crater and its short grass prairies in search of lions, wildebeests and even rare, endangered black rhinos. Avoid the crowded picnic sites and enjoy a private hot gourmet lunch, prepared by your camp staff, as symphonies of wildlife sounds surround you.

    Next, set off for a visit to Maasai village. Here, you get an opportunity to know in-depth the tribe’s traditional, nomadic lifestyle and coexistence with wildlife as you interact with a village elder. Further, you may observe a demonstration of traditional bead work to appreciate the artistic work of local artisans.

    Later, witness a private dance performance at your tented camp.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Ngorongoro Crater Camp or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    Serengeti National Park

    Start the day with a scenic drive to the wildly beautiful Serengeti National Park. Stop en-route for a guided visit of Olduvai Gorge, home to many significant archaeological discoveries.

    Proceed to settle in your camp and then, explore the vast Serengeti lands, one of the oldest and least-changed ecosystems on earth.

    Home to the largest concentrations of wildlife in the world, including lions, hyenas and leopards, the region also hosts the spectacular Great Migration, when a flurry of wildebeests and zebras arrive in search of grass and water.

    Later, unwind as the sun hangs low with refreshing sundowner cocktails.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Kusini or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Serengeti National Park

    Today, embark on morning and afternoon game drives in the protected Serengeti refuge, where flowing rivers support a variety of birds, mammals and reptiles. In addition to the “Big Five” (lions, elephants, buffalo, leopards and rhinos), you may also encounter the lesser-known “Little Five”— ant lions, rhino beetles, leopard tortoises, buffalo weavers and elephant shrews.

    Actively capture the glimpses of cheetahs, while exploring the landscape. You may contribute your photos to help track the spotted predators for the Serengeti Cheetah Project, an effort supported by A&K Philanthropy.

    This evening, share your experiences with the fellow guests over the farewell dinner.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Kusini or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    Depart Arusha

    This day, depart for Arusha and proceed to relax in your dayroom before boarding your onward flight.

    Overnight: Arusha Coffee Lodge (dayroom) or similar
    Meal plan: B

  • Dates & Prices

Tanzania Wildlife Safari

Programme Overview

Immerse yourself in the beauty of Ngorongoro Crater; meet a Maasai village elder to learn about the tribe's nomadic culture; and partake in a Chef's Table experience highlighting local cuisine.

  • Duration: 8 days/ 7 nights

    Highlights: Arusha | Lake Manyara National Park | Ngorongoro Crater | Serengeti National Park

  • A&K Advantages

    • Experience northern Tanzania's best parks and reserves, including intimate Lake Manyara, the stunning Ngorongoro Crater - a UNESCO World Heritage Site - and the iconic Serengeti plains
    • Stay in classic and ideally situated safari lodges with outstanding views
    • Meet with a Maasai village elder to learn about the tribe's nomadic culture
    • Prepare and sample local dishes, including ugali and makande, during a Chef's Table event at your lodge
    • Stop en route to the Serengeti for a tour of archaeologically rich Olduvai Gorge, where the Leakey family discovered fossils linking man to his distant ancestors

  • Day 1

    Arrive Arusha

    After your arrival in Arusha, transfer to your lodge set on the leafy hillsides of Mount Meru, overlooking coffee plantations and Lake Duluti.

    Depending on your arrival time, you will be joined by your A&K Young Explorers Guide, specially chosen for his or her rapport with young guests.

    Overnight: Lake Duluti Serena Hotel or similar
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Lake Manyara National Park

    Begin your exploration with a travel overland to Lake Manyara National Park, a wildlife paradise. Embark on a thrilling morning game drive, with a chance to view hippos wallowing along the shore, pelicans scanning the surface, monkeys leaping between the trees and giraffes under the shade of a 1,000-year-old baobab.

    Proceed to your lodge, atop an escarpment overlooking the vast expanse of the Great Rift Valley.

    This afternoon, set off for an intriguing market visit to Mto wa Mbu. Later, return to your lodge for an exciting Chef’s Table experience in the lodge’s kitchen, where the chef walks you through the preparation and sampling of local dishes, such as ugaali and kachumbari.

    Enjoy time to unwind and refresh, followed by a special welcome dinner with the fellow guests.

    Overnight: Lake Manyara Serena Safari Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Ngorongoro Crater

    This day, take a drive across the Tanzanian border to visit Ngorongoro Crater, a UNESCO World Heritage site, an extinct volcano, now a bucolic sanctuary supporting a wide array of wildlife.

    En-route enjoy organic lunch at the Gibb’s Farm. Further, stroll through the lush grounds or hike to the nearby waterfalls.

    Later, proceed to your lodge perched on the rim of a collapsed, once-active volcano. Enjoy the enchanting scenic beauty of the 14-mile-wide caldera, home to the world’s highest-density of lions, also a haven for elephants, zebras, gazelles and hyenas.

    Overnight: Ngorongoro Serena Safari Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Ngorongoro Crater

    Today, explore the short grass prairies of Ngorongoro Crater, a haven for elephants, wildebeests and gazelles. Watch for flocks of pink flamingos at the Crater’s Lake, as well as other winged wonders soaring above the tree tops.

    Enjoy a picnic lunch before you set off for a visit to Maasai village. Here, you get an opportunity to know in-depth the tribe’s traditional, nomadic lifestyle and coexistence with wildlife as you interact with a village elder. Later, you may observe a demonstration of traditional bead work to appreciate the artistic work of local artisans.

    Overnight: Ngorongoro Serena Safari Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Serengeti National Park

    Drive to Serengeti National Park, recently proclaimed a 7th world wide wonder. During the journey take a tour of the Olduvai Gorge, home to many significant archaeological discoveries.

    Later, settle in your comfortable lodge, set high on a hill with stunning views of the plains and arranged in series of rondavels (round huts).

    Later, take a game drive through the rambling park, site of the Great Migration. Here you may witness wildebeests and zebras in abundance, searching for fresh grass during the migration periods.

    Overnight: Serengeti Serena Safari Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6-7

    Serengeti National Park

    The next two days are for you to explore the arid, wildly beautiful Serengeti plains.

    Rise early for a first-light game drive across the atmospheric park to observe the animals in their natural habitat undertaking their morning regime. You may sight hippos returning to water after miles spent foraging for food overnight, elephants rustling through trees in search of breakfast, and sated lions, arriving back from a hunt with wide, yellow eyes.

    This afternoon, embark on a game drive in search for elusive cheetahs, crocodiles and the striking leopard. Unwind at dusk, as your guide serves you with Sundowner cocktails right on the Serengeti plains.

    Celebrate your last night here with your fellow guests at the gala farewell dinner.

    Overnight: Serengeti Serena Safari Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    Depart Arusha

    This day, depart for Arusha and proceed to relax in your dayroom before boarding your onward flight.

    Overnight: Lake Duluti Serena Hotel (dayroom) or similar
    Meal plan: B

  • Dates & Prices

TANZANIA: ON SAFARI FROM THE SEA TO SERENGETI

Programme Overview

Recall bygone Africa while staying in luxurious tented camps, sitting down to a barbecue lunch and Maasai dance performance in Ngorongoro Crater and exploring beach-fringed Saadani National Park.

  • Duration: 9 days/ 8 nights

    Highlights: Sadani National Park | Ngorongoro Crater | Serengeti National Park

    A&K Advantages

    • Barbeque Lunch at the Ngorongoro Crater with an exclusive Maasai dance performance
    • Visit to Saadani National Park, the only national park in East Africa to offer both safari and the beach
    • Stay in luxurious tented camps in ideal locations
    • A private guided visit to Olduvai Gorge
    • Chef's Table experience of the local cuisine

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE DAR ES SALAAM, TANZANIA

    Arrive in Tanzania, where you will be welcomed by an A&K representative to assist you with transfer to your luxurious hotel.

    Overnight: The Dares Salaam Serena Hotel
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    SAADANI NATIONAL PARK

    This morning, take a short flight from Dar es Salaam to Saadani National Park, the only wildlife sanctuary in Tanzania that borders the sea.

    Begin your stay with a tour of Saadani Village, once, centre of the slave trade, and today a small, quaint fishing village.

    Enjoy a private barbecue lunch on the beach before checking in to your riverside lodge.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Saadani River Lodge
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    SAADANI NATIONAL PARK

    In the morning, set out for a day full of game viewing, featuring safaris by foot, vehicle and riverboat that introduces you to the entire range of Saadani’s wildlife.

    Discover plains game, as well as flamingos, hippos and river-prowling crocodiles; the park is also a birdwatcher’s paradise. Top off a full day of game viewing in a secluded locale with a sundowner before returning to camp.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Saadani River Lodge
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    NGORONGORO CRATER

    After a scrumptious breakfast at the lodge, fly to Lake Manyara National Park for a morning safari.

    Take a brief stop for a toothsome lunch, after which you proceed to Ngorongoro Crater, where a fascinating microcosm of East African wildlife resides, including buffalo, flamingos, lions and leopards.

    In the evening, arrive at your mobile tented camp, perfectly positioned at the rim of the crater, featuring luxury accommodations adorned with mosquito nets, hand woven rugs, crisp linens and en suite bathrooms with flush toilets and hot showers.

    Relax in your veranda and admire the panoramic natural wonderland below.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Ngorongoro Crater Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    NGORONGORO CRATER

    Descend into the Ngorongoro Crater, a vast expanse where animal activity is abundant. Enjoy a secluded hot gourmet lunch as the symphony of wildlife sound envelops you.

    This afternoon, visit with an elder at a Maasai village, where you learn about the tribe’s traditional, nomadic lifestyle and coexistence with wildlife. Also, partake in an artisan visit with a demonstration in traditional bead work and crafts.

    This evening, revel in a private dance performance exclusively for the A&K Guests followed by traditional lip smacking delicacies.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Ngorongoro Crater Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    SERENGETI NATIONAL PARK

    This morning, drive to the fabled Serengeti National Park, stopping in between for a guided visit of Olduvai Gorge. Once settled at camp, set out on the boundless Serengeti, one of the oldest and least changed ecosystems on earth.

    It is also home to the largest concentrations of wildlife in the world, with lions, hyenas and leopards making appearances. The region also hosts the spectacular Great Migration, when a flurry of wildebeests and zebras arrive in search of grass and water.

    Witness the sun set in the evening enjoying your drinks complemented with a traditional African dinner.

    Overnight: Serengeti Migration Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    SERENGETI NATIONAL PARK

    Venture off on morning and afternoon game drives in the protected Serengeti refuge, where flowing rivers support a variety of birds, mammals and reptiles, including the “Big Five” (lions, elephants, buffalo, leopards and rhinos).

    Enjoy a Chef’s Table experience where your camp chef shows you how to prepare several local dishes.

    Later, in the afternoon, you may take an optional balloon flight over the Serengeti plains — a stunning way to experience East Africa at its best.

    Overnight: Serengeti Migration Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    SERENGETI NATIONAL PARK

    The day is at leisure.

    Overnight: Serengeti Migration Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 9

    DEPART ARUSHA

    After an exquisite breakfast, you will be assisted with your transfer to the airport for your flight back home.

    Overnight: Lake Duluti Serena Hotel (dayroom)
    Meal plan: B

The Great Migration Safari In Style: Under Canvas Edition

Programme Overview

Discover East Africa's prolific wildlife on game drives through the region's most stunning parks and reserves, and staying under canvas in luxurious permanent tented camps and private mobile tented sites

  • Duration: 14 Days/ 13 Nights

    Highlights: Nairobi | Amboseli National Park | Tarangire National Park | Ngorongoro Crater | Serengeti National Park | Masai Mara Game Reserve

    A&K Advantages

    • Step out into the wilds of Africa from your own luxury tent on an Under Canvas departure
    • Experience six nights in the heart of the Great Migration - three in the Serengeti and three in Masai Mara
    • Discover Africa's astounding diversity with A&K's expert driver-guides in our own custom-made vehicles
    • Enrich your journey with a private visit to Amboseli Elephant Research Project, as well as a meeting with a Maasai elder to learn about local culture and traditions

  • Day 1

    Arrive Nairobi

    Upon arrival in Nairobi, you are met by an A&K representative and assisted with transfer to your luxurious hotel.

    Overnight: Fairmont The Norfolk
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Amboseli National Park

    Begin your exploration with an overland journey to Kenya’s Amboseli National Park at the foot of snow-capped Mount Kilimanjaro, Africa’s highest mountain and home of the nomadic, vibrantly dressed Maasai tribe.

    The elephant population is prolific here. Amid acacia woodland, scrub brush, freshwater swamps and open plains, watch for buffalo, hippos and waterfowl.

    Tonight, gather with fellow guests for a welcome dinner celebrating the start of your safari adventure.

    Overnight: Tortilis Camp
    Meal plan: B, ,L, D

  • Day 3

    Amboseli National Park

    This day, enjoy the opportunity to closely observe the lions, elephants, leopards, cheetahs and a prolific bird population with game drives.

    Savor lunch and then proceed to the Amboseli Elephant Research Project. Here, learn about elephant family structure, life cycle and behavior.

    Overnight: Tortilis Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Tarangire National Park

    Today, arrive at the Tarangire National Park, crossing the Tanzanian border. Take in the views of this wild wooded savanna with open grasslands and acacia trees.

    Fed and nurtured by the Tarangire River, The Tarangire National Park, is inhabited by an abundant diversity, including majestic lions, elephants and buffalo. Its dusty red landscape also hosts a large bird and lesser game population.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Swala
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Tarangire National Park

    Spend a full day exploring Tarangire National Park amidst the spectacular, rust-hued Great Rift Valley.

    At Tarangire, lookout for hartebeests, gazelles and exotic birds like the bateleur and martial eagle. Catch sight of pythons and the twitching tails of leopards, hiding high above in sausage trees.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Swala
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    Ngorongoro Crater

    This day, travel though the Great Rift Valley. En-route, stroll through the markets in Mto wa Mbu, where stalls brim with vibrant local produce. Further, drive across the Tanzanian border to the Ngorongoro Crater, a UNESCO World Heritage site, an extinct volcano, now a bucolic sanctuary supporting a wide array of wildlife. Climb the outer wall, passing through dense vegetation until a breathtaking view across the crater floor appears.

    Proceed to settle in your exclusive, private mobile camp, set on the rim of the crater and featuring luxury facilities.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Ngorongoro Crater Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Ngorongoro Crater

    Set out on a full-day game drive to explore the panoramic, short-grass prairies along the crater floor, home to rare black rhinos as well as lions, hyenas and gazelles. Observe the flamboyantly dressed Maasai herdsmen, who call the stunning landscape home.

    Next, set off for a visit to Maasai village. Here, you get the opportunity to know in-depth the tribe’s traditional, nomadic lifestyle and coexistence with wildlife by interacting with a village elder. Further, you may observe a demonstration of traditional bead work to appreciate the artistic work of local artisans.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Ngorongoro Crater Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8-10

    Serengeti National Park

    Start the day with a scenic drive to the wildly beautiful Serengeti National Park. Stop en-route for a guided visit of Olduvai Gorge, home to many significant archaeological discoveries.

    The vast Tanzanian landscape is home to the largest concentrations of wildlife in the world, including lions, hyenas and leopards, the region also hosts the spectacular Great Migration, when a flurry of wildebeests and zebras arrive in search of grass and water.

    Over the next two days, explore the protected Serengeti refuge, where flowing rivers support a variety of birds, mammals and reptiles. In addition to the “Big Five” (lions, elephants, buffalo, leopards and rhinos), you may also encounter the lesser-known “Little Five”— ant lions, rhino beetles, leopard tortoises, buffalo weavers and elephant shrews. Actively capture the glimpses of cheetahs, while exploring the landscape.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Kusini
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 11

    Masai Mara Game Reserve

    This day, board a flight to Masai Mara, an outstanding game reserve. After arrival, settle in exclusive mobile tented camp.

    In the late afternoon, embark on a game drive to sight crocodiles basking in the sun; hippos wallowing in muddy water; and monkeys, busy sparking mischief overhead.

    Later, gather for cocktails followed by an elegant dinner.

    Overnight: Exclusive A&K Masai Mara Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 12-13

    Masai Mara Game Reserve

    For the next two days, explore the vast plains of Masai Mara, 583 square miles of rambling, acacia-dotted savanna, woodlands and tree-fringed rivers with game drives.

    Masai Mara showcases a large diversity of ecosystem, including a vast mammal population, lions, elephants, giraffes, a variety of gazelle species and zebras. Cheetahs, rhinos and leopards are also sighted often. Game viewing is never dull in the Mara, especially when the dramatic, annual Great Migration unfolds. Amid stunning scenery and inspiring wildlife, sight Maasai tribesmen traverse the plains or herd goats and cattle.

    Overnight: Exclusive A&K Masai Mara Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 14

    Depart Nairobi

    Today, travel back to Nairobi. After arrival, you will be escorted to your hotel for the day.

    Later, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Overnight: Fairmont The Norfolk (dayroom)
    Meal plan: B

  • Victoria Falls Post-Tour Extension

  • Day 1

    Nairobi

    Rest as you spend the night in Nairobi.

    Overnight: Fairmont The Norfolk
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Victoria Falls

    Begin the day with a morning flight to Livingstone, Zambia. After arrival, you will be met by an A&K representative, who will assist with transfer to your accommodations at Sanctuary Sussi & Chuma. Settle in your luxury tree house at the shores of the Zambezi River.

    Spend the afternoon at leisure; perhaps enjoy the many activities on offer, including game drives in Mosi-Oa-Tunya National Park, Livingstone city tours, nature walks and a sunset boat cruise on the lively river.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Sussi & Chuma
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Victoria Falls

    Today, take a guided walking tour on both sides of Victoria Falls to explore the falls, dropping at a massive 1.2-mile-wide curtain of water over the edge of the falls, with plumes of mist, rainbows and a thrilling roar.

    Begin in Zambia for all-encompassing views and then tour the lush, mist-drenched Zimbabwe side. Return to your lodge and continue enjoying the activities there.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Sussi & Chuma
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Depart Zambia

    After breakfast, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your journey home via Johannesburg.

    Meal plan: B
