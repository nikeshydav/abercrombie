Egypt: Love on the Nile (2012)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 Nights

    Relive the romance of Cleopatra and Mark Anthony's era on an adventure to unforgettable, eternally beguiling Egypt. The pharaohs of a bygone time were considered god-kings, and you too will feel like royalty as you cruise down the Nile on a tranquil four-night cruise on board a privately chartered dahabieh (traditional Egyptian sailboat). Throughout the journey, discover Egypt's most evocative and enduring monuments, including the Pyramids of Giza, the Temple of Kom Ombo and the Valley of the Kings and Queens.

  • Day 1

    India/Cairo, Egypt

    Board flight to Cairo, where the history and romance of ancient Egypt still lives. Upon arrival, you are met by an Abercrombie & Kent representative and transferred to your hotel, where you are assisted with check in. The remainder of your day is at leisure.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Cairo at the First Residence
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    Cairo

    This morning, visit the world-famous Egyptian Museum of Antiquities, home to the relics of Egypt's glorious, bygone era. The pink-granite building was constructed in the 19th century, and original Pharaonic statues and monuments pepper the lush gardens on museum grounds. Tour this impressive museum, home to relics dating back to 4000 BC, among them the treasures of Tutankhamen and the Mummy Room. After lunch, begin your tour of the breathtaking pyramids. The Pyramid of Cheops, the largest of the three, was built around 2690 BC and is one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Marvel at the mysterious Sphinx, 190 feet long and 65 feet high, carved out of a single piece of rock. This evening, enjoy a private dinner on board a felucca (traditional sailboat), listening to the soft sounds of the oud (Egyptian lute) as you glide along the magical Nile.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Cairo at the First Residence
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Cairo/Abu Simbel/Aswan

    Early this morning, fly south to Aswan and then board a short flight to Abu Simbel. Abu Simbel is home to the magnificent temple of Ramses II, with its four 60-foot-high statues of the pharaoh. After exploring this stunning temple, fly back to Aswan and continue overland to your private sailing vessel, the 'Zein Nile Chateau.' With its airy white sails and elegant interiors, this dahabieh (traditional Egyptian sailboat) is the perfect setting for a romantic getaway. Enjoy a lovely lunch on board as you set sail toward Aswan's High Dam, from which you can gaze down the huge lake created to control the floodwaters of the Nile. Continue on your gentle cruise to the exquisite Temple of Philae on the island of Agilika. End the afternoon at the granite quarries, which supplied the ancient Egyptians with most of the hard stone used in pyramids and temples. Here you will find the 1,500-ton Unfinished Obelisk, a glorious example of the technique used by the ancient Egyptians in producing these enormous monoliths.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Zein Nile Chateau
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Aswan/Kom Ombo/Edfu

    Enjoy a leisurely breakfast on board your dahabieh as you continue cruising the river, en route to Kom Ombo. Disembark and explore the lovely Greco-Roman temple dedicated to Haroeris (the Sun God) and Sobek (the Crocodile God). The temple, built in the third century BC, stands at a bend in the Nile where, in ancient times, sacred crocodiles basked in the sun on the riverbank. Afterward, return to your vessel for lunch in the shade as the 'Zein Nile Chateau' sets sail for Edfu. Arrive in the early evening at the well-preserved Temple of Horus (the Falcon God), built in 230 BC by Ptolemy III.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Zein Nile Chateau
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Edfu/Esna/Luxor

    Today, travel to Esna, home to the Greco-Roman Temple of Khnum. The beautifully preserved Great Hypostyle Hall was built during the reign of the Roman Emperor Claudius. The walls of the temple are covered with inscriptions, most of which date back to the Roman period. After contemplating this architectural marvel. Return to your vessel to enjoy another romantic lunch. The balance of your afternoon is at leisure to sit back and enjoy the breathtaking scenery passing by as the 'Zein Nile Chateau' makes her way along the Nile toward Luxor. You also catch a glimpse into daily life on the river's banks, which in many areas has remained almost unchanged for centuries.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Zein Nile Chateau
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Luxor

    This morning, your journey takes you to the famous Valley of the Kings and Queens, where over 60 tombs have been discovered. Afterward, pause by the lovely terraced funerary temple of Queen Hatshepsut, Egypt's first female pharaoh, and pass by the fabled Colossi of Memnon. Following lunch on board the 'Zein Nile Chateau,' pay a visit to the awe-inspiring Temple of Karnak, a huge complex where 134 great columns once supported an enormous roof, an extraordinary engineering feat for the 12th century BC. Continue to the strikingly graceful Temple of Luxor, built by Amenhotep III, Tutankhamon, Haremhab and Ramses II during their respective reigns and dedicated to the god Amun. Along the front of the temple, three large statues remain of Ramses II, while the Colonnade of Amenophotep III features 14 towering papyrus-headed columns. Walk hand in hand amid these timeless temples, reflecting on a breathtaking culture and its legacy. Return to your dahabieh in the afternoon for some time at leisure before dinner.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Zein Nile Chateau
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Luxor/Cairo

    This morning, bid farewell to your 'Zein Nile Chateau' crew as you disembark and are transferred to the airport for your flight back to Cairo. The balance of the day is at leisure. You may wish to arrange a private car and driver for further explorations of the city or to complete some last-minute shopping. Tonight, dine privately in the Churchill Gardens of the historic Mena House Oberoi Hotel, where your table overlooks the magnificent pyramids.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Cairo at the First Residence
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 8

    Cairo/India

    After Breakfast, Transfer to the airport for your homebound flight.

    Meal Plan: B

Peru: Mysteries of Machu Picchu (2012)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 days

    Romance, magic and mystery all make the heart beat a little faster, and Peru is the perfect location to experience all three. Entice your sense of taste when you learn to prepare, and then sample, Peruvian specialties with a cooking lesson from an experienced chef. Explore ancient Inca sites, trying to unravel their mysteries as you feel the magic of the impressive ruins and Andean landscapes; and reconnect with each other at fabulous restaurants and hotels that welcome you as if you were on your honeymoon, capping off your time with an emotional vow renewal ceremony in the mist-shrouded citadel of Machu Picchu.

  • Day 1

    India/Lima, Peru

    Arrive in Lima, a city with remnants of colonial splendor and teeming vitality, where you are met and transferred to your hotel. Your romantic holiday begins the moment you check in and discover honeymoon amenities in your deluxe ocean-view suite.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    Lima

    This morning, meet a Lima chef and visit a local market to shop for ingredients for your cooking class. Leave the groceries in the chef's house and take a walk around the Barranco area, known for its mansions, the Bridge of Sighs and the Dedalo Art Gallery. As you stroll, your mouth waters at the thought of the delicious lunch of ceviche and tapas that you and the chef are about to prepare. When you return home, you can recreate the tempting dishes in your own kitchen and relive memories of your romantic journey. After lunch, embark on a city tour that visits the Plaza Mayor, the heart of the city and original site on which Lima was founded. Continue to the historic Cathedral, passing by the Presidential Palace and the Church of San Francisco with its library of fine paintings and frescoes. When you are ready to settle in for the night, have your private bath butler draw a steamy bath for two - an ideal way to soothe tired muscles and share memories of your exciting day.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Lima/Cusco/Urubamba

    Board a morning flight to Cusco, an Andean community and former capital of the Inca Empire. On arrival, you are transferred to Urubamba, the Sacred Valley of the Incas, and your hotel. The rest of the day is at leisure to adjust to the altitude. This evening, sit down to a special romantic dinner in the hotel and anticipate the amazing sights you are about to see over the next few days.

    Overnight: Hotel Rio Sagrado
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 4

    Urubamba/Chincheros/Urubamba

    Chincheros is a town of Inca origin, and its colorful weekly fair draws country folk from throughout the valley to exchange goods. Wander the market today and see what treasures you may find as souvenirs. Afterward, meet Nilda Callañaupa Alvarez, a native of Chinchero. She learned to weave as a child while tending the flocks in the fields of her village and now regularly travels to the U.S. to organize weaving exhibits and workshops. View some of her work and take time to shop before you have lunch at Huayoccari Hacienda, home to an interesting collection of Colonial and folk art. After lunch, continue to the massive fortress of Ollantaytambo, a formidable stone structure that climbs massive terraces to the top of a high peak. Below the fortress lies a complete Incan town, also called Ollantaytambo, still inhabited and with its original architecture and layout preserved.

    Overnight: Hotel Rio Sagrado
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 5

    Urubamba/Machu Picchu

    Board the deluxe Hiram Bingham train, the most luxurious way to travel the breathtaking landscape to Machu Picchu. Step on board, and you are immersed in an elegant world of polished wood, gleaming cutlery and glittering glass that evokes a romantic ambience. After a light lunch on board, arrive at the breathtaking Incan citadel and begin exploring the ruins in the company of your expert guide. Feel the pulse of this magical place as you visualize the lives of the priests, craftsmen, and servants who inhabited this seemingly uninhabitable citadel and then left, leaving no written records about its destruction. After a busy day of sightseeing, return to your beautiful mountainside retreat, high in the lush Andean clouds forest. It features the look and feel of a rural Andean village set in spectacular grounds with paths that wind past waterfalls and gardens, and trails that wander through wild forest. Snuggle under a thick alpaca blanket in your suite, which offers incredible views of the exotic gardens or the dramatic Vilcanota River.

    Overnight: Inkaterra Machu Picchu Pueblo Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Machu Picchu/Cusco

    Return to Machu Picchu with your guide this morning for a final look at the ruins - and for an even more meaningful event for you. A shaman joins you to perform a vow renewal ceremony for you. Stand before the spirits of the Incas and reaffirm your love in this magical place. Afterward, return to the hotel, renewed and refreshed, for lunch. This afternoon, return to Cusco by Vista dome train and settle in at your hotel, where you receive VIP amenities.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Cusco

    A morning city tour takes you to the Cathedral on the Plaza de Armas and the Santo Domingo Temple, a Dominican church and convent built on the foundations of the Koricancha (place of gold), where the Incas worshipped the sun. Continue just outside Cusco to the ruins of Sacsayhuaman, a testimonial to the Incan architectural skills. Try to slip a piece of paper between the cracks of the massive stone blocks, cut and fitted together with incredible precision without a single drop of mortar to hold them in place. Your afternoon is at leisure. Make sure to visit the hotel spa for a relaxing couple's massage. This evening, enjoy a romantic dinner at Limo restaurant, overlooking the main plaza.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 8

    Cusco/Lima

    Fly to Lima and transfer to your hotel. The remainder of the day is at leisure to do some last-minute shopping, take a walk along the ocean or enjoy the views from your suite.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Lima/India

    Transfer to the airport &Board your international departure flight.

    Overnight: Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner
    Meal Plan: B,L,D
