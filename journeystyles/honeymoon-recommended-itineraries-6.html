South America Honeymoon

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 16 Days

    Highlights:
    Brazil - Rio de Janeiro Trip Highlights:

    • Visit the most beautiful postcard of Rio de Janeiro: Christ the Redeemer statue in the mountain of Corcovado located at 710 meters.

    • A trip through the Tijuca Forest, the largest urban forest in the world, up to the statue.

    • Night tour followed by Barbecue Restaurant dinner and Samba Show.

    Argentina Iguazu Falls Trip Highlights:

    • The thrilling Helicopter journey above the jungle canopy and the breathtaking 275 cascades spread across a gulf of nearly two miles plus the Itaipu dam and the meeting point of the 3 countries (Argentina, Brazil & Paraguay).

    • Full Day visit to the Argentinean side of the falls.

    • Lower Trail:
    A walk along 1600 meters of catwalks detached from the surface of the jungle's soil for its protection, provides a view from below and around the falls.

    • Devils Gorge:
    Departing from Cataratas Station, the train will take you to Garganta Station in about 18 minutes. The stroll along the catwalks is 1200 meters long, across the islands of the delta, leading to the main balcony on the most spectacular fall.

    • Arts tour of Buenos Aires (ARGENTINE AND LATIN AMERICAN ART).

    • Evening, enjoy Dinner and Tango Show - Tango, a dance known as passionate, sensual and very elegant, was all the rage in the 1930's in Buenos Aries.

    Peru Trip Highlights:

    • Board the luxurious Hiram Bingham train, and your picturesque journey bound for Machu Picchu- the Lost City of the Incas, one of the new seven wonders of the world.

  • Day 01

    Arrive Rio de Janeiro by flight

    Upon arrival and after clearing immigrations and customs you will be transferred to your hotel to be transferred to hotel.


    Overnight stay at the hotel.

    Overnight: Copacabana Palace

  • Day 02

    Rio de Janeiro

    Morning after breakfast, enjoy half day Sugar Loaf Mountains tour.
    Duration :4 hrs

    This tour begins with a city panoramic view through downtown Rio, passing by the Sambodrome, the Metropolitan Cathedral, in pyramid form, with internal diameter of 96 meters and height of 80 meters. Get to know the traditional Cinelandia square with the historical buildings, the Municipal Theater, the National Library and the National Museum of Fine Arts. By taking the Aterro do Flamengo, you will be taken to the neighborhood of Urca where it is located one of the largest symbols of the city: the Sugar Loaf. This visit is made in two steps by cable car. The first cable car takes passengers to Urca Hill which is 215 meters high and from where one can have a beautiful view from the Guanabara Bay and its islands, the Rio-Niteroi bridge and Corcovado hill. From this stage, a second cable car will be taken up to the top with 395 meters to find a splendid view from Copacabana beach, the Santa Cruz fortress and some beaches of Niteroi.


    Afternoon free on your own.

    Evening back to the hotel and overnight stay.

    Overnight: Copacabana Palace

  • Day 03

    Rio de Janeiro

    Morning after breakfast at the hotel, enjoy half day tour of Corcovado.
    Duration :4 hrs

    From any place of the city, the visitor can see the most beautiful postcard of Rio de Janeiro: Christ the Redeemer statue in the mountain of Corcovado located at 710 meters above the sea level and inaugurated in 1931. This tour begins in Cosme Velho's neighborhood from where the cog train starts a trip through the Tijuca Forest, the largest urban forest in the world, up to the statue. Once there, a panoramic and extraordinary view of Rio will be seeing including the Sugar Loaf, the Guanabara Bay, the beaches of Southern Zone and the Rodrigo de Freitas lagoon. After the visit of the Corcovado, the tour will continue to Sao Conrado area with a brief stop at the Pepino beach from where you can appreciate the flight of the hang gliding.


    Evening, meet your local guide to enjoy depart for the Rio By Night tour followed by Barbecue Restaurant dinner and Samba Show


    Duration :05 hrs

    Night in Rio is a meeting point and makes the heart beat faster. Music and joy key ingredients to a good night life, go together with a permanent parade of artists in the show houses. A delicious dinner in the typical Barbecue House will be served. After dinner passengers are taken to Plataforma 1 where a show of Samba music with native girls" mulatas will be presented. You will fell the energy so many times shown in Carnival, is an enchantment that touches all, indistinctly.


    Overnight stay at the hotel.


    Overnight: Copacabana Palace

  • Day 04

    Rio de Janeiro

    Morning after breakfast, full day is free on your own.


    Overnight stay at the hotel.

    Overnight: Copacabana Palace

  • Day 05

    Rio de Janeiro / Iguazu Falls by flight

    Morning after breakfast, check out from the hotel. Departure transfer to airport for your flight to Iguassu Falls. Met upon arrival and transfer on private service with guide assistance to Foz do Iguacu National Park to visit the Brazilian side of the falls.


    Welcome to the Iguazu Falls!

    The falls are one of the most impressive sights in the world. Taller than Niagara Falls and twice as wide, Iguazuhas 275 cascades spread across a two-mile left along the path of the River Iguazu, on the border between Brazil and Argentina. Viewpoints from both sides offer different perspectives on a unique geographic spectacle.


    En-route half day visit to the Brazilian side of the falls, including helicopter flight over the falls

    Today explore the side of the falls which offers some spectacular panoramic views. Your guide will lead you on a walking tour through the National Park of Iguazu to a cat-walk, an arm's length from the falls. You may like to climb a 115 ft.-wooden staircase to observe the falls from the top.

    Located in the lush Iguazu National Park - a vast forest made up of tropical wood species such as Brazilian walnut, cedar and peroba - the area is home to a wide array of rare birds such as the macaw, toucan, parrot, as well as monkeys, crocodiles, armadillos and jaguars. The falls are one of the most impressive sights in the world, with a breathtaking 275 cascades spread across a gulf of nearly two miles.

    A helicopter trip is the best way to appreciate the majesty of Iguacu, and is included in your visit to the Brazilian side on sharing basis. The thrilling journey above the jungle canopy and the breathtaking 275 cascades spread across a gulf of nearly two miles plus the Itaipu dam and the meeting point of the 3 countries (Argentina, Brazil & Paraguay). The flight lasts for 35 minutes and guarantees any visitor an experience that will stay in their memory forever.

    After the tour you would be transferred back to hotel at around 1700hrs for check-in.


    Balance of the day is at leisure.


    Lunch & Dinner will be under your own arrangements. Ask your guide for suggestions.

    Overnight: Das Cataratas

  • Day 06

    Iguazu

    Breakfast at hotel.

    Enjoy Full Day visit to the Argentinean side of the falls.

    Pick-up 0900hrs.

    This side of park was created in 1934 and covers some 50,000 hectares, offering more “contact with nature”, since one is can get within a few steps of the thundering cascades. In order to feel the proximity of the falls and observe the beauty of its vegetation, birds and butterflies, it is essential to walk along the footbridges and roads in the Argentinean side of the Park.

    Waterproof clothing is necessary.Arriving at the New Visitors Center, you can access the Nature Interpretation Center of Iguazu National Park, a building which also provides areas for cultural, educational and social activities; then you will find the starting point for an amazing tour including:

    Devils Gorge: Departing from Cataratas Station, the train will take you to Garganta Station in about 18 minutes. The stroll along the catwalks is 1200 meters long, across the islands of the delta, leading to the main balcony on the most spectacular fall, the Devil's Gorge. Time required: two (2) hours. - Difficulty: low, no stairs.

    Upper Trail: The magnificent panoramic view of the falls from the Upper IguazuRiver. Time required: 1.15 hrs. - Difficulty: low, no stairs.

    Lower Trail: A walk along 1600 meters of catwalks detached from the surface of the jungle's soil for its protection, provides a view from below and around the falls. A unique experience of being and feeling part of the natural environment. Time required: two (2) hours. - Difficulty: moderate, with stairs

    After visit you will be transferred back to your hotel with estimated arrival time 1700hrs.

    Balance of the day is at leisure.

    Overnight: Das Cataratas

  • Day 07

    Iguacu Falls / Buenos Aires by flight

    Morning after breakfast, check out. You will visit Argentinean side of the falls on private basis with guide assistance. Later in the afternoon, departure transfer to airport for your flight to Buenos Aires. Upon arrival and after clearing immigrations and customs you will be met a local guide and transferred to the hotel.

    Overnight stay at the hotel.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Buenos Aires

  • Day 08

    Buenos Aires

    Morning after breakfast at hotel, enjoy City tour


    Duration: 6 hours (time for lunch included)


    A different and original approach to discover the city‟s most beautiful places, bars and avenues, to live her cultural life and to see her rich history come alive. A tour covering the outstanding points of interest: “9 de Julio” Avenue; Colon Theatre (Opera House); San Telmo neighbourhood, birthplace of the city; May Avenue and Square, historical and political centre; La Boca district, famous for “Caminito” street; Recoleta district, known for its cemetery and Palermo, wide and beautiful park located in the heart of one of the most elegant areas in the city.

    Overnight stay at the hotel.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Buenos Aires

  • Day 9

    Buenos Aires

    Morning after breakfast, meet the local guide at hotel lobby for Arts tour of Buenos Aires (ARGENTINE AND LATIN AMERICAN ART).

    This tour will allow you to learn about the main tendencies in Argentine and Latin American art, visiting tree of the most exciting museums in town: the National Museum of Fine Arts, the Latin American Arts Museum and the Quinquela Martín Museum.

    The National Museum of Fine Arts (MNBA) houses a vast collection of 11,000 pieces, between canvases, sculptures, tapestry, engravings, drawings and objects, and possesses a small but precious European art collection, with works by Corot, Manet, Boudin, Cézanne, Renoir, Toulouse-Lautrec and Rodin. But this time we‟ll pay special attention to the Argentine art section, which houses pieces by Morel, Pueyrredon, Lopez, Sívori, De la Cárcova, Fader and Quiroz, among others.

    The modern, state-of-the-art Museum of Latin American Art in Buenos Aires (MALBA) was built by Cordoba based architects Gaston Atelman, Martin Fourcade and Alfredo Tapia. MALBA is a space devoted to the collection, conservation, study and divulgation of Latin American art from the early 20th century to the present. Some of the artists in permanent exhibition are Frida Kahlo, Diego Rivera, Antonio Berni, Pedro Figari, Xul Solar, Guillermo Kuitca, among others.

    Also, in the green corridor between the MNBA and the MALBA, we‟ll be able to see many statues and monuments that grace our parks. Near the MALBA we can find the impressive Floralis Generica, a mechanical flower built in aluminum and steel, designed by Argentine architect Eduardo Catalano, which imitates the life cycle of a true flower, opening during the day and closing by night.

    Ending our visit, we travel to the working-class port district of La Boca, where we visit the Benito Quinquela Martín Museum. Donated by the artist himself in 1933, this museum hosts a great part of his work, which depicts the life of the neighborhood and its inhabitants.

    Approximate duration: 5 hours.

    Included services:           Bilingual guide, specializing in art.

    Transportation from and to hotel in a top of the line vehicle.

    Uniformed driver.

    Entry tickets to museums and guided visits.

    Brochure with extended information of the tour and Buenos Aires postcard.

    Note: This tour can only be programmed for the afternoon.

    Evening,enjoy Dinner and Tango Show at Esquina Carlos Gardel

    Buenos Aires is the birthplace of Tango, born in the brothels of the city, just before the beginning of the 1880s century. The word' Tango" refers to both a musical style and the dance performed to it. Tango, a dance known as passionate, sensual and very elegant, was all the rage in the 1930's in Buenos Aries and Paris. Tango is not only a dance but also an obsession. For the ´tanguero´, it is as much as a part of life as eating and sleeping. Erotic and passionate, haunting and melancholic, it involves not only the body but also the soul. Yes, it is just feeling - but in one dance, you can express sad, happy, angry, relax, deep, shallow, cheerful, sorrow, painful, serious, intellectual or just playful feelings. Tango has captured the popular imagination for over one hundred years to become no less than an international cult.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Buenos Airese

  • Day 10

    Buenos Aires – Liman

    Morning after breakfast, check out from the hotel. Departure transfer to airport for your flight to to Lima. On arrival at Lima International Airport, you will be met and transferred to your hotel.

    Lima, the Capital of Peru was founded on 18th January 1535, by Spanish Conquistador Francisco Pizarro, and quickly became known as the City of the Kings. Within 70 years it was the most important city in the Spanish-American empire, and was the focal point for European trade and cultural activity in the region.

    It is a city of rare fascination and unexpected pleasures. Lima has decaying colonial splendor and the teeming vitality of an oriental bazaar; melancholy cloudy winters and warm breezy summers; impoverished urban sprawl and quiet elegant corners among ancient buildings where the night air is scented with jasmine.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel

  • Day 11

    Lima

    Breakfast at the hotel. This morning is at your leisure.

    Lunch on your own

    This afternoon you will embark on a city tour beginning with a visit to the Plaza Mayor, official center of the city and the original site upon which Lima was founded. You will visit the historic Cathedral an architectural masterpiece. From the Cathedral, you will walk past the Presidential Palace where the soldiers guarding the palace offer an excellent photo opportunity, and on to the Church of San Francisco and its library and collection of fine paintings and frescoes.

    After your trip to the historic center, you will be driven through modern Lima to visit the residential districts of Miraflores and San Isidro.

    Dinner on your own

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel

  • Day 12

    Lima - Cusco

    Breakfast at your hotel

    This morning you will be transferred to Lima Airport for your flight to Cusco.

    Upon arrival in Cusco, Andean community and former ancient capital of the Inca Empire, you will be met and transferred to your hotel.

    This afternoon enjoy acity tour which includes the Cathedral on the Plaza de Armas and Santo Domingo, a Dominican church and convent built upon the foundations of the Koricancha, or the „place of gold‟, the Incas‟ principal religious building, dedicated to the worship of the sun.

    Just outside of Cusco your visit this afternoon takes you to the ruins of Sacsayhuaman, a testimonial to Incan architectural skills. Compiled of massive stones (one weighs 125 tons!) you are challenged to try to slip a piece of paper between the cracks of these blocks, cut and fitted together with incredible precision without a single drop of mortar to hold them in place. This ancient fortress, where the greatest battle between the Spanish and Incas took place, overlooks the red-tiled roofs of Cusco and the spectacular lush countryside. Also visit Qenqo (“The Labyrinth”), an Incan shrine dedicated to the Earth Mother; the small fortress of Puka Pukara.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio

  • Day 13

    Cucso / Sacred Valley / Cusco

    Breakfast at your hotel

    This morning depart to the Urubamba Valley, visit the local village of Pisac, known for its famous colorful market. This bustling market attracts tourist from all over the world to try their bartering for local sweaters, ponchos, wall hangings, jewelry and goods.

    Continue next to the Hacienda Huayoccari, the most exclusive and beautiful country manor in the Sacred Valley. You will learn about Pre-Columbian and Colonial Art as you walk through rooms‟ ornate with a fantastic private collection. Lunch will be served at this hacienda.

    After lunch continue to the massive fortress of Ollantaytambo. The fortress, a formidable stone structure that climbs massive terraces reaching the top of a high peak, was the valley‟s main defence against the Antis and was the site of the Incas‟ greatest victory against the Spanish during the wars of conquest. Constructed of rose-colored granite, this huge installation was once a thriving complex of baths, temples and military barracks. Below the fortress lies a complete Incan town, also called Ollantaytambo, still inhabited and with its original architecture and layout preserved.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio

  • Day 14

    Cusco / Poroy/ Machu Picchu

    Breakfast at your hotel

    This morning you are transferred to thetrain station to board the luxurious Hiram Bingham train, begin a picturesque journey bound for Machu Picchu, one of the new seven wonders of the world. During your journey the guide explain points of interest as you settle down to a delicious brunch.

    Your motor coach awaits you at Aguas Calientes station. From here you ascend to Machu Picchu, the Lost City of the Incas. Spend the afternoon exploring the city‟s ruins and feel its pulse as you visualize the lives of the priests, craftsmen and servants who inhabited this awe-inspiring citadel.

    Overnight: Inkaterra Machu Picchu Pueblo Hotel

  • Day 15

    Machu Picchu /Poroy / Cusco

    Breakfast at your hotel

    Today you have the chance to visit the ruins with your private guide again.
    You will then have free time to walk around, climb up the Huaynapicchu Mountain, where one can experience spectacular views of all of Machu Picchu, the valleys and mountains that surround it (please note that there are only 400 visitors allowed per day), or visit the Temple of the Moon or the impressive Inca Bridge.

    Lunch at the Machu Picchu Sanctuary Lodge

    Once back on board the Hiram Bingham, pre dinner cocktails accompanied by live entertainment, set the mood for your sumptuous 4-course a la carte dinner. Arrive back at Poroy station around 10pm where your private vehicle transfers you back to the hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio

  • Day 16

    Cusco/ back Home

    Breakfast at your hotel

    This morning you will be transferred to the airport for your flight back to Lima.Assistance at airport to connect with your international flight.

    • END OF ABERCROMBIE & KENT SERVICES

    BON VOYAGE!

Peru: Mysteries of Machu Picchu (2012)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 days

    Romance, magic and mystery all make the heart beat a little faster, and Peru is the perfect location to experience all three. Entice your sense of taste when you learn to prepare, and then sample, Peruvian specialties with a cooking lesson from an experienced chef. Explore ancient Inca sites, trying to unravel their mysteries as you feel the magic of the impressive ruins and Andean landscapes; and reconnect with each other at fabulous restaurants and hotels that welcome you as if you were on your honeymoon, capping off your time with an emotional vow renewal ceremony in the mist-shrouded citadel of Machu Picchu.

  • Day 1

    India/Lima, Peru

    Arrive in Lima, a city with remnants of colonial splendor and teeming vitality, where you are met and transferred to your hotel. Your romantic holiday begins the moment you check in and discover honeymoon amenities in your deluxe ocean-view suite.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    Lima

    This morning, meet a Lima chef and visit a local market to shop for ingredients for your cooking class. Leave the groceries in the chef's house and take a walk around the Barranco area, known for its mansions, the Bridge of Sighs and the Dedalo Art Gallery. As you stroll, your mouth waters at the thought of the delicious lunch of ceviche and tapas that you and the chef are about to prepare. When you return home, you can recreate the tempting dishes in your own kitchen and relive memories of your romantic journey. After lunch, embark on a city tour that visits the Plaza Mayor, the heart of the city and original site on which Lima was founded. Continue to the historic Cathedral, passing by the Presidential Palace and the Church of San Francisco with its library of fine paintings and frescoes. When you are ready to settle in for the night, have your private bath butler draw a steamy bath for two - an ideal way to soothe tired muscles and share memories of your exciting day.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Lima/Cusco/Urubamba

    Board a morning flight to Cusco, an Andean community and former capital of the Inca Empire. On arrival, you are transferred to Urubamba, the Sacred Valley of the Incas, and your hotel. The rest of the day is at leisure to adjust to the altitude. This evening, sit down to a special romantic dinner in the hotel and anticipate the amazing sights you are about to see over the next few days.

    Overnight: Hotel Rio Sagrado
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 4

    Urubamba/Chincheros/Urubamba

    Chincheros is a town of Inca origin, and its colorful weekly fair draws country folk from throughout the valley to exchange goods. Wander the market today and see what treasures you may find as souvenirs. Afterward, meet Nilda Callañaupa Alvarez, a native of Chinchero. She learned to weave as a child while tending the flocks in the fields of her village and now regularly travels to the U.S. to organize weaving exhibits and workshops. View some of her work and take time to shop before you have lunch at Huayoccari Hacienda, home to an interesting collection of Colonial and folk art. After lunch, continue to the massive fortress of Ollantaytambo, a formidable stone structure that climbs massive terraces to the top of a high peak. Below the fortress lies a complete Incan town, also called Ollantaytambo, still inhabited and with its original architecture and layout preserved.

    Overnight: Hotel Rio Sagrado
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 5

    Urubamba/Machu Picchu

    Board the deluxe Hiram Bingham train, the most luxurious way to travel the breathtaking landscape to Machu Picchu. Step on board, and you are immersed in an elegant world of polished wood, gleaming cutlery and glittering glass that evokes a romantic ambience. After a light lunch on board, arrive at the breathtaking Incan citadel and begin exploring the ruins in the company of your expert guide. Feel the pulse of this magical place as you visualize the lives of the priests, craftsmen, and servants who inhabited this seemingly uninhabitable citadel and then left, leaving no written records about its destruction. After a busy day of sightseeing, return to your beautiful mountainside retreat, high in the lush Andean clouds forest. It features the look and feel of a rural Andean village set in spectacular grounds with paths that wind past waterfalls and gardens, and trails that wander through wild forest. Snuggle under a thick alpaca blanket in your suite, which offers incredible views of the exotic gardens or the dramatic Vilcanota River.

    Overnight: Inkaterra Machu Picchu Pueblo Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Machu Picchu/Cusco

    Return to Machu Picchu with your guide this morning for a final look at the ruins - and for an even more meaningful event for you. A shaman joins you to perform a vow renewal ceremony for you. Stand before the spirits of the Incas and reaffirm your love in this magical place. Afterward, return to the hotel, renewed and refreshed, for lunch. This afternoon, return to Cusco by Vista dome train and settle in at your hotel, where you receive VIP amenities.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Cusco

    A morning city tour takes you to the Cathedral on the Plaza de Armas and the Santo Domingo Temple, a Dominican church and convent built on the foundations of the Koricancha (place of gold), where the Incas worshipped the sun. Continue just outside Cusco to the ruins of Sacsayhuaman, a testimonial to the Incan architectural skills. Try to slip a piece of paper between the cracks of the massive stone blocks, cut and fitted together with incredible precision without a single drop of mortar to hold them in place. Your afternoon is at leisure. Make sure to visit the hotel spa for a relaxing couple's massage. This evening, enjoy a romantic dinner at Limo restaurant, overlooking the main plaza.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 8

    Cusco/Lima

    Fly to Lima and transfer to your hotel. The remainder of the day is at leisure to do some last-minute shopping, take a walk along the ocean or enjoy the views from your suite.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Lima/India

    Transfer to the airport &Board your international departure flight.

    Overnight: Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

Luxury Honeymoon in Kenya

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 days

    Highlights: Celebrate your honeymoon in style with a safari in Africa & relax at the luxury Hotels & lodges i.e. Lewa Safari Camp in Lewa Downs, Sanctuary Olonana in Masai Mara & Kinondo Kwetu in Diani Beach and experience the best game drives safari experience, adventure activities like kite surfing, sailing, scuba diving, horse riding and bush breakfast also enjoy the breeze by the waters of the Indian Ocean.

    • ITINERARY

  • Day 1

    Arrive Nairobi – Fly to Lewa Downs

    On arrival at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport in Nairobi you will be met by an A&K representative and after clearing customs you will be transferred to Wilson Airport about 15 kilometres away for your scheduled flight to Lewa Downs.

    A guide will meet you on arrival and transfer you to the camp. Enjoy a game drive en-route to the camp; you may be rewarded with sightings of lion, elephant and plenty of plains game.

    Lewa Safari Camp comprises twelve luxury tents on Lewa Wildlife Conservancy. Lewa is famed the world over for its community conservation efforts and the fruits of those efforts are visible in the vast quantities of game that live on the 65,000 acre farm. Rarities like rhino and the splay-footed sitatunga antelope are easy to see at Lewa, as are more common elephant, lion and leopard.

    Once at the camp, you will be met by your hosts with a beverage at hand after which you will be escorted to your luxurious tent that consists of a main bedroom, an en-suite bathroom and a private veranda from which you can enjoy views of the landscape and Mt Kenya in the distance.

    After lunch, you have time for a relaxing swim or a nap, followed by your first game drive in a private vehicle booked just for you. Game driving in Lewa is outstanding - Lewa is home to 10 per cent of Kenya’s black rhino population and the single largest population of Grevy’s zebra in the world.

    You will return to camp for a romantic candle lit dinner served by the fireplace in the main lounge.

    Overnight Stay: Lewa Safari Camp

  • Day 2

    Lewa Downs

    Start the second day of your special honeymoon with a wakeup call followed by a private breakfast served on your tent deck. Mornings at Lewa are always special; often you can enjoy spectacular views of a snow-capped Mt Kenya in the distance.

    Today you have the chance to explore Lewa by spending a full day at the conservancy in a variety of ways. You can choose to go on a guided bush walk - there are few better ways to experience nature. Get close to the plains game through an evening horse or camel ride in the picturesque conservancy.

    Lewa Downs are in the heart of the Laikipia region, a broad swathe of lava plateau that reaches the foothills of Mount Kenya on one side and in contrast, the wild deserts of the Northern Frontier District on the other. Outside of the Maasai Mara, Laikipia can boast the greatest wildlife experiences in Kenya.

    Lewa Downs is probably East Africa’s finest conservancy and has been the home of the Craig family since the 1920’s. Delia, the grandmother, was awarded it in a post war colonial land lottery. She had qualified to enter the land hand out through her services as a nurse in the trenches. With a handsome chunk of Kenya to her name, she was soon married to David and they ran the area as a cattle ranch up in to the late 1980’s.

    At this time and with the support of various benefactors, Royal and otherwise, they decided to concentrate on the conservation of game and are now one of the country’s great success stories. Having originally hunted out the rhino themselves, they now have over sixty, both black and white, on their property. They also have a thriving predator population and a plethora of plains game and smaller beasts.

    After a long day in the bush, indulge in a massage at the spa, a wonderful way to ease the fatigue out of tired shoulders and back. Compliment those tanned limbs with a manicure and a pedicure!

    Later enjoy a private romantic dinner served by the pool side, as you watch the sun set over the horizon.

    Overnight Stay: Lewa Safari Camp

  • Day 3

    Lewa Downs

    Today on your last day in Lewa, breakfast will be served in the bush. Take the time to sit back, enjoy the spectacular views, the smells, colours and the gentle breeze. Bush breakfasts are a chance to enjoy a full breakfast in the middle of the plains, surrounded by wildlife

    On your final day you have the chance to interact with your hosts – the Samburu people. Lewa Conservancy borders the Samburu community living in bordering conservancies in the arid lowlands of the north. The Samburu whose name means butterfly are one of the few remaining communities who still practice their traditional culture despite the fast changing world. You have the opportunity to visit a local Samburu village, where you can learn about the culture and also buy hand-crafted beadwork and carvings offered for sale. Remember to bargain!

    As the day draws to a close, your guide will drive you into the bush where a private and secluded vantage point where a sundowner has been prepared. Stop, watch the spectacular sunset as you enjoy the gin and tonic in your hand.

    Later drive back to the camp where a sumptuous dinner will be laid out on your tent verandah.

    Overnight Stay: Lewa Safari Camp

  • Day 4

    Lewa Downs //Masai Mara

    After breakfast this morning, you will be transferred to the airstrip for your scheduled flight to Maasai Mara’s Kichwa Tembo airstrip.

    As you approach the Maasai Mara the beauty of the wilderness and the freshness of air that comes with it are sure to take your breath away. In this vast wilderness, culture mingles easily with the wild and the Mara is home to not only the Maasai people but also the numerous games that include the Big Five.

    A flower girl and your Sanctuary Olonana guide will meet you on arrival and transfer you to the camp. En-route enjoy a first glimpse of the Maasai Mara’s wildlife, you may be rewarded with sightings of elephant, buffalo, giraffe and a host of plain game.

    On arrival at Sanctuary Olonana you will be met by your host with scented towels and a glass of nice welcome juice or iced tea as you are shown to your spacious tent that has floor to ceiling mosquito netting, en suite bathroom, solar lighting and large river view veranda. In your room you find a bottle of sparkling wine, fruits and flowers.

    After lunch there is time for a relaxing swim or a nap, followed by your first afternoon game drive. The Maasai Mara is a fabulous place for seeing big cats and all of the big five, a variety of plains game and over 300 species of birds. The plains offer a rich variety of food for the dominant wildebeest and Topi, eland and buffalo, Thompson’s and Grant’s gazelle. In the Mara River, hippos submerge to snort and grumble while crocodiles sunbathe, mouths agape, on the riverbanks. Around July to October, the Mara plays host to the greatest show on earth – the wildebeest migration.

    You will return to camp for a private candle lit dinner served on your deck under the African stars.

    Overnight Stay: Sanctuary Olonana

  • Day 5

    Maasai Mara

    Start another day of your special safari holiday with a wakeup call and steaming coffee or tea as you watch the rising of the African sun, listen to the sounds of the cascading river before you, the singing of birds and the hippos snorting in the river. Then take in the fresh morning air as you walk to the main camp for breakfast. After a nice sumptuous breakfast it is time for your second extended game drive that will bring you back for lunch.

    Today you can choose to walk with our naturalist and learn about plants, trees, insects and even how to approach animals on foot, as your guide shows you how to spot animal signs which are everywhere but not that easily noticed.

    During your stay there are a variety of activities that can be booked on request. These include a balloon safari. Imagine floating gently over the plains with a bird’s eye view of the game below as the sun rises slowly over the horizon. After around one hour the balloon lands and nearby a full breakfast is served in the middle of the bush with glasses of bubbly to toast your flight!

    Another activity available is an adventure on Lake Victoria. Take an early flight by light aircraft to the lake. Try fishing for the legendary giant Nile perch found in these waters as you explore the myriad of tiny islands or stop at a traditional fishing village and see how the villagers live from day to day before flying back to the Maasai Mara.

    Return for lunch.

    There is time to relax after lunch before your three hour appointment in our mini spa which offers a range of treatments from facials to body massages.
    After an invigorating spa treatment join your guide for a short game drive before driving to a specially chosen vantage point overlooking the Mara plains. Enjoy sundowner drinks as you take in the beauty of the rapid sunset colouring the acacia dotted plains and transforming the world in a matter of minutes. Sit by a log fire and discuss the day's adventures as waiters serve canapés, with the spectacular setting sun on the horizon.

    Return to camp for a candle lit dinner by your deck.

    Overnight Stay: Sanctuary Olonana

  • Day 6

    Maasai Mara

    This morning after breakfast venture out with your expert guide to explore the Mara on a full day game drive.

    Experience a special “Out of Africa” picnic in homage to that magical scene in the film with Meryl Streep, Robert Redford and that wonderful gramophone. Sanctuary Olonana is located in almost exactly the same spot where the Maasai Mara scenes in “Out of Africa” were filmed. Out in the bush on a rise of the escarpment, we recreate a memorable traditional British picnic tea whilst overlooking some of the most spectacular, game-filled scenery on the continent.

    Return to camp later in the afternoon. There is time relax by the pool or in your tent.

    On your last evening in the Maasai Mara, you will be picked up by your guide and driven into the bush where a private and secluded clearing has been prepared for an elegant dinner in the middle of the wilderness. The table setting is as sophisticated and elegant as those found in the finest restaurants. Candles flicker in the darkness, throwing golden lights on the tablecloth as smiling waiters serve a delicious dinner. After dinner retire to the warmth of the campfire with a brandy or other liqueur of your choice to relax and absorb the peace and beauty of an African night.

    Overnight Stay: Sanctuary Olonana

  • Day 7

    Maasai Mara// Diani Beach

    After a sumptuous breakfast at Sanctuary Olonana, bid the staff farewell and depart for Kichwa Tembo airstrip for your scheduled flight to Diani Beach in Kenya’s South coast.

    You will be met by a guide and transferred to Kinondo Kwetu, your abode for this last leg of your honeymoon.

    Kinondo Kwetu is a collection of five houses on Galu Beach south of Mombasa. Run by a Swedish family, it boasts what must be the only sauna on the Kenyan coast! Surprisingly pleasant even at the high temperatures found here, saunas are just one of the myriad activities offered at Kinondo Kwetu – from scuba diving to horseback riding.

    Once you’re settled in and after lunch take a dip in the swimming pool or a relaxing nap in your room. Later enjoy a romantic evening by the beach where dinner will be served.

    Overnight Stay: Kinondo Kwetu

  • Day 8 & 9

    Diani Beach

    Spend the next two idyllic days enjoying the breeze by the waters of the Indian Ocean.

    There are plenty of activities to engage in such as kite surfing, sailing, scuba diving, horse riding on the beach, tennis and many more.

    Overnight Stay: Kinondo Kwetu

  • Day 10

    Diani Beach// Nairobi// Depart

    After nine glorious nights in Kenya it is time to bid the country good bye. After breakfast you will be transferred to Ukunda airstrip for your scheduled flight to Nairobi. Later an A&K representative will drive you to the airport for your homeward flight.

South Africa: The Six-Carat Safari (2012)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 Days

    Rekindle the spark of romance in South Africa, land of exotic wildlife, renowned cuisine and lively culture. Enjoy cellar and vineyard tours at top South African wineries, cast your eyes upon seas as endless as your love in quaint Hermanus and observe the region's wildlife from up close on a safari drives led by a private guide in the unspoiled Thorny bush Private Game Reserve.

  • Day 1

    India/Cape Town

    Arrive at Cape Town International Airport, where an Abercrombie & Kent representative will meet and transfer you to your hotel. The remainder of the afternoon is at leisure to settle in to your hotel or perhaps explore Cape Town, a city set on a soaring, rocky peninsula punctuated by lush valleys.

    Overnight: Cape Grace
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    Cape Town

    This morning, meet an A&K representative for a tour of charming Cape Town. Drive though the suburb of Tamboerskloof ("Drummers' Ravine") to the lower cable car station on the slopes of Table Mountain. Weather permitting; ascend by cable car to the top of this famous mountain, which affords magnificent views of the sea and the city from its summit. Then head to historic Cape Town, starting with a tranquil walk through the lovely Company Gardens, the remnants of the Dutch East India Company's fruit and vegetable gardens. Stroll past the Houses of Parliament, the Tuynhuis ("Townhouse," or Cape Town's "White House"), the National Art Gallery and the National Library. Then continue to the 17th-century Castle of Good Hope. End your explorations at the bustling Victoria & Alfred Waterfront, a working harbor surrounded by eclectic shops and restaurants. This is the perfect setting to arrange for a romantic waterfront dinner.

    Overnight: Cape Grace
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Cape Town

    After breakfast, set off for a day of discoveries en route to Cape Point. Drive past the "bikini beaches" of Clifton and Camps Bay, located at the base of the towering Twelve Apostles Mountains. Continue to the Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve at the end of the Cape Peninsula. The reserve converges in the south at Cape Point, a great pinnacle of rock. Climb the steps or ride a funicular railway - the "Flying Dutchman" - to the top for unparalleled views poised for contemplating with your loved one: southern right whale in spring and dolphin and a variety of seabirds in early summer. The journey continues to Kalk Bay, a quaint fishing hamlet on the False Bay. Around lunchtime here, fishermen offload their catch and it is possible to buy your supper straight from the boat. Travel back toward Cape Town, passing through the elegant suburb of Constantia, the cradle of the South African wine industry. Stop at Kirstenbosch, the world-renowned National Botanical Garden, where a private guide shows you around this fascinating, romantic garden. Return to your hotel, passing the University of Cape Town, Rhodes Memorial and Mostert's Mill, a Dutch windmill dating back to 1797.

    Overnight: Cape Grace
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 4

    Cape Town/Cape Winelands/Cape Town

    Today you explore the Cape Town's famous culinary scene. The Western Cape region is blessed with ideal wine-growing climate, beautiful natural scenery and historic estates - not to mention a bevy of top-notch restaurants. Start your gastronomic journey at Morgenster Estate for a cellar tour and sampling of wine and olive oil (also produced here). Enjoy lunch at the critically acclaimed Cape Winelands restaurant Terroir, on the grounds of the Kleine Zalze winery. Dine on a Provençal-inspired chalkboard menu dominated by seasonal ingredients and local wines. After lunch, visit Waterford Wine Estate, where you sample award-winning wines complemented by a chocolate tasting. Continue to picturesque Stellenbosch, the second oldest town in South Africa. Explore this lovely town's history and shops on your own as you meander its charming streets hand in hand. End the day at Anura Estate, to sample the perfect couple: award-winning wines and handcrafted Forest Hill cheeses.

    Overnight: Cape Grace
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 5

    Cape Town/Hermanus

    Enjoy a leisurely breakfast this morning. Afterward, you are driven to Hermanus, a town on the southern coast of the Western Cape, known as the perfect spot for whale watching. Check in to your luxury retreat and evening at leisure.

    Overnight: Birkenhead House
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 6

    Hermanus

    Spend the day at leisure, soaking in the beauty of your surroundings. Choose to sample excellent Cape cuisine at its fine restaurant or walk hand in hand along its secluded, romantic beach, watching for the spray of whales.

    Overnight: Birkenhead House
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Hermanus/Cape Town/Johannesburg

    After breakfast, drive a couple of hours overland, returning to Cape Town in time to catch your flight to Johannesburg. An A&K representative will meet and transfer you to your boutique hotel, situated on 10 acres of beautifully landscaped gardens. The rest of your day is at leisure to enjoy the hotel's amenities together or explore cosmopolitan Johannesburg on your own.

    Overnight: Saxon Boutique Hotel & Spa
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    Johannesburg/Thorny bush Private Game Reserve

    After a romantic breakfast at your hotel, perhaps overlooking the terrace and lush gardens beyond, take a short flight to Thorny bush Private Game Reserve, located in the world-famous Kruger National Park. Upon arrival, you are picked up and whisked away to Royal Malewane, a beautiful safari lodge in the heart of a private game reserve. Your luxurious accommodations offer unparalleled views of the African bush, coupled with warm hospitality and award-winning cuisine.

    Overnight: Royal Malewane
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 9-10

    Thorny bush Private Game Reserve

    Spend two full days on safari in search of the "Big Five" (elephant, rhino, buffalo, lion and leopard). This unspoiled area is also home to wild dog, wildebeest and plenty of giraffe. Set out on morning and evening game drives, as well as guided bush walks with expert guides and trackers. Rekindle your love over delicious, candlelit meals and South African wines, and sleep under a blanket of the brilliant stars.

    Overnight: Royal Malewane
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    Thorny bush Private Game Reserve/Johannesburg/India

    Bid farewell to this paradise of flora and fauna with a final morning game drive. Fly back to Johannesburg, where you are met by an A&K representative and escorted to the international airport for your return flight home.

    Meal Plan: B
