Signature Indochina & Angkor Wat

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 13 days

    Highlights: Breeze through customs and immigration upon arrival in Hanoi, Siem Reap and Bangkok with special VIP assistance. Experience the legendary hospitality and romance of Southeast Asia by lodging in luxury hotels famous for their colonial-era grace throughout your journey. Absorb the mysterious beauty of Ha Long Bay, studded with soaring limestone islands, on an overnight cruise. Delve into Vietnam's history and culture, descending into the Cu Chi Tunnels near Ho Chi Minh City and travelling like locals do, by cyclo through Hanoi and boat in Siem Reap. Gain a deeper understanding of the Khmer empire with a lecture by an expert in Angkorian history and culture. Enter Angkor Wat through the east entrance to avoid the crowds, and explore it with your expert local guide, who was born and raised in a nearby village. Visit atmospheric, foliage-engulfed Ta Prohm early in the morning, before the heat sets in and other visitors arrive.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Hanoi, Vietnam

    Arrive in Hanoi, where an A&K representative meets and assists you through customs and immigration. You are then transferred to your French colonial-style hotel.

    Overnight: Sofitel Legend Metropole Hanoi

  • Day 2

    Hanoi

    Hanoi is the country's cultural heart, bearing traces of centuries of changing dynasties. Visit Ho Chi Minh's house, a simple structure located among banyan, frangipani and milkweed trees. Continue to the lotus-shaped One Pillar Pagoda, dating back to the first Vietnamese empire independent from China in the 11th century. Also visit the Quan Thanh Temple, the Temple of Literature and the National Museum of Fine Arts, with its excellent collection of folk art, sculpture and lacquerware by contemporary Vietnamese artists. Later, travel the way a local would - by cyclo - through the Old Quarter. You also visit Dr. Huong, a traditional medicine expert, for insight into an ancient form of medical treatments.

    Overnight: Sofitel Legend Metropole Hanoi
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Hanoi/Ha Long Bay

    Travel overland through the verdant Vietnamese countryside to Ha Long Bay, seeing a traditional way of life en route. Board your luxuryjunk (boat), your accommodations for the night. Sail through this magical bay, where thousands of sculpted limestone islands rise from the waters. After lunch on the boat, disembark and trek to a nearby summit for a panoramic view of the sail-dotted bay or swim by a secluded beach, or simply choose to stay on deck and sunbathe. Then visit a fishing village and meet third-generation fishermen before returning to the boat.

    Overnight: MS 'Halong Ginger'
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Ha Long Bay/Hanoi/Danang

    Greet the sunrise with a tai chi lesson on deck. After breakfast, sail to a spellbinding limestone grotto before heading back to shore. Return overland to Hanoi, visiting a pottery village along the way. This evening, fly to Danang and continue to your newly opened beachfront resort on Son Tra Peninsula near China Beach.

    Overnight: InterContinental Danang Resort
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 5

    Danang/Hoi An/Danang

    After breakfast, visit the lovely traditional town of Hoi An, which retains architectural styles of the many nations that used its port; this includes the Old House of Tan Ky, the Fujian Assembly Hall and the 400-year-old Japanese covered bridge. Visit a local home to see how silkworms are raised, and how silk is produced. Afterward, visit a lantern artisan to learn how to make one of the silk lanterns for which Hoi An is known. Return to your resort for an afternoon at leisure.

    Overnight: InterContinental Danang Resort
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 6

    Danang/Ho Chi Minh City

    Fly this morning to Ho Chi Minh City, formerly known as Saigon. The city has recently experienced a resurgence of art and culture; nowadays, it is a vibrant hub of activity, a sharp contrast to the traditional ambience of Hanoi. This afternoon, visit the 19th-century Thien Hau Pagoda, which serves as a site for prayers to Buddha as well as to the Chinese deity Thien Hau Thanh Mau. Explore the surrounding neighborhood of Cholon, the city's Chinatown.

    Overnight: InterContinental Asiana Saigon
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Ho Chi Minh City

    This morning, head out of the city and into the countryside to explore the expansive Cu Chi Tunnel network, a key Vietcong resource during the Vietnam War. You have the opportunity to shoot a rifle, and squeeze into and crawl through the small tunnels, experiencing for yourself what it was like to live this way. Later, savor the unique, diverse flavors of Vietnamese cuisine. In the afternoon, see the city's colonial legacy, including the Central Post Office, the Cathedral of Notre Dame and the Opera House.

    Overnight: InterContinental Asiana Saigon
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 8

    Ho Chi Minh City/Angkor Wat, Cambodia

    Fly to Siem Reap, the town nearest to the ruins of Angkor Wat. Over the next few days, explore the region with your guide, who was born and raised in Cambodia and experienced firsthand the challenges under the Khmer Rouge and the country's optimistic rise from that terrible era. Transfer to your hotel before setting off to explore the complex. Start with a visit to the Bayon Temple, with its 172 large-scale faces bearing beguiling smiles. Then visit the Elephant and Leper King Terraces. All are examples of masterful Khmer engineering and artistry.

    Overnight: Raffles Grand Hotel d'Angkor
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Angkor Wat

    Early this morning, journey to Ta Prohm as it is bathed in the golden light of early morning. Explore it at leisure before other visitors arrive for the day. Return to the hotel for a late breakfast. In the afternoon, explore two less-visited areas: Banteay Srei, also known as the "Citadel of Women," and Banteay Samre, one of the most complete temple complexes of Angkor. You also visit the Landmine Museum, an organization that promotes mine awareness and education.

    Overnight: Raffles Grand Hotel d'Angkor
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 10

    Angkor Wat

    Watch the sunrise over the main temple of Angkor Wat, an unforgettable and quintessential Southeast Asia experience. Then walk amid the moss-peppered pathways, gazing up at the intricately carved tower tops, or climb the steep, narrow temple steps that lead to wide courtyards. Contemplate the temple's spiritual power while you walk in the cool shade of its hallways, stopping to take in the fragrance of burning incense. After lunch, visit Tonle Sap Lake to observe local life as it unfolds along the floating villages that dot the shoreline.

    Overnight: Raffles Grand Hotel d'Angkor
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 11

    Angkor Wat/Bangkok, Thailand

    This morning, fly to Bangkok, enjoying VIP airport assistance. Upon arrival, you are met and transferred to your hotel, located on the banks of the Chao Phraya River. The remainder of your day is at leisure. You may choose to take advantage of your complimentary spa credit this afternoon.

    Overnight: The Peninsula Bangkok
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 12

    Bangkok

    If you wish, rise early to give alms to monks at a little-visited temple. After breakfast, travel by boat to see life along the klongs, an ancient system of canals branching off the Chao Phraya River and still a vital part of many locals' lives.

    Afterward, visit the Grand Palace, one of the most beautiful legacies of the ancient Siamese court. The sprawling, garden-dotted compound includes ornate statues of mythological creatures and more than 100 colorful buildings. Also on the grounds is the 18th-century Wat Phra Kaew, Thailand's most important shrine, which houses the Emerald Buddha. Made of green jade or jasper, it symbolizes the independence, strength and good fortune of the country.

    Next, visit Wat Pho and its lovely gilded Reclining Buddha. The temple resonates with the musical sound of coins as they are dropped into brass offering bowls by the faithful, who circumambulate the giant statue.

    The afternoon is at leisure to unwind in your hotel or peruse some of the nearby galleries and boutiques.

    Overnight: The Peninsula Bangkok
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 13

    Depart Bangkok

    Transfer to the airport to board your homebound flight.

    Meal Plan: B

Signature Italy

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 days

    Enjoy the services of an A&K Guardian Angel, who lends assistance throughout your stay. A&K's Concierge Rail Service escorts you to and from the train from Florence to Venice. Browse the local markets in Rome and Venice, perhaps finding a few Italian treasures to take home with you. Travel through scenic Tuscany and stop for wine tasting at a local wine producer. View some of the greatest treasures of the Renaissance with insight from an expert A&K guide. Sample Venetian cuisine at a traditional bacari (wine bar). Cruise Venice's romantic canals by gondola.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Rome, Italy

    Arrive in Rome, where you are met and transferred to your hotel in the city center. Famous throughout history as a center of prestige, power and intrigue, Rome was built on seven hills. The city has forever altered the Western world's standards of beauty and excellence in such fields as art, religion and government. It boasts a greater number of historic buildings, monuments and ruins than any other city in the world.

    Overnight: The St. Regis Rome (Gold) or Rose Garden Palace (Silver)

  • Day 2

    Rome and Vatican City

    Your local guide joins you in a discovery of the evocative remains of the Imperial City, starting with the Palatine Hill. Continue to the imposing Arch of Constantine; then visit the mighty Colosseum, constructed in AD 80 to seat 50,000 spectators who reveled in gladiatorial contests. Enjoy lunch at leisure before visiting Vatican City. Admire the stunning collection of antiquities, objets d'art in the vast museum; then gaze at Michelangelo's famous painted ceiling in the Sistine Chapel and wander through St. Peter's Basilica, housing Michelangelo'sPietï¿½ and Bernini's exuberant baldachin.

    Overnight: The St. Regis Rome (Gold) or Rose Garden Palace (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Rome

    Glimpse the local character of Rome with a walking tour that begins in the pretty Campo de Fiori, whose local food market delights the senses. Continue through a labyrinth of small lanes to the Piazza Navona, dominated by the remarkable Baroque Fountain of the Four Rivers. Visit the Pantheon, built in the first century AD as a temple. Later, stop at the iconic Trevi Fountain. Legend says that if you throw a coin over your left shoulder into the fountain, you will return to Rome one day. Conclude your tour at the famous Spanish Steps on the Piazza di Spagna. Enjoy a leisurely afternoon to explore independently.

    Overnight: The St. Regis Rome (Gold) or Rose Garden Palace (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Rome/Orvieto/Florence

    Depart for a scenic drive to Florence, stopping in the Umbrian town of Orvieto for lunch. Enjoy a guided walking tour of this ancient city perched atop of a rock. In the magnificent Piazza del Duomo stands the cathedral housing Luca Signorelli's masterpiece fresco Cycle of the Apocalypse. Continue through the breathtaking Tuscan countryside, renowned for its art, history and evocative landscapes. Among the vineyards and olive groves, see hamlets and farmhouses. Afterward, visit a local wine producer and enjoy a wine tasting before continuing to Florence, the birthplace of the Renaissance.

    Overnight: Westin Excelsior, Florence (Gold) or Hotel Santa Maria Novella (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Florence

    Embark on a walking tour of this glorious city. Cross the Arno at the shop-lined Ponte Vecchio, the only bridge not bombed into oblivion during World War II, and visit the graceful red Duomo, designed by Brunelleschi for the cathedral. Continue to the Uffizi Gallery, housing such striking works as Carravagio's Medusa, Botticelli's Birth of Venus, da Vinci's Annunciation and Raphael's Madonna of the Goldfinch. The balance of the day is at leisure.

    See the monument restoration project supported by A&K Philanthropy.

    Overnight: Westin Excelsior, Florence (Gold) or Hotel Santa Maria Novella (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    Florence/Venice

    From Piazzale Michelangelo you have a panoramic view across the city. Visit the Galleria dell'Accademia to see Michelangelo's David. After lunch, A&K's Concierge Rail Service offers assistance at the station when you travel to Venice by train. On arrival, cruise along the Grand Canal to your hotel.

    Overnight: Luna Hotel Baglioni (Gold) or Londra Palace (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Venice

    Spend the morning wandering the market district around the Rialto Bridge. Since the 11th century, Venetians and foreign merchants have traded exotic imported goods from here. At a bacari, a small wine bar, try un' ombra (small glass of wine) with traditional Venetian snacks. Admire the lyrically beautiful Madonna and Child by Giovanni Bellini and Assumption of the Virgin by Titian at the nearby Frari church. This afternoon, stroll through vibrant St. Mark's Square and visit St. Mark's Basilica, one of the finest examples of Byzantine architecture in Venice. Continue to the Doge's Palace, the seat of the main government departments and the residence of the Doge who ruled the Venetian Republic.

    Overnight: Luna Hotel Baglioni (Gold) or Londra Palace (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    Venice /Murano/Venice

    Travel by private launch to the lagoon island of Murano, where for centuries the famous Murano glass has been manufactured. Enjoy a visit to one of the most exclusive glass factories with the chance to browse their excellent selection of glassware, vases and chandeliers. Return to Venice for an afternoon at leisure. This evening, enjoy a gondola ride through the canals.

    Overnight: Luna Hotel Baglioni (Gold) or Londra Palace (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Depart Venice

    This morning, transfer to the airport for your flight home.

    Meal Plan: B

Signature Kenya & Tanzania

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 12 days

    Highlights: Experience East Africa through a blend of time-saving scenic flights and drives that offer a close, intimate view of life in the plains. Stay at ideally located lodges and camps with stunning views. Make your stay more meaningful by stopping at a local school and interacting with the children. Visit Giraffe Centre and the Karen Blixen house and museum, the farmhouse where the Out of Africa author lived

  • Day 1

    Arrive Nairobi, Kenya

    Arrive in Nairobi after an overnight flight. Considered a microcosm of the African continent, Kenya is one of the most exciting and exotic destinations in the world, offering dramatic spectacles of nature.

    Overnight: Fairmont The Norfolk (Gold) or Nairobi Serena Hotel (Silver)

  • Day 2

    Nairobi/Amboseli National Park

    Your safari adventure begins with a scenic flight to Amboseli National Park, known for its thriving big game population and views of towering, snow-capped Mount Kilimanjaro; its Uhuru Peak soars to 19,341 feet. After lunch, explore the acacia woodland, freshwater swamps, scrub brush and open plains in search of wildlife. Part of the park is composed of a dried-up lakebed, where mirages rise from the shimmering heat.

    Overnight: Tortilis Camp (Gold) or Amboseli Serena Safari Lodge (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Amboseli National Park

    Set out at dawn, watching for elephants, zebras and wildebeests along the saltpan trails. Spend midday at leisure before setting off on a late afternoon game drive, when predators begin to roam the plains in search of prey.

    Overnight: Tortilis Camp (Gold) or Amboseli Serena Safari Lodge (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Amboseli/Arusha, Tanzania/Lake Manyara National Park

    Travel by road to Arusha, arriving in time for lunch. Fly to your lodge, located in a picturesque region where shallow, freshwater Lake Manyara - one of a chain of lakes in Africa's Great Rift Valley - is nestled at the foot of a spectacular 2,000-foot escarpment. Choose to walk along a nature trail or simply relax with a book by the pool.

    Overnight: Escarpment Luxury Lodge (Gold) or Lake Manyara Serena Safari Lodge (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Lake Manyara/Ngorongoro Crater

    Discover a wide array of wildlife and many bird species amid a tangle of rainforest-like foliage. After a farm-fresh lunch, continue to the Ngorongoro Crater, ascending its outer wall through dense vegetation.

    Overnight: Ngorongoro Crater Lodge (Gold) or Ngorongoro Serena Safari Lodge (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Ngorongoro Crater

    Wind your way through the landscape of Ngorongoro Crater, one of the most magnificent wildlife arenas in all of Africa, containing a - "Lost World" of animals; its prairies are an ideal spot to see hyenas, buffalo and gazelles. Following a picnic lunch near a hippo pool, visit a Maasai village to experience its culture firsthand.

    Overnight: Ngorongoro Crater Lodge (Gold) or Ngorongoro Serena Safari Lodge (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Ngorongoro Crater/Serengeti National Park

    Fly (drive, if you are on the Silver itinerary) to the vast, wildlife-rich Serengeti National Park, home to lions, leopards, hyenas, cheetahs, giraffes and topi and setting of the Great Migration.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Kusini (Gold) or Serengeti Serena Safari Lodge (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    Serengeti National Park

    Spend your day on morning and afternoon safaris, searching for elephants as well as the "Little Five" - including leopard tortoises and rhino beetles - along plains, rivers and rocky outcroppings.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Kusini (Gold) or Serengeti Serena Safari Lodge (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 9

    Serengeti/Arusha/Nairobi/Masai Mara Game Reserve

    Fly to the endless Masai Mara Game Reserve, Kenya's finest wildlife spectacle and site of the country's largest lion population. This afternoon, a game drive introduces you to the famed reserve, where you find the greatest concentration of species in all of Kenya.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Olonana (Gold) or Mara Serena Safari Lodge (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 10-11

    Masai Mara Game Reserve

    Spend the next two days in the Mara, more than 500 square miles of savannas, woodlands and tree-fringed rivers that shelter an astonishing range of wildlife, made up of more than 400 bird species and nearly a hundred animal species, including all the great predators. Watch crocodiles bask in the sun, hippos wallow in the muddy water and monkeys frolic overhead; see elephants roam the forest edge as well as lanky, graceful giraffes survey the plains. You also may visit a local primary school supported by A&K Philanthropy.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Olonana (Gold) or Mara Serena Safari Lodge (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 12

    Masai Mara Game Reserve

    Return to Nairobi, stopping for lunch at Carnivore Restaurant, a meat-eater's paradise with options for herbivores as well. Afterward, visit the Giraffe Center and Karen Blixen house and museum - the farmhouse where the Out of Africa author lived. Spend the afternoon relaxing in a reserved dayroom prior to your international departure.

    Overnight: Fairmont The Norfolk (dayroom) (Gold) or Nairobi Serena Hotel (dayroom) (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L

Signature Peru & Machu Picchu

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 days

    Highlights: Gain a deeper understanding of Peru's ancient history by visiting the most significant pre-Columbian sites in the Sacred Valley, including Sacsayhuaman and Ollantaytambo. Meet an expert Andean weaver who demonstrates techniques that date back to pre-Columbian times. Feed llama, alpaca and vicuna and learn about shearing and weaving techniques during a visit to Awana Kancha, a breeding center for South American camelids. Take in the stunning Peruvian countryside as you travel on the glass-topped Vistadome train to Machu Picchu

  • Day 1

    Arrive Lima, Peru

    Arrive in Lima this evening and transfer to your hotel. Founded in 1533, the "City of Kings" is a mï¿½lange of colonial structures, pre-Columbian sites and modern conveniences.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel (Gold) or The Westin Lima (Silver)

  • Day 2

    Lima

    This afternoon, explore Peru's capital, visiting the Plaza Mayor and Cathedral. Spend time exploring the Larco Herrera Museum, one of the city's best museums for pre-Columbian artifacts.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel (Gold) or The Westin Lima (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Lima/Cusco/Sacred Valley

    After breakfast, fly to Cusco, an Andean city located at over 11,000 feet above sea level. Upon arrival, transfer to the Sacred Valley of the Incas, the vast green valley that sprawls out far below Machu Picchu, and where an ancient way of life lives on. En route, visit Awana Kancha, a center where South American camelids are bred. Learn about the renowned fibers created from their coats, as well as the ancient weaving techniques still used today. Spend the balance of the day acclimatizing to the altitude.

    Overnight: Sol y Luna Lodge & Spa (Gold) or Aranwa Sacred Valley Hotel & Wellness (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 4

    Sacred Valley

    Start your exploration of the Sacred Valley of the Incas with a visit to the imposing fortress of Ollantaytambo, a formidable stone structure of massive terraces that hug the mountain slopes. A traditional lifestyle continues in the charming nearby town of Chinchero. Afterward, delve deeper into the rich local culture when you visit expert Andean weaver Nilda Callaï¿½aupa Alvarez, who leads a women's weaving collective where you learn firsthand about ancient techniques and about their role in contemporary Andean culture. Return to your hotel.

    Continue to the Kuychi Center, a charity that supports underprivileged children at Niï¿½os del Arco Iris (Children of the Rainbow), a project supported by A&K Philanthropy. Enjoy a barbecue lunch in the gardens.

    Overnight: Sol y Luna Lodge & Spa (Gold) or Aranwa Sacred Valley Hotel & Wellness (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 5

    Sacred Valley/Machu Picchu

    Journey deep into the Andes toward Machu Picchu on board the glass-topped Vistadome train, passing through the spectacular Peruvian countryside along the way. Arrive with plenty of time to begin exploring the breathtaking Incan city. Overlooking the Urubamba River and discovered in a hidden tangle of trees atop the mountain, Machu Picchu forever holds the secret of its passing - its Incan inhabitants left no written records of its purpose or its flawless construction. After a tour of the ruins with your expert guide, there is time to explore on your own.

    Overnight: Machu Picchu Sanctuary Lodge (Gold) or Inkaterra Machu Picchu Pueblo Hotel (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Machu Picchu/Cusco

    Rise with the sun to walk through Machu Picchu in the early morning mist before the crowds arrive. You may opt for a hike to Inti Punku (Sun Gate), the entry point to the ruins from the Inca Trail, or trek up to Huayna Picchu, the high mountain that forms the backbone of the ruins. After your explorations, journey back to Cusco aboard the Vistadome train, savoring incomparable views of the mountain scenery that surrounds you.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio (Gold) or Libertador Palacio del Inka Hotel (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 7

    Cusco

    Discover Cusco on a city tour that includes the Cathedral on the Plaza de Armas and the Santo Domingo Temple, a Dominican church built on the foundations of the Koricancha, the Incas' principal religious building. Later, explore the well-preserved ruins of Sacsayhuaman, a testament to Incan architectural skill. Compiled of massive stones, the walls were cut and placed together with incredible precision and without a single drop of mortar to hold them in place.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio (Gold) or Libertador Palacio del Inka Hotel (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    Cusco/Lima

    Return to Lima for an evening at leisure. Perhaps get in some last minute shopping in a local crafts market or sample some of Peru's culinary offerings.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel (Gold) or The Westin Lima (Silver)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Depart Lima

    Board your homebound flight.

    Meal Plan: B
