Suggested Zambia Accommodation

  • CHIAWA, LOWER ZAMBEZI

    Offering the best guiding in Africa, Chiawa is an outstanding camp set in the wilds of the Lower Zambezi National Park. Sixteen guests are accommodated between five classic safari tents, one honeymoon tent and three superior tents.

    Located in the heart of the Lower Zambezi National Park, Chiawa Camp is superbly sited to enjoy both the cool shade of a grove of mahogany and faidherbia trees, and the denim expanse of the mighty Zambezi River. Sumptuous cuisine is served on the al-fresco dining veranda and post dinner nightcaps are served around the campfire. To best appreciate the park's inhabitants, Chiawa offers guiding both on land and water.

    MCHENJA CAMP

    Set on the banks of the Luangwa River, Mchenja Camp commands magnificent river views. The camp has five stylishly designed tents under thatch, each with outside shower and indoor bath. A small pool allows you to cool off after a busy morning of safari activities.

    Game viewing in this part of the park is exceptional and excursions are conducted on foot or by vehicle. Superb night game drives are available and well worth while as you are bound to see many of Luangwa's large pods of hippo - there are estimated to be at least 50 hippos per kilometre of river.

    OLD MONDORO

    A simple camp, and sister camp of Chiawa, Old Mondoro Bush Camp is sited towards the eastern side of the Lower Zambezi National Park in an open area of faidherbia forest. With no electricity, the camp is lit by paraffin lamps and candles which create a wonderfully romantic ambience.

    Just four en suite bush chalets are made of local pole and thatch construction and set out along a channel of the Zambezi frequented by copious hippo. Activities are focussed around game drives and boating, however with so much activity in or around the camp it isn't necessary to venture far from the lodge.

    SANCTUARY CHICHELE PRESIDENTIAL LODGE

    Once a private Presidential retreat, Chichele occupies one of the most breathtaking sites in the entire South Luangwa National Park. Since the original structures, built circa 1960, the building has been transformed in recent years to create a spectacular safari lodge, designed to complement its natural surroundings

    In order to exploit the wonderful floodplain vistas offered by the site, ten air-conditioned, elegant cottages are built along the edge of the hillside, each providing private panoramic views. Each suite offers en suite facilities and is well equipped with all the little extras you would expect of a lodge of this quality.

    The property also boasts a relaxing swimming pool with views the adjacent valley. Meals may be served in a variety of locations - the luxurious indoor dining room or on the scenic veranda and boma area outside. Game drives are conducted in open 4x4 vehicles, driven by qualified, professional guides. Night game drives using spotlights to enable guests to see nocturnal animals such as leopard and spotted hyena. Game walks are also very popular. Children of all ages welcome, children aged 5- 11 years old are only permitted on the game drives at the sole discretion of the lodge manager.

    SANCTUARY PUKU RIDGE TENTED CAMP

    South Luangwa is one of the greatest wildlife sanctuaries in the world, and not without reason. Set on a rocky ridge and overlooking the Kakumbi dambo (valley), Puku Ridge Camp is well located. This is the perfect spot to revel in the abundance of wildlife and tradition of an old-world safari camp. Puku Ridge Camp is an intimate camp, built unobtrusively close to the wild. The central dining area produces gourmet meals, despite being so far away from a city. Sanctuary Puku Ridge Camp is designed to leave no trace on the environment and has embraced a very natural kind of African chic in its decor.

    The diverse wildlife along the dambo (valley) offers great opportunities for photography. In the evenings relax and unwind as you dine with the night sounds of Africa for a soundtrack. Night game drives allow sightings of nocturnal animals in the South Luangwa National Park. The concentration of game around the Luangwa river and its ox bow lagoons is among the most intense in Africa offering thrilling guided walks, always accompanied by a professional guide and an armed national parks scout.
