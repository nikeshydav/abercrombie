Botswana: Romance Under the Stars (2012)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 Nights

    Relive the ambience of a bygone era on this journey through the riverine forests, expansive delta and spectacular game-filled bush land of Botswana. Romance is in the air - literally - when you sleep under the stars on an elevated platform and share a lantern-lit "star bath" on your private deck. The euphoria continues on a private sundowner cruise as you admire enjoy your beautiful surroundings and the array of wildlife that comes to the water's edge.

  • Day 1

    India/Johannesburg, South Africa

    On Arrival at Johannesburg, where you are met by an Abercrombie & Kent representative and taken to your hotel, set in one of the city's most exclusive residential suburbs amid lush hillside terraces and sculpted gardens. The balance of the day is at your leisure.

    Overnight: The Westcliff Hotel
    Meal Plan None

  • Day 2

    Johannesburg/Maun, Botswana/Okavango Delta

    Fly to Maun and connect to your camp, located on the banks of the Boro River in the Okavango Delta. This labyrinth of waterways, wetlands and islands is an unspoiled paradise for some of Africa's greatest concentration of wildlife and birdlife. Your elegant African-themed suite is set on an elevated platform in the tree line, with a wooden deck that offers expansive views over the river and lagoon. Tonight, get close to nature - and each other - when you roll your bed onto the deck for the unique opportunity to sleep under the stars.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Baines' Camp
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Okavango Delta

    Game-viewing options flourish at Baines' Camp. Resting on 260,000 acres in a private concession neighboring the famed Moremi Game Reserve, the area is superb for viewing wildlife by land in open safari vehicles or on the water from a poled mokoro (dugout canoe). However, sometimes the best way to tune into the bush is on foot. Walking safaris are a specialty at your camp, and skilled guides introduce you to the area's natural wonders.

    Return from a night game drive for a relaxing "star bath," a freestanding traditional Botswana-style zinc bathtub set out on your private deck, surrounded by lanterns.

    Philanthropic Activities: During your time at Baines' you have the opportunity to participate in an educational experience with a group of semi-habituated African elephants. After a few safety tips from your mahout (trainer), you are encouraged to touch each of the elephants and lead one of them while it gently rests the tip of its trunk on your hand. You may even get a "kiss" on the cheek from one of your new friends. At a picnic spot under large shade trees, sit down and relax with cold refreshments and brunch. The elephants are fed pre-cut fodder, and you can observe them eating and playing together after their lunch.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Baines' Camp
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Okavango Delta

    Fly to the Moremi Game Reserve and your camp, situated on the previous hunting grounds of the Batawana Tribe on Chief's Island. The camp overlooks the seasonal Piajio Channel within the Moremi Game Reserve, an area renowned for its abundant wildlife and large variety of bird life. Sanctuary Chief's Camp is made up of 12 luxury bush pavilions - each with a spacious seating area and game-viewing deck with a hammock. While you are here, take a dip in Chief's pool, pamper yourself in the spa and choose from a variety of activities that include game drives and mokoro safaris (in season).

    Overnight: Sanctuary Baines' Camp
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5-6

    Okavango Delta

    Game viewing in the Moremi is nothing short of spectacular. In addition to 400 species of birds, ostrich, antelope, crocodile, wildebeest, kudu and hippo are common here. However, the area has been dubbed the "predator capital" of Botswana, boasting leopard, cheetah, painted wild dog, spotted hyena and a large resident pride of lion. Game drives are conducted in custom-built open vehicles, offering an ideal viewing vantage point. While you are here, you may wish to take an optional helicopter excursion for a different perspective on the wildlife. A specially prepared romantic dinner is also in store for you during one of your evenings at Chief's.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Baines' Camp
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Okavango Delta/Chobe National Park

    The name "Chilwero" means "place of high view" in the local Setswana language, and true to its namesake, Sanctuary Chobe Chilwero is positioned high above the Chobe River. Fly to Chobe National Park today and check in at your award-winning lodge, which offers unparalleled panoramic views across the islands and floodplains, stretching as far as Namibia. Your cottage features either a private garden or a sala (balcony) with a hammock, ideal for cuddling and casual game viewing.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Chobe Chilwero
    Meal Plan: Meals: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    Chobe National Park

    Chobe National Park covers a diversity of landscapes, ranging from riverine forests to floodplains and periodic marshlands. The area is home to one of the largest concentration of elephants in Africa, as well as an abundance of lion, hippopotamus, buffalo, impala, puku, and a large variety of bird species. Embark on your first game drive shortly after sunrise. You can also view water-loving game up close from a different perspective when you board a small boat. End your day with a romantic couple's spa treatment.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Chobe Chilwero
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 9

    Chobe National Park/Victoria Falls, Zimbabwe

    Travel overland this morning, crossing the border into Zimbabwe toward breathtaking Victoria Falls and your accommodations at the world-famous Victoria Falls Hotel. Built in 1904, the Victoria Falls Hotel's gracious colonial style epitomizes the romance of grand travel. Late this afternoon, set out on a private cruise on the Zambezi River aboard Ra-Ikane, a small luxury boat that reflects the ambience of a bygone era. Glide lazily down the river looking for hippo, elephant or buffalo coming to drink at the water's edge and absorb the stillness of the African bush as sunset brings the day to a close.

    Overnight: The Victoria Falls Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    Victoria Falls/Johannesburg/Depart

    The final day of your adventure begins with an exclusive "Flight of Angels" that offers the amazing opportunity to travel above the majestic falls in a helicopter for unrivaled views of the crashing waters. When you are back on the ground, set out on a walking tour of Victoria Falls. Notice the permanent rainbows that crisscross the gorge, producing images that are more like paintings than reality. The unique tropical rainforest that veils the edge of the falls, the rising spray and thundering roar as these waters break against the ragged rocks below make a sight and sound never to be forgotten. Later, transfer to the airport and fly to Johannesburg for your homebound flight.

    Meal Plan: B

LOVE IN INDONESIA – BALI & UBUD HONEYMOONS

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 Days
    Highlights: Indulge in a touch of adventure, culture and luxury as you celebrate your marriage on our Honeymoon in Bali tour. With secluded resorts, golden beaches and lush, rolling hills creating a magnificent backdrop you’ll discover why Bali has earned its reputation as the ultimate romantic holiday destination. Denpasar - Candidasa - Tenganan - Trek - Besakih Temple - Kintamani - Lovina - Banjar Hot Springs - Munduk - Lovina - Ulun Danu - Jatiluwih - Ubud – Seminyak

    • ITINERARY

  • Day 1

    Denpasar//Candidasa//Romantic Dinner

    Arrive this afternoon at Ngurah Rai International Airport in Denpasar where you will be greeted by your private driver. A private transfer (approximately 2 hours) takes you to Candidasa in Karangasem, Bali’s eastern province. Check in to your seaside resort for afternoon tea. Experience the very best of Balinese Massage with this 1 hour his and hers indulgence designed to dissolve tension and stress. Your choice of aromatics enhances your experience. Dinner tonight of a set menu is at Sea Salt restaurant set in a traditional Balinese Bale and floating on a lotus pond.

    Overnight: Alila Manggis

  • Day 2

    Candidasa //Tenganan // Trek // Candidasa

    After a leisurely breakfast depart for the Aga Village of Tenganan. The village is home to the Bali Aga people, Bali’s original people before the arrival of those from the Majapahit kingdom of Java. This is an interesting north – south aligned village compound, very different to the Balinese. They are known for their weaving of traditional ikat and basket weaving. At the end of the village, continue through the large stone gates to meet one of the original family elders who can trace his family history for hundreds of years in the village. He will be more than happy to show the logs of wild bees and you can taste the many varieties of honey produced from these natural hives used for culinary and medicinal purposes.

    Take a winding 2 hour light trek through rice fields, villages and plantations as you head towards Tirta Gangga. Your local guide is an original inhabitant of the Aga village with a wealth of knowledge in his local area. Tirta Gangga, which means 'water of the Gangges', is a sacred water palace that was built in the early twentieth century by the last king of Karangasem. It boasts many decorative ponds and fountains, lush plant life and natural bathing pools.

    NB: there is an additional fee if you want to bathe in the pools. Please bring a change of clothes and a towel. Bathers are not permitted; wear shorts and a T shirt instead. Take lunch of a delicious Megibung traditional feast in a bamboo restaurant with views across the rolling fields and rice terraces at various stages of harvest depending on the season.

    Return to your resort. Late this afternoon, transfer to nearby Padang Bai where you will board the “Paramitha”, your private 15m cruise boat to enjoy a 1.5 hour sunset cruise with unrivalled views of Mt Agung and the colourful Candidasa coastline. You will take a romantic dinner on the upper deck of the boat with the island of Bali as your view. Return to your hotel in Candidasa.

    Overnight: Alila Manggis

  • Day 3

    Candidasa - Besakih Temple - Kintamani - Lovina

    Enjoy breakfast at leisure before travelling through small villages and picturesque landscapes, stopping for pictures and with your guide as you wish. Start with to Besakih Temple, the ‘Mother Temple’ of Bali which is located on the western slopes of Mt Agung, Bali highest volcano (2 567 m), it is the largest and most important temple on the island. Stop enroute in the village of Sibetan to observe traditional weaving and visit a salakplantation where you can taste Bali’s famed ‘snakeskin’ fruit. Take lunch at Maha Giri restaurant; with stunning terracing rice field views against a backdrop of Mt Agung.

    Arrive at Kintamani, offering spectacular views over Lake Batur and its volcano. Mt Batur standing at 1412 meters high is not the highest volcano in Bali, but it is the most active, having erupted more than twenty times during the last two centuries, and is surrounded by a spectacular caldera. Leaving Kintamani through along winding mountain roads before stopping at the temple of Pura Puncak Penulisan for amazing 360 degree cloud shrouded views across the valley – well worth the steep staircase. This temple also holds a vast collection of deified King and Queen Statues dating to the 10-14th Century. Continue to the volcanic sand beaches on northern Bali, arriving late afternoon. Diner at resort

    Overnight : The Lovina

  • Day 4

    Lovina//Day at Leisure

    Today is free to enjoy the many resort facilities or laze on the beach.

    Overnight:The Lovina

  • Day 5

    Lovina // Organic Lunch // Banjar Hot Springs // Munduk

    Visit the Hakone Open Air Museum and its intriguing number of outdoor sculptures. It als holds an extensive Picasso collection. Ride the world's second longest cable car to the top of Mt Owakudani.Take a leisurely breakfast or perhaps one last swim in the ocean before meeting your driver and guide around 1200. Take lunch in an organic restaurant nestled amongst rice paddies and organic gardens on the shores of the Bali Straits.

    After lunch, recline in the healing waters of Banjar Hot Springs. These picturesque hot springs, set amidst lush tropical gardens, contain two main pools with warm sulphur water pouring from the mouths of dragons, or Naga’s as they are locally known. Continue to the cool central highlands of Bali, Munduk. Take afternoon tea with spectacular views across the northern coast valley to the Java Sea. Dinner tonight will be a candlelit set menu in your private Bale surrounded by water with unhindered views of the skyline.

    Overnight:Munduk Moding

  • Day 6

    Lovina // Ulun Danu // Jatiluwih // Ubud

    Munduk is the ideal location for outdoor activities and this morning you’ll have the chance to explore with a light 1 hour exploration of the Coffee & Clove Plantations – learning about the processes and growing. Around 10.00 you will be met by your local guide and driver, travelling south to a vantage point overlooking two superb volcanic lakes: Buyan and Tamblingan. Stop for photos and to enjoy the scenery before descending to the thriving village of Bedugul. Enroute stop at the major water temple of Pura Ulun Danu, founded in the 17th century. This is perhaps one of the most photographed temples in Bali and is dedicated to Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, featuring a beautiful thatched Hindu Meru which seems to float on the lake. This temple is also home to a Buddhist shrine and a small mosque.

    Continue through the vegetable farms before lunch overlooking verdant rice terraces. After lunch, continue to the UNESCO shortlisted rice terraces of Jatiluwih – the oldest in Bali for a photo stop. Travel through the scenic area of Marga to your Resort in Ubud, arriving mid-afternoon. Enjoy an in Villa 30 minute head and shoulder massage on arrival. No visit to Bali would be complete without witnessing traditional dance and this evening, in Ubud, you will be treated to a performance in a nearby village. Take a late dinner one of Bali’s best tables, enjoying the freshest of local ingredients fused with western techniques in the Discovery Menu of the award winning Mosaic Restaurant.

    Overnight: Kayumanis Ubud

  • Day 7

    Cooking Class - Rudana Museum

    This morning at 0700, you’ll be transferred to central Ubud where you will meet your local chef and enjoy a cup of tea or coffee while receiving a short briefing on the day’s program. With your local chef, visit the traditional food market to learn about the intricacies of Indonesian regional cuisines and observe the daily market trade and the exotic fruits, vegetables, herbs and spices that grace Indonesian plates.

    Returning to a traditional Balinese Kitchen via a tour of the rice paddies, you have the opportunity to delve deeper into the techniques of Balinese cooking under the guidance of an expert local Chef. Here, you will learn how to prepare Balinese signature dishes, using time honored techniques and become familiar with the fresh herbs, spices, and other ingredients that make Balinese food delightfully tasty. Also experience the authentic coconut-oil processing and learn to make a simple offering.

    At noon, sit down and enjoy a delicious lunch of your creations. You will be met by your local guide and driver to visit the Rudana Art Museum where you will learn more about the symbolism and cosmology of this impressive building and the collection of fine arts within on a private walking tour by resident expert at the museum. This afternoon there is time to wander the eclectic streets of Ubud at your leisure before transferring back to your Resort. Enjoy a 1 hour spa treatment before a romantic candlelit 5 course dining experience at your Resort.

    Overnight:Kayumanis Ubud

  • Day 8

    Jimbaran or Nusa Dua - Seminyak - Jimbaran or Nusa Dua

    This morning take a leisurely bicycle ride through the quiet villages of the Bongkasa. This easy ride encompasses terracing rice fields, cacao plantations and small village roads. Return to your hotel to freshen and check out before travelling to the southern beaches of Bali. Arrive to your 200m2 Villa with private pool for an evening at leisure.

    Overnight: The Villas

  • Day 9

    Day at Leisure

    Enjoy breakfast in the privacy of your Villa. Today you are at leisure to do as little or as much as you like.

    Overnight: The Villas

  • Day 10

    Jimbaran or Nusa Dua - Denpasar – Departure

    This morning is free at leisure until transferring to Denpasar International Airport for your departure flight.

LUXURY HONEYMOON IN CAMBODIA – TEMPLE & PRIVATE ISLAND

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 Days

    Highlights:Siem Reap - Song Saa Island offers famous of Angkor and the less known Beng Mealea, Exploring the Tonle Sap lake, known as the Great Lake or sea of fresh water & Relax at beautiful beach Sang saa Island.

  • Day 1

    Arrival Siem Reap

    Upon arrival you will be met by an A&K Representative with A&K Signage and your name in the restricted area. He will assist you with VISA ON ARRIVAL application rapid transit through immigration to collect your checked luggage and custom clearance. A&K representative will complete all necessary forms on your behalf.

    After clearing all entrance formalities, met and greeted by your local English speaking guide and thereafter transferred by a private vehicle to your deluxe hotel for check-in. The transfer time is approximately 20 minutes.

    "Nestled between rice paddies and stretched along a river, this former sleepy riverside provincial town of Siem Reap serves as the gateway to the ancient magnificent Angkor and other ruins which formerly the seat of power for the once vast and glorious Khmer empire through the 9th and 15th centuries AD. With a distinct colonial and Chinese style of architecture this town emanated from a cluster of old villages, but today is alive with culture, restaurants and pubs. Siem Reap is the hub for the Angkor Archaeological Park, Designated a World Heritage Site by UNESCO, which encompasses dozens of temple ruins including the legendary Angkor Wat whose archaeological significance and visual impact sets it amongst other wonders of the world, such as the Pyramids, Machu Pichu and the Taj Mahal."

    Balance of the day is free at your own leisure.

    Overnight Stay: Amansara Resort

  • Day 2

    Siem Reap// Visit Angkor Thom, Bayon Temple & Tonle Sap Lake

    Breakfast at the hotel

    Begin your touring this morning at Angkor Thom, the heavily fortified city built in the 10th century by the greatest of the Khmer rulers, King Jayavarman VII (1181 - 1201). With a population in excess of one million inhabitants, Angkor Thom covered an area of 10 square kilometres and was larger than any city in Europe at the time.

    From Angkor Thom's southern gateway, continue to visit the Bayon Temple. The structure is staggering in its construction and most Angkor specialists agree that it is one of the finest examples of classic Khmer architecture. Its third level is stunning; its 49 towers are adorned with 172 smiling faces of the omnipresent Avalokitesvara. Constructed during the reign of King Jayavarman VII, this temple marks the exact centre of Angkor Thom, thus it is the most venerated of the city's temples and also the most mysterious. Set off on an elephant back riding around Bayon temple before exploring the mega building led by your expert guide.

    Leaving the Bayon take a brief stop at the Royal Enclosures to see the Elephant Terrace, the terrace takes its name from the exceptional depiction of elephants and of an elephant hunt, which takes up the majority of the frieze. A little north of the main terrace is another, known as the Terrace of the Leper King after a statue found above it.

    The remaining of the morning, you will be visiting a unique jungle temple which is captivated by the large trees and vines that have grown over it. Ta Prohm was built by King Jayavarman VII (1181-1219) as a Mahayana Buddhist monastery and university. Ta Phrom is, for the majority of visitors, the most romantic experience of Angkor and even Historians have noted its mystical charm. As a result, its photogenic and atmospheric combination of trees growing out of the ruins made such unique and exotic filming backdrop for Hollywood blockbuster, “Tomb Raider”.

    This afternoon, you will be exploring the area with a boat trip on the immense 87 mile long Tonle Sap Lake, also known as the ‘Great Lake’ or ‘sea of fresh water', which lies south of Angkor.

    The Tonle Sap River links the Mekong River to the southern end of the Great Lake. An unusual characteristic of this river is that its water flow changes direction to run in reverse; the source of this phenomenon is in the Himalayan Mountains of Central Asia. When the snows melt the waters descend southward along the course of the Mekong River and its tributaries, passing through China, Laos, Thailand, Cambodia and finally Vietnam. The silted channels of the Mekong River system are insufficient to accommodate the amount of water sent forth by the melting snows and when the Mekong backs up, the impact of the overflow forces the Tonle Sap River to reverse its course each year between July and October and feed into the Great Lake, more than doubling the size of the lake to 1,158 square miles.

    Board a local boat and cruise pass floating houses, fish farms and portraying life on the water that dots the landscape. It get any better than this experience to understand rural Cambodia and also gain a better understanding of a unique ecosystem rich with birds, fish and other wildlife.

    Back to the town, observing the students practicing age old carving and finishing techniques at Chantiers Ecoles (Artisan D'Angkor)- A local woodworking and stone carving workshop/gallery which aims to reintegrate young Cambodians from rural areas to the creation of traditional cultural artworks. This vocational center trains 650 apprentices each year including 160 in fine arts and crafts trades united in the “Artisan d Angkor” production network.

    Overnight Stay:Amansara Resort

  • Day 3

    Siem Reap// Visit jungle temple of Beng Mealea & visit the “crowning jewel” of Khmer

    Breakfast at the hotel

    This morning, drive through local villages and paddy fields explore a remote jungle temple ofBeng Mealea, located about 75 Km from Siem Reap town, and takes almost 02-hour drive. Beng Mealea is now utterly subsumed by jungle. It was built during the reign of king Suryavarman II (1113-1150). There is extensive carving of scenes from Hindu mythology, including the Churning of the Sea of Milk and Vishnu being borne by the bird god Garuda. Enjoy a short walk through the dense jungle and feel like an explorer when you discover the completely overgrown temple. The remains of Boeng Mealea, which are still partly buried under vegetation, consist of perfectly squared-off sandstone building blocks. The outstanding decoration dates from the first half of the 12th century.

    Move on to visit the “crowning jewel” of Khmer architecture and one of Asia's most magnificent archaeological ruins - the religious citadels of Angkor Wat until sunset. Constructed under the reign of Suryavarman II (1113-1150), Angkor Wat is one of the largest religious structures in the world and also the national symbol of Cambodia as you may see on the Cambodian national flag, covering an area 480 acres encircled by an enclosure wall nearly four miles long. Angkor Wat is a representation of the Hindu cosmology and is dedicated to the god Vishnu. The five towers of Angkor Wat are the representation of the five peaks of Mount Meru, home of the gods and centre of the Hindu universe. The temple’s greatest sculptural treasure is its 1 mile long bas-relief that wraps around the walls of the outer gallery along with the 2000 figures of Khmer Devas and Apsaras. Surprisingly, it was finished in less than 40 years by innumerable laborers, master builders and stonemasons. The king actually lived to see it finished. Angkor Wat is stunning at any time of the day, but sunrise and sunset are especially beautiful times to watch the play of light on the stones.

    Overnight: Amansara Resort

  • Day 4

    Siem Reap//Visit Kbal Spean, Banteay Srei, Landmine Museum and Banteay Samre

    Full day visit to Kbal Spean, Banteay Srei, Landmine Museum and Banteay Samre with lunch box

    After breakfast at hotel you will be transferred to Kbal Spean, about 49 km from Raffles Grand Hotel. Kbal Spean ("Bridge Head") is an Angkorian era archaeological site on the southwest slopes of the Kulen Hills to the northeast of Angkor in Siem Reap District, Siem Reap Province, Cambodia. It is situated along a 50 metres (160 ft) stretch of the Stung Kbal Spean River, 25 kilometres (16 mi) from the main Angkor group of monuments. The site consists of a series of stone carvings in sandstone formations carved in the river bed and banks.It is commonly known as the "Valley of a 1000 Lingas" or "The River of a Thousand Lingas". The motifs for stone carvings are mainly myriads of lingams (phallic symbol of Hindu god Shiva), depicted as neatly arranged bumps that cover the surface of a sandstone bed rock, and lingam-yoni designs. There are also various Hindu mythological motifs, including depictions of the gods Shiva, Vishnu, Brahma, Lakshmi, Rama, and Hanuman, as well as animals (cows and frogs).

    Lunch box or lunch at local restaurant (included foods, local bottled water, tea / coffee, soft drinks)

    On the way back visit Banteay Srei : It loosely translates to ‘citadel of the women’, but this is a modern appellation that probably refers to the delicate beauty of the carvings. Built at a time when the Khmer Empire was gaining significant power and territory, the temple was constructed by a Brahmin counselor under a powerful king, Rajendravarman and later under Jayavarman V. Banteay. The walls are densely covered with some of the most beautiful, deep and intricate carvings of any Angkorian temple. The temple's relatively small size, pink sandstone construction and ornate design give it a fairyland ambiance. This temple was discovered by French archaeologists in late 1914.

    Then continue to visit Landmine museum : Founded in 1997 by ex-child soldier Aki Ra, the Cambodia Landmine Museum tells the story of his rise from a 10 year old fighter in the Khmer Rouge to his present place as an internationally recognized hero. The Museum tells the story of landmines in Cambodia and the country's continuing efforts to rid itself of the aftermath of over 35 years of warfare. Here you can see (defused and safe) landmines up close, find out how they work and how you can help Cambodia and the rest of the world in the on-going efforts of clearing these weapons from the face of the earth. In 2010 Aki Ra was chosen by CNN as one of its Top 10 Heroes of the Year. To conclude the sightseeing with Banteay Samre Temple: Banteay Samre temple is about 400 meters east of East Baray, about 2 kilometers from Pradak village and south of the road from Pradak to Phnom Bok. The temple was built in the middle of the 12th century by King Suryavar-man II, it is dedicated to Visnu Brahmaninsm. The proportions of Banteay Samre are splendid. A unique feature is an interior moat with laterite paving, which when filled with water must have given an ethereal atmosphere to the temple. All of the structures around the moat are on a raised base with horizontal moldings, decorated in some areas with figures framed by lotus buds.

    Return to hotel. Dinner on own

    Overnight Stay:Amansara Resort

  • Day 05-07

    Siem Reap// Phnom Penh//Sihanoukville

    Breakfast at the hotel, transfer to Siem Reap airport for your short flight to Phnom Penh. The flight time is 45 minutes.

    Siem Reap / Phnom Penh on K6 100 (08.50 a.m / 09.35 a.m) OR (K6 102 (11.35 am / 12.20 pm)

    Cambodia Angkor Air (K6) is monopoly for domestic flights in Cambodia. This airline uses French-Italian ATR turboprop planes. The configuration is 70-seats (ATR 72) in rows of 4 seats with a middle aisle. Entry-exit is at the back of the plane and standard one-class configuration.

    Once considered as the most beautiful of the French built cities of Indochina, Phnom Penh's charm has managed to survive its tumultuous past. A stroll through the leafy suburbs will find beautiful wide boulevards, sprawling colonial buildings, pavement restaurants, glittering palaces and pagodas, vibrant markets, and a bustling riverfront that made it the Paris of the East before 1970.

    On arrival at Phnom Penh airport, meet our guide and transfer them to a local restaurant for lunch After lunch transfer to Sihanoukville. The drive will take about 3 hours and a half. On arrival take A speed boat of Song Saa Island to the island

    Overnight Stay: Song Saa Island

    **There is a direct flight from Siem Reap to Sihanoukville 3 times per week: K6 # 131 1530 / 1630 Every Monday, Wednesday and Friday

  • Day 8

    Sihanoukville//Phnom Penh//Departure

    Our guide will pick them up from the port at Sihanoukville and transfer by overland to Phnom Penh for the departure flight.

    Dinner at your own account

    Meals Breakfast

LUXURY HONEYMOON IN SRI LANKA

Programme Overview

  • Day 2

    Kotugoda to Sigiriya // Visit to Pinnawela Elephant Orphanage

    After breakfast transfer from Kotugoda to Sigiriya (approximately 175 Kilometres / 4.5 hours) en-route visit the Pinnawela Elephant Orphanage.

    PINNAWELA - The Pinnawela Elephant Orphanage is one of Sri Lanka's most popular attractions. It is situated halfway between Colombo and Kandy and the orphanage has about 75 elephants, making it the world's largest collection of captive elephants. The elephants here range from newborn calves to elderly matriarchs and include orphaned and abandoned elephants, as well as those injured in the wild. Amongst them are its famous residents, Sama, the lame elephant injured by a land mine during the ethnic conflict in the North and Raja, the blind elephant. Experience the sheer delight of watching these great pachyderms frolic in the river at bath time or the baby elephants being fed twice a day.

    On completion continue with the journey the hotel and check-in at the hotel. Rest of the day is at leisure.

  • Day 3

    Sigiriya//Visit the ruins in Polonnaruwa

    After breakfast climb Sigiriya Rock Fortress and thereafter visit the ruins in Polonnaruwa. Return to the hotel for overnight stay

    SIGIRIYA - Thought to be one of the oldest tourist attractions in the world, and declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1982, Sigiriya offers its visitors nothing less than a breathtaking experience. Standing more than 200m tall over the surrounding plains, the rock is said to be a solidified magma plug, from a long eroded and extinct volcano.

    On its summit are the remains of a palace, built (by King Kasyapa) in the 5th century AD. Hidden in a cave along the citadel are some of the most magnificent ancient frescoes in South Asia. Seen today are, what’s said to be left of the 500 paintings of maidens that once covered a large area of the face of the rock. The Mirror wall that leads up to the Lion staircase, its brick masonry wall built of Lime plaster was said to have been so well polished, that it reflected the paintings of the opposite rock wall. Today, 15 centuries later the shine on this wall can still be seen. A total of nearly 1500 writings on the wall, include verses that date way back as far as the 8th century, most of which have now been deciphered

    On completion, proceed to Polonnaruwa and visit the ruins of Polonnaruwa

    POLONNARUWA - The second most ancient of Sri Lankas Kingdoms, today the city of Polonnaruwa remains one of the best planned archeological relic sites in the country, standing testimony to the discipline and greatness of the Kingdoms first rulers. Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1982, its beauty was used as a backdrop to filmed scenes for the Duran Duran music video Save a Prayer. Its green environment, amazing ancient constructions (including a huge lake built in 1200A.D.), good hotels and its warm, friendly people make it an attractive city to visit.

    The Polonnaruwa Visitor Information Centre and Museum, brings this ancient city to life. A visit to the Centre and Museum first, will prove useful before setting out to explore its fascinating sites.

    On completion, return to the hotel.

    Overnight: Vil Uyana (Paddy or Marsh Dwelling) / Ulagalla Wallawwa (Ulagalla Chalet) / Cinnamon Lodge (Deluxe Room)

  • Day 4

    Sirgiya//Kandy//Visit the Dambulla Cave Temple

    After breakfast transfer Kandy (approximately 95 kilometres / 2.5 hours) en-route visit the Dambulla Cave Temple.

    RDAMBULLASTR - The city is home to one of Sri Lankas seven UNESCO World Heritage Sites; the richly painted cave temple complex, is the islands largest and best preserved. Though there are more than 80 documented caves in the area, the most significant are spread over 5 caves, which comprise of statues and paintings related to Lord Buddha and his life.

    The latest archeological site of significant historical importance, the Ibbankatuwa Prehistoric Burial Site, is also found in this great city, within 3 km of the cave temple. At this site, bones of 2700 year old human skeletons were found, which according to scientific analysis, is evidence of civilisations existing long before the arrival of the Indians to the island.

    Ancient history aside, Dambulla is home to the Rangiri Dambulla International Cricket Stadium, well known for being built in just 167 days. Its Iron Wood forest (Namal Uyana) and the largest Rose Quartz mountain range in South Asia make this city special.

    Continue with the journey to Kandy.

    Proceed to the village of Inpawkhone to observe the traditional silk weaving techniques of Inthar people. Inpawkhone is also known for its weaving from the stems of lotus flowers, a time consuming process that results in high quality materials famous throughout the country. We also visit a cheroot factory where local Burmese cigars are rolled by hand.

    Overnight: Kandy House (Deluxe Room) / Theva Residency (Deluxe Room) / Clingendael

  • Day 5

    Kandy// visit the Royal Botanical Garden & temple of the tooth

    After breakfast do a tour of Kandy including visits to the Royal Botanical Gardens and Temple of the Tooth

    KANDY - Locally referred to as Maha Nuwara, meaning The Great City, Kandy is the Hill Capital and one of the most scenic cities in the country. It is also home to one of the most revered places of the Buddhist community in Sri Lanka, and around the world. The sacred Tooth Relic of the Buddha, is housed in the temple, known as the Dalada Maligawa. In one of Asia’s most celebrated festivals, The Esala (Kandy) Perahera (held in July/August), where the inner caskets housing the sacred tooth relic, is taken through the streets in a grand procession of dancers, drummers, torch bearers and over a hundred beautifully costumed elephants. Steeped in history and located at the foothills of the central highlands, the city was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1988.

    Its not only its places of historical significance that make Kandy a popular tourist destination, but also the shops displaying precious and semi-precious stones, skilfully crafted jewellary, handicrafts, colourful batiks and silks; and of course there is the expansive Peradeniya Botanical Gardens, the largest and finest in Sri Lanka - a must do.

    Overnight: Kandy House (Deluxe Room) / Theva Residency (Deluxe Room) / Clingendael
