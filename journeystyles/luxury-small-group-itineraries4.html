BHUTAN & NEPAL: HEART OF THE HIMALAYAS

Programme Overview

Discover Bhutan and Nepal, seeing Tiger's Nest Monastery; taking a rickshaw ride to Durbar Square; and embarking on an elephant-back safari in Chitwan National Park.

  • Duration: 13 Days / 12 Nights

    Highlights: Bhutan | Nepal

    A&K Advantages

    • Spectacular Tiger's Nest Monastery
    • Ride like a Local in rickshaw to Durbar Square and Teleju Temple
    • Elephant Safari at Chitwan National Park
    • Chef's Table experience in the home of a resident
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Delhi, India

    Arrive in Delhi, where you will be received by an A&K representative who will drive you to your airport hotel.

    Overnight: JW Marriott Aerocity
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    THIMPHU, BHUTAN

    On arrival at The Paro International Airport, you will be received by an A&K representative with a tashi khadda who will assist you to your custom built four-wheel-drive vehicle for transfer to Thimphu.

    En route to your hotel, visit the National Memorial Chorten and the holy Tashichho Dzong, a Buddhist monastery and fortress.

    Spend a relaxed evening at the hotel complemented with a traditional local dinner.

    Overnight: Taj Tashi
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    THIMPHU

    After a hearty breakfast, depart for the National Textile Museum and peek into Bhutan’s traditional art of weaving which is an integral part of its culture and heritage. The museum boasts an invaluable collection of antique textile art facts of the country.

    For all the booklovers, explore the National Library which boasts an extensive collection of Buddhist literature where you can also find the world’s biggest published book which weighs 68kilograms and is over two meters tall.

    Overnight: Taj Tashi
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    PUNAKHA

    This morning, you will be driven to the Punakha Dzong via Dochula Pass in your private vehicle.

    Take a brief stop for a tempting local lunch and then, continue the journey to the mystical Chimi Lhakhang, located at the periphery of fertile rice fields, flanked by hundreds of colorful praying flags.

    In the evening return to Thimphu for a toothsome dinner.

    Overnight: Taj Tashi
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    PARO

    Rise early this morning and drive to Paro to catch a glimpse of the religious thangka paintings at the National Museum. Walk through the gallery and see Bhutan’s transition from Stone Age to a modern Mahayanist Buddhist and multi-cultural kingdom.

    After lunch, continue to Rinpung Dzong, a monastery and fortress along a mountain river.

    Enjoy a tranquil and comfortable evening over dinner.

    Overnight: Zhiwa Ling Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    PARO

    After a continental breakfast this morning, hike to Taktsang- Tiger’s Nest Monastery, perched precariously on the side of a mountain with praying flags all around, as you ascend and spend the day with the monks at this magnificent monastery.

    In the evening, visit the ruins of Drukgyel Dzong and Kyichu Lhakhang, one of Bhutan’s most sacred shrines.

    Call it a day with an unperturbed evening at a popular restaurant over a lip smacking local dinner.

    Overnight: Zhiwa Ling Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    KATHMANDU, NEPAL

    This morning, you will be assisted with your flight to Kathmandu.

    On arrival, you will be escorted to your accommodation by an A&K representative.

    In the afternoon, after a great Nepalese lunch, take a rickshaw ride to Durbar Square, a UNESCO world heritage site where you visit the well conserved Teleju Temple, The House of the Living Goddess (Kumari) and stroll through the bazaar and Swayambhunath.

    Overnight: Dwarika’s Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    KATHMANDU

    This morning, explore Bungamati, Rato Machhendranath Temple and the Lokeshwar Shrine.

    In the afternoon, visit a Nepalese family where you get the opportunity to attend a cooking class of the local cuisine.

    In the evening, visit Patan, Durbar Square, the Royal Baths, Golden Temple and Krishna Mandir.

    Overnight: Dwarika’s Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 9

    CHITWAN NATIONAL PARK

    This morning, you will be escorted for your flight to Bharatpur. After settling in, set out on an exciting elephant-back safari.

    Conclude your day with a briefing from a resident naturalist at your jungle lodge followed by a delicious supper.

    Overnight: Barahi Jungle Lodge
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 10

    CHITWAN NATIONAL PARK

    Rise early this morning. After a mouthwatering breakfast, take a nature walk with trained guides at the Chitwan National Park and spot some rare wild animals.

    Later in the afternoon, race your canoe down the Rapti River, and witness the beautiful sun drown into the waters.

    Overnight: Barahi Jungle Lodge
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 11

    KATHMANDU

    This morning, you will be driven for your flight to Kathmandu.

    The day is at leisure.

    In the evening, observe a Nepalese folk dance performance during dinner.

    Overnight: Dwarika’s Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 12

    KATHMANDU

    This morning, wake up to a rich Nepalese breakfast. Witness the monks debate at the Kopan Nunnery. Visit the royal city of Bhaktapur, Pashupatinath and Boudhanath, where a local artisan will share his or her experience with A&K guests.

    Spend your last night in Kathmandu interacting with the fellow guests over a scrumptious farewell dinner.

    Overnight: Dwarika’s Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 13

    DEPART

    After a wonderful breakfast, you will be helped out with your transfer to the airport for your onward flight through Delhi, or continue on an extension tour to Lhasa.

    Overnight: JW Marriott Aerocity
    Meal plan: B

  • Lhasa Post-Tour Extension

    Day 1

    ARRIVE LHASA

    You will be aided with your flight from Kathmandu to Lhasa.

    This day is at leisure.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    LHASA

    This day, explore Potala Palace, former home of the Dalai Lama and visit the Jokhang Temple to join monks as they circumambulate the temple.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 3

    LHASA

    Spend this day at two of Tibet’s great monasteries, Drepung and Sera.

    The evening is at leisure. You may choose to relax at your luxurious accommodation or head out to explore this magnificent city.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    DEPART LHASA

    This morning you will be assisted with your transfers for your flight back home.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B


    Available on the Apr 2, Apr 16, Jun 4, Sep 3, Sep 10, Sep 17, Oct 1 and Oct 15 departures.

Classic Japan

Programme Overview

Gain an in-depth perspective on Japanese culture with lengthy stays in Tokyo and Kyoto. Participate in a Shinto ceremony, receive a lesson in Zen meditation and enjoy entertainment by a geiko (geisha) as well as meiko (apprentice geisha).

  • Duration: 10 days/ 9 nights
    Highlights: Tokyo | Hakone | Kyoto

    A&K Advantages

    • Participate in a Shinto ceremony in the ancient Toshogu Shrine, experiencing Japan's religious rituals firsthand
    • Capture the spirit of Japan with lessons in Zen meditation and the ancient art of the traditional tea ceremony
    • Savor Kyoto's distinctive cuisine and traditions during an exclusive kaiseki banquet with entertainment by a geiko andmaiko (apprentice geisha)
    • Be among the few to stay at Gôra Kadan, one of the country's most exclusive ryokans (traditional inns)
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Tokyo

    Upon arrival in Japan’s exciting capital, Tokyo, you will be escorted to your hotel by an A&K representative. Settle in your hotel located in the upscale Ginza district.

    Overnight: Imperial Hotel

  • Day 2

    Tokyo

    Begin to explore Tokyo with a visit to the Imperial Palace Plaza, the spacious outer garden of the Imperial palace. Stroll through the grounds of Meiji- Jingu, a Shinto shrine dedicated to Emperor Meiji and his wife. Further, visit Asakusa Temple, Tokyo's oldest temple. Pause to look for souvenirs in the tiny shops of Nakamise-dori.

    This evening, join your fellow guests for a welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Grand Hyatt or Similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Tokyo

    Today, follow the Japanese saying: “Don’t say kekko (wonderful) without seeing Nikko.” Travel overland to Nikko National Park, one of Japan's most beautiful nature locales, located in the mountains north of Tokyo. Discover this exquisite region with marvelously carved 17th-century Toshogu Shrine. Later, experience the local culture in-depth as you participate in a special Shinto ceremony with a Shinto priest or miko (priestess).

    Overnight: Imperial Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    Tokyo

    Begin the morning with a guided tour of Ginza, a trendy neighborhood brimming with designer boutiques, chic restaurants and art galleries. Spend an afternoon at leisure and later, set off to explore Tokyo’s contemporary galleries, museums and fine restaurants.

    Overnight: Imperial Hotel
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 5

    Hakone

    This day, travel to Kamakura, where you view the famed Great Buddha at Kotoku-in, an iconic Japanese statue standing over 37 feet tall. Then, visit the unique Hase Kannon Temple, appreciate its flower-filled garden, which is unlike the usual Japanese gardens.

    This afternoon, enjoy your lunch before you travel to explore the forested slopes of Hakone National Park.

    Later, cruise through Lake Ashi, continue with a gondola ride on the Komagatake Ropeway to spectacle a panoramic view of Mount Fuji. This evening, enjoy the hospitality of Gôra Kadan, a top-rated traditional inn. Here, you savor the kaiseki cuisine, a traditional multicourse Japanese meal, in its most authentic way.

    Overnight: Gora Kadan
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    Kyoto

    Explore like a local, as you board the bullet train for Kyoto, once Japan’s capital. Kyoto boasts of more than 2,000 temples and shrines, including 17 UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

    Experience Japan’s true cultural, spiritual and artistic heritage in Kyoto. Begin with a walk around the 1,001 statues of Kannon at Sanjusangendo Temple. Later, attend a lesson in Zen meditation at Kodai-ji followed by a tea ceremony. Unwind with a drink at a local izakaya (Japanese pub) in the evening.

    Overnight: Kyoto Hotel Okura
    Meal plan:B, D

  • Day 7

    Kyoto

    Begin early today, travel to the iconic Zen rock garden, strategically placed in carefully raked white sand at Ryoan-ji. Continue to view the dazzling Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), a Zen Buddhist temple.

    Further, visit ancient Nara, Japan’s first capital. Explore the Nara Park, home to over a thousand tame deer. Proceed to view the world’s largest wooden structure and Japan’s largest bronze Buddha at the eighth-century temple complex, Todai-ji. Later, travel back to Kyoto.

    Overnight: Kyoto Hotel Okura
    Meal plan:B, L

  • Day 8

    Kyoto

    This morning, visit Kiyomizu Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Be engulfed by the splendid panoramic views of the city from the temple’s massive veranda. Walk along the Gojozaka Hills, an area renowned for its pottery studios. Continue for a visit to the home and studio of late Kawai Kanjiro, one of Kyoto’s most famous potters. Resembling a country house, Kanjiro’s beautiful and unique home is filled with his own creations and his personal collection.

    This afternoon, relish a Japanese-style lunch and spend the remaining afternoon at leisure.

    Overnight: Kyoto Hotel Okura
    Meal plan:B, L

  • Day 9

    Kyoto

    Start the day with a guided walk through some of Kyoto’s market areas, including one of Japan's most iconic markets- the Nishiki market.

    The afternoon is for you to explore Japan at your own pace. You could perhaps shop for traditional arts and crafts, including textiles and ceramics.

    Tonight, conclude your journey with an exquisite kaiseki banquet. Appreciate geiko (as a geisha is known in Kyoto) and maiko dance performances, while you sing and pour sake.

    Overnight: Kyoto Hotel Okura
    Meal plan:B, D

  • Day 10

    Kyoto

    This day, you will be assisted with transfer to Kansai or Itami Airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

  • Hiroshima by Bullet Train Post-Tour Extension


    Day 1

    Arrive Hiroshima

    Travel to Hiroshima from Kyoto by a bullet train.

    After arrival, enjoy your lunch before you take a tour of the Peace Memorial Park and Museum, dedicated to the legacy of Hiroshima as the first city in the world to suffer a nuclear attack. Later, you will be assisted with a check into your hotel.

    Overnight: Rhiga Royal Hotel Hiroshima
    Meal plan: L

  • Day 2

    Hiroshima

    This day, travel to the island of Itsukushima and view its Itsukushima Shrine, a Shinto shrine best known for its "floating" torii gate.

    Ferry the Inland Sea, and visit Daishoin Temple, a historic Japanese temple followed by a museum visit.

    Overnight: Rhiga Royal Hotel Hiroshima
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 3

    Hiroshima

    Begin with a tour of Iwakuni Town, cross Kintaikyo (Abacus) bridge to reach the Iwakuni Castle. Further, visit the White Snake Museum. Later, travel back to Hiroshima and transfer to Osaka.

    Overnight: Imperial Hotel Osaka
    Meal plan: B ,D

  • Day 4

    Depart Osaka

    Today, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

Egypt & the Nile

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 Days/ 9 Nights

    Cruise in luxury on Sanctuary 'Sun Boat III,' travelling to off-the-beaten-path Denderah; exploring Egypt's pyramids from the inside out; and sailing the Nile in a felucca (sail boat).
    Highlights: Tokyo | Hakone | Kyoto

    A&K Advantages

    • Sail on a four-night Nile river cruise on board the luxurious and intimate Sanctuary 'Sun Boat III,' travelling to off-thebeaten- path Denderah, one of Egypt's best-preserved and less-frequented temples
    • Enjoy guaranteed entry into one of Egypt's legendary pyramids, which you literally explore from the inside out
    • Ride Like a Local on camel back in Giza and sail the Nile in a felucca (sail boat)
    • Learn to make an Egyptian delicacy on board 'Sun Boat III' as part of a Chef's Table culinary experience
    • Stay at the Palatial Mena House Hotel, with views of the Pyramids of Giza from your own private balcony, and at the luxurious Four Seasons Hotel Cairo at Nile Plaza in a Nile View Room
    • Avoid crowds and the hassle of navigating around other ships, with private docking facilities in Luxor and Aswan
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Cairo

    After your arrival in Cairo, you are met by your A&K representative and escorted to your hotel with splendid views of the pyramids, which you explore tomorrow.

    Overnight: Mena House Hotel

  • Day 2

    Giza

    Begin your day to explore the Great Sphinx and the pyramids of Cheops, Chephren and Mycerinus at Giza Plateau. Explore the interior of one of these legendary pyramids

    Ride on a camelback like a Local before you continue to visit the Solar Boat Museum, where you view Khufu's solar boat remains, the most spectacular of all Egyptian boats.

    Celebrate this evening with fellow guests at the festive welcome dinner, hosted by A&K.

    Overnight: Mena House Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Cairo

    Today, visit the Egyptian Museum of Antiquities, exhibits the world's most extensive collection of pharaonic antiquities, including artifacts dating back to 4000 BC and the famous Mummy Room, featuring the remains of ancient Egypt’s pharaohs and nobility.

    Savor lunch before you begin with your drive to Memphis, capital of the ancient kingdom and now home to an open-air museum and giant statue of Ramses II. Then, visit Sakkara, home to hundreds of tombs and 14 pyramids, including the famous 204 feet high Step Pyramid of Djoser.

    Overnight: Mena House Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    Luxor

    This day, fly to Luxor, where you visit the Temple of Karnak, the largest in Egypt, comprising of a vast mix of decayed temples, chapels, pylons, and other buildings. Then, walk through the Great Hypostyle Hall to view its skillfully aligned columns.

    Later, embark the luxurious Sanctuary ‘Sun Boat III’ for your cruise along the verdant Nile. After lunch on board begin to cruise the west bank of the Nile. Visit the Necropolis of Thebes, the burial site of the Great Pharaohs of the New Kingdom. Continue to discover the Valley of the Kings, the legendary burial ground of the pharaohs. Step inside the tomb of Tutankhamun, to view its interiors.

    Later, visit the charming Temple of Hatshepsut, ancient Egypt’s first female pharaoh.

    Overnight: Sanctuary 'Sun Boat III'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Cruising the Nile

    Continue with your journey and cruise along the scenic route to the Temple of Hathor at Denderah, one of the best-preserved temples in Egypt. Behold the sight of this complex, complete with a massive stone roof, dark chambers, underground passages and columns inscribed with hieroglyphs. Later, return to your deluxe Nile cruiser and sail back to Luxor.

    This afternoon, journey once again to explore the east bank of the Nile to visit the city of Thebes and the striking Temple of Luxor. Along the front of the temple pylons, view the three remaining enormous statues of Ramses II, while behind the East Tower resides the Courtyard of Ramses II. Meanwhile, the Colonnade of Amenhotep III contains 14 papyrus-headed columns, some fully 50 feet in height and 32 feet in circumference, leading to the Court of Amenhotep III.

    Please note for November 17, 2014 – February 2015 Departures:

    Due to unexpected low water levels of the Nile River, the Sanctuary Sun Boats will not be able to sail north of the city of Luxor. To ensure the safety of all guests on board, we will be driving to Denderah for the scheduled sightseeing. The drive time between Luxor and Denderah is approximately 1 hour.

    Overnight: Sanctuary 'Sun Boat III'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    Cruising the Nile

    Continue with your cruise to Edfu and visit the Temple of Horus, considered the best-preserved cult temple in Egypt. Afterward, join your chef onboard for a local cooking lesson.

    Later, disembark at Kom Ombo (hill of gold), dedicated to Sobek the crocodile god, and Horus the falcon-headed god. The temple was so dedicated because crocodiles once basked on this stretch of Nile shore; inside, find several of these mummified crocodiles on display.

    Overnight: Sanctuary 'Sun Boat III'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Aswan

    Begin the day with a cruise to Aswan, continue aboard a motorized boat to Philae Temple on the island of Agilika, relocated here to avoid flood damage from Aswan’s High Dam. Further, visit the granite quarries and view the Unfinished Obelisk, a fine example of Egypt’s stonemasons.

    Conclude the day with a sail through the Aswan islands (weather permitting) in a felucca (small sailboat).

    Overnight: Sanctuary 'Sun Boat III'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    Abu Simbel

    This day, disembark ‘Sun Boat III’ and depart for Abu Simbel.

    After arrival, discover the two historic temples built during the reign of Ramses II, viewing statues of Ra-Harakhte, Ptah, Amun-Ra and Ramses II himself. All four towering sculptures are an impressive sight to watch, as each statue is nearly 70 feet tall; but perhaps more astonishing is the fact that this entire site was disassembled and moved to its present location to save it from the floodwaters of the Aswan High Dam. Afterward, return to Cairo for an afternoon at leisure.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Cairo at Nile Plaza
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 9

    Cairo

    Today, visit Khan el-Khalili Bazaar, a major souk in the Islamic district of Cairo. Stroll through the tightly packed stalls displaying everything from spices and leather goods to jewelry and vegetables, and all on a street that has hardly changed for centuries. Bargaining is expected, so prepare to negotiate. Further, visit the Mosque and Madrasa of Sultan Hassan, built in AD 1356, considered stylistically the most compact and unified of all Cairo monuments, its massive Mamluk-era structure is designed with an egg-shaped dome, intricate details and soaring minarets.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Cairo at Nile Plaza
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 10

    Depart Cairo

    After breakfast, transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

  • Jordan Post-Tour Extension

    Day 1

    ARRIVE AMMAN

    After your arrival in Amman, begin with a city tour to view the Archaeological Museum and Roman Theater. Further, visit Jerash, the 3rd-century “Pompeii of the Middle East,” with its well-preserved Roman ruins. Later, travel back to your hotel.

    Overnight: InterContinental Amman Hotel
    Meal plan: L

  • Day 2

    PETRA

    Today, explore Madaba — the beautiful “City of Mosaics, one of the oldest preserved mosaic map, composed of two million pieces of colored stone, a serving guide to Jerusalem’s layout after its destruction and rebuilding in AD 70. Further, visit Mount Nebo before taking a dip in the Dead Sea.

    Overnight: Mövenpick Petra
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 3

    PETRA

    Today, discover the mysteriously charming city of Petra. Admire the rock-carved rose-red city with rock-cut structures sculpted from mountains. Continue to view the baths, markets, treasury, tombs and temples

    Overnight: Mövenpick Petra
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    AMMAN

    Travel to Wadi Rum, the Valley of the Moon, offering an astounding desert scenery view. Admire the views of rose-tinted mountains and desert vistas.

    Overnight: InterContinental Amman Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    DEPART AMMAN

    This day, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Please Note: The Sanctuary Sun Boat IV will be used instead of Sanctuary Sun Boat III on the January 9 and February 13 departures.

China: Land of Legends

Programme Overview

From the timeless Great Wall to the birthplace of kung fu, immerse yourself in the China of legend. Explore the Longmen Caves, visit pandas up close and view Xian's spectacular Terra Cotta Warriors.

  • Duration: 13 Days/ 12 Nights

    Highlights: Beijing | Luoyang | Luoyang | Xian | Chengdu | Guilin | Hong Kong

    A&K Advantages

    • Explore Shaolin Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and birthplace of the martial arts where you enjoy a thrilling private demonstration of Shaolin kung fu
    • Experience the hospitality of the region's best hotels when you stay at the Four Seasons Hotel Beijing and The Peninsula Hong Kong, both included on the Condé Nast Traveler Gold List for 2014
    • Ride Like a Local on a pedicab through Beijing's hutongs
    • Drift leisurely on a bamboo raft down the most picturesque part of the Li River
    • Go off the beaten track to the less-visited and best-preserved Mutianyu section of the Great Wall
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Beijing

    On your arrival in Beijing, capital of the People's Republic of China, you will be greeted by an A&K Representative, who assists you with transfer to the hotel.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Beijing

  • Day 2

    Beijing

    Start your day with an excursion to Mutianyu, where you explore one of the best-preserved sections of the Great Wall of China, a true wonder of the world. Admire the scenic surrounding countryside and later, enjoy lunch at Xiao Long Pu Restaurant. This afternoon, return to your hotel for time at leisure to unwind. Tonight, gather with fellow guests for cocktails followed by a welcome dinner at your hotel.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Beijing
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Beijing

    Kick start your day with a tai chi class, an internal Chinese martial art, taught by a master instructor at the ancient Temple of Heaven. An exquisite circular wooden pagoda, The Temple of Heaven, is the world’s single most beautiful example of Ming Dynasty (1368-1644) architecture.

    Further, ride like a local on a pedicab through a hutong, a vast yet intimate neighborhood of small winding alleyways that harbor a fast-vanishing way of life. After lunch at a local restaurant, set off to explore the historic Tiananmen Square, a large city square in the center of Beijing. Walk in the footsteps of emperors in the Forbidden City, China’s former imperial palace and now a vast museum housing treasures collected over the centuries by 24 emperors.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Beijing
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4-5

    Luoyang

    Savor a leisurely breakfast before you depart for Luoyang. Then, you are escorted to your hotel for the night.

    The following day, experience a fabled chapter of Chinese history come to life, as you visit famous Shaolin Temple and witness a thrilling private demonstration of Shaolin kung fu by resident monks. This Zen Buddhist monastery is considered the birthplace of kung fu, a profound martial arts system first formalized here in the sixth century by a Buddhist monk from India. Stroll outside the temple through the Pagoda Forest, a collection of the more than 200 stone and brick structures.

    This afternoon, lunch is served at a local restaurant. Later, visit the Songyang Academy of Classic Learning, renowned center of Confucian philosophy. Tonight, experience the colorful nightlife of Luoyang, as you dine at a local restaurant.

    Overnight: Lee Royal Mudu Hotel
    Meal plan: B, D (Day 4); B, L, D (Day 5)

  • Day 6

    Luoyang

    Today, travel outside Luoyang to observe the history and art of ancient China. Discover the spectacular Longmen Caves, one of the finest examples of Chinese Buddhist art. The 2,300 caves and niches here contain more than 100,000 stone statues carved between AD 493 and 1127. Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant before you travel to White Horse Temple, considered the first Buddhist monastery built in China.

    Later, board a high-speed train to Xian, once the last stop on the Great Silk Road and today a melting pot of Eastern and Middle Eastern cultures. Settle into your hotel and later, proceed to experience the local gastronomy with a dinner featuring far-flung locales cuisines.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel, Xian
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Xian

    Set off to explore the Terra Cotta Warriors, one of the greatest archaeological finds of the 20th century. This life-size army created to protect China’s first emperor in his afterlife is a testimony to the remarkable craftsmanship of their highly-skilled sculptors, as each sculpture is crafted with unique facial features.

    Further, indulge in a unique Chef’s Table experience that includes a private noodle-making demonstration in the fabulous Terra Cotta Museum restaurant. After lunch, enter the Ancient Gate of Xian’s impressive City Wall, where you may opt to cycle along its ramparts. This evening, return to your hotel with the remaining day at leisure.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel, Xian
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 8

    Chengdu

    This morning, catch an early flight to Chengdu, the charismatic capital of Sichuan Province. After arrival, you will be assisted with transfer to your hotel. Settle in your accommodation and unwind at leisure, perhaps by indulging in the many amenities of your accommodations.

    Later, visit the Chengdu Panda Breeding and Research Center, an A&K Philanthropy-supported project. Here, you get an unforgettable opportunity to come face to face with the living symbols of China, Chengdu Panda, roaming freely in their natural habitat.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel, Chengdu
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 9

    Chengdu

    Begin the day with a visit to Wenshu Temple, famous for its cultural relics, exquisite garden and solemn halls, is the best-preserved Buddhist temple in Chengdu. Continue to Jinli Street, “The First Street of the Shu Kingdom.” Originally built as a commercial street, Jinli Street was reconstructed in 2004 in the traditional architectural style of Sichuan Province. Here, you observe the making of traditional Chinese handicrafts and folk artisans entertain you with shadow puppetry or hand puppet shows.

    Savor lunch at a traditional restaurant and later, fly to Guilin. After arrival, proceed to your hotel for check in and enjoy the remaining day at leisure.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel, Guilin
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 10

    Guilin

    Drive to Yangdi, where you embark on a leisurely bamboo raft trip on the Li River. Descend towards Xingping, past gently waving bamboo groves and stunning limestone peaks. Observe life along the shore, where fishermen use cormorants to gather their catch and farmers still cultivate rice paddies by hand.

    After your arrival in Xingping, enjoy lunch at Yangshuo. Later, you may opt to ride a bicycle or golf cart through the tranquil countryside. Then, return to your hotel and proceed for diner at the Left Bank Café, a favorite among Guilin locals offering charming views of the Li River.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel, Guilin
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 11

    Hong Kong

    This day, travel to Elephant Trunk Hill, a symbol of the city of Guilin. This popular landmark got its name as the Elephant Trunk Hill because of its resemblance to the image of an elephant drinking water. Continue to Fubo Hill, a visually stunning rock formation that causes the river to eddy and thus is said to subdue waves. It is the site of Sword-Testing Rock, which legend holds was formed by a great general swinging his sword

    Later, explore the Thousand-Buddha Cave, a complex of Buddhist cave grottos filled with hundreds of stone-carved Buddha images.

    This afternoon, enjoy lunch at a local restaurant before discovering the gorgeous Reed Flute Cave, a natural cave carved out of the karst Limestone Mountains over millions of years. Marvel as lights installed in the cave play upon the wondrous shapes of stalagmites and stalactites.

    Depart for Hong Kong, and proceed to settle in your iconic hotel.

    Overnight: The Peninsula Hong Kong
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 12

    Hong Kong

    Explore Hong Kong with a visit to the Dried Food and Herbal Medicine Market in the Western District. Further, visit the incense-filled Man Mo Temple and then continue to Victoria Peak to enjoy a beautiful scenic view.

    Later, step abroad for a sampan ride through the floating villages of Aberdeen Harbor. Take in views of the picturesque harbour, the seafood markets, sampan rides and the Jumbo Floating Restaurant. Also observe the ebb and flow of everyday life of the Tanka community.

    This evening, conclude your journey with pre-dinner cocktail followed by a farewell dinner.

    Overnight: The Peninsula Hong Kong
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 13

    Depart Hong Kong

    After breakfast, you are assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B
