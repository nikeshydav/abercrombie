HONEYMOON IN FINLAND

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 Nights and 11 Days.

    Highlights: An extraordinary adventure in search of the mighty Aurora

    We have included a range of unique arctic experiences from Husky dogs, reindeer herding, ice fishing and snowmobile safaris

    In search for the Northern Lights in the arctic wilderness over ten nights & eleven days.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Finland

    Upon arrival into Kittilä Airport (main airport in Northern Finland), you will be welcomed by your specialist host, whom will look after you throughout your time in Finland. Leaving the airport behind, you will take a 15 minute drive into the arctic wilderness to Levi Fell. Check in to your log chalet and dinner catering. Snowshoeing, searching Northern Lights with Your guide.

    Dinner & overnight stay at the log hut.

    Loghut Information

    Type: log built cottage

    Built: 2008

    Area: downstairs 120 m2+ upstairs 40 m2

    Bedrooms/ alcoves/ other bed places: downstairs 1 bedroom, upstairs 2 bedrooms.

    Beds: 3 double beds.

    Toilets: 1 separate toilet, in bedroom toilet with shower, upstairs toilet with shower

    Sauna: electric sauna + 2 showers

    Equipment: dishes, fireplace / firewood, cable-tv, radio, CD/DVD-player, electric stove+oven, dishwasher, microwave oven, coffee maker, kettle, toaster, fridge, freezer,washing machine, electric clothes drying cupboard, iron, car heating point. Other things to be mentioned: Beautiful views far away. Good stairs. If a sky is clear you should see the Northern Lights.

    Meals: Dinner

  • Day 2

    Finland

    Awaking to the fresh arctic air, you will be served a delicious breakfast catering. After breakfast, it is time to head out and explore.

    Your guide will come and collect you and take you to the dog sledding. You will be taken to the kennels in order to meet your team of dogs who will take you on a dog sledding trip. You will be provided with all equipment, taught how to drive your team and then taken out in to the wilderness. At the beginning the dogs make a lot of noise, but soon it will be completely silent - the only sound is from the feet of the dogs hitting the snow!

    Meals: Breakfast

  • Day 3

    Finland

    Breakfast catering and full day snowmobile adventure in magical forest and landscapes. Evening back to log hut for overnight stay at the hotel.

    Meals: Breakfast

  • Day 4

    Finland

    After a hearty breakfast, visit at local village’s reindeer herder where you can have a reindeer ride and lunch at traditional building.

    Meals: Breakfast

  • Day 5

    Finland

    Morning after breakfast, time for leisure, skiing/ spa on your own

    Later, it is time to fire up the snowmobiles and head out into the arctic wilderness yet again to search Northern Lights.

    Meals: Breakfast

  • Day 6

    Finland

    Morning after breakfast, time for leisure, skiing/ spa on your own. it is time to fire up the snowmobiles and head out into the arctic wilderness yet again to search Northern Lights.

    Meals: Breakfast

  • Day 7

    Finland

    Morning breakfast at the log. Later, visit at MrRaekallio’s cabin in wilderness, full of his famous arctic paintings and artwork. The surroundings are stunning and you will be totally overwhelmed by the shear remoteness and beauty of the vast landscape.
    Traditional lunch.
    Time for leisure, skiing/ spa.

    Meals: Breakfast

  • Day 8

    Finland

    Morning breakfast at the log. It is time to try your hand at ice fishing with your local expert. Evening at leisure.

    Meals: Breakfast

  • Day 9

    Finland

    Morning breakfast at the log. Trip to Rovaniemi Arctic Circle, across the official line of Arctic Circle at Santa Claus Village. Evening back to log and time at leisure.

    Meals: Breakfast

  • Day 10

    Finland /Private Igloo site

    Morning breakfast at the log. Explore neighbor fells and wilderness on car with your guide. In the afternoon you will be arriving at a remote spot surrounded by a pine forest you will notice eight beautiful, romantic glass igloo’s. Located nearby the Chalet.These Igloo’s have been purposely built on the best site for viewing the northern lights. Dinner transfers. Spend the night in your private Igloo and gaze up at the wonderful spectacle of the northern lights!

  • Day 11

    Finland Departure

    After breakfast, check out and private transfer to Kittilä Airport to board flight way back home with pleasant memories of the trip.

FINE FOOD AND ART OF BERLIN

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 Days

    A luxurious 4 day tour of Berlin, capturing the main sights of this beautiful and historic city. From horse drawn carriage rides and food and arts tours to historic museums, this tour includes something special for everyone. You will also be treated to a complimentary upgrade*, free international phone calls and complimentary Wi-Fi at your hotel.

    *offer subject to availability
    Highlights: Brandenburg Gate, Kaiser Wilhelm Memorial Church, Charlottenburg Palace, Museum Island, Potsdamer Platz, Marheineke Halle, Tachles, Hackesche Hofe

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE BERLIN

    Upon arrival at Berlin airport you will be met in the arrivals hall by your driver and privately transferred to your hotel. Located on the historic Pariser Platz by Berlin's most famous sight, the Brandenburg Gate, and near to the elegant shops & boutiques of Friedrichstrasse and Museum Island, the recently redeveloped Hotel Adlon is the Grande Dame of Berlin hotels. The Adlon Kempinski also offers a wonderful spa with heated indoor pool, Jacuzzi, saunas, steam baths and a whole menu of different treatments to add that special touch to your honeymoon.

    During your stay you will be treated to a complimentary upgrade (subject to availability), free international phone calls so you can easily contact your loved ones back home, and complimentary Wi-Fi.

    overnight: Hotel Adlon Kempinski Berlin

  • Day 2

    BERLIN

    Today you will enjoy a half-day overview sightseeing tour (4 hours) with your private vehicle and local English-speaking guide giving you a wonderful introduction to the sights of Berlin. Begin your tour at the newly created Potsdamer Platz, dubbed "Europe's largest construction site" during the mid 1990s and see the nearby Checkpoint Charlie, the famous crossing point between East and West Berlin. Continue to the Kaiser Wilhelm Memorial Church, whose ruin stands as a dramatic reminder of World War II.

    Before you return back to your hotel enjoy a romantic horse drawn carriage ride, sipping a glass of champagne as you are carried along Unter den Linden, see the Gendarmenmarkt and return to Brandenburg Gate through Berlin's most beautiful alleys. (Warm blankets will be provided depending on the weather)

    This afternoon is at your leisure to relax and enjoy the facilities of your hotel.

    Alternatively you may like to visit the Charlottenburg Palace. Dating from 1695, the Palace was restored to its original elegance following World War II. The vast and beautiful palace is now home to the most richly decorated interiors and artifacts in Berlin.

    As another option you may enjoy a short walk to the Museum Island, for a visit to one of the world's greatest museums; the Pergamon Museum which houses a vast collection of ancient artifacts and antiquities. You may also like to visit Berlin Cathedral with its vast dome before walking back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Adlon Kempinski Berlin
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    BERLIN

    Today enjoy a full day with a guide and driver (8 hours) on a food & art tour of Berlin. Start with a visit to the Turkish market in Kreuzberg on the banks of the canal (Tuesday + Friday only).

    Alternatively, visit the Marheineke Halle, an indoor, organic food market also in Kreuzberg which sells flowers, meat, fruit and veg as well as typical Berlin snacks (open Monday - Saturday) You will also take a trip to the Bonbon Macherei, a traditional sweet making shop, in the Heckmann Höfe - one of the hidden courtyards in the Mitte district.

    Finally, pay a visit to 'KaDeWe' or Kaufhaus des Westens, a prestigious department store in West Berlin that was opened in 1907. KaDeWe has a mouthwatering food court on the 6th floor with a selection of food and drink to rival Harrods in London. During your tour your guide will treat you to some delicious snacks along the way, such as a traditional Berlin currywurst (the best of these are found in Kreuzberg apparently), Berliner Weiße (beer with red or green syrup) as well as some patisserie or petit four from kadewe.

    This afternoon you may like to pay a visit to Tacheles, a wacky, colourful artists residence which houses a number of quirky modern works of art. The building is a former department store in the Jewish quarter (Scheunenviertel) of Berlin. It served as a Nazi prison during the Second World War and recently it was taken over by artists who called it Tacheles which is Yiddish for "straight talking." The building is a popular place for visitors, however the lease with the property owner expired at the end of 2008 and the future of the art house is now uncertain.

    Finally, visit the 'Hackesche Höfe,' one of the most famous and attractive of Berlin's interconnecting courtyards in the Mitte district. Formerly a rather neglected area, Hackescher Markt has developed into a cultural and commercial centre since the German reunification and has become famous for its nightlife. Return to your hotel from here.

    Overnight: Hotel Adlon Kempinski Berlin
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    DEPART BERLIN

    Today you will be transferred to the Berlin airport by private vehicle to board your flight.

    Meal Plan: B

Turkey: Turkish Delight (2012)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 6 Nights

    Share gorgeous sunsets, soar over the tuff-filled landscapes of Cappadocia in a hot air balloon and celebrate your love in a very special vow renewal ceremony in Ephesus on this romantic holiday. Throughout your journey, discover Turkey's amazing history together, and interact with local people who share stories about their lives.

  • Day 1

    India/Turkey

    On Arrival at Istanbul, where you are met and provided VIP service through customs and immigration allowing a seamless arrival. Istanbul, at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, is a fascinating city, whose rich Byzantine and Ottoman history and culture combine with modern day bustle to create a metropolis that offers something for every interest. Your beautifully landscaped hotel is set on the banks of the Bosphorus; inside, find a state-of-the-art urban spa with an indoor pool and Turkish bath that offer wonderful opportunities for spending time together. Open-air venues - including an outdoor pool - face views of the city's magical monuments and the natural beauty of the sea.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Istanbul at the Bosphorus
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    Istanbul

    Explore some of Istanbul's masterpieces of Byzantine and Ottoman architecture, beginning with the sixth-century Basilica Cistern. With its great acoustics, the structure today hosts classical music concerts. Visit the timeless Hagia Sophia, one of the greatest surviving examples of Byzantine architecture. Originally built as a church in 360, it has been a museum since 1935. The Turks ruled most of the Mediterranean and western Orient from the 15th-century Topkapi Palace. See it today, and then continue to the Pera district, the present-day heart of Istanbul. Early this evening, share special moments on a private sunset cruise of the Bosphorus. As you sail along, watch the beautiful skyline with its splendid mansions, Judas blossoms, forts and palaces.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Istanbul at the Bosphorus
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Istanbul

    The 16th century was the Golden Age of the Ottoman Empire, and the era's master architect was Sinan. Visit his Suleymaniye Mosque, dominating the skyline with its imposing dome. In the Ottoman world, pious mosque-school-bathhouse complexes sustained the social life of the community, and fabulous markets and bazaars powered the economic life. Visit Sinan's wonderful Rustem Pasa Mosque, known for its Iznik tiles. It is located next to the busy Spice Bazaar. Perhaps you can find a souvenir or two in its 88 vaulted rooms. After wandering through the bazaar, stop for a treat: a taste of Istanbul's best baklava and the opportunity to meet the master pastry chef. No mall is as dazzling as the 3,500-stall Grand Covered Bazaar, and you have a chance to walk through it looking for your own treasures. In the early evening, take the public ferry across the Bosphorus to Kadikoy's extensive public markets on the Asian side, watching the sun as it sets behind the magical domes and minarets. Stroll hand in hand through the markets to Ciya Restaurant, where you dine on exquisite traditional delicacies.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Istanbul at the Bosphorus
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 4

    Istanbul/Izmir/Ephesus/Sirince

    Fly to Izmir, the birthplace of Homer, and visit the remains of the great Ionian city of Ephesus, lying at the foot of Mount Pion. Unmatched as an archaeological site, the sheer size and grandeur of the ruins make this the best-preserved classical city in the eastern Mediterranean. Wander the archaeological park, the local museum and temple, and attend a private opening of the breathtaking Terrace Houses. For lunch, join a local family in their country restaurant for a hands-on lesson in Turkish cooking. Afterward, head to the beautifully preserved village of Sirince, resting on a curved gorge that overlooks endless olive groves reaching toward the Aegean Sea. Spend your night in an authentic cottage with wonderful views over the town, valley and surrounding mountains. Return to Ephesus this evening for an incredible opportunity and poignant event: an opportunity to renew your marriage vows in front of the Celsus Library. Officiated by a local priest and accompanied by the Izmir State Symphony, your wedding blessing becomes a powerful memory to cherish for a lifetime.

    Overnight: Nisanyan Houses
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Sirince/Izmir/Cappadocia

    Millennia-long volcanic activity and erosion turned the volcanic remains into a soft porous stone named tuff, creating a unique landscape in Cappadocia. The valleys of tuff filled with "fairy chimneys" became cave dwellings, monasteries, chapels and cellar villages at the hands of expert carvers. Fly to Cappadocia where you can explore this magical fairyland, marveling at the fascinating rock-carved churches and subterranean settlements. For lunch, travel by horse cart to a quiet place in the country for a picnic barbecue. Feast on a meze (an array of small plates) of salads, kebabs, fried chicken and lamb chops. Afterward, check in to your deluxe traditional Cappadocia hotel, partly carved into the tuff. Admire the antiques, elegant furniture, handmade wool and silk carpets used in the décor throughout the property. Then explore the rock-carved churches of Göreme, now an open-air museum displaying fine examples of ninth- and tenth-century Byzantine wall paintings. Later, privately visit the beautifully restored Dark Church and view its 11th-century frescoes before going on a hike in the famous Red Valley.

    Overnight: The Museum Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 6

    Cappadocia

    Wake with the sunrise and embark on a thrilling hot-air balloon ride over Cappadocia, looking down over the magical landscapes. When you return to land, celebrate with a champagne toast. After a late breakfast, explore another marvel of the region: the "underground city" at Kaymakli. The extensive network of rock-carved subterranean tunnels and hallways, storage and sleeping cells, airshafts and chapels, is more than 140 feet deep. With your guide, follow a lighted route and descend to 70 feet to view the mysterious passageways and dwellings. Then join a local farmer and his family for lunch in their home. This afternoon, visit Göreme Village Weavers Association, where young girls weave traditional rugs at home.

    Overnight: The Museum Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Cappadocia/Istanbul/India

    Board a flight to Istanbul, where you connect with your departure flight home

    Meal Plan: B

Honeymoon in Morocco

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 Days

    Highlights: Sahara Experience, Discover Sahara Desert and Marrakech the Red City, Candlelit dinner in a traditional Moroccan restaurant, Ourzazate, enjoy a private lunch at Ait Benhaddou, Private camp set up just for you in the Desert for a unique overnight under the stars, 1001 night dinner with a romantic atmosphere. A calligrapher will write your names in Arabic to offer you a most beautiful souvenir, Private visit of the Photography House.


    • ITINERARY

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE TO MARRAKECH
    MARHABA!!!

    This is the traditional greeting used in Morocco which conveys the meaning of a warm Hello & Welcome.

    Marhaba to Marrakech!!!

    The red city, magical and coulourful is charming us by its beauty.

    Architectural attractions, historical monuments, mystical medina, thrilling souk and welcoming people are some of the wonders that you will discover.

    Ideal location nears to Atlas Mountains, Sahara Desert and Atlantic Ocean, makes Marrakech the hub of Morocco.

    On arrival into Marrakech airport, you will be greeted and invited to the VIP lounge, while special arrangements are made for you to facilitate and expedite immigration formalities, and collection of your luggage. You are then met by our A&K representative.

    Afterwards you will be transferred to you hotel or riad for check in and relax.

    Special honeymoon:

    • Depending on your arrival time, enjoy a little tour in horse carriage to have an overview of the Pearl of the South
    • Candlelit dinner in a traditional Moroccan restaurant
    • Then get back to your room, candles and roses petals will be displayed for a romantic atmosphere

  • Day 2

    MARRAKECH - OUAZAZATE

    Today depart for Ouarzazate the door of the Desert! The main gateways for caravanners and a great place to discover the Berber culture by visiting beautiful Kasbah.

    Visit Ait Ben Haddou The most exotic and best-preserved Kasbah in the whole of the Atlas region. The village has starred in many masterpieces of the cinema such as Lawrence of Arabia, Jesus of Nazareth, Gladiator and The Mummy. Some of the world’s most elaborate and decorative Kasbahs can be found here, with houses piled up around them, all seeming to defy the laws of gravity as they perch upon the sleep slope.

    In the afternoon enjoy a visit of Kasbahs situated in the surroundings of Ouarzazate.

    Your sightseeing includes a visit to the Glaoui Kasbah of Taourirt. The Glaoui brothers were the most powerful tribal leaders at the turn of the century and the Kasbah of Taourirt is considered to be one of the most beautiful Kasbahs in all of Morocco. It consists of a network of luxury apartments, simple clay houses and crenellated towers which are beautifully decorated with geometric motifs.

    This gorgeous city is located on the caravan route between the Sahara Desert and Marrakech.

    Afterwards you will be transferred to you hotel for check in and relax.

    Special honeymoon:

    • At your arrival near to Ourzazate, enjoy a private lunch at Ait Benhaddou. A tent rose from the sand especially for you in the middle of nowhere.

  • Day 3

    OURZAZATE SIGHTSEEING - DESERT

    This morning discover Ouarzazate and its region, the land of a 101 Kasbahs.

    Ouarzazate was originaly built as a Frech garrison in the 1920s. Today the city is a regional trade center, known for its pottery and carpets. Your visit will include the discovery of the famous Kasbah of Taourirt.

    Then depart by helicopter or aircraft for your Sahara experience discovering the unrevealed Kasbah and Oasis from the sky.

    You will arrive to your private camp set up just for you in the Desert for a unique overnight under the stars. Climb the highest dunes or enjoy a camel ride to admire the colours of a Sahara sunset.

    To end this incredible day, a wonderful dinner is enjoyed by the fire under the starry sky.

    Then sweet dreams in your luxury tent. Just you and nobody else in the whole silent desert.

    Special honeymoon:

    • This night under stars in the Sahara Desert is just so romantic.

  • Day 4

    SAHARA DESERT - SKOURA

    Today you will discover the Tafilalet region and Kasbah before heading to Skoura. On the way, visit in the Oasis Museum near Tinghir and continue your journey along the Dades Valley, known as the Valley of the Kasbahs.

    In the afternoon, arrive to Skoura where you will check in. A 19th century Kasbah welcomes you with its exceptional atmosphere. Once part of a medieval sultan's private hunting ground in the Skoura Oasis it offers refined decoration and a meeting of styles.

    Dar Ahlam means house of dreams. It is a truly unique location where everything is designed to ensure that you are in a true oasis of dreams. This 19th-century Kasbah will leave even the most committed sybarite overwhelmed. Dar Ahlams luxury is all the more striking against the backdrop of the Sahara. It’s a universe whose refined decoration is a subtle mixture of tastes and styles, in which light gives the aura of a museum, where space and quietude are the most dominant characteristics. The property stands in an oasis that once formed part of a medieval sultan’s private hunting ground. Within the walls of the kasbah are eight suites, and three private villas stand in the grounds.

    Special honeymoon:

    • Traditional hammam and massage with enchanting smell of orange flowers and roses petals displayed everywhere

  • Day 5

    SKOURA

    Day at leisure to enjoy all the benefits of Dar Ahlam. Swim in the beautiful heated swimming pool, sit by the fire place, experience a desert jaunt on a camel or a horse.

    Special honeymoon:

    • Sip champagne on a picnic in the Valley of the Roses with a special romantic set up
    • Lunch picnic in the gorges
    • Romantic sundown in the ruins of an old fort

  • Day 6

    SKOURA MARRAKECH

    Today you will depart for Marrakech, the pearl of the south.

    Architectural wonders, historical monuments, the mystical medina with its thrilling souks and welcoming people are just some of the wonders that you will discover in Marrakech.

    Arrive to Marrakech; check in at your hotel or riad.

    Special honeymoon:

    • 1001 night dinner with a romantic atmosphere. A calligrapher will write your names in Arabic to offer you a most beautiful souvenir.

  • Day 7

    MARRAKECH - FULL DAY SIGHTSEEING

    Today, discover the Mysteries of Marrakech.

    Start your visit with the Bahia Palace, a small piece of folly ordered in 1880 by the great vizir Sidi Moussa.

    Afterwards, visit of Si Said Museum to admire beautifully decorated weapons, splendidly decorative clothing and beaten copper articles; whilst traditional Berber jewelry captivates and enchants.

    After lunch, you will visit the Ben Youssef Medersa, one of the jewels of Marrakech. The current structure of this Koranic school was built around 1570.

    Then, prepare yourself for a discovery of the labyrinth of souks and get ready for shopping!! This maze of colourful alleys and small squares are home to a bewildering array of stalls and ateliers devoted to specific crafts.

    You end up at the Djemaa el Fna square, a multitude of street-artists and stalls of dried fruit and orange juice freshly-pressed right before you.

    Also, admire the Koutoubia mosque which is an emblem of Marrakech. Built in the 12th century, the Koutoubia minaret is the most perfect Islamic religious structure in North Africa.

    Special honeymoon:

    • Private visit of the Photography House, opened just for you, animated by the curator of this fabulous place. Then romantic dinner on the terrace, admiring view on the medina animated by lights while drinking a glass of wine.

    • You will be dressed in some traditional Moroccan clothes, just like a princess and a prince to take beautiful pictures that will remain wonderful souvenirs.

  • Day 8

    DEPARTURE DAY

    You will be transferred from your hotel to Marrakech airport; our A&K representative will assist you during the check-in formalities.

    Clients should be at the airport 2h00 before take-off time.

    • END OF ABERCROMBIE & KENT'S ARRANGEMENTS

    WE TRUST YOU ENJOYED YOUR TIME WITH US AND HOPE YOU COME BACK!

    Haere Ra!

    Goodbye!
