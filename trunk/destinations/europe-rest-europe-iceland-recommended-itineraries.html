ICELANDIC ADVENTURE ESCAPE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 3 Nights

    For the more adventurous, this itinerary takes in all the highlights but adds on an activity day in which you can choose to take a helicopter ride over the highlands, enjoy a super-jeep and snow-mobile safari, or go dog sledding and glacier walking.

    Highlights: See Parliament, the National Museum and Hofdi House;Take a helicopter ride into the highlands of Iceland; Enjoy a super-jeep and snowmobile safari; or go dog sledding and glacier walking

  • Day 1

    India/Reykjavik

    On arrival at Keflavik airport, you will be met by an A&K representative and taken on an orientation tour of Reykjavik and its surrounding areas. During your tour you will get to see Parliament, the National Museum and Hofdi House, made famous in 1986 for the summit between Gorbachev and Reagan.You will then be driven to Hotel 101. This modern contemporary hotel is centrally situated a few minutes from the main shopping area. After dinner, if weather conditions permit, you will be taken to a site away from the city lights to try and capture the natural phenomenon of the Northern Lights.

  • Day 2

    The Golden Circle

    After breakfast, you will be driven into the countryside to Thingvellir, the site of the original Althing, considered the oldest democratic parliament in the world established in 930AD. This area is noted for its spectacular lava formations and being the only place in the world where you can see the tectonic plates drift apart. Next it is onto the town of Geyser, which gave its name to all other erupting hot springs. You willsee one of the most active geysers in the area, Strokkur, spouting up to 90 feet into the air. From here you will go to one of the most impressive waterfalls in Europe,Gullfoss (Golden Waterfall). Here you will see thousands of tons of icy water thunder majestically down in a double-fall into a deep gorge from the glacier Langjokull.The tour ends in the mountains beneath the glacier Langjokull the second biggest in Europe. After dinner at the Lava Cave Restaurant, if conditions permit, you may be fortunate to witness the magical Northern Lights.

  • Day 3

    Southern Iceland

    After breakfast, you can choose from three activity days: take a helicopter ride into the highlandsof Iceland; enjoy a super-jeep and snowmobile safari; or go dog sledding and glacier walking.

  • Day 4

    Reykjavik/India

    After breakfast you will be driven to the Blue Lagoon, a natural geothermal spa surrounded by lava fields and black sandy beaches. Relax in the thermal waters before arriving back at the airport for your flight back to India.

ICELANDIC SHORT BREAK

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 3 Nights

    Highlights: see the oldest democratic parliament in the world, protected national park and travel along the south shore

  • Day 1

    Arrive Reykjavik

    On arrival at Keflavik airport, you will be driven to Reykjavik ( a journey of around 40 minutes). The Radisson Blu 1919 hotel, was built for office use in 1919 by the first shipping company in Iceland. It is one of the most beautiful buildings in Reykjavik, a perfect blend of historic architecture and modern interiors.

  • Day 2

    Golden Circle

    Spend the morning visiting Thingvellir, the site of the original Althing - the oldest democratic parliament in the world. This area is noted for its spectacular lava formations and is the only place in the world where you can actually see the tectonic plates drift apart. Walking down the main rift, you can witness how the faults and fissures of the area show the rifting of the earth's crust. The ecosystem of Lake Thingvallavatn is a perfect example of species evolution in nature.

    In the afternoon, continue to Geyser, which gave its name to all other erupting hot springs. Here, you will see one of the most active geysers in the area, Strokkur, spouting up to 90 feet into the air. End the day at the Ranga Lodge - after a dinner of fresh local cuisine, you may be lucky enough to see the Northern Lights.

  • Day 3

    Thorsmork and Glacier Walk

    Start the day travelling in super-jeeps along the south shore of Iceland with its many waterfalls (one of which you can walk behind), black sandy beaches, awe-inspiring mountain ranges and strange lava formations. A sight to behold is the Solheimajokull glacier tongue extending down from its mother glacier, Myrdalsjokull. The Myrdalsjokull has an area of about 590 km² and rests on a volcanically active massif mountain, which last erupted very powerfully in 1918. Just west of this glacier is another much smaller volcano, but now very famous due to its eruption in 2010 - the Eyjafjallajokull.

    Take a walk using crampons and other safety equipment up onto the ice field where you will discover a wonderland of ice sculptures, ridges and deep crevasses. Ice climbing and hiking is becoming more and more popular as a sport in Iceland, as conditions are quite good most of the year.

    Thorsmork is surrounded by rugged and beautiful mountains, glaciers and glacial rivers, with a multitude of small valleys clad with low birch-trees that create endless possibilities for the nature lover. You will find the more than 170 plants, moss and lichen species in this lush area, which was a fjord some 12,000 years ago. The outlet glaciers tumble down into the gullies, some of them ending in spectacular glacial lagoons. The glacial melt-off forms rivers that have to be crossed on the way to Thorsmork, sometimes quite challenging as an innocent stream can change in a matter of hours to a thunderous torrent. Our skilled drivers are very experienced in these driving conditions and will take you safely across any river.

    After lunch at the land of the gods Basar (barbeque), enjoy a light walk into the narrow Stakkholtsgja gorge before heading back to Reykjavik.

  • Day 4

    Blue Lagoon

    Your trip finishes at the world famous Blue Lagoon, a natural geothermal spa surrounded by lava field and black sandy beaches. Enjoy the comfort of the warm lagoon water, the facilities of the VIP area and sample the fantastic cuisine at the Lava restaurant before your private transfer back to Reykjavik airport.

ARCTIC ADVENTURE: POLAR BEAR COUNTRY

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 15 Nights

    Join A&K's seasoned expedition team for an enriching experience like no other to the islands of the High Arctic Svalbard archipelago in Norway, less than 600 miles from the geographic North Pole. Observe the amazing flora that comes alive with unexpected colour under July's midnight sun as our quest takes us in search of the mighty polar bear. Discover Greenland's Bronze Age villages, Inuit settlements and fishing enclaves and search for local wildlife, ending your journey amid the contrast of pastoral tranquillity and stunning volcanoes of Iceland.

    Join our polar bear expert, Mats Forsberg aboard Le Boreal for an unforgettable inaugural voyage to the Arctic. Mats Forsberg has led most of the world's leading polar bear researchers into the extreme Arctic wilderness. An expert on polar bear tracking and observation, Mats has even guided BBC production teams into the region's polar bear habitat.

    Cruise No: LB130717
    Departure Dates: 16-30 July 2013

  • Day 16th July

    India/Oslo, Norway

    Arrive in Oslo, Norway's capital, a mixture of the ancient and the modern, coexisting in close harmony with nature. On arrival, you will be met and transferred to your hotel. This afternoon, meet your local guide for a walking tour. Join fellow travellers this evening for a welcome cocktail reception.

  • Day 17th July

    Oslo/Longyearbyen/Spitsbergen

    Board a charter flight to Longyearbyen, the capital of Svalbard on the main island of Spitsbergen, the sovereign Arctic archipelago of Norway. It is one of the most remote archipelagos on the globe, with nearly two-thirds covered by spectacular glaciers. After a tour of the town, board 'Le Boreal.'

  • Day 18-21st July

    Svalbard Islands

    Very few people have had the opportunity to view these remote Arctic islands and the polar bears that reside in this remote wilderness. In the Svalbard archipelago, polar bears, walrus, musk oxen and an incredibly rich variety of birds are as abundant as cathedrals of ice, glaciers and craggy mountain peaks.Your captain and expedition crew take advantage of local conditions to determine the day's best sightseeing, which may include visits to stunning fjords, a polar research station or a 17th-century Dutch whaling station. As you explore along the Arctic sea ice, search for polar bears as they hunt in their true natural habitat.

  • Day 22-23rd July

    Cruising the Greenland Sea

    Cruise along the Arctic ice edge and through the Greenland Sea, as lecturers recap their presentations on the wildlife, history and geology of the Svalbard and prepare you for the world's largest island: Greenland.

  • Day 24-26th July

    Scoresby Sound/East Greenland

    Explore the rarely visited east coast of Greenland and Scoresby Sound, disembarking in the village of Ittoqqortoormiit. Cruise the intricate fjord network and be on the lookout for musk ox and reindeer. We also plan to visit Uunartoq (the "warming island") discovered in 2005 when Greenland's glaciers receded. Conditions permitting, take a dip in the hot springs on this "new" island.

  • Day 27th July

    Cruising the Denmark Strait

    Enjoy a relaxing day at sea, watching for seabirds and whales from the deck. This evening, gather for the captain's gala farewell dinner.

  • Day 28th July

    Isajardardjur/Vigur Island

    Visit Iceland's Drangajokull Glacier with its sparkling waterfalls and icebergs; the small island of Vigur, home to colonies of puffin and eider; and the village of Isafjordur, where Iceland's fishing heritage is alive and well. Please note that all landings in Iceland locations may be subject to change, weather conditions permitting.

  • Day 29th July

    Breidafjordur, Iceland

    Round the Sneaefellsnes Peninsula to cruis to scenic Breidafjordur, a habitat for seabirds. You also see the extinct volcano of Snaefellsjokull, setting of Jules Verne's Journey to the Center of the Earth.

  • Day 30th July

    Reykjavik, Iceland

    This morning, disembark Le Boreal in Reykjavik as you transfer to the airport for your return flights back to India or enjoy an extended stay in Iceland.
