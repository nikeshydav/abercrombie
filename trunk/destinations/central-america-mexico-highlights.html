Mexico Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Baja California

    Baja California or 'lower California' is most northernmost and westernmost state of Mexico, brimming with natural beauty, a wealth of wildlife and rich history. As a result of its vast biodiversity, waters of Baja California host the migration of exotic marine life such as gray whales, giant sea turtles, and sea lions. This region, separated by rest of Mexico by the Sea of Cortez is a popular tourists destination because of its beautiful sun glistened shorelines.

    The rich colonial history of Baja California has bestowed it with some of Mexico's first Spanish missions. Travelers visiting this beautiful Mexican state also have the fortune of witnessing some stunning remnants of the Pre-Columbian civilizations that offers an exclusive glimpse into its glorious past.

    Whale Lagoons
    Baja California is a famed hotspot for whale watching in North America thanks to its numerous bays and islands acting natural barriers against marine predators allowing for a safe and at the same time an intimate observation for both the whales as well as their viewers. Some of the rare species such as the Pacific Gray Whale, blue whale who migrate all the way from Alaska to reproduce and feast on the rich nutrients offered by the off shore flora and abundant sea life. It may be noted that the best time to view the whales is January through April.

    Loreto
    Loreto is another fantastic location offering great whale watching tours across the Magdalena Bay peninsula or for a diving expedition in the Sea of Cortez.

    Los Cabos
    Los Cabos is located at the southern tip of Baja California and comprises of two cities, Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo. The region is a popular hub for sport fishing and golf as well watching whale that travel south towards warmer waters in the in winters. Travelers can also enjoy other sports such as hiking, mountain biking, sea kayaking, surfing among others.

    La Paz
    La Paz is the gorgeous capital of Baja California Sur and also the first European outpost set up here. This is also where the first Spanish mission was founded and the city boasts of a colonial legacy of its past, along with heavenly beaches showing off spectacular sunsets every evening. La Paz is a great place to start the exploration of Baja California and travelers can also use the ferry service that can take them across the bay to Mazatlan.

    Todos Santos
    Todos Santos is where you can relish the waves, a laid back lifestyle, along with the Hispanic fine arts. Enjoy the Mexican lifestyle at a pace to savor the art galleries, quaint roadside cafes, restaurants offering gourmet cuisine and fine wines in this bohemian capital of Baja California.

    Chiapas

    Chiapas is southernmost state in Mexico, bordering with Guatemala and the Pacific Ocean in the south. It is here, that you can completely immerse yourself in the Mayan culture thanks to the strong presence of the descendants who have kept the Mayan traditions, customs and languages i.e. Tostsil and Tseltal alive for past many centuries. Chiapas also hosts a varied geography, from mist covered mountains to densely pine tree fringed hills, and its rich biodiversity has also bestowed it with an ideal climate for the cultivation of coffee, tropical fruits. Its impenetrable forests are home to howler monkeys, jaguars, and numerous Amazonian creatures.

    It may be noted that standard of service and accommodation can be basic as compared to rest of Mexico.

    Palenque
    Palenque is considered one of the most significant archeological sites of the Mayan civilization, despite its relatively smaller size than Tikan or Copán. It boasts of the finest Mayan sculptures, architecture, inscriptions that throw a brand new light on the Mayan history.

    San Cristóbal de Las Casas
    San Cristóbal de Las Casas is also known by its native name Tzotzil name, Jovel and is considered the culture capital of Chiapas. This quaint colonial town is embraced by several pine jungles and city features numerous cobblestone streets, lively markets, and indigenous artistry.

    Sumidero Canyon
    It is said that the formation of Sumidero Canyons began around the same time as the Grand Canyons in the U.S. The canyon has walls reaching as high as 1000 meters and rivers turning up to 90 degrees at certain points. This majestic natural spectacle is surrounded by Sumidero canyon national park and can be admired by boat tours that offer breathtaking views of its intense waterfalls and colossal cliffs.

    Tuxtla Gutierrez
    Tuxtla Gutierrez is the capital and the largest city of Chiapas and often considered an important financial centre of the state. The vibrant city is also an ideal base to explore the regions in Chiapas and offers an exciting array of cultural hubs and nightlife hotspots.

    Colonial Heartland

    Colonial Heartland is located in the central highland of Mexico clustering within itself several historically rich colonial cities. The laid back but vibrant life here basks under the sun kissed plazas, cobbled streets, and narrow alleys opening up to the extensive courtyards brimming with social activities. Needless to say, a tour through the Colonial Heartland makes up for the best of Mexican sightseeing.

    Colonial Heartland has a great historical importance as it is the centre of Mexican Revolution that sparked in 1911 which has made it the landmark of national pride and independence. The sentiments of liberation and revolution that seeded here have for long inspired the works of many authors and artists.

    San Miguel de Allende
    The enchanting town of San Miguel de Allende is often referred to as San Miguel. It is one of the old silver mining cities and presently an expatriate hub, as a result of which it houses several lively social activities, shops, art galleries, and restaurants frequently present live music or performances.

    Querétaro
    Queretaro is a bright, effervescent and believed to be one of Mexico's cleanest cities. The city comes alive at night with its coordinated lights and grants a whole place a sparkling ambience. It is said to offer a real cultural experience in the heart of Mexico and was awarded UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1996.

    Morelia
    Morelia is a charming colonial city, with an aristocratic heritage and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It was founded in 1541 and went on to become the capital of Michoacán. The colonial Spanish architecture is well preserved here as the new buildings much adhere to the structural design codes.

    Pátzcuaro
    Pátzcuaro is a picturesque town immersed in Indian traditions and home to the Day of the Dead festival. It is renowned for its placid lakes, striking churches, paved streets and lively plazas. Here travelers can also visit the indigenous Indian villages teeming with local arts and crafts as well as the famous Monarch Butterfly sanctuary.

    Guanajuato
    Guanajuato, the stunning colonial town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and one of the old silver mining cities of Mexico. This opulent and charming city has its own unique character and showcases the colonial baroque architecture throughout the maze of its narrow cobbled alleys occasionally culminating at a bustling city courtyards.

    Copper Canyon

    Nearly four times the size of USA's Grand Canyon, the Copper Canyon is the largest in North America stretching up to 10,000 square miles. Its rugged landscape offer great trails for hiking with varied difficulty levels, and visitors seekers can also enjoy other sports activities such as mountain biking and 'float trips' to the Rio El Fuerte.

    The Copper Canyon is also where one can pass through one of the world's most scenic railway journeys - 'Ferrocarril Chihuahua al Pacifico'. The tracks take one over 36 bridges, through 87 tunnels sitting along its 655 km route in Mexico's numerous canyons finally concluding at the Pacific coast. Copper canyon is also home to the Taramuhara Indians who form the second largest native Indian tribe in North America. These natives have dwelled throughout the canyon in caves or nomadic log cabins allowing them to sustain vital presence in the area, and even today visitors occasionally end up sharing trekking routes with them.

    It may be noted that accommodation available in the canyons is usually basic standard than other regions in Mexico.

    El Fuerte
    El Fuerte meaning 'The Fort' in English is a colonial town founded in 1563. Its cobblestone streets, baroque architecture divulge the colonial past of this region. Tourists bound for the famous train trip can also use El Fuerto as an alternate to Los Mochis as a starting or final station.

    Los Mochis
    Los Mochis is the western terminus on the Pacific Coast as it is also the penultimate destination on the Chihuahua-Pacific Railroad, moreover it is also an arrival point for the ferry from La Paz.

    Divisadero
    Divisadero is an agricultural town of the Mexican state of Sonora. This quaint town form a part of the railway tour and offers picturesque views of the Copper Canyon. The train even stops here to bestow enthusiasts with photo-shoot opportunities and to refresh the travelers. One can devour the stunning panoramic views before resuming the journey to the denser and pine covered edge of the canyon.

    Creel
    Creel was a historically logging town in the state of Chihuahua although presently tourism is the biggest industry there. It was founded in 1907 as a railroad terminus and gives access to the main segment of the canyon. Travelers can stopover for a night or two here, have an opportunity to hike up to the canyon or enjoy other activities. Creel enjoys a cooler temperature due to its high altitude and is usually decked with pine trees which also offer it a distinctive pine fragrance that lingers around even long after heading off from here.

    Batopilas
    Batopilas is a compact 19th century mining village that is located about 140 miles south of Creel. In contrast to creel it is blessed with a tropical climate which is landscaped with lush green plains lined with a variety of flora and numerous fruit trees.

    Mexico City

    The city of Mexico combines together with the Federal District collectively known as DF to offer the best understanding of this pulsating nation. Varying with the ancient Toltec archeological site of Teotihuacan and the modern skyline comprising of the sky high buildings, the capital region of Mexico effortlessly brings together the historical splendor and the chic contemporary present right to one platform. The Federal District situated in the valley of Mexico offers quaint weekend getaways to city dwellers and travelers alike.

    Mexico City
    Mexico City is the most important financial hub in Latina America, which is also adorned with numerous cultural and gastronomical hotspots. With famous locations such as the Zocalo, the city is the Mecca of classical music, seafood, sports, art galleries & museums, as well as a UNESCO World Heritage listed historical centre.

    Cuernavaca
    Cuernavaca was called the 'City of Eternal Spring' by Alexander von Humboldt in the 19th century, and rightly so, as it has for long been a perfect getaway for Mexico City inhabitants because of its mild climate and lush green spaces. The city also has a significant historical value and houses several archaic cathedrals, palaces, parks and ancient Aztec archeological sites.

    Taxco
    Taxco de Alarcón or Taxco is a city often associated with silver mining and jewelry crafting along with its charming buildings and pleasant mountainous landscape. The city has a colonial presence which is evident by a number of cathedrals and churches in the city centre.

    Puebla
    Puebla was founded by the Spanish in 1531 and was once the principal city of colonial Mexico. Often known as the City of Angels, downtown Puebla remains a hub for 17th or 18th century imperial art and architecture. Year around pleasant temperature adds to the appeal of this city along with its rich historical heritage.

    Teotihuacan
    This numinous archeological site is home to the famous Pyramid of the Sun, erected by the Teotihuacanos who wandered in the region long before the Aztecs. This ancient city is also home to various other archaic structures such as Pyramid of the Moon, the Avenue of the Dead.

    Oaxaca

    OAXACA The state of Oaxaca is situated in south east Mexico and best known for its indigenous population and culture such as Mixtecs and Zapotecs. The region is blessed with mild subtropical temperature throughout the year and has recently become a centre for green tourism in Mexico. The locals have preserved the history, culture and customs of past and actively protect their environment by engaging in eco-friendly practices which has resulted in keeping intact the pristine natural beauty of the region. The traveler here can also browse through various handcrafted products and locally produced coffee.

    Oaxaca City
    Oaxaca City is the capital and the largest city of Oaxaca state, boasting of a cosmopolitan pace blended with a laidback lifestyle. The city is teeming with a lively art and music culture adding worth to its nightlife and gastronomical culture. Oaxaca is also home to a number of markets and boutique shops specializing in handicrafts and jewelries.

    Valles Centrales
    Valles Centrales, adoringly known only as "Los Valles", is a collection of three river valleys that boast of a rich cultural heritage. Its wonderful countryside possesses wonderful natural beauty showcasing a plethora of pre-Columbian ruins of the region, such as e Dainzú, Lambityeco, Monte Albán, which is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

    Pacific Coast

    Mexico's Pacific Coast offers a great opportunity to explore islands, swim in the tepid waters and lounge around on the white sand beaches with a chilled cocktail in hand. Here is an opportunity for travelers to sit back, take a deep breath and savor life on a secluded resort bestowing much needed serenity or visit a DIY fishing village, surf on the fresh waves, snorkel, scuba dive or simply go whale watching. Simply one of the most comprehensive tourist places in Mexico!

    Puerto Vallarta
    Awaken your senses at Puerto Vallarta, an ideal destination to rejuvenate, devour natural sights, or catch up with a much needed adrenaline rush! Or delight yourself in the city centre with abundant old shops and boutique cafes, showcasing local art & crafts, restaurants where you can taste the local cuisines along with fine wines and interact with welcoming bilingual locals. Costalegre

    This scenic coastal town is snuggled amidst majestic mountains and virgin beaches between Chamela and Barra de Navidad. Bask in the sun here or while away your evening enjoying the sunset from the expansive majestic cliffs and discover the Mexican under-waters brimming with vibrant life forms.

    Zihuatanejo
    Zihuataneio offers several lush beaches in a romantic laid back setting lined with restaurants which serve palapa-style seafood. The beauty of this place is shared and admired by the surfers, backpackers, families and honeymooners alike!

    Acapulco
    Acapulco is a major seaport on the Pacific Coast of Mexico and a glamorous beach holiday destination since the 50's. It is also the largest beach in the country and home to one of the oldest and well known resorts which boasts of having a plethora of famous personalities and Hollywood stars as their guests. Offering a range of luxury hotels and quaint Bed & Breakfasts, overnight accommodations are accompanied by fine restaurants and a lively nightlife.

    Riviera Maya

    Riviera Maya or the Mayan Riviera is the only spot where you can swim right next to the archeological sites like Tulum, Coba and El Rey. This coastline had developed into a major tourist corridor in Mexico and is boasts of pristine white beaches enveloped by blue-green water from the Pacific.

    It is also a top destination for snorkeling and scuba diving as its crystal clear water is home to an extensive coral reef system and a host of marine life within itself.

    Yucatan

    The Yucatan Peninsula is one of the most diverse consisting of the states of Yucatan, Campeche and Quintana Roo. The majestic land of Yucatan today is characterized by it warm friendly locals, imposing ancient Mayan sites, dense rain forests, undiscovered underground caves, colonial heritage and tepid Caribbean Sea waters and beaches. The natives preserve their Mayan traditions by observing the language, religion and cuisine even in this day and age.

    Merida
    Merida is the capital and largest city of the Mexican State of Yucatán. It is also a major historical establishment on the Yucatán peninsula hauling not only the ancient Mayan influence, but also British, French and Dutch that give it an appealing contrast in its character. The tapered paved streets and lush sheltered parks retain its colonial charms even today and the weekend is the time when patrons and street vendors swarm its charming city centre.

    Campeche
    The picturesque Campeche is the capital of state of Campeche and relatively lesser known from the bouquet of beautiful colonial cities in Mexico's Yucatan Peninsula. This colonial city was declared UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1999, thanks to its postcard perfect cobblestone boulevards, meticulously preserves Spanish colonial buildings and impressive Mayan archeological sites.

    Uxmal, Chichen Itza and further Maya ruins
    Uxmal is an ancient city from the Mayan period housing some of the immaculately restored ruins in Yucatán Peninsula. Located 62 km south of Merida, this region is also fastened to other sites such as Chichén Itzá, Caracol and Calakmul through ancient Mayan roads called Sacbes.
