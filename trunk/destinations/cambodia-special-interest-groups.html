DISCOVERING ANGKOR

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 3 days

    Our Discovering Angkor program is the perfect introduction to Angkor, and the ruins of the once vast and glorious Khmer Empire.

    Highlights: Siem Reap, Angkor Wat, Bayon, Angkor Thom, Gala Dinner within Prasart Kravan

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE SIEM REAP

    Upon arrival in Siem Reap your group will be met and assisted through clearing Customs and Immigration formalities before being transferred to your hotel where you'll be met with a traditional Khmer dance and folk music performance.

    Optional services:

    •    VIP fast track immigration service

    •   Visa on arrival

    •   Welcome garland

    •   Cold towels and bottled water on bus

    •   Separate luggage truck and porters

    Upon arrival at the hotel, welcome drinks will be served, followed by a private group check-in and lunch.

    Set in tranquil landscaped gardens along beautiful tree-lined Vithei Charles de Gaulle, Sofitel Royal Angkor Resort and Spa is right near the stunning and historic Angkor Wat complex. Providing elegant accommodation combining French and Khmer architecture close to Siem Reap River, the hotel is ideally situated near traditional markets and local areas of interest and just 15 minutes from Siem Reap International Airport."

    This afternoon's program "Getting to know Angkor" will take you to visit the Angkor National Museum. The world-class museum reveals the legend of the Golden Era of the Khmer Kingdom, your visit here will provide you with an introduction to the artefacts, monuments and history of the Khmer Empire that aims to complement your visit to the ruins at Angkor over the coming days.

    Taking pride in revealing the path of the Khmer Kingdom through state of the art multimedia, sound & lighting displays and striking exhibit's such as the gallery of One Thousand Buddhas, one of the most prestigious collections of Buddha statues and relics ever assembled - the museum is an ideal place to contemplate one of Asia's richest cultures.

    This afternoon you will also visit Les Artisans D'Angkor, a school for underprivileged youngsters where they are taught the ancient crafts of wood and stone carving, lacquerware creation and painting and silk weaving. Stroll through the school where you can see the process of turning a novice student into a trained artisian, the students pieces, all of which are of a very high quality, are available for purchase in the school's store, with the proceeds going towards the running of the school.

    Return to the hotel to freshen up before dinner

    Private group welcome dinner at Sofitel Royal Angkor Resort and Spa

    Live music will be provided by a Cambodian band during dinner.

    Overnight: Accommodation at the Sofitel Royal Angkor Resort and Spa or similar
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    SIEM REAP

    Breakfast at the hotel

    This morning depart the hotel in a procession of local remoks, an open air motorcycle taxi, to visit Angkor Wat in the still morning air when it is not crowded or as hot as it is during the heat of the day.

    Imagine the markets and temples coming alive centuries ago with the dawn of a new day. After this memorable experience, enter Angkor Wat's main enclosure to experience Angkor in an entirely different perspective, with the grounds almost deserted of visitors you will feel like Angkor Wat is yours to explore.

    Constructed under the reign of Suryavarman II, Angkor Wat is one of the largest religious structures in the world, covering an area 480 acres encircled by an enclosure wall nearly four miles long. Angkor Wat is a representation of the Hindu cosmology and is dedicated to the god Vishnu. The five towers of Angkor Wat are the representation of the five peaks of Mount Meru, home of the gods and centre of the Hindu universe. The temple's greatest sculptural treasure is its 1 mile long bas-relief that wraps around the walls of the outer gallery along with the figures of Khmer Devas and Apsaras.

    Continue on to visit the temple of Ta Phrom. Ta Phrom is, for the majority of visitors, the most romantic experience of Angkor. Left as it was found to demonstrate the devastating effect of the surrounding jungles on the temples of Angkor, one of the most spectacular effects of the forest's encroachment on Ta Phrom is the way that the trees have grown into and around the towers and galleries. Seeds carried by birds and dropped onto the temple roofs sprouted aerial roots that trail to the ground, having reached soil they then grow and expand in the cracks between the temples stonework. Intertwined among the blocks, they are partly responsible for the destruction of the monuments and, paradoxically, for supporting them.

    Return to Siem Reap for a private group lunch.

    This afternoon continue your discovery of Angkor with a visit to Angkor Thom, the heavily fortified city built in the 10th century by the greatest of the Khmer rulers, King Jayavarman VII (1181 - 1201). With a population in excess of one million inhabitants, Angkor Thom covered an area of 10 square kilometres and was larger than any city in Europe at the time.

    Enter via the South Gate of Angkor Thom, one of the five 65 foot high gates, passing along a narrow causeways over the city moat, you are flanked by the statues of 54 gods on the left and 54 devils on the right, all seemingly engaged in a game of tug of war, while in fact enacting the famous scene from the Churning Sea of Milk bas-relief. After walking through the causeway and gate, you will have entered Angkor Thom.

    From Angkor Thom's southern gateway continue onto visit the Bayon Temple. The structure is staggering in its construction and most Angkor specialists agree that it is one of the finest examples of classic Khmer architecture. Its third level is stunning; its 49 towers are adorned with 172 smiling faces of the omnipresent Avalokitesvara. Constructed during the reign of King Jayavarman VII, this temple marks the exact centre of Angkor Thom, thus it is the most venerated of the city's temples and also the most mysterious.

    Leaving the Bayon take a brief stop at the Royal Enclosure to see the Elephant Terrace, the terrace takes its name from the exceptional depiction of elephants and of an elephant hunt, which takes up the majority of the frieze. A little north of the main terrace is another, known as the Terrace of the Leper King after a statue found above it.

    Return to your hotel to freshen up for this evening's activities.

    This evening, depart the hotel bound for Prasart Kravan, where you will enjoy an amazing dinner with the backdrop of the amazing Angkorian ruins as the stage. Dinner will be accompanied by live classical music and dance performances.

    Overnight: Accommodation at the Sofitel Royal Angkor Resort and Spa or similar
    Meal Plan: B,L,D
