Tibet Highlights & Sightseeing

  • GYANTSE

    Tibet sightseeing is not complete without a visit to the city of Gyantse. Full of Tibetan character, it is the Tibet you read about in books or see in movies. The most appealing and pleasant of all Tibetan towns, it is situated on the strategically important trade route to Bhutan and India and remains a center of trade and pilgrimage.

    LHASA

    The Tibetan skyline turns a vibrant shade of white and ochre as you enter Lhasa, the capital city of Tibet. One look and you know this hue is subject to the famous Potala Palace which conceals, among its winding maze of corridors, chapels and galleries, the earlier abode of the Dalai Lama. It is perhaps the most celebrated and most visited tourist places in Tibet. The heart and soul of Tibet, Lhasa, in Tibetan language means the Holy Land.

    The Jokhang Temple being 1,300 years old is considered to be the most sacred and holy of Tibet's temples. The A mix of wafting incenses, prostrating pilgrims, prayer wheels and prayer flags, it is built on the site of a lake and houses the statue of Sakyamuni, and is the ultimate pilgrim destination for Tibetan pilgrims. The Drepung Monastery is one of Tibet's Great Six Monasteries and renowned as a great center of learning, having attracted the best and the brightest. It is the site of the burial stupas of some of the Dalai Lamas.
