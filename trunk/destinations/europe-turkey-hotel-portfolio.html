SUGGESTED BODRUM ACCOMMODATION:

  • AMANRUYA

    Inspired by the region's traditional architecture and building methods, the resort has the intimate ambience of a village while still providing a sense of space and luxury. Local stone for structural walls, dark acajau wood for furniture, windows and door fittings, white Turkish marble for floors and hand-carved stone have been used for interior wall details.

    Combining Mediterranean and Ottoman architectural design elements the resort is suitable for stays throughout the year with under floor heating and a fireplace in each Cottage. The gardens feature pergolas with large cushioned daybeds and two separate wooden chaise lounges with shade umbrellas.

    There are a series of levels throughout the resort that boast a variety of courtyards and terraces allowing for many spaces to relax and enjoy your surroundings in peace.

    The informal Beach Club is protected by a headland and curled around a number of well-established trees. It consists of a series of terraces on which sun loungers overlook the small pebble beach, a jetty and an offshore swimming pontoon. Water sports equipment including pedalos and small sailing boats is available. Simple but tasty snacks and drinks are served.

    Turkey is blessed with a variety of microclimates that provides an amazing array of produce. This, combined with the freshest of local seafood, allows the kitchen at Amanruya to deliver an ever-changing seasonal menu. There are four Dining Pavilions serving traditional Turkish foods, continental fare and Asian-inspired dishes including stir-fries, salads and curries. The resort also offers a Terrace and Wine Lounge and a casual dining venue at the Beach Club.

    The swimming pools vary in shape from cottage to cottage - some square and some rectangular - yet measure an average of 35 square metres.

    KEMPINSKI HOTEL BARBAROS BAY

    Located on one of the magnificent bays of Bodrum, the Kempinski has unparalleled views of the Aegean along with a secluded private beach as well as the infinity pool.

    During your stay, you can sample refined cuisine in the hotel's many restaurants and enjoy the blue flagged white-sand beach and crystal clear waters.

    Bar Blue four-storey atrium is the perfect place to sip a Martini whilst the afternoon sun blazes down on you. After sundown head to the terrace and enjoy the live jazz.

    The Six Senses Spa is renowned for its Far Eastern & Asian philosophy and approach to wellness. There are many activities to choose from Nordic walking amongst the Aegean Datca mountains, open air yoga or the daily fitness schedule. The water is a fantastic playground with Gulet cruises and watersports available if you wish.

    For families the Kempikids club means that children's needs are catered for with a variety of activities such as t-shirt painting, sand and pool games and a children's disco. There is even their own Turtle restaurant designed with a specific menu for the smaller client.
