SUGGESTED BALI ACCOMMODATION

  • AMANDARI

    Immaculately upgraded for 2009, Amandari is located in central Bali and heavily influenced by local culture. River-stone walkways weave through lush vegetation interspersed with thatched-roofed suites, each fronted with a Balinese-style gateway and effortlessly combining local teak and coconut wood detail with an elegant simplicity of décor. With wall-sized glass doors opening onto beautiful garden courtyards they are a model of alfresco living, enhanced by glistening pools, a relaxed spa and restaurant with panoramic gorge views.

    Ten minutes from the creative community of Ubud, the resort is the perfect base from which to explore. Temple ceremonies, gamelan performances, village festivals and the resort's full moon celebrations - including days of dance, music, film and exhibitions - offer a truly unique view of Balinese cultural traditions. If you want to get close to nature, we recommend an elephant safari, which explores a tropical forest landscape of rivers, volcanic mountains, rice terraces and sea vistas.

    AMANKILA

    Amankila, or peaceful hill, perches on a cliff above a stretch of unspoilt private beach on the Lombok Strait. The definition of understated chic, it draws inspiration from the architecture of the surrounding Karangasem Regency, with 34 free-standing suites fashioned as beach houses with a twist. All are connected to the reception, restaurant and pool areas by stepped walkways enveloped in the scent of frangipani and tuberose.

    Relax and enjoy the fabulous location by one of the three terraced pools, enjoy the spa treatment or descend to the beach where you can soak up the tropical sun from the comfort of a secluded bale. If you want to explore further afield, take to the sea in a traditional Jukung outrigger or embark on one of a wide range of fascinating cultural excursions. Finish the day with a sunset satay feast, offering an authentic taste of Balinese cuisine with candlelit views across rice fields and Amuk Bay beyond.

    AMANUSA

    Amanusa is a secluded retreat amongst the verdant landscapes of Bali's southern peninsula. A relaxing microcosm of all things Balinese, it commands spectacular views of the island of Nusa Penida and the distant volcano of Gunung Agung. Award-winning architecture and service creates a sense of space and serenity, whilst echoes of Bali's spiritual roots encourage total relaxation.

    Elegantly furnished, thatched suites have four-poster beds, garden courtyards and outdoor terraces with comfortable, shaded daybeds. All are set behind stone walls seeded with mosses and fragrant flowers, and joined by raised walkways leading to one of the region's most prestigious golf courses.For a taste of vibrant local culture, stroll the bougainvillea-lined streets of nearby Nusa Dua, where boutiques and restaurants abound, whilst inspirational cultural tours are only a short bike ride, walk or drive from the resort. If you just want to relax, head to the Amanusa Beach Club, where private grass thatched shelters provide refreshments and delicious snacks on one of Bali's finest white sand beaches.

    BANYAN TREE UNGASAN

    Perched 70 meters above sea level on the cliffs of Bali's southernmost peninsula, close to the famous Uluwatu Temple, Banyan Tree Ungasan offers the highest level of comfort and privacy. Each of the elegant villas combines high quality craftsmanship with contemporary Balinese architecture and exquisite garden design, against a backdrop of panoramic ocean views.

    All villas comprise living and dining areas, a spacious marble bathroom, a lush private garden and an outdoor jet pool. Exquisitely enhanced by a 25-metre infinity pool, each Presidential Villa includes three spacious bedrooms, expansive living and dining areas, a spa treatment room, a fitness area and a multi-purpose room.

    COMO SHAMBHALA ESTATE

    Como Shambhala advocates proactive holistic wellness. Their vision encompasses healthy eating, exercise and self-development, with various inspirational locations worldwide. Offering expert guidance, intuitive healing and opportunities for learning, they also have a product line, Como Shambhala At Home, founded on similar principles of natural, responsible living.

    Como Shambhala Estate is hidden away amidst eight sprawling hectares of dense tropical gardens in Bali's serene rural heartland. The suites exhibit the myriad cultural influences of the Orient, featuring materials as rich and diverse as woven leather from the Philippines, upholstered Thai silks, rare black bamboo and the golden warmth of Javanese teak. A personal butler is on hand, embracing the resort's philosophy, the fulfilment of your every whim.

    FOUR SEASONS JIMBARAN BAY

    Clinging to Bali's south-western coast, the Four Seasons Jimbaran Bay offers panoramic views of the sacred Mount Agung and the sweeping Jimbaran Bay beyond. A delightful beach lies at the foot of 35 acres of landscaped gardens, terraced with sparkling infinity-edge swimming pools and dotted with secluded villas.

    Every villa is built around a private, landscaped courtyard with sunloungers, a plunge pool and thatched alfresco dining area. If you are looking for exclusive, spacious accommodation, the resort's Residence Villas offer the ultimate in intimate luxury, with two, three and four bedroom accommodation available.Choose from four fabulous restaurants, each offering its own take on local and world cuisine, visit the luxurious Indonesian spa, or simply relax by the pool or on the beach. Broaden your repertoire at the cooking school, whilst kids clubs, an array of adventure, sporting and cultural activities offer plenty of entertainment if you want to get out and about.

    FOUR SEASONS SAYAN

    This acclaimed resort tumbles down a picturesque hillside, extending deep into the sacred Ayung River Valley. A romantic hide-away amongst the lush valley vegetation, it offers the ultimate in secluded accommodation, and lies within easy reach of the island's renowned cultural centre at Ubud.

    Sixty generously proportioned and elegantly decorated suites and villas are designed to blend seamlessly with their surroundings, whilst offering the exclusivity of private sun terraces, each villa has plunge pool and dining area. The signature Royal Villa offers palatial, split-level accommodation in a prime river-front location

    KARMA JIMBARAN

    World class architecture brings contemporary style, functionality and luxury together with traditional Bali-influenced decor and design. The spirit of Bali, the most mystical of all the Spice Islands, is prominent in each of the villa havens.

    Sitting like a jewel in the heart of each villa's tropical gardens, is a private pool. With the scent of frangipani trees lingering on the sea breeze, the villas are private sanctuaries that create an environment many seldom wish to leave.

    KARMA KANDARA

    Kandara is the Sanskrit word for great cliff or cave temple and this resort effortlessly lives up to its name. Perched above sweeping cliffs, Karma Kandara overlooks what is arguably Bali's most beautiful beach.

    Kandara is blessed with golden sands and azure waters stretching to the distant horizon. The beauty of the location itself is enhanced by the sight of the towering Mount Agung in the distance.

    ST REGIS

    Tranquillity and barefoot elegance entwine on the soft, sandy beaches of Nusa Dua, the Garden of Bali. The lavishly appointed private residence of St Regis transports guests to a world of beachfront elegance with its signature bespoke service and sublime architecture.

    Surrounded by calm blue lagoons and lush tropical gardens and commanding a prime beachfront location, no detail goes unnoticed. The 97 suites, 24 villas and two Strand Residence guest rooms are sublime in a contemporary and tropical Asian style, each room reflecting the spirit of Bali. The hotel is located near to Ubud Art Village, a prominent centre for fine arts, dance and music, and is also close to the famed Bali Golf and Country Club.

    UMA UBUD

    Uma Ubud is hidden away on the fringe of the Balinese island of Ubud overlooking the Tjampuhan Valley through which the River Oos runs. The hotel occupies a three-hectare site amid fenceless fields carved out of hillsides, backed by the curve of coconut palms and banyan trees wrapped in vines. This topography concentrates both magic and energy in the whispering grass concealing Uma Ubud.

    It is designed to feel like a rural home in the hills, with the intimate Kemiri Restaurant serving delicious local dishes and the holistic Asian-inspired COMO Shambhala Retreat Spa. Only a 5 minute drive away (or 20 minute walk) is Ubud, where you can find numerous boutiques, galleries and restaurants.
