ALASKA CRUISE SAFARI EXPLORER

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 Nights

    2 days of in-depth exploration of Glacier Bay, including kayaking and small boat exploration. Plus quiet nights at anchor in secluded bays,beachcombing and guided hikes.

  • Day 1

    Juneau, Alaska

    Arrive in Juneau,where you are transferred from the airport to board your luxury adventure yacht. Cruise to a secluded wilderness to drop anchor this evening.

  • Day 2-3

    Glacier Bay National Park

    Over the next two days, cruise 60 miles upbay to Grand Pacific and Margerie glaciers, which frequently calve colossal icebergs into the frigid waters. Conditions permitting, weave among the freshly fallen icebergs on an inflatable raft. Spend evenings peacefully anchored in hidden coves and mornings paddling your kayak in this quiet wilderness. View an astounding variety of native species, including puffin, sea lion, mountain goat,bear, moose and eagle.

  • Day 4

    Icy Strait

    Set out on the ultimate day of exploration. This morning, join the captain on the bridge or out on deck to scour the nutrient-rich waters in search for humpback whales, porpoise, sea lion and other wildlife. Marvel at the grand scenery of the Alaskan wilderness as our crew expertly guides us through those areas 'not found in guide books'. Later this afternoon,drop the skiffs and kayaks to further explore the shoreline of a remote bay. Spend the afternoon and evening at Point Adolphus,famous for being a popular feeding area for humpback whales. Watch for the telltale blow of humpback while cruising through Icy Strait. Before retiring for the evening,enjoy the wilderness solitude while relaxing in the upper deck hot tub, or enjoy a nightcap with your fellow travellers in the salon.

  • Day 5

    Frederick Sound

    Cruise around Admiralty Island to more prime humpbackviewing waters in Frederick Sound. Take time for skiff operations and sea kayaking in Saginaw Bay, and cruise up-close to a sea lion colony at the Brothers Islands. Tonight anchor in a remote and picturesque bay.

  • Day 6

    Admiralty Island

    Tuck into Gambier Bay, a large, island-speckled body of water on the south coast of Admiralty Island National Monument. Explore this cool forested bay up-close on kayaks and skiffs.Never logged, the thick evergreen forest crowds right down to the gravel beaches. Here is an outstanding opportunity to spot the brown or grizzly bears for which Admiralty Island is famous. At low tide, they congregate right on the beaches, seeking a shellfish supper. Once againunder way, watch for humpback whale before anchoring at Wood Spit for more exploration by kayak and skiff.

  • Day 7

    Ford's Terror/Endicott Arm

    Marvel at landscapes of cliff-walled fjords slicing into the mountainous mainland as the yacht tucks into an area widely acclaimed as the most beautiful in all of Alaska. Snow gleams on mountain tops overhead as bears amble on the shoreline and mountain goats cling to precarious perches. Every few hundred yards, another sparkling waterfall seems to tumble down the cliffs. Tonight, anchor at Taku Harbor to enjoy a special farewell dinner.

  • Day 8

    Juneau

    After breakfast, you transfer to the airport before returning to Seattle (where we'd recommend you spend a few days exploring) and then on to the UK.

ALASKAN INSIDE PASSAGE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 6 Nights

    Land on a glacier by helicopter, experience unforgettable bear watching at Hoonah and hike or bike round Prince of Wales Island.

  • Day 1

    Juneau

    On arrival this morning at Juneau airport your driver will take you to the Westmark Hotel. After some time to settle in, take a helicopter ride to experience a glacier from the optimum vantage point. Landing on the glacier enjoy a 1 hour guided walk, with ample time for photo opportunities, before returning to your hotel.

  • Day 2

    Juneau/Gustavus

    After breakfast take a short flight to Gustavus, enjoying the spectacular views en-route. On arrival you will be driven to Glacier Bay Bear Track Inn. This afternoon fly to Hoonah with your private guide in search of brown bear.

  • Day 3

    Gustavus

    The adventure today starts with a boat trip up the waterways of Glacier Bay National Park, as your guide explains the natural wonders surrounding you. Look out for a variety of marine wildlife, such as the tufted puffins and pigeon guillemots. You also pass Marble Island to see the sea lions.

  • Day 4

    Gustavus/Lodge at Whale Pass

    This morning journey through Juneau and Ketchikan and continue another 60 minutes by seaplane to the remote lodge at Whale Pass, you home for the next three days. The lodge is perfectly located on the north-west corner of Prince of Wales Island, providing you with the perfect base from which to enjoy and explore the fascinating landscape and wildlife.

  • Day 5-7

    Lodge at Whale Pass

    Over the next three days relax and enjoy the facilities at the Lodge at Whale Pass. Both freshwater and saltwater fishing are on offer and your guide and captain will take you in search of king salmon, rainbow trout and silver salmon. Hiking, sea-kayaking and mountain biking are a great way to see the island. You also get the chance to see the wildlife of the Inside Passage - keep an eye out for harbour seals, black tail deer, grey whale and mink. After your active days, why not unwind in the lodge's recreation room, or indulge in a soothing massage from the lodge's resident massage therapist. Soak in the outdoor hot tub as the sun sets and relish the serene atmosphere of this idyllic location.

  • Day 8

    Lodge at Whale Pass/UK

    Time for one more activity or a stroll down to the beach before being driven to the airport in time for your return flight home.
