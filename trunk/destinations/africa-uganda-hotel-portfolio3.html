Suggested Queen Elizabeth National Park Accommodation

  • Ishasha Wilderness Camp

    Ishasha Wilderness Camp is an exclusive tented camp that offers quality accommodation in the unspoilt remote southern sector of Queen Elizabeth National Park. Each of the ten East African meru styled tents are situated in the wooded area besides the Ntungwe River.

    They are comfortably furnished with each with its own dressing area and private en suite bathroom providing hot water "bush" showers and an eco-friendly toilet. The central dining area which is under thatch is a relaxed area to enjoy meals and relax after a full day safari. There are excellent game viewing opportunities, with buffalo, elephants, kob, topi and other species of antelope all common.

    • Due to the remoteness of the lodge on game drives, you are unlikely to encounter other vehicle.
    • Birdlife is abundant especially with the arrival of the migrant birds are during the months of October through November
    • Visit the ongoing community projects which work tirelessly in reducing the threats to the eco systems through increased economic opportunities for the local community

    IN THE KNOW
    The region is famous for its tree climbing lions which can sometimes be spotted resting in the trees.

    Mweya Safari Lodge

    Mweya Safari Lodge is located in the Queen Elizabeth National Park, which boasts numerous unique attractions, in addition to game viewing and ornithology. Accommodation various from standard rooms to luxury cottages. All rooms are perfectly situated to view the Lakes George and Edward.

    The swimming pool with its poolside bar is a perfect place to enjoy the magnificent views followed by a drink around a roaring campfire before sumptuous dinner.

    • With 612 species of bird the park has one of the highest number of recorded species birds anywhere in the world
    • On a luxury boat, experience the sights and sounds of the Kazinga Channel which connects two Rift Valley lakes: Lake Edward and Lake George
    • Chimpanzees can be trekked in the nearby Kyambura Gorge

    IN THE KNOW
    The park was initially known as Kazinga National Park, before it was renamed in 1954 to commemorate a visit by Queen Elizabeth II.
