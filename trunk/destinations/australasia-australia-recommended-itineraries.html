MELBOURNE NOW

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 Nights

    "Experience the vibrancy and cultural diversity of Melbourne, recognized as one of the world's most livable cities" An adventure awaits you down every street, lane and alleyway with diverse cuisines, the arts, fashion precincts and history scattered throughout the city. Enjoy a change of pace on the city's fringe with serene valleys chequered with award winning wineries, spectacular coastlines and picturesque landscapes with unique Australian wildlife.

    Highlights: Melbourne, Phillip Island , Great Ocean Road.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Melbourne

    • Exciting Hummer ride from the airport
    • Visit iconic St Kilda Beach
    • Colonial Tram Car Lunch
    • Eureka Skydeck

    Experience the vibrant, cosmopolitan city of Melbourne.

    Upon arrival, meet the A&K guide and start your adventure with an exciting Hummer transfer to the hotel. Your driver will take you via the vibrant beach side suburb of St Kilda for a quick beach side stroll and photo stop before arriving at your hotel to check in and refresh.

    Board the iconic glossy, burgundy restaurants on wheels - the first travelling Tramcar Restaurants in the world. This is a charming way to orientate yourself with Melbourne's streets and take in the sights while enjoying a delightful 3 course luncheon with beverages (1pm - 3pm).

    This evening, stand at the top of the city at the Eureka Skydeck - the Southern Hemisphere's highest viewing platform. Eureka is perfectly positioned for 360 degree, floor-to-ceiling views encompassing Melbourne's CBD, sports precinct, Port Phillip Bay, Docklands and everything in between. See the sun set and the city lights begin to dazzle.

    This evening perhaps take the family to one of the many fabulous restaurants followed by a theatre show.
    • Chitty Chitty Bang Bang - 30th January - 24th February
    • War Horse 23 Dec - 10 Feb 2013

  • Day 2

    Phillip Island Full day tour

    • Helicopter flight to Phillip Island
    • Phillip Island Nature Park
    • Koala Conservation Centre

    Victoria's iconic wildlife island is just a short helicopter flight from Melbourne - this is the most incredible start to your Phillip Island adventure.

    Your luxury vehicle will collect you from your hotel and whisk you across the city to the airfield where your private helicopter awaits.

    Enjoy a spectacular flight over the city, along Cape Woolamai and down to Phillip Island, where your host is waiting to collect you for your exciting Phillip Island adventure.

    Get up close and personal with our most iconic of marsupials at the Koala Conservation Centre, while immersing yourself in the Australian bush.

    Dinner is an unforgettable experience - dine on delicious local produce including fresh seafood whilst enjoying the panoramic ocean views.

    End your unforgettable day with one of the world's great wildlife wonders - the Penguin Parade.

    Relax and recap on the comfortable drive home.

  • Day 3

    Great Ocean Road 2 day/1 night journey

    • Victoria's iconic surf Beach (Bells Beach) - option for surf or paddle board lesson
    • Otway Fly Treetop Walk

    Victoria's Great Ocean Road is an amazing experience at any time of the year.

    One of the most beautiful coastal roads in the world, the Great Ocean Road stretches 550 km along the southern coast of Victoria.

    Travel it and you will discover some of the world's most inspiring scenery, an ever-changing hinterland and charming towns and hamlets. Marvel at the huge cliffs, roaring seas, tranquil coves, magnificent waterfalls and secluded beaches.

    Get an insight into Australian surfing culture at Bells Beach, there will be time for a surf, kayak or stand up paddle board lesson if you are feeling active! Discover seaside villages such as the popular town of Lorne, where you can stroll on the stunning beach. See koalas in the wild at Kennett River.

    Visit the Otway Fly Treetop Walk is a 1 hour rainforest walk experience that is approximately 2km in total and features a 600mt long and 30mt high steel structured treetop canopy walkway that takes you right into the treetops. It's the longest and tallest walkway of its type in the world! Included in this walk is the kids Prehistoric Path - where there is a dinosaur around every corner! There is also the exhilarating Otway Fly Zip Line Tour is a 3.5 hour experience and includes 2.5 hours of Zip Line activity (including training and simulation), 8 cloud stations, 6 flights and 2 suspension bridges and 1 hour for the Treetop Walk. This Zip Line Tour is the only Zip Line Tour for Victoria!

    This evening overnight in Apollo Bay with dinner at local restaurant La Bimba.

  • Day 4

    Great Ocean Road to Melbourne

    • 12 Apostles
    • Moonlight Cinema at Royal Botanic Gardens with picnic hamper

    View the sculptured beauty of The Twelve Apostles today. Many of these stacks of flat-bedded limestone raise 65 metres above sea level and are an inspiring sight to see. Visit Loch Ard Gorge and hear stories from your host about the tragic and romantic shipwreck whilst marveling at the landmark. Visit the quaint seaside village of Port Campbell with sandy beaches and picturesque cliff tops before your journey home to Melbourne. You may like to do a scenic flight to view the incredible stretch of coastline from the air or re-board the vehicle and travel through farmland back to Melbourne.

    This evening, visit the Royal Botanic Gardens - Moonlight Cinema for an evening under the stars at an outdoor cinema screening new release, classic and cult movies. We will arrange a picnic and a blanket for you to enjoy your last evening in Melbourne.

  • Day 5

    Depart Melbourne

    Meet the A&K chauffeur for a private transfer to the airport for departure flight way back home.

CLASSIC AUSTRALIA

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 11 days

    An ideal itinerary for the first time visitor to Australia, showcasing the best of the country: cosmopolitan Melbourne, iconic harbour city Sydney, the Central Australian monolith Uluru and the Great Barrier Reef.

    Highlights: Melbourne, Coliban Park, Uluru, Palm Cove, Lizard Island and Sydney.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Melbourne

    On arrival into Melbourne you will be met by your guide and chauffeur and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel. Your accommodation is available for immediate occupancy and the remainder of the morning is at leisure.

    After meeting your guide visit Cook's Cottage before taking a walk through the unique Royal Botanic Gardens laid out over 150 years ago and reputed to be the finest in the Southern Hemisphere. Delight in the over 10,000 species and 50,000 individual plants in the 38 hectare gardens, including trees and plants of great cultural value. Many of these plants are irreplaceable, endangered or even extinct in the wild and have become a natural sanctuary for native wildlife including black swans, bell birds, cockatoos and kookaburras that fill the air with their distinctive song.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne, Club Lounge King Room

  • Day 2

    Melbourne - Coliban Park - Melbourne

    This morning drive into the countryside travelling to one of Victoria's oldest and most delightful country properties, Coliban Park. At this private sheep station, established in 1917, watch sheep being shorn and learn first hand about one of Australia's most important industries. Enjoy lunch with the Barber family in their traditional family homestead. A highlight will be a tour of the property, perhaps seeing kangaroos in the wild.

    This evening enjoy drinks and canap?s in the Club lounge.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne, Club Lounge King Room
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Melbourne - Uluru

    Fly to the rich, red Outback. Explore the beautiful valleys at Kata Tjuta (the Olgas), sacred to the Aborigines. Gaze up at Uluru (Ayers Rock), one of the oldest rock formations on Earth.

    On arrival in Uluru you will be met and transferred to your accommodation at Ayers Rock Resort.

    Drive into the desert this afternoon to visit the Kata Tjuta (which means many heads), a series of 36 massive, vividly coloured sandstone domes rising from the plains. In ancient times these domes may have been a single formation bigger than Uluru itself. Walking through the valleys and gorges of this spiritual place is a haunting experience and gives the opportunity to learn a little about the unique desert flora and fauna of Central Australia. Enjoy a champagne toast as the sun sets behind you.

    Overnight: Sails in the Desert, Standard Room
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Uluru - cairns - Palm Cove

    On today's early morning tour drive out to view the magnificent colours of Uluru at sunrise. If you are feeling energetic perhaps attempt the 1,144 foot climb to the summit of the Rock for a spectacular panorama of the surrounding desert. With a circumference of eight kilometres, the Rock is sacred to the local aboriginal tribes, and during a walk around the base see hallowed sites and view paintings of ancient Aboriginal tribal myths before returning to the hotel.

    Later today, fly to yet another Australian treasure, Palm Cove beach in the tropics of Far North Queensland.

    Overnight: Sea Temple, 1 Bedroom Apartment
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Palm Cove - cairns - Lizard Island

    After a sumptuous breakfast at Sea Temple meet your chauffeur for transfer to Cairns Airport. Board your charter flight for Lizard Island, located on Australia's Great Barrier Reef, the world's largest coral reef system. During the flight you will see the amazing Great Barrier Reef from the air, a sight not to be missed.

    Overnight: Lizard Island Lodge, Anchor Bay Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Lizard Island

    Explore the wonders of Lizard Island - one of just two Barrier Reef islands completely surrounded by the reef itself - including white sand beaches, luxurious accommodation and fine food.

    Sample an array of activities, such as scuba diving at the famous "Cod Hole" or try reef or blue water fishing (not included). Leave time to pamper yourself with an island spa treatment (not included) or simply relax under one of the swaying palms.

    During your time at Lizard Island you will enjoy a truly unique and exclusive experience with a visit to the Lizard Island Research Centre. This Research Centre attracts marine scientists from around the globe in their quest to conserve and manage the world's delicate coral reef systems. The environmental source for some 800 research publications, the Research Station is the only field station operated by the Australian Museum and is internationally recognised as a leading marine research facility.

    Led by either Lyle or Ann, the Directors of the Island's Research Station, watch Australian and international marine scientists at work and have a first hand look at a range of marine research projects, many of which utilise the state of the art seawater aquarium system enabling controlled experiments with living reef organisms.

    Overnight: Lizard Island, Anchor Bay Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Lizard Island

    Relax on Lizard Island or choose one of the optional activities available, see Day 6.

    Overnight: Lizard Island Lodge, Anchor Bay Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    Lizard Island - Cairns - Sydney

    After breakfast on Lizard Island farewell the Great Barrier Reef as your charter flight takes you back to Cairns to connect with a scheduled service to the picturesque city of Sydney.

    On arrival in Sydney meet your personal guide and transfer by private vehicle to the Four Seasons. Superbly situated right on Sydney Harbour in the city's historic Rocks area, the Four Seasons Sydney Hotel has wonderful views of the Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge. It is only a short walk from Sydney's major shopping and business districts. Enjoy Executive Club access including evening drinks and canap√©s daily.

    Your guide can suggest an array of fine harbour side restaurants for dining this evening (not included) and assist with reservations should you require them.

    Overnight: Four Seasons, Opera View Junior Suite
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Sydney

    You are a guest this morning on a private tour of the famous Sydney Opera House, the busiest performing arts centre in the world. Since opening in 1973, it has brought countless hours of entertainment to millions of people and continues to attract the best in world-class talent year after year. There are nearly 1000 rooms in the Opera House including the five main auditoria, a reception hall, five rehearsal studios, four restaurants, six theatre bars, extensive foyer and lounge areas, sixty dressing rooms and suites, a library, an artists' lounge known as the "Green Room" as well as administrative offices and other services.

    Later enjoy a leisurely privately chartered harbour cruise. Your captain will point out the sights of this beautiful harbour and its icons, the Opera House and the Sydney Harbour Bridge. Enjoy a leisurely light lunch on board while cruising past homes built around this magnificent harbour.

    This afternoon is at leisure for a visit to Taronga Zoo, the Museum of Contemporary Art or another of Sydney's myriad attractions (not included).

    Overnight: Four Seasons, Opera View Junior Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L>

  • Day 10

    Sydney

    Today is at leisure. Perhaps spend time exploring the Rocks, the original settlement of Sydney. Wander through the narrow streets built with convict labour and browse through the dozens of specialty shops. Or perhaps take a Sydney ferry as it delivers the locals to stops around the harbour or take the hydrofoil to Manly and return on the ferry - the choice is yours!

    You may even choose to climb the Harbour Bridge, go sea kayaking or travel to the magnificent Blue Mountains. (Optional, not included).

    Overnight: Four Seasons, Opera View Junior Suite
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    Depart Sydney

    Transfer by private car to Sydney Airport, depart for your flight home.

    Meal Plan: B

Its beautiful tree-lined boulevards, many parks and gardens and beautiful Victorian terrace houses, great restaurants, wonderful theatre and art galleries together with international sporting events all combine to make this elegant city one of the world's most livable.

Room amenities reflect the hotel's exceptional standards, and include plush beds, beautiful furniture and Italian marble bathrooms with deep soaking tub and walk-in shower. Spacious rooms overlook a district awash with Victorian architecture. Lizard Island is the most northerly island resort located directly on the Great Barrier Reef, north of Cairns. Repeatedly voted one of the top ten island resorts in the world it is renowned for spectacular fishing, particularly in marlin season (September-December) and world famous diving opportunities such as the "Cod Hole".

It's magnificent natural harbour spanned by the 'coat hanger', the Sydney Harbour Bridge, and the man-made sails of the Sydney Opera House are just some of its most widely photographed views.

SOUTH AUSTRALIAN SPLENDOURS

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 6 days

    This itinerary is ideal for an outdoor lovers escape, taking in all the attractions of South Australia.

    Highlights: Privately guided touring through the Barossa, Flinders Ranges and Clare Valley. Truly luxurious accommodation throughout. Kangaroo Island - wildlife and scenery in abundance.

  • Day 1

    Adelaide - Barossa Valley

    Meet your guide and drive north to the picturesque Barossa Valley, try your hand at wine making, meet local characters such as Diana Laidlaw and enjoy a relaxing lunch at a local restaurant.

    At Pancake Estate Wines in the Barossa Valley meet Diana Laidlaw, a retired Member of the South Australian Parliament (1982-2002) and a gifted ceramic artist. In 2005, Diana was rewarded with a Member of the Order of Australia for services to the parliament, the arts and improved transport systems. Today she takes an active role in the family business that has been in operation since 1968 and develops her craft with a studio and gallery on site. Taste estate grown wines and tour the winery, studio and gallery with Diana.

    Overnight: The Louise, Marananga Suite
    Meal Plan: L,D

  • Day 2

    Barossa Valley - Flinders Ranges

    This morning depart for the rich and rugged landscape of the Flinders Ranges. Stay at Rawnsley Park Station, a working sheep station offering secluded luxury eco-villa accommodation. The remainder of the day is free to enjoy the stunning surroundings.

    Overnight: Rawnsley Park Lodge, Luxury Eco Villa
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Flinders Ranges - Clare Valley - Jamestown

    This morning head south to the Clare Valley, renowned for its historic towns, fertile soils and elegant wines. Spend the evening at North Bundaleer, one of the last grand pastoral homesteads built in South Australia. Enjoy a sense of occasion with dinner in the grand dining room then retire to the elegant drawing room or the comfortable library with its eclectic selection of books.

    Overnight: North Bundaleer Homestead, Red Room Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Jamestown - Kangaroo Island

    After breakfast visit Sevenhill Cellars, the oldest vineyard in the Clare Valley before continuing on to Adelaide for your flight to Kangaroo Island. Stay at the stunning Southern Ocean Lodge, located at Hanson Bay and commanding spectacular ocean, beach and wilderness views. The 21-suite lodge offers the ultimate fusion of nature, luxury and exquisite dining. After dinner take a Koala Night Walk tour or a Southern Star Gaze.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge, Flinders Suite

  • Day 5

    Kangaroo Island

    Discover the magnificent coastal scenery and abundant wildlife for which the island is renowned. Southern Ocean Lodge is Australia's first true luxury lodge, offering a unique and exclusive travel experience on Kangaroo Island in South Australia. Floating atop a secluded cliff on a rugged stretch of coast, the lodge commands peerless views of the wild Southern Ocean and pristine Kangaroo Island wilderness. Sensitive, intimate and sophisticated, Southern Ocean Lodge is a sanctuary of refined comfort and luxe. Activity options, exclusive to guests include: walking among the sea lions at Seal Bay Conservation Park or viewing koalas, penguins and kangaroos all in their natural habitat.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge, Flinders Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Kangaroo Island - Depart

    Explore Kangaroo Island further today, try a spot beach fishing or relax with a spa treatment before your return flight to Adelaide.

    Meal Plan: B
