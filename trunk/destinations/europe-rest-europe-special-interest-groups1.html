EXPLORING AN ANCIENT CIVILISATION

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 12 days

    A special interest programme exploring Greece, with particular focus on the history and legacy of ancient civilisations.

    Highlights: Athens,Cape Sounion,Delphi, Kyllini,Olympia,Nafplion, Epidaurus,Mycenae,Corinth, Heraklion,Elounda,Rhodes Town, Iaslissos,Kamiros,Embona,Lindos

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE ATHENS

    On arrival at Athens Airport you will be met and transferred by private car to your hotel where your tour director will be waiting to meet you. This evening enjoy welcome drinks followed by dinner.

    Overnight: Hotel Grande Bretagne
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    ATHENS

    This morning a guest lecturer provides a fascinating introduction to the ancient civilisation of Greece with clear and illuminating insights into the main themes that lie behind the treasures you will be visiting on this journey.

    Afterwards start exploring Athens, founded in the 12th century BC and whose period of glory in the 6th and 5th centuries BC has left us with some of the world's greatest artefacts of civilisation. The highlight of the day will be a fascinating exploration of the stunning Acropolis hill, adorned with the mighty Parthenon, the Temple of Athena Nike, the Erechtheion and the Propylaia, reminders of the glory of Athens in her heyday of the 5th century BC. Complementing this visit will be a guided tour of the restored Acropolis Museum that brings together all the pieces unearthed or salvaged from the archaeological site.

    Overnight: Hotel Grande Bretagne
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    ATHENS - CAPE SOUNION - ATHENS

    A morning at leisure to relax and stroll, or you may wish to join your local guide for a visit to the impressive Benaki Museum.

    This afternoon you will visit the renowned National Archaeological Museum, one of the richest and most important museums in the world, whose collection of antiquities features stunning pieces from Neolithic times through Greek civilisation to Roman art.

    Afterwards drive out of Athens to Cape Sounion, the sacred headland of Homer, whose delicate marble temple dedicated to Poseidon stands atop a cliff that commands the Aegean Sea. The tour has been designed specifically to ensure that you can appreciate the temple at sunset when it is at its most beautiful. Returning to Athens, dine in one of the city's fine restaurants.

    Overnight: Hotel Grande Bretagne
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 4

    ATHENS - DELPHI - KYLLINI

    Leave Athens this morning and drive through rugged mountain scenery along the shores of the Gulf of Corinth, arriving at the sacred religious centre of Delphi dramatically positioned on terraces against the backdrop of Mount Parnassos. Explore this extensive and fascinating archaeological site as birds of prey wheel in the clear sky above, visiting such masterpieces as the great Temple of Apollo, home of the famous oracle, the sturdy Temple of the Athenians and the extensive Ancient Theatre. Visit also the archaeological museum, a treasure trove of artefacts and boasting the Charioteer of Delphi, one of the most beautiful Greek statues from the 5th century BC.

    Later drive over the sweeping bridge that connects the Peloponnese peninsula to the mainland, passing olive groves and citrus plantations, and arrive the early evening at the hotel where you dine à la carte.

    Overnight: Grecotel Mandola Rosa Suites & Villas
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 5

    KYLLINI - OLYMPIA - NAFPLION

    Depart early this morning to Olympia, which enjoys a wonderful sheltered location below the wooded slopes of Mount Kronion and remains one of the most famous of ancient sites. Started in the 8th century BC and held every four years at the late summer full moon, the Games reached the height of popularity in the 5th century BC when competitors from the farthest colonies came to take part. Hostilities amongst warring factions ceased for the duration, and the athletics were complemented by great fairs and festivities.

    Today your local expert guide brings to life the excitement and atmosphere of the Games as you visit the remains of the enormous Temple of Zeus, the Palaestra training centre, and the stadium itself with its 630 ft running course and surviving vaulted entrance.

    Afterwards have lunch in a local restaurant before driving through countryside rich in agricultural produce to the delightful old town of Nafplion situated on a rocky peninsula projecting into the calm waters of the Argolic Gulf. Check into your hotel and dine tonight à la carte either inside the hotel or in one of the many restaurants in the old town.

    Overnight: Hotel Amphitryon
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    NAFPLION - EPIDAURUS - NAPFLION

    A morning at leisure to enjoy the fine facilities of the hotel or perhaps join your tour director for a walk through the charming old town of Nafplion, with its mixture of ancient Greek and Venetian influences.

    This afternoon drive to the nearby archaeological site of Epidaurus to admire the magnificent 4th century theatre whose elegance, perfection of design and harmonious proportions made it the most famous in the ancient world. Today the theatre is beautifully preserved and retains the astonishing acoustics that enabled the actors to be heard without difficulty by 14,000 spectators. The theatre was the showpiece of a sanctuary dedicated to Aesclepius, god of medicine, and there will be time to enjoy the delightful setting of the site at leisure.

    Tonight enjoy dinner in one of the town's finest restaurants.

    Overnight: Hotel Amphitryon
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 7

    NAFPLION - MYCENAE - CORINTH - ATHENS - HERAKLION - ELOUNDA

    Drive to Mycenae, the city "rich in gold" as described by Homer, dating from the second millennium BC and which, under the tyrannical rule of its warrior kings, gave birth to the important Mycenaen civilisation. Visit the site hewn from a rocky hill and ringed by barren mountains, with its proud Lion Gate and Royal Tombs, whose majestic beehive funerary chambers were filled with treasures.

    Later stop to view the extraordinary Corinth Canal, spectacularly hewn out of the rock and just wide enough for ships to pass, and afterwards enjoy a visit of the impressive remains of the acropolis of wealthy Corinth, testimony to the importance of the town as a trading centre in ancient times.

    Continuing to Athens, board a plane to Crete and transfer to your hotel, arriving in time for dinner à la carte.

    Overnight: Elounda Beach Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 8

    ELOUNDA - HERAKLION - ELOUNDA

    From about 2800 BC to 1000 BC, Crete was the centre of the brilliant Minoan civilisation. This morning, after time at leisure to relax, an invited guest speaker will outline the main themes and characteristics of this extraordinary legacy that mixed myth with historical reality.

    Afterwards drive to the Palace of Knossos which, according to legend, was the labyrinth of King Minos and where he kept the dreaded Minotaur, a man's body with the head of a bull, who was fed on live humans. The beautifully decorated Royal Apartments bear witness to the wealth and sophistication of this kingdom which many believe was brought to an end by a huge tidal wave caused by a volcanic eruption on nearby Santorini. Returning to the hotel, dinner this evening is at leisure.

    Overnight: Elounda Beach Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 9

    ELOUNDA - HERAKLION - RHODES TOWN

    A charming day spent exploring Crete, the largest island in Greece, with its wild and rugged landscape interspersed with areas of fertility and rural beauty. On this journey of discovery there will be visits to see how the old, traditional ways of making olive oil and wine are still practiced, and a chance to watch potters crafting their wares. Lunch will be in a traditional setting and will feature local specialties. The day finishes with a visit to the beautiful Byzantine monastery of Agaratho.

    Transfer to Heraklion for dinner before taking a late evening flight to the island of Rhodes.

    Overnight: Rodos Park Suites
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 10

    RHODES TOWN

    In ancient times, most especially under the Romans, the island of Rhodes became the principal maritime power in the eastern Mediterranean. Later this strategically important centre was championed by the Knights of Jerusalem as the last bastion of Christianity before the Arab world, but eventually the town fell to the Turks who ruled for over four centuries. This legacy of wealth and occupation can be seen on a walking tour of the fascinating old town this morning, concentrating on the impressively walled citadel, one of the architectural masterpieces of the Mediterranean world. Visit impressive gothic buildings such as the Palace of the Grand Masters and the Knights' Hospital, and wander delightful lanes and alleys full of atmosphere and history.

    Returning to the hotel, the remainder of the day is at leisure to relax or you may wish to join a short cruise along the coast in a traditional caique. This evening enjoy a gastronomic farewell dinner in the hotel.

    Overnight: Rodos Park Suites
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 11

    RHODES TOWN - IALISSOS - KAMIROS - EMBONA - LINDOS - RHODES TOWN

    According to the ancient Greeks, Rhodes is "more beautiful than the sun" and certainly the island's natural beauty has been one of its greatest assets. Today's excursion takes in the rugged and beautiful coastline of the island with some beautiful views to be enjoyed along the way. Stop at the site of the ancient city of Ialissos on the slopes of Mount Filerimos, from where a marvellous panorama of the island may be enjoyed. Continue to Kamiros, one of the three great cities of Rhodes in antiquity, where white marble ruins of Hellenistic and Roman buildings blend in with the green of the pine trees and the azure of the sea. Lunch is taken in a traditional mountain village called Embona, which has the reputation of offering the best local food and the nicest tavernas. After lunch continue on to Lindos whose fortress, towering above the town atop a sheer rock, marks the site where three civilisations have left their mark. There's time to enjoy the lively town below before perhaps joining the specialist local guide by foot to the top of the rock to admire the 20 Doric pillars that mark the site of the ancient acropolis, and the sweeping views of the island. Returning to Rhodes at the end of the afternoon, there is a farewell cocktail party at the hotel tonight before dinner at leisure.

    Overnight: Rodos Park Suites
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 12

    DEPART RHODES

    Transfer privately to the airport to board your homebound flight.

BERLIN, PRAGUE & ST. PETERSBURG

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 days

    A 10-day journey to Berlin, Prague, and St. Petersburg, reliving the majestic historical times of these great cultures.

    Highlights: Berlin, Potsdam, Prague, Cesky Krumlov, St Petersburg

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE BERLIN

    Upon arrival at Berlin airport you will be welcomed to Germany by your local representative as you exit customs into the arrivals hall, and transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in procedures.

    Overnight: Hotel de Rome

  • Day 2

    BERLIN

    Today you will enjoy a full-day sightseeing tour (8 hours) with your local English-speaking guide and private vehicle. Begin your tour with a visit to the Reichstag, new home of the German Parliament with its stunning Norman Foster designed dome. Then carry on to the newly created Potsdamer Platz, dubbed `Europe's largest construction site' during the mid 1990s. Visit the nearby Checkpoint Charlie, the famous crossing point between East and West Berlin, you will also visit the Museum dedicated to the checkpoint. After lunch at leisure, travel to the Museum Island, where your English-speaking guide will accompany you around one of the world's greatest museums, the Pergamon Museum (4 hours). The Pergamon Museum houses a vast collection of ancient artifacts and antiquities. You will also visit Berlin Cathedral with its vast dome. After sightseeing you will be transferred back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel de Rome
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    BERLIN - POTSDAM - BERLIN

    Depart Berlin today with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide to the city of Potsdam (8 hours). Your first stop will be Cecilienhof Palace. Built in the early part of the 20th century, the palace served as an important place in world history when the Potsdam Agreement was signed here in 1945 by Truman, Stalin and Churchill. It is said that each party had a private entrance to the castle so that no one party would know the arrival time of the others. After lunch at leisure, you will continue to the Sanssouci Gardens, the royal residence of Frederick the Great. Strolling through the gardens you will discover the Chinese teahouse, housing a small but elaborate porcelain collection, a Dutch mill, a Greek temple and even Roman ruins, erected on a hill opposite the dream palace of Sanssouci. The rococo-style Sanssouci is the best-known palace in the complex; it rests on a hilltop of vineyards and the inside is a display of original furnishings. You will enjoy the benefit of a tour conducted by your own specialist guide of the palace.

    Overnight: Hotel de Rome
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    BERLIN - PRAGUE

    Today you will be met at your hotel and privately transferred with your representative to the Berlin railway station to begin your rail journey. Here you will be shown to your pre-reserved 1st class seating and your luggage will be handled onboard for you. Upon arrival at the Prague railway station you will be met from the door of the train and your luggage will be handled for you. You will then be privately transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in.

    Overnight: The Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    PRAGUE

    Meet your English-speaking local guide for a full day (8hrs) tour of Prague today. First enjoy a walking tour of the Old Town, with its cobbled streets, and the Jewish Quarter, birthplace of Franz Kafka. See the Gothic Town Hall in Old Town Square, famous for its astronomical clock. Visit the Jewish Museum which houses the largest collection of Judaica in Europe, the Old New Synagogue and the Jewish Cemetery. You may also enjoy a walk across the 14th century Charles Bridge. In the afternoon, meet your driver and enjoy a tour to Prague's Castle District. Hradcany Castle was the seat of Bohemian kings and Holy Roman emperors. Visit the Cathedral of St. Vitus, one of the finest and most richly endowed in central Europe, and the Basilica of St. George which was founded in 905. Stroll along the Golden Lane, a row of tiny houses dating from the 16th century. Complete your tour with a visit to the Sanctuary of Our Lady of Loreto, an important place of pilgrimage, and the Strahov Monastery, with its glorious Baroque decorations, where you will enjoy a private tour of the ornate library.

    Overnight: The Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    PRAGUE - CESKY KRUMLOV - PRAGUE

    Today you will enjoy a full day excursion (10 hours) into the Czech countryside with your English-speaking local guide and private vehicle. Visit Cesky Krumlov, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, considered one of the prettiest towns in Bohemia. The highlight of the tour is a visit to Krumlov Castle, which dates back as far as the 12th century. Lunch and afternoon at leisure.

    Overnight: The Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    PRAGUE - ST PETERSBURG

    Today you will be privately transferred to St Petersburg airport with assistance of your representative. Upon arrival at St Petersburg airport you will be met in the Customs hall by an airport representative holding your name sign and assisted through immigration formalities and customs. After clearing Customs, proceed through the doors to the Airport Hall. At this point, your guide will be waiting for you and you are transferred to your hotel where you will be welcomed to Russia by your local representative and assisted with check-in procedures.

    The balance of the day is free to relax after your journey, or you may choose to explore on your own and get a first impression of this magnificent city.

    Overnight: Hotel Astoria
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    ST PETERSBURG

    This morning meet your English-speaking guide for a visit (4 hours) to one of the most famous museums in the world, the Hermitage. Originally commissioned by Catherine the Great as a winter palace, the museum now houses over three million exhibits. Some of the interior highlights include the Ambassador's Staircase, the Throne Room and the Ballroom. You will also see the Scythian gold room or the Diamond room by special arrangement.

    This afternoon's sightseeing (4 hours) with your private vehicle and English speaking guide will take you out of the city to Tsarskoe Selo ('the Tsar's village'), more commonly known as Pushkin. Enjoy a three course lunch at the Podvorie Dacha restaurant followed by a visit to the beautiful Catherine's Palace, the formal parks which stretch to Rastrelli's blue and white Hermitage building, and the workshop where the world famous amber room is being meticulously restored. Also enjoy a troika ride through the ornate gardens.

    Overnight: Hotel Astoria
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    ST PETERSBURG

    You will begin your day (8 hours) with your English-speaking guide by driving along the coast to Petrodvorets, the summer palace of Peter the Great. Entry for the early opening at the Grand Palace has been arranged. The lavish décor of the buildings is rivaled only by its famous Grand Cascade of fountains. After lunch at leisure return by hydrofoil (summer months only) to St Petersburg, where you will be visiting the Russian Museum.

    Overnight: Hotel Astoria
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    DEPART ST PETERSBURG

    Today you will be privately transferred to St Petersburg airport with assistance of your representative.

    Meal Plan: B
