Africa safaris & Luxury holidays


South Africa is said to be one of the most culturally diverse and rich lands the world over, and rightly so. Its mixture of picturesque landscapes, rich wildlife dense forests, arid deserts, towering mountains and savannah grasslands are a visual treat, a myriad of colors. The best time to visit the northern parts of Africa (Kruger, Gauteng, Limpopo) is during the winter months (June to September) and the southern parts (Cape Town, Garden Route, Eastern Cape) during summer months (November to March).

A luxury holiday is the best to experience the spectacular expanse of South Africa. With exclusive access to premium accommodations, exotic wildlife and diverse destinations, South Africa luxury holidays with Abercrombie & Kent, India, stand out from the crowd. There is something here for everyone; for wildlife enthusiasts, photographers, gastronomists, nature lovers, thrill seekers, honeymooners, wine lovers, as well as for those who just want to relax and unwind.

Explore the Western Cape vineyards that carpet the stunning landscapes of Franschhoek, Paarl and Stellenbosch. The celebrated Garden Route, an ideal destination for nature lovers, follows Africa's southern coast along windswept cliffs, sandy bays, sheltered forests and mountains. Witness the ‘Big Five,’ fish and hike in the Drakensberg or visit the battlefield of the Anglo Zulu wars, not to miss out Hluhluwe and Mkuzi game reserves, some of the best game viewing in the country.

Traverse the winelands of South Africa for a private wine tasting followed by chef table dining and enjoy a sumptuous meal. Experience the vibrancy of the locals, the colors of the city and the lively, cosmopolitan buzz of Cape Town City at the Victoria and Alfred waterfront, with Table Mountain as a backdrop. Mpumalanga has striking mountain ranges, but is best known for the Kruger safari and the adjacent game reserves of the Sabi Sands, Timbavati and Manyeleti – without a doubt, one of the best places to see the ever elusive leopard, among other exotic animals, on a South Africa safari. Madikwe Game Reserve is another location that is ideal for safaris and a Madikwe safari is accessible via a three hour drive, or a short flight from Johannesburg. Situated in the far north of the North West Province, with areas of open plains and woodland, it is divided by escarpments, which makes it an exceedingly diverse area for the beautiful animals that live there.

Some of South Africa's most picturesque and panoramic landscapes may only be witnessed by rail. The South Africa train travel with The Blue Train or Rovos Rail combines luxury accommodation, interesting excursions, gourmet food and beautiful surroundings to create a unique train journey not to be missed, and truly unforgettable.

Visitors to South Africa should be sure to pack: camera, camcorder, batteries and film, insect repellent, sun block, binoculars and a torch, sunglasses and a hat. Anti-Malaria tablets are absolutely necessary when visiting the Kruger National Park and surrounding game viewing areas in South Africa. No vaccination certificates are required from visitors into South Africa. If you are entering South Africa from a yellow-fever zone, you will need a valid international yellow-fever vaccination certificate. We strongly suggest that you consult your physician before departure. The sun in the Southern Hemisphere is extremely strong - we recommend an effective sunscreen and a hat during the summer. It is advisable to avoid the noonday sun.
