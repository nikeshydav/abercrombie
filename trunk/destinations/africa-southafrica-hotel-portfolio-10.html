Suggested Winelands accommodation

  • Babylonstoren

    Babylonstoren is one of the best preserved werfs (farm yards) in the Cape Dutch tradition. Babylonstoren lies in the Drakenstein Valley between Franschhoek and Paarl. Surrounded by the Simonsberg, Du Toitsklooff and Franschhoek Mountains. Babylonstoren is in the heart of the Cape Winelands, It is some 60 km from the city of Cape Town and an easy 45 minute drive from Cape Town International Airport.

    The fourteen cottages strung out along the garden and vineyards offer the functional simplicity of farm structures combined with considerable style. The style was developed in the Cape Colony of the 17th and 18th century where outer walls are whitewashed and decorated with ornate gables and thatched roofs. Interiors are pleasantly cool in summer (due to thick walls) and are heated by open hearths in winter.

    • Cycle through the vineyards and orchards or amble down the spruit which is lined with 7000 clivias.
    • Enjoy eating at Babel, the farm restaurant. Everything is fresh and comes from either Babylonstoren or neighbouring farms.
    • Work out in the gym or enjoy a relaxing spa which offers sauna, steam baths and an indoor plunge pool. There is also a swimming pool for hot summer days.

    Cellars Hohenort

    In the Northern Cape Province of South Africa, at the foot of the Korannaberg Mountains, lies Tswalu Kalahari. Founded in 1995, it is one of the largest private conservation projects in Africa. The 100,000 hectare reserve (over half the size of the Masai Mara) is home to thousands of animals, including the rare desert black rhino, sable and roan antelope, Cape buffalo and cheetah.

    Extensively refurbished in 2008, accommodation at Tswalu epitomises the stylish yet rustic elegance typical of luxury game reserve accommodation. Motse consists of eight authentic stone-walled and thatch-roofed bungalows. For those looking for exclusivity and personal service, Tarkuni is ideal for a small party or family due to its privacy and excusive use policy. In keeping with the African style of traditional thatched roofs, the main lodge boasts a galleried library, outdoor and indoor dining areas and wine cellar. Sundowners on lantern-lit dunes, boma dining and Lekgaba Lookout offer a variety of eating experiences. Activities include game drives in open-sided, canopied safari vehicles, horse riding with a qualified equestrian and bush walks led by experienced field guides.

    Constantia Uitsig

    Nestling against Table Mountain's eastern flank, Constantia Uitsig Hotel & Spa is one of South Africa's most refined and luxurious small hotels.

    Each of the 16 guest rooms are individually furnished with private patios leading onto landscaped gardens. Uitsig Restaurant, one of South Africa's best restaurants offers Mediterranean Provençal cuisine with a strong Italian influence. La Colombe also continues to go from strength to strength as does the River Cafe. The spa with its six treatment rooms offers a signature treatment that is the essence and element of nature - Les Aromes du Vin. There are plenty of golf courses nearby, but cricket enthusiasts don't need to travel so far as Constantia Uitsig has its own oval.

    Franschhoek Country House & Villas

    Franschhoek Country House & Villas is a restored manor house dating back to 1890 and has the ambience of a French provincial village with water features, fountains, cobblestones and olive trees. 14 rooms and 12 exclusive Villa Suites which have spectacular views of the surrounding mountains, fountain courtyard and swimming pools.

    The Villa Suites boast many luxuries including an entertainment facility with satellite television, DVD player and a music system that extends from the lounge to the bedroom and bathroom. The Monneaux Restaurant is consistently rated as one of the top restaurants in South Africa. More energetic activities available to guests include access to mountain bikes and wine tasting on horseback.

    Grande Provence Estate

    Set on a 30 hectare working vineyard, The Owner's Cottage at Grande Provence is a testament to the period Huguenot grandeur and modern luxury. Comprising of five suites, including one bridal suite, the property dating back some 300 years, oozes elegant contemporary chic.

    Perfect for a family or small group of friends the property includes a conservatory, lounge, swimming pool and spa area. The restaurant and wine-tasting areas have an industrial feel, the use of stainless steel and iron blend with soft tones to create a stylish ambience. Years of heritage and great wine making combine to produce the estate's flagship wine, Grande Provence 2004 is a particularly good red.

    Grande Roche Hotel

    Situated in the heart of the historic and picturesque wineland town of Paarl, the immaculately restored Cape Dutch homestead at Grande Roche dates back to the 18th century, and offers guests a variety of sumptuous accommodations in the form of 29 luxury suites and five executive rooms.

    Grande Roche combines luxury accommodation with gourmet cuisine and exceptional service. The award winning cuisine at Bosman's Restaurant is a fusion of imaginative and innovative epicurean delights that will tantalize the most discerning of palates. Bistro Allegro, the second dining option, serves simple, yet equally delicious cuisine in an informal environment or by the pool.

    La Residence

    Nestled within a 30 acre private wine and olive estate in the Franschhoek Valley, a mere forty-five minutes drive from Cape Town. At La Residence, world-class service is the order of the day.

    Although from the exterior the building has been designed along the lines of a rustic Tuscan Villa, La Residence offers 11 individually furnished suites, each with palatial bathrooms, exquisite linen, silks, plush Persian rugs, and an eclectic mix of objets d'art. A property of extreme opulence, the Marharani Suite is complete with white porcelain bed and antique tapestry headboard.

    French panache extends to every corner of La Residence. Comfortable sofas, majestic oriental hardwood antiques, and soothing silk tones create an air of grace and elegance. Expansive patios and landscaped gardens offer magnificent opportunities to relax, admire the views from the infinity pool over the olive grove and torwards the mountains. Every fine detail has an air of magic, giant open fireplaces blaze in the main lodge during winter and enormous four metre high patio doors open wide in summer to let in the cool mountain breeze. Food is of the highest quality, locally sourced fine produce is used to create bountiful banquets at breakfast and sumptuous romantic dinners.

    Le Quartier Francais

    A hidden gem of captivating charm, Le Quartier Francais is set in the delightful enclave of Franschhoek. Within a one hour drive of the vibrant city of Cape Town, this is an ideal base for exploring the Western Cape and of course the Winelands.

    Hosting a range of accommodation options, the Auberge houses 15 luxury rooms and two suites. Simple and spacious, the bedrooms feature Egyptian linens, deep down duvets and comfortable sitting areas. The Four Quarters offer four exclusive, contemporary suites overlooking a central courtyard - serviced by a full time butler. The Cottage offers two en suite bedrooms (one twin, one king), two lounges with fireplace and private pool and provides the perfect place for a family stay. Any stay at Le Quartier Francais is bound to be influenced by the superb gastronomy available. Contemporary and creative, the finest of local seasonal ingredients are used in The Tasting Room with a selection of award-winning courses. A variety of lunch and dinner options are on offer at the iCi. The Screening Room is a cinematic experience with the style and decadence of days gone by. A new special experience gives guests the opportunity to learn some of the tricks of the trade and cook with the chefs.

    River Manor Country House & Spa

    Located in the vibrant university town of Stellenbosch, two beautiful historical houses with National Monument status have been developed into the River Manor Country House and Spa.

    Eighteen spacious en suite rooms have been tastefully decorated with a colonial African theme and furnished with antiques, the interiors boast substantial character. Enjoy poolside breakfasts before exploring the winelands or relaxing at the health and beauty spa. There are countless activities in the locale from personalised wine tastings to a round of golf at the nearby Stellenbosch Golf Club. Horse riding, hiking, trout fishing, craft shopping and helicopter trips are all on offer.
