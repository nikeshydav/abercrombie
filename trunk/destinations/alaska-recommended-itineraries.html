EXPERIENCE DENALI NAT PARK AND KANTISHNA TOUR

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 6 days

    Visit Anchorage, Talkeetna, Denali national park, Kantishna, scenic flight from Katishna to Denali National Park.

  • Day 1

    Anchorage Arrival

    Upon arrival at Anchorage, Private transfer from Anchorage airport to hotel. Depending on your arrival time, you may have time to take part in optional tours.

    Overnight Hotel Captain Cook or similar

  • Day 2

    Anchorage to Talkeetna

    This morning depart on a scenic drive with your private driver guide from Anchorage to Talkeetna. Driving time will be 2.5 plus hours today, depending on the number of stops.

    Depart Anchorage and follow the Glenn Highway northbound. A few interesting stops along way are the Alaska Native Heritage Center (admission not included) and the Eklutna Indian Village (admission not included). Talkeetna is your destination today. It is a small but unique gold mining community, famous for its special charm and as the expedition base for mountaineers seeking to summit Denali. You will have time for some great optional excursions, including McKinley flight-seeing and Jetboat excursions (not included)

    Overnight in Talkeetna at the Talkeetna Alaskan Lodge

  • Day 3

    Talkeetna to Denali

    This morning depart on a scenic drive with your private driver guide. Driving time will be approx. 3 hours, depending on the number of stops. On the drive from Talkeetna to Denali you have several opportunities of great views of the Alaska Mountain Range and Mt McKinley (weather permitting). Upon arrival in Denali you have time for lunch (not included).

    At 1:00pm you will depart on a wildlife bus tour through Denali National Park to Kantishna (not private, with other people). Along the way, your naturalist driver/guide will discuss the history of the park and help you search for moose, bear, caribou, and other animals. Stops will be made to enjoy the scenery and photograph wildlife. The bus will arrive at the lodge at 8:00pm.

    Dinner is included today (alcoholic beverages are not included). Overnight at Kantishna Roadhouse

  • Day 4

    Denali National Park / Kantishna

    Spend the day enjoying a naturalist-led guided hike or walk, panning for gold, mountain biking through the backcountry, or flight seeing. In the evening, enjoy a presentation focusing on the local area, read a book from the extensive natural history library, or just relax and share stories from the day. All outdoor activities (except flight seeing) are included.

    All meals are included today (alcoholic beverages are not included).

    Overnight at the Kantishna Roadhouse

  • Day 5

    Denali to Anchorage

    Today enjoy a scenic flight from Kantishna back to the Denali park entrance, with fantastic views of the Alaska Mountain Range and the park (not private, with other people).Back at the park entrance and just after 12 noon, you will depart for Anchorage on the Alaska Railroad on a deluxe dome rail car. The train travels south through the Matanuska-Susitna Valley, making stops in Talkeetna and Wasilla. Stroll through the train, visit the dining car and pick up souvenirs at the gift shop. Upon arrival in Anchorage this evening (approx. 8:00pm), private transfer from the train station to your hotel.

    Overnight Hotel Captain Cook or similar

  • Day 6

    Anchorage Departure

    This morning your program ends. Transfers are not included!

TASTE OF DENALI TOUR (4 NIGHTS & 5 DAYS)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 5 days

    Visit Anchorage, Denali national park, travel in deluxe dome train rail car from Denali to Anchorage.

  • Day 1

    Anchorage Arrival

    Upon arrival at Anchorage, private transfer from Anchorage airport to hotel, Depending on your arrival time, you may have time to take part in an optional tour.

    Overnight Hotel Captain Cook or similar

  • Day 2

    Anchorage to Denali

    This morning depart on a scenic drive with your private driver guide from Anchorage to Denali. Driving time will be 5 plus hours today, depending on the number of stops.

    Depart Anchorage and follow the Glenn Highway northbound. A few interesting stops along this highway are the Alaska Native Heritage Center (admission not included) and the Eklutna Indian Village (admission not included). Continuing on the Parks Highway towards Denali National Park, you drive through the Matanuska-Susitna Valley and Denali State Park.

    Overnight near the Denali park entrance at the Grande Denali Lodge or similar

  • Day 3

    Denali National Park

    Experience Denali National Park with a drive & fly tour (with other people, not private),At 6am, depart on a comprehensive 6-hour Denali bus tour that travels to the end of the 95 mile park road to the remote Kantishna region. Your naturalist driver/guide will help you search for bears, caribou, Dall sheep, and moose while explaining the geologic history of the park. Upon arrival in Kantishna, enjoy a 2 hour stop for lunch at a lodge and an activity, such as gold panning or a nature walk. The return trip provides a different prospective as you take a scenic bush plane flight past Mt. McKinley and back to the park entrance. As the tallest mountain on the continent, it is best seen from the air! Everyone gets a window seat for the best views of glaciers, valleys, and the remote wilderness of Denali National Park.

    After returning about 4:00pm, the evening is free to explore the Visitor Center or go for a walk.

    Overnight near the Denali park entrance at the Grande Denali Lodge or similar

  • Day 4

    Denali to Anchorage

    Sleep in this morning or enjoy a short excursion before departing. Just after 12 noon, you will depart for Anchorage on the Alaska Railroad on a deluxe dome rail car. The train travels south through the Matanuska-Susitna Valley, making stops in Talkeetna and Wasilla. Upon arrival in Anchorage this evening (approx. 8:00pm), private transfer from the train station to your hotel.

    Overnight Hotel Captain Cook or similar

  • Day 5

    Anchorage Departure

    This morning your program ends. Transfers are not included!

WINTERLAKE & TUTKA BAY WILDERNESS LODGE COMBINATION FAVORITE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 days

    Visit Anchorage, stay at Winterlake Lodge, Redoubt Bay for bear viewing tour, stay at Tutka bay wilderness lodge

  • Day 1

    Anchorage Arrival

    Upon arrival at Anchorage, Private transfer from Anchorage airport to hotel. Depending on your arrival time, you may have time to take part in optional tours.

    Overnight Hotel Captain Cook or similar

  • Day 2

    Anchorage to Winterlake Lodge

    Private transfer from the hotel to the air taxi company in Anchorage from where you take a scenic flight in a floatplane to Winterlake Lodge. Flight time from Anchorage to Winterlake Lodge is approx. 50 minutes.

    All meals during the stay at Winterlake Lodge are included. (Alcoholic beverages are not included).

    Winterlake Lodge

    Lodge Information: Beautiful Winterlake Lodge is located on the southern boundary of Denali National Park, on the historic Iditarod Trail. It is one of the best lodges in Alaska and offers seclusion and spectacular scenery. Trimble and Hayes Glacier, as well as the entrance to Rainy Pass can all be seen from the lake. Guests enjoy opportunities for hiking and wildlife viewing with many species of Alaska birds, wildflowers, and berries that are rarely seen in other locales. As the lodge is located on the Iditarod Trail it also is a check point during the famous dog sled race (Iditarod) every March.

    Lodge setting and accommodation: The main lodge offers a comfortable main room for relaxing by the fire and viewing the lake from large picture windows. There is a cozy dining area and large kitchen for the preparation of the lodgs signature gourmet cuisine. Facilities also include a large meditation yoga room, hot tub on the main deck, and a wood-burning sauna. These are the sites of a wellness program that includes massage therapy. There are 5 guest cabins on the property, each with private bath, including a family cabin with 3 sleeping rooms. There is wireless internet access in the main lodge.

    Fine Dining: Lodge owner and renowned chef Kirsten Dixon offers world-class culinary classes at the lodge with visiting chefs and specialized culinary programs throughout the year. Gourmet meals are served from a daily menu (changed every day and posted in the main lodge). In keeping with tradition, Alaska regional cuisine is featured. Guests are welcome to come into the kitchen at any time and help cook! Special dishes may be made on request. The lodge also offers frequent lakeside barbecues of Alaska salmon and King Crab.

    Activities: Most activities are included; daily activity sheets are posted in each cabin. Daily activities may include narrated walks, hiking, canoeing and kayaking; watching wild birds; berry picking. A helicopter has been dedicated at the lodge that takes guests on flightseeing tours, fishing, glacier trekking or to the dog camp up on top of a glacier (helicopter excursions are NOT included and at additional costs; fishing license is not included and at additional cost).

    There is also plenty of time to relax and spoil the senses: cooking classes (offered daily); wine and cheese tastings; Yoga and stretch classes, therapeutic massages, soaking in the hot tub or swimming in the lake are other options!

    Overnight at Winterlake Lodge

  • Day 3

    Winterlake Lodge

    Enjoy your stay at Winterlake Lodge.

  • Day 4

    Winterlake Lodge to Redoubt Bay to Tutka Bay Wilderness Lodge

    Today depart Winterlake Lodge and fly to Redoubt Bay (not private, with other people). At Redoubt Bay you will be greeted by friendly lodge staff and will enjoy a guided bear viewing tour by boat. A lunch is included. In the afternoon flight to Tutka Bay Wilderness Lodge (not private, with other people).NOTE: Please note that depending on availability the stop at Redoubt Bay for bear viewing could also be made on day 2 from Anchorage to Winterlake Lodge!

    Tutka Bay Wilderness Lodge at Kachemak Bay

    Lodge Information: The lodge is located in Kachemak Bay, near Homer on the Kenai Peninsula and adjacent to Kachemak Bay State Park. Situated between Halibut Cove and Seldovia, the secluded Tutka Bay reaches seven miles into the glacier studded Kenai Mountains. These glaciers join one another to form the massive Harding Ice Field, which presses down the other side of the mountains into Kenai Fjords National Park. The Lodge buildings are situated on a narrow strip of land that is bordered on two sides by water. Extravagant decks and walkways connect each building which are placed along the edge of the
    Land, with ample views of the water and lush wilderness. The remote Tutka Bay Wilderness Lodge offers a unique Alaskan experience for adventure travelers seeking less traveled places!

    Accommodation: The lodge offers 5 spacious and charming accommodations. Each has a private bath, satellite TV and views of the ocean, mountains and forest. From the Lodge solarium, guests can enjoy sweeping views of the islands and inlets of Little Tutka Bay, as well as the impressive Jakolof Peak. There is wireless internet access in the main lodge.

    Fine Dining: Meals are included. The hearty, gourmet dinners feature Alaska seafood, including halibut, shellfish and wild run salmon. Breakfast and dinner are served family style in the Lodge solarium. Picnic lunches are packed to take on day excursions. Special diets can be accommodated with advance notice.

    Activities: Daily guided activities included in price are: sea kayaking, local sport fishing, beachcombing and tidal-pool exploration, coastal touring by boat, nature trail hiking through old

    growth forests, hiking in the Kachemak Bay State Park Trail System for example to Grewingk Glacier, birding and wildlife viewing. Deep sea salmon/halibut fishing charters, flight seeing and bear viewing tours can be arranged for an additional cost.

    Lodge-based activities included in price are: use of sauna and hot tub on the deck, morning yoga classes, one complimentary massage (with 2+-night stay only), cooking classes and wine tasting.

  • Day 5

    Tutka Bay Wilderness Lodge

    Enjoy your stay at Tutka Bay Wilderness Lodge.

  • Day 6

    Tutka Bay Wilderness Lodge to Anchorage

    Today depart from Tutka Bay Wilderness Lodge by small floatplane and fly back to Anchorage (not private with other people)
    Upon arrival in Anchorage, private transfer to your hotel.

    Overnight Hotel Captain Cook or similar

  • Day 7

    Tutka Bay Wilderness Lodge to Anchorage

    This morning your program ends. Transfers are not included!
