SUGGESTED PARIS ACCOMMODATION

  • FOUR SEASONS HOTEL GEORGE V

    A landmark since 1928, this legendary hotel is perfectly situated in the heart of the Golden Triangle of Paris, minutes from the Louvre and the glittering Champs-Elysées. Inside, the George V is all grand, sweeping architecture awash with superb period detailing and fabulous 18th Century tapestries.

    The rooms too are impossibly grand. Amongst the largest in Paris, they are furnished in the traditional French style, with the added luxury of marble bathrooms and all the modern conveniences.

    This hotel is the ideal base from which to explore Paris. It is a leisurely stroll from the River Seine or the Champs-Elysées and a short walk from the Arc de Triomphe or the Eiffel Tower, whilst Paris's famous boutiques are within easy reach.

    HOTEL DANIEL

    Decorated in the French 18th Century style the entire property is reminiscent of a journey along the Silk Road with chinoiserie and Far Eastern touches coupled with almond green and pearl grey hues. The hand painted wallpaper recreates the atmosphere of a Chinese Garden.

    The 26 guest rooms are highly decorative and furnished with unique pieces brought back from travels throughout the world - no two are the same. Bathrooms are clad in marble or zellige Moroccan earthenware.

    HOTEL DE CRILLON

    Overlooking the Place de la Concorde and at the end of the Champs-Élysées, the Hotel de Crillon offers an unrivalled location for anyone looking to take in the sights of Paris. The lobbies, courtyards and terraces all echo the grandeur of the renaissance age.

    The restaurants and bars are places where Parisians come to be seen. The hotel is a favourite for labels during Paris fashion week and always has a buzz that has kept it at the heart of the city's luxury scene.

    LE MEURICE

    Le Meurice is a sumptuous Parisian palace with a twist. In 2007 Philippe Stark was challenged with adding some modern and fun touches to the gilded mirrors, frescos and chandeliers of Le Meurice.

    He didn't disappoint and took inspiration from Salvador Dali who spent around a month a year at the hotel. Some of the touches are designed to surprise and leave guests doing-double takes in the lavish surroundings of the hotel's lobbies. In one, guests sit under a giant handpainted canopy amongst tables with loud cowhide print sofas and dainty chairs offering just three legs. Dali would have loved it.

    MANDARIN ORIENTAL

    Mandarin Oriental, Paris is situated on one of the most fashionable streets in the world, Rue Saint-Honoré, surrounded by haute couture and steps from the Louvre Museum. Interiors are amongst the most spacious in the capital and combine Parisian sophistication with art deco details and oriental touches. This is coupled with a dining experience by Thierry Marx and the legendary service of Mandarin Oriental.

    Each room has Bang and Olufsen flat screens with integrated multimedia technology and music docking station. The wonderful spa with its private suites as standard ensure that you are well and truly pampered. We love the fact that all treatments begin with Tian Quan Therapy a Chinese bathing ritual and steam shower.

    Sur Mesure by Thierry Marx is a true sensory experience that allows the food to speak for itself in white surroundings. Restaurant Camélia is a beautifully light space designed as an extension of the garden; the menu is inspired by traditional brasserie cuisine with fine seasonal produce.

    THE RITZ

    The Ritz is a hotel everyone has rightly heard of. Located in the plush 1st arrondissement of Paris, the hotel boasts location, history, glamour and luxury. Its distinctive service and discretion have maintained its place at the forefront of the world's best hotels.

    The names of the suites give a glimpse of who has been a regular guest both from times past and more recently. Choose from the Coco Chanel suite, the Windsor Suite and the Elton John to name a few. The Imperial Suite is classed as a national monument of France - an indication of the regard the French have for The Ritz as a symbol for their country.
