French Honeymoon

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 20 Days

    Highlights: • Sightseeing of Paris city including major highlights and evening enjoy romantic 4 Course menu dinner including live musical animation.
    • Sightseeing tour to Versailles with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. Situated to the west of Paris, Versailles is the location of the huge palace that was constructed in the 17th century for Louis XIV, the 'Sun King'.
    • Full day (8 hour) sightseeing tour including Beaune and Nuits Saint Georges, well known as the wine capital of Burgundy.
    • Opportunity to visit Aiguille du Midi by Cable Car (highest point of Europe) or visit the Mer de Glace (famous glacier which is about to disappear slowly and gradually in coming years)

  • Day 1

    India/Paris

    Arrival Paris
    Upon arrival at the Paris airport, you will be met and transferred by your Guardian Angel and private vehicle with English-speaking driver to your hotel in Paris, where you will be assisted with check-in.

    Day at leisure.
    This evening discover Paris, the city of lights and enjoy a dinner cruise on the Seine.
    8:30PM board an entirely glass-encased boat for your romantic dinner including live musical animation. Your 4-course menu will include some wine (1 bottle for 4 guests) and a glass of champagne with dessert.
    Special Offer - Value Added Honeymoon Couples - Bouquet of Flowers and Bottle of Champagne

    Overnight: Sofitel Paris Le Faubourg
    Luxury Double/Twin
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    Paris

    Today you will enjoy a full day (8 hours) sightseeing tour in Paris with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. This morning an overview tour will include famous Parisian sights such as the Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Champs Elysées and the Ile de la Cité, where you will visit the beautiful Notre Dame Cathedral and the Sainte Chapelle, a pearl of Gothic architecture. This afternoon visit the world-famous Louvre Museum. Originally the Royal residence of the Kings of France, the sprawling Louvre palace contains one of the most important art collections in the Western world, spanning from remotest antiquity to the mid 19th cent, with the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo as perhaps its two most famous exhibits, and the upturned glass pyramid by I.M. Pei at the entrance adding a contemporary touch.

    Optional/ alternative tour & NOT included in the itinerary

    This afternoon your guide will take you to a renowned "Parfumeur" for a unique private workshop (4hrs). You will step behind the scenes and learn about the key stages in the composition of a fragrance. You will discover the techniques and the ingredients before becoming a "nose" yourself! You will work with a selection of high quality ingredients and design your own personal blend of notes. You will name and label your 17.5 fl oz bottle of personal fragrance and take it away with you as a souvenir of this olfactory experience.

    9.00 PM - enjoy the cabaret theatre show at the Moulin Rouge (1/2 bottle of Champagne included). See the world-renowned showgirls and French Cancan dancers on the Moulin Rouge's historic stage.

    Make your own way back to the hotel after the show.

    Overnight: Sofitel Paris Le Faubourg
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Paris/ Versailles/ Paris

    Enjoy a half-day (up to 5 hours) sightseeing tour to Versailles with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. Situated to the west of Paris, Versailles is the location of the huge palace that was constructed in the 17th century for Louis XIV, the 'Sun King'. It became the model for grand houses of royalty all over Europe. Take a tour of the opulent State Apartments that culminate in the magnificent Gallery of Mirrors. You may like to take a stroll in the formal ornamental gardens that are embellished with many fountains, and see the Grand Canal that stretches into the distance across the park. Return to Paris following your visit.

    Inclusions: Transfer in a private, air-conditioned vehicle with English-speaking driver (1-2 pax Citroen C6 or similar, Services of an English-speaking local guide, Entrance fees to Versailles Palace Gardens and State Apartments

    Exclusions: Any meals or entrance fees not specified & Tips

    Overnight: Sofitel Paris Le Faubourg
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Paris/ Dijon

    Morning after breakfast, check out and you will be transferred to the station to board train to Dijon.

    Concierge Rail service from Paris to Dijon.

    After lunch at leisure, meet your local guide for a 3-hr walking tour of the city. Once the capital city of the mighty Dukes of Burgundy before coming under the control of the French crown, Dijon is still the capital of the Burgundy region and its wonderful old quarters are among the most interesting in France. Stroll through a network of narrow streets and small squares with fine 15th-16th century mansions and the 13th century gothic church of Notre-Dame before coming up behind the Ducal Palace, which houses a huge museum of French and Flemish art. Full of fascinating shops, Dijon's famous mustard is sold everywhere as well as the blackcurrant liqueur.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel La Cloche
    Superior Double
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Burgundy Region

    This morning you will meet your private vehicle and English-speaking driver-guide for a 3-hr walking tour of the city First, visit Beaune, known as the wine capital of Burgundy, where will visit the Hospices de Beaune (also known as the Hotel Dieu) a former hospital founded in 1443 to provide free care for men who had fought in the Hundred Years War. Today Beaune is famous for its tiled roofs and Flemish architecture which have become icons of Burgundy. Continue on to the wine country between Beaune and Dijon and visit the Clos de Vougeot famous for its wine-making barn. Also see the famous villages such as Gevrey-Chambertin or Marsannay-la-Côte before returning to Dijon.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel La Cloche
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    Dijon/ Lyon

    Today you will be met at your hotel and privately transferred with your Guardian Angel to the Dijon railway station. Here you will be shown to your pre-reserved 1st class seating.

    Upon arrival at the Lyon railway station an E/S assistant will meet you and accompany you for your transfer to the hotel.

    This afternoon embark on a HD (up to 5hrs) city tour with your guide. The capital of the Rhone-Alps region, Lyon is the second largest conurbation in France. Two broad rivers, the Saône and the Rhône run majestically through the heart of the city, south of which they merge. The long narrow strip between the two rivers is thecenter of Lyon: Place Carnot,
    Place Bellecour and Place des Terreaux are linked by fashionable shopping streets and surrounded by fine Renaissance buildings. Across the Saône, Le Vieux Lyon, is a picturesque and well-restored district with numerous impressive Gothic and Renaissance buildings. Visit the The Maison des Canuts (silk workers of the 19th century) is a living museum that reveals the richness and quality of the silk industry in Lyon and it is the only place where manual looms, invented by Jacquard, are in use. Your tour will give you the opportunity to learn about the history of silk, the life of the silk workers.

    Overnight: Sofitel Lyon Bellecour
    Superior Queen Room
    Meal Plan: Meals: B

  • Day 7

    Lyon at leisure

    Full day at leisure in Lyon.
    You can choose from Plenty of activities from Cruise on river or to take tram ride to visit the highest point and famous church of Lyon or may be have a leisure walk in the city to understand local people and their culture

    Overnight: Sofitel Lyon Bellecour
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    Lyon / Annecy

    Private transfer from Lyon to Annecy with English speaking driver. (Approx 3 hours drive)

It is known as "Europe's cleanest lake" because of strict environmental regulations introduced in the 1960s. It is a popular tourist destination known for its swimming and water sports. The lake was formed about 18,000 years ago, at the time the large alpine glaciers melted. It is fed by many small rivers from the surrounding mountains (Ire, Eau morte, Laudon, Bornette and Biolon), and from a powerful underwater source, the Boubioz, which enters at 82 m depth.

    Rest of the day at leisure to explore the place on your own.

    Overnight: Hôtel Impérial, Annecy
    Deluxe with park view
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Annecy/ Chamonix

    Private transfer from Annecy to Chamonix with English speaking driver.

    Rest of the day at leisure to enjoy the many activities the resort has to offer!

    Cham' is a destination popular the world over. In the streets of this high-mountain town you'll hear languages from every corner of the globe. But the people who come to this 'summit meeting' share the same way of thinking, the same love for mountaineering, its legends and its ethics. When you're out shopping in the town centre, wherever you are look up and you'll see 'it' there. 4,810 metres (15,781 ft) of rock, snow, ice and dreams of things to come.

    Overnight: Hameau Albert 1
    Classic Double/Twin
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    Chamonix

    At leisure to go up the Aiguille du Midi by Cable Car or visit the Mer de Glace (Glacier)

    Overnight: Hameau Albert 1
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    Chamonix

    At leisure on your own.

    You may choose from ample activities available from River rafting to Luge ride, spa to fishing etc.

    Overnight: Hameau Albert 1
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 12

    Chamonix/ Geneva/ Nice

    Private transfer from Chamonix to Geneva airport to board flight to Nice (NOT included in tour cost)

    Upon arrival in Nice you will be met by your local guardian angel and transferred to your hotel in Nice.

    Rest of the day is free on your own. Overnight stay at the hotel.

    Overnight: Le Méridien Nice
    Deluxe Double/twin
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 13

    Nice

    Today meet your local English-speaking guide for half-day sightseeing (4hrs) in Nice. You will explore the beautiful Old Town, and colorful open-air market and take the lift up to the Château Hill (Colline du Château) area for unrivalled views of the city.

    Inclusions: Services of an English-speaking local guide, Lift to the Château Hill Exclusions: Any meals or entrance fees not specified & Tips

    After lunch at leisure, set off to spend the afternoon exploring the Principality of Monaco with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide (4hrs). This exceptional city-state is just east of Nice, a land steeped in history and also a Mediterranean mini Las Vegas. The capital, Monaco, is a fairy tale city standing on a rocky headland almost surrounded by sea, and contains the palace complete with guards, the tropical gardens offering excellent views, and the museum containing Roman antiquities and prehistory collections.

    Look around the Place du Casino, possibly the most famous casino in the world, in the center of Monte Carlo. Go on to the well-known Rock, crowned by the Palais Princier (open April through October) , where the royal family lives, seeing the extravagance of the 16th and 17th century frescoes, as well as tapestries, gilt furniture and paintings.

    Overnight:Le Méridien Nice
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 14

    Nice/ Carcassonne

    Private transfer from Nice to Carcassonne with English Speaking driver. Stop en route (about 1 hr) for lunch at leisure.

    Transfer time = approx. 5 hours

    As there is not direct train between the 2 cities, we have based the transfer by private vehicle.

    Overnight: Hotel de la Cite
    Deluxe Double/Twin Classic Room with city view
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 15

    Carcassonne/Bordeaux

    Morning enjoy 2,5hrs hour walking tour of the medieval walled-city with a local guide.

    Evening transfer to Carcassonne station to board a direct train to Bordeaux.

    Upon arrival in Bordeaux, meet your local guardian angel and driver for your transfer to the Grand Hotel .Evening at leisure.

    Overnight: Grand Hotel de Bordeaux & Spa
    Superior Double
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 16

    Bordeaux

    Today you experience historic Bordeaux with your guide on a walking tour (3hrs) to see some of the citys famous monuments and buildings. See the 18th-century Place de la Bourse and the 13th-century Cathedral of St. Andre, now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Admire the neoclassical facades of the Bordeaux Law Courts, St. Andre Hospital and the 18th-century Palais Rohan, now the City Hall. When you explore Old Bordeaux, walk past the Cailhau Gate and the Grosse Cloche, the former belfry of the town hall in the Middle Ages.

    Afternoon at leisure to do some shopping or enjoy the hotels spa.

    Optional - 6hr excursion to St-Emilion with English Speaking driver-guide

    Only a short distance from Bordeaux, the village of St. Emilion was built into a limestone hill. The cobbled streets of this medieval town are lined with stores selling the wines the region is known for. In the eighth century, Emilion, a hermit, lived in a cave with an underground spring. Over the next 500 years, his cave was enlarged to create Eglise Monolithe, one of Frances largest underground rock churches. Visit the cave and then take time to stop at some of the local vineyards, like the famous Château Franc Mayne or Chateau La Tour du Pin Figeac

    Overnight: Grand Hotel de Bordeaux & Spa
    Superior Double
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 17

    Bordeaux/St Pierre des Corps

    Your GA and driver will accompany you to Bordeaux station for your direct high speed train (TGV) to St Pierre des Corps.

    Upon arrival in St Pierre des Corps you will have a short transfer to Tours city center. Balance of the day at leisure.

    You may decide to look around Tours independently. Tours is a city that grew around the tomb of St Martin, its 3rd bishop at the end of the 4th century. It has now become the largest town in Touraine. Rich merchants built its Renaissance and neo-classic mansions and it is home to the Musée des Beaux Arts, a remarkable series of salons decorated in silk and wood panelling. See the Cathedral of St. Gatien with its Renaissance lanterns and 13th century stained glass as well as the Place Plumereau and Rue Briconnet, center of the old medieval quarter.

    Overnight: Hotel de l'Univers
    Superior Double
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 18

    St Pierre des Corps

    Embark on a full day (8hrs) s/s excursion with your English-speaking driver-guide to explore the Loire Valley by private vehicle. You will visit the Chateau de Chenonceau that straddles the river and is one of the best-known Chateaux of the Loire region. Continuing on to Amboise you will visit the imposing Royal Chateau and the Clos Lucé, a handsome Renaissance manor where Leonardo da Vinci spent the last four years of his life. The museum contains working models of some of da Vinci's inventions. End the day with a visit and tasting at some renowned cellars overlooking the River Loire.

    Overnight: Hotel de l'Univers
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 19

    St Pierre des Corps / Paris

    Private transfer to St Pierre des Corps TGV station to board the train to Paris.

    Upon arrival at Paris Gare Montparnasse station, transfer to your hotel for your last overnight in France. Spend the balance of the day as you please.

    Overnight: Sofitel Paris Le Faubourg
    Luxury Double/Twin
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 20

    Paris

    Departure Paris
    Private transfer from Paris to Roissy CDG Airport with English Speaking driver for departure flight way back home.

    Meal Plan: B

Provence and the Cote d'Azur

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 Nights

    Highlights: Papal palace of Avignon. Vineyards of Châteauneuf-du-Pape. Charming Provencal towns. Footsteps of van Gogh and Cezanne.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Marseille - Avignon

    Arrive Marseille where you are privately transferred with your Guardian Angel to the hotel in Avignon.

    Overnight: La Mirande, King Room (Platinum) or La Mirande, Queen Room (Gold)

  • Day 2

    Avignon - Châteauneuf-du-Pape - Avignon

    Dominated by its Papal Palace, Avignon is a vibrant Provencal town with much character, and today you discover its glories with a walking tour that includes a visit to the great Palace where the Popes reigned supreme during their 'schism' with Rome, and whose mighty rooms express some of the power and the glory of the period. Stop at the local antique street market to pick up a souvenir before enjoying a sweeping panorama of the town with the mighty Rhône River flowing beside it.

    This afternoon is a visit to Châteauneuf-du-Pape with private vehicle and English-speaking driver driver-guide. It was here, in the rolling hills of the southern Rhône Valley that the Popes of Avignon planted vineyards in the 14th century. There are some 3,200 hectares of vines in the area including five villages cultivating 13 different varieties such as Grenache, Syrah, Mourvedre and Cinsault. The characteristics of Châteauneuf wines are very varied and, like all Rhone Valley wines, full flavoured. Your visit will of course include some tasting.

    Overnight: La Mirande, King Room (Platinum) or La Mirande, Queen Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Avignon - Les Apilles - Avignon

    Today meet your local English-speaking driver-guide for a full day sightseeing tour to St Rèmy and Les Baux. Start with a visit to the delightful Saint-Rèmy-de-Provence, a charming town with ancient, narrow streets lined with lovingly restored old houses, shady squares and wonderful fountains. The beauty of the area and its light has inspired many artists, the most famous being Vincent van Gogh. The town was also the birthplace of Nostradamus; the well known 16th century physician and astrologer was born in a house of the avenue Hoche, in the old quarter.

    You will visit the former monastery of Saint-Paul-de-Mausole. As well as being one of the most beautiful religious monuments in Provence, it is also famous for having accommodated Vincent van Gogh from May 1889 to May 1890. Also tour Glanum, set in a remarkably attractive landscape surrounded by the Alpilles. This Roman site counts among the major classical sites of the South of France.

    Overnight: La Mirande, King Room (Platinum) or La Mirande, Queen Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Avignon - Carpentras - Mount Ventoux - Avignon

    Meet your English-speaking driver-guide for another full day of sightseeing and head to Carpentras. This beautiful Provencal town lies between the Rhône River and the Durance and is protected by the impressive Mont Ventoux on one side and the Vaucluse Mounts on the other. In the Middle Ages the town was home to a large Jewish community and the 1367 synagogue is the oldest in France.

    Try and visit Carpentras on a Friday as an exceptional Provencal market takes over the city streets every Friday morning.

    After lunch at leisure your driver-guide may take you to a local patisserie. Its speciality is a very tasty vanilla meringue whose shape was inspired by the small rocks on Mont Ventoux! Drive on to Mont Ventoux, which towers over the landscape and surrounding villages. Amazingly the bare summit looks snowcapped even in the summer months. From the top (1,915m) you will enjoy magical views of the Rhône Valley and see why the mythical Mount Ventoux was declared a Biosphere Reverse by UNESCO in 1990. Return to Avignon at the end of the day.

    Overnight: La Mirande, King Room (Platinum) or La Mirande, Queen Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Avignon - Aix-en-Provence - Vence

    This morning your English-speaking driver will transfer you from Avignon to Vence.

    En route you will stop in Aix-en-Provence where you will meet a local English-speaking guide for a half-day walking tour of Aix-en-Provence. The former capital of the region, Aix-en-Provence is a city of art and light, with several art schools and universities. At the heart of the city is Cours Mirabeau, a lovely avenue of plane trees, lined on one side with wonderful terrace cafes and bookshops. This is the perfect place to sit in the sunshine or in the dappled shade of the trees, sipping a cup of coffee and watching the world go by. The centre of the city of Aix is built around the old town, ringed by boulevards and squares. Discover the town following in the footsteps of Cèzanne. Visit the Musèe-Atelier de Paul Cèzanne where Cèzanne painted some of his most well-known works - there is also a striking collection of simple objects which once featured in his still life paintings.

    After lunch at leisure, continue your journey to St. Paul de Vence and check in to your hotel in the heart of the French Riviera.

    Overnight: Le Mas de Pierre, Prestige (Platinum) or Le Mas de Pierre, Classic (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    St. Paul de Vence

    Meet your local English-speaking guide and private vehicle for a half-day sightseeing tour the medieval village of St. Paul de Vence, still a magnet for contemporary artists. Visit the Fondation Maeght, a privately-owned modern art museum set on a wooded cliff top. This foundation contains one of the most important collections in Europe, with paintings, sculptures drawings and other works by Braque, Calder, Chagall, Giacometti, Lèger or Mirò.

    The afternoon is at leisure.

    Overnight: Le Mas de Pierre, Prestige (Platinum) or Le Mas de Pierre, Classic (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 7

    Vence - Saint Tropez - Grimaud - Vence

    A full day excursion today to St. Tropez and Grimaud. Before becoming one of the most luxurious spots on the French Riviera, the resort of St. Tropez was a small fishing village, but its popularity with writers and artists from Collette to Picasso has made it the fashionable place to be seen by celebrities, who throng to its sun-kissed streets and beaches. St. Tropez combines rustic Provence and chic Côte d'Azur, with its winding medieval streets, pastel painted fisherman's cottages and port with fishing boats alongside super yachts and glamorous boutiques. The nearby village of Grimaud is also worth a visit. Dating from Roman times, it is one of the most "flowered" villages in France. Though fully restored it successfully maintains its typical provencal character. Points of interest include a windmill, fully restored in 1990, several chapels including the Chapel of the Penitents and the ruins of its medieval castle.

    Overnight: Le Mas de Pierre, Prestige (Platinum) or Le Mas de Pierre, Classic (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    Vence - Nice - Vence

    This morning you will be met by your private vehicle and English-speaking driver to be transferred into Nice. Upon arrival, spend the morning tasting delicacies in the Old Town, learn all about Provencal olive oils and have a light Mediterranean meal prepared with only the freshest ingredients from the market. Your guide will be a food critic and cookbook author who is fascinated with Nice and its unique culinary tradition, a result of its history and climate. You will be free to spend time at leisure in the city for sightseeing or shopping before being transferred back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Le Mas de Pierre, Prestige (Platinum) or Le Mas de Pierre, Classic (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 9

    Depart Nice

    Transfer to Nice airport by private vehicle for onward flight arrangements

    Meal Plan: B

Simply French

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 Nights

    Highlights: Paris, City of Light. Focus on regional food and wine. Behind the scenes of the Eiffel Tower. Visit a dedicated chocolatier or fromagerie with a Parisian food critic.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Paris

    Bienvenue à Paris! A warm A&K welcome to the French capital and a private transfer to your hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel Le Meurice, Deluxe Room (Platinum) or Hotel Daniel, Classic Room (Gold)

  • Day 2

    Design Your Day Paris

    Rich in culture, this breathtaking city on the River Seine is a study in sophistication. Today you will have an introduction to the iconic sights of the city: the Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Champs Elysées and the Ile de la Cité, beautiful Notre Dame Cathedral, and the 13th century Sainte Chapelle, a pearl of Gothic architecture. During your visit to the Eiffel Tower you are treated to a unique behind-the-scenes tour. Enter the original engine room, which still operates the elevators, discover the old bunker hidden under the Champ de Mars and admire the beautiful view over the esplanade. During this exclusive visit, many intriguing facts about the history of the Tower are revealed, from the initial project to the challenges of its construction.

    This afternoon, tour one of the city's finest museums. Choose from: the world's largest art museum, the renowned Louvre, home to one of the most important collections in the Western world, with some 35,000 works on display including Da Vinci's 'Mona Lisa' and the 'Venus de Milo'; the lesser-known but exceptionally interesting Musée Marmottan, where paintings from Monet's private collection of his own work and that of his fellow artists are exhibited; or the Musée d'Orsay, housed in a converted railway station and exhibiting examples of Impressionist, Fauvist and late Romantic works.

    Overnight: Hotel Le Meurice, Deluxe Room (Platinum) or Hotel Daniel, Classic Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Design Your Day Paris

    Comb the streets of Paris this morning with a local restaurant critic and food expert, uncovering and tasting some of the city's gourmet secrets. Your tour could include a glimpse into the life of a dedicated chocolatier whose handmade delicacies are made in true artisan style, or a tour of a cheese cellar beneath one of the city's authentic fromageries.

    This afternoon, follow your own heart. Travel further afield to Giverny, where Monet lived and worked in his country house and garden from 1883 to 1926, and experience his intimate surroundings, including the famous Japanese bridge and lily pond in the gardens. Or take a trip to Chantilly, visiting the Chateau de Chantilly with its marvellous illuminated medieval manuscripts, tapestries and paintings as well as the Grandes Ecuries - the grandest stables in France - where you will see various breeds of horses and equine paraphernalia. You may also choose to visit Versailles to admire the splendid palace of the great Sun King Louis XIV, visiting the grand State Apartments and the extensive gardens

    Overnight: Hotel Le Meurice, Deluxe Room (Platinum) or Hotel Daniel, Classic Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Paris - Dijon - La Bussiere Sur Ouche

    Enjoy A&K's Concierge Rail service as you travel First Class by train south-east to Dijon, Burgundy's provincial capital. On arrival, enjoy a walking tour in the Old Town, where you will be delighted by the magnificent half-timbered houses and the Palais des Ducs, which houses the Fine Arts Museum. Continue to your hotel, a converted Cistercian abbey set in 15 acres of beautiful parkland. The old buildings have been transformed into a luxurious country house hotel where you can relax and enjoy the unique, refreshing atmosphere

    Overnight: Abbaye de la Bussiere, Deluxe Room (Platinum) or Abbaye de la Bussiere, Superior Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Beaune

    Today, spend a full day in the picturesque wine country surrounding Beaune. Visit the Clos de Vougeot, one of the largest single vineyards in Burgundy producing grand cru wines, and indulge in a little tasting. Explore the wine capital of Beaune, with its 15th-century Hospice whose elaborate tiled roof is an icon of the Burgundy region

    Overnight: Abbaye de la Bussiere, Deluxe Room (Platinum) or Abbaye de la Bussiere, Superior Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    La Bussiere Sur Ouche - Avignon

    Journey south to Avignon, enjoying A&K's Concierge Rail Service. The remainder of your day is at leisure

    Overnight: La Mirande, Avignon, King Room (Platinum) or La Mirande, Avignon, Queen Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Avignon

    Dominated by its Papal Palace, Avignon is a vibrant Provencal town with abundant character. Discover its glories with a walking tour including a visit to the very impressive fortified Palace. Make your way to the antique street market to pick up a memento before witnessing a sweeping panorama of the town and the mighty Rhône River. Afternoon is at leisure

    Overnight: La Mirande, Avignon, King Room (Platinum) or La Mirande, Avignon, Queen Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    Avignon

    It's time to explore the region. Start with a visit to the delightful market in St Remy de Provence, where you will find a splendid array of regional produce including wild mushrooms, olives, honey, dried herbs and spices. Visit the Roman antiquities, followed by an olive-oil tasting at a mill. Afterwards, visit the ruined medieval citadel of Les Baux and the astonishing nearby "Cathedral of Images" carved out of the rock. The day concludes with a visit to the impressive Pont du Gard, a Roman aqueduct built with astonishing daring as part of a complex irrigation system.

    Overnight: La Mirande, Avignon, King Room (Platinum) or La Mirande, Avignon, Queen Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Depart Marseille

    Adieu la France! Transfer to Marseille airport where your journey ends

    Meal Plan: B

Paris, Loire Valley and Bordeaux

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 Nights

    Highlights: Some of the world's finest food and wine. Romantic cities and beautiful chateaux. World Heritage sites and iconic hotels. Choice of accommodation.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Paris

    Welcome and private transfer to your hotel.

    Overnight: Le Meurice, Deluxe Room (Platinum) or Relais Christine, Superior Room (Gold)

  • Day 2

    Paris

    Visit the world famous Louvre Museum, home to one of the world's most important art collections. The Louvre is housed in a magnificent building which was transformed from a medieval fortress into a Renaissance palace for King François. The main entrance is now covered by a large glass, steel rod and cable pyramid designed by the American architect I.M. Pei. It is home to one of the most important art collections in the Western world, spanning the period from remotest antiquity, through to about 1850. Continue to the Marais, one of Paris's most historic districts. Visit the famous Place des Vosges, lined with pink brick and covered arcades, and the Musée Carnavalet, which documents the history of Paris.

    Overnight: Le Meurice, Deluxe Room (Platinum) or Relais Christine, Superior Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Paris

    Join a Paris restaurant critic and foodie who will spend the morning introducing you to some of the city's best suppliers. Take a glimpse into the life of a dedicated chocolatier or explore a cheese cellar beneath one of the city's true fromageries

    Overnight: Le Meurice, Deluxe Room (Platinum) or Relais Christine, Superior Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Paris - Onzain

    This morning you are driven to the Loire Valley via Chartres where you will see the magnificent Gothic cathedral with its world famous stained glass windows

    Overnight: Domaine des Hauts de Loire, Junior Suite (Platinum) or Domaine des Hauts de Loire, Superior Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Loire Valley

    Starting in Amboise you will visit the Clos Lucé (closed on the 14th November 2011 and re-opening on the 1st April 2012), a handsome Renaissance manor where Leonardo da Vinci spent the last four years of his life. The museum contains working models of some of da Vinci's inventions. Continue to Chateau de Chenonceau that straddles the river and is one of the best known chateaux of the Loire region. Finally, visit the largest of the Loire Chateaux, the Chateau de Chambord

    Overnight: Domaine des Hauts de Loire, Junior Suite (Platinum) or Domaine des Hauts de Loire, Superior Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    Onzain - Bordeaux

    Transfer to St Pierre des Corps railway station and board the TGV train to Bordeaux

    Overnight: The Regent Grand Hotel, Deluxe Room (Platinum) or The Regent Grand Hotel, Superior Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Bordeaux

    A morning tour of Bordeaux. The city is set against a backdrop of neoclassical architecture, wide avenues and pretty parks. You will visit the riverfront esplanade, and the Museum of Fine Arts. Meet a Bordeaux local who welcomes you into her typically Bordelaise home to share a south-western French lunch accompanied by local wines

    Overnight: The Regent Grand Hotel, Deluxe Room (Platinum) or The Regent Grand Hotel, Superior Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 8

    Design Your Day Bordeaux

    With world class vineyards all around you have a choice to make. Explore St. Emilion, a medieval town and UNESCO World Heritage Site noted for its production of fine wines. Visit one of France's largest underground churches and the 13th century ramparts which encircle the village as well as some local vineyards. Or head into the Médoc wine region where you visit several of the most prestigious wine châteaux in the world, Mouton-Rothschild or Chateau Lanessan for example

    Overnight: The Regent Grand Hotel, Deluxe Room (Platinum) or The Regent Grand Hotel, Superior Room (Gold)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Depart Bordeaux

    Transfer to Bordeaux airport for onward flight.

    Meal Plan: B
