ALASKAN BACKCOUNTRY

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 11 Nights

    Enjoy Alaska's largest city - Anchorage. Experience dogsledding and enjoy fantastic opportunities to bear watch.

  • Day 1

    London/Anchorage

    On arrival into Anchorage airport, your driver will take you to Girdwood. This 45-minute drive to the Hotel Alyeska resort, along the Turnagain Arm of Cook Inlet, is one of the world's great drives. The 127-mile Seward Highway is recognized for its scenic, natural, historical wonders.

  • Day 2

    Heli Glacier Dog Sledding

    Head out on your first Alaskan Adventure - Helicopter-supported dogsledding. This is the quintessential Alaskan experience. Stop at the homestead of 4-Time Iditarod Champion Martin Buser for a private visit with his more than 60 dogs and extensive collection of Alaskan artifacts. Martin guided each of the four Iditarod champion teams and is always a favourite for the title. His previous wins were in 1992, 1994, 1997 and 2002.

  • Day 3

    Denali National Park

    The wilderness backcountry of the Denali National Park supports big game species such as black bear, grizzly and moose. Forests and high alpine tundra are also home to porcupine, marmots and beavers.

  • Day 4-5

    Activities within Denali National Park

    Today choose from a range of extraordinary activities available at North Face Lodge - biking along the park road on an independent adventure; fishing in Moose Creek for Arctic Grayling or Wonder Lake for Mackinaw Trout; or paddling a Canoe as you trace "Denali's" reflection in Wonder Lake.

  • Day 6-7

    Tutka Bay

    This morning, meet your pilot for your scenic flight to Anchorage. Fly along Mt. McKinley and the awe-inspiring Alaska Range to the North Face of Denali and over a number of the north side glaciers. Tutka Bay is a splendidly rugged fjord that reaches six miles into the glacier-studded Kenai Mountains. Your lodge is situated on ten acres and includes six guest cabins, main lodge, and a large viewing deck with sauna and hot-tub.

  • Day 8

    Redoubt Bay

    This morning, after breakfast, you will board your float plane for a scenic 60 minute flight to Redoubt Bay Bear Sanctuary. The Redoubt Bay Lodge is a prime location for watching brown bears as it has one of the densest bear populations in the state. You will have the opportunity to see bears up close as they come down to the lake to fish for salmon.

  • Day 9-10

    Winterlake Lodge

    We have planned a day of adventure via helicopter into the pristine wilderness surrounding Winterlake Lodge. In consultation with the Lodge hosts, you are able to make your own detailed itinerary of your exploration into the backcountry.

  • Day 11

    Winterlake Lodge

    Today enjoy a morning of relaxation or choose a lodge-based activity. After lunch, board your float plane bound for Anchorage, where your driver will take you to the Hotel Captain Cook for the evening.

  • Day 12

    Anchorage

    Your driver will meet you this morning in the hotel lobby to drive you to the Anchorage Airport in plenty of time for your departure flight.

Family Alaska

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 Days

    View Alaska's pristine wilderness (populated by moose, bears, humpback whales and sea lions) on an adventure-packed journey that includes, fishing, geocaching and taking a scenic flight over Denali.

    Highlights: • See calving glaciers and marine wildlife, such as humpback whales, orcas, sea lions and harbor seals, on a Kenai Fjords cruise
    • Take a scenic train ride on the Alaska Railroad GoldStar Dome train from Seward to Girdwood
    • Experience a private wildlife rafting trip and riverside barbecue dinner at Kenai National Wildlife Refuge
    • See wild bears as they fish for spawning salmon - a unique opportunity - at Redoubt Bay
    • Embark on a private salmon fishing trip on the Susitna River
    • Flight-see over Denali National Park, landing on a glacier at the base of Mount McKinley
    • Meet with Martin Buser, a four-time Iditarod champion, and his team of huskies

  • Day 1

    Anchorage, Alaska/Kenai Peninsula

    Arrive in Anchorage to begin your adventure, complete with glacier trekking and wildlife encounters in America's unspoiled wilderness. At a welcome reception this evening, meet and get to know the families who share your journey.

    overnight: Hotel Alyeska

  • Day 2

    Kenai Peninsula

    Visit the Alaska Wildlife Conservation Center en route to the Kenai River. This nonprofit organization rehabilitates injured or orphaned animals and returns them to the wild. During your visit, you may see local wildlife, including bears, moose, bison, elk and a variety of birds. This afternoon, take a float trip through Kenai National Wildlife Refuge. Beavers and otters make their homes along the water, and bears and moose are commonly spied on shore. Tonight, sit down to a barbecue dinner, followed by a riverside campfire and s'mores roast.

    Overnight: Hotel Alyeska
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 3

    Kenai Peninsula

    Cruise the Kenai Fjords National Park, and witness the crash of calving glaciers. The park occupies more than half a million acres and is covered by an ice pack extending more than 700 square miles. You may see humpback whales, orcas, puffins and sea lions at close range; look to the skies for a glimpse of bald eagles overhead. Ride the double-decker Alaska Railroad GoldStar Dome train, which is adorned with original Alaskan art and features an upper-level, open-air platform and a glass-covered observation deck offering sensational views of the countryside.

    Overnight: Hotel Alyeska
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 4

    Kenai Peninsula/Redoubt Bay/Talkeetna

    Travel by float plane over Cook Inlet to Redoubt Bay Bear Sanctuary, where a large population of bears prowl the river in search of a meal. Upon arrival, you are briefed on proper "bear etiquette" before taking to the creeks on pontoon boats. Watch safely from your pontoon boat as they snatch salmon from the water - sometimes even in mid-leap. Continue to the town of Talkeetna, where your lodge is poised on a hillside, with stunning views of Mount McKinley (conditions permitting).

    Overnight: Talkeetna Alaskan Lodge
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 5

    Talkeetna

    The Susitna River teems with some of the world's best salmon and trout, and this morning, you set off on a fishing trip. Afterward, enjoy a riverside picnic lunch to take in the beauty of the surrounding landscapes. Although you may not rake in as many fish as the bears you saw yesterday did, with the supplied fishing equipment - tackle, boots and rain gear - you should have a very rewarding morning nonetheless. This afternoon, go on a geocaching adventure, a high-tech treasure hunt using handheld GPS devices, on the grounds of your lodge.

    Overnight: Talkeetna Alaskan Lodge
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 6

    Talkeetna

    Continue your exploration of the Denali area with a kayak trip on the placid waters of Byers Lake (equipment is provided). Take a gentle but invigorating hike on a well-maintained forest trail; your guide introduces Alaska's plant and animal life along the way. This afternoon, you may explore the quaint downtown of Talkeetna, a three-square-block area centered on the Village Park; 13 of its buildings are on the National Register of Historic Places. Step out to the banks of the Talkeetna River to look for wildlife and enjoy spectacular views of Mount McKinley and the Alaska Range. You may also visit Denali National Park's Talkeetna Ranger Station, the last stop for climbing expeditions headed up Mount McKinley.

    Overnight: Talkeetna Alaskan Lodge
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 7

    Talkeetna/Anchorage

    Take a thrilling flight over the peaks and glaciers of Denali National Park. Fly over Don Sheldon Amphitheater (a natural formation named for a renowned Alaskan pilot), the two-mile-deep Great Gorge and the Kahiltna Glacier, a popular route for climbers attempting the summit of Mount McKinley. Your pilot, linked to you by two-way headset throughout the flight, explains the highlights you see. Land on a glacier's surface, and disembark to experience the majesty of Alaska's beauty, with snow-veiled peaks surrounding a vast snowfield stretching in every direction. This afternoon, meet and have lunch with four-time Iditarod champion Martin Buser, and meet his Alaskan husky team.

    Continue to Anchorage and enjoy a brief city tour, including the downtown district, and a demonstration of the traditional Alaska Ulus. Celebrate your Alaskan adventure at a farewell dinner tonight.

    Overnight: Hotel Captain Cook
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 8

    Depart Anchorage

    Board your homebound flight.

    Meal Plan: B

ALASKA CRUISE SILVERSEA

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 Nights

    Highlights:- Experience the dramatic sounds of the glacier 'calving'
    - Visit Alaska's oldest and most scenic towns
    - Cruise Inside Passage
    - Vancouver with its stunning skyline

  • Day 1

    UK/Anchorage

    Depart the UK for Alaska flying via Seattle. Spend your first Alaskan night in one of the state's best properties, The Captain Cook Hotel, or add on some additional days to explore this wonderful and varied city.

  • Day 2

    Embark Seward

    Seward is one of Alaska's oldest and most scenic towns. One of the Kenai Peninsula's major communities, it lies at the south end of theSeward Highway. It's also the jumping off point for your luxury Alaskan cruise aboard the Silver Shadow.

  • Day 3

    Cruising the Hubbard Glacier

    Advancing 7 feet a day, the Hubbard Glacier is an awe-inspiring highlight on this cruise. Experiencing the dramatic sounds of the glacier 'calving' (breaking apart and crashing into the icy sea) during your voyage will be unforgettable. The ship will venture close to the glacier itself where you experience the blue intensity of the ice. Unbelievably, it takes around 400 years for the ice to traverse the length of the glacier.

  • Day 4

    Sitka

    Only the smallest excursion vessels can dock at Sitka. This small town is sandwiched between high mountains and the Pacific Ocean. It's hard not to like Sitka, with its eclectic blend of native, Russian, and American history and its dramatic and beautiful setting.

  • Day 5

    Cruising Tracyarm/Sawyer Glacier/Juneau

    Squeezed between glacier-carved walls nearly 3000 feet high, Tracyarm Fjord is spectacular. Literally hundreds of waterfalls cascade down the sides of the fjord. Giant sections of ice break off and tumble into the freezing waters. It's a magical and unforgettable experience. Juneau, Alaska's capital and third-largest city, is on the North American mainland but can't be reached by road. Its dramatic hillside position and historic buildings provide an unusual frontier feeling, but the city's cosmopolitan nature comes through in its fine museums and noteworthy restaurants. In addition to enjoying the city itself, take the tramway to the alpine trails at the summit of Mt.Roberts. The Juneau area offers densely forested wilderness areas, quiet bays for sea kayaking and excellent wildlife spotting opportunities.

  • Day 6

    Skagway

    This seaport sits on a deepwater fjord, the Taiya Inlet, at the head of Lynn Canal, which stretches 90 miles from Juneau between banks that seem untouched by human hands. Skagway is perhaps the easiest port in Alaska to explore on foot. Spend the day exploring the wilderness.

  • Day 7

    Ketchikan

    Known for its colourful totem poles, rainy skies, steep streets, and lush island setting, Ketchikan is a favourite stop for travellers on this cruise. Seaplanes depart for Misty Fjords, and commercial fishing boats laden with salmon head home to Tongass Narrows.

  • Day 8

    Cruising the Inside Passage

    The famed Inside Passage is another much anticipated adventure. The sealife here is abundant. Look out for humpback whales spouting, seals, bears, sea otters and bald eagles buzzing overhead.

  • Day 9

    Vancouver

    The final day of your cruise arrives into the vibrant Canadian city of Vancouver with its stunning skyline and bustling harbour. We'd recommend extra time in this area to explore Whistler, Victoria Island and perhaps one of our specially chosen wilderness resorts for more close up wildlife watching opportunities.

    There's the chance today to spend some time independently exploring the city, doing some shopping for gifts or relaxing at the hotel.

    1st August - Transfer to Calgary and flight home
    After breakfast your private SUV will transfer you on the two hour journey from the Fairmont Banff Springs to Calgary Airport for your flight home.
