Australia Highlights & Sightseeing

  • The Great Barrier Reef

    The Great Barrier Reef is world's largest coral reef spread out over 1600 miles. This biggest living structure in the world is large enough to be seen from the outer space, making it one of the most fascinating places to see in Australia.

    Australia's most incredible natural wonders, it consists of over 2,900 individual reefs and 900 islands brimming with life. The reef supports soft corals, turtles and brightly colored fish.

    The Daintree Rainforest

    About 135 million years old, the Daintree Rainforest on the north east coast of Queensland offers some of the most magnificent sceneries on the Australian continent. Intense waterfalls, diverse flora and fauna encompass a vast area of 1,200 sq km.

    This World Heritage listed tropical rainforest is considered as spectacular as the reef itself and is home to various prehistoric and rare life forms. It also houses a "Daintree Important Bird Area" where nearly 430 species of birds dwell. Follow the hiking trails, admire the scenic backdrop or take a refreshing swim in its fresh water bodies. You can also explore the life in Daintree Rainforest at nature's own pace!

    Read more

    The Red Centre

    The Red Centre in the Australian Outback is the metamorphic heart of Australia. The world's largest monolith Uluru (Ayers Rock) and mystical 36 Kata Tjuta domes, sand dunes, jagged rock structures and native Aboriginal inheritance bestows The Red Centre with an authentic Australian experience.

    Extreme heat, endless extensive spaces and an ever dominating hue of intense red overwhelms visitors as they enter Uluru. One can still witness the Aboriginal symbolic rock paintings in yellow, brown, red and white ochre depicting the timeless stories of the native Australians.

    Read more

    Kakadu National Park

    Kakadu National Park is over 40,000 year old terrain which is a landscape teeming with life and cultural Aboriginal heritage of the region. The region proudly preserves the first remains of human occupation in the Australian island and showcases a diverse complex ecosystem which has remained unaffected by the European settlement in the rest of the continent.

    It is a numinous land which still bears the spiritual signatures and legacy of the natives of Australia. Kakadu is home to one of the most illustrious rock art sites in the world as well as some of the richest and diverse flora in North Australia. Over 17,000 plants & vegetation inhabit the landscape of the region. Moreover it is also brimming with wildlife with 280 bird species, approximately 60 wild animals and over 117 reptiles, among them 11 types of turtle and tortoise. Yet, it is the crocodile that, perhaps, interests visitors the most.

    The Flinders Ranges

    Flinders Ranges is the largest mountain range in South Australia. These ruggedly spectacular mountains rise sharply out of vast landscape and extend to pastoral runs created by springs and waterholes. Here you will discover a lost world inside a vast stone basin, moonscapes and barren lakes, opals and fossils, national parks and Aboriginal rock art.

    The Flinders Ranges date back upto a billion years and as result, is an explorers' delight for being a goldmine of undiscovered fossils underneath its core. This ancient landscape is a brilliant blend of flora fauna and wildlife making it a must visit for any tourist visiting Australia. Nature lovers can admire the centuries old red gum trees with some of the deepest root systems.

    Sydney

    Sydney the iconic harbor city is Australia's most populated city as well. This capital of New South Wales offers a complete tourist experience. Sightseeing Australia Sydney is incredible with its throbbing nightlife, delightful restaurants, retail outlets, and endless attractions.

    Sydney Tower presents a 360 degree view extending as far as the Blue Mountains on a clear day. The best way to explore this vibrant city is to start from its focal point, Circular Quay and stroll further to the Harbour Bridge, and Sydney Opera House, the remarkable structure which is often considered an architectural masterpiece of 20th century.

    Kangaroo Island

    Wallabies, goannas, koalas, kangaroos, echidnas, dolphins, sea lions, penguins, fur seals, eagles, whales and ospreys: Say hello to some of the natives of Australia's third largest island - The Kangaroo Island.

    This region is often regarded as Australia's great scenic treasures and is an interesting mix of attractive small townships and fascinating wilderness. The Kangaroo Island features a contrasting landscape of desert dunes, stunning beaches, forests, and farmlands. This ancient Island is also swarmed by ecologists and animal lovers for the rare observation of the seals and penguins. If you are a nature lover Kangaroo Island is certainly a fabulous place to stay and explore its many facets and its interesting and welcoming lifestyle.

    Tasmania

    Tasmania is the Island state in South of Australia, separated by the Bass Strait. Because of its large and relatively unblemished natural state, it is also regarded as the 'Island of Inspiration', moreover over 40% of region here in protected in form of national parks and reserves. The island boasts of excellent hiking trails, wild surfing opportunities and mountains to climb.

    A rich natural landscape characterized with wilderness, mountains, cloudy white beaches, existing Aboriginal heritage, along with modern colonial settlement Tasmania is truly "a world apart, not a world away".

    The Great Ocean Road

    The Great Ocean Road is one of the most spectacular and iconic coastal drives in the world. It is an Australian National Heritage listed 243-kilometre (151 mi) stretch of road between the Victorian cities of Torquay and Warrnambool.

    Witness not only the scenic natural coastlines but also the famed 12 Apostles, adventurous surf breaks and waterfalls, making the whole stretch a fulfilling destination for families and young travelers alike.

    Lord Howe Island

    The Lord Howe Island Group is a geological wonder! It is an outstanding example of oceanic islands of volcanic origin containing a unique biota of plants and animals. The island is a natural treasure, inscribed on UNESCO's World Heritage Site of global natural significance.

    Here you will observe that the most of the island is virtually untouched forest with many of the plants and animals found nowhere else in the world. This is complemented with a range of spectacular landscapes, including volcanic mountains, diverse low-lying rainforests, and palm forests. Crystal clear waters teem with marine life and rare coral is protected as part of the Lord Howe Island Marine Park. To preserve its sensitive small island environment only 400 tourists are allowed at any one time.
