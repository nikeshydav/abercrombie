Argentina Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Buenos Aires

    Being the most effervescent style capital of the continent, Buenos Aries has been dubbed as the "Paris of the South America". An exuberant street life, bustling art galleries & museums, fine restaurants, hip bars and an alluring mix of republican architecture characterize the capital city of Argentina.

    Spend a few days over here to immerse yourself in the life of Buenos Aires, stroll around the boutiques and cafés of Palemo, then go on to explore the elegant historical quarters of Montserrat, Recoleta and San Telmo. This seductive port city doesn't possess a dominating monument or landmark unlike many other capital cities, rather it is dotted with various hubs, each with its own unique character and details. When here, don't forget to travel around Mar del Plata, Plaza de Mayo, Carmelo, Punta Del Este or visit one of the many fine dining restaurants and indulge yourself with superior quality Argentinean wine and steak that the country is illustrious for.

    The Iguazú Falls

    The most monumental waterfall in the world is situated on the border of Brazil and Argentina forming a natural frontier between the two nations. These majestic falls extends to 2700 m embracing 275 separate cascades among which the infamous "Devil's Throat" the largest which is over 80 m high and 700 m long. The falls are surrounded by two national parks on either sides empowering the flora and fauna of hundreds of rare and endangered species caimans, toucans and various parrots.

    The sheer magnitude and supremacy of Iguazu Falls often evokes awe inspiring emotions in the visitors especially when viewed from the Zodiac trip. Famously, upon her first visit the former First Lady, Eleanor Roosevelt reportedly exclaimed "Poor Niagara" impressed by the raw force of nature of the falls.

    Salta

    Salta is a vibrant metropolis located in the Lerma Valley at an altitude of 1152 ft above sea level. The city was founded as a strategic commercial and military point between Lima and Buenos Aires which explains its fine colonial architecture and character.

    Dubbed Salta la Linda ("Salta the beautiful"), this city is a major tourist destination owing to its old, colonial buildings, tourism friendliness, remarkable weather and the natural scenery of the valleys surrounding it. North of Salta showcases splendid illustrations of structures such as Seven Colors hills in Purmamarca. Extend the visit to neighboring towns such as Cachi and Cafayate offer insight into Salta's aristocratic history. While you're there, do not forget to sip on the region's unique Torrontes wine.

    Mendoza

    Mendoza is snuggled up at the foothills of the Andes' eastern slopes, offering a gateway into Argentina's flourishing wine regions and home of Malbec grape. From Mendoza one can travel further and explore, Aconcagua, South America's highest mountain.

    Mendoza is without a doubt one of the most interesting cultural and gastronomic hubs of the country. A short stay dedicated to exploring the great food, wine and lifestyle can be a very fulfilling experience of an Argentinean holiday. Almost 70% of Argentina's wine production is credited to Mendoza's wineries that feature thriving vineyards where guests are welcomed to visit and sample wines with the vineyard owners.

    Bariloche

    Bariloche is regarded as "Little Switzerland" due to its resemblance to an alpine paradise blessed with beautiful surrounding landscape. It is no surprise that this region was inhabited by Germans and Austrians and now is a lively winter sports destination offering skiing, snowboarding, trekking slopes.

    Past the town skyline, Bariloche offers a dynamic landscape dominated by snowcapped peaks leaning over sapphire blue lakes. The region is also crowded with water sport enthusiasts indulging in fly-fishing, golf, hiking, horse-back riding, and kayaking. Discover one of the hidden gems Bariloche, which is one of the most gorgeous tourist places in Argentina as well as a convenient entry point into Chile via an adventurous journey through road or boat.

    Southern Patagonia

    Sightseeing in Argentina in unfinished until a visit to the Southern Patagonia boasting of some of the most dramatic landscapes in the world! The vast and wild plains of Southern Patagonia stretches over 370 kms from north to south showcasing some of the most powerful forces of nature. This imposing contiguous extra-polar ice field is the second largest in the world and features a number of UNESCO'S World Heritage Listed Sites.

    Visitors can witness a jaw dropping site of the moving glacier of Perito Moreno, while listening to the haunting creaking sounds as the shards of ice calve off into the lake below. Or watch the penguins, and discover what the life is like down here, at the very foot of the world. Argentina's southernmost region offers an unspoiled and untamed place on earth where very few inhabitants make their living through activities such as fishing, sheep ranching, and natural resource extraction.

    El Chaltén a small town in the Santa Cruz province is often a base to begin the Patagonian expedition from where one can visit the majestic Monte Fitz Roy, which at 3,500 meters remains technically the most challenging peaks to climb in the world. Then there is Ushuaia, the capital of province of Tierra del Fuego, situated on the Beagle Strait. This southernmost city in the world and commonly used as a base for hiking, winter sports around the region and cruises to Cape Horn and Antarctica.

    The Valdes Peninsula

    Valdes peninsula is a fauna reserve which has been declared as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. In this fascinating wild-life sanctuary, sea wolves, elephant seals, killer whales, penguins, and a variety of birds share living space with each other. This also one of the rare spots in the whole world where the Southern White whales reproduction takes place.

    Visitors can take boat cruises from the coastline and admire these species in their natural habitat. Those with an adventure streak can also go scuba diving and snorkeling, or trekking at one of the cliffs in the region.
