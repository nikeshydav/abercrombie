  • CASA PARATY

    The charming and private Casa Paraty is in the heart of the colonial town of Paraty located between Rio and Sao Paulo along Brazil's stunning Costa Verde. Paraty is nestled in between the imposing mountains and the ocean, a pretty and dramatic location. This private house's simple colonial exterior hides a luxurious property bursting with state of the art features and local decoration and offers the very best accommodation in the region.

    Your local cook can prepare meals in the full service kitchen and choose to dine inside or out next to the pool & barbeque. This beautiful house has a state of the art kitchen and an open plan lounge and dining room opening out onto a walled garden and pool. On the first floor you find the elegant Master suite and a further twin room, both ensuite, with a triple attic bedroom on the second floor with views out to sea.

    CASAS BRANCAS

    Casa Brancas is a chic Mediterranean-style pousada typical of the region. Nestled in the hills overlooking the stunning bay of Búzios, the hotel is a little slice of paradise, with Mediterranean architecture, multilingual staff and a refined, laid-back atmosphere.

    The hotel's 32 cosy rooms are individually decorated and tucked away around small courtyards and terraces, nearly all offering superb ocean views. Their white-washed exterior walls, curved into organic shapes, conceal calm, homely interiors, where cool, muted décor provides a backdrop for elegant carved wooden furniture and accent colours in greens and reds. All have cooling stone floors and huge windows to let the coastal light flood in.

    INSOLITO

    Insólito boutique hotel has an unrivalled location, fantastic sea views and brings a new luxury to this pretty town. Despite its superb, secluded location, the hotel is only a short taxi ride away form the excitement of Rua Das Pedras (the main street in the town), which buzzes with shops, restaurants and bars.

    The 12 rooms are individually and lovingly designed by the French owner, Emanuelle Meeus de Clermont-Tonnerre, who tours the world sourcing new furniture and fashions for the property. All reflect different aspects of culture, art or fashion while still achieving a stylish simplicity.

    Designer touches are on display throughout - the Pigmento Ovo table in the living room, the wonderful rustic wooden sofas by Elma Chavez, the recycled rubber carpets by Cecília Machado, the photos by Claudia Andujar, stickers by André Diniz and the young Brazilian designer Juliana Llussa.
