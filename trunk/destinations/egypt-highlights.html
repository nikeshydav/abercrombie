Egypt Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Cairo

    With spectacular pyramids, the mystifying Sphinx, the imposing Nile and year round Sun, the capital city of Egypt is a cultural gem and has tourists flocking to its shores to soak up its fascinating and mystical history and enjoy the many activities that map up a memorable holiday diary.

    Cairo, or 'Al Qahira' fuses ancient history with modern day comforts and you'll find plenty of activities to plug you in and your holidays in Egypt cannot be called complete without a visit to this beautiful city. Take a walk through the fantastic Khan El-Khalili market and spend some time bargaining for gold, silver, glass and ceramics. Or head to any Arabic nightclub and be a witness to an exotic belly dancing session. The Great Pyramids of Giza and the Egyptian Museum are popular sites to visit on holidays in Cairo.

    Luxor

    If you are looking for plenty of spectacular sights to visit, a holiday to Luxor certainly won't disappoint. Ancient royal tombs, sprawling vast temples, bustling markets and miles and miles of golden desert sand is what you will find on your holiday calendar to Luxor.

    Earlier known as Thebes and once an ancient Egyptian capital of the New Kingdom Pharaohs, Luxor today, is known for the world's 'greatest open-air museum.' From the tomb of Tutankhamen in the Valley of the Kings, Colossi of Memnon and the magnificent views of the sunset at the majestic temple complexes of Karnak to the exhilarating cruises across the Nile, Luxor is home to more than half of the country's antiquities making it the perfect choice for people looking to dive deep into the cultural sea.

    Aswan

    An all time favorite winter destination, Aswan or 'Jewel of the Nile' is Egypt's sunniest southern city and a serene Nile valley destination. Located about 899 km from Cairo, this small Egyptian city is home to the Aswan High Dam, and Temple of Philae and ABy Simbel. Mountain, cliffs and jagged outcroppings formed by pink and grey granites are a sight to behold.

    The perfect destination to take a leisurely stroll and relax in a delightful cultural setting, you can wander down the broad walkway popularly known as the cornice, to watch boats sailing unhurriedly across the Nile, then stop at one of the many floating restaurants to enjoy some Nubian music and freshly caught fish. Aswan, earlier known as Nubia, is where the Nile flows at its majestic best and serves as a great starting point for a Nile cruise.

    Sharm El Sheikh

    Sun soaked days and warm, balmy nights. Blessed with lovely climate all year round, Sharm-el-Sheikh is the diving mecca with rare tropical fish, deep reef walls and beautiful corals. Located on the southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula, it is well known for its crystal clear waters, beautiful sandy beaches, dazzling nightlife, breathtaking views and a stunning underwater world.

    Naama Bay is the central area with hotels aplenty. Water lovers can indulge in snorkeling, sailing, wind and kite-surfing or even skydiving or parachuting here. Create the perfect backdrop for a relaxing and memorable holiday when you choose to spend some retreat-time at Sharm-el-Shaikh, the oceans of which boast of a colorful marine life. St. Catherine Monastery and Ras Mohamed National Park are other areas to visit.

    The Western Desert

    Arid though beautiful. Travel through miles and miles of golden dunes, witness inexplicable rock formations, take a halt at an oasis and spot a mirage. This is Egypt's Western Desert. Explore the adventurous side of the desert in this rugged beauty. Spanning roughly 262,000 square miles of dunes, canyons, oases, mountainous plateaus and valleys, this tremendously overwhelming sight of magical landscape is full of strange limestone formations.

    Hiking, bird watching, camel and jeep safaris, fossil hunting and camping are some eventful opportunities to be enjoyed. Key attractions are Valley of the Golden Mummies and the oasis towns of Bahareva, Farafra, Dakhla, Kharga and Siwa. Swathed in veils of myth and mysteries, you need to unravel the Western Desert layer by layer.

    Alexandria

    Also called as the 'Pearl of the Mediterranean,' Alexandria is the second largest city and a fusion town of history and modernity. About 225 kilometres from Cairo, it has more the Mediterranean touch. Named after the great explorer and conqueror Alexander the Great in 331 BC, it gained importance as the capital of the Greco-Roman Egypt.

    The setting for the stormy relationship between Cleopatra and Mark Antony, Alexandria was also the center of learning in the ancient world and houses the legendary lighthouse that was once the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Famous for its exotic fish restaurants, this vibrant city houses attractions like Bibliotheca Alexandrina, Fort Qaitbay, the Roman Amphitheatre, the Alexandria National Museum and Pompey's Pillar. Re-live the ancient charm in the beautiful city of Alexandria.
