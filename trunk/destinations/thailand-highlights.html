Thailand Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Bangkok

    Once nothing more than a small hamlet on the banks of the historical Chao Phraya River, Bangkok today, is much more than just the capital of Thailand. It is the confluence of Asia's richest cultures. Known to the Thais as Krung Thep, or the city of angels, Bangkok was forever immortalized if not for the wrong reasons in Murray Head's 80s pop classic 'One Night in Bangkok.'

    Revel in ancient marvels. Shop for some bright, fragrant blooms at the flower market or get in touch with your spiritual streak at the numerous shrines and quiet temples hazy with incense smoke and low chants coming from saffron-clad monks. The lively collection of noodle stalls, bustling flea markets and designer shopping malls ensure no visitor is ever left bored. Rejuvenate your senses with a traditional Thai massage in the late afternoon to soothe your tired mind or dance the night away at its many happening bars and nightclubs, Bangkok beckons you to indulge in every temptation.     Ayutthaya

    Once the capital of Siam for over four centuries, Ayutthaya lies at a short distance north of Bangkok. In times of power, it stretched well into Laos, Cambodia and Myanmar. As merchants traveled into the city, it became an important commercial center for trade and commerce.

    Explore and appreciate Thai history here. Marvel at the grandeur reflected through numerous magnificent structures and ruins that generously dotted the island 417 years ago. Studded with archaeological marvels, headless Buddhas and crumbling but still awe-inspiring buildings and artifacts, can only give us an insight into the magnificence and beauty the city once heralded.

    Chiang Mai

    Rightly called 'The Rose of the North,' this 'Culture Capital' of Thailand is a charming and laidback 700 year old town, famous for sightseeing, trekking, shopping, resorts and wellness spas! This is Thailand's second largest city and here is where you will find century old temples, bustling street markets, cozy guest houses and café's at every corner and pagodas at every bend in the road.

    The gateway to the ancient Lanna Kingdom, it is the perfect base from where to explore Northern Thailand and and the more ethnic minorities and hill tribes that still inhabit the area.

    Chiang Rai

    At 488 miles north of Bangkok, Chiang Rai is the north-most province of Thailand. Located within the Golden Triangle area where Myanmar, Laos and Thailand converge, it is also known as the gateway to Myanmar, Laos and Southern China.

    Known for its stunning mountain scenery, Chiang Rai is home to several different hill tribes of which Hmong, Karen, Lahu, Lisu, Akha and Mien are the most important ones. Forced out of their native countries Myanmar, China and Tibet by civil war and political pressures, each tribe has its own fascinating set of colorful customs, culture, clothing and language. Famed for its sea of mist at sunrise, the pleasant climate produces colorful blooms and the large meadow on top provides a breathtaking view of Laos that leaves the visitor in awe.

    Phuket

    Life truly is a beach at Phuket, Thailand's largest island! Blessed with magnificent coves and bays, powdery and palm fringed white sand beaches, shimmering turquoise waters, uninhabited islands, sheer limestone outcrops, warm, hospitable people, wonderful accommodation options and great seafood, Phuket is a delightful experience for a truly memorable getaway.

    Perfect for pure relaxation, luxurious spa therapy and water activities, it is a water-sport enthusiast's paradise, its blue-green crystal clear water making for an ideal snorkeling, sailing and scuba- diving destination. A convenient starting point for island hopping to the many nearby islands, as well as tours of Phang Nga Bay, sightseeing in Thailand isn't complete until you've taken a stopover at Phuket.

    Koh Samui

    Lounge your days away on the beach while soaking up the sun and cooling it off on turquoise waters. Lying just off the eastern coast of the country and sitting snugly in the gulf of Thailand, Koh Samui is Thailand's third largest island. With soft, sandy beaches shaded by towering palms, delicious, fresh seafood and a buzzing, vibrant nightlife, Koh Samui has a magical formula that seems to cast a spell on one and all.

    First settled because of its suitability for growing a large number of coconuts that could be harvested and sent to Bangkok and other parts of the world, Koh Samui is characterized by its beautiful long, white beaches, crystal clear waters and verdant hinterland and has, thus, developed into one of the best known beach destinations in the world.

    Krabi

    An ideal getaway destination, Krabi is chock full of white, sandy beaches, fascinating coral reefs, numerous islands and lush, abundant forests with coves and waterfalls.

    Not very far from Phuket, An ideal place for eco and adventure activities, Krabi is famous for canoeing, sea kayaking, swimming, scuba diving, island hopping, sun bathing and rock climbing. Not very far from Phuket, Krabi is a coastal province that encompasses many beaches and one of the most beautiful islands in Asia, Phi Phi, which was the location for the film, The Beach.
