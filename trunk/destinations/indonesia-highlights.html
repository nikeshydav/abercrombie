Indonesia Highlights & Sightseeing

  • BALI

    Small obviously doesn't mean limited. Rightly so with Bali, the most visitor friendly island, mapping the whole arc of sightseeing in Indonesia. This small though beautiful destination is the jewel in Indonesia's crown and is an ultimate hit with adventure seekers and relaxation seekers alike. The foamy waters of its white sandy beaches are ideal for diving, snorkeling and a host of other water sports while its thick, dense jungles, teeming with screeching monkeys and shrill cries of the cicada and hidden treasures in age-old stone temples shout out for exploration.

    Ubud offers a wealth of arts and crafts whilst Nusa Dua is an ideal base to explore the nearby beaches of Sanur, Legian and Kuta. East Bali, often referred to as 'Old Bali,' boasts of a myriad mix of religion, royal culture and architectural splendor. The region teems with villages whose way of life has changed little over the centuries, such as the closed, religious community of Tenganan.

    KOMODO & MOYO

    A hilly and desolate yet beautiful island, Komodo is one of the 17,508 islands that make up Indonesia. Enclosing a surface area of 390 square kilometers and a permanent population of over 2,000, it lies sandwiched between Flores and Sumbawa. Descendents of former convicts, the population here primarily adheres to Islam, though there are also Hindu and Christian congregations too. Part of the Lesser Sunda chain of islands and Komodo National Park, it is the only place in the world to spot the fearsome yet legendary Komodo dragon! The world's largest lizard, it can reach over 3 meters in length, weigh up to 100 kg and feeds on animals as big as deer and buffalo. Komodo is home to superb beaches and un-spoilt corals, making the island a popular destination for divers.

    Lying 3 kilometers off the north coast of Sumbawa, the pristine milieu of Moyo Islands consists of rugged coastline and grassy savannah and is home to all sorts of wildlife. A coastal paradise where you can take a refreshing dip in the Moyo waterfalls, the main attraction of the island lies beneath the surf. See the jungle tree with its faux jungle 'oranges,' swim in the clear, sublime waters of the Flores Sea and see the bright corals below. Dive from the Angel Reef if you are the adventurous type. The 40 meter vertical drop is an instant adrenalin rush and is a great vantage point for Longfin Bannerfish, Triggerfish, Batfish and Blacktips. The deadly yellow blue ringed Octopus can be spotted if you decide to dive from Panjang Reef.

    LOMBOK

    Lambok may not be the fabled image of Bali, but it certainly has better beaches and far lesser crowds and therefore, is a much thronged tourist attraction in Indonesia. Lambok's wonderful coastlines are its trump card - pristine, sandy beaches with wild, foamy surf in the beaches of the south; sweeping bays and coves on the west coast and the magical coral reefed Gili islands in the northwest.

    Immerse yourself in the wonderful natural scenery, rich culture and incredible diversity. Get off the beaten track as you roam around places unknown, take a variety of unusual transportation, meet interesting people and getting to know their stories, and discover the local Sasak culture. Life, will then, be no less than a party.
