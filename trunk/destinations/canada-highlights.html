Canada Highlights & Sightseeing

  • ALBERTA

    Alberta is a province located in western Canada which was named after Princess Alberta, the fourth daughter of Victoria, the Queen of Canada and Albert, Prince Consort.

    This vast region stretches out over 1200 kms which has rendered it diverse climatic conditions and topography to go along with it. Towards the north, you find Northwest Territories or the world famous national parks of Banff and Jasper along The Rockies at the western border, whereas the prairie land in the south is host to a diverse wildlife such as buffalo, grizzly and black bears, wild moose, white tail deer.

    And there is not just wildlife to be admired, but a range of activities can be undertaken as well including, swimming and fishing in its many lakes while soaking up its natural beauty and a wholesome relaxing ambience.

    Summer is the time when Alberta comes alive with cultural activities. Edmonton, in particular has been rightly dubbed as 'The Festival City' thanks to its popular folk festivals boasting of local heritage, music, arts, cuisine which usually take place in the Churchill Square. Also, if you are in Calgary during July make sure to partake in Canada's biggest rodeo festival, 'The Stampede'.

    BRITISH COLUMBIA

    Canada's most westerly province, British Columbia extends from the Rocky Mountains in the east to the Pacific Ocean in the West. This vast expanse is blessed with enviable natural beauty which is as diverse as it is stunning. There are mountains to climb, rivers to raft in, tepid lakes to lounge around, and woodlands to hike.

    Tofino on the west coast of Vancouver Island in the Clayoquot Sound UNESCO Biosphere Reserve is a popular tourist hub during the summer because of its beautiful natural beauty, hot springs, and camping sites. In fact, there is no better destination for winter sports than Vancouver and Whistler, which were also hosts to the 2012 Winter Olympics and Paralympic Winter Games. Its renowned steeps and deep terrains are perfect for a once-in-a lifetime ski experience and the region is teeming with world class lodges and ski chalets.

    British Columbia is also home to a large percentage of indigenous Nuu-chah-nulth population belonging to the First Nation, who have been dwelling here for over 10,000 years and have successfully preserved their legends, arts and language all this while.

    MANITOBA

    Manitoba used to be a hub for fur traders in the 17th century, however today it is a popular hotspot for adventure seekers and outdoor lovers. This striking Prairie land is a popular Canada tourist attraction and offers a range of fun and adventure activities for visitors of all taste and preferences such as canoeing, fishing, hiking and much more. Also, experience the renowned Canadian hospitality in the charming Pembina Valley villages.

    Winnipeg, the capital city flaunts a unique concurrence of the big city buzz and small town allure. The Interlake region is where you'll discover the legends and remarkable history of the place.

    Yukon

    Thanks to its sparsely populated expanses, snowcapped mountains and glacial water bodies, Yukon can bestow one with some of the most unique Canada sightseeing moments. In summer months, the long cold winter days are replaced with warm sun and bright spring like hues decking its natural landscape. Visitors have abundant opportunities to enjoy, paddling across the Yukon River, flying over majestic Mount Logan or taking up the challenge of ice climbing, as well a chance to catch a rare sight of Canadian wildlife.

    However fun doesn't stop during the winter as well, as that is the perfect time to try dog sledging. And, it is also the appropriate time to devour the mesmerizing sights of the cosmic wonders of aurora borealis in the clear polar night sky.
