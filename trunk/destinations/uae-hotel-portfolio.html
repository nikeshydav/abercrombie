SUGGESTED DUBAI ACCOMMODATION

  • Al Maha Desert Resort And Spa

    The resort lies nestled in a verdant oasis in one of Arabia's largest nature reserves - home to gazelle, oryx, numerous species of birds, and some of Arabia's most impressive dune and desert landscapes.

    The Al Maha Desert Resort & Spa is secreted amongst 86 square miles of sand dunes and desert plain, with the Hajar Mountain providing a dramatic backdrop.


    Built in the style of an ancient Bedouin settlement, with the neutral toned 'tents' shading individual suites, this secluded, internationally-awarded hideaway resort is redolent with the history of the region. This sanctuary located in the sands, offers tented style suites featuring Arabian antiques and handcrafted furniture and are themed red for sand, blue for sky or green for oasis.

  • Anantara Dubai The Palm Resort & Spa

    Anantara Dubai The Palm Resort & Spa is located on the eastern crescent of Dubai's iconic Palm Jumeirah. This world famous landmark is located close to Dubai Marina and only 45 minutes from Dubai International Airport.


    Surrounded by lush gardens, 293 beautifully appointed rooms and villas are uniquely designed with contemporary Asian flair overlooking the lagoons or the Palm. Complementing such a prestigious destination, resort facilities include a private beach, three lagoons, water sports, a shoreline infinity pool, a fitness studio, two tennis courts and an Anantara Spa with 10 treatment rooms, all designed with relaxation and luxurious indulgence in mind.

    Read more

  • Atlantis, The Palm

    Atlantis, The Palm is ocean-themed and the flagship resort at the apex of The Palm Jumeirah's Crescent. There are several unique elements at the resort which focus on the extensive marine habitats. At The Lost Chambers, you can meander through underground passageways with 20 fresh and sea water exhibits. At Aquaventure, the 42 acre water themed park, you can take the body slide into a tube and through the shark tank.


    The accommodation here is utterly sumptuous, from the bars, lobbies and restaurants to the rooms and suites. The huge ShuiQi Spa and Fitness set over two floors of the Royal Towers provides a wealth of treatments, bathing options and water therapies.


    Activities are in abundance whether it is the choice of two beaches at Aquaventure or the Royal beach or the two swimming pools one with families in mind. Coupled with the tennis centre, Sanctuary nightclub or the shopping offerings you are spoilt for choice from dawn to well into the night.

  • Banyan Tree Al Wadi

    Amid stretches of desert sand in tranquil Wadi Khadeja, an oasis of peace awaits at Banyan Tree Al Wadi. A sanctuary surrounded by the evergreen ghaf tree. Experience magical Arabian nights and the romance of living like nomads, complete with Asian-inspired hydrotherapy spa facilities, a dedicated nature reserve, a private beach club and an 18-hole championship golf course.


    The activities on offer are outstanding with the world-renowned Banyan Tree Spa or a round of golf at the Al Hamra course to a class in falconry, horseback-riding, camel safaris and bike trails. An Arabian wildlife experience awaits within the nature reserve, home to local desert fauna such as Arabian gazelles, camels and oryxes, offering you a unique opportunity to interact with and observe these gentle creatures up close.

    Read more

  • Burj Al Arab

    In its relatively short tenure on the Dubai coastline this instantly recognizable hotel has attracted international attention and awe, ensuring its place as one of the most photographed structures in the world. Consistently voted the world's most luxurious hotel, Burj Al Arab is designed to resemble a billowing sail and soars to a height of 321 metres, dominating the Dubai skyline. Illuminated at night by choreographed lighting representing water and fire - Burj Al Arab is individual, inspired and hugely impressive.


    An all-suite luxury hotel, it reflects the very finest that the world has to offer. An extensive range of other facilities includes classic sports car rental, Rolls Royce chauffeurs, an outdoor pool, beach access, water sports, shopping and a fitness centre. The hotel operates the Sinbad's Kid's Club for children aged one to 12 years.

    Read more

  • Desert Palm

    Set amidst green fields and palm trees, Desert Palm offers sensational accommodation - in terms of design, it is Arabia touched with the avant-garde.


    Desert Palm has 28 'naturally modern' guest suites and pool villas, each showcasing its own distinctive design while embodying the simplicity and elegance of the estate's verdant surrounding. Offering total privacy, every room exudes an undeniable elegance and grace, where simple, yet distinctive architecture is counterpointed by high tech luxuries and designer amenities. Facilities and activities include horse riding, polo matches, cookery classes, wine tasting, rare bird watching, tennis, squash and many, many more. This is a great property for couples as it is not geared towards children particularly.

  • JA Palm Tree Court & Spa

    This family beach hotel offers spacious low rise suites and junior suites with private balconies or terraces overlooking the lush gardens, waterfalls and streams. The 134 all suite, JA Palm Tree Court & Spa comprises of six, three-level sea facing residences, steps away from the beach and offers tropical beach-resort views.


    With a wide choice of dining venues ranging from Arabian to Italian cuisine our favourite is the beachfront Captain's Bar where you can enjoy light lunch during the day or relax to the sound of the waves at sunset. The Spa overlooks the beach and offers over 40 innovative treatments. Head to the stables and enjoy a ride on a horse or camel, play a game of tennis, work out in the state-of-the-art gym or play a friendly match of football. Three temperature-controlled, outdoor swimming pools compliment the warm waters of the Arabian Gulf. Choose from an extensive range of water sport activities on offer.

  • Jumeirah Beach Hotel

    Designed to resemble an ocean wave in profile, this ultra-modern beachfront resort spreads over 26 storeys. Interior design is inspired by the four natural elements of Earth, Air, Fire and Water, with wall and accent colours to match.


    The 598 rooms and suites are beautifully appointed - awash with sumptuous fabrics, plush furniture and the latest technology - whilst décor is warm and homely. The suites are equally well put together, but also feature their own private sun terraces.The 19 beachside villas are each little havens of private luxury. All have their own plunge pools, discreetly tucked away in a private garden. Offers one the biggest choices of dining in Dubai, with over 20 restaurants and bars.

    Read more

  • Madinat Jumeirah Al Qasr

    Al Qasr means 'The Palace' and this hotel is the centre-piece of the Madinat Jumeirah resort. Designed in keeping with a Sheikh's summer residence, Al Qasr is approached via a tree-lined avenue, adorned with beautifully sculpted Arabian horses. Al Qasr is simply breathtaking - a tribute to age-old architectural styles surrounded by water, colourful canopies, ornate bridges and interwoven pathways.


    The large guest rooms are exquisitely decorated in classic Arabian style, finished with dark wood and antique style furnishings. It really does feel like living in a palace. No fewer than nine restaurant serve everything from steaks and seafood to Spanish, Asian and Vegetarian cuisine, a further 15 restaurants are dotted across the wider resort. We recommend the sensational Friday brunch, which is set across the entire ground floor of the resort and includes exciting activities to divert the children.

  • Madinat Jumeirah Dar Al Masyaf

    Approached through an enchanting central courtyard in which the sun dapples terracotta tiles, palms and gardens, this hotel is designed to echo the style of Arabian summer courtyard houses. The resort is crisscrossed with tranquil waterways and dotted with secluded terraces - it is hugely relaxing retreat.


    Dar Al Masyaf's rooms and suites are light, bright and spacious, and represent the resort's premium accommodation, tucked away amongst lush gardens. All are finished with authentic Arabian furnishings, intricate detailing and have large, marble bathrooms with walk-in rain showers and bathtubs, as well as wide terraces and an exclusive butler service.

  • One & Only Royal Mirage

    The beachfront Palace with its 231 rooms is adorned with ornamental arches and set amidst abundant gardens with meandering water features and streams. Interiors are contemporary with an Asian twist.


    At the heart of the resort the 170 rooms at the Arabian Court is crafted in the spirit of the Orient with dramatic architecture and a rich landscape of courtyards and fountains. The rooms are beautifully appointed, featuring carved arches, colourful mosaics and dramatic artwork. All rooms and suites are sea-facing with private balconies or terraces and interconnecting rooms are possible making it great for those with families. The Residence & Spa is an enclave of exclusivity - an intimate sanctuary with two private restaurants, The Dining Room and The Library, reserved exclusively for its guests.

  • The Address Downtown Dubai

    Located in the happening heart of Downtown Dubai, the hotel has the world's tallest tower, Burj Khalifa and the stunning Dubai Fountain on its doorstep and is flanked by The Dubai Mall, the world's largest shopping and entertainment centre.


    Standing an impressive 63 storeys high and featuring 196 lavish rooms and 626 serviced residences, The Address Downtown Dubai offers an extensive range of gourmet experiences, a signature Spa, a fully equipped Fitness Centre, chilled pools and indulgent amenities and services.
