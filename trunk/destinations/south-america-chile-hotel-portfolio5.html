Suggested Wine and Surrounds accommodation

  • Casa Lapostolle

    The Casa Lapostolle residence is located on the Clos Apalta wine estate in the Colchagua Valley near Santa Cruz. Casa Lapostolle at Clos Apalta offers extraordinary views over 650 hectares of nature, from plains and vineyards to the wild, forested Apalta hillsides and the surrounding mountains.

    The Casa has four private and sumptuously appointed casitas, located in the grounds, and nestled into the native forested hillside of the valley. All create a real sense of serenity and privacy, whilst their terraces are ideal settings for quiet contemplation or intimate meals.

    The casitas are named Cabernet Sauvignon, Petit Verdot, Merlot and Carmenére in deference to the grape varieties planted in the Apalta vineyard. Inside wooden ceilings soar above polished wooden floors counterpointed by white walls. Beautiful furniture and furnishings complete the look, which is very much 'upscale apartment'.

    Casa Silva

    The Viña Casa Silva opened this exquisite hotel and restaurant in 2005, an authentic Colchagua Valley historic house and typically Chilean in design - a recent renovation has added all the creature comforts, without compromising on the building's history and traditions.

    The rooms are beautifully presented, with polished wooden floors, pure white wooden panel detailing and sophisticated wall coverings throughout. Furnishings are simple, yet elegant and supremely comfortable, whilst small details like the lime-washed doors add a rustic, ranch flavour.

    La Casona at Matetic

    Matetic vineyard is located in the Casablanca Valley between Santiago and the Chilean coast. This completely enclosed valley, which is 9,000 hectares in length and runs parallel to the ocean. It is known for its extraordinary light and possesses the ideal climatic and topographic conditions for both red and white wines.

    The Viña Matetic Guesthouse is a century-old estancia set among wild gardens. The winery itself is one of the most architecturally avant-garde in Chile - a stunning hillside facility with a theatrical oval barrel cellar and a pair of grand tasting rooms with a waterfall.

    The guesthouse is colonial in construction, in keeping with the traditions of the area. The rooms were remodelled and decorated in 2004 by the famed architect and designer Max Cummins. All are furnished with regal four-poster beds and wingback chairs, thick damask curtains, and lots of florals and stripes - creating a cosy, homely feel.
