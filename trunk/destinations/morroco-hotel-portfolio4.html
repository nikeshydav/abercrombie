Suggested Marrakech accommodation

  • Amanjena

    Located a short drive from Marrakech, the property sits within a walled enclave dotted with fountains and pools and is an exquisitely crafted interpretation of Moorish tradition and opulence.

    Lying within an oasis of palms and olive trees, Amanjena echoes the rose-red walls of Marrakech and the Berber villages dotted amongst the High Atlas Mountains. The hotel is modern take on a traditional style - soaring domes and imposing, elegant Pavillions create a stunning, tranquil retreat.

    Read more

    Angsana Riad Si Said

    As you admire the intricate zellig or while you relax in a hamman, you will soon discover that your stay will be a truly exotic and unforgettable experience.

    The hotel has seven comfortable and beautifully appointed rooms and suites. Varied in size and layout, they have heating and air conditioning along with a television, a fireplace, a complimentary mobile phone and, of course, an en suite bathroom.

    Meanwhile, the hotel is ideally placed as a base from which to explore the bustling atmosphere of the Djema el Fna and the medina's intricate medieval streets.

    Read more

    Dar Zemora

    The soft glow of the Moroccan lanterns flicker as you dine in an oasis of tranquility. Set in the Palmeraie at the end of a drive lined with olive trees you will find Dar Zemora.

    Dar Zemora is a beautiful country house style hotel set in spacious grounds in the Palmeraie but only a short step away from the hustle and bustle of Marrakech's Medina. The hotel has three luxurious and spacious suites, each with their own private terraces and three elegant double bedrooms. All are individually designed with traditional Moroccan finishes but boasting contemporary comfort to create atmospheric, yet luxurious accommodation.

    Read more

    Kasbah Agafay

    Savour the tranquility of this once derelict ruin on Marrakech's distant horizon. Kasbah Agafay, just a short from bustling Marrakech is a 150-year old fort commands magnificent panoramic views over the hills.

    Kssour Agafay

    Just a minute's walk from Djemaa-el-Fna, Kssour Agafay (the sister property of Kasbah Agafay) has been restored using original 15th century features and is replete with fine carving, wall paintings and stonework.

    La Sultana Marrakech

    This intimate and sophisticated hotel is situated at the entry of the Imperial City's walls, just next to the Royal Palaces and among houses, mosques and gardens from all epochs. It has the atmosphere of an old palace.

    La Sultana has the atmosphere of an old palace, with Moroccan patterns on the walls and carved into wooden furnishings. The hotel is built in the style of a traditional Moroccan riad, with 28 individually decorated rooms and suites concentrated around a central patio. Every room is enhanced with African-inspired wood and copper accents, a fireplace, antique furniture, a painted or sculpted ceiling and a marble bathroom.

    A worthy member of Small Luxury Hotels and of Great Hotels of the World, La Sultana is set in lush gardens bursting with colours and aromas, and where a stroll will bring you to pergolas, caïdales tents and massage cabins. This really is a unique hotel, in both design and atmosphere. No stone has been left unturned, and no original detail neglected in creating a wonderfully peaceful retreat in the heart of Marrakech.

    Read more

    Le Meridien N Fis

    Located in the Menara district and situated in five and a half hectares of park, Le Meridien N'Fis towers over the magnificent Andalusian gardens. The hotel offers 277 bedrooms, three restaurants, one bar and health.

    Les Jardins de la Médina

    Lie in a hammock nestled in the shade of age-old trees listening to the the rustling of palm trees in the breeze and the gentle drops of water from the fountain.

    An authentic Moroccan riad dating back to the 19th century, this Eden's garden is shaded by orange, olive and jacaranda trees and flowered balconies. This hotel is a tranquil oasis a few minutes' walk from the main square.

    The rooms and suites are spacious, light and airy, opening onto terraces and balconies, which are ideal for a relaxed breakfast. The hotel also features an Ecole de Cuisine, where guests can learn the skills and techniques required to create authentic Moroccan dishes.

    Read more

    Palais Sebban

    Weave your way through the maze of connecting hallways and stop in one of the many small seated areas located in alcoves, perfect for tranquil relaxation and a glass of mint tea.

    Riad Kaiss

    Riad Kaiss is an exceptional example of the traditional architectural style of Morocco. It was once the harem of the Vizir, connected via underground tunnels to the Bahai Palace.
