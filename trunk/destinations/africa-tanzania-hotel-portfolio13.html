Suggested Zanzibar Accommodation

  • 236 Hurumzi Ltd

    Scan the skyline of Zanzibar's Stone Town, and along with mosque minarets, Hindu temple towers and church spires, you'll spot the Tower Top Restaurant of 236 Hurumzi.

    This hotel, formerly known as Emerson & Green stands proud in full glory, reminiscent of ancient Arabian nights but now offering guests a beautiful stay. The ten guestrooms each represent Zanzibari traditions in a distinct way. Sizeable beds combine with larger than normal stone bathtubs to provide ultimate bathing pleasure. As for the Tower Top, this is undoubtedly one of the most romantic restaurants in the world. Watching the sun setting into the Indian Ocean is pure spectacle.

    Baraza

    In Zanzibari culture, the Baraza or bench has for centuries been a focal point of community life. Saunter on the award winning Bwejuu fine sandy beach, or be amazed by the coral reefs along the eastern shore. Start your day with a yoga session or perhaps a relaxing treatment in the spa.

    A fusion of design with beautiful arches, intricate hand-carved décor, hand made furniture and intricate brass lanterns adorn the spacious villas. Relax on your expansive terrace with private plunge pool whilst sipping your favourite cocktail. Mouth-watering cuisine awaits in the dining room and round off the evening with a digestif in the Sheesha Bar.

    Fundu Lagoon

    Only accessible by boat and set on a beautiful island beach, Fundu Lagoon operates with palpable love for the natural environment. Wooden walkways and sandy paths lead to 16 thatched tented rooms, all large and discreetly private with fantastic ocean views.

    The sound of the waves will surely lull you to sleep. This resort offers true escapism based on complete comfort and high levels of service, including an open-sided restaurant presenting outstanding international cuisine, a wonderful infinity pool and a calming spa. Snorkelling, diving, explorations by canoe and sunset cruises on a catamaran or dhow are just some of the many pleasures in store for you.

    Kilindi

    Kilindi is a piece of functional art scattered along a picturesque-perfect stretch of Northern Zanzibar. Kilindi brings a welcome dash of zest to the island, standing watch over the time-honoured Dhow routes, along a crescent of coral-bright sand.

    Each of the fifteen pavilions embrace cascading rain-water pools, lush tropical gardens and domed private spaces of rest, indulgence and well-being.

    The style is known as Zanzibaroque, a homage to the unique synthesis of the island's endemic social and historical culture. The domes collect the rainwater, the sleeping areas straddle its storage, and the pools circulate it. Kilindi term this logically-eco, rather than eco-logical, because it seems the obvious thing to do.

    Kilindi believe that high-yield/low volume tourism is the only way forward for tourism in developing countries, and that luxury in Africa is not something that can be commodified and branded, it can only be experienced. Experience is the new luxury, the pavilions maximise the light and space, paying homage to the views around.

    Mnemba Island

    Imagine a scattering of just ten rustic palm-frond bandas hidden in the shade of a tropical forest fringed by a soft white coral-sand beach, with spectacular reefs beyond. It's no dream but daily reality at Mnemba Island Lodge on this tiny Indian Ocean bolthole.

    With en suite bathrooms and private verandas, thatched dining and bar areas with beautiful open views, and baskets of fresh fruit, fish and lobsters brought by traditional outriggers to the shore, be prepared for an exquisite and exotic sojourn. A protection zone surrounds the island, conserving the magnificent coral reefs where giant turtles and dazzling tropical fish drift dreamily through the lagoons.

    Ras Nungwi

    On the northernmost peninsula of Zanzibar, Ras Nungwi Beach Hotel is a cluster of whitewashed, palm-thatched cottages in rich tropical gardens overlooking the sparkling Indian Ocean. This is the place to relax and enjoy the beauty of the natural surroundings without forsaking first-class comforts.

    All 32 rooms are tastefully furnished, with hand-crafted four-poster beds and indigenous decor, balconies, air-conditioning and de luxe bathrooms. One of the most unspoilt beaches on the island, fringed by a wide and thriving coral reef, is ideal for walking, swimming and all forms of watersport. The sounds of the ocean round off your relaxing day in paradise.

    The Palms Hotel

    Located on the peaceful south eastern coast of the island, lying on a flawless beach, The Palms is the ultimate sanctuary for the discerning traveller. The six private villas are beautifully designed, the decor a triumphant marriage of colonial grandeur and simple elegance.

    On the veranda, a silk-draped four-poster Zanzibar Bed accents a sunken, midnight blue plunge pool with long views over the Indian Ocean. Indeed, all the luxurious comforts and amenities of a five-star resort are offered here, along with the most personal attention you could ever desire. The fusion of cultures in Zanzibar has resulted in exotic cuisine, served on the colonnaded terrace of The Plantation House.

    Zanzibar Serena Inn

    This is your haven of tranquillity and opulence amongst the bustle of Zanzibar's exotic melange of sultan's palaces, Portuguese forts, ancient harbours, dark slave markets and bright bazaars.

    Ideally situated on the seafront of ages-old Stone Town, the hotel is charming, intimate and romantic with interiors echoing the island's rich Arab influence. All 51 bedrooms and the private swimming pool overlook the azure waters of the Indian Ocean and the lazy dhows that plough beneath the restaurant's windows. The local specialities are freshly caught seafood with spices. Guests are invited on excursions to experience a succession of hidden dazzlingly white beaches, spice gardens and private watersports.
