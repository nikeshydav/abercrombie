Welcome to New Zealand!

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 14 days

    Highlights: Auckland, Rotorua, Christchurch, Franz Joseph, Wanaka, Queenstown

    Unveil the secrets of both North and South Islands of New Zealand with a chauffeur driven vehicle and customized guide services during sightseeing. Best for those who adore handholding and guidance during their holidays to enjoy every aspect/ offerings of the country visiting.

  • Day 1

    Welcome to New Zealand!

    On arrival into Auckland meet your A&K guide and transfer by private vehicle to your accommodation.

    Hilton Auckland
    This contemporary hotel is located 300 metres out to sea on Princes Wharf offering superb views across Auckland's harbour and city. Built in the concept of a luxury ship, the hotel is the focal point of the wharf development and boasts an upbeat mix of apartments, retail outlets, restaurants, and cafes and bars. The hotel is also within easy walking distance of Auckland's CBD and Viaduct Basin, the hub of the city's new restaurant and nightlife scene.

    Overnight: Deluxe Harbour View Room
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    Morning at leisure

    This afternoon meet your A&K guide in the lobby and depart on your half day private sightseeing tour of Auckland. Today's sightseeing tour of Auckland includes a scenic drive along the waterfront, the quaint Victorian village of Parnell and panoramic views from the extinct volcano - Mount Eden. At the Auckland Museum see a world-renowned collection of Polynesian artefacts including a 25-metre long war canoe built in 1836. On conclusion you will return to your accommodation.

    Overnight: Hilton Auckland Deluxe Harbour View Room
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 3

    Depart by private vehicle to your accommodation in Rotorua. A journey of approximately three hours, travelling south through the lush pasturelands of the Waikato, stopping enroute for lunch (own expense) and a bush walk (optional) at the settlement of Te Aroha. Discover Te Aroha's rich history when 'taking the waters' was a fashionable pastime and a cure for everything. Be transported back in time to the gold mining days of Waiorongomai Valley where fortunes were made and lost, where you can still follow old miners' trails and discover relics of the gold rush days. On completion continue to your accommodation in Rotorua.

    Treetops Lodge and Estate, Rotorua
    Nestled in 2,500 acres of 800-year old native forest, award-winning Treetops Lodge & Wilderness Experience is a life-long dream realised by owner and passionate naturalist, John Sax. This uniquely New Zealand experience is a sanctuary and retreat of breathtaking terrain, offering the ultimate in world-class accommodation and adventure. Located in Rotorua the Maori heartland and trout fishing capital of the world the Lodge's elegant, ecology-inspired architectural style was derived from the country's pioneering past, with timber and stone finishes echoing the natural beauty and simplicity of the great outdoors. Treetops offer seven streams, on-site trout fishing, four lakes, and over 70 kilometres of adventure trails.

    Overnight: Villa - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 4

    Today's full day tour begins with a visit to the beautiful Government Gardens and the Rotorua Museum housing exhibits devoted to the 1886 eruption of Mount Tarawera, a nearby dormant volcanic mountain. You will then visit Rainbow Springs where you will take a tour through Kiwi Encounter, a hatchery and nursery for the unique and treasured 'Kiwi' bird. Enjoy lunch (own expense) at a local restaurant. After lunch enjoy a stroll through the magnificent Redwood Forest before returning to your accommodation.

    Insider Access Kiwi Encounter
    This Insider Access tour is all about becoming better acquainted with New Zealand's elusive kiwi. Your tour will begin with a brief introduction at the incubation room and hatchery where you are able to see the equipment working. You will then continue to the Kitchen where the artificial diet for the young kiwi is prepared and you can see a variety of interesting food including worms and insects! Visit the nursery room filled with "brooders" - breeding units where the kiwi chicks stay in the nursery until they are approx 400 grams in body size then they are moved to outdoor enclosures where they grow until they are 1.2kg. From here you will enter the indoor nocturnal area where kiwi enclosures are completely open with no partitions between the kiwi and visitors! Your tour concludes with a static display of Kiwi Encounter that provides a wealth of information and audio visual of kiwi.

    Overnight: Treetops Lodge and Estate, Rotorua Villa - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 5

    This morning meet your pilot in the lobby and make your way to your waiting helicopter to depart on your White Island / Mount Tarawera Active Volcano Tour.

    Have you ever wondered what it is like standing on the edge of a live volcanic crater?
    Fly to White Island and land for a guided walk in the crater of New Zealand's most active Volcano, once home to factory workers who mined the island for Sulphur. Ruined remains of this hard life still exist today. During your flight be on the lookout for dolphins, a common sight in the Pacific Ocean. Your return flight includes an exclusive Mount Tarawera landing. On conclusion you will be returned to your accommodation.

    You will have time at leisure until late afternoon when you are collected from your accommodation by private vehicle and transferred to connect with the scheduled evening cultural experience.

    Te Po Combo is an Indigenous Evening Experience plus the Te Puia Daytime Experience - a feast of storytelling, entertainment and Maori cuisine, with a daytime guided tour included.

    Enjoy an exclusive guided tour starting at 4:30pm with one of Te Puia's world renowned guides. It includes a visit to the New Zealand national carving and weaving schools where you can watch these disciplines in practice under the guidance of our master carvers and weavers. The tour will include a visit to the award winning interactive galleries and Kiwi House, where you can see New Zealand's flightless bird up-close, followed by a visit to the mud pools and geysers.

    Te Po evening experience begins at Te Keketanga-a-Rangi (Heavenly Origins), a space comprising twelve monumental carvings reaching skyward. Hear stories of the past prior to assembling at the waharoa (gateway) of our marae. Experience an authentic Ma.ori welcome ceremony, warrior's challenge and kapa haka (performing arts) concert.

    The Maori Feast is a culinary opportunity like no other. The range of traditional and indigenous inspired dishes have been designed to create a memorable cultural experience.

    On conclusion you will be met and transferred by private vehicle back to your accommodation.

    Overnight: Treetops Lodge and Estate, Rotorua Villa - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 6

    Meet your A&K chauffeur in the lobby and transfer by private vehicle to the airport to connect with your domestic flight to Christchurch.

    On arrival in Christchurch you will be met by your A&K guide and taken on a private afternoon sightseeing tour. Today's sightseeing tour includes a scenic drive along the River Avon the Botanical Gardens. Drive to the top of the Port Hills for a wonderful vista of Christchurch and the surrounding countryside including quaint Lyttleton Harbour. Visit the International Antarctic Centre that focuses on the great southern continent and join an exclusive tour. Enjoy a leisurely punt on the Avon River before being returned to your accommodation.

    Insider Access Old Antarctic Explorer Tour
    Join an Old Antarctic Explorer on an exclusive and personalised tour of Christchurch's International Antarctic Centre discovering New Zealand's Scott Base, The Snow & Ice Experience and the Antarctic Storm where you will experience extreme wind chill Antarctic conditions. Enjoy a cruise to Antarctica on board an Antarctic Cruise Ship in the brand new 4D Extreme Theatre or enjoy watching the penguins being fed in the New Zealand Penguin Encounter.

    Rations are available in the Antarctic Café & Bar and a signed copy of an Antarctic book is yours to take away at the end of your 3-hour personalised Antarctic experience.

    Includes:
    * All day pass to Antarctic Centre including penguin feeding & all activities.
    * Light lunch in cafe at reserved table with old Antarctic explorer.
    * Signed Antarctic book on departure with personal note.

    The George Christchurch
    The George, a luxury boutique hotel, is the essence of supreme comfort and is renowned for offering a personalised stay and attentive staff as well as two award winning restaurants. Located in the heart of Christchurch overlooking the picturesque Hagley Park and river Avon you will find a peaceful retreat. Impeccable service in an intimate ambience is guaranteed at The George, the only Christchurch Hotel with a one-to-one staff to guest ratio.

    Overnight: Premium Executive Room
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Meet your A&K chauffeur in the lobby and transfer by private vehicle to the Christchurch train station to connect with the Tranz Alpine Train to Greymouth.

    The TranzAlpine scenic train journey travels between Christchurch and Greymouth, from one coast of New Zealand to the other. Climb into the Southern Alps and descend through lush beech rain forest to the West Coast town of Greymouth.

    On arrival in Greymouth meet your A&K driver / guide who will escort you down the West Coast of New Zealand over the coming days. You will depart Greymouth for Franz Josef, a journey of approximately two and a half hours. The road to Franz Josef is rural, but with a wild streak. The farmland is trying to turn back into forest, and the beauty of the rivers is raw and primeval. On arrival in Franz Josef you will be set down at your accommodation.

    Westwood Lodge
    Westwood Lodge has a warm relaxed atmosphere in a quiet rural setting and is only minutes from the world-renowned Franz Josef Glacier. The lodge is set in bush and farmlands with views of the mountains. It is just a short stroll to the township of Franz Josef and to all activities. It is the perfect place to relax and unwind, with just the right level of personal attention and unobtrusive service.

    Overnight: Coach House Suite - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    Meet your A&K driver / guide in the lobby and transfer by private vehicle to connect with your private scenic helicopter tour.

    This premier flight lets you experience two of New Zealand's finest National Parks. Discover the gigantic expanse of the Franz Josef Glacier and Fox Glacier snowfields with a landing at high altitude. Step out of your helicopter and experience how it feels to stand in snow. The flight includes fantastic views of the Tasman Glacier and Mount Cook.

    *Flight duration 40 minutes (including snow landing)
    *Flights operate: on demand
    *Clients are required to reconfirm their bookings 24 hours prior

    After your helicopter tour meet your driver / guide and enjoy an afternoon touring this area.

    Franz Josef Glacier Village's natural credentials are impeccable and there is a huge variety of natural attractions just a short distance from the Franz Josef Glacier Village. Lakes, rainforests, surging waterfalls and the glacial rivers of ice mean that the area is an adventure and leisure destination that is ready to be explored. The Village itself is the hub of the region with restaurants, nightlife and shopping.

    This afternoon / evening a private hot pool has been reserved for you (45 minutes) at the Glacier Hot Pools. Your driver / guide will transfer you and from the pools this evening. Nestled in lush rainforest, the Glacier Hot Pools is a haven of natural tranquillity and the perfect way to unwind and experience a blissful state of pure relaxation after travelling along Te Tai o Poutini (The West Coast) or enjoying one of its many attractions. The pools are conveniently located in the heart of Franz Josef township on Cron Street, and can be enjoyed all year round from 12 noon to 10pm (Last entry 9pm).

    Overnight: Westwood Lodge Coach House Suite - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 9

    Today make your way to your accommodation in Wanaka, a journey of approximately four hours. Discover the rugged scenery along one of New Zealand's most awe-inspiring highways. Your journey will take you via the Haast Pass, the southern most pass that connects the communities of the East and West, is humbling. Marvel at the vast rainforests to the clearest waterfalls that eclipse this untouched landscape. Enjoy breathtaking sights, from high mountain peaks to blue lakes before arriving into Wanaka.

    Whare Kea Lodge
    Situated amongst the mountains of New Zealand's spectacular Southern Alps on the shores of glacial Lake Wanaka lays a private alpine retreat. Whare Kea Lodge is designed to capture the setting's unique feeling appearing to float above the water giving magnificent vistas in an intimate atmosphere. All of the six suites have a separate bath and shower and have direct access to the sun deck.

    Overnight: Master Suite - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 10

    Today you will enjoy a Wanaka Nature Encounter Tour! You will be collected from your accommodation and transferred to connect with the scheduled boat cruise on the pristine waters of Lake Wanaka to Mou Waho Island Nature Reserve. Enjoy a guided walk to the spectacular summit that reveals the island's secret - A Lake on top of an Island! - Enjoy a serene "High Tea" here, up close and personal with rare native birds, and plant a tree as part of this unique eco experience. On conclusion you will return to Wanaka where you will meet your guide and enjoy lunch before continuing on to the 4WD part of your day. After lunch (own expense) experience the stark beauty and solitude of the spectacular high country, bordering a World Heritage area by 4WD. You will explore the private farm tracks on an historic Wanaka sheep, cattle and deer station. From the site of Nehenehe, an ancient Maori settlement abandoned in the 1830's, to the ruins of Wanaka's first European settler's farmhouses, this journey offers a unique insight into Wanaka's rich history, heritage and modern-day farming life. On conclusion of your tour you will be returned to your accommodation.

    Lake Wanaka Cruise and Island Nature Walk Information
    • Total walking time 1.5 hours
    • Requirements: average health and fitness, strong treaded sports shoes or hiking boots, suitable warm clothing, good sweater or jacket

    Overnight: Whare Kea Lodge Master Suite - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 11

    Meet your A&K driver / guide in the lobby and transfer by private vehicle to Queenstown, a journey of approximately one hour. Your journey today is alongside the Clutha River, Lake Dunstan and the Shotover River, through to Queenstown. The vineyards of the Gibbston area and bungy jumpers at the Kawarau River Bridge are interesting distractions along the way.

    The remainder of today is at your leisure.

    Matakauri Lodge
    Matakauri Lodge offers absolute comfort as well as stunning panoramic views. The Lodge and its adjoining suites are luxuriously appointed and have been designed to be in harmony with the lakefront environment and surrounding bush. It is one of New Zealand's most private and serene vacation retreats, nestled among bush on the edge of Lake Wakatipu, just 10 minutes drive from Queenstown. Lodge facilities included a business centre, full equipped gymnasium, heated infinity pool and Jacuzzi.

    Note: During cocktails and dinner, gentlemen are required to wear a jacket (the Lodge has jackets available for guest use at Reception).

    Overnight: Deluxe Suite - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 12

    Today you will be whisked by helicopter to the World Heritage listed Milford Sound flying over secluded alpine lakes, lush rainforest, through deep bush-fringed valleys and past rugged mountain peaks. Take in the beauty of the Southern Alps on your way into Milford Sound, experiencing this amazing glacial creation. View Lake Quill, the source of the spectacular Sutherland Falls - the second highest waterfall in the Southern Hemisphere. Sweep the full length of the fiord, flying to a remote West Coast beach to view seal colonies. Before heading for home, weather permitting, you have a chance to land 'on top of the world' where you can stand among the ancient ice flows of the Fiordland Glaciers. Enjoy a picnic lunch before flying back to Queenstown and transfer to the hotel.

    Overnight: Matakauri Lodge Deluxe Suite - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 13

    Today's tour of the Wakatipu basin includes a visit to the historic gold-mining settlement of Arrowtown, local artist studios and the Lakes and District Museum, followed by the Kawarau Bridge made famous by A.J. Hackett and over one million bungy jumpers. After private winery experience and lunch you will return to Queenstown to take the Skyline Gondola cable car ride to Bob's Peak for spectacular panoramic views over Lake Wakatipu. On conclusion you will be returned to your accommodation.

    Private Mount Edward Winery Experience
    Mount Edward winery is situated in the Gibbston sub region of Central Otago, some 25km east of Queenstown. Founded in 1997 by the godfather of Central Otago Pinot Noir, Alan Brady, the winery grows limited quantities of premium pinot noir, single vineyard designates, and small batch classic white varieties. The first Mount Edward vineyard, "The Drumlin", is situated on the steep hillside slope next to the winery and is testament to both hard work and vision, the 30 deg slope a reminder that nothing great can come easily.

    Tour includes:
    * 1.5 hours time with winemaker.
    * Wine tasting (minimum 5 wines).
    * Barrel tasting.
    * One on one private appointment.
    * Lunch.

    Overnight: Matakauri Lodge Deluxe Suite - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 14

    Meet your A&K chauffeur in the lobby and transfer by private vehicle to the airport to connect with your onward flight.

    Auckland to Rotorua (self drive)
    Depart Auckland on the southern motorway, suburban scenery gradually gives way to the farmland of South Auckland, an area favoured by bloodstock breeders. You'll enjoy motorway speeds all the way to Bombay, a major market gardening area.

    The Waikato River will accompany you for much of your journey through the green countryside. Places of interest along the way include the wine growing area of Te Kauwhata and the coal mining town of Huntly, before you get to Hamilton. The city of Hamilton is carved in two by the mighty Waikato River.

    Continuing to Rotorua you'll come across the town of Tirau, known for its antique shops and animal-shaped corrugated iron buildings. Enjoy plantation pine forest and a refreshing patch of native bush, Fitzgerald Glade, before you encounter the unusual terrain of the Mamaku district. Mini volcanic cones dot the landscape, some showing their solidified lava core. The last part of your journey takes you past the gondola and luge rides of Mt Ngongataha. Rotorua sits squarely on the Pacific Ring of Fire, so volcanic activity is part of the city's past and present. Explore the geothermal areas and discover the unique culture of New Zealand's Maori people. Rotorua is entertaining in any weather, at any time of the year.

Learn about the rich culture, volatile landscape and legendary figures that have shaped this fascinating region.     Today make your way to your accommodation in Wanaka, a journey of approximately four hours. Discover the rugged scenery along one of New Zealand's most awe-inspiring highways. Your journey will take you via the Haast Pass, the southern most pass that connects the communities of the East and West, is humbling. Marvel at the vast rainforests to the clearest waterfalls that eclipse this untouched landscape. Enjoy breathtaking sights, from high mountain peaks to blue lakes before arriving into Wanaka.

    Whare Kea Lodge
    Situated amongst the mountains of New Zealand's spectacular Southern Alps on the shores of glacial Lake Wanaka lays a private alpine retreat. Whare Kea Lodge is designed to capture the setting's unique feeling appearing to float above the water giving magnificent vistas in an intimate atmosphere. All of the six suites have a separate bath and shower and have direct access to the sun deck.

    Overnight: Master Suite - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 10

    Today you will enjoy a Wanaka Nature Encounter Tour! You will be collected from your accommodation and transferred to connect with the scheduled boat cruise on the pristine waters of Lake Wanaka to Mou Waho Island Nature Reserve. Enjoy a guided walk to the spectacular summit that reveals the island's secret - A Lake on top of an Island! - Enjoy a serene "High Tea" here, up close and personal with rare native birds, and plant a tree as part of this unique eco experience. On conclusion you will return to Wanaka where you will meet your guide and enjoy lunch before continuing on to the 4WD part of your day. After lunch (own expense) experience the stark beauty and solitude of the spectacular high country, bordering a World Heritage area by 4WD. You will explore the private farm tracks on an historic Wanaka sheep, cattle and deer station. From the site of Nehenehe, an ancient Maori settlement abandoned in the 1830's, to the ruins of Wanaka's first European settler's farmhouses, this journey offers a unique insight into Wanaka's rich history, heritage and modern-day farming life. On conclusion of your tour you will be returned to your accommodation.

    Lake Wanaka Cruise and Island Nature Walk Information
    • Total walking time 1.5 hours

    Overnight: Whare Kea Lodge Master Suite - Twin / Double Occupancy

    This morning continue your drive south to Queenstown, a journey of approximately one hour. Your journey today is alongside the Clutha River, Lake Dunstan and the Shotover River, through to Queenstown. The vineyards of the Gibbston area and bungy jumpers at the Kawarau River Bridge are interesting distractions along the way.

    The remainder of today is at your leisure.

    Matakauri Lodge
    Matakauri Lodge offers absolute comfort as well as stunning panoramic views. The Lodge and its adjoining suites are luxuriously appointed and have been designed to be in harmony with the lakefront environment and surrounding bush. It is one of New Zealand's most private and serene vacation retreats, nestled among bush on the edge of Lake Wakatipu, just 10 minutes drive from Queenstown. Lodge facilities included a business centre, full equipped gymnasium, heated infinity pool and Jacuzzi.

    Note: During cocktails and dinner, gentlemen are required to wear a jacket (the Lodge has jackets available for guest use at Reception).

    Overnight: Deluxe Suite - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

    Today you will be whisked by helicopter to the World Heritage listed Milford Sound flying over secluded alpine lakes, lush rainforest, through deep bush-fringed valleys and past rugged mountain peaks. Take in the beauty of the Southern Alps on your way into Milford Sound, experiencing this amazing glacial creation. View Lake Quill, the source of the spectacular Sutherland Falls - the second highest waterfall in the Southern Hemisphere. Sweep the full length of the fiord, flying to a remote West Coast beach to view seal colonies. Before heading for home, weather permitting, you have a chance to land 'on top of the world' where you can stand among the ancient ice flows of the Fiordland Glaciers. Enjoy a picnic lunch before flying back to Queenstown.

    Overnight: Matakauri Lodge Deluxe Suite - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

    Today's tour of the Wakatipu basin includes a visit to the historic gold-mining settlement of Arrowtown, local artist studios and the Lakes and District Museum, followed by the Kawarau Bridge made famous by A.J. Hackett and over one million bungy jumpers. After private winery experience and lunch you will return to Queenstown to take the Skyline Gondola cable car ride to Bob's Peak for spectacular panoramic views over Lake Wakatipu. On conclusion you will be returned to your accommodation.

    Private Mount Edward Winery Experience

Founded in 1997 by the godfather of Central Otago Pinot Noir, Alan Brady, the winery grows limited quantities of premium pinot noir, single vineyard designates, and small batch classic white varieties. The first Mount Edward vineyard, "The Drumlin", is situated on the steep hillside slope next to the winery and is testament to both hard work and vision, the 30 deg slope a reminder that nothing great can come easily.

    Tour includes:
    * 1.5 hours time with winemaker.
    * Wine tasting (minimum 5 wines).
    * Barrel tasting.
    * One on one private appointment.
    * Lunch.

    Overnight: Matakauri Lodge Deluxe Suite - Twin / Double Occupancy
    Meal Plan: B

    This morning, make your way to Queenstown Airport to connect with your onward flight. Please ensure you allow enough time to drop off your rental car & check in for your onward flight.

Two Islands, Many Faces ( Affordable Luxury)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 11 days

    Highlights: Auckland, Rotorua, Queenstown, Franz Josef Glacier, Greymouth and Christchurch.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Auckland

    Welcome to New Zealand!

    On arrival into Auckland meet your A&K guide and transfer by private vehicle to your accommodation.Rest of the day is at leisure on your own.

    Crowne Plaza Auckland
    Crowne Plaza Auckland is located in the very heart of 'The City of Sails' above the unique boutique shopping complex "Atrium on Elliott'. It's only a short stroll to Queen Street, Aotea Convention Centre, Sky Tower and the Viaduct Basin. Due to its unique design the accommodation rooms start from level 16, ensuring many guest rooms provide excellent views over downtown Auckland.

    Overnight: Crowne Plaza Auckland
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    Auckland

    You will be collected from your accommodation this morning for your scheduled half day Auckland Highlights Tour.

    See why Auckland is known as one of the most beautiful cities and regions in the world!

    Highlights include:

    * Mount Eden, Auckland city's highest point with amazing views;

    * Auckland Domain, one of the city's 48 volcanoes with a view of Auckland Museum;

    * The old and beautiful suburb of Parnell with its many colonial homes;

    * Tamaki Drive with sweeping views across the WaitemataHarbour;

    * Mission Bay, Auckland city's most well-known beach;

    * See Rangitoto Island, at 600 years the youngest of all 48 volcanoes in Auckland;

    * The Auckland Harbour Bridge which spans the glorious WaitemataHarbour;

    * Auckland's beautiful marine village of Devonport with beautiful Mount Victoria.

    * Includes: free Auckland central pick up, all transport, snacks and hot/ cold drinks.

    * Clients to bring: clothing for the day's weather conditions, walking shoes, sun hat, sunscreen and camera.

    On conclusion of your tour you will be set down at the rental car office where you will collect a midsize automatic car which has been reserved for you until drop off at Rotorua Airport on day 5.

    Kruse GPS
    Note: When you collect your rental car please ask the rental car company for your Kruse GPS system - this has been reserved for you until drop off on day 10. Please ensure you take it with you when you change rental cars.

    Your KRUSE GPS Travel Guide provides: Audio commentary: Listen to commentaries that inform, delight and entertain, as you pass through the landscape. Discover New Zealand's Maori and European history. Hear historic characters bring our unique legends to life. Discover the 'Best of NZ' by being alerted on screen by a button that recommends a historic pub or a fantastic cafe and much more

    GPS navigation: Be guided along scenic highways using the GPS navigation to your chosen destination.
    Once you have collected your rental car make your way back to your accommodation. The remainder of today is at your leisure.

    Auckland is a colourful vibrant city, often called the 'City of Sails'. Located on the spectacular sparkling WaitemataHarbour its major landmarks are the Harbour Bridge, the Sky Tower and Rangitoto Island, the region's youngest volcano. Visitors can enjoy the magnificent harbour, the many and varied activities of the city or walk and hike in the nearby hills where native flora and fauna abound.

    Overnight: Crowne Plaza Auckland
    Meal Plan: Breakfast

  • Day 3

    Auckland/Rotorua

    Today you will make your way to Rotorua, a journey of approximately three hours. Depart Auckland travelling through suburban scenery which gradually gives way to farmland and green countryside.

    You'll come across the town of Tirau, known for its antique shops and animal-shaped corrugated iron buildings. Enjoy plantation pine forest and a refreshing patch of native bush, Fitzgerald Glade, before you encounter the unusual terrain of the Mamaku district. Mini volcanic cones dot the landscape, some showing their solidified lava core. The last part of your journey takes you past the gondola and luge rides of Mt Ngongataha before reaching Rotorua City.

    NovotelRotorua Lakeside
    This international standard hotel is located on the shores of picturesque Lake Rotorua with commanding views over the lake and surrounding hinterland. The hotel features 199 well-appointed rooms, and is only a five minute stroll to the city centre and convention centre.

    Overnight: NovotelRotorua Lakeside
    Meal Plan: Breakfast

  • Day 4

    Rotorua

    Morning at leisure.

    This afternoon you will be collected from your accommodation for your afternoon scheduled Rotorua Tour.

    Spend the afternoon experiencing Rotorua's Maori culture and geothermal activity and see how they are intricately entwined. Your first highlight is a guided tour through TePuia - Rotorua's premier cultural attraction. This includes a visit to the wood carving school, art and craft gallery, RotowhioMarae, weaving display, kiwi house and finally the Pohutu geyser. Next you visit the Lakefront area to see the Maori War Canoe and learn more about Maori history and customs. You will enjoy a short visit to Kuirau Park to view a recent geothermal eruption site. To finish the day you will visit the pristine and tranquil lakes of this amazing volcanic region, including views of Mount Tarawera with explanation of volcanic and Maori history before returning to your accommodation.

    TePuia
    Boiling Mud Pools
    Rotorua Lakefront - Maori War Canoe
    Kuirau Park
    Government Gardens - Sulphur Bay
    Blue and Green Lakes
    Lake Tarawera - Lake Okareka

    Overnight: NovotelRotorua Lakeside
    Meal Plan: Breakfast

  • Day 5

    Rotorua / Queenstown

    Today make your way to Rotorua Airport to return your rental car and connect with your domestic flight to Queenstown. Please ensure you take your GPS system with you to fix to your next vehicle on arrival in Queenstown.

    Fly: Rotorua / Queenstown (via Christchurch) 1150 / 1510 (DOMESTIC FLIGHT NOT INCLUDED)
    On arrival in Queenstown make your way to the rental car counter to collect another midsize automatic car which has been reserved for you until drop off at Greymouth train station on day 10.

    Once you have collected your rental car make your way to your accommodation.

    Crowne Plaza Queenstown
    Overlooking Lake Wakatipu with its majestic mountain backdrop, Crowne Plaza Queenstown offers contemporary, stylish accommodation in an outstanding in-town location. Each of the 139 superbly appointed guest rooms has its own private balcony with views of the lake, mountains or private courtyard. In-room features include waffle bathrobes, quality amenities, LCD TV, cordless telephone, in-room safe and high speed broadband access. Designer duvets provide encapsulating comfort with plush chocolate bed throws and New Zealand urban artwork decorate the walls. Crowne Plaza Queenstown offers all the services expected of an upscale hotel including valet car-parking, business centre, ski drying room, 24 hour room service and purpose built leisure concierge.

    Overnight: Crowne Plaza Queenstown
    Meal Plan: Breakfast

  • Day 6

    Queenstown

    You will be collected from your accommodation by courtesy vehicle and transferred to connect with your scheduled Milford Sound Tour.

    Travel by fixed wing plane with panoramic views of spectacular alpine scenery, lush rainforest, and finally Milford Sound. Once on the ground at Milford board your luxury vessel for a Scenic Cruise the full-length of Milford Sound. The vessel provides excellent viewing and there is plenty of time available to enjoy the waterfalls, rainforest, mountains and wildlife. Enjoy an Indian picnic lunch on board before returning to Queenstown by fixed wing plane. On arrival in Queenstown you will be transferred by courtesy vehicle back to your accommodation.

    Overnight: Crowne Plaza Queenstown
    Meal Plan: Breakfast and Lunch

  • Day 7

    Queenstown

    Day at leisure

    The first people to discover Queenstown's rare beauty were Maori pounamu (jade) hunters. Then came the gold miners, in search of another kind of treasure. Today Queenstown is treasured for its magnificent scenery, adventure opportunities and luxury lifestyle. While Queenstown is renowned for adventure, you'll also find mellow, relaxing attractions. In recent years, the region has acquired wineries, golf courses, art galleries, exceptional restaurants and fabulous day spas.

    Overnight: Crowne Plaza Queenstown
    Meal Plan: Breakfast

  • Day 8

    Queenstown/ Lake Wanaka/ Framz Joseph

    Today make your way to Franz Josef, a journey of approximately six hours.Depart Queenstown for Wanaka over the Crown Range Road. It's a challenging drive, but the views are amazing and you'll probably encounter friendly keas (mountain parrots). If the weather does not permit driving over the Crown Range Road, you can take the more conservative route that runs beside the Shotover River, Lake Dunstan and the Clutha River. The vineyards of the Gibbston area and bungy jumpers at the Kawarau River Bridge are interesting distractions along the way, before arriving in Wanaka

    Leaving Wanaka, Lake Hawea is the first scenic highlight of this journey, followed closely by the northern end of Lake Wanaka. At the wilderness town of Makarora you can fuel up and refresh before you take on the dramatic twists and turns of the Haast Pass. Enjoy waterfalls and river scenery as you arrive in rainforest country.

    Just north of Haast is Lake Moeraki, a peaceful forest lake with good fishing - a 40 minute walk takes you to the penguin colony at Monro Beach. Just before Fox Glacier is the western end of the Copland track. In half a day you can walk to the hot springs at Welcome Flat. After a short drive north, you'll arrive at Franz Josef Glacier.

    Scenic Hotel Franz Josef Glacier
    Scenic Hotel Franz Josef Glacier sits at the edge of the Westland World Heritage Park, and is just moments from one of New Zealand's most spectacular natural attractions, the Franz Josef Glacier. It makes the most of its alpine location and offers quality accommodation in two separate hotel 'wings'. Stay in either the Douglas or Graham wing, and enjoy the beautiful surroundings within Franz Josef village. The Scenic Hotel Franz Josef Glacier has 130 rooms, comprising 46 rooms in the Graham Wing and 84 in the Douglas Wing, the latter with desk space and balconies.

    Overnight: Scenic Hotel Franz Josef Glacier
    Meal Plan: Breakfast

  • Day 9

    Franz Josef Glacier

    Day at leisure

    Franz Josef Glacier Village's natural credentials are impeccable and there is a huge variety of natural attractions just a short distance from the Franz Josef Glacier Village. Lakes, rainforests, surging waterfalls and the glacial rivers of ice mean that the area is an adventure and leisure destination that is ready to be explored. The Village itself is the hub of the region with restaurants, nightlife and shopping.

    Overnight: Scenic Hotel Franz Josef Glacier
    Meal Plan: Breakfast

  • Day 10

    Franz Josef Glacier /Greymouth/ Christchurch

    Today you will make your way to Greymouth, a journey of approximately two and a half hours. Depart the Glaciers and travel via Hokitika to Greymouth. Hokitika was first settled in 1860, after the discovery of gold on the west coast. It still has the feel of a 'frontier town', and there are some lovely old buildings to admire. Greenstone (NZ jade) is the town's treasure these days - you can see it being polished and sculpted.

    The road between Hokitika and Greymouth provides great views of the wild west coast. Detour to the beach and breathe the salt spray air. Driftwood is the ornamentation of choice for west coast beaches - every shape, size and colour. Shantytown, just south of Greymouth, is a reconstructed 1880s gold rush town.

    On arrival in Greymouth you will return your rental car and connect with the Tranz Alpine Train to Christchurch.

    The TranzAlpine scenic train journey travels between Greymouth and Christchurch, from one coast of New Zealand to the other. Today's journey will see you travelling through lush beech rainforest and into the Southern Alps. You will travel through the spectacular gorges and river valleys of the Waimakariri River followed by farmland and the fields of the Canterbury Plains before arriving in Christchurch.

    On arrival in Christchurch you will be met by your A&K chauffeur and transferred by private vehicle to your accommodation.

    The George Christchurch
    The George, a luxury boutique hotel, is the essence of supreme comfort and is renowned for offering a personalised stay and attentive staff as well as two award winning restaurants. Located in the heart of Christchurch overlooking the picturesque Hagley Park and river Avon you will find a peaceful retreat. Impeccable service in an intimate ambience is guaranteed at The George, the only Christchurch Hotel with a one-to-one staff to guest ratio.
    Premium Executive Room - Twin / Double

    Overnight: The George Christchurch
    Meal Plan: Breakfast

  • Day 11

    Christchurch Departure

    Meet your A&K chauffeur in the lobby and transfer by private vehicle to Christchurch airport to connect with your onward flight.

    Meal Plan: Breakfast

Twin Island Journey

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 days

    Embark on a journey emcompassing the highlights of the country's main islands: Metropolitan Auckland and the geothermal region of Rotorua await you in the North Island, while the less populated South Island offers pristine landscapes and rugged adventure.

    Highlights: Auckland, Rotorua, Queenstown, Christchurch.

  • Day 1

    Auckland

    Upon arrival in Auckland you will be met by your guide and transferred to your hotel, relaxing for the remainder of the morning.

    This afternoon set off for an overview of the city from the summit of Mount Eden - a dormant volcano that is the highest natural point in Auckland - before visiting Auckland Museum which is renowned for its displays on the Pacific peoples and volcanic activity.

    Overnight: Mollies

  • Day 2

    Auckland - Rotorua

    After a morning tour of Auckland, drive south towards Rotorua, known as "Sulphur City" for the volcanic furnaces that fuel its bubbling mud pools and geysers. Lunch at Oraka Deer Park before visiting the Rotorua Museum, arriving at your luxurious lodge late this afternoon.

    Overnight: Solitaire Lodge

  • Day 3

    Rotorua

    Enjoy a leisurely morning at your lodge situated on Lake Tarawera. Rotorua is known for its lakes as well as its geothermal activity and watersports options. Later board the 'Clearwater' for a private lunch cruise with a stop at Hot Water Beach for a relaxing swim in the natural thermal waters.

    Overnight: Solitaure Lodge

  • Day 4

    Rotorua - Queenstown

    Enjoy a behind the scenes visit to a local Maori community and listen to their ancient Maori Legends.

    Later today fly to Queenstown, a city that draws adventurous travellers from all over the world. Upon arrival you will be met by your guide and transferred to your luxury lodge on the shores of Lake Wakatipu.

    Overnight: Blanket Bay Lodge

  • Day 5

    Queenstown

    Weather permitting, a helicopter whisks you off the front lawn of the lodge for a flightseeing excursion to Milford Sound.

    Land at a scenic lookout of one of the world's most picturesque areas for a delicious picnic lunch. Return to Queenstown where the afternoon is free to explore at your leisure.

    Overnight: Blanket Bay Lodge

  • Day 6

    Queenstown

    Queenstown lives up to its reputation for rugged adventure today.

    Select from a number of activity options, including a Dart River jet boat safari, a vineyard and artists excursion, a round of golf at Millbrook or a Skippers Canyon private tour travelling through some of the most spectacular scenery in New Zealand.

    Overnight: Blanket Bay Lodge

  • Day 7

    Queenstown - Christchurch

    Board a flight to Christchurch where you will be met at the airport by your guide.

    Depart on a sightseeing tour of this very English city situated on the Canterbury Plains. Visit the International Antarctic Centre and take an exciting outdoor ride on a genuine Antarctic vehicle - the Hagglund. Enjoy a light lunch at a local café before a relaxing cruise on a punt on the tree-lined Avon River.

    Overnight: The George

  • Day 8

    Depart Christchurch

    This morning board a flight for your onward journey.
