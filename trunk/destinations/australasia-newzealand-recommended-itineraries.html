NORTH KIWI ADVENTURE ESCAPE

  • Duration: 8 Days / 7 Nights

    The zeal to explore the extremes will attract you for an activity filled time at the North Island of New Zealand.

    Programme Overview

    Auckland - Rotorua, Tauranga - Lake Taupo

    A&K Advantages
    Enjoy Jet boating at Auckland Harbour for an adrenalin rush
    Spend the day on a kayak and walking tour of the famed Rangitoto Island
    Do the exhilarating Auckland Bridge climb
    Take a ride on the world’s only Shweeb - a human powered monorail racetrack
    Step onboard a custom built boat for an encounter with the dolphins
    Enjoy a scenic flight over the Tauranga region & the varied geothermal areas of the Rotorua
    Enjoy a cultural evening at Te Po
    Visit the famed Huka Falls, the most visited natural attraction in New Zealand
    Take a boat cruise to witness the Maori Rock Carvings around Lake Taupo
    Head to Tongariro National Park for the legendary Tongariro Alpine Crossing
    Stop at Bulli Point for some Cliff diving

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE AUCKLAND

    On arrival at Auckland, you will be met by a representative and privately transferred to your accommodation. Relax & unwind from the flight and post lunch be met by a guide for your private sightseeing of Auckland.

    The sightseeing tour will include a scenic drive along the waterfront, the quaint Victorian village of Parnell and panoramic views from the extinct volcano - Mount Eden. At the Auckland Museum see a world-renowned collection of Polynesian artifacts including a 25-metre long war canoe built in 1836. On conclusion you will return to your accommodation.

    Overnight: The Hilton or similar
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    AUCKLAND

    Enjoy breakfast and a late start to the morning. Head out towards the harbour for an adrenalin rush!

    Jet boating was invented in New Zealand so make the most of this with the experts! Step on board a jet boat & take a 35 minutes adrenalin pumping tour of the Auckland harbor with high speed spins and tricks! The supercharged jet boats are built to carry up to 24 passengers and depart hourly from the waterfront. Most trips pass under the harbour bridge and explore the harbour and is definitely a must do while in Auckland.

    Some time at your own pace to explore the city or you can drive to one of the amazing award winning winery for a wine tasting session followed with dinner.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    AUCKLAND

    Explore the landmark of the Auckland region and see Auckland city from another perspective. Spend the day on a kayak across the Waitemata harbour where you may see wildlife such as Little Blue Penguins and Cooks Petrels followed by a walking tour of the famed Rangitoto Island.

    After your safety briefing and paddling instruction you will kayak across the Waitemata harbour, translates to 'sparkling waters', where you may see wildlife such as Little Blue Penguins and Cooks Petrels.

    On arrival at Rangitoto Island, which is the largest and youngest of Auckland’s volcanoes, you will start the leisurely walk to the summit lasting for about an hour. Upon reaching the top you will be treated to the finest 360 degree views that the Auckland region has to offer, making the trek totally worthwhile.

    At the return to the water’s edge, a healthy hearty kiwi lunch will be awaiting you, with cold and hot drinks to match! Enjoy the meal and area before heading back to the sheltered waters of St Helier's Bay.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel or similar
    Meal Plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    AUCKLAND – ROTORUA

    Start your day at the crack of dawn & leave for another truly unique experience. Head out for the Auckland Bridge climb – the only way to truly appreciate the stunning & iconic Harbour Bridge! You could climb under; go over & around Auckland's most iconic landmarks. The climb takes around an hour and a half & is a personally guided tour. Get on top & enjoy the unbelievable 360 degree view of the city.

    At the end of the climb, if you still need more adrenaline rush, head for the Bungy Jump experience off the bridge! Located at a 10minute walk, meet the experts who will provide you with all the gear for you to make the most of this amazing experience!

    Enjoy breakfast and depart to Rotorua either on a private chauffeured vehicle or on a self-drive. The self-drive vehicle will be handed over to you at the hotel.

    Driving Distance: 74 kms approx.
    Duration: 1.5 hrs approx.

    You could either carry on with the chauffer guide or enjoy your self-drive to Rotorua and beyond. Check into your hotel & head for some more exciting activities!

    Agroventures Adventure Park offers five truly unique New Zealand adventures suitable for all ages and comfort levels .They have the world’s only Shweeb - a human powered monorail racetrack. Swing from 40m towards the earth at 130kmph on NZ’s one and only Swoop and the Southern Hemispheres’ only wind tunnel the Freefall Xtreme.

    Spend an hour or the entire day – guaranteed to give you an unforgettable experience and a sense of accomplishment that will remain for long after you have waved farewell.

    Overnight: Koura Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    ROTORUA – TAURANGA

    You have another early start to the day. Wake up early & drive for approximately an hour to the town of Tauranga for another experience of a lifetime – an encounter with the dolphins!

    Driving Distance: 64 kms approx.
    Duration: 1 hrs approx.

    The Bay of Plenty has an abundance of marine life. Step onboard a custom built boat for the sea safari that starts at 8am daily and the tour takes approximately 5hours to complete. Look out for dolphins, whales, orcas and more. You could either sit back & watch the marine life, or take a plunge in & swim with them in their natural habitat. Light snacks & refreshments will also be available during the tour.

    After this exhilarating experience, enjoy your lunch and later depart for a scenic flight over the region. Enjoy the views of the Tauranga region before heading over vast expanses of flourishing native and pine forests to the many and varied geothermal areas of the Rotorua. You could choose either a helicopter or a floatplane to view some of the most dramatic geothermal scenery & activity the destination has on offer.

    Rotorua is also the heartland of New Zealand's Maori culture, and you can head back to Rotorua visiting Te Po for a cultural evening experience. Enjoy a performance of song and dance, followed by food cooked in traditional hangi style (an earth oven). Hear stories of the past & experience an authentic Maori welcome ceremony followed for a truly traditional cultural experience.

    Overnight: Trinity Wharf Hotel or similar
    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 6

    ROTORUA - LAKE TAUPO

    Check out of your hotel this morning & enjoy about an hour and a half’s scenic drive towards Lake Taupo.

    Driving Distance: 85 kms approx.
    Duration: 1.5 hrs approx.

    Check into your hotel at Lake Taupo & depart to visit the famed Huka Falls, the most visited natural attraction in New Zealand. The Waikato is New Zealand's longest river & it moves gracefully from Lake Taupo. Just before the Huka Falls it enters a shallow ravine of hard volcanic rock and the effect is simply magical! A foot bridge right at the top of the falls puts you in a prime position to get up close and witness the spectacular display of more than 220,000 liters of water blasting by every second.

    Post lunch; take a boat cruise to witness the Maori Rock Carvings around Lake Taupo. In the late 1970s master carver Matahi Whakataka-Brightwell had completed his 10-year training with Maori elders & came to Lake Taupo to mark the occasion with a significant carving. He took inspiration from Ngatoroirangi, a visionary Maori navigator who guided the Tuwharetoa and Te Arawa tribes to the Taupo area over a thousand years ago. Get up & close to some of these amazing rock art – the main carving being over 10 metres high and was completed over four long summers!

    This evening, perhaps enjoy a pre-dinner drink at the lodge & later enjoy a meal prepared with the freshest of ingredients organically grown around the lodge; overlooking the stunning lake & mountains.

    Overnight: Lake Taupo Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    LAKE TAUPO

    Head to Tongariro National Park for the legendary Tongariro Alpine Crossing! It is rated as ‘the best full day trek in New Zealand’ and listed by many as one of the 'top 10 day treks in the world'. Lonely Planet even said that a walk amidst the volcanoes of Tongariro is a life-changing experience not to be missed. The diverse and dramatic scenery and unique land forms are spectacular and you will experience some of the most scenic and volcanically active areas of the Tongariro National Park.

    Stop by Bulli Point for some Cliff diving, Lake Taupo style! Take the plunge into the fresh blue water -there are three ledges at Bulli Point with the highest being seven metres.

    Later this evening, maybe you could stop by the town center and enjoy some eclectic shopping or visit one of the quaint cafes that line the friendly streets.

    Overnight: Lake Taupo Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    LAKE TAUPO – AUCKLAND DEPARTURE

    Head to the airport at Lake Taupo for your short 1hr duration flight to Auckland to connect to for international departure or extend your stay & visit the South Island starting from Queenstown. The self-drive vehicle if opted for can be handed over at the airport to the representative.

    Meal Plan: B

SOUTH KIWI ULTIMATE ADVENTURE

  • Duration: 11 Days / 10 Nights

    Insatiable thirst for adrenaline rush is all you need to experience the exhilarating activities from the South Island.

    Programme Overview

    Queenstown – TeAnau - Lake Wanaka - Franz Josef - Kaikoura - Christchurch

    A&K Advantage
    --Get an adrenaline rush on the original Ziptrek Eco tour in Auckland
    Skydive for an experience that will change you forever
    Enjoy the Dart River Jet wilderness experience at Glenorchy
    Journey into famous Glowworm Caves at Te Anau
    Head out for the famed Flight of Kea at Lake Wanaka
    Combine an awesome scenic helicopter flight over Franz Josef glacier with a unique guided walk among the ice caves
    Thaw yourself in comfort in the Glacier Hot Pools for a truly rejuvenating experience
    Drive down the West Coast Soak & in the scenery that New Zealand is renowned for
    Step onboard a unique marine experience and get an opportunity to sight high-spirited Dusky Dolphins
    Take a hot air balloon ride over Christchurch
    Enjoy an exclusive and personalised tour of Christchurch's International Antarctic Centre
    A punt on the Avon River

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE QUEENSTOWN

    On arrival at Queenstown, you will be met by a representative who will assist you with your transfer to the hotel.

    Light lunch & some time at leisure followed by some adrenaline rush! Take a Ziptrek Eco tour - NZ’s #1 original Zipline tour. Situated on Skyline Gondola hill, it is a spectacular and exhilarating eco-adventure through the forest canopy high above Queenstown. Get into the security of your harness & zip over tree tops from one tree to the other for an amazing experience.

    At the end of the ride, drive towards the iconic Skyline. You could either take the famed Gondola ride or enjoy a luge ride and then head to the restaurant for a lovely meal. The market kitchen is a casual cafe dining – both indoor & outdoor and provide great panoramic views of the city; along with great dining options – from freshly baked dishes, pizzas, sandwiches, ice creams, crepes, coffees and other refreshments.

    Overnight: Sofitel Queenstown Hotel and Spa or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    QUEENSTOWN

    Start your day with another adrenaline rush and must do while in Queenstown - head out for the Nzone and skydive with the best in the business! Get harnessed to an experienced qualified jumpmaster & jump out of an aircraft into thin air for an experience that will change you forever! Leave with some great pictures and some amazing memories.

    No trip to Queenstown would be complete without a visit to the home of Bungee! Started way back in 1988 by pioneers Henry Van Ash &A J Hackett and a short drive from Queenstown city centre, the Kawarau Bungy Centre has something to offer everyone. We would recommend you to be brave & do the 43m jump, or you could view the jumps from the decks, or sit back and relax at one of the cafes.

    Drive out with a guide for an hour towards Glenorchy for the Dart River Jet wilderness experience! An authentic New Zealand experience that will take you deep into the World Heritage area of Mt Aspiring National Park! Experience a unique combination of breath taking scenery and exciting jet boating on braided, glacier fed rivers & learn about the areas’ history from your guide. Walk amongst the ancient beech forest, unchanged for 80 million years. Journey by 4WD safari vehicle; stopping to view some of New Zealand’s most spectacular scenery that formed the epic backdrop for many international movies, including ‘The Lord of the Rings’. And if you would like to explore the scenic drive by yourself, a self-drive vehicle will be handed over to you at the hotel.

    Driving Distance: 46 kms approx.
    Duration: 1 hr approx.

    Later in the evening maybe you could stop by the famous Skybar which offers a wide range of wines and platters. Additionally through the summer months a number of artists display their original artworks at the lounge making for an extra special evening!

    Overnight: Sofitel Queenstown Hotel and Spa or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    QUEENSTOWN - TE ANAU

    Leisurely enjoy a late start to the day. Check out of your hotel & drive over to Te AnauTe Anau..

    Driving Distance: 220 kms approx.
    Duration: 3.5 hrs approx.

    Enjoy lunch at this lovely setting and then drive for a couple of hours towards Te Anau. Check into your hotel and head towards the famous Glowworm Caves. A journey into the Te Anau-Au cave system starts with a scenic cruise across Lake Te Anau. Once inside the caves visitors can view the still developing cave formations, accompanied by the sound of flowing water, and drift through caverns inhabited by glowworms.

    Overnight: Fiordland Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    TE ANAU

    Te Anau is the 'gateway to the fiords' being the closest town to Milford Sound, with a scenic two and a half hour’s drive. The drive to Milford is simply stunning and it is recommended you give yourself plenty of time to accommodate for the steep & windy climb; along with the numerous photo stops or viewing opportunities to soak in the spectacular scenery & landscape.

    Once you arrive at Milford, described by Rudyard Kipling as the "eighth wonder of the world", you could head off with a guide on a day walk truly exploring the area. This will be a leisurely 4-5 hours walking. The walk begins with a gentle stroll via the Glade Burn to historic Glade House for lunch and a hot drink.

    Alternately, you could take one of the cruises around the Fjord and comfortably travel the length into the open sea and return in a couple of hours with some stunning images & memories.

    Another unique feature of this stunning Fiordland is the underworld. You could visit the Milford Discovery Center & the Milford Deep Underwater Observatory for a guided dive allowing you access to sights rarely revealed to human visitors! Get underwater & watch the fascinating & unique world as the mountains plunge down as steep rock walls till they reach the floor of the fiord at depths of 100-450m.

    Overnight: Fiordland Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    TE ANAU – LAKE WANAKA

    Head out this morning for some more visual delights; drive for approximately three hours towards Lake Wanaka for some totally awe inspiring landscaped and magical, unforgettable experiences! From the amazing Fiordland region through to the beautiful lakeside town of Wanaka, the drive is purely sublime!

    Driving Distance: 230 kms approx.
    Duration: 3.5 hrs approx.

    Check into your hotel & head out for some adventure!
    You could choose from either the Basecamp Wanaka Climbing Centre - a unique indoor climbing centre with indoor and outdoor climbing walls and Clip 'n Climb. Run on automatic belays, giving you total peace, comfort & security with your climbing experience.

    Alternately, you could indulge in the Wanaka Paintball Games! Or head to the lake for some aqua fun – paragliding, kayaking etc.

    We highly recommend a drink at one of the lakeside bars in Wanaka, whilst watching the sun set over the snowcapped mountains as a perfect end to this marvellous day!

    Overnight: Whare Kea Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    LAKE WANAKA

    Head out for the famed Flight of Kea. The experience includes and exciting walk in the wilderness, a gorgeous scenic Helicopter flight to Milford Sound, offering spectacular views over Mount Aspiring, referred to as the Matterhorn of the south and topped with a gourmet picnic at the Whare Kea Chalet.

    OR
    For the not so faint hearted the hinterland of Wanaka offers tahr as the ultimate game for the hunters. New Zealand is probably the only place worldwide where this magnificent beast can be hunted freely.

    OR
    Choose from a selection of flights with the experienced pilots who will whisk you away to any number of scenic viewpoints in the Lake Wanaka and Fiordland region. Fulfill any of your desire, to land on a glacier, take a walk in the snow, view the spectacular Milford Sound from the air or plan your own spot. You could take to the skies by Helicopter, Vintage Tiger Moth or a well-appointed Cessna Aircraft.

    Overnight: Whare Kea Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    LAKE WANAKA – FRANZ JOSEF

    Discover the rugged scenery along one of New Zealand's most awe-inspiring highways. Journey for approximately four hours & make your way to your accommodation in Franz Josef. Passing the Mount Aspiring National Park, your journey will take you via the Haast Pass, the southernmost pass that connects the communities of the East and West and is a humbling experience. Marvel at the vast rainforests to the clearest waterfalls that eclipse this untouched landscape and enjoy breathtaking sights, from sparkling blue lakes to the high mountain peaks.

    Driving Distance: 285 kms approx.
    Duration: 4 hrs approx.

    Drive to Franz Josef Glacier Heli Hike & discover up close the magnificent phenomenon of the stunning West Coast glaciers. Combine an awesome scenic helicopter flight over Franz Josef glacier with a unique guided walk among the ice caves, pinnacles and séracs of this pristine alpine environment. The experienced and professional helicopter pilots’ informative commentary will show-case the exciting landscape for the adventure ahead. You will be provided a raincoat and over trousers, boots, crampons, woolen socks, gloves and a hat to make this a comfortable experience!

    End your day with a drive to the Glacier Hot Pools & thaw yourself in comfort! Surrounded by native bush, fed with pure local glacier water and nestled in lush rainforest, The Glacier Hot Pools is a secluded haven of natural tranquillity and the perfect way to relax after travelling. An evening under the stars, sheltered by nature’s leafy canopy at The Glacier Hot Pools will be a truly rejuvenating experience.

    Overnight: Te Waionui Forest Retreat or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    FRANZ JOSEF - KAIKOURA VIA WEST COAST & GREYMOUTH

    Check out early this morning and head for one of the world’s most scenic drives. Meet your driver guide & head out onto the West Coast on the Great Coast road towards Greymouth approximately three hours’ drive from Franz Josef and rated as one of the Top 10 Coastal Drives in the world according to Lonely Planet. Soak in the scenery that New Zealand is renowned for and stop every now & then for some stunning pictures. The West Coast scenery is supposed to be so stunning it is guaranteed to take your breath away! The Great Coast Road traverses Charleston, Fox River, Punakaiki, Barrytown, Rapahoe and Runanga on its way from Westport to Greymouth.

    Driving Distance: 172 kms approx.
    Duration: 2.5 hrs approx.

    Enjoy lunch at Greymouth and then drive approximately another five and a half hours towards Kaikoura.

    Driving Distance: 326 kms approx.
    Duration: 4.5 hrs approx.

    Arrive at Kaikoura& check into hotel.

    Make the most of being in the Southern Hemisphere & get a closer look at the Milky Way, a delightful sight. Meet your guide and do the Kaikoura Night Sky tour before turning in for the night.

    Overnight: Select Braemar Lodge & Spa or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    KAIKOURA - CHRISTCHURCH

    Located on a rocky peninsula, the Kaikoura Township protrudes from lush farmland beneath the mountains. The waters off the peninsula offer a complex marine system providing an abundantly rich habitat for marine mammals and seabirds and some of the most magnificent creatures with which we share our planet. Among them are Giant Sperm Whales that can be seen all year round making Kaikoura one of the most popular whale watching locations in the world.

    By 10am, step onboard this unique marine experience and from the comfort of your boat get an opportunity to sight high-spirited Dusky Dolphins, the rare and endangered Hectors Dolphin, New Zealand Fur Seals and a wide variety of sea birds including the Royal Albatross making it an unforgettable journey with some great photographic opportunities.

    The curious NZ Fur Seals & Dusky Dolphins are only a few paddle strokes. Kayak with these along with the local guides who will introduce you to them around the Kaikoura Peninsula.

    Some time at leisure to enjoy the area at your pace and then head out towards Christchurch.

    Driving Distance: 181 kms approx.
    Duration: 3 hrs approx.

    Arrive at Christchurch & check into hotel. Unwind and enjoy some dinner before turning in for the night.

    On arrival, check in and overnight at hotel.

    Overnight: The George Christchurch or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    CHRISTCHURCH

    Wake at the crack of dawn for an experience of a lifetime! Take a hot air balloon ride over Christchurch and watch the sun rise over the city. Once you reach the launch site, you will get a preflight briefing & the opportunity to help the crew inflate the balloon. Step onboard & cruise over the city for some spectacular sights & pictures.

    Return on land and celebrate with a champagne breakfast!

    Later, join an Old Antarctic Explorer on an exclusive and personalised tour of Christchurch's International Antarctic Centre discovering New Zealand's Scott Base, The Snow & Ice Experience and the Antarctic Storm where you will experience extreme wind chill Antarctic conditions. Post a light lunch at the center; enjoy a cruise to Antarctica on board an Antarctic Cruise Ship in the brand new 4D Extreme Theatre or enjoy watching the penguins being fed in the New Zealand Penguin Encounter. At the end of your 3-hour personalised Antarctic experience you leave with a signed copy of an Antarctic book which will be yours to take away!

    Afternoon at your own pace & end your day with 30minute calming punt on the Avon! It’s an iconic Christchurch experience not to be missed! Dinner followed by overnight stay.

    Overnight: The George Christchurch
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    CHRISTCHURCH DEPARTURE

    Meet a representative who will transfer you to the airport for your onward connection.

    Meal Plan: B

TWO ISLANDS, MANY FACES

  • Duration: 10 Days / 9 Nights

    Fun filled activities, spectacular experiences and stunning landscapes with indefinite views will leave you with memories to cherish for life.

    Programme Overview

    Auckland - Rotorua - Queenstown - Wanaka - Mount Cook - Christchurch

    A&K Advantage
    - Step onboard a cruise on the beautiful Hauraki Gulf Marine Park and encounter marine mammals endemic
       to this region
    - Enjoy the full day learning about Auckland’s Maori Culture
    - Visit the SKYCITY & the iconic Sky Tower
    - Drive out to see the famous Waitomo Glowworm Caves and Hobbiton Movie Set
    - Fly through the trees in the Rotorua Canopy Tours
    - Watch New Zealand’s nature stories come to life in the amazing Rainbow Springs Park
    - Step aboard a cruise to experience a historic journey and the fascinating stories of the local Maori people.
    - Take a breathtaking scenic flight to the World Heritage listed Milford Sound
    - Enjoy a cruise & a guided walk on the pristine waters of Lake Wanaka to Mou Waho Island Nature Reserve
    - Depart for Mt. Cook for the amazing Glacier Explorers Experience
    - Enjoy an exclusive and personalised tour of Christchurch's International Antarctic Centre

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE AUCKLAND

    On arrival at Auckland, you will be met by a representative & transferred to your accommodation. You will get some time to relax & unwind from the flight and post lunch be met by a guide for your private sightseeing of Auckland.

    The sightseeing tour will include a scenic drive along the waterfront, the quaint Victorian village of Parnell and panoramic views from the extinct volcano - Mount Eden. Head out towards the harbour early afternoon for a marine encounter with the Whale & Dolphins.

    Considered to be one the most biologically and geographically diverse marine parks in the world with over 22 species of dolphins and whales, step onboard your boat on the beautiful Hauraki Gulf Marine Park and encounter marine mammals endemic to this region over the hour and half tour.

    On conclusion of the trip, return to your hotel for the overnight.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel or similar
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    AUCKLAND

    Enjoy the full day learning about Auckland’s Maori Culture. Meet with a local Maori, who will guide you around the fantastic city and region of Auckland, giving you lots of interesting information about their history, places, people and culture throughout your tour.

    The tour starts at 9am and highlights Auckland’s stunning West Coast giving you an opportunity to experience some of New Zealand’s finest culture & scenery. Explore some important historic sites and learn more about the fascinating Maori history along the way. Walk on the crater’s edge of an ancient volcano & discover dramatic cliff tops, stormy seas, unique black sand beaches and much more on the famous ‘Wild West Coast’.

    End your day at another iconic Auckland location & experience. Visit the SKYCITY located in the heart of the city below the iconic Sky Tower. Get on top & experience 360 degree spectacular views of the city. You could walk around the pergola at SkyWalk or take a controlled free-fall to ground level at SkyJump.

    The SKYCITY also has a number of famed restaurants & cafes that serve fresh seasonal and local produce. Enjoy your time here at your pace & return to your hotel for the night.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    AUCKLAND - ROTORUA

    Check out of your hotel this morning and leave to spend the day visiting two world-famous visitor attractions on a single day. Drive out in a chauffeured vehicle or a self-drive vehicle, which will be handed over to you at the hotel, to see the famous Waitomo Glowworm Caves and Hobbiton Movie Set, made famous by the movie trilogies “The Lord of the Rings” and “The Hobbit”.

    At Waitomo, you will experience the famous Glowworm Caves on a 45-minute tour. After lunch in Waitomo, we continue via the small country town of Cambridge to “The Shire’s Rest”, so you can have the wonderful opportunity to see the original Hobbiton Movie Set, which was the setting for Peter Jackson's "The Lord of the Rings" and "The Hobbit" movie trilogies.

    At the end of the tours, head towards Rotorua.

    Driving Distance: 228 kms approx.
    Duration: 1.5 hrs approx.

    Enjoy the drive for about an hour & on arrival; check in to hotel for dinner & overnight

    Overnight: Hotel Black Swan or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    ROTORUA

    Easy start to the day but one filled with adventure & fun!

    Post breakfast, head for an experience not to be missed - the Rotorua Canopy Tours. Fly through the trees with New Zealand's only native forest zipline canopy tour & enjoy a 3 hour wilderness experience in native New Zealand forest on the outskirts of Rotorua. Be amazed by the conservation story, and the threats to native New Zealand wildlife.

    Head out later towards the amazing 22 acre park Rainbow Springs Park. The new Rainbow Springs will bring New Zealand nature stories to life. Not only are you able to see endangered native species like the Kiwi and Tuatara, watch the feeding of the animals, a live bird show and now with the Big Splash water ride, that story comes to life.

    Overnight: Hotel Black Swan or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    ROTORUA – QUEENSTOWN

    Fly this morning from Rotoura to Queenstown, and hand over your self-drive vehicle at the airport to the representative, in case you were exploring by yourself. On arrival at Queenstown, you will be met by a representative and assisted with your check into hotel.

    Later, aboard a cruise experience an incredible historic journey, the fascinating stories of the local Maori. Alpine Maori lived here for over 500 years; find out what they did to survive... Maori thrived here, find out how... Then, virtually overnight, they abandoned their traditional ways, find out why... The Maori Guides, dressed in genuine clothing, will step you through the fascinating story of how Alpine Maori lived, their mythology and how they adapted.

    Overnight: Sofitel Queenstown or similar
    Meal Plan: B, L

  • Day 6

    QUEENSTOWN

    Take a breathtaking scenic flight to the World Heritage listed Milford Sound. Fly over secluded alpine lakes, lush rainforest, through deep bush-fringed valleys and past rugged mountain peaks. Take in the beauty of the Southern Alps on your way into Milford Sound, experiencing this amazing glacial creation. View Lake Quill, the source of the spectacular Sutherland Falls - the second highest waterfall in the Southern Hemisphere. Sweep the full length of the fiord, flying to a remote West Coast beach to view seal colonies.

    Overnight: Sofitel Queenstown or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    QUEENSTOWN - WANAKA - MOUNT COOK

    Today make your way to Wanaka on a chauffeured vehicle or self-drive as per your preference. If you opt for self-drive, the vehicle will be handed over to you at the hotel. Enjoy breathtaking sights, from high mountain peaks to blue lakes before arriving into Wanaka.

    Driving Distance: 68 kms approx.
    Duration: 1 hr approx.

    Arrive & then head for a cruise on the pristine waters of Lake Wanaka to Mou Waho Island Nature Reserve. Enjoy a guided walk to the spectacular summit that reveals the island's secret - A Lake on top of an Island! - Enjoy a serene lunch here, up close and personal with rare native birds, and plant a tree as part of this unique eco experience.

    On conclusion of the cruise, you will head out towards Mount Cook village for an approximately three hours’ drive stopping along the scenic drive for some spectacular photo opportunities.

    Driving Distance: 200 kms approx.
    Duration: 3 hrs approx.

    Overnight: Hermitage Aoraki Mount Cook or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    MOUNT COOK – CHRISTCHURCH

    Depart early morning on a scenic flight over the Alpine region & explore the stunning beauty of this area. Tracking alongside the magnificent Lake Pukaki, making your way high above the Southern Alps, you will take in amazing sights of the Tasman and Murchison glaciers, majestic Mt Cook and the spectacular Fox and Franz Josef glaciers for some spectacular visuals.

    Later, depart for Mt. Cook-Glacier Explorers experience -The only tour of its kind in New Zealand and one of the only accessible glacial lakes in the world containing icebergs.

    Soak in the natural sights and perhaps stroll around the area before driving to Christchurch.

    Overnight: The George Christchurch or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    CHRISTCHURCH

    Wake at the crack of dawn for an experience of a lifetime! Take a hot air balloon ride over Christchurch and watch the sun rise over the city. Once you reach the launch site, you will get a preflight briefing & the opportunity to help the crew inflate the balloon. Step onboard & cruise over the city for some spectacular sights & pictures.

    Return on land and celebrate with a champagne breakfast!

    Later, join an Old Antarctic Explorer on an exclusive and personalised tour of Christchurch's International Antarctic Centre discovering New Zealand's Scott Base, The Snow & Ice Experience and the Antarctic Storm where you will experience extreme wind chill Antarctic conditions. Post a light lunch at the center; enjoy a cruise to Antarctica on board an Antarctic Cruise Ship in the brand new 4D Extreme Theatre or enjoy watching the penguins being fed in the New Zealand Penguin Encounter. At the end of your 3-hour personalised Antarctic experience you leave with a signed copy of an Antarctic book which will be yours to take away!

    Afternoon at your own pace & end your day with 30minute calming punt on the Avon! It’s an iconic Christchurch experience not to be missed! Dinner followed by overnight stay.

    Overnight: The George Christchurch
    Meal Plan: B, L

  • Day 10

    CHRISTCHURCH DEPARTURE

    Meet your representative who will assist you with your transfer to the airport for your onward connection. If you were driving by yourself, please hand over the vehicle to the representative at the airport.

    Meal Plan: B

NEW ZEALAND – FOOD & WINE LOVER’S PARADISE

  • Duration: 13 Days / 12 Nights

    New Zealand’s award winning wines, delectable fresh food made with the freshest produce and enticing farmer’s markets will melt you in your knees.

    Programme Overview

    Waipara - Marlborough - Bay of Many Coves - Wellington - Hawkes’ Bay - Auckland

    A&K Advantage
    - Enjoy a gourmet lunch at this family-run winery
    - Visit the Waipara farmer’s market for an authentic feel of local produce like olive oils, venison, breads, vegetables, nuts and more
    - Taste the award-winning wines along your journey
    - Head to Herzog for a cooking Master class
    - Visit Moruara Island & spend a day cruising through the Queen Charlotte Sound eco-marine haven
    - Sail across the Cook Strait from Picton to Wellington.
    - Appreciate tea in a Gong Fu Cha Private Tea Appreciation Workshop at Divine Kapitea
    - Ride the rapids of the scenic Otaki River under the careful eye of qualified guides
    - Explore the best local produce with a private guided food tour
    - Take a scenic helicopter ride over the spectacular Mount Hector
    - Visit the Te Awa Winery with a personal tour of the cellars
    - Visit the SKYCITY for 2 ultimate experiences of SkyWalk and SkyJump.
    - Enjoy an evening cruise along the Wickemata harbor

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE CHRISTCHURCH – WAIPARA

    On arrival you will be met by a representative who will assist you with your private transfer to Pegasus Bay in the Canterbury region.

    Driving Distance: 60 kms aprrox.
    Duration: 45 mins approx.

    Visit the fine Waipara winery; a family-run winery with a reputation of region’s best wine and food. Relish a gourmet meal at this winery, which is amongst the top five wine producers nationally as per Robert Parker's buyer's guide and the restaurant was named Best Winery Restaurant in NZ by Cuisine magazine for two consecutive years. The spread of wines available include award winning Rieslings, chardonnay, and pinot noir. Taste the wines while you admire the floor-to-ceiling stacks of oak aging casks.

    You will be introduced to local foods and wines to pair along. Later meet the wine makers, viticulturists and the owners of this spectacular vineyard.

    Overnight: Dry Paddocks Country Retreat or similar
    Meal Plan: L

  • Day 2

    WAIPARA

    Waipara Springs produce a range of wines that have consistently won national and international recognition. From Sauvignon blanc, Riesling and rich Chardonnay to intensely concentrated Pinot noir, Waipara Springs vineyards have been setting high benchmarks for New Zealand wineries.This morning, visit the Waipara farmer’s market. It is stocked with local produce like olive oils, venison, breads, vegetables, nuts and more.

    Later visit the Mud House Wine Company located at the Waipara Springs. Taste the award-winning wines as you enjoy the scenic views.

    Overnight: Dry Paddocks Country Retreat or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    WAIPARA

    Depart for the renowned wine producing region of Marlborough.

    Driving Distance: 264 kms approx.
    Duration: 3 hrs approx.

    Marlborough is New Zealand’s flagship wine region. Marlborough and Sauvignon Blanc are greatly responsible for placing New Zealand on the International wine scene. It is often referred to “Kei puta te Wairau” by the locals, which translates to “The place with a hole in the cloud” and is one of the country’s driest region. The right combination of cool yet a heavily sunny climate, with low rainfall, moderately fertile soil and free draining contribute to variety of the available wines.

    You will be met by an A&K Representative and assisted with check-in.

    You will be met by a representative who will accompany you for the vineyard visits & introduce you to the famed and award winning wineries.

    Hunters Wine
    An original family owned winery established more 3 decades ago, it is an award winner in the field of wines, its owner’s entrepreneurship and for it’s beautiful landscape.

    Neudorf Vineyards
    You will be introduced to the different wines matured at the vineyard at the cellar door experience at this vineyard. This winery is a result of a persistent effort by its owners for over 3 decades and produces award winning wines

    Herzog
    Herzog is another family owned New Zealand winery and restaurant. The passion for wine and food along with a commitment to excellence and quality has been the driving force behind this amazing winery.

    Continue the wine tasting into the evening and pair your favoured wines with local delicious food items in an award winning restaurant.

    Overnight: Dry Paddocks Country Retreat or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    MARLBOROUGH

    This morning you will be assisted by an A&K representative to wine estates. Highfield Estates being one of them.

    Return to Herzog for a Master cooking class and get introduced to the ultimate “behind the scenes” insight into the kitchen. You will also be educated on wine pairings with the meal you learn.

    Overnight: Vineyard Cottage or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    MARLBOROUGH – BAY OF MANY COVES

    Drive to the region of Bay of Many Coves and hand over the vehicle to the representative.

    Driving Distance: 33 kms approx.
    Duration: 30 mins approx.

    Hop on a private chartered yacht for a short ride (30 mins) to Bay of Many Coves, which offers some exciting water based activities and experiences, like swimming with the dolphins, kayaking and motor yacht cruising.

    Visit the Moruara Island to spend a day cruising through the Queen Charlotte Sound eco-marine haven. It is abundant in sea animals like seals, dolphins, penguins, gannets and shearwaters. The surrounding Sound also has a resident pod of Hector’s Dolphins, which are thought to be world’s rarest marine dolphin.

    Savour the delights of fresh local seafood and produce and enjoy the local delicacies (fresh seafood, wild meats, home-crafted and creative cuisine).

    Overnight: On board Private Yacht
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    BAY OF MANY COVES – WELLINGTON

    Sail across the Cook Strait from Picton to Wellington. The Cook Strait journey is amazing and the most unforgettable, as you come across some of the world’s most breathtaking scenery and wildlife.

    On arrival you will be assisted to your self-drive vehicle and later for check-in to the hotel.

    Appreciate tea in a Gong Fu Cha Private Tea Appreciation Workshop at Divine Kapitea, followed with an oriental High Tea indulgence. “Gong Fu Cha” is the ancient art of Chinese tea brewing experience, which has highly therapeutic effects in a relaxing ambience taking tea appreciation to a whole new level.

    OR

    Ride the rapids of the scenic Otaki River under the careful eye of qualified guides. There is enough for all to experience adventure at different levels, from taking control of a raft, swimming the rapids, jumping off a rock, experiencing the waterfall in all its glory, to simply relaxing and enjoying the view. To add to a twist to this experience you could opt for night rafting, where you will be guided by the glow worms that line the banks and the sounds of the approaching rapids.

    Overnight: Greenmantle Estate or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    WELLINGTON

    You will spend the day on a private guided food tour. Explore the best local produce, visiting well known coffee makers, chocolate tasting and relish lunch at one of the renowned restaurants.

    Take a scenic helicopter ride over the spectacular Mount Hector. During the winter season it’s often snow capped at an altitude of 5000ft. Listen to informative lores of the Tararua Ranges of how skiers would tramp from the Otaki Forks to ski the slopes near Kime Hut during the 1920s & 1930s.

    Visit the Te Papa Museum and the Weta Workshop. Weta workshop is a conceptual design and physical manufacturing facility for the creative and the entertainment world. It is an award winning company famed for the costumes of Lord of the Rings.

    Overnight: Greenmantle Estate or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    WELLINGTON – HAWKES BAY

    This morning you will take a short 55 mins flight to Napier Airport. On arrival you will be privately transferred to your hotel and assisted with check-in.

    The Hawke's Bay region offers a wealth of exciting Food & Wine excursions. It is New Zealand’s second largest wine growing region and home to over 70 wineries. Attend wine tasting, wine appreciate, food and wine pairing sessions to increase your knowledge on this field.

    Hawke’s Bay is privileged with two of the most popular markets in the country. Black Barn Grower’s Market which is organized every Saturday morning over summer and the Hawke’s Bay Farmer’s Market where you could find the freshest produce every Sunday morning.

    Visit the Te Awa Winery with a personal tour of the cellars, private tasting and followed by a three course lunch.

    Hawke’s Bay region is rich with bounty from the land, sea and rivers owing to the Mediterranean climate. On a food tour you have the opportunity to meet the local producers of cheese, olive oils and orchard fruit and taste their products.

    Overnight: Farm at Cape Kidnappers or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    HAWKES BAY

    This morning head off for the vineyard visits.

    Te Mata Estate, is one of New Zealand's top wineries producing the classic Hawke's Bay red. Since it is made in small quantities, a chance to taste it should never be missed.

    Mission Estate Winery is located in the Taradale hills overlooking Napier. It is the country's oldest winery, dating back to 1851 and produces award-winning wines, including the Mission Jewelstone range which can be bought or tasted at the cellar door. You could enjoy a meal with a seat on the terrace which provides a spectacular view of the vineyard and Napier.

    Back at the Farm at Cape Kidnappers, you could experience a quad bike tour of the 6,000 acre working sheep and cattle farm.

    Overnight: Farm at Cape Kidnappers or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    HAWKES BAY

    Locate the Kiwi’s habitat this morning as you walk along with an experienced guide from Environmental Services. It is a fun experience to identify and explore the shy bird’s habitat.

    Craggy Range Winery, is located beside a small lake with the towering heights of Te Mata Peak beyond. This vineyard offers an amazing milieu for wine making and tasting.

    Afternoon can be well spent on a fishing session, since Hawke’s Bay offers a wide range of fishing opportunities. The bay's major rivers, the Mohaka, Tutkuir, Ngaruroro, Waipawa and Tukituki, are well populated with famed wild brown and rainbow trout.

    Overnight: Farm at Cape Kidnappers or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    HAWKES BAY – AUCKLAND

    Depart for a short flight of 1 hr 5 mins from Napier Bay to Auckland.

    This region is home to about 100 vineyards and wineries, known for rich, Bordeaux style reds, as well as the boutique vineyards on Waiheke Island.

    Artisan Wines is the producer of high-quality wine using grapes sourced from around New Zealand. Appreciate excellent wine and food in the serene beauty of Oratia Valley as you taste award-winning Auckland wines in the boutique winery. Saturday is a good day because a farmers' market is organized on the grounds of this vineyard. You can try a wide range of Artisan wines alongside local produce, much of it being organic.

    Babich Wines, is a family owned vineyard, which been making wine in New Zealand for nearly 100 years beginning first in the far north where Josip Babich joined his brothers from Croatia and planted grapes near the gum fields. The Henderson cellar site has a range of tastings and snacks. The 72-acre site is unique; years ago it was amid farmland but now is almost surrounded by houses as the population in west Auckland has grown.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 12

    AUCKLAND

    Explore Ponsonby and Jervois Roads at your pace or for an ultimate rush visit the sky city for 2 ultimate experiences of sky walk and sky jump.

    This afternoon, visit the Villa Maria Winery, which has grown from a small company founded by the Fistonich family to one of the country's biggest producers. It is renowned for its consistency, and depending on your time of travel you could enjoy one of the outdoor music concerts hosted during summer. The tasting room offers selections from most of the company's vineyards.

    A spectacular way to spend an evening is Auckland is aboard the yacht cruising along with Wickemata harbor. Relax on this dinner cruise enjoying the panoramic view of the city all lit up and beautiful. You may also lend a hand to the captain.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 13

    DEPART AUCKLAND

    You will be privately transferred to the Airport to board your international flight.

    Meal Plan: B
