IDYLLIC GREECE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 days

    Visit the beautiful land of Gods and Godesses for a honeymoon or romantic holiday to Greece.

    Enjoy a walking tour of Plaka & The Acropolis whilst in Athens, take a visit to the site of the Ancient Olympics and see the most fascinating sights of in Corinth, Mycanae and Nafplion.

    Charter a private yacht for a romantic day sailing off the coast of Kyllini and enjoy spending time as newlyweds exploring this gorgeous country.

    Highlights:Athens, Acropolis, Plaka, Delphi, Athena's Sanctuary, Corinth, Nafplion, Ancient Olympia, Kyllini

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE ATHENS

    Upon arrival at Athens airport you will be welcomed to your special holiday to Greece by your Guardian Angel as you exit customs into the arrivals hall, and transferred to your hotel where you will be met by your local Guide and assisted with check-in procedures.

    Overnight: Electra Palace

  • Day 2

    ATHENS

    Today you will be met by your English-speaking guide for a half-day walking tour of Athens (4 hours). See the complex of monuments that most symbolise Athens - the Acropolis. The highlight of your tour to the Acropolis will be the Parthenon, one of the most important ancient monuments in the Western world, which was dedicated to the Goddess Athena and conceived in 447BC.

    Afterwards, enjoy a walk through the ancient district of Plaka, one of the most picturesque, romantic and interesting parts of the city.

    Add-on Option 1: Enjoy a half-day of sightseeing outside of Athens with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide (4 hours). Venture along the scenic coast to Cape Sounion and visit the ancient ruined Temple of Poseidon. This temple is believed to date from the 5th century BC and was built by Poseidon who ranked second only to Athena herself in terms of power. The Temple enjoys an impressive position, high on the cliffs. After sightseeing, return to Athens.

    Overnight: Electra Palace
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    ATHENS - DELPHI - ATHENS

    Today, enjoy a full day excursion (10 hours) from Athens, which will begin at the ancient site of Delphi. Delphi was home to Apollo and its position between the Phaedriades cliffs and Mt. Ayios make it one of the most enchanting sites in Greece.

    At Delphi you will be introduced to the major ruins such as Athena's Sanctuary, the Pentelic marble Round Building and Apollo's Temple. On your return from Delphi, stop en-route at Ossios Loukas to admire the architecture and mosaics of the monastery, which is still inhabited to this day. The views across from the Monastery are fantastic.

    Overnight: Electra Palace
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    ATHENS - CORINTH - MYCANAE - NAFPLION

    Today, meet your expert guide and private vehicle as you are transferred to your hotel in Nafplion. Along the journey, enjoy a full day sightseeing tour to Corinth & Mycanae (8 hours). Visit the site and museum of ancient Corinth, which was one of the wealthiest cities of Ancient Greece in the 6th century BC.

    Continue on to the ancient citadel of Mycenae, first settled by the Neolithic people in the 6th millennium BC. After sightseeing, arrive to relax at your hotel in Nafplion.

    Overnight: Nafplia Palace
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    NAFPLION

    Nafplion is one of Greece's most romantic and prettiest towns. Your expert guide leads you on a 3 hour walking tour through the narrow streets of the old town which are paved with marble and filled with elegant Venetian houses and gracious neoclassical mansions. During the tour, visit Akronafplia Fortress and the Archaeological Museum of Nafplion and be sure to spend time strolling through the beautiful parks and squares.

    Add-on Option 2: This afternoon, drive to the nearby archaeological site of Epidaurus to admire the magnificent 4th century theatre whose elegance, perfection of design and harmonious proportions made it the most famous in the Ancient World. Today the theatre is beautifully preserved and retains the astonishing acoustics that enabled actors to be heard without difficulty by all of the 14,000 spectators. The theatre was the showpiece of a fine Sanctuary dedicated to Aesculapius, god of medicine. There will be time to enjoy the delightful setting of the site before returning to your hotel.

    Add-on Option 3: This afternoon, meet your guide and private vehicle at your hotel, and make your way to the Palamidi fortress that dominates the setting of Nafplion. This castle was built during the Venetian occupation in the early 19th century, 999 steps lead up to the fort comprising of three forts and red stone bastions.

    Overnight: Nafplia Palace
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    KYLLINI

    This morning, meet with your expert guide and private vehicle and make your way West to towards Olympia. Enjoy a tour of the site of Ancient Olympia. The site was primarily a sanctuary dedicated to Zeus, however, it is of course also the location of the ancient Olympic games. Today there is a museum on-site which displays many of the site's priceless treasures. Continue on your journey to your hotel on the Olympia coast, in Kyllini.

    Overnight: Grecotel Olympia Riviera Thalasso
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    KYLLINI

    Today you will charter a luxury private yacht off the stunning coastline of Kyllini to enjoy idyllic time.

    Overnight: Grecotel Olympia Riviera Thalasso
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    KYLLINI

    Today is at leisure to explore and to enjoy the exceptional facilities at your hotel.

    Overnight: Grecotel Olympia Riviera Thalasso
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    KYLLINI

    Today is at leisure to explore independently or enjoy the exceptional facilities at your hotel.

    Overnight: Grecotel Olympia Riviera Thalasso
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    DEPART KYLLINI

    Today you will be privately transferred to Athens airport by your driver.

ON A PRIVATE TOUR OF ATHENS AND CYCLADES ISLANDS

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 15 days

    A 15-day itinerary starting and finishing in Athens and including a 10-day private Yacht Tour around the Cyclades Islands.

    Highlights: Athens, Cape Sounion, Delphi, Mykonos, Santorini

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE ATHENS

    Upon arrival at Athens airport you will be welcomed to Greece by your guide (who will spend the next 15 days with you) as you exit customs into the arrivals hall, and transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in procedures.

    Overnight: Hotel Grande Bretagne

  • Day 2

    ATHENS

    Today see the complex of monuments that most symbolise Athens - the Acropolis. The highlight of your tour to the Acropolis will be the Parthenon, one of the most important ancient monuments in the Western world, which was dedicated to the Goddess Athena and conceived in 447BC. Depending on timing, you may be able to see the Changing of the Guard in Constitution Square. Afterwards you will enjoy a walk through the ancient district of Plaka, one of the most picturesque and interesting parts of the city. This afternoon, continue to the National Archaeological Museum one of the richest and most important museums in the world with its formidable collection of unique exhibits such as sculpture, pottery and jewellery.

    Overnight: Hotel Grande Bretagne
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    ATHENS - CAPE SOUNION - ATHENS

    Begin your tour of Athens at the Benaki Museum this morning, a fascinating display of eclectic Greek and Oriental art housed in the Benaki family house. Drive past the Panethenaic Stadium, a pure marble masterpiece. Timing dependent, you also may be able to see the elaborate Changing of the Guard in Constitution Square. This afternoon, venture along the scenic coast to Cape Sounion where you will visit the ancient ruined Temple of Poseidon. This temple is believed to date from the 5th century BC and was built by Poseidon who ranked second only to Athena herself in terms of power. The Temple enjoys an impressive position, high on the cliffs. After sightseeing you will return to Athens.

    Overnight: Hotel Grande Bretagne
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    ATHENS - DELPHI - OSIOS LOUKAS - ATHENS

    Enjoy an excursion from Athens, which will begin at the ancient site of Delphi. Delphi was home to Apollo and its position between the Phaedriades cliffs and Mt. Ayios make it one of the most enchanting sites in Greece. At Delphi you will be introduced to the major ruins such as Athena's Sanctuary, the Pentelic marble Round Building and Apollo's Temple. On your return from Delphi you will stop en-route at Ossios Loukas to admire the architecture and mosaics of the monastery, which is still inhabited to this day. The views across from the Monastery are fantastic.

    Overnight: Hotel Grande Bretagne
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    ATHENS - KEA

    Later this morning you will be privately transferred to the port with the assistance of your guide and board a private yacht for 10-night cruising in between Cyclades islands, available to you from about midday today.

    The object of any cruise should be enjoyment, relaxation, learning and visiting new destinations whilst using the yacht to bring you to the locations not always on the major tourist routes. The below itinerary has been planned so that you have about 2 hours of cruising in the morning and 2 hours cruising in the afternoon allowing plenty of time for exploring the sightseeing as well as relaxation. Please note that the suggested itinerary is subject to weather conditions at the time of cruising and is flexible as to the Cyclades Islands you may wish to visit.

    Later this afternoon / evening you may like to explore the island of Kea, it is an exceptionally picturesque island. On the south side of Nikolaos Bay - which was a pirate stronghold in the 13th c. - is the little port of Korissia, built on the side of ancient Korissia. There are remains of the ancient town walls and a Sanctuary of Apollo. The famous lion - carved from the native rock in the 6th c. BCE - can be seen just north-east of Kea town. Another highlight is the beautiful anchorage of Poleis. Vourkari is a small bay with many traditional taverns, small shops and bars and is certainly worth a visit.

    You will be confirmed for 10 nights on the Iraklis L, inclusive of crew, as well as an Advance Provisioning Allowance 'APA' (APA covers such expenses as fuel, marina & harbour fees, food & beverages for the charter party [on board], satellite communications if used, local taxes if any.

    Overnight: Iraklis L
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    SYROS

    Syros is the capital of Cyclades group of islands. Its central situation makes it the principal centre of administration, commerce and fisheries in the Cyclades and a focal point of the shipping routes in the Central Aegean. For a sailing holiday there are several good bays to anchor: Varis in the south and Delfino, Megas Lakkos & Kini along the west coast. Syros is also famous for the local sweets named loukoumi, its traditional restaurants and the cultural events that take place every summer.

    Overnight: Iraklis L
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    DELOS & MYKONOS

    Today continue to Delos & Mykonos. Delos is an uninhabited island of many archaeological treasures. Delos was first a place of pilgrimage before becoming a thriving commercial port in the 3rd and 2nd centuries BC.

    Mykonos is the most popular island of Cyclades Group, famous from the international jet set visitors and the celebrating atmosphere which is overarching the island. A very active night-life in combination with beautiful beaches and excellent restaurants. Mykonos is a beautiful island of whitewashed houses and lovely beaches. Wander through the narrow streets in Mykonos Town and visit the fascinating Maritime Museum and Lena's house to see how the locals live. See the Wind Mills in Mykonos Town and enjoy a visit to a local Bakery.

    Overnight: Iraklis L
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    PAROS

    Travel to Paros today, considered as one of the most beautiful island of the Cyclades. Three bays cut deep inland - in the west the sheltered Paroikia Bay, with the island's capital that serves as the main sailing port. In the north the bay which shelters the little town of Naoussa, which in Roman times was the island's main port for the shipment of Lychnites marble; and in the east the flat Marmara bay. Paros is also known in Greece for its ideal weather conditions for windsurfing.

    Overnight: Iraklis L
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 9

    IOS

    Ios is a small island in the heart of the Cyclades. IOS is said to be the island of youth, where young people from all over the world meet and enjoy their summers. Ios is famous for its vivid nightlife with an uncountable number of bars, cafés, clubs and restaurants and for its beaches with crystal clear water. The little port of Ios, with the domed Church of Ayia Irini (17th c.) lies in a sheltered bay on the west coast of the island. One kilometre up the fertile Kato Kampos Valley, conspicuously and picturesquely situated on the hillside, is the chief place on the island, Ios.

    Overnight: Iraklis L
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 10

    SANTORINI

    Many reasons have made Santorini island famous worldwide. Its great wines, the international and local cuisine but most of all, the villages which are situated on cliffs and offer breathtaking view over the submerged volcano. The island also has impressive beaches with the sand of tour choice: black, red or white! Thira together with the smaller islands of Thirasia and Aspro are part of a volcanic crater, which has been engulfed by the sea. In the centre are the Kammeni islands, the cones of later volcanoes, which came into being in historical times. Hot springs and emissions of gas bear witness to continuing volcanic activity. You may like to visit Fira, where you can walk to the edge of the caldera for a spectacular view and also visit the prehistoric Museum of Fira. You may also like to explore Oia for a stroll through the streets. Oia is a very ancient name and is about 6 miles from Fira, overlooking the northwest of the island. You may also wish to enjoy wine tasting at a vineyard at Antoniou.

    Overnight: Iraklis L
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 11

    FOLEGANDROS

    Folegandros is a small and rocky island with wild beauty and a spectacular capital perched on a cliff with medieval dwellings. On a flat expanse above the pretty harbor of Karavostasi is Hora or Folegandros, which consists of two sections. In the old settlement in the Kastro area, the outer walls of the houses form the sort of defensive precinct to be found else where in the Cyclades. The new quarter is just as pretty, with narrow alleys, stepped walls, courtyards, wooden balconies and typically Aegean churches. The finest church on the island that of Our Lady, stands above a section of the ancient wall.

    Overnight: Iraklis L
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 12

    MILOS

    Milos is the fifth largest island in the Cyclades, famous for its volcanic earth, where thousands of years of volcanic action resulted in fascinating golden-white beaches. Enter the island through its natural harbor Adamas which is said to be the largest and safest in the Mediterranean. The capital Plaka is 220m above sea level and offers a view of the harbor and the typical Greek cobbled streets. You may like to visit the Archaeological Museum or walk up to the Kastro which was built during the 13th century by the Venetians. Or you may like to visit Phylakope whcih is said to be the oldest town of the Cyclades.

    Overnight: Iraklis L
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 13

    SIFNOS

    The principal port on Sifnos is Kamares, which is very well sheltered. From there roads lead to the must-see places along the coasts and indeed these also proof beautiful anchorages and should really be visited by boat. The medieval village of Kastro - with remnants of its ancient walls - but also the bays of Faros, Vathi and Platis Gialos are absolutely worth exploring: ranslucent turquoise water, combined with good taverns. However, the best restaurants can be found in Apollonia.

    Overnight: Iraklis L
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 14

    SERIFOS & KYTHNOS

    Serifos is an island renowned for its excellent food and relaxed atmosphere. Most of the anchorages in the south are now used by fish farms. Moreover, apart from Livadi and the Monastery of the Taxiarchs in the north, there is much to be seen. Its highest point is Mount Tourlos with 483 m. The island's main sources of income are its modest agriculture and its open-cast iron mines, which have been worked since ancient times.

    Kythnos is an unspoiled island. The coast is much indented - with many beautiful anchorages and ports - and for the most part falls steeply down to the sea. The two charming inland villages, Chora (Kythnos town) and the lively little Dryopis, can best be visited by scooter or bike. The two main ports are Mericha and Loutra. Mericha - only minutes sailing away from the oddly shaped Sandbar Bay - is the smaller of the two. Loutra on the north-east nowadays even has a new harbour with new moles providing the best protection from the Meltemi on the island.

    Overnight: Iraklis L
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 15

    KYTHNOS - ATHENS / DEPART ATHENS

    This morning return to Athens for disembarkation at about midday. You will be privately transferred to the airport where your guide will say her/his good-byes to you.

    Meal Plan: B

ANCIENT GREECE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 days

    In conjunction with the Starwood Group we are offering exclusive 3 nights for the price of 2 deals at each of the hotels featured in this itinerary - See our Special Offers page for more details. Terms and Conditions apply

    A 9 night tour exploring the archaeology and heritage of Athens, Santorini and Mykonos.

    Highlights: Athens, Mykonos, Santorini

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE ATHENS

    Arrive at Athens Airport to a warm welcome by your local representative before privately transferring to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in procedures.

    Dinner suggestion: Offering unsurpassed views of the Acropolis, Syntagma Square and the ancient wonders of Athens, the airy Grande Bretagne rooftop restaurant offers an inspired Mediterranean menu that matches the unique locale with choices as remarkable as the vistas.

    Overnight: Hotel Grande Bretagne

  • Day 2

    ATHENS

    No trip to Athens would be complete without a visit to the Acropolis, the complex of monuments which provides such a stunning backdrop to the city. Join your private driver and listen, captivated, as your expert English-speaking guide tells you of the significance of the Acropolis and the Parthenon, one of the most important ancient monuments in the Western world which dates back to 447BC when it was dedicated to the Athena, Goddess of Wisdom.

    Continue your half-day sightseeing (4 hours) back in the main city and enjoy a picturesque stroll through the ancient district of Plaka. One of the most interesting areas of the city, Plaka is scattered with craft shops, cafes and restaurants; the perfect place to relax and soak up the city's atmosphere.

    This afternoon is at leisure to relax or explore Athens independently. You might like to unwind in the well-equipped spa at the hotel, or sip a cocktail at the tranquil pool bar on the rooftop.

    Overnight: Hotel Grande Bretagne
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    ATHENS - CAPE SOUNION - ATHENS

    Your half-day sightseeing takes you outside of Athens along the scenic coast to Cape Sounion and the ancient ruined Temple of Poseidon. The Temple enjoys an impressive position, high on the cliffs, and is believed to date from the 5th century BC. According to Greek mythology it was built by Poseidon, who ranked second only to Athena herself in terms of power. After finding out more about this fascinating ruin, return to Athens and enjoy the rest of the day at leisure.

    Overnight: Hotel Grande Bretagne
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    ATHENS - SANTORINI

    Allow us to privately transfer you to Athens Airport in time for your flight to Santorini. On arrival your local representative is waiting to welcome you to the island and transfer you to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in procedures.

    Overnight: Vedema Resort
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    SANTORINI

    Today's private half-day tour begins with a visit to Fira. Informed by the expert knowledge of your local guide, you can venture to the edge of the caldera for a spectacular view before visiting the Archaeological Museum housing artefacts from Akrotiri, the prehistoric settlement destroyed by the massive erruption of 1600 B.C.

    Continue to the charming village of Oia for a stroll through the narrow streets as your guide tells you about the history of the blue-domed churches and introduces you to a selection of shops, art galleries and taverns.

    Spend the rest of the day at leisure after being privately transferred back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Vedema Resort
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    SANTORINI

    Spend a relaxing day enjoying the exceptional facilities offered by your hotel.

    Overnight: Vedema Resort
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    SANTORINI - MYKONOS

    Privately transfer to Santorini Airport today in time for your flight to Mykonos. Your local representative is ready to meet you on arrival and transfer you to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in.

    Overnight: Santa Marina
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    MYKONOS

    Discover the beauty of Mykonos with its whitewashed houses and lovely beaches during your morning excursion. Wander through the narrow streets in Mykonos Town and visit the fascinating Maritime and Archeological Museums with your private guide. Later travel inland to the intriguing village of Ano Mera to visit the Monastery of the Panayia Tourliani, which was founded by two monks.

    Overnight: Santa Marina
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    MYKONOS - DELOS - MYKONOS

    Take a transfer by public boat to the uninhabited neighbouring island of Delos with your English-speaking guide. Home to many archaeological treasures, Delos was first a place of pilgrimage before becoming a thriving commercial port in the 3rd and 2nd centuries BC. Return back to Mykonos by public boat to enjoy the remainder of the day at leisure.

    Overnight: Santa Marina
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    DEPART MYKONOS

    Privately transfer to Mykonos Airport for your departure flight via Athens.

    Meal Plan: B

TAILOR MADE GREECE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 11 days

    This itinerary is ideal for those interested in history, architecture and enjoy picturesque views.

    Highlights: Athens, Corinth Canal, Nafplion, Mycenae,the Peloponnese, Delphi, Amphissa, Lamia and Trikala, Kalambaka, Dion, Thessaloniki

  • Day 1

    DEPART USA

    Overnight flight

  • Day 2

    ARRIVAL ATHENS

    Upon arrival at Athens airport you will be met by your Abercrombie & Kent 'Guardian Angel' and privately transferred to your hotel. The remainder of the day is at leisure to relax after your journey.

  • Day 3

    FULL DAY ATHENS

    Today you will be met by your private vehicle and English-speaking guide for a full-day sightseeing tour of Athens (8 hours). See the complex of monuments that most symbolises Athens - the Acropolis. The highlight of this morning's tour to the Acropolis will be the Parthenon, one of the most important ancient monuments in the Western world, which was dedicated to the Goddess Athena and conceived in 447BC. After a leisurely lunch, continue sightseeing. Spend time exploring the renowned National Archaeological Museum, one of the richest and most important museums in the world with its formidable collection of unique exhibits including sculpture, pottery and jewellery. Afterwards, you will enjoy a walk through the ancient district of Plaka, one of the most picturesque and interesting parts of the city.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    ATHENS - CORINTH -NAFPLION

    Leaving Athens mid-morning, drive to the Corinth Canal, spectacularly hewn out of the rock and just wide enough for ships to pass. Visit the impressive remains of the acropolis of "wealthy Corinth", testimony to the importance of the town as a trading center in ancient times. Continue to the seaside town of Nafplion.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    NAFPLION - EPIDAVROS - NAFPLION

    This morning is at leisure to enjoy the fine facilities of the hotel, or perhaps a walk through the charming old town of Nafplion with its mixture of ancient Greek and Venetian influences. Visit the Folklore museum, displaying costumes, housewares and much more, from the Middle ages to the 19th century. This afternoon, drive to the nearby archaeological site of Epidaurus to admire the magnificent 4th century theatre whose elegance, perfection of design and harmonious proportions made it the most famous in the Ancient World. Today the Theatre is beautifully preserved and retains the astonishing acoustics that enabled actors to be heard without difficulty by all of the 14,000 spectators. The Theatre was the showpiece of a fine Sanctuary dedicated to Aesculapius, god of medicine. There will be time to enjoy the delightful setting of the site before returning to your hotel.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    NAFPLION - MYCENEA - DELPHI

    A full day's driving today takes you first to Mycenae, the city "rich in gold" as described by Homer, dating from the second millennium BC and which, under the tyrannical rule of its warrior kings, gave birth to the important Mycenaen civilization. Visit the site hewn from a rocky hill and ringed by barren mountains, with its proud Lion Gate and Royal Tombs whose majestic "beehive" funerary chambers were filled with treasures. Drive through the Peloponnese, passing olive groves and citrus plantations, before driving over the sweeping bridge that connects the Peloponnese to the mainland. Check into your hotel late afternoon.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    DELPHI - KALAMBAKA

    This morning you will visit Delphi, dramatically positioned on terraces against the backdrop of Mount Parnassos. Explore this extensive and fascinating archaeological site, visiting such masterpieces as the great Temple of Apollo, home of the famous oracle, the sturdy Temple of the Athenians and the extensive Ancient Theater. Visit also, the archaeological museum, a treasure trove of artifacts and boasting the Charioteer of Delphi, one of the most beautiful Greek statues from the 5th century BC.

    After lunch at your leisure, continue your journey through Amphissa, Lamia and Trikala before reaching Kalambaka.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    METEORA

    Meet your English-speaking guide and vehicle for a full day excursion. Today you will visit the well-known Meteora with its awe inspiring Byzantine Monasteries constructed on top of huge rocks, which seem to be suspended in mid-air. See the Great Meteoron Monastery which was founded in the 14th century (although most of what visitors see today dates from the 16th century). The current structures were built by monks from Mount Athos. Varlaam Monastery can be accessed by a narrow bridge that runs from the main road on the Meteora. Varlaam Monastery's most pleasant features are the garden and the monks who sometimes sit there and chat with visitors. The old refectory is now a museum. Varlaam contains frescoes by the well-known iconographer Frangos Katelanos and houses a collection of relics, carved wooden crosses and icons.

    NB: excursion not recommended for travellers with limited mobility

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    DION - VERGINA - PELLA - THESSALONIKI

    This morning you will visit Dion with your private vehicle & English-speaking guide. Before his campaign against the Persians, Alexander the Great offered a sacrifice here. Continue to Vergina, the ancient capital of Macedonia, to visit the Royal Tombs of Philip II, the father of Alexander the Great, the tomb of the Prince and the golden treasures exhibited there. Arrive in Pella which was at the end of the 5th century B.C the flourishing capital of Alexander the Great's Macedonian kingdom. It is also there that Alexander the Great was born in 356BC

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    THESSALONIKI

    Meet your private vehicle & English-speaking guide for a full day (8hrs) tour of Thessaloniki, capital of northern Greece. Also known as Salonica, Thessaloniki was also the second city of the Byzantine Empire.
