Australian Family Adventure

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 18 days

    Experience the very best of Australia. See iconic Sydney, surf Bondi beach and explore the Blue Mountains. Snorkel the Great Barrier reef, be amazed by native wildlife on Kangaroo Island and learn about Aboriginal culture in the Red Centre.

    Highlights: Tour the Sydney Opera House. Surf on Bondi Beach. Visit the Blue Mountains. Experience the Sydney bridge climb. Learn about aboriginal culture in Queensland. Private touring at Ayers Rock. See spectacular views of Melbourne from the Eureka skydeck.

  • Day 1

    Sydney

    On arrival into Sydney's International Airport you will be met and transferred to your hotel. Your room has been pre-registered for immediate occupancy.

    Sydney, the capital of the state of New South Wales, is Australia's most well known city. With its extraordinary icons both natural and man-made this is a city that never fails to please the eye. It's magnificent natural harbour spanned by the 'coat hanger', the Sydney Harbour Bridge, and the man-made sails of the Sydney Opera House are just some of its most widely photographed views.

    Four Seasons Hotel Sydney Superbly situated right on Sydney Harbour in the city's historic Rocks area, the Four Seasons Sydney Hotel has wonderful views of the Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge. It is only a short walk from Sydney's major shopping and business districts.

    Overnight: Full Harbour View Premier Room - Twin/Double Interconnecting with Full Harbour View Room
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    Sydney

    Meet your guide and commence a full day exploration of Sydney, which will be tailored to suit your interests.

    Today's tour begins at Sydney's historic Rocks area known for its narrow cobbled streets and convict buildings. In the quaint suburb of Paddington see tiny Victorian houses decorated with cast iron "lacework", much of which came to Australia as ballast in the holds of the wool and grain clippers over 100 years ago. Next we visit the Sydney Opera House for a private one hour tour.

    The Sydney Opera House is the busiest performing arts centre in the world. Since it's opening in 1973, it has brought countless hours of entertainment to millions of people and has continued to attract the best in world-class talent year after year. There are nearly 1000 rooms in the Opera House including the five main auditoria. There is also a Reception Hall, five rehearsal studios, four restaurants, six theatre bars, extensive foyer and lounge areas, sixty dressing rooms and suites, library, artists' lounge and canteen known as the "Green Room", administrative offices and extensive plant and machinery areas.

    Next we take the drive to Bondi Beach for a Bondi Beach tour. Discover the home of surf lifesaving and the famous Bondi Beach lifesaver. You will learn about the colourful history of Bondi, how surf lifesaving began, Bondi's shark attacks and the famous Black Sunday. Take an exclusive tour inside Australia's oldest surf lifesaving club and enjoy the opportunity to have your photo taken with a Bondi lifesaver and Surf Rescue board right on Bondi's golden sand.

    Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant with your guide before heading to the bridge climb office to commence the 'climb of your life". Your guide & chauffeur will drop you off at the bridge climb, please make your own way back to the hotel on conclusion.

    Bridge Climb - Twilight climb Bridge Climb, a three-hour adventure - is at 5 Cumberland Street just a short walk from the hotel. Climb the world-renowned Harbour Bridge - one of Sydney's most exciting activities. Built in 1932, it took almost 70 years for the Bridge to open to people with a sense of adventure. Feel the excitement while walking over the catwalk to climb the pylon and then across the arch on the way to the summit - 134 metres above sea level. Marvel at the spectacular 360 degrees views of one of the worlds' great harbour cities.

    Note: For safety reasons please wear sneakers. Climbers will be breathalysed prior to the climb! Cameras are not allowed - official photos will be taken during the climb.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Sydney Full Harbour View Premier Room - Twin/Double Interconnecting with Full Harbour View Room
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Blue Mountains

    Meet your driver / guide in the hotel lobby. Today's tour travels west across the coastal plain to the Blue Mountains to unlock a piece of Australia many don't know exists. Visit outstanding lookouts, see magnificent rock formations and unusual plants and wildlife. Look over the magnificent Jamieson Valley and the Three Sisters. Enjoy some easy bushwalks through the beautiful Australian bush. After a barbeque lunch in a very unique location, drop into a hidden valley to seek out the many kangaroos that graze in the wild (conditions permitting). Return to Sydney at conclusion of the tour.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Sydney Full Harbour View Premier Room - Twin/Double Interconnecting with Full Harbour View Room
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 4

    Sydney - Port Douglas

    Meet the chauffeur in the hotel lobby and transfer by private vehicle to Sydney airport for your onward flight to Cairns (flight cost not included).

    On arrival into Cairns, meet your chauffeur and transfer to Port Douglas. Afternoon at leisure!

    Located on the secluded beachfront of Four Mile Beach, Sea Temple Resort & Spa Port Douglas has been designed with the utmost luxury and sophisticated relaxation in mind. This resort blends seamlessly with the surrounding landscape echoing the natural features of this tropical oasis and centring around an expansive lagoon and island bar. The resort comprises of 194 rooms plus Day Spa, Restaurant, bars and Signature 18 Hole Links style Golf Course in one of the most internationally famous locations in Australia. Sea Temple Port Douglas offers tropical elegance in a stunningly beautiful location, sophisticated and stylish, perfectly positioned for your rejuvenation.

    Overnight: Apartment - 2 bedroom - Swim out with rollaway
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Port Douglas

    Day at leisure.

    Overnight: Apartment - 2 bedroom - Swim out with rollaway
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    Port Douglas/Great Barrier Reef

    Full day tour to the Great Barrier Reef - optional (additional costs apply)

    Experience natures finest with Australia's best! Let us fly you where two world's meet. Experience the exhilaration of flying over the rainforest canopy of the World Heritage Rainforest, then as you see the green change to the vivid blues of the water, you are flying over the 4th Natural Wonder of the World - The Great Barrier Reef. Flying low over the Reef, our pilot will show you the wonders that live beneath the sea. Landing at Agincourt Reef, on the very edge of the Great Barrier Reef, join the Quicksilver vessel and floating platform for a day of activities.

    Quicksilver has facilities for both swimmers and non-swimmers to enjoy this stunning reef world. Explore the reef snorkelling, from a semi submersible, or from the underwater observatory.

    Relax as you return to Port Douglas aboard the wave-piercing catamarans and reflect on the magnificence of nature's finest.

    Included in your day:

    • Spectacular Outer Reef helicopter flight

    • Hot and cold tropical buffet lunch

    • Qualified snorkelling safety officers on hand

    • All snorkelling equipment provided

    • Underwater observatory fish and coral viewing

    • Coral viewing by semi-submersible vessels

    • Accommodation transfers

    Overnight: Sea Temple Resort and Spa Port Douglas Apartment - 2 bedroom - Swim out with rollaway bed
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 7

    Port Douglas - Cooya Beach

    In a well appointed luxury vehicle, traverse the breathtaking Coral Sea coastline en route to magestic Cooya Beach. You will join Yaba Yabaju of the Kuku Yalanji people, the traditional owners of the land, for an interactive cultural experience. Meander through these primordial habitats with your indigenous guide as you are initiated into the ancient stories and history of the area. Gather food; throw spears and experience traditional hunting in the mudflats, mangroves and beach habitats.

    Overnight: Sea Temple Resort and Spa Port Douglas Apartment - 3 bedroom - Swim out
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 8

    Port Douglas

    Day at leisure!

    Overnight: Sea Temple Resort and Spa Port Douglas Apartment - 3 bedroom - Swim out
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Port Douglas - Ayres Rock

    Meet a chauffeur and transfer by private vehicle to Cairns airport for your onward flight to Ayers Rock. (flight cost not included).

    On arrival, you will be met by a private driver/guide who will transfer you to your accommodation. This afternoon, The Kata Tjuta and Dunes tour introduces you to the Central Australian arid desert. Leaving mid-afternoon, our guide will explain the nature of the desert and it's development. A short walk to the dune lookout puts one in touch with your environment and lets you delight in the spectacular panoramic view of Kata Tjuta (the Olgas) - a very sacred place to the Aboriginal people. Arriving at Walpa Gorge (Olga Gorge) we walk into this mystic place, to hear how it has evolved over millions of years. The scale of the walls and the wind far above, add a sense of mystery here, which profoundly affects all that participate. The sky blue deepens and the great domes of Kata Tjuta (the Olgas) flush red as the tour retires to the sunset viewing area to celebrate the fantastic colour changes wrought by the sunset with the complimentary champagne. Sit back and absorb the desert solitude or relax and chat to your guide about Australia's red centre. The spectacle fades as you return to Yulara beneath a sky of brilliant stars.

    Sails in the Desert provides comfort and luxury right in the heart of Central Australia. Relax in spacious, airy and exquisitely furnished rooms, enjoy a cocktail in the award winning park-like grounds, or sit back and relax under the magnificent white sails that gives the hotel its name.

    Overnight: 1 x standard room & 1 x Deluxe suite -Twin/Double Interconnecting
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    Ayres Rock

    This morning you will meet your guide and commence a sunrise tour of Uluru. This tour to Uluru provides the opportunity to witness the spectacular sunrise without rushing, all the while absorbing an exposition of Uluru's natural history and deep significance to the Anangu people. Short walks will bring you close to this wonderful and powerful place, viewing rock art, waterholes and appreciating the unique flora and fauna of Uluru. A special feature is the appreciation of the exquisite as well as the dramatic sculpturing of Uluru. This tour provides time to amble, to absorb images and to gain an insight into Uluru's remarkable presence. The final step is to the Uluru and Kata Tjuta Cultural Centre.

    This afternoon, embark on a Dot Painting Workshop. Join an Aboriginal artist at the Uluru Cultural Centre for a fun introduction to Aboriginal art. An Aboriginal guide will provide an introduction to their culture and how this is expressed in their artworks. You can then complete your own painting which you can take home as a memento of your experience.

    Enjoy the remainder of the afternoon & evening at leisure!

    Overnight: Sails In The Desert 1 x Standard Room -Twin/Double Interconnecting with Deluxe suite
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    Ayres Rock - Kangaroo Island

    Meet your representative in the hotel lobby and transfer to Ayers Rock airport by private vehicle. Board your flight to Adelaide. On arrival into Adelaide, you will transfer to your onward flight to Kangaroo Island (a 30 minute flight), arriving at approximately 6pm. A Southern Ocean Lodge representative will then transfer you to the property.

    Southern Ocean Lodge is Kangaroo Island's only world-class wilderness lodge. Located at Hanson Bay and commanding spectacular ocean, beach and wilderness views, the 21-suite escape offers the ultimate fusion of nature and luxury. Featuring an environmentally sensitive design, eco chic interiors, premium lodge facilities, personal service, contemporary dining and a signature spa retreat, guests can discover the magnificent coastal scenery and abundant wildlife for which the Island is renowned in impeccable style. With exceptional guided experiences and adventures included in the rate, all guests receive a personalised experiential touring itinerary of the Island's unique natural attractions - the complete Kangaroo Island experience!

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge, Ocean family suite with rollaway
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 12

    Kangaroo island

    Day at leisure.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge, Ocean family suite with rollaway
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 13

    Kangaroo Island

    Enjoy a full day exploring Kangaroo Island with your Lodge guide. Sightseeing may include a visit to Seal Bay at sunrise before your breakfast, followed by an introduction to Kangaroo Island's wildlife, maritime heritage and incredible coastal landscape. See the Remarkable Rocks, Admirals Arch and the Cape du Couedic Lighthouse.

    Please note - All touring on Kangaroo Island is scheduled touring. Private touring can be arranged at an additional cost.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge, Ocean family suite with rollaway
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 14

    Kangaroo Island - Melbourne

    This morning you will be transferred to Kingscote airport for your flight to Adelaide (flight included). From here connect with your onward flight to Melbourne (flight not included).

    Melbourne, the capital of the state of Victoria, is known as the Garden, Arts and Sports capital of Australia. Its beautiful tree-lined boulevards, many parks and gardens and beautiful Victorian terrace houses, great restaurants, wonderful theatre and art galleries together with international sporting events all combine to make this elegant city one of the world's most livable.

    On arrival into Melbourne you will be met at the domestic terminal and transferred to your hotel. Enjoy the afternoon & evening at leisure!

    The Park Hyatt Melbourne is the city's most deluxe hotel. It is located at the 'top end' of Collins Street, directly opposite Melbourne's grandest cathedral and a few steps from Parliament House, the central business district and the beautiful Fitzroy Gardens. The luxurious oversized rooms are a haven for relaxation and comfort.

    Club lounge rooms occupy the top floors of the Park Tower and enjoy premium views of the city and surrounding Fitzroy Gardens. Guest in the Club lounge Rooms are invited to utilise our exclusive Park Lounge, located on level 19. Park Lounge offers a private sanctuary with floor-to-ceiling windows and breathtaking views over Melbourne. An open fireplace creates an atmosphere of warmth and provides the perfect setting to enjoy a leisurely glass of wine or a casual coffee in the afternoon.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne, Twin interconnecting with a Park Suite

  • Day 15

    Melbourne

    Full day private tour of Melbourne with guide and vehicle (8 hours). We begin our tour of Melbourne with an Aboriginal Heritage Walk through the Royal Botanic Gardens with a private Aboriginal guide. The gardens rest on a traditional camping and meeting place for the local Aboriginal custodians of the area - the Boonwurrung and Woiworung people and the walk is a rich and vibrant cultural experience. Share in the wealth of local plant lore and see the land through the eyes of your Aboriginal guide.

    Next we take you to the Queen Victoria Market - Melbourne's oldest market bustling with produce, delicatessen and Australian souvenirs. We drive past Federation Square and Melbourne's sporting precinct which includes the M.C.G, Rod Laver Arena and Olympic Park. Driver through the Bayside suburbs including vibrant St Kilda and enjoy lunch with your guide.

    After lunch, we visit the Eureka Skydeck.

    Nothing you have ever experienced will prepare you for the awe inspiring views from the highest viewing platform in the Southern Hemisphere. Located in the CBD adjacent to the Yarra River at Southbank in Melbourne's iconic Eureka Tower, Skydeck will change the way you look at Melbourne and maybe the world. Skydeck 88 with the world's only Edge Experience is an experience above all else. Your tour will conclude at your hotel.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne, Twin interconnecting with a Park Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 16

    Melbourne

    Private Lanes & Arcades - 2 course Lunch - (3-4 hours) 3 people only. This morning you will be met in the hotel lobby for your private half day walking tour of Melbourne. This tour takes in Melbourne's 'hidden secrets' - the fabulous places that only the locals know about. Discover her celebrated arcades & alleyways - home to the caf√© culture, the independent fashion labels, the hippest bars, the finest architecture, the multicultural restaurants, the commissioned street art & the heartbeat of this city. Differing from Sydney, Melbourne needs to be explored & discovered - so join your local guide & see the best of Melbourne. You will conclude your walk with lunch at a local restaurant, with the afternoon at your leisure to relax - or continue the exploration on your own.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne, Twin interconnecting with a Park Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 17

    Melbourne - Great Ocean Road - Melbourne

    Great Ocean Road Tour Full Day private excursion driver/guide. Leaving the city we head over the magnificent Westgate Bridge. This bridge offers awesome views of the Docklands, as well as a panoramic view of the city skyline. We soon arrive at Geelong and take you down to Corio Bay. Corio Bay is a wonderful mix of old wharves, wool stores and a fully restored 1930s sea bathing complex. See wonderful examples of century old housing and the 100 brightly painted bollards dotted around the bay depicting some of Geelong's local historic characters, each having its own story to tell. At Torquay the Great Ocean Road starts.

    Known as the world's most inspiring coastal drive, we follow its winding trail through the lush Otway rainforests, to the breathtaking, windswept drama of the Shipwreck Coast. For much of the way the road clings to the coastline, twisting and turning, every bend revealing a sight more spectacular than the last.

    At Lorne we will visit a lookout perched high on the hillside where you really get to see the Great Ocean Road from a bird like perspective. This will be followed by morning tea where we can possibly view some of the local surfers 'ripping it up.' Another highlight of the day is a stop at Kennett River. In this area, high in the trees is a colony of wild koalas. See these creatures in their natural habitat, hanging from trees and looking after their young, oblivious to their human onlookers.

    A sumptuous lunch is served at Apollo Bay before we make our way to the Shipwreck coast area of the Great Ocean Road; our first stop, The Twelve Apostles. The mighty Twelve Apostles are world-recognised icons of the Great Ocean Road. These giant rock stacks soar from the swirling waters of the Southern Ocean and are a central feature of the spectacular Port Campbell National Park. The dramatic and imposing limestone cliffs that are the backdrop to the Apostles tower up to 70 metres, while the tallest of the rock stacks is around 45 metres high. Venturing further west we arrive at Loch Ard Gorge. Your first view will be of towering cliffs, sparkling blue-green sea and a small, sandy beach. It's hard to imagine that the drama of one of Victoria's most tragic shipwrecks was played out at this very spot more than 120 years ago, giving its name to the gorge. Our guide will tell you the story and have you totally engrossed and mesmerised. Time permitting we get to see another one of the amazing rock formations that dot this coastline before heading inland through dairy farm country on the way back to the city.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne, Twin interconnecting with a Park Suite Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 18

    Melbourne - Depart

    Meet your Abercrombie & Kent chauffeur and transfer to the airport. Please note: Whenever breakfast is included it is in the hotel/lodge restaurant. Should you choose to have breakfast in your room there may be an additional charge to your own account.

    Meal Plan: B

Hayman Island and Descarada Relaxation

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 5 days

    This itinerary takes you to the beautiful Hayman Island and cruises in the stunning Whitsundays, Queensland Australia.

    Highlights: Hayman Island, Whitsundays

  • Day 1

    Arrive Whitsunday Island, Board Descarada

    Arrive at Hamilton Island Airport and transfer by luxury launch to nearby Hayman Island.

    Depending on your flight arrival you may like to spend some time enjoying the Hamilton Island cafes, pools and restaurants.

    Later this afternoon (approximately 5pm) you will be met by DescaradA for your two night cruise. Other clients aboard DescaradA will have already boarded in Airlie Beach.

    DescaradA Charters set new standards in luxury cruising, accommodating a maximum of eight guests in four private cabins.

    The beautifully appointed 70ft motor yacht is spread across three levels of spacious living, offering the perfect facilities for leisurely holiday making. DescaradA comes fully equipped with jet skis, kayaks, nine-seater rigid inflatable tender and a minimum crew of three to see to the guests' every need. For private charters, scuba diving can be arranged on request with tank fill station and scuba gear ready onboard.

    Spend the evening meeting your fellow guests and over sunset drinks before dining on a fresh gourmet dinner prepared by your crew.

    Overnight: DescaradA, Double Ensuite Cabin
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    Aboard Descarada

    After a delicious breakfast it's time to see the islands, beaches and inlets of the Whitsundays. Opportunities to fish, snorkel, jet-ski or simply laze in the sun are yours today.

    Throughout the day the crew take care of all meals and customize the days activities to suit your needs and the conditions on the day.

    Overnight: DescaradA, Double Ensuite Cabin
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Arrive Hayman Island

    After another morning exploring the islands, beaches and waterways, DescaradA will set you down at Hayman Island Marina (approximately 3pm), where you will be met by Hayman representatives and transferred to the resort for check in.

    Multi-award winning Hayman Island is situated close to the Great Barrier Reef in the famous crystal clear waters of the Whitsunday Passage. The classic architecture is closely integrated with the superb natural beauty of its surroundings. Majestic palms, lush tropical plants and exotic blooms fill the gardens and walking tracks meander through the natural bush land, home to many colourful birds and wildlife. Enjoy a variety of cuisine at La Trattoria, Oriental or Azure or picnic on secluded beaches or candlelight dinners on the balcony (surcharge applies for in room dining).

    Your Pool or Lagoon Suite accommodation is inclusive of daily mini-bar.

    Pool Suites
    On Levels 1, 2 and 3 of the Pool Wing, overlooking the famous Hayman Pool and tropical gardens. Suites on Level 3 also offer views of the Hayman Beach and Coral Sea. Each suite has a separate bedroom, dressing room and lounge/dining room. Special features include a double balcony and large marble bathroom with separate shower and bath. The suite area is 75 square metres. An adjoining twin bedroom can be added to selected suites to create a 2-bedroom suite. Suite guests receive the personalised concierge service.

    Lagoon Suites
    On Levels 1, 2 and 3 of the Lagoon Wing, overlooking the Lily Lagoon, Lagoon Pool and tropical gardens. Suites on Level 3 also offer views of Hayman Beach and the Coral Sea. Each of these luxurious suites has a separate bedroom, dressing room area, guest bathroom and lounge room. D√©cor reflects a relaxed resort feel, featuring soft, natural textures, cool marbles and rich, warm woods. A large balcony is furnished with an outdoor teakwood setting. Special features include a double balcony, spacious living/dining room, walk-in robe and open plan bathroom with centrepiece bath and double vanity. The suite area is 90 square metres. An adjoining twin bedroom can be added to selected suites to create a 2-bedroom suite. Suite guests receive the personalised concierge service.

    Overnight: Hayman Island, Pool or Lagoon Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Hayman Island

    Enjoy the full day at leisure on Hayman Island. Optional activities include seaplane trips to the Reef, scuba diving, reef fishing, sailing, tidal golf on the beach, tennis, or swimming in one of the three resort pools.

    Overnight: Hayman Island, Pool or Lagoon Suite
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 5

    Depart Hayman Island

    This morning check out of your accommodation and transfer by luxury launch from Hayman Island to Hamilton Island for your departing flight or onward arrangements.

    Meal Plan: B

An Australian Adventure

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 16 days

    A 16 day itinerary that explores the very best of Victoria and New South Wales in luxury

    Highlights: Melbourne, Daylesford, Port Fairy, Robe, Kangaroo Island, Barossa Valley, Flinders Ranges, Adelaide, Sydney

  • Day 1

    Arrive Melbourne

    On arrival into Melbourne you will be met by your guide who will welcome you to Australia & escort you to your chauffeur driven vehicle.

    From here you will transfer by private vehicle to rural Victoria. Today is a real treat - an exclusive experience - where you are welcomed into the home of the Barber family, to learn a little of what it is like to be an Aussie family 'living off the land'. This is a genuine operating sheep property which has been in the Barber Family name since 1917. The magnificent 6500 acre property is located approx 90 minutes from Melbourne. Meet Duncan Barber a 4th generation farmer of this land and enjoy an exclusive visit to his sheep farm.

    On arrival at the property you are treated to a delicious home-baked morning tea in the delightful surroundings of the Homestead, built in 1859. The history of the property and the passion the family have for it will become apparent as you enjoy your time getting to know your hosts Duncan, his wife Jess and Duncan's mother Rita.

    After morning tea, it is time for you to watch as Duncan shears a sheep. Within the confines of this historic woolshed built in 1944, you will learn more about the wool industry and the rich 150 year history which can be traced back to the Elector of Saxony. The finer details of preparing and classing a fleece and the process a fleece goes through will be detailed, and this beautiful product that ends up as fabric made into Italy's finest men's suits (Zegna) is sure to impress and enthrall. This is also a chance to watch Duncan work his Australian Sheep Dogs & to plant an Australian Native Tree to commemorate your visit to the property before returning to the homestead for a three course lunch with the Barber family. Your lunch will include Australian wine, beer and water.

    To conclude your day, Duncan will take you for a drive around the property to spot kangaroos in their natural environment before you re-join the vehicle and journey back to Melbourne. You will depart Coliban Park in the late afternoon with a sense of having broken through the tourist crust & seen the real Australia.

    Journey one hour to the township of Daylesford - home to the Lake House.

    Overnight: Lake House Daylesford
    Meal Plan: L

  • Day 2

    Daylesford

    After a big day yesterday, today is at your leisure at Lake House. Lake House is noted in the Conde Nast Traveller as one of the top hotels in the world. There are 33 rooms and suites - 24 with picturesque waterfront views and 9 in a garden setting. It was named Australia's Best Boutique Hotel in the Luxury Travel Gold List.

    The Salus Day Spa was voted Australia's Best Spa in the GT Travel Awards and is an oasis of tranquillity offering guests an indulgent way to "take to the waters" with all manner of hydrotherapy on offer. Two tree house cabins overlooking Lake Daylesford and house hot mineral water tubs - perfect for a pre -pamper soak.

    Since 1984 Lake House has gathered an impressive array of awards - most recently Country Restaurant of the Year in The Age Good Food Guide. For lovers of good living - Lake House is heaven. Relax and enjoy your spectacular surrounds.

    Overnight: Lake House Daylesford
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Daylesford - Melbourne

    After breakfast this morning meet your representative for a full day exploration of the Daylesford & Hepburn Springs region.

    Witness a charming piece of Europe at the Lavandula Swiss-Italian farm. Discover an oasis of tranquility, surrounded by farmland and rolling hills, constructed by Italians who stayed after the gold rushes. Take a walk around the stone farmhouse buildings and cobbled courtyard, surrounded by a stunning garden, lavender fields, a small creek and distant hills. A fine morning tea awaits with seasonal produce from the garden. Play petanque on the lawn, feel the grass between your toes. Relax.

    Explore Daylesford's Convent Gallery bursting with fascinating history. Set upon a hill overlooking the town centre you'll witness stunning exhibitions of local fashion jewellery, sculpture and fine art.

    Cliffy's (the local produce store) overflows with character. Colourful Persian rugs adorn and the menus are written on paper bags. They even grow their own veggies out the back. Enjoy mouth watering regional delights.

    In the mood for a little pampering? Since 1895, the Hepburn Bathhouse has been a 'must-do' when visiting Australia's premier spa destination. Relax and rejuvenate. Choosing from a range of relaxing pleasures such as facials, massages and indulgent mineral bathing with rejuvenating spa product.

    You'll be filled with fond memories for the leisurely drive back to Melbourne.

    The Park Hyatt Melbourne is the city's most deluxe hotel. It is located at the 'top end' of Collins Street, directly opposite Melbourne's grandest cathedral and a few steps from Parliament House, the central business district and the beautiful Fitzroy Gardens. The luxurious oversized rooms are a haven for relaxation and comfort.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 4

    Melbourne - Port Fairy

    This morning you will be met in the hotel lobby by historian and raconteur Kenneth Park for a private look at elegant Melbourne. This 1.5 walking tour takes in Melbourne's 'hidden secrets' - the fabulous places that only the locals know about. Discover her celebrated arcades & alleyways - home to the caf√© culture, the independent fashion labels, the hippest bars, the finest architecture, the multicultural restaurants, the commissioned street art & the heartbeat of this city. Differing from Sydney, Melbourne needs to be explored & discovered - so join your local guide & see the best of Melbourne.

    Returning to your hotel to check out, collect your vehicle & begin your self-drive adventure along the Great Ocean Road!

    Take the four hour journey along this spectacular coast road - passing Lorne, Apollo Bay & the 12 Apostles, affording you hundreds of wonderful photo opportunities along the way. MELBOURNE NOW

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 Nights

    "Experience the vibrancy and cultural diversity of Melbourne, recognized as one of the world's most livable cities" An adventure awaits you down every street, lane and alleyway with diverse cuisines, the arts, fashion precincts and history scattered throughout the city. Enjoy a change of pace on the city's fringe with serene valleys chequered with award winning wineries, spectacular coastlines and picturesque landscapes with unique Australian wildlife.

    Highlights: Melbourne, Phillip Island , Great Ocean Road.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Melbourne

    • Exciting Hummer ride from the airport
    • Visit iconic St Kilda Beach

    Experience the vibrant, cosmopolitan city of Melbourne.

    Upon arrival, meet the A&K guide and start your adventure with an exciting Hummer transfer to the hotel. Your driver will take you via the vibrant beach side suburb of St Kilda for a quick beach side stroll and photo stop before arriving at your hotel to check in and refresh.

    Board the iconic glossy, burgundy restaurants on wheels - the first travelling Tramcar Restaurants in the world. This is a charming way to orientate yourself with Melbourne's streets and take in the sights while enjoying a delightful 3 course luncheon with beverages (1pm - 3pm).

    This evening, stand at the top of the city at the Eureka Skydeck - the Southern Hemisphere's highest viewing platform. Eureka is perfectly positioned for 360 degree, floor-to-ceiling views encompassing Melbourne's CBD, sports precinct, Port Phillip Bay, Docklands and everything in between. See the sun set and the city lights begin to dazzle.

    This evening perhaps take the family to one of the many fabulous restaurants followed by a theatre show.
    • Chitty Chitty Bang Bang - 30th January - 24th February
    • War Horse 23 Dec - 10 Feb 2013

    Phillip Island Full day tour

    • Helicopter flight to Phillip Island
    • Phillip Island Nature Park
    • Koala Conservation Centre

    Victoria's iconic wildlife island is just a short helicopter flight from Melbourne - this is the most incredible start to your Phillip Island adventure.

    Your luxury vehicle will collect you from your hotel and whisk you across the city to the airfield where your private helicopter awaits.

    Enjoy a spectacular flight over the city, along Cape Woolamai and down to Phillip Island, where your host is waiting to collect you for your exciting Phillip Island adventure.

  • Day 3

    Great Ocean Road 2 day/1 night journey

    • Victoria's iconic surf Beach (Bells Beach) - option for surf or paddle board lesson
    • Otway Fly Treetop Walk

    Victoria's Great Ocean Road is an amazing experience at any time of the year.

    One of the most beautiful coastal roads in the world, the Great Ocean Road stretches 550 km along the southern coast of Victoria.

    Travel it and you will discover some of the world's most inspiring scenery, an ever-changing hinterland and charming towns and hamlets. Marvel at the huge cliffs, roaring seas, tranquil coves, magnificent waterfalls and secluded beaches.

    Get an insight into Australian surfing culture at Bells Beach, there will be time for a surf, kayak or stand up paddle board lesson if you are feeling active! Discover seaside villages such as the popular town of Lorne, where you can stroll on the stunning beach. See koalas in the wild at Kennett River.

    Visit the Otway Fly Treetop Walk is a 1 hour rainforest walk experience that is approximately 2km in total and features a 600mt long and 30mt high steel structured treetop canopy walkway that takes you right into the treetops. It's the longest and tallest walkway of its type in the world! Included in this walk is the kids Prehistoric Path - where there is a dinosaur around every corner! There is also the exhilarating Otway Fly Zip Line Tour is a 3.5 hour experience and includes 2.5 hours of Zip Line activity (including training and simulation), 8 cloud stations, 6 flights and 2 suspension bridges and 1 hour for the Treetop Walk. This Zip Line Tour is the only Zip Line Tour for Victoria!

    This evening overnight in Apollo Bay with dinner at local restaurant La Bimba.

    Great Ocean Road to Melbourne

    • 12 Apostles
    • Moonlight Cinema at Royal Botanic Gardens with picnic hamper

    View the sculptured beauty of The Twelve Apostles today. Many of these stacks of flat-bedded limestone raise 65 metres above sea level and are an inspiring sight to see. Visit Loch Ard Gorge and hear stories from your host about the tragic and romantic shipwreck whilst marveling at the landmark. Visit the quaint seaside village of Port Campbell with sandy beaches and picturesque cliff tops before your journey home to Melbourne. You may like to do a scenic flight to view the incredible stretch of coastline from the air or re-board the vehicle and travel through farmland back to Melbourne.

    This evening, visit the Royal Botanic Gardens - Moonlight Cinema for an evening under the stars at an outdoor cinema screening new release, classic and cult movies. We will arrange a picnic and a blanket for you to enjoy your last evening in Melbourne.
