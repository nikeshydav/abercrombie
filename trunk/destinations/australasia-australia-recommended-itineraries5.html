The Romance of Australia

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 17 days

    17 days Australia

    Melbourne, Daylesford, Coliban Park, Adelaide, Kangaroo Island, Barossa Valley, Cairns, Lizard Island, Sydney, Blue Mountains

  • Day 1

    Arrive Melbourne

    On arrival into Melbourne you will be met by a guide and chauffeur and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel. Your accommodation is available for immediate occupancy.

    Melbourne, the capital of the state of Victoria, is known as the Garden, Arts and Sports capital of Australia. Its beautiful tree-lined boulevards, many parks and gardens and beautiful Victorian terrace houses, great restaurants, wonderful theatre and art galleries together with international sporting events all combine to make this elegant city one of the world's most liveable.

    The remainder of the day is at your leisure in Melbourne.

    Sofitel Melbourne is located at the Paris End of Collins Street offering rooms with stunning views from levels 36 to 50, contemporary furnishings, marble bathrooms and state of the art technology. The 35th Floor provides contemporary dining in Cafe La and cocktails in the Atrium Bar. Sumptuous high teas are available in Sofi's on Level 1 and the new executive lounge on Level 35, Club Sofitel, offers private check in, evening drinks and canapés.

    Overnight: Sofitel Melbourne
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    Melbourne

    This morning meet your private driver and guide in the hotel lobby and commence your full day touring of Melbourne!

    Experience Melbourne's hidden treasures with a guided walk through the beautiful Lanes and Arcades of the city, whilst listening to tales of historical interest and marvelling at its stunning architecture. Discover local designers and specialty retailers exclusive to Marvellous Melbourne!!

    This afternoon enjoy lunch at a local restaurant.

    Commence an afternoon tour of Melbourne. Visit Cooks Cottage before taking a unique walk through the Royal Botanic Gardens laid out over 150 years ago and reputed to be the finest in the Southern Hemisphere. Delight in the over 10,000 species and 50,000 individual plants in the 38 hectare gardens, including trees and plants of great cultural value. Many of these plants are irreplaceable, endangered or even extinct in the wild and have become a natural sanctuary for native wild life including black swans, bell birds, cockatoos and kookaburras that fill the air with their distinctive song.

    Nothing you have ever experienced will prepare you for the awe inspiring views from the highest viewing platform in the Southern Hemisphere. Located in the CBD adjacent to the Yarra River at Southbank in Melbourne's iconic Eureka Tower, Skydeck will change the way you look at Melbourne and maybe the world. Skydeck 88 with the world's only Edge Experience is an experience above all else.

    Travel past Victorian terrace houses, St Kilda Beach, the city's exclusive suburbs and the Arts precinct returning to the hotel at the 'Paris End' of the city.

    Overnight: Sofitel Melbourne
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Melbourne - Coliban Park - Daylesford

    This morning meet your driver & journey into the countryside travelling to one of Victoria's oldest and most delightful country properties - Coliban Park. At this private sheep station, watch sheep being shorn and learn first hand about one of Australia's most important industries. Enjoy lunch with the Barber family in their traditional family homestead. A highlight will be a tour of the property, perhaps seeing kangaroos in the wild.

    Your tour will conclude at your overnight accommodation.

    Lake House is nestled on the shores of idyllic lake Daylesford in the heart of Victoria's Spa Country. An eclectic mix of artists studios, cafes, award winning restaurants, boutique day spas and holistic healing centres - Spa Country exudes a sophisticated bohemian feel.

    Popular haunt of seasoned travellers, Lake House boasts one of Australia's top restaurants, a New York Wine Spectator award winning wine list of some 10,000 bottles. The menu offers modern Australian cuisine paying homage to the region's excellent seasonal produce.

    Lake House is noted in the Conde Nast Traveller as one of the top hotels in the world. There are 33 rooms and suites - 24 with picturesque waterfront views and 9 in a garden setting. It was names Australia's Best Boutique Hotel in the Luxury Travel Gold List.

    The Salus Day Spa was voted Australia's Best Spa in the GT Travel Awards and is an oasis of tranquillity offering guests an indulgent way to "take to the waters" with all manner of hydrotherapy on offer. Two tree house cabins overlooking lake Daylesford and house hot mineral water tubs - perfect for a pre-pamper soak.

    Since 1984 Lake House has gathered an impressive array of awards - most recently Country Restaurant of the Year in The Age Good Food Guide. For lovers of good living - Lake House is heaven.

    Overnight: The Lake House
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 4

    Daylesford - Melbourne - Adelaide - Kangaroo Island

    Today enjoy a tour of the Daylesford region enroute to Melbourne airport. Witness a charming piece of Europe at the Lavandula Swiss-Italian farm. Discover an oasis of tranquility, surrounded by farmland and rolling hills. Constructed by Italians who stayed after the gold rushes. Take a walk around the stone farmhouse buildings and cobbled courtyard, surrounded by a stunning garden, lavender fields, a small creek and distant hills. A fine morning tea awaits with seasonal produce from the garden. Play petanque on the lawn, feel the grass between your toes. Relax.

    Explore Daylesford's Convent Gallery bursting with fascinating history. Set upon a hill over looking the town centre you'll witness stunning exhibitions of local fashion jewellery, sculpture and fine art.

    Cliffy's (the local produce store) overflows with character. Colorful Persian rugs adorn and the menus are written on paper bags. They even grow their own veggies out the back. Enjoy mouth watering regional delights.

    In the mood for a little pre-lunch pampering? Since 1895, the Hepburn Bathhouse has been a 'must-do' when visiting Australia's premier spa destination. Relax and rejuvenate. Choosing from a range of relaxing pleasures such as facials, massages and indulgent mineral bathing with rejuvenating spa product.

    You'll be filled with fond memories for the leisurely drive back to Melbourne. Your tour will conclude at Melbourne Airport.

    Fly Melbourne to Kingscote (via Adelaide).

    On arrival into Kingscote, meet the Southern Ocean Lodge representative and transfer to your accommodation.

    Southern Ocean Lodge aims to provide an iconic accommodation experience on Kangaroo Island and is designed to be a sanctuary of comfort, style and personal intimacy, redefining the Australian standard.

    Located at Hanson bay on the Island's south west coast, the Lodge site adjoins both the Flinders Chase and Cape Bouguer / Kelly Hill National Parks. Set back from a cliff above the pounding surf the sensitive design showcases a full frontal panorama and is within easy reach of all of Kangaroo Island's major attractions. Modern luxury and design integrity meld with Kangaroo island vernacular and an amazing coastal colour palette, creating a unique eco chic style.

    Its location, stunning views, contemporary environmental design, locally sourced food and beverages, personalised interpretative activities and modern wellness spa ensures its status as a South Australian icon.

    All lodge activities, food and beverages are included during your stay at Southern Ocean Lodge.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Kangaroo Island

    Widely regarded as Australia's Galapagos and brimming with diverse wildlife and natural beauty, Kangaroo Island is a place of extraordinary contrasts. With a rich heritage demanding rediscovery and a world-class food and wine culture, the Island offers endless opportunities for exploration and adventure.

    Southern Ocean Lodge offers guests a complete Kangaroo Island experience. With exceptional guided adventures and experiences included, all guests will receive a personalised experiential touring itinerary of the Island's unique natural attractions.

    The Lodge activity programme allows guests to experience Kangaroo Island in a stylish and relaxed manner - there is no need to spend hours travelling between attractions quite simply because the lodge is located at the heart if the Island's major attractions. In order to ensure an intimate, private and personal experience, tours will operate with no more than 10 guests per group, in customised four-wheel drive vehicles with private naturalist guides. Visits to natural attractions will be carefully timed at visitor quiet times to maintain a private safari feel.

    Activities include watching the sea-lions at sunrise with a morning coffee, fresh fruit or danish before arriving back at the lodge in time for a gourmet breakfast, Kangaroo Island wildlife tours and drives, sunset sundowners and canapés, beach fishing, clifftop nature walks and koala spotting/stargazing night walks amongst other activities.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Kangaroo Island - Adelaide - Barossa Valley

    This morning depart Kangaroo Island for Adelaide.

    On arrival into Adelaide, meet your private driver/guide for your full day Barossa Valley tour.

    The Barossa Valley is one of Australia's most celebrated wine growing regions. Just a one and a half hour drive north of Adelaide. Drive through lovely countryside originally settled by English and German pioneers in the mid 19th century. Taste the excellent local wines and enjoy a relaxing lunch at a local restaurant. The tour includes a VIP private tasting at Peter Lehmann Wines. A selection of Super Premium and Cellar Door Exclusive wines are showcased in an intimate private room which is accompanied with a taste plate of fresh regional produce for you to indulge on during your tasting.

    Enjoy a "Make Your Own Blend" Tour - the first of its kind in the Barossa Valley. An interactive and fun wine experience for anyone who has wanted to be a winemaker for a day! After a tour of the winery and cellars, you will be invited to the Winemakers Laboratory to try your hand at making your own wine from Genache, Shiraz and Mourvedre, just like Penfold's own Bin 138. It's a fascinating and satisfying experience, blending to suit your own personal taste. Take your wine home in a personalised bottle!

    Your tour will conclude at your Barossa Valley accommodation.

    Set atop a gentle hill, The Louise Barossa Valley is a luxury 5 star vineyard retreat surrounded by hundreds of acres of vines overlooking the world renowned Barossa Valley. Just fifteen suites offer absolute luxury and inspired design with private terraces, soft contemporary furnishings and spacious ensuites.

    The acclaimed on-site restaurant at The Louise is Appellation. Offering seasonal cuisine nightly, for casually elegant dining, under the guidance of Executive Chef Mark McNamara. Appellation's extensive wine list presents the best of the Barossa Valley, the rest of Australia and iconic wines of the world. The Louise Barossa Valley, is Australia's premier vineyard retreat combined with a world class destination dining experience.

    Overnight: The Louise
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 7

    Barossa Valley - Adelaide - Cairns - Lizard Island

    This morning meet your chauffeur in the resort lobby and transfer by private vehicle to Adelaide airport.

    Fly Adelaide to Cairns.

    On arrival into Cairns, meet your chauffeur and transfer by private vehicle to the Cairns General Aviation Airport.

    Depart Cairns for Lizard Island onboard your private fixed wing flight.

    Imagine an island so pristine, so untouched, that when you walk one of the twenty-four secluded beaches there are no other footprints. Lizard Island is the most northerly island resort located directly on the Great Barrier Reef.

    Recently voted one of the top ten island resorts in the world it is renowned for spectacular diving and fishing, particularly in marlin season. Choose from a wide choice of activities or simply take a break and enjoy the peace and tranquillity of this natural environment. Ospreys' restaurant is the ideal setting for tropical delights and local seafood. Optional water sports include catamaran sailing and scuba diving the world famous "Cod Hole".

    Overnight: Lizard Island
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 8

    Lizard Island

    The next four days are spent at this beautiful multi-award-winning island resort. It is the most northerly resort on the World Heritage listed Great Barrier Reef and was discovered by Captain Cook in 1770 and named after the monitor lizards that inhabit the island. Fine food, 24 private beaches, outstanding snorkelling and world renowned scuba diving at the Cod Hole, tennis, great walks, sailing, water skiing, wind surfing are just some of the options available. It is also renowned for its big game fishing available from September - November. Of course there is always just 'relaxing under a palm tree'.

    A highlight of your time on Lizard Island will be the visit to the Lizard Island Research Station.

    Lizard Island Research Centre attracts marine scientists from around the globe in their quest to conserve and manage the world's delicate coral reef systems. The environmental source to some 800 research publications, the Research Station is the only field station operated by the Australian Museum and is internationally recognised as a leading marine research facility.

    Led by either Lyle or Ann, one of the Directors of the Island's Research Station, watch Australian and international marine scientists at work and have a first hand look at a range of marine research projects, many of which utilise the state of the art seawater aquarium system enabling controlled experiments with living reef organisms.

    This first ever behind the scenes tour is an exclusive.

    Overnight: Lizard Island
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

    Lizard Island

    Overnight: Lizard Island
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

    Lizard Island

    Overnight: Lizard Island

    Lizard Island

    Overnight: Lizard Island

  • Day 12

    Lizard Island - Cairns - Sydney

    This morning an Island representative will escort you to the airstrip for your onward flight to Cairns.

    Depart Lizard Island for Cairns onboard your private fixed wing flight. On arrival into Cairns General Aviation Airport you will be transferred by private vehicle to the Cairns Domestic Airport. Depart Cairns for Sydney.

    On arrival into Sydney, meet your guide and transfer by private vehicle to your accommodation.

    Sydney, the capital of the state of New South Wales, is Australia's most well known city. With its extraordinary icons both natural and man-made this is a city that never fails to please the eye. It's magnificent natural harbour spanned by the 'coat hanger', the Sydney Harbour Bridge, and the man-made sails of the Sydney Opera House are just some of its most widely photographed views.

    Superbly situated right on Sydney Harbour in the city's historic Rocks area, the Four Seasons Sydney Hotel has wonderful views of the Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge. It is only a short walk from Sydney's major shopping and business districts.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Sydney
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 13

    Sydney

    Meet your guide and chauffeur in the lobby. Today's tour of Sydney begins with a visit to Taronga Zoo.

    Enjoy a private 'behind the scenes' visit to Taronga Zoo with your own expert senior Zoo Keeper. This special tour has been designed to encourage interaction between visitors and Australian animals. Enjoy a close-up introduction to Australia's unusual wildlife including excellent photographic opportunities with koalas, kangaroos and echidnas. Spend time in the Zoo';s kitchen preparing lunch for its animals before venturing into the Nightlife House to enjoy close encounters with Australia's unique nocturnal creatures. On completion of the tour morning tea will be served at Treetops Café with your Zoo Keeper.

    Continue on for lunch at one of Sydney's finest local restaurants. After lunch, visit the world-famous Bondi Beach, Paddington and the Rocks region of Sydney.

    Visit the Sydney Opera House for a private 'behind the scenes' tour. Situated on the foreshore of Sydney Harbour; the Sydney Opera House is one of the world's most recognisable buildings. Your tour will conclude at your accommodation.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Sydney
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 14

    Sydney

    Enjoy a full day at leisure in Sydney.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Sydney
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 15

    Sydney - Blue Mountains

    Meet your private driver / guide in the hotel lobby. Today's tour travels west across the coastal plain to the Blue Mountains to unlock a piece of Australia many don't know exists. Visit outstanding lookouts, see magnificent rock formations and unusual plants and wildlife. Look over the magnificent Jamieson Valley and the Three Sisters. After a barbeque lunch drop into a hidden valley to seek out the many kangaroos that graze in the wild (conditions permitting). Your tour will conclude at your Blue Mountains accommodation.

    Wolgan Valley Resort & Spa is a luxury conservation based resort in the Blue Mountains. As Emirates Hotels & Resorts' first hospitality development outside Dubai, it has been built on the same principles as Emirates' acclaimed Al Maha Desert Resort & Spa.

    It is located in the Wolgan Valley of the Greater Blue Mountains World Heritage area, less than three hours drive from Sydney, where 4,000 acres of land has been incorporated into a conservancy reserve. Located between two national parks and spread out at the foot of towering sandstone cliffs, the resort is truly integrated into its environment.

    Overnight: Wolgan Valley Resort
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 16

    Blue Mountains

    Enjoy a full day at leisure at Wolgan Valley Resort to partake in included scheduled activities.

    The resort is private to Wolgan guests, providing them exclusivity on all activities. Each activity has been developed with the belief that great life experiences are often personal, individual, and in some way emotionally moving.

    Today, life's luxuries are about having time, time to experience things alone or together with those who mean the most to us.

    Activities are offered at various skill and fitness levels as well as personal interests, and are suitable for older guests, families, and highly active, more adventurous visitors. From enjoying a lunch in absolute solitude alongside a forest stream; to a late-night BBQ under the stars; or a social sundowner with friends after an afternoon four-wheel drive excursion, the Wolgan Valley experience is likely to remain alive in memories long after.

    While staying at the resort, guests are free to do as much or as little as they like.

    Overnight: Wolgan Valley Resort
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 17

    Blue Mountains - Depart Sydney

    A leisurely start to the day, meet your private chauffer for your transfer to Sydney International Airport. You will be supplied with a picnic lunch for your transfer.

    On arrival into Sydney, check-in for your flight home.

    Meal Plan: B,L

The best of Australia - A platinum programme

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 16 days

    16 days - Platinum programme The Best of Australia

    Highlights: Melbourne, Adelaide, Kangaroo Island, Alice Springs, Ayers Rock, Cairns, Mossman, Lizard Island, Cape Tribulation, Sydney

  • Day 1

    Arrive Melbourne

    On arrival into Melbourne and after clearing customs, you will be met by a guide and transferred by private vehicle to your accommodation. Your room will have been pre-registered for immediate occupancy.

    Melbourne, the capital of the state of Victoria, is known as the Garden, Arts and Sports capital of Australia. It's beautiful tree-lined boulevards, many parks and gardens and beautiful Victorian terrace houses, great restaurants, wonderful theatre and art galleries together with international sporting events all combine to make this elegant city one of the world's most liveable.

    After some time to relax & refresh, meet your local guide in the hotel lobby for an afternoon walking tour that will introduce you to the hidden secrets of Melbourne. Join historian and raconteur Kenneth Park for a private look at elegant Melbourne. Experience the city's labyrinth of atmospheric arcades and alleyways - a wonderful way to learn Melbourne's architectural and cultural heritage. If you have a special interest Kenneth will tailor-make his walk just for you! (1.5hr walking tour).

    Park Hyatt Melbourne

    The Park Hyatt Melbourne is the city's most deluxe hotel. It is located at the 'top end' of Collins Street, directly opposite Melbourne's grandest cathedral and a few steps from Parliament House, the central business district and the beautiful Fitzroy Gardens. The luxurious oversized rooms are a haven for relaxation and comfort.

    Club lounge rooms occupy the top floors of the Park Tower and enjoy premium views of the city and surrounding Fitzroy Gardens. Guest in the Club lounge Rooms are invited to utilise our exclusive Park Lounge, located on level 19. Park Lounge offers a private sanctuary with floor-to-ceiling windows and breathtaking views over Melbourne. An open fireplace creates an atmosphere of warmth and provides the perfect setting to enjoy a leisurely glass of wine or a casual coffee in the afternoon. Park lounge offers a full buffet breakfast (served in Trilogy Bistro), evening drinks with canapés, and all day service of tea and coffee.

    For dinner this evening we recommend a restaurant reservation at The Press Club

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    Melbourne

    After breakfast this morning you will be met by your driver and transfer by private vehicle an hour & a half out of the city to rural Victoria. Today is a real treat - an exclusive experience - where you are welcomed into the home of the Barber family, to learn a little of what it is like to be an Aussie family 'living off the land'. This is a genuine operating sheep property which has been in the Barber Family name since 1917. The magnificent 6500 acre property is located approx 90 minutes from Melbourne. Meet Duncan Barber a 4th generation farmer of this land and enjoy an exclusive visit to his sheep farm.

    On arrival at the property you are treated to a delicious home-baked morning tea in the delightful surroundings of the Homestead, built in 1859. The history of the property and the passion the family have for it will become apparent as you enjoy your time getting to know your hosts Duncan, his wife Jess and Duncan's mother Rita.

    After morning tea, it is time for you to watch as Duncan shears a sheep. Within the confines of this historic woolshed built in 1944, you will learn more about the wool industry and the rich 150 year history which can be traced back to the Elector of Saxony. The finer details of preparing and classing a fleece and the process a fleece goes through will be detailed, and this beautiful product that ends up as fabric made into Italy's finest men's suits (Zegna) is sure to impress and enthrall. This is also a chance to watch Duncan work his Australian Sheep Dogs & to plant an Australian Native Tree to commemorate your visit to the property before returning to the homestead for a three course lunch with the Barber family. Your lunch will include Australian wine, beer and water.

    To conclude your day, Duncan will take you for a drive around the property to spot kangaroos in their natural environment before you re-join the vehicle and journey back to Melbourne. You will return to the city in the late afternoon with a sense of having broken through the tourist crust & seen the real Australia

    For dinner this evening we recommend a restaurant reservation at Gingerboy.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Melbourne - Adelaide - Kangaroo Island

    This morning meet your driver in the hotel lobby for your private vehicle transfer to the domestic airport for your flight to Adelaide. On arrival into Adelaide you will be met by your guide who will escort you to the Regional Air Express check in counter for your flight to Kangaroo Island. Your guide will also assist with any luggage storage you may choose to do at this time.

    IMPORTANT NOTE: for the journey to Kangaroo Island there is a luggage limit of one suitcase of checked luggage, (maximum of 15 kilos), & 7kg hand luggage per person.

    International guests: Excess baggage can be stored, if you are travelling with Rex in both directions, in a secured facility for a fee of $5.50 per bag at Adelaide Airport, (arrange at Rex Check In) - Unlimited hire until return. Luggage can also be left with concierge at your Adelaide hotel.

    On arrival onto Kangaroo Island - a flight of 35 minutes - you will be met by your Lodge representative & transferred to your home for the next 3 nights.

    Southern Ocean Lodge Southern Ocean Lodge is Kangaroo Island's only world-class wilderness lodge. Located at Hanson Bay and commanding spectacular ocean, beach and wilderness views, the 21-suite escape offers the ultimate fusion of nature and luxury. Featuring an environmentally sensitive design, eco chic interiors, premium lodge facilities, personal service, contemporary dining and a signature spa retreat, guests can discover the magnificent coastal scenery and abundant wildlife for which the Island is renowned in impeccable style. With exceptional guided experiences and adventures included in the rate, all guests receive a personalised experiential touring itinerary of the Island's unique natural attractions - the complete Kangaroo Island experience! All touring, gourmet meals, beverages & daily replenished mini bar is included in your stay - where you are invited to make yourself at home in the Lodge.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Kangaroo Island

    These 2 days are at your leisure to participate in the Lodge experiences & eco-sightseeing including Seal Bay, Flinders Chase National Park, Remarkable Rocks, beach fishing, cliff top guided walks, canapés with the kangaroos at dusk, star gazing - or of course you may simply choose to pick a South Australia wine from the cellar, sit in the Great Room & take in the spectacular Southern Ocean.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Kangaroo Island

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Kangaroo Island - Alice Springs - Ayers Rock

    After breakfast this morning you will be transferred to Kingscote airport for your flight via Adelaide & Alice Springs to Ayers Rock. On arrival into Ayers Rock you will be met by your Longitude 131 representative & transferred to your accommodation.

    The giant red monolith has come to represent Australia itself. The massive sandstone rock - the summit of a buried mountain - generates an almost mystical power. 'The Rock' is 8.8 kilometres around the base, 3.6 kilometres long, 2.4 kilometres wide and towers 348 metres above the surrounding plain. The Anangu people believe they are the direct descendents of the ancestral spirits who gave them the laws and rules that they live by. They believe they are responsible for the protection of the lands and ask visitors to respect their wishes not to climb the rock. Its significant power can be felt particularly in the early morning or at sunset as the Rock undergoes an extraordinary transformation of colour.

    After arriving at Longitude 131° and settling in, meet in the Dune House for a 3pm departure to the Cultural Centre where you will learn the cultural and spiritual meanings of Uluru & Kata Tjuta. Afterwards you will travel to Uluru, where we will take a short walk into Kantju Gorge. On arrival at the gorge we will serve refreshments 'inside' Uluru and watch the sunset. You will return to Longitude 131° at approximately 7.15pm.
    On your return, relax in the Dune House over pre-dinner drinks and canapés before enjoying a gourmet dinner with your fellow travellers or for a special occasion a private dinner by the pool or on the Dune Top.

    Longitude 131 The new 'safari' style luxury tented site is the first of its kind in Australia. Built on the very edge of Uluru Kata Tjuta National Park, the fifteen individual tents have uninterrupted views over Uluru. Relax in the free-form swimming pool and enjoy an aperitif on the balcony as the sunsets over the desert, or simply lie in bed and watch the stars rise over the outback. Inside the tent, the atmosphere is lavish with expansive stone floors, beautiful bed linen and unique bathrooms and air conditioning. This is a special place where you will connect with the desert landscape and its magnificent natural wonders. At the Dune House enjoy a quiet drink, good food and the company of newfound friends.

    Overnight: Longitutde 131
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 7

    Ayers Rock

    Depart Longitude 131° at 6am bound for Kata Tjuta. Upon arrival you will begin your journey with a guided tour of Walpa Gorge, where the natural history of Kata Tjuta is explained in great detail, learning how eons of weathering and continental upheaval have created what is seen today. You will return to Longitude 131° at approximately 10am.

    Spend your afternoon at leisure, perhaps have a swim in the pool or take the opportunity to enjoy any of our optional tours, such as a camel ride, Harley ride or helicopter tour over Uluru and Kata Tjuta.

    Enjoy sunset drinks and canapés on the Dune Top from 6pm. You will then be transferred to our outdoor dining venue - Table 131°, where you'll have an unforgettable dining experience under the stars. Return to the Dune House for complimentary after dinner drinks.

    Overnight: Longitude 131
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    Ayers Rock - Cairns - Mossman

    Depart the Dune House at 6am for Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park to view the sun rising over Uluru. You'll then continue to the Mutitjulu Waterhole to view cave paintings and hear of the Kuniya/Liru Tjukurpa story. You will return to the Dune House at 9.15am for a well-earned continental buffet and a la carte cooked breakfast. From here you will be transferred to the airport for your direct flight to Cairns.

    On arrival into Cairns you will be met by your driver and transferred 1.5 hours along the coastal Pacific Highway into the rainforest region & Mossman.

    Silky Oaks Lodge & Healing Waters Spa

    Silky Oaks Lodge is situated on the banks of the Mossman River, at the gateway to the World Heritage listed Daintree Rainforest. Each private cabin is appointed with handcrafted Australian colonial timber furniture and has its own private verandah overlooking billabongs (waterholes) or rock swimming pools. Included is a tropical breakfast daily. Optional activities available include spa and massage treatments, freshwater swimming, tennis, bike riding and guided walks through the rainforest during the day and evening to look for kangaroos, birds and platypus.

    Overnight: Silky Oaks Lodge
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Mossman - Cape Tribulation - Mossman

    After breakfast this morning meet your guide in the hotel lobby for your full day 4WD exploration of the Daintree Rainforest & Cape Tribulation. You will travel through the rich farming areas toward the World Heritage Listed forest region of Cape Tribulation, where you step aboard a personalized craft for a cruise on the mirrored smooth waters of the Daintree River. You will cruise close to riverbanks and under the overhead canopy of the rainforest seeking out the vivid Ulysses Butterfly, Kingfishers and various other wildlife. The cruise takes you through silent backwaters where crocodiles can normally be seen basking on the muddy banks.

    Arriving on the northern side of the river you will then enter the Cape Tribulation section of the Wet Tropics World Heritage protected rainforest. The journey takes you up the beautiful Alexandra Range passing through rainforest waterfalls and on to the first stop for home-style morning tea deep in the rainforest at the Jindalba boardwalk. Jindalba (the aboriginal name for this place) is also where you literally immerse yourself inside the protected forests and go on a guided walk where you view plants of ancient linage right through to examples of more recent species of flowering plants.

    Continuing along the Cape Tribulation road, you then stop at the magical Thornton Beach. Here your guide will highlight the amazing wildlife and ecosystems unique to this part of the world - "where the rainforest meets the sea". You are often the only people on this stretch of wilderness beach and many who have travelled remark how they feel like Robinson Crusoe. This is also where Cooper Creek uniquely meets up with the Coral Sea.

    Lunch is a special treat today, deep under the Rainforest canopy. You will enjoy a delicious lunch of tropical rainforest-inspired local produce. You have the chance for a private rainforest walk here as well, and may possibly spot rainforest lizards, birds, butterflies and perhaps even marsupials! This is also your opportunity to immerse yourself in the fresh, clear, rainforest creek.

    A lasting impression will be at the outstanding panoramic picture postcard view at Alexandra Range Lookout. From here you'll have a spectacular view of the mouth of the Daintree River, Snapper Island and on a clear day right through to Port Douglas. One final treat for the day is the crossing on the Daintree River Ferry - the iconic vehicle punt which transfers you out of the world heritage rainforest and on to your home-ward road. You will be back at your accommodation early in the evening.

    Please note during wet season the itinerary may change due to higher water levels.

    Overnight: Silky Oaks Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 10

    Mossman - Cairns - Lizard Island

    After breakfast this morning meet your driver in the Lodge lobby for your private vehicle transfer to the General Aviation airport for your flight over the Great Barrier Reef to Lizard Island. On arrival onto Lizard you will be met by your Island representative & orientated with the Island.

    Imagine an island so pristine, so untouched, that when you walk one of the twenty-four secluded beaches there are no other footprints. Lizard Island is the most northerly island resort located directly on the Great Barrier Reef, north of Cairns. Repeatedly voted one of the top ten island resorts in the world it is renowned for spectacular fishing, particularly in marlin season (September-December) and world famous diving opportunities such as the "Cod Hole".

    Choose from a wide choice of activities including guided nature walks, kayaking, private beach picnics or pamper yourself with a spa treatment at Azure Spa. Whatever your choice you'll appreciate the peace and tranquillity of this natural environment.

    Picture yourself sipping French champagne while taking full advantage of the seascape views at idyllic Ospreys Restaurant. With an accent on fresh seafood and superb local produce, the daily changing menus draw on the flavours of modern Australian, Asian and European cuisine.

    Most recent accolades include featuring in Condé Nast Traveller's 2010 Gold List as only one of two Australian hotels named for Best Hotels for Leisure Facilities and ranking 8th place in the category 'Top 20 Resorts Oceania' in the US Condé Nast Traveller Readers' Choice Awards

    Overnight: Lizard Island
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 11

    Lizard Island

    For a few hours today you will be treated to an exclusive & private tour of enormous environmental importance. The Lizard Island Research Centre attracts marine scientists from around the globe in their quest to conserve and manage the world's coral reef systems. The environmental source to some 800 research publications, the Research Station is the only field station operated by the Australian Museum and is internationally recognised as a leading marine research facility. Led by one of the Directors of the Island's Research Station, watch Australian and international marine scientists at work and have a firsthand look at a range of marine research projects, many of which utilise the state of the art seawater aquarium system enabling controlled experiments with living reef organisms. This first ever behind the scenes tour is an exclusive.

    Overnight: Lizard Island
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 12

    Lizard Island

    Today is at your leisure to participate in resort activities, snorkel the reef right off the beach, take a private picnic to a secluded beach or simply relax & enjoy your spectacular surrounds.

    Overnight: Lizard Island
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 13

    Lizard Island - Cairns - Sydney

    Today you will fly back over the Great Barrier Reef to the General Aviations airport where your driver will await to take you to the domestic airport for your flight to Sydney. On arrival into Sydney you will be met by your local guide and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel.

    Sydney, the capital of the state of New South Wales, is Australia's most well known city. With its extraordinary icons both natural and man-made this is a city that never fails to please the eye. It's magnificent natural harbour spanned by the 'coat hanger', the Sydney Harbour Bridge, and the man-made sails of the Sydney Opera House are just some of its most widely photographed views.

    Four Seasons Hotel Sydney is superbly situated right on Sydney Harbour in the city's historic Rocks area. The Four Seasons Sydney Hotel has wonderful views of the Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge. It is only a short walk from Sydney's major shopping and business districts.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Sydney
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 14

    Sydney

    After breakfast this morning meet your guide in the hotel lobby for your morning sightseeing tour of the harbour city.

    Today's tour begins at Sydney's historic Rocks area known for its narrow cobbled streets and convict buildings. Continue past the historic Mint and Hyde Park Barracks and stop at Mrs Macquarie's Chair for wonderful views of the Harbour, Opera House and Harbour Bridge. From here journey on by private vehicle to the Sydney Opera House for a private, behind-the-scenes tour of this Australian icon.

    50 years ago a young Danish architect named Jorn Utzon dreaming of Bennelong Point, designed a masterpiece which pushed the boundaries of creativity and science - and put Sydney on the map forever. Step inside the world's most recognisable sails for a one hour tour and experience the miracle of architecture and engineering. A work of art that came into existence against impossible odds - to become one of the busiest performing arts centres in the world! Working with a new enriching interactive and audio visual component, where images are projected onto the fabric of the building - our guides will take you on an emotional journey, engrossing you in a story to rival any opera plot with its dramatic twists and turns. You'll visit one of the major venues - where there are live performances every day. Some 1500 performances, in every genre you can imagine, are staged each year to audiences of around one million.

    From here your guide will escort you along Circular Quay to Campbell's Cove pontoon where your private charter vessel awaits.

    This afternoon you will be orientated with Sydney from the most enviable vantage point - on the water! Cruising on Sydney Harbour is a quintessential Australian experience, and today you will experience 'Calypso' - a 52 foot motor cruiser that is crewed & privately yours.

    Whilst cruising this iconic harbour you will have the opportunity to swim, fish or kayak. However most choose to sit back with a glass of Australian beer or wine & take in the amazing sights - the Harbour Bridge, the Opera House, Luna Park, Fort Denison and the waterfront homes of the rich & famous. A barbeque lunch is served on board, while anchored in a secluded cove. And as the afternoon sun comes in we suggest making your way to the bow and settling in the sunbeds for the perfect views of the harbour city as you come back in to moor. Your 4 hour experience on the water ends at Campbell's Cove pontoon in the late afternoon, just a short walk from your hotel.

    For dinner this evening we recommend a restaurant reservation at Aria.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Sydney
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 15

    Sydney

    Meet your driver in the hotel lobby for your transfer to the waterfront suburb of Rose Bay. Here your seaplane awaits for a journey to lunch that your will never forget, affording your truly breathtaking views of Sydney, her harbour & all the Sydney icons.

    A mere 20 minutes flying time from the hustle and bustle of Sydney is the exquisitely tranquil and award winning Cottage Point Inn. This beautiful restaurant is perched on the water's edge in the heart of the Ku-ring-gai Chase National Park and is renowned for offering exceptional modern Australian cuisine. Upon landing, your pilot taxis the aircraft to the restaurant's seaplane dock, where Cottage Point Inn's friendly staff offers a warm welcome and any number of great Australian wines! After a leisurely 3 course à la carte lunch reboard your seaplane and return to Sydney Harbour. To top off a magical afternoon we offer you an incredible photo opportunity flying a circuit of the Harbour Bridge and Opera House, before landing at Rose Bay. Your driver awaits to return you to your hotel at around 4.00pm.

    For dinner this evening we recommend a restaurant reservation at Tetsuya or Icebergs at Bondi.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Sydney
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 16

    Sydney - Home

    After breakfast this morning meet your driver in the hotel lobby for your private vehicle transfer to Sydney International airport for your onward journey.

    Meal Plan: B

PRISTINE WATERS OF NINGALOO REEF

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 days

    Take a scenic Flight over Cape Range National Park, Be amazed at Ningaloo Marine Park with opportunities for guided Snorkelling and Kayaking or enjoy the great outdoors with Bush Walks and Quad biking. Swimming with Whale Sharks is available March to July.

    Highlights: Fly return Perth to Learmonth. Scenic Flight over Cape Range National Park Ningaloo Marine Park. Guided Snorkelling, Kayaking, Bush Walks & Quad biking. Swimming with Whale Sharks (March to July)

  • Day 1

    Arrive Perth

    Meet your guide for a private sightseeing tour including a Swan River cruise.

    The Swan River is Perth's natural heartbeat - the city's location right on the river banks makes the capital one of the most beautiful in the world. The view of the river and city from Kings Park is among Western Australia's most scenic - city sky scrapers shining against a brilliant blue Perth sky, green parklands and the shimmering blue river combine to create a stunning vista.

    Overnight: The Richardson Hotel, Deluxe Room

  • Day 2

    Perth - Cape Range National Park

    This morning fly to Exmouth then board a charter plane for 20 minute scenic flight over the impressive limestone plateaus of Cape Range National Park. Hidden in the dunes discover Sal Salis with just eight eco wilderness tents complete with surprisingly luxurious comforts. All meals, beverages and guided activities are included and an underwater garden lies just metres from the pristine white sand. Sunset drinks and dinner are served with views to the reef.

    Overnight: Sal Salis, Wilderness Tent
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Ningaloo Reef: Swim With the Whale Sharks

    Embark on an adventurous Whale Shark cruise aboard a luxurious vessel. Snorkel Ningaloo Reef and relax with a gourmet lunch on board.

    The Whale Shark is the largest member of the shark family, the largest fish in the world and suprisingly, a harmless plankton eater! Reported to reach 18 metres in length, the whale shark is easily identified by its pattern of white spots. Thought to mainly inhabit worldwide temperate and tropical seas, it is rarely seen in shallow coastal water, but is a daily visitor to the Ningaloo Reef between late March and July. Up to 12 Whale Sharks have been spotted close to the reef feeding (Whale Shark tours operate March - July). Manta Rays with wingspans several metres wide, as well as a variety of tropical fish and turtles can be seen year round.

    Overnight: Sal Salis, Wilderness Tent
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Corqal Bay

    Collect your vehicle in Exmouth and drive to Coral Bay, the southern gateway of Ningaloo Reef. Board a one hour Sub-Sea explorer tour before riding a quad bike along the stunning coastline.

    Coral Bay is as idyllic as the name suggests. With a sweeping bay, bobbing yachts and the world-class Ningaloo Reef just two steps off the beach two nights in this destination is divine.

    Overnight: Ningaloo Reef Resort, Ocean Apartment
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Manyara - Ngorongoro

    Renowned for its marine life, today's three hour cruise on Coral Bay invites dolphins, dugongs, manta rays, turtles and an array of fish sightings.

    Overnight: Ningaloo Reef Resort, Ocean Apartment
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    Coral bay - Exmouth - Perth

    This morning is at leisure to explore the region. Drive to Exmouth and return your vehicle at the airport before your flight to Perth.

    Overnight: The Richardson Hotel, Deluxe Room
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Depart Perth

    Transfer to Perth Domestic Airport for your flight home (B). (Flight not included).

    Meal Plan: B

THERE'S NOTHING LIKE AUSTRALIA

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 13 days

    This itinerary showcases places that you would have seen on the Oprah Winfrey show on Australia. It is an ideal itinerary for the first time visitor to Australia.

    Highlights: Melbourne, Kangaroo Island, Uluru, Hamilton Island, Sydney

  • Day 1

    Arrive Melbourne

    On arrival into Melbourne you will be met by your guide and chauffeur and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel. Your accommodation is available for immediate occupancy.

    Melbourne, the capital of the state of Victoria, is known as the garden, arts and sports capital of Australia. Its beautiful tree-lined boulevards, many parks, gardens and beautiful Victorian terrace houses, great restaurants, wonderful theatre and art galleries, together with international sporting events all combine to make this elegant city one of the world's most liveable.

    After meeting your guide visit Cook's Cottage before taking a walk through the unique Royal Botanic Gardens, laid out over 150 years ago and reputed to be the finest in the Southern Hemisphere. Delight in the over 10,000 species and 50,000 individual plants in the 38 hectare gardens, including trees and plants of great cultural value. Many of these plants are irreplaceable, endangered or even extinct in the wild and have become a natural sanctuary for native wild life including black swans, bell birds, cockatoos and kookaburras that fill the air with their distinctive song. Travel past Victorian terrace houses, the city's exclusive suburbs and the Arts precinct before returning to the hotel at the 'Paris End' of the city.

    The Park Hyatt Melbourne is the city's most deluxe hotel. It is located at the 'top end' of Collins Street, directly opposite Melbourne's grandest cathedral and a few steps from Parliament House, the central business district and the beautiful Fitzroy Gardens. The luxurious oversized rooms are a haven for relaxation and comfort. This evening enjoy all inclusive evening drinks and canapés in the Club lounge.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne

  • Day 2

    Melbourne

    This morning meet your local guide raconteur Kenneth Park, a museum and art curator by profession, who will take you on a private walking tour of the National Gallery of Victoria. The NGV's Australian Collection is located at Federation Square displaying one of Australia's finest Aboriginal Art collections. All of the very best Australian artists are represented in the collections.

    Explore Federation Square - a mixed-use development covering an area of 3.2 hectare and centred around two major public spaces. The entire precinct is built on top of a concrete deck over busy railway lines and was opened in 2002. It is Victoria's second most popular tourist attraction, attracting 8.41 million visitors in 2009. Its design has caused much controversy since its first planning and continued to divide opinion after its opening. Despite this, the precinct continues to expand. Federation square is the site at which Oprah Winfrey was greeted by thousands of fans on her visit to Melbourne in December 2010. Kenneth also takes you through the city's labyrinth of atmospheric arcades and alleyways - a wonderful way to learn Melbourne's architectural and cultural heritage. Have a special interest? Kenneth will tailor-make his walk just for you!

    This evening enjoy drinks and canapés in the Club lounge.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Melbourne - Kangaroo Island

    After breakfast this morning meet your chauffeur in the hotel lobby and transfer by private vehicle to the Melbourne Domestic Airport. Depart Melbourne for Kangaroo Island (via Adelaide).

    Kangaroo Island is the place where you can quietly 'go bush' and see native Australian animals and birds as they were meant to be seen in the wild. Half the native bushland on Kangaroo Island remains just as it was when British navigator Matthew Flinders put a name to this untamed wilderness in 1802. And more than one-third of the Island is National or Conservation Park. Kangaroo Island is a true wildlife sanctuary. Owing to its isolation from the mainland, the Island has suffered less from the impact of European settlement and retains more than half of its native 'old-growth' vegetation - a vast area of some 2,250 square kilometres. The Island has been spared the damage done by foxes and rabbits, ensuring the integrity of native bushland and animal and bird populations have thrived. Kangaroo Island's prolific wildlife includes penguins, Australian sea lions, New Zealand fur seals, varied birdlife, koalas and echidnas, all revelling in the protected environs.

    On arrival in Kangaroo Island you will be transferred to your accommodation at Southern Ocean Lodge. Southern Ocean Lodge is Kangaroo Island's only world-class wilderness lodge. Located at Hanson Bay and commanding spectacular ocean, beach and wilderness views, the 21-suite escape offers the ultimate fusion of nature and luxury. Featuring an environmentally sensitive design, eco chic interiors, platinum lodge facilities, personal service, contemporary dining and a signature spa retreat, guests can discover the magnificent coastal scenery and abundant wildlife for which the Island is renowned in impeccable style.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Kangaroo Island

    This morning you will meet your naturalist guide and travel by private 4WD passing some of the islands 1.3 million sheep growing some of the world's finest wool. Walk quietly with the guide through the bush where koalas live in the tall eucalyptus trees overhead. Look for wallabies that are almost extinct on the mainland and the local kangaroos found only on the Island.

    Enjoy a barbecue lunch with South Australian wines before heading to Seal Bay Conservation Park for a walk among the sea lions on a beautiful sandy beach. See old bulls bearing the scars of territorial disputes and learn of the seal's unique breeding biology. In the afternoon visit the site of an old whaling station and ospreys' nest.

    This afternoon enjoy the facilities at Southern Ocean Lodge or take a quiet bushwalk amidst this pristine Australian nature reserve.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Kangaroo Island - Adelaide - Uluru

    This morning depart Kangaroo Island for Uluru (Ayers Rock) via Adelaide. On arrival into Uluru you will be met by a representative of Longitude 131° and transferred to your accommodation.

    The new 'safari' style luxury tented site is the first of its kind in Australia. Built on the very edge of Uluru Kata Tjuta National Park, the fifteen individual tents have uninterrupted views over Uluru. Relax in the free-form swimming pool and enjoy an aperitif on the balcony as the sunsets over the desert, or simply lie in bed and watch the stars rise over the outback. Inside the tent, the atmosphere is lavish with expansive stone floors, beautiful bed linen and unique bathrooms and air conditioning. This is a special place where you will connect with the desert landscape and its magnificent natural wonders. At the Dune House enjoy a quiet drink, good food and the company of newfound friends.

    Uluru is a giant red monolith that has come to represent Australia itself. The massive sandstone rock - the summit of a buried mountain - generates an almost mystical power. The Rock is 8.8 kilometres around the base, 3.6 kilometres long, 2.4 kilometres wide and towers 348 metres above the surrounding plain. The Anangu people believe they are the direct descendents of the ancestral spirits who gave them the laws and rules that they live by. They believe they are responsible for the protection of the lands and ask visitors to respect their wishes not to climb the rock. Its significant power can be felt particularly in the early morning or at sunset as the Rock undergoes an extraordinary transformation of colour.

    This afternoon is at your leisure at Longitude 131°. You will have the opportunity to select your touring in the Red Centre upon check-in.

    Overnight: Longitude 131
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 6

    Uluru

    This morning, you may choose to enjoy an exclusive walking tour of the local bushland. An interpretive guide will accompany you, explaining in great detail the dynamic ecology that exists in this harsh environment. Guests will learn what food sources exist around them, which plants can be used for medicinal purposes, how to find tracks left by the movements of nocturnal fauna, and where to look to spot daytime fauna such as the local bird-life (using spotting binoculars). Each guest is encouraged to identify the many different plants, tracks and wildlife as they smell, touch and taste their bush surroundings.

    To climb the world's largest monolith is against the wishes of the traditional owners, the Anangu, because the path you would otherwise follow is the traditional route taken by their ancestral Mala (rufus hare-wallaby men) on their arrival at this scared place. A base walk will, however, offer you a deeply personal, moving and lasting experience.

    Departing Longitude 131° mid-afternoon, this tour travels into the Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park Cultural Centre, a vibrant and engaging place of learning with more than a few surprises (don't miss the bulging folder of letters from previous visitors apologising for souveniring pieces of rock - the tales of regret and 'mysterious ill fortune' are fascinating). With a greater understanding of one of the world's truly ancient cultures, guests drive around Uluru and see for themselves the sites, which are so sacred to the traditional owners. A walk into the Mutitjulu waterhole offers each guest a unique insight into the sheer physical and spiritual enormity of Uluru. During the walk, the guide will point out cave paintings and other relevant sites important to Anangu and the early explorers.

    You will be returned to Longitude 131° to freshen up before enjoying sunset champagne and canapés atop our secluded sand dune with stunning 360° views of Uluru and Kata Tjuta. From there, you will be taken to an exclusive desert setting where dinner will be served under a glittering canopy of stars. A resident astronomer will join you to explain how our sun, earth and galaxy interact, the almost unfathomable distances involved in space travel, and how our ancestors navigated land and sea on countless voyages of discovery. Guests are regaled with tales of the southern night sky and famous constellations such as the signs of the zodiac, Seven Sisters, Southern Cross and Orion's Belt.

    Overnight: Longitude 131°
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Uluru - Sydney

    Enjoy a morning at leisure to laze by the pool or join a sunrise camel ride across the dunes (optional extra).

    Later this morning a representative of Longitude 131° will meet you and transfer you to Ayers Rock Airport for your flight to Sydney. On arrival meet your driver and guide for transfer to your accommodation.

    Superbly situated right on Sydney Harbour in the city's historic Rocks area, the Four Seasons Sydney Hotel has wonderful views of the Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge. It is only a short walk from Sydney's major shopping and business districts.

    Enjoy evening drinks and canapés in the Executive Club lounge.

    Overnight: Four Seasons
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    Sydney

    This morning meet your guide and depart for a special sightseeing tour of Sydney. Today's tour begins at Sydney's historic Rocks area known for its narrow cobbled streets and convict buildings. In the quaint suburb of Paddington see tiny Victorian houses decorated with cast iron "lacework", much of which came to Australia as ballast in the holds of the wool and grain clippers over 100 years ago. Return to your hotel after visiting Sydney's famous Bondi Beach.

    Sydney, the capital of the state of New South Wales, is Australia's most well known city. With its extraordinary icons both natural and man-made this is a city that never fails to please the eye. Its magnificent natural harbour spanned by the 'coat hanger', the Sydney Harbour Bridge, and the man-made sails of the Sydney Opera House are just some of its most widely photographed views.

    Transfer to nearby Rushcutters Bay where you board a private yacht for an afternoon sail on Sydney Harbour. Enjoy spectacular scenery including the Opera House, Harbour Bridge and the many lovely homes built around this magnificent harbour while relaxing on the deck soaking up the sun and the fresh sea air. If the weather is warm enough enjoy a swim.

    Enjoy evening drinks and canapés in the Executive Club lounge.

    Overnight: Four Seasons
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Sydney

    Meet your guide in the lobby this morning. Today's tour of Sydney begins with a visit to the Sydney Opera House for a private 'behind the scenes' tour. Situated on the foreshore of Sydney Harbour; the Sydney Opera House is one of the world's most recognisable buildings.

    Later this afternoon please make your way to the Bridge Climb Office at 5 Cumberland Street for a twilight Bridge Climb. The Sydney Harbour Bridge Climb is a 3.5 hour guided journey to the top of the harbour cities most famous landmark. Climb the world-renowned Harbour Bridge - one of Sydney's most exciting activities. Built in 1932, it took almost 70 years for the Bridge to open to people with a sense of adventure. Feel the excitement while walking over the catwalk to climb the pylon and then across the arch on the way to the summit - 134 metres above sea level. Marvel at the spectacular 360 degrees views of one of the worlds' great harbour cities. Back at the Executive Club Lounge enjoy drinks and canapés as you recount the wonders of Sydney City.

    Overnight: Four Seasons
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 10

    Sydney - Hamilton Island

    After breakfast this morning meet your chauffeur in the lobby for transfer to Sydney Airport and your flight to tropical Hamilton Island.

    Arriving early afternoon you will be met by a representative of qualia and transferred by private island buggy to your exclusive accommodation. Qualia is embedded in 30 acres of tropical garden landscape on the northern most tip of Hamilton Island. Accessible by direct flights from Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane and Cairns, guests are offered the positively indulgent choice of 60 individual pavilions. Each pavilion has been erected with the least possible intervention of the surroundings and consist largely of natural materials such as rough hewn stone walls and hoop pine. Each of the beautifully appointed pavilions offers ocean views with your choice of a private plunge pool or stunning sun deck.

    This afternoon visit the Hamilton Island Nature Reserve, a small and intimate wildlife park, one of the few places in the world where you can experience the thrill and excitement of cuddling a koala. View a range of furry marsupials as well as Australia's unique snakes, lizards and even a crocodile. The Nature Reserve was Oprah's first stop in Australia, where she cuddled Elvis the koala. Your admission includes unlimited visitation during your stay.

    Overnight: qualia
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    Great Barrier Reef

    After breakfast drive your own private buggy down to the Hamilton Island Marina this morning. Board the large, high-speed, air-conditioned catamaran to the idyllic Great Barrier Reef for a day of unsurpassed adventure.

    Reefworld, located at Hardy Reef, is your base for exploring this natural wonder. Hardy Reef is one of the most beautiful & prolific of nature's coral gardens, confirmed by the vibrant coral and abundance of marine life experienced whilst snorkelling or diving. So many colours and thousands of fish! Tour the reef without getting wet aboard the large spacious semi-submersibles and learn from informative commentary delivered by experienced Reef Interpreters. Reefworld's facilities also include a large, seated underwater viewing chamber; a safe and secure diving area; a large sundeck with boundless views of the surrounding reef systems; a tailor-made viewing area on deck to see the massive Queensland Groper living beneath the platform; change rooms; fresh water showers; children's swimming enclosures and extensive shaded areas.

    Overnight: qualia
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 12

    Whiteheaven Beach Heli-Picnic

    This morning prepare for a stunning adventure to conclude your Australian trip of a lifetime. Fly by helicopter to Whitehaven beach and on the way take in the breathtaking aerial view of the surrounding Whitsunday Islands and fringing reefs. Whilst at Whitehaven soak up the sun and relax with chilled sparkling wine and picnic lunch. This tour provides the perfect opportunity for fantastic photos and video footage not to be forgotten. A delicious picnic complete with sparkling wine, juice and water, shade umbrella and picnic blanket are yours for a delightful 1.5 hours on one of the most beautiful beaches in the world. Take your swimmers, towel and sunscreen for swimming in the clear turquoise waters.

    Overnight: qualia
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 13

    Depart Hamilton Island

    After breakfast enjoy time at leisure for a last swim or maybe a spa treatment (optional extra) to prepare you for your flight. Later this morning transfer by private buggy to Hamilton Island Airport for your departing flight connecting through Sydney and Honolulu.

    Meal Plan: B
