Suggested Madeira accommodation

  • Choupana Hills Resort & Spa

    African and Asian influences come together in this lavish resort hotel on the island of Madeira. The international influences are epitomised by countless sculptures and artefacts of a quality normally seen in national museums.

    The main features of the buildings are wood and stone, combined with glass facades to let in the clear light of the Atlantic. Thirty-four bungalows made mainly of wood and stone are set on stilts and all 60 rooms are spacious, beautifully furnished and offer the latest technology. A further four suites offer even more space with the added luxury of outdoor Jacuzzis.

    This hotel occupies a stunning location high in the hills. The views are beautiful, taking in the port far below and the azure sea beyond.

    The Vine

    The Vine Hotel in Madeira is situated ideally in the historic centre of the capital Funchal, steps away from the Cathedral, the New Funchal Centrum Complex and close to the Funchal Tourist Harbour.

    Generous space and pared-down sophistication are the trademarks of this superb new property, which takes its name from the vines used to make the island's world-famous Madeira wine. The Vine's gleaming façade, all sleek lines in silver and glass, has created a new landmark in the centre of Fuchal
