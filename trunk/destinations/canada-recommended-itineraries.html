Private Grizzly Bear Viewing

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 5 day/4 night

  • This magical adventure is designed around co-existing with the awe-inspiring grizzly bear. This 5 day/4 night trip sees you flying into the absolute wilderness on a private viewing experience bringing you face to face with one of North America's most beautiful creatures. This trip is completely private, designed around you. We allow just four/five guests, guaranteeing an absolute intimate experience with the bears.

    Guests begin their experience by flying into Whitehorse, the capital of Yukon. You may wish to spend a day or two here exploring Miles Canyon, Grey Mountain and the amazing salmon fish ladders which will be in full swing at this time of year. It's a two hour beautiful drive from Whitehorse to the small community of Atlin - population 450. The surrounding area is home to Taku River Tlingit First Nation.

    At Atlin you will already feel a sense of the surrounding wilderness, but it's from here you will board a chartered helicopter leaving behind this small town and head into complete isolation. Here there are no roads within four million acres of undisturbed land.

    Your guide and host will be Phil Timpany, a world renowned expert on Grizzly Bear behaviour. Phil has 38 years of experience in the world of the grizzly bear. Part bear whisperer, part bear psychologist and renowned bear expert, Phil is the ideal companion for bear viewing in Canada. Phil is often a source for the Canadian media whenever an usual bear-related event occurs. Phil will lead you into the serene wilderness surrounding the Taku River - the largest pristine watershed in the Western Hemisphere.

    Grizzlies are normally solitary but congregate during the salmon season. The Nakina River is the birthplace of Chinook, Pink and Sockeye salmon who return to spawn. This relatively short period brings a healthy population of grizzly bears to feed as they prepare for winter hibernation.

    Home is the Nakina River Camp, a clean but rustic base to observe the bears. The bears will continuously approach and even enter the camp on the search for salmon. In fact the bears will congregate around the camp.

    Over three days in remote wilderness, you'll have many opportunities to view the bears, the millions of spawning salmon at your feet, other local wildlife and the hundreds of eagles circling above, also feeding on the spawning salmon. Over breakfast you can expect to see numerous bears within close range. Phil will explain their behaviour and the personalities of these fascinating animals.

    Throughout your stay, you will walk upstream and downstream to various viewing sites. As you go, you'll see bear rubbing trees and you learn about bear forage, social behavior and their seasonal use of the river valley. This is truly one of the most intimate viewing experiences of wildlife anywhere.

Spirit Bears of Canada with Mark Carwardine

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 Nights

    8th - 15th September 2012
    The Kermode bears of British Columbia are a unique subspecies of the black bear. Due to their pale and sometimes white colour they are often referred to as the spirit or ghost bear and are revered by Native Americans.

    Highlights: - Accompanied throughout by Mark Carwardine
    - Go in search of the spirit bears
    - Stay in a luxury floating resort on Princess Royal Island

  • Saturday 8th September 2012 - London/Vancouver

    Depart London for your flight to Vancouver. Upon arrival in the evening you will be met and transferred to the Shangri La Hotel.

  • Sunday 9th September 2012 - Vancouver

    A full day at leisure to explore the spectacular harbour city of Vancouver.

  • Monday 10th September 2012 - Vancouver/Princess Royal Island

    Transfer to the South Terminal Airport for our chartered flight from Richmond to Bella Bella. From Bella Bella the adventure begins with a 45 minute seaplane flight to King Pacific Lodge, located on the spectacular Princess Royal Island.

  • Tuesday 11th to Thursday 13th September 2012 - Princess Royal Island

    Over the coming days there will be chance to explore this beautiful and remote part of Canada from your base at King Pacific Lodge. Moored in the shelter of Barnard Harbour, the lodge is positioned to capture the spectacular views of the wilderness. All meals are included and served in the dining room with views of the ocean.

    Mark and a local native guide will accompany you to go in search of the Kermode (spirit) bears. Found only on Princess Royal and its neighbouring islands, this rare all-white black bear is one of Canada's ecological treasures. According to local legend, if you are lucky enough to see one of these very special bears, you will be rewarded with good fortune.

    King Pacific Lodge has a range of other activities available for you to explore this remote part of the Canadian wilderness including kayaking and hiking trails. There is a spa for muscle relaxing treatments. At night explore the night sky through the high-powered telescope.

  • Friday 14th September 2012 - Princess Royal Island/Vancouver/London

    Time to leave this wonderful area. Depart by seaplane to Bella Bella for our chartered flight back to Vancouver to connect with your international flight to London, arriving on Saturday 15th September. Extensions throughout Canada can be arranged.

British Columbia Luxury Cruising

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 6 Nights

    This 7-day/ 6-night once in a lifetime trip provides the very best of British Columbia by land, air and sea. This luxurious adventure is perfect for active families and couples seeking nature, elegance, activities and the gourmet experience.

  • Day 1

    India/Vancouver

    Upon your arrival at the Vancouver Airport, your driver will meet you for the brief drive to downtown Vancouver. For dinner tonight, you will dine at Market by Jean Georges, which is an outstanding gourmet experience, one of the finest in the city. Your hotel is the shimmering Shangri-La Hotel in an Executive King Balcony Room.

  • Day 2

    Vancouver/Gulf Islands

    You will be transported to Steveston to board your floating hotel - the Pacific Yellowfin - for three days of Gulf Islands cruising. Brunch will be on board as you set sail for Montague Harbour. Later, cycle on Galiano Island, hike in Bluffs Park, kayak in the marine park, or go beachcombing. You spend the night in one of the staterooms on-board.

  • Day 3

    Gulf Islands

    Set sail in search of whales and to explore the other Gulf Islands. Enjoy the many activities and features available to you on the Pacific Yellowfin. Highlights can include a winery visit or cycling on Mayne, Pender or Saturna Islands. Enjoy a picnic lunch on land, or return to the Yellowfin for afternoon tea.

  • Day 4

    Gulf Islands/Vancouver Island

    Set sail for Vancouver Island. In Victoria, visit world-famous Butchart Gardens and enjoy High Tea at the Fairmont Empress Hotel, or explore the inner harbour followed by lunch at SookeHarbour House. Alternatively, visit Brentwood Bay for lunch, or cycle the Ardmore and Peninsula.

  • Day 5

    Vancouver Island/Salt Spring Island

    Set sail for Ganges Harbour on Salt Spring Island, where you'll be transported to your next hotel on the largest of the Gulf Islands. Tonight you will dine in the Manor House, which has been hailed as the best in Canada.

  • Day 6

    Salt Spring Island

    Explore Salt Spring on a private gourmet safari. Travel the island sampling local specialties including cheeses, beers, award-winning wines, greens, smoked salmon, crab and other surprises. Take in a bit of history as you visit restaurants, wineries, art studios and galleries.

  • Day 7

    Ganges Harbour/Vancouver Departure

    After breakfast, catch your private charter seaplane from Ganges Harbour back to Vancouver. From there, you'll be transported to the airport in time for your departing flight way back home.
