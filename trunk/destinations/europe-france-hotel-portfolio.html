SUGGESTED BARGES ACCOMMODATION

  • ANJODI

    The eight passenger Anjodi is of classic Dutch design and has been specifically rebuilt to navigate the 300 year old Canal du Midi. She has been completely refurbished in African hardwoods, hand-crafted panelling and shining brass, creating a comfortable and charming ambience.

    Aboard the Anjodi, you will cruise the Canal du Midi and skirt the sunbathed shores of the Mediterranean before winding inland through ancient villages and Roman fortifications in the Languedoc wine region.

    Particular highlights include the tree lined routes from the lakeside fishing town of Marseillan, home to Noilly Pratt Vermouth, the world's oldest canal tunnel at Malpas, and Narbonne, where you can enjoy private wine tastings and discover Cathar history before visiting Carcassonne.

    LA BELLE-EPOQUE

    La Belle Époque, a state of the art luxury hotel barge, has accommodation for 13 passengers and cruises the idyllic waterways of the Burgundy Canal. The wide deck, sun-deck and spa pool offers superb vantage points from which to watch rural France meander lazily by as the barge threads its way through unspoilt bucolic landscapes.

    Highlights include excursions to sample local Chablis and explore the hilltop village of Flavigny sur Ozerain, where the film Chocolat was shot, as well as the towns of Montbard and Noyers. On board La Belle Époque your Captain leads a crew of five, comprising Master Chef, Tour guide, Housekeeper and Stewardess.

    LA RENAISSANCE

    This beautifully appointed barge was entirely refitted in 2009 to create a luxuriously relaxed cruise experience. The Renaissance's lounge has comfortable seating and a cosy bar. The library is stocked with a range of novels and reference books, as well as a top of the range music system.

    The Renaissance cruises down the River Loire, visiting the hilltop village of Sancerre for a wine tasting tour. Guests can explore Château-Landon and its 11th Century Abbey before travelling on to one of the oldest canal side villages in Burgundy, Rogny-Les-Sept-Ecluses. Marvel at the staircase of seven locks (now adopted as a French National Monument) standing as a testimony to the 500 year history of the canal.

    L'IMPRESSIONNISTE

    The 13 passenger L'Impressionniste began life as a cargo barge in Holland, before being transformed into the powerful canal and river cruiser she is today. She has a light and airy interior reminiscent of the turn of the century era and is decorated with a selection of prints, fabrics and wall colourings.

    Crewed by a knowledgeable Captain, Master Chef and crew of three, she travels through Burgundy - visiting Dijon, famed for its mustard, and Ducal Palace. Wine connoisseurs will delight in visits on the Route des Grands Crus and gastronomes will enjoy lunch at the Michelin-starred restaurant at Abbaye de la Bussiere.
