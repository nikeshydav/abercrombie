Arabian Odyssey

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 5 Nights

    Wild wadis, untouched coastline and dramatic mountain scenery. Bedouin Style Camp in the Wahiba Sands. 16th and 17th century Forts and Castles.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Muscat

    Welcome to the hidden gem of Arabia! Private transfer to your hotel, situated on a sandy cove surrounded by the dark and majestic Hajar Mountains.

    Overnight: The Chedi (Chedi Deluxe Room) or Shangri-La Barr Al Jissah Resort - Al Husn

  • Day 2

    Muscat

    Today, enjoy a morning sightseeing tour of the enchanting port city of Muscat, beginning with the magnificent Sultan Qaboos Grand Mosque, a religious landmark and splendid example of Islamic architecture. Visit Bait Al Zubair, formerly a private home and now a showcase for traditional Omani crafts and culture, and the prestigious Bait Muzna gallery. The remainder of the day is at leisure

    Overnight: The Chedi (Chedi Deluxe Room) or Shangri-La Barr Al Jissah Resort - Al Husn
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Muscat-Sur-Ras Al Jinz

    Drive south-eastward along the stunning coast dotted with white beaches and coves of turquoise waters. En route you will wind your way through the mountains to visit Wadi Arbayeen. After a picnic lunch, continue your journey to Sur, a coastal town where Arabian ocean-going dhows are still built in the traditional manner. This evening, in Ras Al Jinz, visit the nearby turtle-nesting area where, in the company of a ranger, you can watch the sea turtles coming ashore to lay their eggs.

    Overnight: Ras Al Jinz Scientific Centre (Standard Room)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Ras Al Jinz-Wahiba Sands

    This morning, return to Sur and visit the dhow yards to observe the construction of these traditional Arabic vessels. Enjoy lunch before departing for the Wahiba Sands. As you enter the Wahiba Sands, stop to visit the local Bedouins, and enjoy a cup of traditional Arabic coffee, the welcome drink of the Middle East. Overnight stay at a Bedouin-style camp

    Overnight: Desert Nights Camp (Tent)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Wahiba Sands - Jebel Shams - Nizwa

    Today's journey takes you to the rugged interior and Jebel Shams: at over 3000m, it is the highest mountain of the Jebel Al Akhdar range. The gravel road leading to Jebel Shams traverses some spectacular scenery. From the top, there is an almost vertical drop to the wadi floor 1000m below. In Al Hamra, stroll through a date farm and observe ancient falaj irrigation systems still in use. After lunch at the Bait Al Safa Museum, continue to Nizwa, the former capital of Oman

    Overnight: Golden Tulip Hotel (Standard Room)
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 6

    Nizwa - Al Bahla - Jabrin - Muscat

    Begin the day by exploring spectacular Nizwa Fort, constructed by Sultan bin Saif in 1660, and the city's souk, which is renowned for its silver, brass and copper craftsmanship. Before returning to Muscat, visit the village of Bahla, known for its pottery and indigo-dyeing, and Jabrin Castle. Your Oman adventure ends with a transfer to the airport

    Meal Plan: B,L

Musandam Post Tour Extension

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 2 Nights

    Highlights: Rugged coastlines. Deep water fjords. Dhow cruise and dolphin viewing. Visit Iranian merchants at a local market.

  • Day 1

    Muscat - Khasab

    This morning fly from Muscat to Khasab, on the beautiful Musandam Peninsula. Jutting out into the Strait of Hormuz at the entrance to the Persian Gulf, the Musandam lies at the most northern tip of the Sultanate, separated from the rest of Oman by a thin strip of land belonging to the United Arab Emirates. The Musandam is defined by rugged coastal mountains cut by the dark waters of the deep water fjords that earn the peninsula its nickname of the "Norway of the Middle East". This afternoon drive inland into the rugged coastal mountains which in winter and spring, blossom with meadows of wildflowers and afford spectacular year-round vistas of the surrounding dark ink-blue waters.

    Overnight: Golden Tulip Hotel (Panorama Room)

  • Day 2

    Khasab

    Today enjoy a pleasant half day dhow cruise to Telegraph Island. Sailing past the tiny fishing hamlets, your dhow will almost certainly be joined by families of dolphins playing in the bow-wake. Over 20 of the world's species of dolphin are found here in large numbers, along with other abundant sea life, including whale sharks and manta rays. The Musandam is also a bird watcher's paradise, and you may sight cormorants, masked boobies and steppe eagles. Return to the hotel and the remainder of the day is at leisure.

    Overnight: Golden Tulip Hotel (Panorama Room)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Khasab - Dubai

    After breakfast in your hotel enjoy a tour of Khasab, including the market area where an active trade goes on with Iranian merchants, and Khasab Fort, built by the Portuguese in 16th century. After your tour transfer to Dubai for your connecting international flight

    Meal Plan: B

Musandam Pre-Tour Extension

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 3 Nights

    Highlights: Isolated Musandam Peninsula. Rugged coastal mountains and deep water fjords Dhow cruise. Khasab Fort.

  • Day 1

    Dubai - Khasab

    Upon arrival at Dubai International Airport, you will be met and transferred across the Omani border to Khasab, on the beautiful and isolated Musandam Peninsula. Jutting out into the Strait of Hormuz at the entrance to the Persian Gulf, the Musandam lies at the most northern tip of the Sultanate of Oman, separated from the rest of Oman by a thin strip of land belonging to the United Arab Emirates. The Musandam is defined by rugged coastal mountains cut by the dark waters of the deep water fjords that earn the peninsula its nickname "Norway of the Middle East". Enjoy the afternoon at leisure.

    Overnight: Golden Tulip Hotel (Panorama Room)

  • Day 2

    Khasab

    Enjoy a pleasant full day dhow cruise around the fjords, visiting Telegraph Island and sailing past tiny fishing hamlets clinging to the massive cliffs. A picnic lunch will be served onboard

    Overnight: Golden Tulip Hotel (Panorama Room)Golden Tulip Hotel (Panorama Room)
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Khasab

    Today tour Khasab, including the market area where an active trade goes on with Iranian merchants and the Khasab Fort, built by the Portuguese in 16th century. Continue to the village of Qada in Wadi Tawi, where pre-historic rock art can be seen. After time for lunch, travel inland to the rugged coastal mountains which rise upward from the sea dramatically. The highest point in the Musandam is Jebal Harim (Mountain of Women), which rises to over 2000m only 12km from the coast

    Overnight: Golden Tulip Hotel (Panorama Room)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Khasab - Muscat

    Departing Khasab board your morning flight to Muscat

    Meal Plan: B

Salalah Extension

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 2 Nights

    Highlights: Oman's southernmost city. Ancient frankincense trade. Rock inscriptions, pre-Islamic coins and early Chinese Pottery. Legendary Queen of Sheba.

  • Day 1

    Muscat - Salalah

    Depart Muscat on your evening flight to Salalah, Oman's southernmost city and capital of the Dhofar region. Salalah exhibits a subtle African flavour not found elsewhere in Oman. The surrounding countryside, despite lying close to the Arabian Desert, enjoys a tropical climate for most of the year.

    Overnight: Hilton Hotel (Seaview Balcony Room)

  • Day 2

    Salalah

    This morning visit the fishing village of Taqah, an old town with an interesting castle surrounded by watchtowers and Taqah stone houses. Proceed onward to Khor Rori creek, the site of the ruined city of Samhuram and capital of ancient Arabia's frankincense trade. Excavations have produced evidence of an ancient city with trade links by sea to Far Eastern destinations and even with distant Greece. Mirbat, the ancient capital of Dhofar, was an important town as early as the 9th century for its trade in frankincense, horses and slaves. See some of the old houses famous for their woodcarving. One of Dhofar's best known historic sites is Bin Ali's Tomb. The twin-domed structure is a fine example of medieval architecture. Return to the hotel to relax before heading out this afternoon to visit Prophet Job's Tomb, perched high up in the mountains. Continue to Mughsail beach to see the blow-holes. Also visit a local wadi and bird sanctuary.

    Overnight: Hilton Hotel (Seaview Balcony Room) Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Salalah - Muscat

    Enjoy the morning at leisure before transferring to the airport and your flight to Muscat

    Meal Plan: B
