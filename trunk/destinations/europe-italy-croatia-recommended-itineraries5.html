SHORT BREAK TO SPLIT, HVAR AND TROGIR

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 days

    An ideal short break, post cruise or post Italy tour for those wanting to experience a little of Croatia.

    Highlights: Diocletian's Palace in Split, the glamorous island of Hvar, the UNESCO World Heritage site of Trogir.

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE SPLIT

    Upon arrival into Split Airport, you will be met by your local representative and transferred the short distance to your hotel, just in time to watch the sunset from the terrace of your the hotel's fabulous champagne bar. Le Meridien Lav is beautifully situated on the seafront with spectacular views of the Adriatic Sea. For those not wanting to venture too far, the hotel boasts a number of activities, both relaxing nad invigorating, from tennis and volleyball to a whole host of beauty treatments and relaxation therapies.

    Overnight: Le Meridien Lav

  • Day 2

    EXPLORE SPLIT AND DIOCLETIAN'S PALACE

    Enjoy a private half-day sightseeing tour of Split. This fascinating city was first settled at the end of the third century AD when the Roman Emperor Diocletian built his palace here. The importance of Diocletian's Palace far transcends local significance because of its level of preservation and the buildings of succeeding historical periods built within its walls, which today form the very heart of old Split. The palace is a city within a city where many locals still live inside its walls. Outside of the palace, the architecture of the city is a living story reflecting previous rules including the Venetians, Austrians, French, Ottomans and Byzantines.

    The afternoon is yours to relax by the private beach, play tennis, luxuriate in the spa or maybe enjoy watersports in the Adriatic Sea.

    Overnight: Le Meridien Lav
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    EXPLORE THE GLAMOROUS ISLAND OF HVAR

    Travel with your driver by ferry to the island of Hvar. The island is fast becoming the most fashionable of Mediterranean resorts attracting the jet set with its upmarket bars, restaurants and pretty marina. On arrival in Hvar Town meet your local expert guide for a tour of the town, best known for its medieval buildings which are the legacy of Venetian rule. The town is the site of the oldest municipal theatre in Europe which opened in 1612. Perched high on a hill overlooking the town, the gleaming white limestone walls of the medieval Spanjol Fortress afford breathtaking views for those who climb to their summit. Outside of Hvar Town, the island is extraordinarily green and lush, filled with lavender, pines and aromatic Mediterranean flora.

    During the summer Hvar is a must stop destination for the European glitterati onboard their motor yachts - spend time over a lazy lunch people watching, admiring the views over the glistening bay and yachts before returning to the mainland.

    Overnight: Le Meridien Lav
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    TROGIR

    Mid-morning your personal guide and driver will be waiting to show you one last special piece of Croatia, the town of Trogir. Trogir is set on a small island just off the mainland and is listed in UNESCO's list of World's Heritage Sites as the best preserved Romanesque-Gothic complex in the whole Central Europe. Trogir was first inhabited by Greeks from the nearby island of Vis and later developedby the Romans as 'Tragurium'. Attacked and destroyed by Saracens, the town was abandoned and later revived, seeing artistic growth under Hungarian kings and later under Venetian rule. Its rich heritage is still evident in its architecture today.

    There is time to enjoy one last lunch relaxing by the pretty quayside in Trogir before continuing to the airport for your journey home.

    Meal Plan: B

Verona & Venice

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 days

    A 7-day trip of romance around Venice and Verona, taking pleasure in exploring time-honoured palaces, impressive ruins and mouth-watering cuisine.

    Highlights: Verona, Lake Garda, Bologna, Padua, Venice

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE VERONA

    Upon arrival into Verona Airport, your local representative will be waiting in the arrivals hall for your private transfer to your luxurious hotel in the heart of the city.

    Overnight: Due Torri Hotel

  • Day 2

    VERONA AND LAKE GARDA

    This morning you will be met at your hotel by your local English-speaking guide for a 2 hour walking tour of Verona. The city owes its romantic reputation to Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet, which was set here. Your tour will include Piazza delle Erbe, Juliet's House and the Roman Arena, one of the largest and finest preserved amphitheatres.

    After seeing Verona, head out of the city with your guide and private driver to beautiful Lake Garda. You will visit Desenzano del Garda, one of the liveliest and most popular towns on Lake Garda, where you will see Roman ruins dating back to the 4th century. You will then go on to visit Sirmione to visit the ruins of a Roman villa which exceeds over a large area and was once thought to be the residence of the Latin Poet Catullus; it is one of the most impressive examples of an ancient Roman dwelling in Northern Italy. Return to Verona from here.

    Overnight: Due Torri Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    BOLOGNA 'THE FAT ONE'

    Transfer today to the railway station where you have pre-reserved First Class seating on the express train bound for the city of Bologna. The journey takes just 50 minutes, upon arrival into Bologna you will be met from the train door by your expert local guide to begin your tour of this fascinating city (there is a 5-10 minute walk from the railway station to the city center).

    Bologna is the main city of the Emilia Romagna region, the bread basket of Italy. With its wealth of produce, Bolognese are famous for their love of food hence why the city has earned the name 'The Fat One'. Your tour today will begin in the sidestreets and markets where all the fine meats, cheeses, oils and pastas are sold. Visit the Basilica of San Petronius, construction on this cathedral began in the 14th century and work was still in progress on this vast building some 300 years later. It still stands unfinished with the wings of the transept missing and the facade only partially decorated, lacking most of the marble face the architects planned several hundreds of years ago. You may also visit the complex of four churches known collectively as Santo Stefano and the intricate medieval streets around the piazzas.

    After some time at leisure, return to Verona by train where you will be met from the train door and transferred privately back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Due Torri Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    VERONA TO VENICE VIA PADUA FOLLOWED BY AN EVENING PRIVATE VISIT OF ST. MARK'S BASILICA

    Depart Verona today, stopping in the city of Padua where you meet and expert local guide. Dating from 1183BC the city claims to be northern Italy's oldest, however the major attraction is the Scrovegni Chapel. The chapel houses a remarkable cycle of frescoes completed in 1305 by Giotto. It was commissioned by Enrico degli Scrovegni, a wealthy banker, as a private chapel once attached to his family's palazzo. It is also called the "Arena Chapel" because it stands on the site of a Roman-era arena. The fresco cycle details the life of the Virgin Mary and has been acknowledged by many to be one of the most important fresco cycles in the world.

    Travel to Venice as far as the road can take you before boarding your private boat to arrive in true style at your hotel, located next to the world famous St. Mark's Square.

    This evening is a truly spectacular experience as you visit St. Mark's Basilica privately where the beautiful mosaics take on an entirely different look when illuminated after dark.

    Overnight: Luna Hotel Baglioni
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    VENICE - SAN MARCO DISTRICT

    Your English-speaking local guide will meet you today for a 3-hour walking tour of Venice. Your tour will include visits to the Doge's Palace, where you can enjoy the secret itineraries of this spectacular palace. Once the home to Venice's rulers and to the offices of State, the Doge's Palace is a triumph of Gothic architecture. It was also where countless prisoners were held captive and tortured. Visit the underground dungeon of the Doge's Palace and step back in time to see what it was like to be prisoner under the Doge's rule. A fascinating maze of cells is linked by corridors and staircases on both sides of the canal. Famous inmate Casanova made a dramatic escape across the rooftop in 1756. Your tour will also take you down some of the fascinating but lesser know alleyways and narrow streets not far from San Marco.

    Overnight: Luna Hotel Baglioni
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    HIDDEN VENICE

    Your English-speaking local guide will meet you today for a 3-hour walking tour of Hidden Venice, to explore the fascinating but lesser know alleyways and narrow streets not far from San Marco. You can admire the churches and monuments that testify to the long tradition of the Venetian history and culture, seeing the many different architectural styles that have evolved over the centuries, from late Byzantine to Gothic, from Lombard to Renaissance through to Baroque.

    Overnight: Luna Hotel Baglioni
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    DEPART VENICE

    Travel in style again today as your private speedboat awaits you at your hotel's pier to bring you across the Lagoon to Venice Airport.

ROMANTIC ITALY

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 days

    This is an idyllic honeymoon or romantic 7 day break to Italy. It encompasses the main sights this beautiful country has to offer.

    Travel the stunning canals of Venice by Gondolier, re-enact famous scenes from Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet in Verona, picnic in the grounds of an Umbrian vineyard and wonder through Rome at your leisure.

    Highlights: Venice, Grand Canal, Frari church, Verona, Cortona, Assisi, Rome

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE IN VENICE

    Upon arrival at Venice airport you will be welcomed to Italy by your local Guardian Angel and transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in procedures. On arrival, speed across the Lagoon to your noble palace hotel with its amazing position right on the Grand Canal.

    Pulling up to your hotel's own jetty you will not fail to be amazed as you enter the genuine historical monument of a 14th century Venetian Palace adorned with masterpieces by the likes of Tiepolo. In the evening your gondolier awaits to serenede you along the Grand Canal, through the Rialto Bridge to your romantic restaurant of choice.

    Overnight: Hotel Londra Palace

  • Day 2

    LOVER'S VENICE

    Explore the Romantic, less explored, side of Venice with your expert local guide today. Meander through the labyrinth of narrow streets and passageways in the old artisans' quarter where picture postcard images seem to appear around every corner. Visit the Frari Church which houses masterpieces by Titian, Bellini & Canova.

    Overnight: Hotel Londra Palace B

  • Day 3

    VERONA

    Travel back to dry land today, and the short distance to the Roman city of Verona, famed for its connection to Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet. The family homes of Romeo and Juliet, the Capulet's and Montague's, are still standing, today you have the chance for a private romantic moment together on Juliet's balcony, or even renew wedding vows in this truly special location. The remainder of your day will be at leisure.

    Overnight: Due Torri Hotel

  • Day 4

    UNDER THE TUSCAN SUN AND ASSISI

    Join your driver-guide on a journey through to central Italy today and the stunning Tuscan countryside. Explore the region around Cortona, synonymous with the romantic novel and film "Under the Tuscan Sun". The peace and tranquillity of the views rolling across Tuscany's Val d'Orcia are truly spectacular.

    Enjoy the remainder of the day in Assisi at leisure. Your hotel's amazing spa sits within the 2000 year old Roman structure of the building - truly unique. Rooms are in part of a converted church, many with incredible views over the dramatic Umbrian landscapes.

    Overnight: Hotel Nun

  • Day 5

    A ROMANTIC ITALIAN PICNIC

    Enjoy the morning at leisure or explore the fascinating town of Assisi.

    Then your afternoon begins at a family run Umbrian vineyard, where in its heart a luxury picnic has been laid out for you in the serenity of the Italian countryside with the medieval city of Assisi as your backdrop. Take your time, as long as you want, your personal waiter will continue to serve estate wines as long as you may wish to linger.

    Overnight: Hotel Nun Palace

  • Day 6

    TRANSFER TO ROME

    After a romantic and leisurely breakfast your driver will be delighted to drive to your hotel in Rome where you will enjoy the rest of your day at leisure.

    In the evening your driver will bring you to the outskirts of Rome for one of the most memorable and romantic meals of your life. The Solo Per Due restaurant is truly one of a kind where the intimate (the world's smallest) restaurant is dedicated just to you and your partner with the whole experience tailored around your tastes and preferences.

    Overnight:Hotel Rose Garden Palace

  • Day 7

    DEPART ROME

    Today you will be transferred by private vehicle with an English-speaking driver to the Rome Fiumicino airport to board your departure flight.

Family Italy: Emperors and Artists

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 days

    During this interactive Family journey, play the role of a Roman soldier at a gladiator school in Rome, toss your own pizza, learn how gelato is made and explore historic cities at your own pace.

    Highlights: • Take a gondola ride - the perfect way to explore Venice
    • Privately traverse secret passageways below the Doge's Palace
    • Stop at a traditional gelateria and taste your favorite flavo
    • Learn the secret of making tortellini in Bologna, the city that claims to have invented this pasta
    • Visit Pisa, with a chance to ascend the famous Leaning Tower
    • Participate in an exclusive session with a "gladiator" at a training center in Rome
    • Make your own pizza at a pizzeria in Rome

  • Day 1

    Arrive Venice, Italy

    Arrive in Venice and take a private launch to your hotel. Venice is like no other city because it is made up of hundreds of islands, some connected by bridges. The canals are its streets, and you can get around either by water taxi or on foot. Even the fire engines and police vehicles are boats that travel through the canals. This evening, meet other travelling families during a welcome dinner at the hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel Danieli
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    Venice

    Take a walk with your guide through St. Mark's Square, which is filled with cafs, people and pigeons. Gaze at the Basilica, Bell Tower and the pink-and-white marble Doge's Palace - the former seat of governmental departments and residence of the Doge, ruler of the Venetian Republic. Beneath the Doge's Palace lies a reminder of its gruesome past: a maze of cells linked by corridors and staircases, where countless prisoners met an untimely end. Stay close to your guide as you explore - you wouldn't want to be locked in. Throughout your time in the city, look for the winged lion that represents St. Mark - its symbol is emblazoned on the Venetian flag. Spend the afternoon at leisure and take a gondola ride through the canals tonight.

    Overnight: Hotel Danieli
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Venice/Bologna/Florence

    Drive to Bologna, where you learn the secret of making tortellini during a family cooking lesson. After lunch, there is time at leisure to explore Bologna, one of Italy's best-preserved medieval city centers. See the 14th-century Basilica San Petronio and the church complex of Santo Stefano. Arrive in Florence at the end of the afternoon.

    Overnight: Helvetia & Bristol
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 4

    Florence

    Set on the Arno River below the foothills of the Apennines, Florence is a living legacy of the Renaissance. Walk through historic Florence, seeing palaces and the Duomo (Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore), and stop at the famous Uffizi Gallery, housing a magnificent art collection that includes works by Titian, Caravaggio, Raphael and Giotto. The museums hold famous works of art, but the historic streets and buildings, too, hold fascination and cultural significance. Next to the Uffizi is the Palazzo Vecchio, once the home of the Medici family. The tower's patrol path has been recently restored. Follow your guide along the path and learn about the guard's cunning plans to thwart invaders. Once you conquer the path, you are rewarded with views of the city.

    See many of the city's age-old monuments, including one that A&K Philanthropy is helping to restore. No tour of Florence is complete without a visit to one of the city's best gelaterie to sample your favorite flavor. This evening is at leisure to relax or continue exploring on your own.

    Save the Florentine Monuments: is restoring the monuments that are the cultural heritage of Florence and a world treasure.

    Overnight: Helvetia & Bristol
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Florence/Pisa/Florence

    In Pisa, visit the Baptistery and Duomo in the beautiful Campo dei Miracoli (Square of Miracles). The famous Leaning Tower, started in the 12th century, is also located there. When workers completed the third floor, they noticed that the tower started to sink into the ground, and construction ceased for 90 years. If you are ready for the challenge, climb the 294 spiral steps to the top to look out over the city. After lunch at a local restaurant, return to Florence.

    Overnight: Helvetia & Bristol
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 6

    Florence/Rome and Vatican City

    Take a train to the "Eternal City" of Rome, where you witness over 2,000 years of history at every turn. Visit the Vatican and the Sistine Chapel. Gaze up at the frescoed ceiling, which took Michelangelo four years to paint; it is one of the most renowned artworks of the High Renaissance period. Continue to St. Peter's Basilica, built on the site where Peter the Apostle - thought to be the first Pope - was crucified and buried. The balance of the day is at leisure.

    Overnight: Parco dei Principi Grand Hotel & Spa
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 7

    Rome

    Discover Ancient Rome, stopping at the Forum, its political heart. Wander through 900-year-old ruins and see the triumphal Arch of Constantine. Visit the storied Colosseum, imagining what it must have been like in ancient times when gladiator fights and chariot races brought spectators from all over. This afternoon, you can train with Roman "gladiators," who give you a glimpse into this amazing time in history. Attend a farewell dinner, where you make your own pizza.

    Overnight: Parco dei Principi Grand Hotel & Spa
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 8

    Depart Rome

    After breakfast, transfer to the airport and board your homebound flight.

    Meal Plan: B
