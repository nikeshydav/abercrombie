Suggested New South Wales accommodation

  • Byron at Byron Resort and Spa

    With its stylish design and architecture, the multi award winning Byron at Byron Resort and Spa is inspired by its pristine surroundings. Set within a stunning subtropical rainforest, the resort provides an idyllic escape in a relaxed atmosphere whilst offering all the special touches you would expect from a luxury resort.

    The stylish, open plan main building is the heart of the resort with verandas that overlook the spectacular rainforest canopy. Interior design is fresh, vibrant and airy, with many areas opening onto outdoor areas.

    The spacious luxury accommodation comprises 92 one bedroom air conditioned suites. Each suite contains a kitchen, separate lounge and dining area, plasma TV as well as two enclosed balconies. Luxurious bathrooms have deep free standing bathtubs and separate shower.

    Capella Lodge

    Resting above the jewel-toned lagoon, Capella Lodge is the only Island accommodation that commands spectacular views of the ocean and awe-inspiring twin peaks, Mounts Gower and Lidgbird.

    Just nine contemporary suites complement the island's pristine natural environment and provide a luxurious, serene refuge. Spacious, light filled interiors feature timber floors, marine ply joinery and custom furniture pieces that create a sophisticated beach house ambience. All suites feature king beds with superior cotton linens, Bose music system, TV, DVD, telephone and wireless internet. An in-suite bar offers locally-sourced epicurean treats.

    Reflecting the sheer rise of the mountainous cliffs beyond, basalt stone features in striking ensuite bathrooms where an oversized rain shower and island-inspired Capella Spa toiletries invite guests to relax and revitalise. Slick slab vanities contrast the organic feel of handcrafted ceramic and resin bathware.

    Four Seasons Hotel Sydney

    Situated overlooking Sydney Harbour in the historic Rocks district, a short walk from the city's shopping and business centres, Four Seasons is vibrant and elegant, with dramatic harbourfront views.

    Its 531 rooms - including 121 suites - are generously proportioned and decorated in contemporary grand-luxe style, with rich silks, sophisticated marbles and exotic Honduran mahogany. Bathrooms are lavishly decorated with Italian spider gold marble and Italian limestone - most including a deep soaking tub and separate glass-walled shower. Each room features an LCD television with DVD player and iPod/MP3 connectivity, as well as high-speed Internet access and multi-line telephones with private voice mail.

    Stunning views distinguish the various rooms and suites. City-view accommodations overlook the Sydney skyline, George Street or Walsh Bay, while opera-view accommodations showcase the Opera House and Circular Quay. Harbour-view accommodations overlook the Sydney Harbour Bridge, Darling Harbour, the Opera House and Circular Quay.

    Jonah's

    Jonah's is one of Australia's most spectacular ocean retreats. The perfect location to escape, relax and indulge, Jonah's is the only Relais & Chateaux hotel in Sydney.

    Since it first opened as a roadhouse in 1929, Jonah's has put the exclusive enclave of Whale Beach on the international map. Famed for its luxury, privacy and seclusion, Jonah's counts Sir Lawrence Olivier, Sir Anthony Hopkins, Mick Jagger, Jerry Hall and Bono as some of its special quests.

    Fully refurbished in 2005, each of Jonah's eleven rooms is stylishly appointed with luxurious furnishings. Each lovely room features a private balcony, spa and spectacular 180o views over the Pacific Ocean. Luxurious and discreet, Jonah's prides itself on its friendly and casual atmosphere, and outstanding service.

    Lilianfels

    Lilianfels Blue Mountains Resort & Spa is a renowned haven of relaxation in one of Australia's most beautiful destinations. The five star hotel is housed in a historic country mansion set amidst two acres of manicured gardens, overlooking some of the most spectacular scenery in the Blue Mountains, in New South Wales. It is the perfect base from which to explore the Upper Blue Mountains including Katoomba, Leura, Mount Victoria and Blackheath.

    The rooms have the ambiance of an exclusive luxury retreat. From the crispest sheets and fluffiest towels to the individually decorated guest rooms, no detail has been overlooked. Decorated in lush fabrics, the rooms are themed in pink, green or lilac, as well as delightful floral designs. The marble bathrooms offer separate bath and shower with plush towelling bath robes.

    Observatory Hotel

    The Observatory Hotel, which has won almost every prestigious domestic and international award, is a superb urban retreat known for its tranquil, boutique atmosphere, yet offers easy access to Sydney Harbour Bridge and big city action. A 'Leading Hotel of the World' it offers guests the highest standards in accommodation, cuisine and personal service.

    Spacious, elegant and beautifully appointed, the 100 rooms and suites at The Observatory Hotel are welcoming and luxurious. All are individually decorated, to create the comfortable, cosy atmosphere of a 'home away from home'. Elegantly appointed with mahogany custom-made furniture, each room features King or Twin beds and is decorated with the utmost attention to detail.

    Rich tapestry curtains, set off by rope tasselled tiebacks, frame Colonial-style sash windows which provide charming views of Observatory Hill and historic Walsh Bay.

    Park Hyatt Sydney

    This luxurious hotel is on the very edge of Sydney Harbour, with spectacular views of the Bridge and the Opera House, and has been transformed through an extensive renovation. Every aspect of the hotel has been dramatically transformed, setting a new benchmark in contemporary luxury.

    'High design' guest rooms feature floor-to-ceiling glass that open onto private balconies - to make the most of Park Hyatt Sydney's coveted harbourfront location. With seamless glass balustrades and natural timber flooring, balconies are furnished with custom designed outdoor furniture from which to enjoy the magnificent views.

    The room interiors create a natural feel with rich layers of chocolate, cream and beige hues set off by luxurious new furnishings and fittings, naturally-textured fabrics, subtle plush carpets, designer lighting and the latest technology. Modern, spacious bathrooms are designed to offer complete flexibility, with the choice of remaining private from the main bedroom, or transforming into an open plan design.

    The Grace Hotel

    Built by Grace Bros. in the 1920s as a showpiece for their successful retail business, The Grace Hotel has been beautifully restored to its former glory. It is now one of Sydney's most prominent historical landmarks, a fine example of Art Deco design with a distinguished Neo-Gothic exterior.

    All rooms are spacious and modern, with clean contemporary lines in evidence throughout. The décor is dominated by warm, earthy colours, whilst the furniture is both stylish and comfortable.

    The Grace is an ideal base from which to explore Sydney - being only a few minutes walk from Darling Harbour, Cockle Bay and King St. Wharf as well as the best shopping at the Queen Victoria Building David Jones city stores, Pitt St. Mall, and of course Myers in the Sydney Central Plaza. The Rocks, Circular Quay, Harbour Bridge and the iconic Sydney Opera House are all within easy reach.

    Tower Lodge, Hunter Valley

    Tower Lodge is a small five-star luxury hotel in the Hunter Valley - intimate and exclusive, it accommodates a maximum of 24 people at any one time. Its low-rise Spanish Mission-style structure is sympathetic to the surrounding landscapes and home to attentive, exemplary service.

    Radiating out from Tower Lodge's central courtyard, along portico passageways are just 10 individually decorated lodge rooms, plus the Oriental and the Chairman's Suite. All are furnished with antiques, treasures and collectables, rich fabrics and recycled old timbers. The rooms vary in size, but all feature bedroom areas, lounge space and desks. In every room there is a music system, IDD telephone, television with pay channels and DVD.

    The Oriental room has polished timber floors and paneling, whilst the wall spaces feature Chinese and Oriental artwork. The Chairman's Suite is a freestanding building complete with its own tower and viewing platform. The bedroom features a 300 year old, hand carved Rajasthani king bed and opulent sitting area with a Louis writing desk and chair. The marble bathroom has a deep soaking, elevated spa bath and separate shower.

    Wolgan Valley

    Emirates Hotels & Resorts' first development outside Dubai, Wolgan Valley resort is a restored homestead set in astonishing landscapes. World-leading conservation techniques have been employed in bringing the original building back to its former glory - and then transforming it into a wonderful luxury resort.

    All the 40 free-standing luxury suites, each with its own private terrace and swimming pool, perfectly echo the original heritage architecture - to ensure they blends seamlessly with the vastness and dramatic beauty of Australia's Hinterland terrain. Constructed from wood and stone in a 'Federation' style, all are superbly appointed, for instance double-sided fireplaces in local sandstone separate the bedroom from the lounge.

    Meanwhile, the fixtures and fittings have been carefully selected to reflect the Federation period - right down to the last hinge, light switch and glass blown shade. Warm, earthy tones and thick, neutral linens, are complimented by locally-crafted wooden furniture that typifies the lodge's timeless style and elegance.
