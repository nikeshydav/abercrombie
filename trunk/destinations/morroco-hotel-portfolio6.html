Suggested The South accommodation

  • Dar Ahlam

    Perfect for contemplation or a dip in the beautiful heated swimming pool, the private towering rusty red-brick fortress surrounded by a palm grove makes Dar Ahlam a 'House of Dreams'.

    Facilities and activities include a Hammam and solarium, mountain biking, hiking, horseback riding, quad-biking, buggy, camel and mule rides, and hiking.

    Dar Kamar

    Get away from it all in the "the House of the Moon", a splendid former pasha Glaoui courthouse. Walk with the locals down an alleyway in the middle of Taourirt Kasbah to find a discrete doorway that leads to Dar Kamar.

    La Gazelle d'Or

    A warm and welcoming atmosphere awaits at the blissfully secluded La Gazelle d'Or. Surround yourself with glorious bougainvillea-filled gardens and fragrant citrus groves, close to the ancient walled city of Taroudant.

    This hotel feels like a home from home, but with other-worldly charm. The 33 suites and pavilions are hidden away in peaceful gardens, and behind the flower-covered walls of stone cottages. All are wonderfully appointed with Moroccan mosaics, hand-woven rugs and antiques and are individually decorated. They have cosy log fires, spacious lounges and private terraces - a great spot for a leisurely breakfast.

    Enjoy a casual breakfast served on your terrace, lunch by the pool and a formal jacket and tie dinner in the tented dining room. Most of the ingredients that make up the divine cuisine come from the hotels organic farm.

    Le Berbere Palace

    Built in Kasbah style, Berbere Palace is a desert oasis offering cool shady terraces and traditional Moroccan hospitality. The 222 rooms, duplexes and suites are reached via a network of alleyways and gardens.

    Built in Kasbah style, Le Berbere Palace is a desert oasis of cool shady terraces and traditional Moroccan hospitality, with fragrant alleyways surrounded by jasmine and roses. Built in the Moorish style, this hotel transports you to another world, whether via an intimate breakfast on the terrace of your bungalow or candlelight dinner during the Ouarzazate starry night.

    Inside, you find opulence at every turn, from décor to furniture and furnishings - this hotel is palatial in style. What's more, it is a superb base from which to explore the vastness of the surrounding desert, whether by Land Rover, camel or mountain bike.

    Sahara Desert Camping

    From Erfoud you continue toward the Oasis city of Rissani where you will discover the oasis agriculture and visit an old Kasbah. Leave the oasis and its palm trees to enter the desert for just under an hour's drive.
