Spain & Portugal Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Madrid

    Madrid is Europe's highest city characterised with hot summers and cold winters. Out of all attractions in Spain, visitors flock to Madrid to start off on a Spanish journey. It is home to three art museums: the Prado, the Thyssen-Bornemisza and the Centro de Arte Reina Sofia which puts Spain on the world map of art. Dominated by the Palacio Real, it has served as a seat of the Head of the state since the mid-18th century.

    Within reach are Toledo and the city of El Greco. El Escorial which is a part Palace and part monastery and is a mixture of three cultures. Head to Segovia and experience a Roman aqueduct and the fairytale-like Castle set on a rocky terrain.

    Barcelona

    An architectural delight, Barcelona is one of the most exciting cities in Europe. It is set on the Mediterranean coast and is home to the Picasso Museum in the unspoiled medieval Gothic Quarter.

    Enjoy the Modernist structures with Antoni Gaudí's still unfinished Sagrada Familia Basilica and the undulating Casa Milá. On a day's excursion; tour the Monastery of Montserrat, the Dali Museum at Figueres and take a stroll in the eclectic Ramble Boulevard-a Spanish charm.

    Read more

    Northern Spain

    Greenery, Atlantic coast and country vineyards is how you describe the Northern Spain. An ideal Spanish luxury trip takes place in the green Galicia, Basque country, Navarra kingdom, Rioja vineyards and Pyrenees.

    Look for the Guggenheim Museum, stay in San Sebastián- an elegant resort town and try the tapas bars and restaurants for a gastronomical experience in the alleyways.

    Read more

    Andalucia

    Think of traditional Spain and Andalucia will strike you. The bullrings, white villages, flamenco dancing and splendid beaches to lie down on, characterise the area. Its heart Andalucia lies in the city of Carmen, Seville. Watch out for a huge Cathedral and 'Giralda' tower, Morrish-style Alcázar Palace and the Barrio Santa Cruz with its labyrinth of perfumed streets and squares.

    Moorish Spain has for 800 years been celebrated by the existence of Granada. Islamic art and culture has flourished for a long time and you can see the specimen via its intricate plasterwork, tiling, carving and gardens of Granada's Alhambra. Also see a 14th century Palace fortress, the Alcázar de los Reyes Cristianos in Cordoba set in an old Jewish quarter.

    Lisbon

    Amongst the tourist places in Portugal, the capital- Lisbon ranks number one. Lying between the Moorish Alfama and the Bairro Alto at the mouth of Tagus River, Lisbon's major attractions are- Calouste Gulbenkian Museum, the National Museum of Decorative Tiles and St George's Castle. On the western edge is Belém from where discoverers set on sail.

    It is home to the National Coach Museum, the Monument to the Discoverers and the Jeronimos Monastery. Nearby Sintra is a World Heritage Site, Palácio Nacional de Sintra that is a summer retreat for the Portuguese kings. There is another such site named Palacio da Pena which is famous for its exquisite architecture.

    Read more

    Oporto

    Second to Lisbon is the city of Oporto. Try the Ribeira quayside district for its fashionable shopping street in the uptown or go sightseeing into the lanes of Oporto. Major attractions here are the Cathedral and the Palacio da Bolsa that has Arabian rooms but is now a stock exchange building.

    You can also wish to be escorted to the port wine lodges of Vila Nova de Gaia where we at A&K can even arrange some private visits. Extend your journey to the wine area of the Douro River valley and go boat cruising to not miss out on any scenic splendour.

    Read more

    Coimbra

    Coimbra lies between Oporto and Lisbon and is home to the country's oldest university which was found in 1920. It has the most beautiful library in Europe. Best to be explored on foot, this old town has an enchanting lot of stores and two cathedrals set on a steep hill.

    Try the afternoon tea at Bucaco Palace Hotel which was once a royal residence in the Bucaco National Forest.

    Read more

    Batalha

    While enroute to Lisbon, you can spot Portugal's two great religious foundations. One is the Dominican Abbey of Santa Maria da Vitoria in Batalha (UNESCO World Heritage Site) which is Gothic Portuguese architecture while the other is the Cistercian monastery of Alcobaca, the largest in the country.

    The Azores

    The Azores are comprised of nine tiny rugged islands. Come here to explore its natural volcanic history and to enjoy the activity of whale watching from May to October

    Posited in the Gulf Stream, these islands experience a warm and pleasant climate attracting visitors to enjoy the colourful flora. Stay on the islands of Faial and the capital São Miguel.

    Madeira

    Situated in the Gulf Stream like the Azores, Madeira enjoys a favourable climate and thus makes it a great winter destination. A mountainous and subtropical island, Madeira has rich plant life.

    it is famous for its wine industry and the fireworks show that happens during New Year celebrations. The island's capital-Funchal is most visited and is used as a base for cruise lines.
