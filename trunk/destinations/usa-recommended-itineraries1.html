CLASSIC NEW ENGLAND

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 14 Nights

    Highlights: Boston's Italian North End, bustling financial district, Freedom Trail, Manchester Village known for its mansions, art centres and other cultural activities, Newport, Rhode Island, Plymouth - "America's hometown" to see Plymouth Rock and the Mayflower II

  • Day 1-3

    Boston

    Following your flight you will be transferred into the city to The Fairmont Battery Wharf in historic Boston. The hotel is in a great location from which to explore this historic city. Boston's Italian North End, bustling financial district, Freedom Trail, Faneuil Hall Marketplace and TD Garden are all very close by..

  • Day 3-5

    Scarborough, Maine

    Collect your rental car and drive north leaving Boston to Portsmouth, New Hampshire. This small city has been a working port since colonial days. The Strawberry Bank area is one of America's oldest neighbourhoods. Continue up the coast to Maine. In Ogunquit take a leisurely walk along the Marginal Way, a magnificent mile-long footpath which follows the edge of the Atlantic atop rugged cliffs. Continue on to Scarborough where your first stop is the Black Point Inn.

  • Day 5-7

    Holderness, New Hampshire

    Today travel through Maine's picturesque lakes region and into New Hampshire's Squam Lake, best known for the film "On Golden Pond" with Henry Fonda and Katherine Hepburn. The location is also the setting for your two night stay at The Manor on Golden Pond.

  • Day 7-9

    Woodstock, Vermont

    Scenic routes and covered bridges will take you across into Vermont, the Green Mountain state. Today see Quechee Gorge, Vermont's "little Grand Canyon" before winding into Woodstock. A national US magazine named Woodstock "the prettiest small town in America". Your hotel for the next two nights is the wonderful Woodstock Inn.

  • Day 9-11

    Lenox, Massachusetts

    This morning travel through the Green Mountains to Manchester, Vermont. Manchester Village is known for its mansions, art centres and other cultural activities. Visit Hildene, the former estate of Robert Todd Lincoln, Abraham Lincoln's son. After lunch wind along the scenic Route 7, a famous drive for viewing autumn foliage, into Lenox for the next 2 nights. In Lenox visit Tanglewood, the Boston Symphony's summer home. In neighbouring Stockbridge visit the Norman Rockwell Museum, which houses his paintings of America's past. Stay at the grand 19th-Century country house of Wheatleigh.

  • Day 12

    Newport, Rhode Island

    Today drive through central Massachusetts and to Rhode Island's coast. Newport is where you will find the opulent summer homes of the Vanderbilts and the Astors, the church where John F Kennedy married Jacqueline Bouvier and the Old Stone Mill, believed to be built by the Vikings around 1100AD. Spend two nights in this waterfront town with so much history.

  • Day 13-14

    Chatham, Cape Cod

    Today continue into Plymouth, "America's hometown" to see Plymouth Rock and the Mayflower II, an exact replica of the ship that carried the pilgrims to the New World. Cross the Cape Cod Canal to a world of charm and beauty. For the next 2 days drive along the Cape Cod National Seashore to explore lighthouses, quaint coastal communities and natural scenic beauty. Visit the lively artist colony of Provincetown, take a whale watch, or just lay on one of the Cape's many sandy beaches. Your hotel for 2 nights is the wonderful Chatham Bars Inn. Chatham Bars Inn is an idyllic retreat on Cape Cod noted for its excellent service, fine cuisine and beautiful surroundings. The Inn is nestled near the heart of the village of Chatham and overlooks Pleasant Bay and the Atlantic Ocean.

  • Day 15

    Sandwich, Boston and flight to India

    Before returning to Boston, visit Sandwich and its notable Heritage Museum and gardens or the Sandwich Glass Museum, displaying a collection of cranberry glass, once the town's primary industry. Return your car at Boston airport before your overnight flight back to the India

DEEP SOUTH HOSPITALITY

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 14 Nights

    This is a great journey for those wanting an allround, authentic 'Deep South' experience.

    Highlights: Today explore your beautiful surroundings by foot, on horseback or by bike;Drive to Nashville, the capital of country music;Stay at the Monmouth Plantation...

  • Day 1

    India/Atlanta

    On arrival into Atlanta this evening, you'll collect your rental vehicle and drive to the St Regis hotel.

  • Day 2

    Atlanta

    The day is yours to explore the city of Atlanta. The main museum district is just a short stroll from your hotel.

  • Day 3

    Atlanta/Great Smoky Mountains

    This morning head out of Georgia to Blackberry Farm, where you'll spend the next two nights.

  • Day 4

    Great Smoky Mountains

    Today explore your beautiful surroundings by foot, on horseback or by bike. The activities on offer are endless, and allow you to enjoy the tranquility and splendour of this 4,200 acre estate.

  • Day 5

    Great Smoky Mountains/Nashville

    After breakfast, drive to Nashville, the capital of country music, and check into Loews hotel. Spend the afternoon immersing yourself in the music and the stories of the people who made it. Visit the 'Country Music Hall of Fame', which pays homage to country music's finest. Continue to RCA Studio B, where many of these artists recorded their most famous work.

  • Day 6

    Nashville/Memphis

    Today make your way towards Memphis, where Blues, Soul and Elvis Presley are the order of the day.

  • Day 7

    Memphis

    Today visit Graceland, once the home of Elvis Presley. The tour provides a fascinating (and surprising) insight into the life of the legendary star.

  • Day 8

    Memphis/Greenwood

    Cross the border into Mississippi and drive a couple of hours to Greenwood where The Help was filmed. Visit Tunica en route, considered the gateway to Blues Alley, and where Finding Graceland was filmed. Stop for some of Tunica's catfish before continuing onto the Alluvian in Greenwood.

  • Day 9

    Greenwood/Natchez

    Your journey continues as you travel down south to Natchez, where you will stay at the Monmouth Plantation, a small country mansion occupying an enviable position in Natchez. As the oldest town in Mississippi,Natchez has plenty of historical sites to visit and explore.

  • Day 10

    Natchez/Baton Rouge

    Your experience of the old plantations continues as you drive down into Louisiana, and its state capital Baton Rouge.

  • Day 11

    Baton Rouge/New Orleans

    After a leisurely breakfast, we recommend a trip to Houmas House Plantation. Their interactive tour takes you into the wealth, grandeur and lavish life of the Sugar Baron. The Greek Revival style mansion, twelve acres of formal gardens and two award winning restaurants give the impression of a wealthy plantation house.

  • Day 12

    New Orleans

    Located in the heart of downtown New Orleans, adjacent to the French Quarter, your hotel is only a short stroll to the music clubs of Bourbon Street.

  • Day 13

    New Orleans/Mobile

    Alabama is the next destination, with Mobile being the first stop. Not only was Mobile home to the first Mardi Gras in 1703, it is also host to America's family Mardi Gras - the celebrations here rival no other.

  • Day 14

    Mobile/Montgomery

    Spend the morning exploring Mobile, then head north towards Montgomery, home to Rosa Parks and a hotbed of political activity during the Civil Rights Movement of the 1960's.

  • Day 15

    Montgomery/Atlanta

    Head back to Atlanta for your evening flight back to India.
