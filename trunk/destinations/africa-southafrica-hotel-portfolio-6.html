Suggested Kruger & Mpumalanga accommodation

  • Lion Sands - Ivory Lodge

    The Lion Sands Ivory Lodge safari experience has an ever-changing, magical rhythm in a world of glimmering landscapes and unparalleled wildlife. Set on the banks of the Sabie River, a stay at Ivory Lodge ensures the ultimate private safari experience.

    There are six ultra luxurious suites with en suite bathroom, outdoor and indoor shower, courtyard, lounge with fireplace, wooden viewing deck, patio and private plunge pool. The bedroom and bathroom areas are air-conditioned and also have overhead fans. Each of the suites has breathtaking views over the Sabie River, where you can view game from the luxury of your own private deck, telescopes are provided if you don't have binoculars to hand.

    If you can manage to leave the luxury of your suite, you can accompany a ranger and tracker on a bush walk or view the 'Big Five' from your own private safari vehicle. The vehicles at Ivory Lodge are well designed with individual seats and storage boxes complete with binoculars, wildlife books and beanbags for steadying cameras. Private dining can be arranged in the comfort of your suite or at the indoor or outdoor dining areas. Enjoy a visit to the Lalamuka Health Spa - "Lalamuka" means unwind in Shangaan. For convenience, treatments can be offered on the deck of your suite overlooking the Sabie River. Ivory Lodge is an experience that promises to thrill, to revive the spirit and enliven long-dormant senses.

    Lion Sands - River Lodge

    Built on the banks of the Sabie River, this fourth generation family owned and managed property has exclusive traversing rights of the Lion Sands Private Game Reserve. Excellence in service have been passed down from generation to generation.

    Lion Sands River Lodge has 20 luxurious thatched bedrooms nestled amongst 800 year old trees and decorated in cool creams and beiges. All bedrooms have en suite bathrooms with indoor and outdoor showers, air-conditioning, ceiling fans, mini-bar and telephone access. The private wooden decks provide the perfect place to watch the rising sun over Kruger National Park. The rooms and public areas are linked via wooden walkways. The lounge and bar are classically decorated with African artefacts adorning the walls. For the more active there are two rim-flow swimming pools as well as a health spa and gymnasium.

    Near to the lodge, the river island deck provides a wonderful vantage point to quietly sit and watch the amazing bird life of the area. For the ultimate safari experience, why not sleep out under the stars at the Lion Sands Chalkley Tree House, a unique opportunity to get close to nature in a safe environment. Conservation is taken very seriously at Lion Sands and they are the only reserve in the Sabi Sands area to employ a full time ecologist, who is responsible for ensuring the preservation of the land.

    Little Garonga

    Little Garonga is the latest safari camp to opened in the Makalali Game Reserve. Just three luxury suites linked by pathways, Little Garonga was designed with exclusive use in mind.

    The Hambleden Suite forms the heart of Little Garonga and doubles up as a family suite with a separate childrens room. Pathways link The Hambleden Suite with two further fabulous suites, namely The Buckingham and The Chiltern. In total the camp sleeps six adults and two children. With its own boma, massage sala, dedicated guide, vehicle, chef and host you can be assured a personal touch and complete privacy.

    Londolozi Tree Camp

    Said to be the most stylish family run camp in Africa, just six suites ensure an intimate bush experience. The ultimate in opulent indulgence relax on wooden decks with a glass of champagne in hand as you survey the ancient landscape below.

    Elegance and simplicity are the hallmarks of Tree Camp and can be seen in every detail and feature, from the private swimming pools and salas, to the Ralph Lauren wallpaper and chocolate plaited leather beds. Spectacular game viewing and exceptional leopard sightings are commonplace in this rich wild wonderland. Conservation has not been forgotten and many land management as well as animal protection projects are in operation.

  • Londolozi Varty Camp

    Founded in 1926, Varty is the original camp at Londolozi. The camp fire has burned in the same spot where the Varty forefathers set up their tents 82 years ago and embodies the significance of this camp and the Varty family's custodianship.

    Today's camp comprises of eight chalets and two superior chalets, each with private plunge pool and elevated decks with expansive river views. The camp has a down to earth and comfortable feel with emphasis on family heritage, this means that children have always had a place at Londolozi and in particular at Varty.

    Mala Mala Main Camp

    Main Camp at Mala Mala is set in beautiful surroundings; the ochre coloured buildings are clustered amidst shady trees and manicured lawns overlooking the Sand River.

    The 18 individually decorated, spacious and comfortable rooms and suites are traditionally thatched and enjoy spectacular views. Despite the comfort offered, Mala Mala retains an authentic camp atmosphere, where the attention to detail for which Mala Mala is world famous is obvious. Fantastic cuisine is served in an authentic African manner, under the stars in a reed enclosed boma, a tradition for over 60 years. The guiding at Mala Mala sets it apart from the rest as experienced rangers will bring your safari to life.
