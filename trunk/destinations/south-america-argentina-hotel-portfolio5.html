Suggested Patagonia accommodation

  • Casa de los Sauces

    Built in ten acres of parkland on the banks of the Calafate River, Casa de Los Sauces is an authentic Patagonian lodge offering 18 guest rooms in three elegant houses, all individually furnished.

    Opened in September 2007, Casa do los Sauces is a luxury hotel in El Calafate, the gateway to Parque Nacional Los Glaciares. Defined by rustic charm, it recreates the hospitality and spirit of the Patagonian farms of the beginning of the last century, with an architectural style that combines rustic features with European influences.

    The Casa has 18 guest rooms in three elegant houses, all individually furnished and with reading rooms warmed by Patagonian stone fireplaces. They are wonderfully quiet, cosy spaces.

    Australis Expedition Cruises

    Cruceros Australis is an expedition cruise line offering three and four day itineraries, cruising the stunning southernmost fjords of Chile and Argentina, inaccessible by road or plane.

    Both the Via Australis and the Stella Australis are very well appointed vessels, with a host of facilities. They are perfect way to experience the trip of a lifetime - whether a three-day cruise from Ushuaia, Argentina, to Punta Arenas, Chile, or a four-day cruise from Punta Arenas to Ushuaia. Highlights include sailing through the Beagle Channel and the Strait of Magellan whilst also stopping at Cape Horn, the southernmost tip of South America.

    Eolo

    Eolo sits in splendid isolation in the vast scenery of Patagonia. Eolo is designed to emphasise the sheer beauty of the surrounding area. Simple and unassuming on the outside, it sits perfectly within the vastness of Patagonia - making the most of the landscapes and views with terraces and courtyards open to huge skies.

    The rooms too exude an understated charm. Simple yet elegant, they do not even attempt to compete with the views from over-sized windows. Instead, they provide calm, homely spaces in which to unwind after a long day of exploration - though all the modern conveniences are in evidence.

    Hosteria Alta Vista

    Located between the Glaciers National Park and the town of El Calafate lies the Hosteria Alta Vista which forms a part of Estancia Anita, the biggest sheep farm in the area. Hosteria Alta Vista forms a part of Estancia Anita, the biggest sheep farm in the area at around 165,000 acres. A charming rural retreat, it exudes an intimate, cosy atmosphere and is a great base from which to explore the nearby glaciers and discover more about traditional Patagonian ways of life.

    Originally an inn constructed in the 1930s, it has been extensively updated, without compromising on a range of lovely original features - the façade, windows and old doors have been retained. The interiors have been more recently refurbished to offer a cosy, warm environment, which is very welcome at the end of a day spent in the great outdoors.

    Los Notros

    Hidden in the forests of Los Glaciares National Park, Los Nostros offers a welcome respite from the rugged steppes of Patagonia. Los Nostros is a perfect base from which to reach the glaciers and lakes for which the region is famous. It stands in a privileged position right opposite Perito Moreno glacier - the world's only advancing glacier - and, as such, affords truly unforgettable views.

    The hotel itself is comfortable but not ostentatious. On the outside, it resembles a forest lodge, with its wood paneling and stone foundations. Inside, it is cosy and homely, with exposed wood detailing dominating the décor throughout the public areas. The rooms are simple, yet comfortable, with décor based on wooden ceilings, walls in mustard yellow, vivid reds or greens, tasteful furnishings in muted shades and natural fabrics.
