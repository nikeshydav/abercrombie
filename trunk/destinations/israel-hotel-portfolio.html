AMERICAN COLONY HOTEL, JERUSALEM

  • AMERICAN COLONY HOTEL, JERUSALEM

    The American Colony Hotel has been providing luxurious hospitality for more than 120 years. Personified by classic Arabian arches, elaborate furnishings and opulent suites, this elegant boutique hotel prides itself in offering guests an enchanting 'east-meets-west' experience. The hotel retains considerable period charm and character.

    Consisting of 86 rooms in three separate buildings: The Main Building, the East House and the Palm House, each building with its own garden. All rooms are designed to provide guests with luxury, comfort and convenience. Superior rooms are located primarily in the main building, upgrade to a Pasha Classic with its Ottoman architecture of the 1800's or the Pasha Deluxe which were built for three wives of the Turkish Pasha.

    Five non-kosher restaurants and bars, each with their own ambiance. The Arabesque with its continental and Arabic cuisine, Val's Brasserie Lounge beside the pool is the perfect spot for afternoon tea

    HIGHLIGHTS

    • Legendary get-away in the heart of Jerusalem
    • Elegant boutique hotel in a distinctively tranquil and romantic setting
    • Within walking distance of all of Jerusalem's major sights and within ten minutes of the Damascus Gate in the Old City.
    • Heated outdoor pool May - October & fitness centre with sauna
    • Courtyard garden that between April and October is the place of choice for a light lunch or evening drink
    • We recommend the Pasha Classic rooms a small price to step back in time to the 1800's

  • DAN HOTEL, TEL AVIV

    The Dan Tel Aviv Hotel reflects Tel Aviv's dynamic lifestyle. Stay at the Dan Hotel and any major attraction in the city is a walk away. Tel Aviv's famous nightlife is literally across the road. Follow the curve of the promenade and you are in romantic Jaffa filled with bobbing fishing boats in an ancient harbour, with a romantic artists' quarter and flea market waiting to be explored.

    The Dan is housed behind an iconic, Technicolor façade courtesy of Israeli artist YaacovAgam. Inside, look for original 1960s and 1970s furniture, an ultra-informed concierge, a full-service spa and a beachside towel service in summer. If the beach looks crowded, head for the sanctuary of the Dan's own outdoor seawater pool and sun deck.

    Sea-facing or city view, the 280 Dan Tel Aviv rooms and suites are all equipped with Wi-Fi, iPod connections and subtle olive-coloured furnishings. There's a trio of seafront suites with private balconies, while the aptly named Sea View Executive Rooms come with prime Mediterranean views.

    HIGHLIGHTS

    • Overlooking the Mediterranean and splendid beaches edged by the city's famous seafront promenade
    • Designer stores, commercial centres and the city's famed after-dark attractions are all on the doorstep
    • Hayarkon 99, the flagship restaurant with formal classic dining in an intimate atmosphere
    • Indoor and outdoor swimming pools with sweet and salt water
    • Danyland Club for children with a separate children's swimming pool

  • DAVID INTERCONTINENTAL, TEL AVIV

    Sleep well in the 550 comfortable spacious rooms and suites, which overlook the sea and city. For relaxation or recreation the hotel offers a fabulous outdoor swimming pool, children's pool and sun deck that serve as the summer venue for events. For indoor activities all hotel guests can use the fully equipped fitness centre free of charge.

    The recently refurbished Jaffa Court offers a vast buffet with international selections as well as a breakfast terrace while the equally renovated Aubergine serves a variety of á la carte options. The new Atrium Lobby Lounge and outdoor terrace bar serves drinks and a light menu, the Palm Pool Bar has an open-grill to go with its cocktails. For late-night entertainment, visit the Inca Cigar and Sports Bar.

    Rikki's Spa is the ultimate retreat for body and soul. The nine treatment rooms offer the finest in Shiatsu, Ayurveda, Thai or Swedish massage from a well trained professional staff.

    HIGHLIGHTS

    • Modern luxury hotel located overlooking the sea in south Tel Aviv
    • Close to sandy beaches
    • All rooms are identical: large, well equipped and with a view of the sea
    • Close to old Jaffa and the Old Jaffa Railway station now redeveloped as a centre for boutiques, café and artists
    • Great for those wanting to be in a modern hotel in a fashionable district of Tel Aviv
    • Seasonal children's club for younger guests

  • THE KING DAVID HOTEL, JERUSALEM

    The King David hotel Jerusalem, a six-storey landmark opened in 1931, offers 283 guest rooms and suites. All uniquely designed to fit the special architectural design details of the building, many of the rooms are overlooking the unique old city walls of Jerusalem. Stately pink quartz exteriors and exotic public rooms are decorated with motifs that recreate a bygone romantic era. Superbly located on an elevated site, the King David Hotel Jerusalem offers incomparable views of the walls, minarets and domes of the Old City.

    Spacious private gardens that enclose green lawns, a tennis court, adults' and children's swimming pools (heated during the season), as well as a children's playground and quiet corners for relaxing. After an invigorating swim, the ideal way to savour the beautiful garden setting is at the pool bar over a refreshing drink or a light meal. Exercise options include a sleek and contemporary new Fitness Centre offering state-of-the-art training equipment by TechnoGym coupled with a Spa with a wide range of skin and body treatments. Play a game of tennis or relax in the Finnish sauna after wandering in the Old Town.

    HIGHLIGHTS

    • The King David Hotel is a unique landmark in the heart of Jerusalem
    • Within walking distance to the Old Town
    • Rooms have views of the New City or Old City
    • Extensive lawns, gardens and pool area - one of the best in Jerusalem
    • La Regence is one of the finest dining experiences in Jerusalem
    • We recommend the Deluxe New City View rooms and the Deluxe Old City View rooms due to the added space and option of a balcony

  • THE SCOTS HOTEL, TIBERIAS

    The luxurious Scots hotel was established by the Scottish Church in 1999 in the historical compound of the Scottish Hospital, and uniquely combines the 19th century basalt buildings with a modern and up-to-date hotel with 68 rooms located in the heart of Tiberias.

    The hotel comes with layers of history and character that no other hotel in the area can match. The public areas and gardens have a colonial atmosphere and the hotel has a Ceilidh Bar with 80 types of whisky on offer and a spa boasting a range of treatments.

    Unique rooms are of uniform modern standard. Antique rooms located in the Doctor's House are steeped in history and boast exposed basalt stone walls and high ceilings. The Antique rooms with Lake View are well worth the extra.

    Extending down to the Sea of Galilee and across to the hotel's swimming pool, the beach garden is a great place to relax.

    Enjoy an archaeological visit on your doorstep. The path starts at the restaurant's balcony and ends at the Visitors' Centre via the Gazebo Terraces. The path includes relics from the Roman and Byzantine periods; these relics have been kept in their natural surroundings for over 2,000 years.

    The hotel's restaurant Torance is situated in the David building which was originally the maternity hospital. You can eat on the restaurant's balcony surrounded by lush gardens, almost all year round. The combination of Israeli cuisine with Galilean cooking tradition and influences from Scotland contributes to a flavour-rich culinary experience, turning every meal into an unforgettable gourmet journey. The various dishes use local produce, herbs grown in the hotel's garden, cheeses, fruit and vegetables, homemade jams and a selection of wines all from the farms of the North.

    HIGHLIGHTS

    • Set in beautiful gardens with an unrivalled view of the Sea of Galilee
    • Steeped in history and charm
    • Half sized Olympic pool and located in the beach garden with magnificent views
    • Israeli cuisine with Galilean cooking tradition and influences from Scotland
    • We recommend the Antique rooms as they are more luxurious
