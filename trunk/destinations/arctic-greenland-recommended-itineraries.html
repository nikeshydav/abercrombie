GREENLAND GLACIER EXPERIENCE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 3 Nights

    Highlights: huge icebergs rising more than a hundred meters above sea level; enjoy an Ice Fjord cruise; spend an unforgettable night out on the Icecap.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Ilulissat

    Arrive in Ilulissat via Iceland by flight. Ilulissat is breathtaking - all year round, huge icebergs rising more than a hundred meters above sea level are stranded at the mouth of the fjord. The icebergs are products of the mightiest, most productive glacier in the northern hemisphere: the Ilulissat Glacier which produces 20 million tons of ice daily.

    Transfer to Hotel Arctic, a modern hotel in a sublime location. Your guide will introduce you to the area including the old part of town, and its beginnings as a Danish trading station, the busy fishing harbor, the old church and the museum.

    Enjoy an Ice Fjord cruise the same day. The Ice Fjord contains one of the biggest and most active glaciers in the world. Its 3,000 km2 area advances at a rate of 20 meters per day. The Ice Fjord off Ilulissat resembles a giant park full of sculptures constantly changing. Shapes, colours and sounds from the mountains of ice are hard to forget - you are an eyewitness to a totally enchanting natural phenomenon.

  • Day 2

    Rodebay

    Following breakfast at the hotel it's time to put on polar suits for the dog sled ride with a local hunter to the hunters' settlement Rodebay. Experience the magnificence of nature and witness the cooperation between the sled driver and the dog team. Tonight you'll spend an unforgettable night out at our expedition camp right out on the Icecap. You'll definitely need all the extra polar coats and polar sleeping bags provided. The Northern lights illuminate the night skies, making Rodebay a favourite location in the summer as well as winter. With around 300 days a year under cloudless skies, Greenland is one of the best places in the world to see the northern lights.

  • Day 3

    Rodebay / Ilulissat

    Spend the morning exploring the icecap with its deep blue beauty. Breakfast in Rodebay before we drive back to Ilulissat.

  • Day 4

    Depart Greenland

    Transfer to airport for departure by plane to Iceland.

GREENLAND IGLOO ADVENTURE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 3 Nights

    Highlights: Polar bear excursion; meet and experience the lives of the Inuit; helicopter flight over the coastline, mountains and fjords.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Greenland

    After landing in Iceland from the UK, fly onto Scoresbysund in Greenland, the longest fjord in the world. The flight to Greenland from Iceland takes about 2 hours, so we land at Constable Point around 10:00 local time. Meet your local guides, who will go through all the necessary equipment needed for the expeditions ahead; as well as how to behave around the teams of sled dogs, which are waiting for you outside the airport. Jump onto the sleds, which take you out into the frozen fjord on your way to base camp for the next couple of days: the Igloo Village.

    The four hour journey is breath-taking with mountains towering on one side and gigantic icebergs on the other. Be on the look-out for local wildlife: you never know what kind of creatures you'll run into on the ice.

  • Day 2

    Polar Bears

    Today is dedicated to discovering the stunning polar bear. You'll drive out onto the ice on dog sleds, heading towards the area where local guides will have been out on the lookout for the bears. Out on the ice there's the chance to sight other arctic wildlife: arctic foxes, arctic hare, musk oxen, wolves, seals and even walruses. The exhilaration of the journey is focused on spotting a family of bears out on the ice. Your guides will be able to introduce these fascinating and at times fearsome creatures.

  • Day 3

    Kap Tobin

    Your dog sleds will take you out today to meet and experience the lives of the Inuit. You'll first visit the old deserted village of Kap Tobin. Walk among the abandoned houses of this ghost town where your guides will explain what made the people abandon it, leaving so much behind.

    Drive along the coastline until you reach the village of Ittoqqortoormiit, where you will stay for your last night in Greenland. We will then have a chance to walk around the village and examine how the Inuit live. After spending some time mingling with the locals and getting a true understanding of life, there's a dinner with some of the local people.

  • Day 4

    Depart Greenland

    After breakfast, the groups are transported back to the airport by helicopter. This 15-minute flight is really something: flying between the breathtaking mountains, along the coastline and over the beautiful frozen fjord where you started your trip. Fly back to Iceland, arriving in Reykjavik before transferring to a flight to the UK or staying sometime in Iceland.

ARCTIC ADVENTURE: POLAR BEAR COUNTRY

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 15 Nights

    Join A&K's seasoned expedition team for an enriching experience like no other to the islands of the High Arctic Svalbard archipelago in Norway, less than 600 miles from the geographic North Pole. Observe the amazing flora that comes alive with unexpected colour under July's midnight sun as our quest takes us in search of the mighty polar bear. Discover Greenland's Bronze Age villages, Inuit settlements and fishing enclaves and search for local wildlife, ending your journey amid the contrast of pastoral tranquillity and stunning volcanoes of Iceland.

    Join our polar bear expert, Mats Forsberg aboard Le Boreal for an unforgettable inaugural voyage to the Arctic. Mats Forsberg has led most of the world's leading polar bear researchers into the extreme Arctic wilderness. An expert on polar bear tracking and observation, Mats has even guided BBC production teams into the region's polar bear habitat.

    Cruise No: LB130717
    Departure Dates: 16-30 July 2013

  • Day 16th July

    London/Oslo, Norway

    Arrive in Oslo, Norway's capital, a mixture of the ancient and the modern, coexisting in close harmony with nature. On arrival, you will be met and transferred to your hotel. This afternoon, meet your local guide for a walking tour. Join fellow travellers this evening for a welcome cocktail reception.

  • Day 17th July

    Oslo/Longyearbyen/Spitsbergen

    Board a charter flight to Longyearbyen, the capital of Svalbard on the main island of Spitsbergen, the sovereign Arctic archipelago of Norway. It is one of the most remote archipelagos on the globe, with nearly two-thirds covered by spectacular glaciers. After a tour of the town, board 'Le Boreal.'

  • Day 18-21st July

    Svalbard Islands

    Very few people have had the opportunity to view these remote Arctic islands and the polar bears that reside in this remote wilderness. In the Svalbard archipelago, polar bears, walrus, musk oxen and an incredibly rich variety of birds are as abundant as cathedrals of ice, glaciers and craggy mountain peaks.Your captain and expedition crew take advantage of local conditions to determine the day's best sightseeing, which may include visits to stunning fjords, a polar research station or a 17th-century Dutch whaling station. As you explore along the Arctic sea ice, search for polar bears as they hunt in their true natural habitat.

  • Day 22-23rd July

    Cruising the Greenland Sea

    Cruise along the Arctic ice edge and through the Greenland Sea, as lecturers recap their presentations on the wildlife, history and geology of the Svalbard and prepare you for the world's largest island: Greenland.

  • Day 24-26th July

    Scoresby Sound/East Greenland

    Explore the rarely visited east coast of Greenland and Scoresby Sound, disembarking in the village of Ittoqqortoormiit. Cruise the intricate fjord network and be on the lookout for musk ox and reindeer. We also plan to visit Uunartoq (the "warming island") discovered in 2005 when Greenland's glaciers receded. Conditions permitting, take a dip in the hot springs on this "new" island.

  • Day 27th July

    Cruising the Denmark Strait

    Enjoy a relaxing day at sea, watching for seabirds and whales from the deck. This evening, gather for the captain's gala farewell dinner.

  • Day 28th July

    Isajardardjur/Vigur Island

    Visit Iceland's Drangajokull Glacier with its sparkling waterfalls and icebergs; the small island of Vigur, home to colonies of puffin and eider; and the village of Isafjordur, where Iceland's fishing heritage is alive and well. Please note that all landings in Iceland locations may be subject to change, weather conditions permitting.

  • Day 29th July

    Breidafjordur, Iceland

    Round the Sneaefellsnes Peninsula to cruis to scenic Breidafjordur, a habitat for seabirds. You also see the extinct volcano of Snaefellsjokull, setting of Jules Verne's Journey to the Center of the Earth.

  • Day 30th July

    Reykjavik, Iceland

    This morning, disembark Le Boreal in Reykjavik as you transfer to the airport for your return flights or enjoy an extended stay in Iceland.
