Suggested Victoria accommodation

  • Chateau Yering Hotel

    In the heart of the Yarra Valley lies iconic Chateau Yering. The hotel was reborn in 1997 as a superb five star luxury property with period fittings throughout, and offering luxury accommodation, service and dining.

    The hotel's elegant interiors are the work of designer, Elly Milner, who was tasked with creating a style that reflected the history of the building (which dates back to the mid-17th Century) whilst offering the comfort and convenience of an upscale hotel. Accordingly, the sumptuous interiors showcase fabrics, furnishings, furniture and exquisite objet d'art from Europe and the UK - their textures, colours and lighting creating an ambience of refined, indulgent grandeur.

    The 35 luxury period suites, including River and Stable Suites, follow a similar theme. Bringing together old world elegance with modern amenities, they are wonderfully cosy and redolent with a sense of history - whilst large windows ensure light floods throughout their spacious interiors. All have large comfortable beds, well chosen antique furniture and luxurious marble bathrooms.

    Chateau Yering Hotel

    Set on the Great Ocean Road, directly opposite a beautiful sandy beach and surrounded by rolling countryside Chocolate Gannets' 5 star self contained Sea Villas provide luxury accommodation with spectacular views of the southern ocean and its ever changing moods and hues.

    Each Villa can accommodate up to 4 guests. Clean, crisp, and beautifully decorated, the villas have double spa baths overlooking rolling waves, open fires, heating/aircon, leather sofas, fully equipped kitchens and laundry rooms. Floor-to-ceiling glass windows opening onto expansive decked areas offer stunning ocean views.

    This contemporary hotel is just metres from the beach, where guests can start the day horse riding or, later, walk to Apollo Bay to explore this quaint town and its restaurants.

    Claerwen Retreat

    Claerwen is a unique holiday retreat. Set in stunning natural surroundings, it offers a holiday experience focused on relaxation, self-discovery and personal growth. It is also a great spot from which to explore Victoria's dramatic west coast - the beautiful beaches of Apollo Bay, the Otway rainforests, surf beaches like Moonlight Head and Johanna, and The Twelve Apostles are close at hand.

    Its guesthouse and two cottages are nestled in 130 acres of bush and fern gullies. Simply decorated in pure white and with wooden floors, all the studios, suites and cottages are elegantly relaxed spaces - and all offer superb views from the retreat's elevated location. All are well equipped with kitchen, dining areas and en suite bathrooms.

    Park Hyatt, Melbourne

    Sophisticated and contemporary, the Park Hyatt offers an exclusive sanctuary in the heart of Melbourne. It is the perfect base from which to explore this beautiful city and surrounding areas.

    The rooms are havens of luxury amidst the bustle of historic Melbourne, featuring Madrona paneled walls and rich furnishings with a hint of art deco. Room amenities reflect the hotel's exceptional standards, and include plush beds, beautiful furniture and Italian marble bathrooms with deep soaking tub and walk-in shower.

    The Lake House

    Lake House lies on the shores of beautiful Lake Daylesford. Walking tracks lead from the property, around the lake and into the Wombat State Forest - which is home to diverse local fauna and the abundant mineral springs the area is renowned for. The nearest one bubbles out right on Lake House's doorstep.

    The hotel fits perfectly with the area's sophisticated bohemian feel, afforded by an eclectic mix of artist's studios, cafes, award winning restaurants, boutique day spas and holistic healing centres.

    The rooms and suites exemplify this spirit, with beautiful interiors enlivened with original art and craft pieces. All have either garden or lake views.
