Rwanda safaris & luxury holidays


A tiny, landlocked republic in Africa, Rwanda lies cradled on the eastern rim of the Albertine Rift and the watershed between Africa's two largest river systems: the Nile and the Congo. The mountainous country enjoys a moist, temperate climate all year round, making it an ideal holiday destination. Often called the "land of a thousand hills", Rwanda has a number of unique delights to offer to travelers. Travel to Rwanda and unearth the hidden gem with tailor-made itineraries with Abercrombie and Kent.

Bidding easy access to the adjoining countries of Uganda, Tanzania, Burundi and Democratic Republic of Congo as well as to Kenya, Rwanda is an ideal location for travel within the region as a visit here can easily expand to include many of these great African destinations. Being amazingly diverse, holidays in Rwanda are the perfect blend of spectacular landscape and rich wildlife. Misty, moody rainforest in the south gives rise to lush, forested volcanoes in the north. Enclosed by step terraced hillsides, Lake Kivu is a wonderfully bubbling inland sea whereas the undulated, grassy plains of Akagera in the east are interspersed with light, acacia woodlands. Abode to one-third of the world's remaining mountain gorillas, one-third of Africa's bird species, several species of primates, volcanoes, game reserves, artistic crafts and warm, sociable people, this South African country attracts a huge number of tourists every year.

Originally the domain of hunter gatherers, trekking gorilla in Rwanda is seen by many as the best adventure on the planet, and no trip to Africa would be complete without sighting gorillas in their mist in Rwanda's national parks.

Weather
The driest months in Rwanda are between July and September and from December to January. The rains tend to fall between April and May and then again from October to November. However, trekking for gorillas involves entering the rainforests so you need to be prepared to get wet and muddy most of the year.
