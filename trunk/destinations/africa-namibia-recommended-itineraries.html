Desert Discovery

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 Nights

    Highlights: A unique safari experience, Deserts, mountains and savannah, Giant dunes and the oldest desert on earth, Exceptional photographic opportunities, Remote north-west of Namibia, Dramatic landscapes, Visit the famous Himba Tribe, Walking in remote mountain and river valleys

  • Day 1

    Arrive Windhoek

    Welcome to the capital of Namibia and transfer to your hotel.

  • Day 2

    Windhoek - Sossusvlei

    An exciting day as your journey begins with a light aircraft flight south into the private 37,000 hectare Kulala Wilderness Reserve - the gateway to Namibia's Sand Sea with its towering dunes and clear starry skies. Here you will find desert-adapted wildlife such as ostrich, springbok and gemsbok. Smaller creatures such as bat-eared fox and aardwolf can be seen at night in the cool desert air. One bird, the aptly named dune lark, has its entire global distribution limited to the area, so dependent is it on the area's characteristic sands.

    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Sossusvlei

    There is much to see in this pristine wilderness area including the spectacular dunes at Sossusvlei which are among the highest in the world. Activities include a morning visit to the towering dunes, scenic nature drives by open 4WD vehicle and guided nature walks.

    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Sossusvlei - Ongava Game Reserve

    Today's fight via Swakopmund takes you to your camp for the next two nights, located in the much-lauded Ongava Game Reserve to the south of the Etosha National Park. Etosha is Namibia's premier wildlife destination and one of Africa's largest game reserves. In dry season large herds of wildlife teem around the waterholes and the endless plains offer breathtaking vistas. The Ongava Game Reserve shares a boundary with the Etosha National Park.

    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Ongava Game Reserve

    Activities revolve around day and night drives, visiting hides that overlook waterholes and walks with experienced guides. The Ongava Game Reserve features much of the characteristic wildlife of the area, with both desert-adapted black and white rhino to be seen as well as lion. Game drives and daytrips into the easily accessible Okaukuejo area of Etosha National Park are rewarding, with possible sightings of lion, elephant, gemsbok, springbok, red hartebeest, and white and black rhino. The Ongava Game Reserve is unique in that it is one of the few private game reserves in southern Africa where there is a chance of seeing both black and white rhino. For those who enjoy close wildlife encounters, tracking white rhino on foot with a guide is a highlight not to be missed.

    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Ongava Game Reserve - Hartmann Valley

    This morning join your scheduled flight from Etosha to Serra Cafema Camp in the extreme north-west of Namibia on the banks of the Kunene River in the Hartmann Valley. The Kunene River is the only permanent source of water in this region and creates a lush oasis along its banks - a winding band of green surrounded by the lunar landscape of the Namib Desert which stretches to the Serra Cafema mountain range in the north. Your base for the next three nights is an intimate and peaceful tented camp with a unique mix of rustic and luxury elements nestled amongst the shady Albida trees on the banks of the Kunene. Rapids just below camp provide a calming ambiance with the lulling sounds of the gurgling waters soporific after a day exploring one of the driest, most starkly beautiful regions in the world.

    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7-8

    Hartmann Valley

    Two full days to explore. Activities revolve around the exploration of the surrounding Namib Desert sands and the lush oases along the river, a journey of contrasts. Informative nature drives and carefully guided quad bike excursions through the fragile dunes allow guests to experience the true Namib in its unbridled glory. The utmost care is taken to tread lightly on the dunes and preserve the unique and delicate habitats of this landscape. Boating on the Kunene River provides guests with the opportunity to venture through the lush riverside oases. Crocodiles and water birds seem out of place in this surreal, lunar-like landscape. Walking in the remote mountain and river valleys is also a highlight, as is a visit to a Himba settlement - one of the last truly nomadic people in Africa. The fairy circle phenomenon is also best viewed in the Hartmann Valley .

    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 9

    Hartmann Valley - Windhoek

    This morning join your flight to Windhoek International airport where your adventure ends.

    Meal Plan: B

THE CALL OF THE NAMIB

Programme Overview

  • Traverse the starkly beautiful, towerings and dunes of Sossusvlei, perhaps trekking by foot and on an ATV and searching for its unique and hardy wildlife.

    Duration: 5 Days/4 Nights

    Highlights: Sossusvlei | Serra Cafema

    A&K ADVANTAGE

      • Get an adrenalin rush with dune bashing at Sossusvlei.
      • Traverse the desert sand dunes in a 4WD Land Cruiser.
      • Take part in boating on the Kunene River and watch out for the Kunene crocodiles.
      • Enjoy a visit by the native Ovahimba families and also visit the Himba Community to get an opportunity to learn about their lifestyle and traditions.
      • Private transfers arranged for arrival and departure.
      • The services of an A&K Representative*

      * An A&K Representative is always at hand to provide unparalleled 24/7 support, be it local travel tips or reconfirming arrangement details, to almost anything. Should you ever need help or advice, dial your A&K Representative’s mobile phone for service at any time
    • Detailed Itinerary

      Day 1

      ARRIVAL WINDHOEK – SOSSUSVLEI

      On arrival at the Windhoek International Airport, an A&K Representative will meet and assist you to connect to your shared aircraft for Sossusvlei. On arrival at the Sossusvlei Airstrip a representative will meet and transfer you to the lodge. The Kulala Desert Lodge lies in the heart of the Namib Desert, bordering the Namib Naukluft Park and offers magnificent views of the famous red dunes of Sossusvlei and vast open plains. The fifteen thatch-and-canvas chalets with en-suite bathrooms and showers are set on wooden platforms to provide maximum airflow. For outdoor enthusiasts, bedrolls can be placed on the rooftop for sleeping under the stars. Settle in and perhaps get a look at some of the activities on offer.

      Overnight: Gold Class – NA; Silver Class – Kulala Desert Lodge
      Meal Plan: D

    • Day 2

      SOSSUSVLEI

      Depart for an early- morning guided game drive to the spectacular dunes through a private gate on the Tsauchab River and indulge in an activity specific to this area- dune bashing at Sossusvlei. Return to the lodge for lunch and then the rest of your day is at leisure.

      Overnight: Gold Class – NA; Silver Class – Kulala Desert Lodge
      Meal Plan: B,L,D

    • Day 3

      SERRA CAFEMA

      Time permitting enjoy a morning activity. Early morning ballooning is highly recommended! After breakfast you will be transferred to the Sossusvlei Air strip for your private light aircraft transfer to Serra Cafema. On arrival at Serra Cafema airstrip, you will be met by a camp representative and transferred to the camp. Serra Cafema Camp is one of the most remote camps in all of Southern Africa. The small 16 bedded camp, whose only access is by aircraft, is located in the extreme north-west of Namibia. The camp is situated under big shady Albida trees overlooking the Kunene River and comprises of 8 canvas and thatched chalets that are raised off the ground, each with its own en suite bathroom. Activities include enjoying the breath-taking landscape scenes with Springbok, Ostrich and Oryx dotted here and there, traversing the sand dunes in 4WD Landrovers and boating on the Kunene River where you can watch for the Kunene crocodiles. Serra Cafema is often visited by the native Ovahimba families who live in the nearby vicinity. You also visit the Himba Community and this isolated area where the Himba people continue their traditional semi-nomadic way of life and guests have an opportunity to learn about their lifestyle and customs, when they are in the area. The evening is at leisure to unwind.

      Overnight: Gold Class – Serra Cafema Camp; Silver Class – NA
      Meal Plan: B,L,D

    • Day 4

      SERRA CAFEMA

      The day is at leisure to enjoy the activities on offer. Quad biking Excursion, Boating trips on the Kunene River, Nature drives and walks in Hartmann's Valley or a full-day outing to Marienfluss Valley with a packed picnic lunch are some options you could choose from.

      Overnight: Gold Class – Serra Cafema Camp; Silver Class – NA
      Meal Plan: B,L,D

    • Day 5

      DEPART SERRA CAFEMA

      Time permitting enjoy an activity and breakfast, after which you will be transferred to the Serra Cafema airstrip on your wilderness air private light aircraft transfer to Windhoek International Airport. On arrival at Windhoek, connect on to your onward flight.

      Meal Plan: B
