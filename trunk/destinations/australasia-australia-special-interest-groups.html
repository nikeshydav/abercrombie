Pristine Waters of Ningaloo Reef

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 days

    Take a scenic Flight over Cape Range National Park, Be amazed at Ningaloo Marine Park with opportunities for guided Snorkelling and Kayaking or enjoy the great outdoors with Bush Walks and Quad biking. Swimming with Whale Sharks is available March to July.

    Highlights: Fly return Perth to Learmonth. Scenic Flight over Cape Range National Park Ningaloo Marine Park. Guided Snorkelling, Kayaking, Bush Walks & Quad biking. Swimming with Whale Sharks (March to July)

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE PERTH

    Meet your guide for a private sightseeing tour including a Swan River cruise.

    The Swan River is Perth's natural heartbeat - the city's location right on the river banks makes the capital one of the most beautiful in the world. The view of the river and city from Kings Park is among Western Australia's most scenic - city sky scrapers shining against a brilliant blue Perth sky, green parklands and the shimmering blue river combine to create a stunning vista.

    Overnight: The Richardson Hotel, Deluxe Room

  • Day 2

    PERTH - CAPE RANGE NATIONAL PARK

    This morning fly to Exmouth then board a charter plane for 20 minute scenic flight over the impressive limestone plateaus of Cape Range National Park. Hidden in the dunes discover Sal Salis with just eight eco wilderness tents complete with surprisingly luxurious comforts. All meals, beverages and guided activities are included and an underwater garden lies just metres from the pristine white sand. Sunset drinks and dinner are served with views to the reef.

    Overnight: Sal Salis, Wilderness Tent
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    NINGALOO REEF: SWIM WITH THE WHALE SHARKS

    Embark on an adventurous Whale Shark cruise aboard a luxurious vessel. Snorkel Ningaloo Reef and relax with a gourmet lunch on board.

    The Whale Shark is the largest member of the shark family, the largest fish in the world and suprisingly, a harmless plankton eater! Reported to reach 18 metres in length, the whale shark is easily identified by its pattern of white spots. Thought to mainly inhabit worldwide temperate and tropical seas, it is rarely seen in shallow coastal water, but is a daily visitor to the Ningaloo Reef between late March and July. Up to 12 Whale Sharks have been spotted close to the reef feeding (Whale Shark tours operate March - July). Manta Rays with wingspans several metres wide, as well as a variety of tropical fish and turtles can be seen year round.

    Overnight: Sal Salis, Wilderness Tent
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    CORAL BAY

    Collect your vehicle in Exmouth and drive to Coral Bay, the southern gateway of Ningaloo Reef. Board a one hour Sub-Sea explorer tour before riding a quad bike along the stunning coastline.

    Coral Bay is as idyllic as the name suggests. With a sweeping bay, bobbing yachts and the world-class Ningaloo Reef just two steps off the beach two nights in this destination is divine.

    Overnight: Ningaloo Reef Resort, Ocean Apartment
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    CORAL BAY

    Renowned for its marine life, today's three hour cruise on Coral Bay invites dolphins, dugongs, manta rays, turtles and an array of fish sightings.

    Overnight: Ningaloo Reef Resort, Ocean Apartment?
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    CORAL BAY - EXMOUTH - PERTH

    This morning is at leisure to explore the region. Drive to Exmouth and return your vehicle at the airport before your flight to Perth.

    Overnight: The Richardson Hotel, Deluxe Room Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    DEPART PERTH

    Transfer to Perth Domestic Airport for your flight home (B). (Flight not included).

    Meal Plan: B

Nature's Way - An Australian Adventure

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 16 days

    A bespoke special interest programme showcasing the best of the country, including a visit to the historic Coliban Park sheep station, 4WD excursion to the Daintree Rainforest and Cape Tribulation, private front of house tour of the Sydney Opera House and charter cruise on Sydney Harbour.

    Highlights: Melbourne, Kangaroo Island, Adelaide, Uluru, Cairns, Mossman, Lizard Island, Sydney.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Melbourne

    On arrival into Melbourne you will be met by your guide and chauffeur and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel. Your accommodation is available for immediate occupancy.

    Melbourne, the capital of the state of Victoria, is known as the garden, arts and sports capital of Australia. Its beautiful tree-lined boulevards, many parks, gardens and beautiful Victorian terrace houses, great restaurants, wonderful theatre and art galleries together with international sporting events all combine to make this elegant city one of the world's most liveable.

    After meeting your guide visit Cook's Cottage before taking a walk through the unique Royal Botanic Gardens, laid out over 150 years ago and reputed to be the finest in the Southern Hemisphere. Delight in the over 10,000 species and 50,000 individual plants in the 38 hectare gardens, including trees and plants of great cultural value. Many of these plants are irreplaceable, endangered or even extinct in the wild and have become a natural sanctuary for native wild life including black swans, bell birds, cockatoos and kookaburras that fill the air with their distinctive song.

    Travel past Victorian terrace houses, the city's exclusive suburbs and the Arts precinct before returning to the hotel at the 'Paris End' of the city.

    The Park Hyatt Melbourne is the city's most deluxe hotel. It is located at the 'top end' of Collins Street, directly opposite Melbourne's grandest cathedral and a few steps from Parliament House, the central business district and the beautiful Fitzroy Gardens. The luxurious oversized rooms are a haven for relaxation and comfort.

    This evening enjoy all inclusive evening drinks and canapés in the Club lounge.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne

  • Day 2

    Melbourne - Coliban Park

    This morning meet your guide and drive into the countryside travelling to one of Victoria's oldest and most delightful country properties - Coliban Park. At this private sheep station, watch sheep being shorn and learn first hand about one of Australia's most important industries. Enjoy lunch with the Barber family in their traditional family homestead. A highlight will be a tour of the property, perhaps seeing kangaroos in the wild.

    This evening enjoy drinks and canapés in the Club lounge.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne

  • Day 3

    Melbourne

    This morning meet your guide and drive to Healesville Sanctuary. Enjoy an exclusive encounter with wildlife you will discover nowhere else but behind-the-scenes at Healesville Sanctuary. This premium experience is led by a Sanctuary Keeper and brings you directly into the world of a working sanctuary where you will be privy to rare and intriguing insights into restricted areas including the world class platypus exhibits and Australian Wildlife Health Centre wildlife rescue hospital. The Sanctuary is home to a host of other Australian natives - koalas, Tasmanian devils, lyrebirds, echidnas and more.

    This special tour is designed to forge rich and enduring connections with some of Australia's most captivating wildlife including the creatures of the night, reptile species and the orphaned and injured wildlife in care. Exclusive photographic and feeding opportunities are provided during this highly interactive wildlife journey.

    This afternoon visit the Yarra Valley. Sit back and allow the experts to introduce you to the famous food and wine of the Yarra Valley region. The place where Victorian winegrowing started, the Yarra Valley is now home to over 55 wineries, most of which offer cellar door tastings and sales. Many are as serious about food as they are about their harvest, matching the wine they make with local produce in restaurants located in beautiful vineyards.

    The Yarra Valley is richly endowed with some of the most beautiful countryside in Victoria. Distant blue ranges, rolling hills strung with vines, towering trees, and verdant valleys with pristine rivers make up the spectacular scenery on offer around Marysville and Warburton.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne

  • Day 4

    Melbourne - Kangaroo Island

    After breakfast this morning meet your chauffeur in the hotel lobby and transfer by private vehicle to the Melbourne Domestic Airport. Depart Melbourne for Kangaroo Island (via Adelaide).

    Kangaroo Island is the place where you can quietly 'go bush' and see native Australian animals and birds as they were meant to be seen in the wild. Half the native bushland on Kangaroo Island remains just as it was when British navigator Matthew Flinders put a name to this untamed wilderness in 1802. And more than one-third of the Island is National or Conservation Park. Kangaroo Island is a true wildlife sanctuary. Owing to its isolation from the mainland, the Island has suffered less from the impact of European settlement and retains more than half of its native 'old-growth' vegetation - a vast area of some 2,250 square kilometres. The Island has been spared the damage done by foxes and rabbits, ensuring the integrity of native bushland and animal and bird populations have thrived. Kangaroo Island's prolific wildlife includes penguins, Australian sea lions, New Zealand fur seals, varied birdlife, koalas and echidnas, all revelling in the protected environs.

    On arrival in Kangaroo Island you will be transferred to your accommodation at Southern Ocean Lodge. Southern Ocean Lodge is Kangaroo Island's only world-class wilderness lodge. Located at Hanson Bay and commanding spectacular ocean, beach and wilderness views, the 21-suite escape offers the ultimate fusion of nature and luxury. Featuring an environmentally sensitive design, eco chic interiors, platinum lodge facilities, personal service, contemporary dining and a signature spa retreat, guests can discover the magnificent coastal scenery and abundant wildlife for which the Island is renowned in impeccable style.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge

  • Day 5

    Kangaroo Island

    After breakfast this morning meet your chauffeur in the hotel lobby and transfer by private vehicle to the Melbourne Domestic Airport. Depart Melbourne for Kangaroo Island (via Adelaide).

    Kangaroo Island is the place where you can quietly 'go bush' and see native Australian animals and birds as they were meant to be seen in the wild. Featuring an environmentally sensitive design, eco chic interiors, platinum lodge facilities, personal service, contemporary dining and a signature spa retreat, guests can discover the magnificent coastal scenery and abundant wildlife for which the Island is renowned in impeccable style. This morning you will meet your naturalist guide and travel by private 4WD passing some of the islands 1.3 million sheep growing some of the world's finest wool. Walk quietly with the guide through the bush where koalas live in the tall eucalyptus trees overhead. Look for wallabies that are almost extinct on the mainland and the local kangaroos found only on the Island.

    Enjoy a barbecue lunch with South Australian wines before heading to Seal Bay Conservation Park for a walk among the sea lions on a beautiful sandy beach. See old bulls bearing the scars of territorial disputes and learn of the seal's unique breeding biology. In the afternoon visit the site of an old whaling station and ospreys' nest.

    This afternoon enjoy the facilities at Southern Ocean Lodge or take a quiet bushwalk amidst this pristine Australian nature reserve.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge

  • Day 6

    Kangaroo Island - Adelaide - Uluru

    This morning depart Kangaroo Island for Uluru (Ayers Rock) via Adelaide.

    On arrival into Uluru you will be met by a representative of Longitude 131 and transferred to your accommodation.

    The new 'safari' style luxury tented site is the first of its kind in Australia. Built on the very edge of Uluru Kata Tjuta National Park, the fifteen individual tents have uninterrupted views over Uluru. Relax in the free-form swimming pool and enjoy an aperitif on the balcony as the sunsets over the desert, or simply lie in bed and watch the stars rise over the outback. Inside the tent, the atmosphere is lavish with expansive stone floors, beautiful bed linen and unique bathrooms and air conditioning.

    This is a special place where you will connect with the desert landscape and its magnificent natural wonders. At the Dune House enjoy a quiet drink, good food and the company of newfound friends.

    Uluru is a giant red monolith that has come to represent Australia itself. The massive sandstone rock - the summit of a buried mountain - generates an almost mystical power. The Rock is 8.8 kilometres around the base, 3.6 kilometres long, 2.4 kilometres wide and towers 348 metres above the surrounding plain. The Anangu people believe they are the direct descendents of the ancestral spirits who gave them the laws and rules that they live by. They believe they are responsible for the protection of the lands and ask visitors to respect their wishes not to climb the rock. Its significant power can be felt particularly in the early morning or at sunset as the Rock undergoes an extraordinary transformation of colour.

    This afternoon is at your leisure at Longitude 131. You will have the opportunity to select your touring in the Red Centre upon check-in.

  • Day 7

    Uluru

    This morning, you may choose to enjoy an exclusive walking tour of the local bushland. An interpretive guide will accompany you, explaining in great detail the dynamic ecology that exists in this harsh environment. Guests will learn what food sources exist around them, which plants can be used for medicinal purposes, how to find tracks left by the movements of nocturnal fauna, and where to look to spot daytime fauna such as the local bird-life (using spotting binoculars). Each guest is encouraged to identify the many different plants, tracks and wildlife as they smell, touch and taste their bush surroundings.

    To climb the world's largest monolith is against the wishes of the traditional owners, the Anangu, because the path you would otherwise follow is the traditional route taken by their ancestral Mala (rufus hare-wallaby men) on their arrival at this scared place. A base walk will, however, offer you a deeply personal, moving and lasting experience.

    Departing Longitude 131° mid-afternoon, this tour travels into the Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park Cultural Centre, a vibrant and engaging place of learning with more than a few surprises (don't miss the bulging folder of letters from previous visitors apologising for souveniring pieces of rock - the tales of regret and 'mysterious ill fortune' are fascinating).

    With a greater understanding of one of the world's truly ancient cultures, guests drive around Uluru and see for themselves the sites, which are so sacred to the traditional owners. A walk into the Mutitjulu waterhole offers each guest a unique insight into the sheer physical and spiritual enormity of Uluru. During the walk, the guide will point out cave paintings and other relevant sites important to Anangu and the early explorers.

    You will be returned to Longitude 131° to freshen up before enjoying sunset champagne and canapés atop our secluded sand dune with stunning 360° views of Uluru and Kata Tjuta.

    From there, you will be taken to an exclusive desert setting where dinner will be served under a glittering canopy of stars. A resident astronomer will join you to explain how our sun, earth and galaxy interact, the almost unfathomable distances involved in space travel, and how our ancestors navigated land and sea on countless voyages of discovery. Guests are regaled with tales of the southern night sky and famous constellations such as the signs of the zodiac, Seven Sisters, Southern Cross and Orion's Belt.

    Overnight: Longitude 131°

  • Day 8

    Uluru - Cairns - Mossman

    This morning a representative of Longitude 131 will meet you and transfer you to Ayers Rock Airport for your flight to Cairns.

    On arrival into Cairns you will be met by your guide and transferred by private vehicle to your accommodation, a stunning one hour drive along some of Australia's most scenic coastline.

    Silky Oaks Lodge is situated on the banks of the Mossman River, at the gateway to the World Heritage listed Daintree Rainforest. Each private cabin is appointed with handcrafted Australian colonial timber furniture and has its own private veranda overlooking billabongs (waterholes) or rock swimming pools. Tropical breakfast makes a fresh start to your day and optional activities available include spa and massage treatments, freshwater swimming, tennis, bike riding and guided walks through the rainforest during the day and evening to see kangaroos, birds and platypus.

    The remainder of the day is at leisure to relax in tropical surroundings.

    Overnight: Silky Oaks Lodge

  • Day 9

    Mossman

    After breakfast depart on a private 4WD safari of World Heritage listed Cape Tribulation and the Daintree Rainforest. The area comprises nearly 900,000 hectares of rainforest and has existed in its present state for many thousands of years. Explore the area with an expert naturalist guide whose diverse knowledge of botany, ornithology and zoology will help unlock the secrets of the rainforest. Cruise on the Daintree River and after lunch in a local restaurant enjoy a guided walk through the rainforest to discover a myriad of bizarre and fascinating plants and animals.

    During your tour to Cape Tribulation today you will spend an hour with Rainforest Rescue, a philanthropic organisation. Meet a representative who will provide a detailed presentation covering the Daintree Rainforest, its globally valued biodiversity and the importance of saving and planting rainforests. Next participate in planting a tree and learn about the species and the correct way to plant the tree to maximise its chance of survival.

    Overnight: Silky Oaks Lodge

  • Day 10

    Mossman - Cairns - Lizard Island

    This morning visit a private ethno-botanical garden in the heart of the Daintree Rainforest. Located at the end of a jungle-clad valley, the Botanical Ark is in a remote location. Enjoy an interpretive garden tour with the founder of this tropical oasis, Alan Carle. Be inspired as you learn about the intrinsic link between rainforest plants and cultures and sample some of the most unusual and interesting tastes and sights imaginable. Relax and unwind on the veranda of this truly beautiful Queensland home as you are served morning or afternoon tea with a very exotic fruit tasting. Following your visit, transfer by private vehicle to the Cairns General Aviation Airport. Depart Cairns for Lizard Island in a private twin-engine light aircraft charter flight.

    Imagine an island so pristine, so untouched, that when you walk on any one of the 24 secluded beaches there are no other footprints. Lizard Island is the most northerly island resort located directly on the Great Barrier Reef.

    Recently voted one of the top ten island resorts in the world it is renowned for spectacular diving and fishing, particularly in marlin season. Choose from a wide choice of activities or simply take a break and enjoy the peace and tranquillity of this natural environment. Ospreys' restaurant is the ideal setting for tropical delights and local seafood. Optional water sports include catamaran sailing and scuba diving the world famous "Cod Hole".

    Overnight: Lizard Island

  • Day 11

    Lizard Island

    The next two days are spent at this beautiful multi-award-winning island resort. It is the most northerly resort on the World Heritage listed Great Barrier Reef and was discovered by Captain Cook in 1770 and named after the monitor lizards that inhabit the island. Fine food, 24 private beaches, outstanding snorkelling and world renowned scuba diving at the Cod Hole, tennis, great walks, sailing, water skiing, wind surfing are just some of the options available. It is also renowned for its big game fishing available from September - November. Of course there is always just 'relaxing under a palm tree'.

    A highlight of your time on Lizard Island will be the visit to the Lizard Island Research Station.

    Lizard Island Research Centre attracts marine scientists from around the globe in their quest to conserve and manage the world's delicate coral reef systems. The environmental source to some 800 research publications, the Research Station is the only field station operated by the Australian Museum and is internationally recognised as a leading marine research facility.

    Led by either Lyle or Ann, the Directors of the Island's Research Station, watch Australian and international marine scientists at work and have a first hand look at a range of marine research projects, many of which utilise the state of the art seawater aquarium system enabling controlled experiments with living reef organisms. Overnight: Lizard Island

  • Day 12

    Lizard Island

    See Day 11.

    Overnight: Lizard Island

  • Day 13

    Lizard Island

    Lizard Island - Cairns - Sydney

    This morning an Island representative will escort you to the airstrip for your onward flight to Cairns.

    Depart Lizard Island for Cairns in a private twin-engine light aircraft charter flight. On arrival into Cairns General Aviation Airport you will be transferred by private vehicle to the Cairns Domestic Airport. Depart Cairns for Sydney.

    On arrival into Sydney you will be met by your guide and depart for a special sightseeing tour of Sydney. Today's tour begins at Sydney's historic Rocks area known for its narrow cobbled streets and convict buildings. In the quaint suburb of Paddington see tiny Victorian houses decorated with cast iron "lacework", much of which came to Australia as ballast in the holds of the wool and grain clippers over 100 years ago. Return to your hotel after visiting Sydney's famous Bondi Beach.

    Sydney, the capital of the state of New South Wales, is Australia's most well known city. With its extraordinary icons both natural and man-made this is a city that never fails to please the eye. Its magnificent natural harbour spanned by the 'coat hanger', the Sydney Harbour Bridge, and the man-made sails of the Sydney Opera House are just some of its most widely photographed views.

    Superbly situated right on Sydney Harbour in the city's historic Rocks area, the Four Seasons Sydney Hotel has wonderful views of the Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge. It is only a short walk from Sydney's major shopping and business districts.

    Enjoy evening drinks and canapés in the Executive Club lounge.

    Overnight: Four Seasons

  • Day 14

    Sydney

    Meet your guide and chauffeur in the lobby. Today's tour of Sydney begins with a visit to the Sydney Opera House for a private 'behind the scenes' tour. Situated on the foreshore of Sydney Harbour; the Sydney Opera House is one of the world's most recognisable buildings.

    Continue on for lunch at one of Sydney's finest local restaurants. After lunch transfer to your private charter vessel for a magical cruise on Sydney Harbour. The 46 foot motor cruiser 'Highlander' is a beautifully crafted vessel with woodwork, elegant furnishings and all modern comforts to ensure that a cruise on one of the world's great harbours is a memorable experience. Upon completion, you will be transferred back to your hotel.

    Enjoy evening drinks and canapés in the Executive Club lounge.

    Overnight: Four Seasons

Dreamtime Artists

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 6 days

    An itinerary like no other - the opportunity to visit Aboriginal artists in their own studios with an expert art guide, something that is rarely, if ever, extended to the general public. The programme also includes a scenic flight over the Bungle Bungles and an overnight at a cattle station.

    Highlights: The Aruluen Centre and the Papunya Tula Artists' Cooperative in Alice Springs, Warmun Art Centre in Turkey Creek, Balgo Hills, Bullo River Station and Darwin.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Alice Springs

    Arrive in Alice Springs, named after the wife of Sir Charles Todd, Postmaster General of South Australia, and its permanent water spring.

    Situated on the banks of the Todd River with its magnificent river red gums, the Alice Springs Resort combines efficient and friendly outback service with modern, comfortable facilities.

    Visit the Aruluen Centre which exhibits a rotating collection of contemporary Aboriginal art to gain an early understanding of the scope and meaning of this unique style.

    Overnight: Alice Springs Resort

  • Day 2

    Alice Springs

    This morning, meet the Arrernte owners of Mparntwe (Alice Springs) at the Aboriginal Art & Cultural Centre. Spend the day visiting local artists in the Alice Springs region, including the Papunya Tula Artists' Cooperative where you have the rare opportunity to join them in their work environment.

    Overnight: Alice Springs Resort

  • Day 3

    Alice Springs - Halls Creek

    Arrive by private plane at Yuendumu where you view artwork at the Warlukurlangu Art Centre, representing some 160 Warlpiri and Anmatjerre artists. This 20-year-old art centre is owned by the Aboriginal people and offers brightly coloured paintings, mostly in the dot style, to admire or purchase. After lunch, continue to Balgo Hills, a remote Aboriginal community where the painters are considered the most innovative of Central Australia.

    Overnight: Kimberley Hotel Halls Creek

  • Day 4

    Halls Creek - Bullo River Station

    This morning, travel to another Aboriginal community, Warmun, home to dozens of internationally renowned ochre painters. View or purchase their work at Warmun Art Centre at Turkey Creek, every dollar of revenue is returned back to the artist community.

    Fly over the Bungle Bungles for a bird's eye view of these colourful sandstone domes.

    Arrive at Bullo River Station, home to over 9,000 cattle, and enjoy a relaxing day and evening with the station's owners.

    Bullo River Station was made famous by top-selling author Sara Henderson. Her American husband Charles Henderson developed Bullo River in the 1950s and their story of station life is vividly chronicled in Sara's books.

    Overnight: Bullo River Station

  • Day 5

    Bullo River Station - Darwin

    Fly to Darwin, where you spend the afternoon exploring galleries and museums, and enjoying the melting pot of cultures that has formed the personality of modern Darwin. Deepen your appreciation for the region's art and culture by touring the Gallery of Indigenous Art and the Museum and Art Gallery of Northern Territory.

    Overnight: Mantra on the Esplanade

  • Day 6

    Depart Darwin

    Continue your exploration of Darwin's galleries, or explore additional sights in this culturally diverse city, before transferring to the airport for your homebound flight.
