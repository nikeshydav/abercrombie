Travel to Chile


Chile is the only country in the world with the largest north to south reach with 4,350 KMs of coastline, which blesses it with a number of unique features. The chili shaped country is home to world's driest point with Atacama Desert in northern expanses and a rugged yet spectacular landscape assorted with mountains, lakes and glaciers before merging into the ocean at Cape Horn.

Present day Chile is one of the most developed South American country, with Santiago as its pinnacle representing the charming old world charm with modern contemporary culture in the country. Making the capital city as base one can discover a number of natural wonders of Chile. The majestic Andes Mountains stand tall as natural guards in the backdrop, now home to a number of ski resorts all within 2 hours drive from Santiago. You may also drive down to Valparaiso that surrounds the city from three sides and houses the rare wine grapes such as Carmenere which made Chilean wine among the world's most famous.

Travel to Chile up in the north to San Pedro de Atacama, an adobe town, just out of the fables. Travelers can arrange to explore other the magnificent natural wonders in proximity such as EL Tatio, Licancabur, Salar de Atacama. As you move south, the panorama transforms into a greener landscape before climaxing at the Lake District, boasting of a natural tapestry of tranquil emerald green glacial lakes, ancient temperate rainforests forest, temperamental rivers, and snow-clad conical peaks. Here you will find the concentration of the traditional Chilean culture and also native home to Mapuche inhabitants, the largest ethnic group in Chile.

As you move further in your journey and leave Lake District behind, the topography becomes evidently striking with the natural beauty of Southern Patagonia of Chilean region. Showing off some of the most spectacular sceneries of archeological site of Valdivia, Monte Verde, as well as awe inspiring Torres del Paine National Park and Cape Horn, Chilean Patagonia is not just a treat to the eyes, but for the soul!

Easter Island is another UNESCO World Heritage Site calling for exploration, being the remotest inhabited region found on globe. It is also a true archeological site famous for the 887 extant Moai monumental statues found here, believed to be over 500 years old. These statues were created by the early Rapa Nui people and much of the island is protected within Rapa Nui National Park.
