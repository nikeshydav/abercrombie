Luxury Rail Journeys

Rovos Rail

Rovos rail is rightly deserving of the international acclaim it receives for creating truly world class travel experiences. Described by many as 'The World's Most Luxurious Train', Rovos Rail reflects the romance and atmosphere of a bygone era. Refurbished using traditional and sympathetic techniques the wood panelled coaches are in concourse condition and set the scene for a truly unique journey through some of Africa's finest scenery. Hauled by steam, diesel and electric locomotives, just 72 priviledged passengers can enjoy this elegant mode of travel at a time.

Boasting the largest train suites in the world, comfort is assured aboard the sleeper carriages. Modern conveniences sit side by side with period Edwardian features and each suite is complete with en suite bathroom, twin or double bed and all the amenities you would expect of a modern five star hotel. For an extra special experience the Royal Suites, each of which take up half a carriage benefit from the their own private lounge area, full bathroom with Victorian bath and separate shower.

Accommodation
Enjoy fine cuisine in five star luxury as you travel through breathtaking scenery. Dress code is smart-casual in the day and jacket and tie for the evenings. One of three trains, the 'Pride of Africa' links some of Africa's greatest destinations. From the 48-hour journey between Cape Town and Pretoria to the 13 day journey linking Cape Town to Dar-es-Salaam in Tanzania. Other journeys include the game reserves of Mpumalanga, the mighty Victoria Falls, desert landscapes of Namibia, the lush canefields of KwaZulu Natal, the magnificent Garden Route, but perhaps the most impressive - the Cape to Cairo.

The Blue Train

The Blue Train combines the comfort of a hotel with the elegance and sophistication of luxury train travel. Since it's inception in 1946, it has become synonymous with the ultimate in luxury and personal service. Perhaps the best way to see the ever changing landscapes of South Africa, the train allows you to sit back and soak up the splendour of this diverse and interesting country.

All suites have en suite facilities with a choice of bath or shower and are air-conditioned. There are two categories of suite, deluxe or luxury, the latter having the addition of full size bath tubs, CD and DVD players. On board, there is a separate dining car and lounge. Dress code onboard the train is smart-casual during the day but a jacket and tie are compulsory for gentlemen in the evening. Ladies dress elegantly for dinner. A gourmet menu of freshly cooked cuisine combined with the finest of South African wines makes dining on the Blue Train a memorable experience.

Accommodation
The Blue Train's most popular journey is between the jacaranda city of Pretoria and the mother city of Cape Town, however it can also be privately chartered to other destinations for special groups and celebrations. There are actually two train sets to ensure there are regular scheduled departures. They are very similar in décor but have a slightly different composition. Train one sleeps 74 guests and train two 58. Both benefit from the latest suspension system which offers one of the smoothest rides that can be found on any train anywhere in the world.
