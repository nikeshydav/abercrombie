  • Alto Atacama Lodge and Spa

The chef prepares cuisine based on classic Mediterranean and Chilean recipes with a dash of native flavour

    Awasi

    Experience exceptional service from the moment you arrive at this chic property located a short stroll from the centre of San Pedro de Atacama. Awasi stands in oasis-like grounds, where flagstone paths meander through gardens landscaped with local trees and grasses - a rambling furnished patio with cushioned conversation and dining areas is at the heart of the hotel.

    Throughout, the hotel is built from walls of adobe and smooth San Pedro River stones to create an authentic feel. Its eight cabañas, with their pointed thatched roofs, mimic the neighbouring village, Aldea de Tulor. Spacious studies in rustic chic, all have private patios and outdoor showers, and are furnished with dark wood and wicker, twig sconces and handmade dolls. Authentic décor leans on natural materials and desert colours, which mirror the orange-to-aqua spectrum of an Atacama sunset.

    Each cabaña is assigned a private guide and four-wheel drive vehicle, ensuring Atacama's exciting excursions are tailored to every guest.

    Explora Atacama

    Located close to San Pedro de Atacama, Explora Atacama is an ideal base for exploring the fantastic desert, salt plains and pre-Inca sites of the surrounding area. Cuisine is healthy and well-balanced, ideal for people going trekking, horseback riding.

    Explora Atacama is located on a plain known as the Ayllu de Larache, once inhabited by an ancient Atacameño community. In the area surrounding the lodge, their ancient pathways and buildings have been maintained, and form a labyrinth that invites exploration.

    Tierra Atacama

    The modern architecture of this property contrasts well with the stark, yet stunning surrounds of the Atacama Desert. Tierra Atacama is a boutique hotel on the edge of San Pedro de Atacama. Architecturally, this hotel is superb, mixing the old and the unashamedly modern.

    Built on the site of the old cattle corral, it retains the corral's ancient adobe walls at its entrance, preserving them as a monument to the history of the area.

    But beyond those traditional walls, the design is a thoroughly modern take on traditional themes. Stone walls support angular, asymmetric buildings, where slick roof lines stand above huge expanses of glass - whilst the beautifully landscaped gardens hide an abundance of water features and secret relaxation areas.
