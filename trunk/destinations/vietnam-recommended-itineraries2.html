Sapa

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 Nights

    Explore Sapa and the area around this frontier town in the Lao Cai province in northwest Vietnam. It is one of the main market towns in the area where many ethnic minority groups such as H'mong, Dao and Tay live.

  • Day 1

    Hanoi/Lao Cai

    This evening transfer to Hanoi Railway Station for your overnight train to Lao Cai, the gateway to Sapa. You will overnight in a soft sleeper compartment aboard the Victoria Express.

  • Day 2

    Lao Cai/Sapa

    On arrival in the border town of Lao Cai transfer to the former hill station of Sapa. In the terraced hills and mountains of this area, the expansive green landscape is enlivened by the colors of its hill tribe population - flashes of shining silver jewelry from the necks and wrists of indigo-clad H'mong and the scarlet turbans worn by the Red Dao. It is here you will find hands stained with color from fabric dyes intricately embroidered into clothes, hats and purses.

    On a morning walking tour of Sapa, visit the busy market where Dao, H'Mong, Tay and other ethnic minority people converge from the surrounding countryside to sell their handicrafts and buy provisions. A light trek takes you to Sapa's quaint hill tribe villages set against Indochina's highest peak, Mt. Fansipan in the Hoang Lien Son Mountain Range, a picnic lunch will be had en-route. (Please note that appropriate footwear must be worn for the trek. If conditions are wet, programs may be diverted)

    The afternoon and evening is yours to continue discovering the area. Overnight at the Victoria Sapa Resort & Spa.

  • Day 3

    Sapa

    Today take a light trek to a different valley to enjoy the sceneries of mountain rice terraces, waterfalls, buffaloes at work. Observe people at work in the fields, witness the traditional dyeing of clothes, and cross rivers by suspended bridges before returning to Sapa for lunch and an afternoon at leisure. Overnight at the Victoria Sapa Resort & Spa.

  • Day 4

    Sapa/Ethnic Market/Lao Cai/Hanoi

    After breakfast take a short drive to visit a local market. Different markets are open throughout the week, such as the Ban Cam on Tuesday, Cao Son on Wednesday, Lung Khau Nhin on Thursday, Can Cau on Saturday and Bac Ha on Sunday. You'll see mostly Flower Hmong but Phu La, Black Dao, Tay and Nung hill tribe groups also gather in these markets. Browse the variety of local products that are not found in other areas, such as the interesting animal market where pot-bellied pigs, cows and buffalos are traded.

    This afternoon take a short walk in a hill-tribe village, visiting local homes to understand more of daily tribal life, culture and traditions. In the late afternoon return to Lao Cai where you will have use of a hotel room to freshen up before taking the overnight Victoria Express train back to Hanoi.

  • Day 5

    Hanoi

    Arriving early this morning in Hanoi, you will transfer to your hotel or central Hanoi for your onward departure.

Highlights of Vietnam

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 Nights

    Highlights: Hanoi; Duong Lam Village; Hue; Danang; Hoi An; Ho Chi Minh City; Mekong Delta

  • Day 1

    Arrive Hanoi

    On arrival at Hanoi Airport, you will be met by an Abercrombie & Kent representative, and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel. Hanoi, the capital of Vietnam, has an eclectic mix of Asian and European influences with wide tree-lined avenues, beautiful lakes and over 600 temples. Hanoi has a lot to offer the visitor: see Ho Chi Minh's Mausoleum and the 11th century Quan Thanh Temple before exploring the bustling street life of the Old Quarter.

    On arrival, take a short visit to the mausoleum of the historic figure and father of modern Vietnam, Ho Chi Minh (viewed from outside; closed Monday and Friday and from October - November). Proceed to a wooden ethnic minority-style house which was his temporary residence from 1954 - 1969. In the same complex you will see the One Pillar Pagoda, founded by King Ly Thai To in 1049. This structure has become an important symbol for the people of Hanoi.

  • Day 2

    Hanoi/Street Food with Daniel/City tour

    Hit the streets of Hanoi for a half day street food tour with American chef/culinary instructor and food writer, Daniel Hoyer. A Hanoi resident and the author of five cookbooks, Daniel leads gastronomic adventure tours in Latin America and Southeast Asia.

    Start the day with a Vietnamese ca phe (coffee) or tra (tea) with the locals at one of the many sidewalk cafes in the historic French Quarter, along with some fresh pastries that will be picked up on the way. An overview of the Vietnamese food scene, particularly in Hanoi will be given plus an introduction on the upcoming day's activities.

    Continue to a local market within the city to learn about and taste some of the ingredients used in the local cuisine and to watch the buying and selling rituals of a Vietnamese market. Daniel together with your Vietnamese guide will introduce you to some of the vendors, explain about the ingredients for sale and assist you with purchasing items to take home (if desired). Many photo opportunities will be available in this bustling, vibrant and colorful scene. Lunch at one of the great restaurants or street food operations in Hanoi, (location depending on the interest and depending on how venturous the group is) where you will sample and often observe the preparation of some of the city's best like, Cha Ca, Bun Cha, Bo la Lot, Bun Rieu, Nem Ran, etc. Throughout the morning Daniel will assist you with your choices and explain the various dishes to you. Following lunch, say goodbye to Daniel, transfer back to your hotel or continue to your onward destination.

    Your next stop will be the Temple of Literature, Vietnam's first university. Constructed in 1070, the gardens and well-preserved architecture offer a relaxing glimpse into Vietnam's past.

    Attend a traditional Mua Roi Nuoc show, a uniquely northern Vietnamese art form especially designed for depicting scenes from rural life and episodes of national history. Mua Roi Nuoc literally means "puppets that dance on water." The tradition dates back as far as the tenth century when it originated in the Red River Delta area of northern Vietnam. When the rice fields would flood the villages, villagers would entertain each other using this puppet form.

  • Day 3

    Hanoi/Duong Lam Village/Hue

    Duong Lam, situated northwest of Hanoi, is one of the few remaining ancient villages in the country. It has been recognized as a national relic boasting a twelve-hundred year history with many houses dating back 400 years.

    A tour to this area will give you a great chance to explore the ancient houses while wandering the narrow alleyways, also visit temples dedicated to the two kings born there, and most interesting of all, chat with locals living in the ancient houses.

    After your meal, continue driving to Mia Pagoda (another hamlet of Duong Lam), built in the 15th century, some 287 statues of all sizes can be seen here, along with famous sculptures such as the statue of Buddha and the statues of the 'Eight Vajra Deities'. Then, visit Ngo Quyen temple and Phung Hung temple, places dedicated to two national heroes - Ngo Quyen and Phung Hung. On the way back to Hanoi, stop at Tay Phuong Pagoda. The pagoda offers beautiful architecture and ancient and unique sculptures. It was one of the first pagodas built in Vietnam and has magnificent dragons and demons carved into the wooden roof. From here continue back to Hanoi, a 45-minute drive. On arrival in Hue, you will be met by an Abercrombie & Kent representative, and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel for check in.

  • Day 4

    Hue

    In the morning, time at leisure, relax at your hotel. This afternoon drive to explore the Mausoleum of Tu Duc and Khai Dinh's tombs. Back to Hue, stroll around Dong Ba Market with your guide. The market is another of the must see sites in Hue, the stalls are teeming with color and a great place to catch a glimpse of everyday life.

    Dine tonight at the private 'garden house' known locally as 'nha vuon' of one Hue's most famous architects. This traditional style house, which is set in a large and attractive formal garden, has connections with the ancient Hue Royal Imperial Court. The traditional architecture has long been associated with the natural environment and the ancient belief of 'feng-shui'.

  • Day 5

    Hue/Danang/Hoi An

    This morning depart your hotel for one hour panorama cyclo tour through this Old City to Tinh Tam Lake where Emperor used to come to relax; full of lotus flowers in summer there are many local style coffee shops around the lake where university students come to study.

    The cyclo then stops at Imperial Citadel for your visit. It is where the Nguyen Dynasty ruled between 1802 and 1945. The ancient citadel reflects the pomp and ceremony of the dynastic rulers as well as the scars of many years of turmoil, both ancient and modern (the Royal Citadel was badly damaged in the latter stages of the war) but retains a sense of grace and majesty even today. Continue by car to visit Thien Mu Pagoda, constructed in 1601, the 21-meter tall tower is the centerpiece of the pagoda. After your own lunch, leave Hue by road for Hoi An via the scenic fishing village of Lang Co and the Pass of Ocean Clouds. Stop in Danang to visit beautiful Non Nuoc Beach and the Cham Museum containing the finest collection of Cham sculpture in the world. Continue driving to Hoi An.

    The ancient town of Hoi An was one of the major trading centres of south-east Asia in the 16th century, and was declared a World Cultural Heritage Site in 1999. Hoi An has a distinctive Chinese atmosphere with narrow streets and colourful houses decorated with lacquer panels engraved with Chinese characters.

  • Day 6

    Hoi An/Tra Que Village/City tour

    Pick up from hotel and transfer to the centre of Hoi An. From here ride a bicycle to a local farm in Tra Que Village (depending on the location of your hotel you may be able to cycle directly). Take a trip around the vegetable garden and join the farmers in preparing the land and fertilising it with seaweed. Continue gardening activities such as raking, sowing, watering greens, picking greens etc. After farming activities, you will be shown how to prepare tam huu, a local delicacy before the ride back to Hoi An.

    Your afternoon tour takes you through Hoi An. Hoi An is a small city in central Vietnam on the coast of the South China Sea. A former harbour town, its situation on the estuary of the Thu Bon River made it an important trading centre in the 16th and 17th centuries and many Chinese, Japanese, Dutch and Indians settled there. A UNESCO World Heritage site, its buildings portray a unique blend of local and foreign influences and make it a good well-preserved example of a south eastern trading port of the time. Enjoy a charming tour of this quaint town and visit merchants' houses, a Hokkien temple, the Japanese Bridge and the colourful riverside market.

  • Day 7

    Hoi An

    A day is free at leisure. Relax on the private beach or by the resort pool or perhaps rent a bicycle to ride into Hoi An to explore the picturesque centre of town with its restored 18th century merchants' houses. Hoi An also boasts some of the best silk shops, art galleries and handicraft shops in Vietnam as well as dozens of excellent restaurants serving the delicious local specialties.

  • Day 8

    Hoi An/Danang/Ho Chi Minh City

    After breakfast, you will be met and transfer to Danang for flight to Ho Chi Minh City. On arrival in Ho Chi Minh City, you will be met by an Abercrombie & Kent representative, and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel. Ho Chi Minh City, located near the Mekong Delta, is the largest city in Vietnam. It began as a small fishing village and the area that the city now occupies was originally swampland and inhabited by the Khmer people of Cambodia. In 1698, a Vietnamese nobleman was sent by the Nguyen rulers of Hue to establish a Vietnamese administrative centre which resulted in detaching the area from the Cambodians who were not strong enough to intervene at that time. In 1859, the city - known as Saigon - was conquered and influenced by the French and today, the core is still adorned with wide elegant boulevards and historic buildings. Afternoon tour visits the Presidential Palace, Notre Dame Cathedral and the War Museum.

  • Day 9

    Ho Chi Minh City/Mekong Delta/Ho Chi Minh City

    Early morning transfer to Cai Be for a day private cruise excursion departing from our own pier in Phu An hamlet at around 10:00 am. Upon embarkation on Cai Be Princess boat, clients are welcomed with fresh cotton towels.

    The tour will start with the visit of Cai Be colorful floating market, cruising among local sampans coming from all provinces of the delta full of fruits and vegetables. Your sampan will proceed to Dong Hoa Hiep island located between Vinh Long and Cai Be. During a 40 minutes cruise, you will be able to discover beautiful landscapes along the river and observe the typical Mekong delta rural life. These evergreen islands among the Mekong River still remain unknown to many people. Visit at an ancient mandarin house dating from the 19th century where the family will welcome you with a cup a tea.

    Back on board, we will lead to you to observe the activity of the biggest rice stock market. Further, others stops will allow you to see a traditional bricks and tiles factory and how locals are using palm leaves for houses. Cruising 20 minutes further to Phu An hamlet passing by Cai Be beautiful church, we will have a visit at some interesting home factories to see how rice paste is made, but also rice cookies, coco candies, as well as longan fruits drying process. Lunch will await you at Le Longanier, a superb Indochinese villa located in a lush tropical garden by the river, surrounded by fruit orchards.

    After a short walk along a beautiful canal, the excursion will end at approx. 3.30pm at Ba Bon Bridge where your own private transportation vehicle will await you. Your own private transportation vehicle will await you for your transfer back to Ho Chi Minh City.

  • Day 10

    Ho Chi Minh City/Departure

    After breakfast at hotel, you will be transferred to airport for departure flight.
