Zimbabwe Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Victoria Falls

    Visit the Victoria Falls during a full moon, and watch the glistening moonlight play around with the foamy spray, producing a lunar rainbow, the moonbow! (Better than a rainbow, we promise). Affectionately known as 'Vic Falls,' this 'tourist' capital of Zimbabwe, sending five million liters of water thundering and plummeting headlong into a chasm of white suds one twenty eight meters below, is one of the world's greatest natural wonders. A World Heritage Site, these falls carry scores of legends, romance and myths with itself. Long before it was discovered by the Scottish missionary and explorer Dr. David Livingstone, the local Batonga people had already christened it as Mosi - Oa - Tunya, meaning 'the smoke that thunders.'

    The best time to visit would be the dry season since the mist in the air does not obscure views or play havoc with sightseeing. The Victoria Falls are without doubt one of the world's grandest natural spectacles and every viewpoint reveals something new.

    Hwange National Park

    Africa is home to safaris and Zimbabwe is home to Hwange National Park, a wildlife sanctuary famous the world over for its size and wildlife population of buffalo, zebra, giraffe and, of course, elephants. The largest park in Zimbabwe, it lies south of the Victoria Falls, and covers 14, 650 square kilometers of untainted African bush.

    It is also home to many predators and endangered species such as wild dogs and a very large and varied wildlife. To travel to Hwange National Park today is to see what Africa was 150 years back!

    Mana Pools National Park

    Sightseeing in Zimbabwe is incomplete without a visit to the Mana Pools. 'Mana' meaning four in the local Shona language, implies to the four large pools inland from the Zambezi River. These pools are the remnant of ox-bow lakes that the Zambezi River carved out thousands of years ago and changed its course northwards with the 'Long Pool' being the largest of the four stretching some six kilometers! One of Zimbabwe's 'Big Five' parks, the game here is rich and the freedom to walk around and explore makes for some very special wildlife moments.

    A World Heritage Site, this park is synonymous with the Zambezi River, elephants, lions, remoteness and wilderness. This pool has a large population of hippopotamus, crocodiles and over 350 bird species and aquatic wildlife. Elephants are regular visitors and the herd comes out of the thickly vegetated areas for a drink! Time to get up close and personal with the big-eared, large, grey kinds!

    Kariba

    One of Africa's best kept secrets, Lake Kariba is a freshwater paradise on the Zambezi River. Built in 1960 and stretching across 280 kilometres, it is the world's second largest man-made lake and boasts of a thriving ecology.

    Houseboat tours and fishing are a few of the many things tourists can enjoy! Tiger fish is a prized catch; being able to catch a few is an impressive feat! A wide variety of accommodation is readily available ranging from tented to luxury accommodation and in and around the Kariba area. Houseboat tours along the river allow tourists to view the native wildlife of the river bank. The Matusadona National Park borders the lake and the shores of the park teem with game offering unparalleled game viewing experiences in both land and water.

    The Eastern Highlands

    With the lush green mountain stretch forming a natural border with Mozambique, the Eastern Highlands have been rightly called the 'Scottish Islands' of Africa. Breathtakingly beautiful, the highlands greatly appeal to hikers and birding enthusiasts and stretch through Vumba to Chimanimani.

    Due to the high altitude, the climate is more temperate and the charm of the area lies in the rolling hills and scenic drives. Sparsely populated, it stretches along the border between Zimbabwe and Mozambique, offering a refreshing rocky strip that leaves the visitor wondering what happened to the Africa they'd imagined sitting on their armchairs back home! Horse riding is a popular way to take in the fresh air. This makes for n ideal setting for trout fishing, which is a popular feat. Evenings are spent around log fires in cozy accommodation that lie dotted around the area. Nyanga and Vumba boast of world class accommodation with an equally as good 18-hole golf course.

    Gonarezhou

    Its literal translation being 'the place of many elephants,' Gonarezhou lies in the southeast corner of Zimbabwe, bordering the Kruger National Park. The country's second largest game reserve after Hwange National Park, it offers an unmatched wilderness experience owing to its mammoth size, rugged terrain and non-permanent camps.

    It is the jewel in the crown of the Great Limpopo Transfrontier Park, a peace park, incorporating South Africa's Kruger National Park and Mozambique's Limpopo National Park which allows animals to roam freely between the three sanctuaries. Luxury accommodation is available at Singita Pamushana with a number of other lodges.

    The Great Zimbabwe Ruins

    History fascinates. Savor a piece from yesterday and reveal mysteries, myths and legends. Get in touch with the Great Zimbabwe Ruins, a World Heritage Site, the name been derived from the Shona language; 'dzimba dza mabwe' meaning 'house of stone.'

    It, with its massive stoned walls constructed from millions of granite walls fitted together without mortar, remains the largest ancient stone structure in Sub-Saharan Africa. It borders the Lake Mutirikwi National Park which is home to Zimbabwe's remaining rhinoceros population.
