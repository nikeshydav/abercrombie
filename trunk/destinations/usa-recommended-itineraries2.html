LOUISIANA PLANTATION HOMES

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 5 Nights

    This 5 night/6 day itinerary allows you to explore the state of Louisiana. Visit New Orleans, before being transported into a bygone era as you head to Baton Rouge and Lafayette,both areas that showcase the infamous plantation house.

    Highlights: PParis, Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Champs Elysées, Notre Dame Cathedral, Versailles, Avignon, Nice, St Jean du Cap Ferrat

  • Day 1

    New Orleans

    Fly into Atlanta to connect with your onward flight to New Orleans. Alternatively drive from Atlanta,spending a few nights in Alabama en-route.Once in New Orleans, collect your hire car and head to The Pavillon Hotel, an architectural landmark that has long held an elevated position in New Orleans Society.

  • Day 2

    New Orleans

    Boasting an ideal location in the heart of downtown New Orleans, adjacent to the French Quarter and only a short stroll to the music clubs of Bourbon Street, you can step out of the hotel and experience the best that this exciting and eclectic city has to offer.

  • Day 3

    New Orleans/Baton

    Rouge After a leisurely breakfast enjoy the scenic drive to Baton Rouge, the state capital of Louisiana, and home to some of the most spectacular examples of Antebellum homes and plantations in the country. Baton Rouge is a thriving state capital with a rich history dating back to the 17th century. Downtown has an array of fabulous restaurants, bars and shops. The next few nights will be spent at Bocage Plantation, perfectly positioned on the River Road, and ideally located for exploring the nearby plantation homes. History speaks for itself at this old plantation, where paintings by the likes of Rembrandt and Peale adorn the walls.

  • Day 4

    4 Baton Rouge

    This morning we recommend a trip to Houmas House Plantation. Their interactive tour takes you into the wealth, grandeur and lavish life of the Sugar Baron. The Greek Revival style mansion, twelve acres of formal gardens and two award winning restaurants give the impression of a wealthy plantation house, and give you a true pre-war experience.

  • Day 5

    Baton Rouge/Lafayette/Baton Rouge

    This morning head out on a day trip to Lafayette, an area that is immersed in both Cajun & Creole cultures. A must when in Cajun Country is a trip through the bayous and swamps still predominantly owned and inhabited by the Cajuns. Learn about the history of the city with a visit to the Acadian Cultural Centre or Vermilionville, where 19th century bayou villages are designed to take you back in time and operate as a modern day incarnation of how they used to live. After your day trip, head back to Baton Rouge for your final night in the Bocage Plantation. Alternatively, if you wish to re-live the history of the Victorian era, you may wish to stay at the infamous Bois des Chenes Inn. A historic plantation home that survived the civil war, this bed and breakfast is a true representation of the eclectic Victorian era, and is part and parcel of the history of Lafayette.

  • Day 6

    Baton Rouge/India

    Head to the airport for your connecting flight to India. Alternatively, why not extend your stay and head north into Mississippi, or west to Alabama.

CALIFORNIA FOOD AND WINE TOUR

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 Nights

    Highlights: San Francisco; California's finest wine producing regions; Napa Valley; Pacific Coast Highway; Santa Barbara.

  • Day 1

    Arrive San Francisco

    Arrive into San Francisco, the gateway to California's finest wine producing regions. Tonight, stay at Cavallo Point, a beautiful lodge right underneath the Golden Gate Bridge.

  • Day 2

    San Francisco

    Explore the stunning city of San Francisco and in the evening dine at the acclaimed Murray Circle, noted for its wine list.

  • Day 3

    San Francisco

    Today enjoy a morning at the Murray Circle Cooking School. In the afternoon enjoy an optional sail across the Golden Gate Bay, passing Alcatraz, the city skyline and the bay's inlets and islands.

  • Day 4

    Napa Valley

    Today head north and into Napa Valley, offering some of California's very best wines - especially Cabernet Sauvignon. Begin your private tasting at the intimate Hall Rutherford winery, whose wine was voted the second best in the world by Wine Spectator in 2011. For lunch head to Auberge Du Soleil and enjoy a wonderful feast on the terrace in this Michelin starred restaurant. In the afternoon head to Opus One, the pioneer of Napa wines set up by Baron Philippe de Rothschild and Robert Mondavi. Enjoy a tasting in their spectacular cellar. If your stomach allows dinner is served in the three-starred restaurant of your hotel Meadowood.

  • Day 5

    Napa Valley

    Today check out some private wineries that are closed to the public. In the evening you could dine at the world famous three-starred French Laundry restaurant in Yountville.

  • Day 6

    Sonoma Valley

    The Sonoma valley on the other side of the Mayacamas Mountains is the regional home to Pinot Noir. Once again we can introduce you to some special wineries including Loxton Cellars, Ravenswood Winery and Landmark Vineyard. Spend the night at the Carneros Inn located where the Sonoma and Napa Valleys meet.

  • Day 7

    Big Sur

    Head to the picturesque town of Big Sur to stay at the Post Ranch Inn, visiting wineries in Carmel Valley enroute.

  • Day 8

    Big Sur

    Relax in Big Sur, in the evening enjoy a memorable dinner hovering over the Pacific Ocean at Sierra Mar, the restaurant at Post Ranch Inn.

  • Day 9

    Pacific Coast Highway

    Today take a private insider access tour of Hearst Castle, one of the most visited attractions in the state. End the day in Santa Barbara.

  • Day 10

    Santa Barbara

    Tour the rolling hills of the Santa Barbara wine country, visiting Sanford Winery and Vineyards, where it all started in 1971. Next enjoy a lunch at the lovely Los Olivos Café and Wine Merchant. End the day with stops at Foley Family and Firestone Vineyards.

  • Day 11

    Los Angeles

    Arrive into LA

SOUTH WEST NATIONAL PARKS

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 Nights

    Highlights: Scenic flight over the Big Bend National Park; Marfa Mystery Ghost Lights; Canyons of the Rio Grande; Joshua Tree National Park

  • Day 1

    Big Bend National Park

    Arrive at El Paso and pick up your rental car. Drive through the West Texas hill country to the ranch-home which will be your base for the next five days.

  • Day 2

    Big Bend National Park

    After breakfast, you'll be picked up in a light aircraft for a scenic flight over the Big Bend Nation Park and Rio Grande River. Your pilot is a former Big Bend National Park Ranger, who knows this country intimately. Arrive near the National Park entrance, where you will be met by a 4-wheel drive vehicle and guide for a full day of backcountry travel and hiking in Big Bend.

  • Day 3

    Big Bend National Park

    Enjoy a morning of relaxation or activity at the ranch. After lunch, drive to nearby Marfa for a private tour of the contemporary art collections and galleries that have made Marfa one of the leading centers of contemporary art in the U.S. Your Marfa visit will be capped by a chance to view the Marfa Mystery Ghost Lights over nearby Mitchell Flat.

  • Day 4

    Big Bend National Park

    After breakfast, enjoy a picturesque drive along the Rio Grande to the town of Terlingua where you will meet your rafting guide for your trip through one of the many canyons of the Rio Grande. Stop along the river for short hikes into side canyons and a picnic lunch.

  • Day 5

    Palm Springs

    Fly to Palm Springs and pick up your rental car. Drive to your hotel for an afternoon of relaxation and a chance to browse the local shops, galleries and museums.

  • Day 6

    Joshua Tree National Park

    After breakfast your private guide will meet you at your hotel for a full day of adventure in Joshua Tree National Park. Your naturalist guide will stop at several points along the way for hikes, plant identification and historical context. In the afternoon you can visit some of the major features of the Park, including the largest of the Joshua trees.

  • Day 7

    Joshua Tree National Park

    Today you will have a full day with one of the leading desert naturalists from the Desert Institute at Joshua Tree National Park. Explore the landscape of the Park, focusing on geology, cultural history, flora and ecology found in the vast valleys, rock mazes, desert cactus gardens, and Joshua Tree forests.

  • Day 8

    Palm Springs

    Palm Springs has the largest concentration of mid-20th Century modern architecture in the U.S. This morning, your private guide will take you on a tour of the best examples of Desert Modern architecture. Following dinner at your hotel, enjoy a private stargazing tour.

  • Day 9

    Depart
