Suggested Lake District accommodation

  • Cumbres Patagonicas

    The Hotel Cumbres Patagonicas enjoys a stunning panorama, located as it is right on Lake Llanquihue, one of the principal lakes in this region and close to the alpine-style town of Puerto Varas.

    From its elevated position on the shore of Lake Llanquihue, this hotel affords superb views. In fact, in each room, the placement of windows has been carefully considered to ensure they all make the most of the vistas beyond. Make no mistake, although this hotel is beautifully put together and the service is impeccable, both play second fiddle to the surrounding.

    In terms of architecture, Cumbres Patagonicas is unashamedly alpine, with the exterior dominated by steeply pitched roofs and wood cladding. Inside, the public areas continue the theme, particularly the main lounge area with its wood panelling, roaring fire and exposed, rough stone detailing.

    Quincho Country House

    Set in four hectares of mature woodland, Quincho Country House is an exquisite lodge, with amazing views of Osorno and Calbuco volcanoes and Lake Llanquihue. Other facilities include a cosy living room with a huge open fire, a kitchen and a wide range of activities and excursions.

    Quincho Country House is the very definition of 'exclusive retreat. With accommodation for just eight guests, it provides truly personalised experience set in wonderful landscapes. Accordingly, guided excursions to national parks, fjords and islands, as well as the un-missable fish market at Angelmo, are all tailored to suit every guest's personal interests.

    The Cliffs Preserve

    The Cliffs Preserve is an eco-retreat and private preserve that comprises more than eight thousand acres of pristine forest and six miles of South Pacific coast. Located at the intersection of two eco-systems, and is home to a unique profusion of wildlife.

    Activities and excursions are not to be missed - strike out in search of the spectacular puma and the diminutive pudú deer, or stand in awe at the power of the Pacific, locally home to the blue whale and Magellan penguin.
