The best of Australia - A platinum programme

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 16 days

    16 days - Platinum programme The Best of Australia

    Highlights: Melbourne, Adelaide, Kangaroo Island, Alice Springs, Ayers Rock, Cairns, Mossman, Lizard Island, Cape Tribulation, Sydney

  • Day 1

    Arrive Melbourne

    On arrival into Melbourne and after clearing customs, you will be met by a guide and transferred by private vehicle to your accommodation. Your room will have been pre-registered for immediate occupancy.

    Melbourne, the capital of the state of Victoria, is known as the Garden, Arts and Sports capital of Australia. It's beautiful tree-lined boulevards, many parks and gardens and beautiful Victorian terrace houses, great restaurants, wonderful theatre and art galleries together with international sporting events all combine to make this elegant city one of the world's most liveable.

    After some time to relax & refresh, meet your local guide in the hotel lobby for an afternoon walking tour that will introduce you to the hidden secrets of Melbourne. Join historian and raconteur Kenneth Park for a private look at elegant Melbourne. Experience the city's labyrinth of atmospheric arcades and alleyways - a wonderful way to learn Melbourne's architectural and cultural heritage. If you have a special interest Kenneth will tailor-make his walk just for you! (1.5hr walking tour).

    Park Hyatt Melbourne

    The Park Hyatt Melbourne is the city's most deluxe hotel. It is located at the 'top end' of Collins Street, directly opposite Melbourne's grandest cathedral and a few steps from Parliament House, the central business district and the beautiful Fitzroy Gardens. The luxurious oversized rooms are a haven for relaxation and comfort.

    Club lounge rooms occupy the top floors of the Park Tower and enjoy premium views of the city and surrounding Fitzroy Gardens. Guest in the Club lounge Rooms are invited to utilise our exclusive Park Lounge, located on level 19. Park Lounge offers a private sanctuary with floor-to-ceiling windows and breathtaking views over Melbourne. An open fireplace creates an atmosphere of warmth and provides the perfect setting to enjoy a leisurely glass of wine or a casual coffee in the afternoon. Park lounge offers a full buffet breakfast (served in Trilogy Bistro), evening drinks with canapés, and all day service of tea and coffee.

    For dinner this evening we recommend a restaurant reservation at The Press Club

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    Melbourne

    After breakfast this morning you will be met by your driver and transfer by private vehicle an hour & a half out of the city to rural Victoria. Today is a real treat - an exclusive experience - where you are welcomed into the home of the Barber family, to learn a little of what it is like to be an Aussie family 'living off the land'. This is a genuine operating sheep property which has been in the Barber Family name since 1917. The magnificent 6500 acre property is located approx 90 minutes from Melbourne. Meet Duncan Barber a 4th generation farmer of this land and enjoy an exclusive visit to his sheep farm.

    On arrival at the property you are treated to a delicious home-baked morning tea in the delightful surroundings of the Homestead, built in 1859. The history of the property and the passion the family have for it will become apparent as you enjoy your time getting to know your hosts Duncan, his wife Jess and Duncan's mother Rita.

    After morning tea, it is time for you to watch as Duncan shears a sheep. Within the confines of this historic woolshed built in 1944, you will learn more about the wool industry and the rich 150 year history which can be traced back to the Elector of Saxony. The finer details of preparing and classing a fleece and the process a fleece goes through will be detailed, and this beautiful product that ends up as fabric made into Italy's finest men's suits (Zegna) is sure to impress and enthrall. This is also a chance to watch Duncan work his Australian Sheep Dogs & to plant an Australian Native Tree to commemorate your visit to the property before returning to the homestead for a three course lunch with the Barber family. Your lunch will include Australian wine, beer and water.

    To conclude your day, Duncan will take you for a drive around the property to spot kangaroos in their natural environment before you re-join the vehicle and journey back to Melbourne. You will return to the city in the late afternoon with a sense of having broken through the tourist crust & seen the real Australia

    For dinner this evening we recommend a restaurant reservation at Gingerboy.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Melbourne - Adelaide - Kangaroo Island

    This morning meet your driver in the hotel lobby for your private vehicle transfer to the domestic airport for your flight to Adelaide. On arrival into Adelaide you will be met by your guide who will escort you to the Regional Air Express check in counter for your flight to Kangaroo Island. Your guide will also assist with any luggage storage you may choose to do at this time.

    IMPORTANT NOTE: for the journey to Kangaroo Island there is a luggage limit of one suitcase of checked luggage, (maximum of 15 kilos), & 7kg hand luggage per person.

    International guests: Excess baggage can be stored, if you are travelling with Rex in both directions, in a secured facility for a fee of $5.50 per bag at Adelaide Airport, (arrange at Rex Check In) - Unlimited hire until return. Luggage can also be left with concierge at your Adelaide hotel.

    On arrival onto Kangaroo Island - a flight of 35 minutes - you will be met by your Lodge representative & transferred to your home for the next 3 nights.

    Southern Ocean Lodge Southern Ocean Lodge is Kangaroo Island's only world-class wilderness lodge. Located at Hanson Bay and commanding spectacular ocean, beach and wilderness views, the 21-suite escape offers the ultimate fusion of nature and luxury. Featuring an environmentally sensitive design, eco chic interiors, premium lodge facilities, personal service, contemporary dining and a signature spa retreat, guests can discover the magnificent coastal scenery and abundant wildlife for which the Island is renowned in impeccable style. With exceptional guided experiences and adventures included in the rate, all guests receive a personalised experiential touring itinerary of the Island's unique natural attractions - the complete Kangaroo Island experience! All touring, gourmet meals, beverages & daily replenished mini bar is included in your stay - where you are invited to make yourself at home in the Lodge.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Kangaroo Island

    These 2 days are at your leisure to participate in the Lodge experiences & eco-sightseeing including Seal Bay, Flinders Chase National Park, Remarkable Rocks, beach fishing, cliff top guided walks, canapés with the kangaroos at dusk, star gazing - or of course you may simply choose to pick a South Australia wine from the cellar, sit in the Great Room & take in the spectacular Southern Ocean.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Kangaroo Island

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Kangaroo Island - Alice Springs - Ayers Rock

    After breakfast this morning you will be transferred to Kingscote airport for your flight via Adelaide & Alice Springs to Ayers Rock. On arrival into Ayers Rock you will be met by your Longitude 131 representative & transferred to your accommodation.

    The giant red monolith has come to represent Australia itself. The massive sandstone rock - the summit of a buried mountain - generates an almost mystical power. 'The Rock' is 8.8 kilometres around the base, 3.6 kilometres long, 2.4 kilometres wide and towers 348 metres above the surrounding plain. The Anangu people believe they are the direct descendents of the ancestral spirits who gave them the laws and rules that they live by. They believe they are responsible for the protection of the lands and ask visitors to respect their wishes not to climb the rock. Its significant power can be felt particularly in the early morning or at sunset as the Rock undergoes an extraordinary transformation of colour.

    After arriving at Longitude 131° and settling in, meet in the Dune House for a 3pm departure to the Cultural Centre where you will learn the cultural and spiritual meanings of Uluru & Kata Tjuta. Afterwards you will travel to Uluru, where we will take a short walk into Kantju Gorge. On arrival at the gorge we will serve refreshments 'inside' Uluru and watch the sunset. You will return to Longitude 131° at approximately 7.15pm.
    On your return, relax in the Dune House over pre-dinner drinks and canapés before enjoying a gourmet dinner with your fellow travellers or for a special occasion a private dinner by the pool or on the Dune Top.

    Longitude 131 The new 'safari' style luxury tented site is the first of its kind in Australia. Built on the very edge of Uluru Kata Tjuta National Park, the fifteen individual tents have uninterrupted views over Uluru. Relax in the free-form swimming pool and enjoy an aperitif on the balcony as the sunsets over the desert, or simply lie in bed and watch the stars rise over the outback. Inside the tent, the atmosphere is lavish with expansive stone floors, beautiful bed linen and unique bathrooms and air conditioning. This is a special place where you will connect with the desert landscape and its magnificent natural wonders. At the Dune House enjoy a quiet drink, good food and the company of newfound friends.

    Overnight: Longitutde 131
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 7

    Ayers Rock

    Depart Longitude 131° at 6am bound for Kata Tjuta. Upon arrival you will begin your journey with a guided tour of Walpa Gorge, where the natural history of Kata Tjuta is explained in great detail, learning how eons of weathering and continental upheaval have created what is seen today. You will return to Longitude 131° at approximately 10am.

    Spend your afternoon at leisure, perhaps have a swim in the pool or take the opportunity to enjoy any of our optional tours, such as a camel ride, Harley ride or helicopter tour over Uluru and Kata Tjuta.

    Enjoy sunset drinks and canapés on the Dune Top from 6pm. You will then be transferred to our outdoor dining venue - Table 131°, where you'll have an unforgettable dining experience under the stars. Return to the Dune House for complimentary after dinner drinks.

    Overnight: Longitude 131
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    Ayers Rock - Cairns - Mossman

    Depart the Dune House at 6am for Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park to view the sun rising over Uluru. You'll then continue to the Mutitjulu Waterhole to view cave paintings and hear of the Kuniya/Liru Tjukurpa story. You will return to the Dune House at 9.15am for a well-earned continental buffet and a la carte cooked breakfast. From here you will be transferred to the airport for your direct flight to Cairns.

    On arrival into Cairns you will be met by your driver and transferred 1.5 hours along the coastal Pacific Highway into the rainforest region & Mossman.

    Silky Oaks Lodge & Healing Waters Spa

    Silky Oaks Lodge is situated on the banks of the Mossman River, at the gateway to the World Heritage listed Daintree Rainforest. Each private cabin is appointed with handcrafted Australian colonial timber furniture and has its own private verandah overlooking billabongs (waterholes) or rock swimming pools. Included is a tropical breakfast daily. Optional activities available include spa and massage treatments, freshwater swimming, tennis, bike riding and guided walks through the rainforest during the day and evening to look for kangaroos, birds and platypus.

    Overnight: Silky Oaks Lodge
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Mossman - Cape Tribulation - Mossman

    After breakfast this morning meet your guide in the hotel lobby for your full day 4WD exploration of the Daintree Rainforest & Cape Tribulation. You will travel through the rich farming areas toward the World Heritage Listed forest region of Cape Tribulation, where you step aboard a personalized craft for a cruise on the mirrored smooth waters of the Daintree River. You will cruise close to riverbanks and under the overhead canopy of the rainforest seeking out the vivid Ulysses Butterfly, Kingfishers and various other wildlife. The cruise takes you through silent backwaters where crocodiles can normally be seen basking on the muddy banks.

    Arriving on the northern side of the river you will then enter the Cape Tribulation section of the Wet Tropics World Heritage protected rainforest. The journey takes you up the beautiful Alexandra Range passing through rainforest waterfalls and on to the first stop for home-style morning tea deep in the rainforest at the Jindalba boardwalk. Jindalba (the aboriginal name for this place) is also where you literally immerse yourself inside the protected forests and go on a guided walk where you view plants of ancient linage right through to examples of more recent species of flowering plants.

    Continuing along the Cape Tribulation road, you then stop at the magical Thornton Beach. Here your guide will highlight the amazing wildlife and ecosystems unique to this part of the world - "where the rainforest meets the sea". You are often the only people on this stretch of wilderness beach and many who have travelled remark how they feel like Robinson Crusoe. This is also where Cooper Creek uniquely meets up with the Coral Sea.

    Lunch is a special treat today, deep under the Rainforest canopy. You will enjoy a delicious lunch of tropical rainforest-inspired local produce. You have the chance for a private rainforest walk here as well, and may possibly spot rainforest lizards, birds, butterflies and perhaps even marsupials! This is also your opportunity to immerse yourself in the fresh, clear, rainforest creek.

    A lasting impression will be at the outstanding panoramic picture postcard view at Alexandra Range Lookout. From here you'll have a spectacular view of the mouth of the Daintree River, Snapper Island and on a clear day right through to Port Douglas. One final treat for the day is the crossing on the Daintree River Ferry - the iconic vehicle punt which transfers you out of the world heritage rainforest and on to your home-ward road. You will be back at your accommodation early in the evening.

    Please note during wet season the itinerary may change due to higher water levels.

    Overnight: Silky Oaks Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 10

    Mossman - Cairns - Lizard Island

    After breakfast this morning meet your driver in the Lodge lobby for your private vehicle transfer to the General Aviation airport for your flight over the Great Barrier Reef to Lizard Island. On arrival onto Lizard you will be met by your Island representative & orientated with the Island.

    Imagine an island so pristine, so untouched, that when you walk one of the twenty-four secluded beaches there are no other footprints. Lizard Island is the most northerly island resort located directly on the Great Barrier Reef, north of Cairns. Repeatedly voted one of the top ten island resorts in the world it is renowned for spectacular fishing, particularly in marlin season (September-December) and world famous diving opportunities such as the "Cod Hole".

    Choose from a wide choice of activities including guided nature walks, kayaking, private beach picnics or pamper yourself with a spa treatment at Azure Spa. Whatever your choice you'll appreciate the peace and tranquillity of this natural environment.

    Picture yourself sipping French champagne while taking full advantage of the seascape views at idyllic Ospreys Restaurant. With an accent on fresh seafood and superb local produce, the daily changing menus draw on the flavours of modern Australian, Asian and European cuisine.

    Most recent accolades include featuring in Condé Nast Traveller's 2010 Gold List as only one of two Australian hotels named for Best Hotels for Leisure Facilities and ranking 8th place in the category 'Top 20 Resorts Oceania' in the US Condé Nast Traveller Readers' Choice Awards

    Overnight: Lizard Island
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 11

    Lizard Island

    For a few hours today you will be treated to an exclusive & private tour of enormous environmental importance. The Lizard Island Research Centre attracts marine scientists from around the globe in their quest to conserve and manage the world's coral reef systems. The environmental source to some 800 research publications, the Research Station is the only field station operated by the Australian Museum and is internationally recognised as a leading marine research facility. Led by one of the Directors of the Island's Research Station, watch Australian and international marine scientists at work and have a firsthand look at a range of marine research projects, many of which utilise the state of the art seawater aquarium system enabling controlled experiments with living reef organisms. This first ever behind the scenes tour is an exclusive.

    Overnight: Lizard Island
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 12

    Lizard Island

    Today is at your leisure to participate in resort activities, snorkel the reef right off the beach, take a private picnic to a secluded beach or simply relax & enjoy your spectacular surrounds.

    Overnight: Lizard Island
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 13

    Lizard Island - Cairns - Sydney

    Today you will fly back over the Great Barrier Reef to the General Aviations airport where your driver will await to take you to the domestic airport for your flight to Sydney. On arrival into Sydney you will be met by your local guide and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel.

    Sydney, the capital of the state of New South Wales, is Australia's most well known city. With its extraordinary icons both natural and man-made this is a city that never fails to please the eye. It's magnificent natural harbour spanned by the 'coat hanger', the Sydney Harbour Bridge, and the man-made sails of the Sydney Opera House are just some of its most widely photographed views.

    Four Seasons Hotel Sydney is superbly situated right on Sydney Harbour in the city's historic Rocks area. The Four Seasons Sydney Hotel has wonderful views of the Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge. It is only a short walk from Sydney's major shopping and business districts.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Sydney
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 14

    Sydney

    After breakfast this morning meet your guide in the hotel lobby for your morning sightseeing tour of the harbour city.

    Today's tour begins at Sydney's historic Rocks area known for its narrow cobbled streets and convict buildings. Continue past the historic Mint and Hyde Park Barracks and stop at Mrs Macquarie's Chair for wonderful views of the Harbour, Opera House and Harbour Bridge. From here journey on by private vehicle to the Sydney Opera House for a private, behind-the-scenes tour of this Australian icon.

    50 years ago a young Danish architect named Jorn Utzon dreaming of Bennelong Point, designed a masterpiece which pushed the boundaries of creativity and science - and put Sydney on the map forever. Step inside the world's most recognisable sails for a one hour tour and experience the miracle of architecture and engineering. A work of art that came into existence against impossible odds - to become one of the busiest performing arts centres in the world! Working with a new enriching interactive and audio visual component, where images are projected onto the fabric of the building - our guides will take you on an emotional journey, engrossing you in a story to rival any opera plot with its dramatic twists and turns. You'll visit one of the major venues - where there are live performances every day. Some 1500 performances, in every genre you can imagine, are staged each year to audiences of around one million.

    From here your guide will escort you along Circular Quay to Campbell's Cove pontoon where your private charter vessel awaits.

    This afternoon you will be orientated with Sydney from the most enviable vantage point - on the water! Cruising on Sydney Harbour is a quintessential Australian experience, and today you will experience 'Calypso' - a 52 foot motor cruiser that is crewed & privately yours.

    Whilst cruising this iconic harbour you will have the opportunity to swim, fish or kayak. However most choose to sit back with a glass of Australian beer or wine & take in the amazing sights - the Harbour Bridge, the Opera House, Luna Park, Fort Denison and the waterfront homes of the rich & famous. A barbeque lunch is served on board, while anchored in a secluded cove. And as the afternoon sun comes in we suggest making your way to the bow and settling in the sunbeds for the perfect views of the harbour city as you come back in to moor. Your 4 hour experience on the water ends at Campbell's Cove pontoon in the late afternoon, just a short walk from your hotel.

    For dinner this evening we recommend a restaurant reservation at Aria.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Sydney
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 15

    Sydney

    Meet your driver in the hotel lobby for your transfer to the waterfront suburb of Rose Bay. Here your seaplane awaits for a journey to lunch that your will never forget, affording your truly breathtaking views of Sydney, her harbour & all the Sydney icons.

    A mere 20 minutes flying time from the hustle and bustle of Sydney is the exquisitely tranquil and award winning Cottage Point Inn. This beautiful restaurant is perched on the water's edge in the heart of the Ku-ring-gai Chase National Park and is renowned for offering exceptional modern Australian cuisine. Upon landing, your pilot taxis the aircraft to the restaurant's seaplane dock, where Cottage Point Inn's friendly staff offers a warm welcome and any number of great Australian wines! After a leisurely 3 course à la carte lunch reboard your seaplane and return to Sydney Harbour. To top off a magical afternoon we offer you an incredible photo opportunity flying a circuit of the Harbour Bridge and Opera House, before landing at Rose Bay. Your driver awaits to return you to your hotel at around 4.00pm.

    For dinner this evening we recommend a restaurant reservation at Tetsuya or Icebergs at Bondi.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Sydney
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 16

    Sydney - Home

    After breakfast this morning meet your driver in the hotel lobby for your private vehicle transfer to Sydney International airport for your onward journey.

    Meal Plan: B

PRISTINE WATERS OF NINGALOO REEF

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 days

    Take a scenic Flight over Cape Range National Park, Be amazed at Ningaloo Marine Park with opportunities for guided Snorkelling and Kayaking or enjoy the great outdoors with Bush Walks and Quad biking. Swimming with Whale Sharks is available March to July.

    Highlights: Fly return Perth to Learmonth. Scenic Flight over Cape Range National Park Ningaloo Marine Park. Guided Snorkelling, Kayaking, Bush Walks & Quad biking. Swimming with Whale Sharks (March to July)

  • Day 1

    Arrive Perth

    Meet your guide for a private sightseeing tour including a Swan River cruise.

    The Swan River is Perth's natural heartbeat - the city's location right on the river banks makes the capital one of the most beautiful in the world. The view of the river and city from Kings Park is among Western Australia's most scenic - city sky scrapers shining against a brilliant blue Perth sky, green parklands and the shimmering blue river combine to create a stunning vista.

    Overnight: The Richardson Hotel, Deluxe Room

  • Day 2

    Perth - Cape Range National Park

    This morning fly to Exmouth then board a charter plane for 20 minute scenic flight over the impressive limestone plateaus of Cape Range National Park. Hidden in the dunes discover Sal Salis with just eight eco wilderness tents complete with surprisingly luxurious comforts. All meals, beverages and guided activities are included and an underwater garden lies just metres from the pristine white sand. Sunset drinks and dinner are served with views to the reef.

    Overnight: Sal Salis, Wilderness Tent
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Ningaloo Reef: Swim With the Whale Sharks

    Embark on an adventurous Whale Shark cruise aboard a luxurious vessel. Snorkel Ningaloo Reef and relax with a gourmet lunch on board.

    The Whale Shark is the largest member of the shark family, the largest fish in the world and suprisingly, a harmless plankton eater! Reported to reach 18 metres in length, the whale shark is easily identified by its pattern of white spots. Thought to mainly inhabit worldwide temperate and tropical seas, it is rarely seen in shallow coastal water, but is a daily visitor to the Ningaloo Reef between late March and July. Up to 12 Whale Sharks have been spotted close to the reef feeding (Whale Shark tours operate March - July). Manta Rays with wingspans several metres wide, as well as a variety of tropical fish and turtles can be seen year round.

    Overnight: Sal Salis, Wilderness Tent
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Corqal Bay

    Collect your vehicle in Exmouth and drive to Coral Bay, the southern gateway of Ningaloo Reef. Board a one hour Sub-Sea explorer tour before riding a quad bike along the stunning coastline.

    Coral Bay is as idyllic as the name suggests. With a sweeping bay, bobbing yachts and the world-class Ningaloo Reef just two steps off the beach two nights in this destination is divine.

    Overnight: Ningaloo Reef Resort, Ocean Apartment
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Manyara - Ngorongoro

    Renowned for its marine life, today's three hour cruise on Coral Bay invites dolphins, dugongs, manta rays, turtles and an array of fish sightings.

    Overnight: Ningaloo Reef Resort, Ocean Apartment
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    Coral bay - Exmouth - Perth

    This morning is at leisure to explore the region. Drive to Exmouth and return your vehicle at the airport before your flight to Perth.

    Overnight: The Richardson Hotel, Deluxe Room
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Depart Perth

    Transfer to Perth Domestic Airport for your flight home (B). (Flight not included).

    Meal Plan: B
