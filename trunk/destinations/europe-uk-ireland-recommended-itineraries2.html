England and Wales

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 12 days

    A 12-day itinerary taking in the highlights of England and Wales, arriving in London and departing from Manchester.

    Highlights: London, Windsor Castle, Hampton Court, Oxford, the Cotswolds, Stratford, Warwick Castle, York, Yorkshire Dales, Ullswater, the Lake District, Chester, North Wales, Snowdonia.

  • Day 1

    Arrive London

    Upon arrival at the airport you will be met in the arrivals hall by your representative and privately transferred to your central London hotel.

    Overnight: The Milestone Hotel

  • Day 2

    London

    This morning, an expert local guide and chauffeur will be waiting to meet you for a full day sightseeing tour of London. You will start by visiting the Tower of London, home of the 'Beefeaters' where you will enjoy a private reception to see the dazzling Crown Jewels. You will also gain excellent views of Tower Bridge from the medieval palace. Proceed to St. Paul's Cathedral, rebuilt by Sir Christopher Wren after the Great Fire of London in 1666

    Head along the south bank of the River Thames, and enjoy a ride on the London Eye, from the top of which you will gain spectacular views of the city in your private capsule. Cross over to Westminster to the Houses of Parliament, of which Big Ben is one of the iconic symbols of England. Next visit Westminster Abbey, the national church of England that has been the scene of many state occasions including the Queen's Coronation. Continue down Whitehall and past Downing Street to Trafalgar Square, where the National Gallery of Art is overshadowed by Nelson's Column. Finally return to your hotel driving past Buckingham Palace, the Queen's official residence in London. The staterooms of the palace may be visited in August and September.

    Overnight: The Milestone Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Windsor Castle and Hampton Court

    This morning, you will meet your driver-guide for a full day excursion west along the River Thames. First visit Hampton Court Palace which became a favourite royal residence after being confiscated by Henry VIII from Cardinal Wolsey in the 16th century. In the company of a Tudor courtier, visit the Great Hall and the Tudor kitchens, and see the Royal Tennis Court on which King Henry played. You will also see the ornamental gardens, with its infamous maze, as well as the largest vine in England.

    Travel to Windsor through Runnymede Meadow, where the Magna Carta was signed by King John in 1215. On arrival you will then tour Windsor Castle, the largest inhabited castle in the world and the Queen's favourite residence. Subject to opening restrictions, you may see the State Apartments, St. George's Chapel and Queen Mary's Doll's House. You will also gain excellent views of Eton College from the castle ramparts.

    Overnight: The Milestone Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    London - Oxford - The Cotswolds

    This morning, you will meet your driver-guide, who will be waiting with your private vehicle to take you on a full day journey into the English countryside. You will head first to Oxford, home to England's oldest university, where you may take a walk through the impressive buildings that are still home to the university's main ceremonial and educational functions.

    You will then proceed to Woodstock, stopping at Blenheim Palace for a tour. This home of the Duke of Marlborough, designed by the architect Vanbrugh, was the birthplace of wartime leader Sir Winston Churchill. A vast park, landscaped by Capability Brown, surrounds the palace.

    Afterwards you will continue to your hotel in the delightful English village of Lower Slaughter.

    Overnight: Lower Slaughter Manor
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    The Cotswold Villages

    Your driver-guide will lead you on an excursion to the Cotswold Hills, regarded as one of the most quintessentially English parts of the country. You will see the famed cottages and villages built out of the honey-coloured limestone that is so characteristic of the region in places such as Chipping Campden, Burford and Bourton-on-the-Water. You may also make an appointment to view works of art and antiques in the renowned galleries of Broadway and Stow-on-the-Wold.

    This part of the world is also renowned for its traditional English pubs and tea rooms. Take time to enjoy a pint of local ale with a Ploughman's lunch, or try an afternoon cream tea in quaint old-fashioned surroundings.

    Overnight: Lower Slaughter Manor
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    Cotswolds - Stratford - Warwick - York

    Your driver-guide will lead you on a full day journey north. You will first stop in Stratford-upon-Avon, the market town that is revered as the birthplace of William Shakespeare. Tour Shakespeare's birthplace in the town centre, and visit Ann Hathaway's Cottage in the nearby village of Shottery. You may also wish to visit Holy Trinity Church, where Shakespeare is buried. The church lies on the banks of the Avon beyond the world-famous theatre.

    Journey then to Warwick Castle, often regarded as the best-preserved medieval castle in England. Dominating the River Avon, an exhibition of Edwardian life has been recreated in the staterooms by Madame Tussauds.

    Later continue on a long journey along the highway to York, where you will be confirmed for 2 nights at Middlethorpe Hall.

    Overnight: Middlethorpe Hall
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    York

    You will have a day with your driver-guide to explore one of the most historic English cities. You will visit York Minster, the largest medieval cathedral north of the Alps, and browse around the stores of the quaint streets including the Shambles. You may also walk on the ancient walls that circle the city.

    This afternoon, you may prefer to take an excursion into the countryside surrounding York. Castle Howard is a fine mansion in the hills to the east of the city. With its large landscape garden, it was used as the setting for the television adaptation of 'Brideshead Revisited'. An alternative excursion will take you west into the Yorkshire Dales National Park, an area made famous in the books by local novelist James Herriott.

    Overnight: Middlethorpe Hall
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    York - Yorkshire Dales - Lake Ullswater

    After breakfast, meet your private vehicle and driver-guide for your onward tour through the Yorkshire Dales and into the Lake District.

    Your journey takes you across the Yorkshire Dales via Wensleydale, and the market towns of Middleham and Hawes, where the famous Wensleydale Cheese creamery is located. There will be an opportunity to visit Middleham Castle, a 12th century motte and bailey fortification and a favourite home of the infamous King Richard III. The James Herriot books about the Yorkshire veterinarian were set in the area around nearby Hawes. Your driver will stop en route for you to take lunch at leisure.

    During the afternoon, continue across the country into the Lake District National Park where you will check into your hotel. Nestled in twelve acres of private gardens and woodland on the shore of stunningly beautiful Lake Ullswater and surrounded by magnificent fells, Sharrow Bay Country House Hotel is a haven of peace and tranquility with superb views of the spectacular Ullswater landscape.

    Overnight: Sharrow Bay Country House Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    The Lake District

    Today, join your driver-guide for a full day excursion through this beautiful corner of England. Travel through some of the most spectacular scenery of the region on your way to Hawkshead, close to Lake Windermere. Hawkshead is a small village with attractive alleyways and squares. Hawkshead has literary associations and you may like to visit either Wordsworth's Grammar School or the Beatrix Potter Gallery here.

    From here continue northwards up to Grasmere where you will visit Dove Cottage. Built in the early 17th century, the cottage served as an inn for some 170 years before closing and then becoming home to William Wordsworth in 1799 for nine years. It was here that Wordsworth wrote some of his most famous poetry and his sister, Dorothy, wrote her journals.

    Overnight: Sharrow Bay Country House Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    Lake Ullswater - Chester

    Leave Ullswater this morning and head south with your driver-guide. You may stop at Levens Hall, a fine Elizabethan manor house that boasts one of the finest topiary gardens in England. From here continue past the industrial cities of Liverpool and Manchester to Chester.

    On arrival in Chester, take a walking tour of the city centre. Chester is the only town in England to preserve a complete circuit of medieval walls, from which you can walk and see the site of the Roman amphitheatre. Inside the walls, you may visit the colourful sandstone cathedral, and browse along the medieval half-timbered shopping arcades called The Rows.

    Overnight: Chester Grosvenor
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    North Wales - Snowdonia

    Today, your driver-guide will lead you on a full day excursion into North Wales. You will head west to the fortified town of Conway, where you will see the impressive town walls and ruined castle. You will then travel along the Vale of Conway into Snowdonia National Park, where you will drive over some breathtaking scenery. The Llanberis Pass will take you down to Caernarfon, where Edward I's sizeable castle dominates this largely Welsh-speaking town.

    Time permitting, you may visit the Italianate village of Portmeirion. This 'ideal village' was created by architect Clough Williams Ellis to demonstrate how people should be able to live in beautiful surroundings. It is possible to arrive at the village by a miniature steam train on the Ffestiniog railway, one of the small trains built to carry slate from the surrounding mountains down to the ports.

    Overnight: Chester Grosvenor
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 12

    Depart Manchester

    Today you will be privately transferred to Manchester airport for your departure flight.

    Meal Plan: B

Ireland

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 days

    A 9-day itinerary taking in the highlights of the Republic of Ireland, beginning in and ending in Dublin.

    Highlights: Dublin, Wicklow, Powerscourt, Glendalough, Cashel, Cork, Kerry, Cliffs of Moher, Galway, Connemara

  • Day 1

    Arrive Dublin

    Upon arrival at Dublin airport you will be met in the arrivals hall by your local representative and privately transferred to your hotel. Your representative will assist you with check-in procedures.

    Overnight: Merrion Hotel

  • Day 2

    Dublin

    This morning, join an expert Dublin city guide for a panoramic tour of the Irish capital. On the tour, see Leinster House where the Irish government is based, the Presidential Palace in Phoenix Park, Dublin Castle, St. Patrick's Cathedral, grandiose O'Connell Street, and the elegant Georgian squares that are so symbolic of the city. Stop at Trinity College where the Old Library houses the beautiful Book of Kells, arguably the finest 8th century manuscript that has survived.

    Visit Trinity College, founded in 1592 by Queen Elizabeth 1 on the site of an Augustinian monastery. Originally a Protestant College, it was not until the 1970's that Catholics started entering the university. Among the many famous students to attend the college were playwrights Oliver Goldsmith and Samuel Beckett, Oscar Wilde, Bram Stoker (creator of the novel Dracula), Jonathan Swift and political writer Edmund Burke. Trinity's lawns and cobbled quads provide a pleasant haven in the heart of the city. Trinity houses the Book of Kells in the Old Library with its superb barrel-vaulted ceiling. The most richly decorated of Ireland's medieval illuminated manuscripts, the Book of Kells may have been the work of monks from Iona, who fled to Kells in 806 AD after a Viking raid. The book, which was moved to Trinity College in the 17th century, contains the four gospels in Latin. The scribes who copied the texts also embellished their calligraphy with intricate interlacing spirals as well as human figures and animals. Some of the dyes used were imported from as far as the Middle East.

    This afternoon you have two choices:

    Option 1
    Visit the National Museum of Ireland. In the Treasury see some of the finest examples of medieval Celtic metalwork, including the Tara Brooch and the Ardagh Chalice. Walk round to the National Gallery of Ireland that houses a fine collection of Irish paintings dating from the 17th to the 20th century among its fine old master collections.

    Option 2
    Step back in history and visit the shrine of Irish Independence - Kilmainham Gaol. Between 1796 and 1924 Kilmainham Gaol housed many political prisoners, the last being Eamon De Valera, later to become Taoiseach (Prime Minister) and then President of Ireland who was released from the jail on July 16 1924. It was here that the ringleaders of the 1916 Easter Rising, including Patrick Pearse and James Connolly were shot by firing squad in the stone breaking yard. The jail is now a museum, restored to its original grim condition with dark passages, punishment cells and an execution yard.

    Overnight: Merrion Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    The Wicklow Mountains

    Meet your driver-guide to enjoy a full day excursion into the Wicklow Mountains that lie to the south of Dublin.

    The avenue of Powerscourt leading to the Palladian house echoes the magnificence of the whole estate, being a mile long and lined by over 2,000 beech trees. In addition, the 47 acres of gardens are remarkable for their grandeur of scale whilst combining great delicacy and refinement of detail. The charming walled garden, the striking terraces, fine statuary and varied trees are linked by carefully designed walks and set in the magnificent surroundings of the Wicklow Mountains. The house was gutted by a fire in 1974 but has recently been reborn. An exhibition brings to life the rich history of the estate, which is now home to the best of Irish design in gifts, clothes, and furniture in the Avoca Stores and the interiors Gallery.

    From Powerscourt, continue on to Glendalough, known as the Valley of the Two Lakes. Glendalough is one of Ireland's national monuments - for scenic, religious, historic and archaeological reasons. Traveling through the Wicklow Gap is like entering an Irish Shangri-La, so shrouded is it in beauty, mystery and tranquillity. In the distance, the Wicklow Mountains fold gracefully into one another. The mountain forests of pine and spruce are reflected in the clear waters of the two lakes that give the valley its name.

    Glendalough was a place of religious pilgrimage for centuries following its establishment as a monastic centre by St. Kevin in the 6th century. By the 12th century it was an important diocese. However Viking raids did Glendalough much damage and, after a great fire in 1398, it declined as a religious centre. The tour will take you through the remains of the main monastic complex, through the remarkably well-preserved St. Kevin's Church and the imposing 100ft high Round Tower.

    Overnight: Merrion Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Dublin - Rock of Cashel - County Kerry

    Enjoy a full day of touring with your driver-guide as you travel through rich pasturelands. The inland countryside route passes into County Tipperary, home to the Rock of Cashel that dramatically overshadows the town below. The ruined 13th Century cathedral and the round tower perch dramatically on top of this outcrop.

    From here you can choose to visit either the town of Cobh or Blarney Castle.

    Option 1
    Blarney Castle is a small ruined tower house that dates back to the 15th century and has become a national icon as the home of the Blarney Stone. This stone, rumoured to have been brought to Ireland in the time of the crusades, is embedded in the parapet of the castle, and is said to bestow the 'gift of the gab' on all those who kiss it. The castle is situated in beautiful grounds which are adjacent to the Blarney Woolen Mills complex, one of the largest retail outlets in Ireland where fine local goods can be purchased. Please note: the climb up Blarney Castle is difficult, and is not recommended for those who have difficulty with stairs.

    Option 2
    Steep narrow streets climb the hill above Cobh's fine natural harbour giving wide views across the bay. Its name, pronounced 'cove' is simply the Irish for 'haven'. Now a popular resort and yachting centre, Cobh was the headquarters of the Royal Yacht Club, founded in 1720 and the oldest such club in the world. Cobh was once the main departure point for emigrants to America and was a stopping place for transatlantic liners. The Sirius sailed from Cobh in 1838 to make the first ever Atlantic steamer crossing and the doomed Titanic called there in 1912. Towering over Cobh is the magnificent Gothic-style cathedral which was built in 1868, faced with blue granite and supported inside by pillars made from local marble topped with intricately carved capitals. Its carillon of 42 bells is claimed to be the largest in Ireland.

    On to Killarney with its spectacular scenery, unusually mild climate and rapid changes in light and weather, this is a bewitching place of intense beauty. Ireland's highest mountains, Macgillycuddy Reeks, raise a jagged edge to the sky while at their feet rest three still lakes, studded with little islands, set among forests and lush glens thick with grand trees, wild fuchsias and scented orchids.

    Overnight: Sheen Falls Lodge
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Ring of Kerry

    Today tour one of the best-loved coastal drives in Europe with your driver-guide on a full day excursion (8 hours) around the Ring of Kerry. The road follows the shoreline of the Iveragh Peninsula, passing some of the most beautiful mountain and moorland scenery in the whole of Ireland. Between the mountains and coast, this route passes by many land and seascapes and you will gain fabulous views over the bays to the neighbouring Dingle and Beara peninsulas. En-route pass Derrynane House, the home of Daniel O'Connell, the 'Great Liberator' of Ireland.

    In the midst of Killarney National Park stands Muckross House, set in the shadow of Macgillycuddy's Reeks, the highest mountains in Ireland. You can explore its lovely gardens and traditional farms which recreate farming methods that were used back in the 1930s. Between April and July, the gardens are even more attractive as they are beautifully embellished in red and pink flowers of Rhododendrons. Return to your hotel from here.

    Overnight: Sheen Falls Lodge / Killarney Park
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    County Kerry - County Clare - Cong

    Depart Kerry with your driver-guide for a full day excursion. You can choose here whether to visit the Cliffs of Moher, or whether to visit the village of Adare, Limerick and Bunratty Castle.

    Option 1
    Reach the towering Cliffs of Moher; at over 600 feet tall these sandstone cliffs are Ireland's highest. They are exposed to the fiercest of Atlantic storms and large colonies of cliff nesting seabirds birds can be spotted here. From the Cliffs one can see the Aran Islands and Galway Bay. Continuing on from the cliffs, drive through The Burren, an area of limestone rock covering the majestic mountains and tranquil valleys with gently meandering streams. The coast road will then take you around Galway Bay, famed for its oysters. Time permitting, you may wish to stop in Galway the lively and picturesque city with an avant-garde culture. Continuing past Galway City you will enter Connemara, the Irish Lake District, where your hotel is located.

    Option 2
    Explore the picturesque village of Adare, well known for its thatched cottages and medieval ruins. Cross the River Shannon at Limerick into County Clare. At Bunratty Castle, you can see the largest 15th century tower house in Ireland, in the grounds of which are a series of traditional thatched cottages that have been carefully reconstructed.

    Continue past Galway City to your hotel that enjoys a delightful setting overlooking a bay.

    Overnight: Ashford Castle
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Connemara

    Enjoy a full day with your driver-guide to explore the windswept landscape of Connemara where barren mountains surround small lakes. The Connemara coast is wrapped around the Twelve Pins and, jutting out into the Atlantic, is a landscape of wild, rocky bog land and of ragged coastline coloured with the autumnal shades of seaweed and pine trees on tiny pincushion islands mirrored in the Loughs. On the shores of one such lake sits Kylemore Abbey, still home to a living community of nuns. The walled garden in the grounds of the abbey has been faithfully restored to its former glory.

    Drive north past Killary Harbour, Ireland's only fjord, into County Mayo, where you will pass the holy mountain of Croagh Patrick to which there is an annual pilgrimage each year. Return to your hotel from here.

    Overnight: Ashford Castle
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    West Connaught - Strokestown - Kildare

    For your final full day of touring, your driver-guide will take you back towards Dublin. Visit Galway City and either Castletown House or Strokestown House. In Galway you can have time at leisure to explore the quaint city perched on the edge of the bay.

    Option 1
    Continuing your journey, stop at Strokestown Park, where you can visit the Irish Famine Museum, dedicated to the victims of the potato blight in the 19th century.

    Option 2
    Visit Castletown House, Ireland's largest and earliest Palladian style house. It is very important in terms of European architectural heritage; famous for its stucco work and elaborate Baroque style.

    Overnight: K-Club
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Depart Dublin

    Today you will be privately transferred to Dublin airport for your departure flight.

    Meal Plan: B

Summer Escape to Scotland and Ireland

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 days

    An ideal summertime break to Scotland and Ireland, especially appealling to golf enthusiasts and those who enjoy country pursuits.

    Highlights: Gleneagles, Edinburgh, Dublin, Powerscourt

  • Day 1

    Arrive Scotland - Transfer to Gleneagles

    Welcome to Scotland! You will arrive at Edinburgh or Glasgow airport where you will be met by your representative, who will ensure your trip is running smoothly. You will then be transferred in your private vehicle to the Gleneagles Hotel where you will be assisted with check-in.

    Spend the afternoon settling into this magnificent Scottish sporting estate. Try your hand at one of the many activities available; learn to handle a falcon or to control a gun-dog, or try your hand at shooting a few clay targets.

    Overnight: Gleneagles Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    Stirlingshire and the Trossachs

    Today you will be joined by a driver-guide who will take you on a full day excursion into the Stirlingshire countryside to the southwest of Gleneagles. Start by visit Stirling Castle that sits atop a rocky outcrop surveying the countryside that was the venue for two famous battles where the armies of William Wallace and Robert the Bruce defeated the invading England forces. It was in the Chapel Royal of the castle that the infant Mary was crowned Queen of Scots in 1543.

    This afternoon, you may want to take a traditional tour Scottish scenery. If so, drive into the Trossachs, a beautiful area of mountains, forests and lakes often known as Rob Roy country after an infamous outlaw. In the middle of the region is Loch Katrine, where you can take a boat trip on the SS Sir Walter Scott, a historic steamship.

    If you prefer something a little more contemporary, travel to Falkirk which is an industrial town on the junction of two of Scotland's canals which run at very different levels. In order to connect them more efficiently, the Falkirk Wheel was constructed as part of the Millennium celebrations. This enormous structure is the world's finest rotating boat lift; take a short cruise on a barge to experience the wheel in motion.

    Overnight: Gleneagles Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Gleneagles at leisure

    Today is yours to enjoy more of the facilities at Gleneagles. The hotel has three 18-hole golf courses, one of which will host the Ryder Cup in 2014. There is also a fine equestrian centre, where horseback riding can be arranged. Trout fishing is available in the lochs on the estate, and you may also want to try your hand at off-road driving.

    If you would like to take an additional excursion this afternoon, your driver-guide could take you to Drummond Castle, which is noted for its splendid formal garden. Alternatively, why not indulge in a real Scottish experience by visiting the Glenturret Distillery of malt whisky? Now styled as the Famous Grouse Experience, you will learn all about the process of production of whisky as you tour the distillery complex. Oh - and don't forget to try your wee dram at the end of the tour!

    Overnight: Gleneagles Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Gleneagles - Edinburgh - Dublin - Powerscourt

    Check out from Gleneagles today, and your chauffeur will transfer you into Edinburgh, the capital city of Scotland. Here you will be joined by an expert local guide for an orientation tour of one of the most beautiful and dramatically situated cities in Europe. The highlight of your visit is a tour of Edinburgh Castle, where you will see the 'Honours of Scotland', or the Scottish Crown Jewels, and obtain stunning views of the city. At the end of your tour, you will be transferred to Edinburgh airport in time for your afternoon flight to Dublin.

    On arrival at Dublin airport, your local guide will be waiting to meet you. You will be escorted to your private vehicle and transferred to the Ritz Carlton Hotel that lies in the Wicklow Mountains to the south of Dublin.

    Overnight: Ritz-Carlton Powerscourt Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Dublin City

    This morning, your chauffeur will transfer you into Dublin city center. Here you will meet a local city guide who will take you on a sightseeing tour of the Irish Capital. After travelling around the elegant Georgian Squares for which the city is renowned, you will visit Trinity College, where you will see the Book of Kells, a fabulous example of early Irish manuscript illumination that is on display in the Old Library. You may also wish to visit the nearby Treasury of the National Museum, where the finest examples of medieval Celtic metalwork are on display, or the National Gallery that houses a fine collection of paintings.

    Later, if history is your passion you could choose to visit St. Patrick's Cathedral, the largest church in Ireland, and tour the staterooms of Dublin Castle, longtime symbol of British rule in Ireland. If however you prefer something more unusual, why not join a tour of the city by 'Duck', a WWII amphibious vehicle from which you will see the major city landmarks from both land and water!

    Overnight: Ritz-Carlton Powerscourt Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    The Wicklow Mountains

    This morning, you will meet a driver-guide for a tour into the southern part of the Wicklow Mountains. Drive across the bleak moorlands to Glendalough, the 'Valley of the Two Lakes', where St. Kevin established a monastic community during the Dark Ages. At this evocative and haunting site, it is possible to imagine life in these earlier times as you wander through the ruins of the churches, and past perfectly preserved round tower.

    Also in this area is the village of Avoca that served as the location for 'Ballykissangel' on TV. It is home to the renowned Avoca Handweavers, the finest manufacturers of quality woolen fashions in Ireland.

    Spend the rest of the day enjoying the facilities of the Ritz Carlton Powerscourt. Take a walk to visit Powerscourt House adjacent to the hotel? The house, now housing shops that sell Irish artisanal products, overlooks the beautiful Powerscourt Gardens. From the terrace, you will admire a delightful panorama that leads to the Sugarloaf Mountain. Always colorful, the gardens boast many trees and ornaments.

    Overnight: Ritz-Carlton Powerscourt Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Depart Dublin

    Say farewell to the Emerald Isle as you are transferred by your chauffeur back to Dublin airport for your departure flight.

SCOTTISH PRIVATE ESTATE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 days

    Live like a Laird on a private nature reserve in the Scottish Highlands amidst a glorious natural landscape. Outdoor activities: 4x4 Wilderness experiences, Ranger guided walks, mountain biking & Trout fly fishing. Optional: whisky tasting, pony trekking, clay bird

    Highlights: Edinburgh Castle, Castle of Mey shooting.

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE IN SCOTLAND

    You are met at the airport and assisted with Customs formalities by your private local representative after which you will be transferred to your hotel in Edinburgh's city centre.

    Our local representative will meet you at the hotel reception and assist you with checking in. He/she will run through your itinerary with you and answer any questions you may have. Your local representative is then available by telephone throughout your vacation to answer any queries.

    Overnight: Balmoral Hotel

  • Day 2

    SIGHTSEEING IN EDINBURGH

    Today you will meet an expert local guide for a half day walking tour of the 'Old Town' of Edinburgh. You will first head up to Edinburgh Castle, where you will see the 'Honours of Scotland', or the Scottish Crown Jewels, and admire stunning views of the city. You will then continue down the Royal Mile to St. Giles Cathedral, the national church of Scotland where you will hear stories of the walls that once divided the building, and of the buildings past and present that surrounded it.

    Overnight: Balmoral Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    EDINBURGH-SCOTTISH HIGHLANDS

    This morning, transfer by private vehicle to Edinburgh airport for your flight to the far north of Scotland (flight cost not included).

    Upon arrival at Wick airport, you will be met by your guide for the journey to your Highland retreat. As you are so close to the north coast of Scotland, you may visit John O' Groats, the most northerly village in Great Britain, and 836 miles from Land's End. Alternatively, you could visit the Castle of Mey, the romantic house that was the private home of the Queen Mother during her lifetime; a very personal visit, you can also see the garden that was created for her here in spite of the harsh local weather.

    Drive south across the windswept Caithness landscape, and head into Sutherland, one of the most scenic parts of the Scottish Highlands. Your Lodge, on it's own Scottish private estate and reserved exclusively for you for the next 4 nights, is located in a wonderful mountain setting not far from Skibo Castle, the former home of Andrew Carnegie. You will be welcomed by the staff and enjoy dinner this evening.

    Overnight: The Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 4

    OUTDOOR PURSUITS IN THE SCOTTISH HIGHLANDS

    Today is dedicated to activity. This morning, you will join one of the estate's Rangers for a guided three hour walk exploring the estate's nature reserve. The estate is made up of 23,000 glorious acres of Scottish wilderness at the narrowest point of the British Isles. From the highest point it is possible to see both the Atlantic Ocean and the North Sea. As well as enjoying the spectacular scenery and fresh air of the Highlands, you will learn from the Ranger about the pioneering work of the estate which aims to restore much of the native forest and the wildlife, such as wild boar, which is once again starting to roam freely in it's natural habitat. Your visit to the estate will contribute directly to this conservation project.

    After returning to the Lodge for lunch, you will have an afternoon free to explore the landscape by mountain bike.

    Upon return to the Lodge, it is time to unwind and enjoy a game of snooker in the Games Room or rest your aching limbs in the Lodge's sauna. Spa treatments may be booked on request (not included and advance booking recommended).

    Overnight: The Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    WILDERNESS & WILDLIFE EXPERIENCE BY 4 X 4

    Today is dedicated to natural wildlife as you take a 4 x 4 Wilderness and Wildlife Experience tour with a Ranger. The estate's flora and fauna is a unique experience which includes the remnants of the 'Great Wood of Caledon' which covered much of Caledonia during Roman times. Birdlife at the estate is exceptional and include golden eagle, osprey, peregrine falcon and the merlin. We recommend you bring binoculars to truly appreciate the wildlife opportunities.

    Other wildlife includes the notoriously shy badger, which may be observed in a their hide. It may also be possible to spot deer, elk, Highland cattle, pine martin, red fox, and otter. Red squirrels, which have become almost extinct in the British Isles are currently being reintroduced on the estate and so keep your eyes peeled. One of the estate's conservation projects includes a study on wild boar, supervised by Oxford University's Wildlife Conservation Research Unit. Your Ranger will detail these projects to you first hand.

    Return to the Lodge for an ample lunch and then venture out for an afternoon with your choice of either trout fly fishing (1 April -30 September) or to observe salmon leaping (seasonal). A selection of Orvis fishing rods and tackle are available at the Lodge for use during your stay.

    This evening, relax back at the comfortable Lodge. You may like to enjoy an optional whisky tasting by the fireside whilst your chef prepares your evening meal. (Tasting not included but can be arranged on request).

    Overnight: The Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    OPTIONAL CLAY BIRD SHOOTING, PONY TREKKING OR EXCURSION

    Today you may choose simply to relax at the Lodge or explore the eastate further.

    Alternatively you may like to take advantage of one of optional activities such as clay bird shooting or pony trekking through the lochs and glens of the estate. There are pony tracks throughout the estate and your guide will inform you of the points of interest along the way, such as the ruins of old crofts left as silent evidence of the infamous Highland Clearances.

    For those wishing to explore the Highlands further there are a range of places to visit within driving distance. On the east coast, Dornoch is a quaint historic town that is home to a small cathedral church that was the venue for the wedding of Madonna and Guy Richie. Dornoch is also home to the most northerly of Scotland's great links golf courses, the Royal Dornoch.

    To the north of here lies Dunrobin Castle, home of Lord Strathnaver. Built in a fairytale French style, this castle is one of the most unusual in appearance in Scotland; it has many richly furnished rooms to explore, and overlooks an extensive park. In the opposite direction in Tain is the Glenmorangie Distillery where many people's favorite brand of malt whisky is produced; distillery tours and tastings are available on all weekdays, and weekends during the Summer.

    Heading west through the mountains, you are treated to some of the finest scenery that Scotland has to offer. The mountains of this region present a bleak, glacially eroded landscape where it is possible to imagine the warfare that took place between the Highland clans in bygone eras. People stopping at the striking Falls of Shin may be able to see salmon attempting their great leap over the cascade in order to get upstream. Those who continue further west into the Assynt region see some famous mountains like Suilven and Quinag before reaching the Atlantic Coast. Lochinver and Ullapool are two attractive small towns that have excellent views over the ocean and the surrounding coastal hills.

    Overnight: The Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    HIGHLANDS - GLENEAGLES VIA LOCH NESS, BEN NEVIS AND GLENCOE

    Today you will see some of Scotland's best-known places on a full day journey to Gleneagles with your guide and private vehicle. Leave Alladale and drive south to Inverness, the 'capital' of the Scottish Highlands, along the River Ness until you reach Loch Ness, home of the fabled monster. You will visit the ruins of Urquhart Castle that dominate the deepest part of the lake, and from where the monster has been seen more often than from any other place!

    Continue along the Great Glen past Ben Nevis, the highest mountain in Scotland, to Fort William that stands at the head of Loch Linnhe. Your route continues along Glencoe, scene of the infamous massacre of the MacDonalds by the Campbells. At the head of this valley, cross the wild and grandiose upland plain called Rannoch Moor. On reaching Crianlarich, turn east and head to Gleneagles Hotel.

    Overnight: Gleneagles
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    THE TROSSACHS & ABERFOYLE

    This morning your guide will lead you on an excursion into the Trossachs, the scenic region to the west of Gleneagles that was the lair of the infamous outlaw Rob Roy McGregor. Drive through the woods and hills of the region to Loch Katrine, where you will take a scenic boat cruise on this lonely and picturesque stretch of water.

    Return to Gleneagles via Aberfoyle, once the home of the Scottish Wool Centre where various breeds of sheep are reared whose fleeces are used locally.

    Overnight: Gleneagles
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    GLENEAGLES AND OPTIONAL EXCURSION TO STIRLNG

    You may choose to spend a full day enjoying the excellent leisure facilities on the Gleneagles estate.

    For those seeking a more cultural experience, one possible excursion is to Stirling, where the castle is one of the most important historically in Scotland. The impressive Great Hall and the Chapel Royal dominate the plains around Stirling Bridge, where the 'Braveheart' William Wallace defeated the English army in 1297. To the south of Stirling, the Battlefield of Banockburn is where Robert the Bruce finally sent the English out from Scotland in 1314.

    Also near Gleneagles are the beautiful formal gardens of Drummond Castle where a guided tour with a horticulturalist can be arranged. If you think you have seen these gardens before, they were used as a main location in the film 'Rob Roy', based around the life of the notorious outlaw who lived in this area of central Scotland.

    Overnight: Gleneagles
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    DEPART SCOTLAND

    This morning, check out of your hotel and transfer by private vehicle to the airport for your return journey home.

    Meal Plan: B
