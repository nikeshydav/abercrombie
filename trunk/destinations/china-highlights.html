China Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Beijing

    Beijing also romanized as Peking is capital of People's Republic of China. One of the most populous cities of the world, Beijing has been one of the Four Great Ancient Capitals of China, making it the political and cultural centre of the country for a long time. In addition to that Beijing is also the chief hub for scientific and educational developments, a key transportation hub and the centre of international trade and communications.

    Beijing is a tourist paradise with landmarks such as The Great Wall, the Forbidden City, the Temple of Heaven, the Tian'anmen and the Summer Palace. The city is renowned for its opulent historical palaces, temples and ancient structures.

    Guilin

    Guilin has been for centuries an inspiration for Chinese poets and artists owing to its spectacular natural beauty and grandeur. As a result of its breathtaking scenery rich in limestone crags, deep caves, and lush paddy fields it is one of the major destination for China sightseeing.

    Guilin in Chinese means "forest of Sweet Osmanthus" thanks to a large number of sweet Osmanthus trees in the region. The landscape of Guilin is marked with Karst formations as well as with hills and mountains such as the elephant trunk hill and the cat mountain. Travelers here can visit Li River, Reed Flute Cave, Tunnel Cave and Fuboshan Mountain.

    Hong Kong

    Hong-Kong is one of the most sought after holiday destinations in South East Asia. This ultra modern city was built on an ancient Chinese civilization and for centuries have had influences from various cultures owing to its colonial past. As a result the city has earned the title of East Meets West and is also one of the most densely populated areas of the world.

    Honk Kong, billed as 'Asia's world city,' offers wonderful destination for those in need of some retail therapy, thanks to its famed shopping hub. Start your tour from Victoria Harbour, the centerpiece of Hong Kong for the most popular attractions and the best themed amusements. During your holidays in Hong Kong also visit Victoria Peak, Man Mo Temple and Lantau Island.

    Lijiang

    The old town of Lijiang beckons visitors to explore its ethnic charms, historical milieu and architectural landscape. Lijiang is located in Yunnan province and is set in a dramatic setting which represents the harmonious fusion of different cultural traditions to produce an urban landscape of outstanding quality.

    This exceptional ancient town is home to 354 bridges altogether which take several forms. It is from these structures that Lijiang derives its name, the 'City of Bridges'. Since there is no direct rail link the only way to reach this town is by air or coach to discover its Old Town, Baisha and Longguan.

    Shanghai

    With a population of over 23 million Shanghai is the largest city of People's Republic of China. Historically as well, Shanghai was one of the most progressive and prosperous cities of the Far East.

    This global city attracts tourists from all over the world thanks to its vibrant concoction of various cultures. Visitors here can admire various important landmarks such as The Bund, Old Town, Yu Garden, French Concession and Pudong.

    Tibet

    Tibet, snuggled up in the Himalayan range and shining like a beacon at an altitude of 4,900 metres, has been dubbed as "The Roof of the World".

    Traditional Tibetan culture is a whole world apart for many with its Buddhist monasteries, untouched natural beauty and nomadic herdsmen who shepherding sheep and yak. City of Lhasa offers plenty of possibilities of explorations such as Potala Palace, Sera and Drepung Monasteries and Jokhang Temple.

    Read more

    Xian

    The prolific city of Xian is home to the legendary Terracotta Warriors and Horses and Small Wild Goose Pagoda which have captured the fancy of history buffs from all over the world.

    Bearing a remarkable historical heritage over 4,000 prehistoric sites and tombs have been unearthed here. Xi'an has also witnessed the great changes of the Chinese nation just like a living history book, with over 13 dynasties such as Zhou, Qin, Han and Tang who based their capitals here.

    Yangzi River

    Yangtze River is a phenomenal water body with over 6,400 KM in length making it the third largest river in the world. It has recently been garnering attention of geologist from all over the world for mysteriously turning red colored.

    At par with Yellow River, Yangtze has played an important role in flourishing Chinese culture, history and economy of China. Even today it boasts of the largest hydro-electric power station in the world with The Three Gorges Dam, which boasts of steepness as well as beauty of colossal scale.
