Introduction to Iceland


If you're thirsty for adventure, Iceland sounds perfect to the core. Nestled between Europe and North America, Iceland is not just a destination, it's an adventure. The name, however, doesn't fit as only 10% of this country is covered by ice and has a surprisingly mild climate.

Teeming with wild, glacial beauty and a quirky culture full of myths and legends, the mention of the word Iceland brings to mind a plethora of glaciers, icebergs, geo-thermal wonderlands and active volcanoes. The fantastic scenery, challenging hikes, friendly natives and eerily remote wilderness are perfect for a long-weekend escape. Very few visitors travel to Iceland without being deeply touched by its sheer beauty and leave the country without a twinge in their hearts.

Holidays in Iceland with Abercrombie and Kent are as remarkably wonderful and amazingly diverse as the country itself. With A&K, you find yourself in close proximity to the people and the places that shape up this wonderful country and make it so incredible.

Plenty of activities that map your travel to Iceland ensure that every base stands covered. The capital city of Reykjavik is renowned for Hallgrimur's Church, geothermal bath, kaleidoscope music scene and a potent nightlife. From here, you can take in the therapeutic thermal waters at the famed Blue Lagoon Spa, look out for whales off the coast, try your hands at ice climbing, ice-fishing and quad-biking or hike through one of the many exciting trails. And, if you're fortunate enough you may be able to witness the Northern Lights, which would be the perfect way to end a vacation to this fantastic country.

Best Time to Go Iceland

Best time to go to Iceland

June to September is a great time to enjoy a mild ocean climate thanks to the Gulf Stream. It is relatively mild in winter and cool in summer but this is when you can enjoy almost 24 hours of daylight. The weather can change several times during the day. In midwinter, whilst there is little daylight, it is one of the best times to see the Northern Lights (Aurora Borealis).
