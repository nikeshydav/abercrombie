Morocco Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Rabat

    Morocco's capital city-Rabat lies on the Atlantic coast and replaced Fes for its position when in 1912 Morocco became a French protectorate. At that time Rabat was upturned into a French-style city on the order of French administrator General Louis Hubert Lyautey. A true architectural wonder, sightseeing in Morocco can start from the Mausoleum of Mohammed V which got completed in 1971.

    It contains tombs of late Moroccan kings and his two sons. With its typical white silhouette and green-tiled roof, the building stands as a true example of Alaouite architecture. Get the feel of a true Moroccan medina with a visit to the Oudaya Kasbah which was built in the 16th and 17th centuries. Re-live history with a visit to the ancient city of Sala that is located right outside the main city walls, a first to be built by the Berbers.

    Casablanca

    Serving as an inspiration to the famous Hollywood movie Casablanca, this cosmopolitan is the economic capital of the kingdom. Casa is its local name and makes up as the largest and most populated cities in the country. After Mecca in Saudi Arabia, Morocco is home to the largest religious monument in the Arab world with the Hassan II Mosque.

    This mosque has been designed by a French architect Michel Pinseau giving it a glass floor with huge rooms worth housing 25,000 worshippers. Its courtyard can however fit in 80,000 people. The mosque boasts of having the world's tallest minaret at 210 metres (689ft).

    Read more

    Meknes

    Boasting of a rich history, Meknes is a known UNESCO World Heritage Site. When Moulay Ismail acceded to the throne in 1672, Morocco started off its golden age. Lined with an extravagant line of monuments and buildings, Meknes is referred to as the 'Versailles of Morocco'.

    Out of the many architectural wonders, Bab Mansour is one of the most striking gates of all. Among other sites are the Roman ruins of Volubilis which is a proclaimed World Heritage Site, as short trip from the city of Meknes.

    Read more

    Marrakech

    The snow-capped Atlas Mountains, the Koutoubia Mosque with its huge minarets that are visible from any open-space terrace in the city and many more architectural wonders inside the medina - the old fortified city come together to make Marrakech a must-visit. Known as the 'red city', Marrakech is an embodiment of charm, culture and beauty.

    Walk around Djemaa el Fna which is the largest market in Morocco and the biggest square in Africa to buy souvenirs and dance with the local musicians, dancers and story tellers that will fascinate you with Arabian and Desert stories. Visit the El Bahai Palace which boasts of gardens and tranquil courtyards. Also try the Menara and Majerolle gardens or hop onto a traditional horse-drawn carriage for a feel of true Morocco.

    Read more

    Fes

    Home to the most enchanting souks and unspoiled natural beauty, Fes makes up for being one of the four imperial cities in Morocco with other three being Marrakech, Meknes and Rabat.

    Authenticity at its door, Fes will give you a feel of being on a trip back-in-time where you must set your eyes on the many Quranic schools called the madrassas. A walk around the historic Nejjarine Fountain is a must in Fes.

    The North

    Tourist places in Morocco are aplenty and you must try these little towns that make Morocco a charming place to be in.

    Chefchaouen

    A charming town on the way of Fes (southwards) can impress you with the Rif Mountains that surround it. With a picturesque image of bright blue and white buildings with red-tiled roofs, it's an old reminiscent of old-world charm.

    Tanger
    Try out the main attractions like the Medina, Petit Socco (market), Museum of Moroccan Art, the Market Square, the Kasbah and Grand Socco. Stop at Bay of Tanger to enjoy the gorgeous view. Also you must visit the Cape Spartel which is the meeting point of Mediterranean and Atlantic Sea. Also don't miss out on the Caves of Hercules where the legend dictates that Hercules rested after having created the Straits of Gibraltar.

    Asilah
    At Asilah go look at the fishing port with its whitewashed houses inside the 15th century ramparts strewn all around. It is home to a large number of galleries so you can quench your thirst for art while taking a part in the festivals held every year.

    The Sahara

    Taroudant

    It is a market town where the Souks sell the product of the Souss Valley. Visit the old Kasbah Quarter to have a look at the Palaces of Saadians.

    Erfoud-Erg Chebbi and the Sahara Desert
    A dream destination, this small town is sitting at the edge of the Sahara Desert. Welcoming many Hollywood filmmakers, this town has served as a great film set with dormant volcanoes and endless dunes. With sand dunes surrounding you in vast expanse, settle down in a desert camp where you can enjoy all the privacy in the world and can view the sunset in its essence.

    The Atlantic Shore

    Essaouira

    Essaouira is a harbour town with unspoiled beaches and a hot-spot for water-sports fans. Situated on the Atlantic coast, you can reach it within two and a half hours drive from Marrakech. Narrow paths of the old quarter are lines with blue doors and whitewashed houses giving rise to activities like windsurfing and kite surfing. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this medina is an 18th century fortified town which is also famous for its Gnaoua Festival of world music.

    Al-Jadida
    Famous with the Hollywood filmmakers, it is where portions of Orson Welles' Othello was filmed in the 1940. Also visit the Portuguese battlements and underground cistern.

    Oualidia
    This coastal town has stunning lagoons and natural landscape to die-for. Indulge in a gastronomic experience while trying out their famous sea-activities.

    Agadir
    Sandy beaches with endless sunshine can give you a great Agadir tan. Famous for convening meetings, seminars and incentives; it's a great place to re-discover yourself with recreational and leisure-activities.

    The Atlas Mountains

    A one hour drive from Marrakech, Atlas Mountains stand across the Northern Africa for a 2400 kilometres stretch through Morocco, Algeria and Tunisia. These mountain ranges are divided into three- High Atlas, Middle Atlas and Ant-Atlas and are all found in Morocco. Jbel Toubkal is the highest peak with 4160 metres elevation in South-western Morocco while M'Goun at 4070 metres is the second highest mountain.

    Explore the Berber villages and their charm in the High Atlas range. Adventure lovers can enjoy a half-day trekking trip in the mountains in the forests of juniper and oak trees. You could even do the same being mounted on a horse back. End your trek with the views of the gorgeous Tadawt plateau and sip in the refreshing mint tea at a Berber house.
