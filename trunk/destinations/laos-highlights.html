Laos Highlights & Sightseeing

  • LUANG PRABANG

    As you step into the city, color is the first thing that greets you. Overgrown, emerald trees peppered with scarlet and bright orange blooms, monks in saffron and burnt sienna, resplendent gold and ruby wats are probably just a few of the many vivid things. Encircled by mountains and set 700 meters above sea level, this city is considered to be the religious heart of Laos. Strikingly situated on the peninsula formed by the confluence of the Nam Khan and Mekong Rivers, the subtle Theravada Buddhist temples and the rich meld of French Indochinese architecture imparts a unique vibe.

    This was the ancient royal capital of the Lan Xang Kingdom until the administrative seat was moved to Vientiane in 1545. Luang Prabang still remains the main centre for Buddhist learning in Laos and is the perfect location for some spiritual contemplation.

    VIENTIANE

    When in Vientiane do as Vientiane does and slow down your pace right then and there. Compared to the hectic, bustling capitals in other parts of southeast Asia, this capital city of Laos is studded with crumbling French mansions, bougainvillea-lined streets, roadside stalls of steaming noodles and is placed somewhere between a big town and a quaint, sleepy city.

    It delivers that much-needed riverside break where the best thing you can do is grab a mug of cold Beerlao - the Lao National Beer and enjoy the sun's spectacular show as it sets over the Mekong.

    SOUTHERN LAOS

    Flat, fertile and verdant, the Southern Laos terrain stands in complete contrast to its northern counterpart. Refreshingly raw, the cooler climes of the Bolaven Plateau and the three isolated and unexplored eastern provinces make Southern Laos an assortment of many offerings.

    The most obvious starting point is Pakse, the Mekong riverside capital of Champasak. Gloriously laidback, it offers a few fading glimpses of French rule. Make sure to visit the temple ruins of Oum Muong, dating back to the 9th century that is dedicated to Rudani, a consort of Lord Shiva.

    Sightseeing in Laos is not complete until you have visited the Khone Phapheng falls, aptly called the Niagara of the East. The largest in Southeast Asia, these falls are an awesome spectacle especially during the rainy season. The Bolaven Plateau is a patchwork of jungle sprinkled with tea and coffee plantations and many colorful, remote, minority villages with deep rooted, customs, beliefs and traditions. Elephant trekking and mountain biking are some options for adventure seekers.
