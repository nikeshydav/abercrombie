Family Costa Rica

Programme Overview

  • Speed between lush trees by zipline, float down the rivers of Tortuguero National Park in search of hidden fauna and spend two nights at a hotel in the shadow of the booming Arenal Volcano.

    Duration: 7 Days

    Highlights: • Gain firsthand knowledge of Costa Rica's ecological wonders with the help of an expert naturalist guide
    • Spend a day kayaking in stunning Caño Palma
    • Embark on an exhilarating zipline tour though the rainforest's canopy
    • Visit Tortuguero, where you discover the village history, stop at a school and learn about ongoing efforts to secure running water and improved medical care
    • Explore Finca Burio (Burio Farm), where owner Don Juan guides you through the plantation
    • Walk above a rainforest canopy on suspended bridges, viewing natural wonders from a unique vantage point

  • Day 1

    Arrive San Jose, Costa Rica

    Arrive in San Jose, and settle in to your colonial hacienda-style hotel, located on a flourishing, 30-acre coffee plantation.

    Overnight: Marriott Hotel

  • Day 2

    San Jose/Tortuguero

    This morning, embark on a heart-thumping zipline adventure between six panoramic platforms. Watch for local wildlife as you zoom through tree canopies and across the magnificent Molinete River within the verdant natural surroundings of the 1,200-acre Rain Forest Aerial Tram Park. Later this afternoon, board a private boat from the Caño Blanco dock, continuing to Tortuguero and your lodge, nestled amid a tangle of tropical foliage.

    Overnight: Tortuga Lodge
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 3

    Tortuguero

    Today, you start your adventure exploring this tropical paradise. You may take a guided bird walk before voyaging down the slow- moving tropical rivers that define Tortuguero National Park, one of the last large, intact tropical rainforests in Central America. Search for wildlife hidden within the thick flora, perhaps spider, howler and white-faced monkeys; three-toed sloths; river otters; caimans; iguanas; river turtles; basilisk lizards; and poison dart frogs. This region is also perfect for birders, as it showcases a multitude of bird species. Later, explore the village of San Francisco. If time permits, you may also visit a neighborhood school (when in session), or join local children in a game of soccer.

    Overnight: Tortuga Lodge
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 4

    Tortuguero

    This morning, head north by motorized boat to Caño Palma (Palm Canal) for an exciting kayak adventure, travelling to places motorized boats cannot reach. Paddle through the wildlife-rich canal, visiting one of the most magical places in the flooded forest. The Tortuguero area is situated on an ancient floodplain and consists of lowland tropical wet forest; it is the richest biological ecosystem in all of Costa Rica. Later this afternoon, take some time to relax and unwind, gathering the children for a garden walk. Those who prefer may also have fun in the sun at the pool.

    Overnight: Tortuga Lodge
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 5

    Tortuguero/Fortuna (Arenal)

    Depart on a short private charter flight to Arenal. Upon arrival, a private van and driver transfers you to your hotel at the base of the Arenal Volcano. Marvel at the famed volcano's near-perfect cone, which soars 5,357 feet above sea level and has a crater over 450 feet deep. Afterward, visit Finca Burio (Burio Farm), which is owned by Don Juan. Take a comprehensive tour through the plantations with the farmer himself, while learning about the benefits and uses of fruit you may never have seen. You may also watch for many resident birds, and learn to process sugar cane at a trapiche (sugar mill). Next, sit down for an organic lunch, prepared just for you by Don Juan's wife, Doña Yamileth.

    Overnight: Arenal Nayara Hotel
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 6

    Fortuna

    After breakfast, set out for the picturesque Arenal hanging bridges with your private guide. The site was designed to blend with the natural environment, while leaving the forest habitat virtually intact. As you walk, absorb the diversity of flora and wildlife that call the rainforest home; meanwhile, see imposing views of the volcano (weather permitting) from your perch. Later this afternoon, visit the Hidalgo family's hot springs, a less-populated alternative to the overcrowded hot springs found elsewhere in the Arenal area. Tucked nearly a mile off the main road connecting Lake Arenal Dam and La Fortuna, the springs consist of five free-flowing pools situated amid a natural river gorge. The water temperature in the pools can get as high as 109 degrees Fahrenheit. Cool off after your dip with blended smoothies and cocktails made with delicious local ingredients at the pool area bar. Afterward, enjoy an authentic homestyle meal during your farewell dinner at the on-site restaurant, which is modeled after the first house that Don Manuel and Doña Hortemida Hidalgo built when they settled on the farm in 1940.

    Overnight: Arenal Nayara Hotel
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 7

    Depart San Jose

    This morning, return to San Jose. Later this afternoon, board your homebound flight, or continue to your next Latin American destination.

    Meal Plan: B

TURTLES & TOUCANS

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 13 Nights

    Explore some of Costa Rica's less visited spots, ideal for spotting turtles nesting along the Pacific Coast or discovering a wealth of tropical birds in the region around Turrialba before heading to the tranquil Pacific shores for some relaxation by the beach.

    Highlights: Incredible Flora and fauna; Cultural insight; Stunning scenery

  • Day 1

    London/San José

    Fly from London Heathrow via Miami to San José. Two nights at the Finca Rosa Blanca, located amongst the coffee and fruit plantations of the Central Valley.

  • Day 2

    San José

    Today is free to relax at your hotel in the spa and the infinity pool or stroll through the coffee plantations with their knowledgeable guides. Or perhaps take a tour to the nearby La Paz waterfalls at Poas Volcano.

  • Day 3

    San José/Tortuguero

    Begin with an early drive to Tortuguero's dock through banana plantations and Braulio Carrillo National Park. Board a comfortable launch for the two-hour canal cruise to Tortuguero with a chance of seeing caiman and numerous bird species on the way. Arrive at Manatus Lodge, your base for the next two nights.

  • Day 4

    Tortuguero

    Enjoy an early morning group boat tour through the canals and a visit the local town. The National Park is a major nesting area for green sea turtles and protects over 300 additional species including endangered green macaws and jaguars.

  • Day 5

    Tortuguero/Turrialba

    After breakfast, return to boat from the lodge to Caño Blanco and then by road to San José to meet your private transfer to Turrialba, passing sugarcane and coffee plantations and spectacular views of the towering Turrialba volcano to arrive at the Casa Turire Hotel.

  • Day 6

    Turrialba

    Visit the Guayabo Archaeological Monument, a mysterious 'lost city' veiled in the shadow of the Turrialba volcano. Ancient stone carvings, still functioning Pre-Columbian aqueducts and paved roads recede into the lush cloud forest, all hinting at Costa Rica's past civilisations.

  • Day 7

    Turrialba

    Enjoy a guided walk to the Hacienda Experience, a unique 100 year-old family-owned farm nestled in the mountains. Learn about conventional and organic agriculture while touring sugar, coffee and macadamia plantations.

  • Day 8

    Turrialba

    Relax at the hotel today and explore the hotel's own tropical gardens hunting for toucans and parakeets and other colourful tropical birds which are regular visitors to this area. The more adventurous could try white water rafting on the Pacuare River.

  • Day 9

    Turrialba/Nicoya Peninsula

    Take a short flight to Punta Islita, a spectacular stretch of coastline on the isolated Nicoya Peninsula. Stay at the beautiful hilltop Hotel Punta Islita overlooking the Pacific Ocean.

  • Day 10-12

    Nicoya Peninsula

    Explore this vast expanse of coastline with its beaches, small charming villages and many walking trails. Surfing is popular here and there are many other activities available from your coastal retreat.

  • Day 13

    Nicoya Peninsula/San José

    Fly back to San José and overnight at a pretty boutique hotel in San Jose. The afternoon is yours to explore the city, perhaps heading to the Botanical Gardens or the Gold Museum.

  • Day 14

    San José/London

    Transfer to the airport for your flights via Miami to London Heathrow.

CLASSIC COSTA RICA AND PANAMA

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 13 Nights

    Explore a breath-taking variety of landscapes, micro-climates, biodiversity and cultures from active volcanoes to rich cloud forest reserves. See the extraordinary engineering feat of the Canal, the natural wonders of pristine rainforest and stretches of unspoilt and uncrowded sandy beaches.

    Highlights: Rich eco-systems of pristine rainforest; Spectacular lava displays from Ar enal volcano; Romantic coastal retreat on Nicoya Peninsula

  • Day 1

    London/Panama City

    Fly to Panama City, arriving early evening. Stay at The Canal House in the heart of Casco Viejo, Panama's historic quarter, adjacent to the newly restored Panama Canal Museum.

  • Day 2

    Panama Canal

    Trace Panama's history at the Canal Museum and head to Miraflores to watch huge vessels passing through.

  • Day 3

    Panama City

    Journey by train through the jungle to Colón and explore the ruins of the ancient colonial port of Portobelo. Take a short boat ride on the Rio Piedra and travel by dugout canoe to visit an Embera Indian community, one of seven indigenous tribes. Meet the community chief who will teach you about their traditions, culture and beliefs.

  • Day 4

    Panama City/San José

    Fly from Panama City across to San Jose where you will stay at the Grano de Oro. This afternoon you may choose to explore some of the finest tropical gardens or discover exhibits of pre-Colombian gold at a nearby museum.

  • Day 5

    San José/Osa Peninsula

    Depart San Jose for your flight to Puerto Jimenez on the Osa Peninsula. Here is a wealth of wildlife and vistas within one of the most biologically intense places on earth. Stay at the beautiful Lapa Rios in a private 1,000 acre reserve overlooking the point where the Golfo Dulce meets the wild Pacific Ocean.

  • Day 6-7

    Osa Peninsula

    Choose from tours to discover the flora and fauna of this rainforest environment - perhaps a guided walk venturing into the nearby Corcovado National Park or the magic of the rainforest at night to see nocturnal mammals and their habitats by flashlight. Even from your balcony you will be able to enjoy this fascinating natural paradise.

  • Day 8

    Osa Peninsula/Arenal

    Take a return flight from Puerto Jimenez to San José, followed by a drive through the lush highlands of central Costa Rica to La Fortuna in the shadow of the spectacular Arenal volcano.

  • Day 9

    Arenal

    Free day to discover some amazing landscapes today or perhaps take an optional tour along the Skyway Tram leading up to one of the highest points of the Arenal Reserve or be adventurous and try the Sky trek, suspended above the forest canopy.

  • Day 10

    Arenal/Nicoya Peninsula

    Take a short private charter flight today from Arenal across to the coast to Punta Islita, a spectacular stretch of coastline on the isolated Nicoya Peninsula. This peninsula is separated from the mainland by the Gulf of Nicoya and noted for its incredible ecotourism. Stay at the beautiful hilltop Hotel Punta Islita overlooking the Pacific Ocean.

  • Day 11-12

    Nicoya Peninsula

    Explore this vast expanse of coastline with its beaches, small charming villages and many walking trails. Surfing is popular here and there are many other activities available from your coastal retreat.

  • Day 13

    Nicoya Peninsula/London

    Fly from the domestic airstrip at Punta Islita to overnight in San Jose. Day 14 San José/London Transfer to the airport for your flights via Miami to London Heathrow.

ACTIVE COSTA RICAN ADVENTURE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 13 Nights

    Discover jungle landscapes and volcanic peaks as well as enjoying some exhilarating active adventures amongst verdant tropical surroundings. A great trip for the more active or families, this also includes a few days close to Santa Teresa, known for its great surfing.

    Highlights: Take a hiking tour to a nearby National Park and waterfalls; Choose from a variety of tours to explore the surrounding rainforest; Mountain biking, river rafting, horse-riding and canopy zip-lining

  • Day 1

    London/San José

    Fly from London Heathrow via Miami to San José. Two nights at the Finca Rosa Blanca, located amongst the coffee and fruit plantations of the Central Valley.

  • Day 2

    San José

    Today is free to relax at your hotel in the spa and the infinity pool or stroll through the coffee plantations with their knowledgeable guides or take a hiking tour to a nearby National Park and waterfalls.

  • Day 3

    San José/Turrialba

    Depart San Jose to travel via a river rafting trip along the Pacuare River to arrive at your beautiful ecolodge, Pacuare Lodge. The adventure continues after lunch with a canopy tour via a series of rappels through the trees of the forest.

  • Day 4

    Turrialba

    With the lodge's own naturalist expert guides, choose from a variety of tours to explore the surrounding rainforest such as hiking, a visit to the shaman of the Cabecar indian community, kayaking or horse-riding.

  • Day 5

    Turrialba/Arenal

    Depart the Lodge by river descending some exhilarating rapids with a picnic lunch on the river. Continue on to Arenal to arrive at your hotel overlooking the volcano. Arenal is the youngest and most active volcano in Costa Rica and if you are lucky, clear evening skies will offer you a night time firework display from your hotel terrace.

  • Day 6

    Arenal

    Free day to discover some amazing landscapes today or take an optional tour canyoning or abseiling down to a stunning waterfalls surrounded by rainforest.

  • Day 7

    Arenal/Bajos del Toro

    Transfer from Arenal to El Silencio, your tranquil lodge in Bajos del Toro, set in 500 acres of private tropical forest between Poas and Juan Castro Blanco National Park amid picturesque towns and spectacular mountain scenery.

  • Day 8

    Merida

    Mountain biking, river rafting, horse-riding and canopy zip-lining are some of the active pursuits in offer here.Or if you are ready to relax a little, indulge in the beautiful jungle Esencia spa.

  • Day 9

    Bajos del Toro/Santa

    Teresa Depart the highlands and tropical cloudforests for the beautiful beaches of the Nicoya Peninsula on the Pacific Coast. Head to Pranamar Villas, a beautiful oceanfront hotel right on the spectacular Santa Teresa beach.

  • Day 10-12

    Santa Teresa

    Enjoy world-class surfing here as well as the yoga retreat or simply relax by the ocean. If you crave more adventure there are options to try such as windsurfing or scuba diving.

  • Day 13

    Santa Teresa/San José

    Fly back to San José from the nearby airstrip at Tambor and overnight at a pretty boutique hotel in San José. The afternoon is yours to explore the city, perhaps heading to the Botanical Gardens or the Gold Museum.

  • Day 14

    San José/London

    Transfer to the airport for your flights via Miami
