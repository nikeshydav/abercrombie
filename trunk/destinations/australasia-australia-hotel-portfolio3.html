Suggested South Australia accommodation

  • Abbotsford Country House

    Enjoying arguably the best views in the Barossa and set on 50 acres of farmland, Abbotsford is run as a fully hosted, traditional and luxurious country retreat that feels like a home away from home in the Barossa.

    Nestled close behind the magnificent homestead in Abbotsford's sunken garden are two guest cottages that, like the homestead, are built in true Colonial Georgian style. Its eight, spacious, individually decorated suites, each with large en suite bathrooms, are furnished with antiques collected over 12 years from Australia and the United Kingdom.

    Guests have the choice of Garden View Deluxe Suites enjoying views over Abbotsford's sunken garden and the vineyards beyond or Valley View Deluxe Suites with their panoramic valley vistas. All have ultra premium beds, high quality linens and numerous other little touches ensure Abbotsford's guests are always comfortable and well rested.

    Arkaba Homestead

    The Arkaba Homestead, dating back to 1851, is a classic Flinders building, with thick stone walls, deep shady verandas and corrugated roofing all set against the backdrop of the rugged Elder Range.

    Four traditional style bedrooms are located in the homestead, each with an en suite bathroom and access to the veranda, whilst The Coachman's Cottage in the corner of the garden provides a fifth guest room with en suite bathroom. The rooms are air conditioned and have been designed with the homestead's heritage in mind. A mix of king and twin beds have premium mattresses, 500 threadcount cotton sheets and a choice of pillows to ensure a great nights sleep.

    Hosts, Pat and Sally Kent, offer a friendly Australian welcome and the kind of informal but attentive service that is only possible when you are one of a few guests.

    Collingrove Homestead

    An authentic Barossa mansion, Collingrove (c1856) was conceived as a little piece of England and is set amongst the great gums of the Barossa Valley. Today it is a wonderful retreat set in some of Australia's most beautiful landscapes.

    Guest accommodation is individually styled, with all rooms featuring French Provincial hand carved beds, fresh white linen and French Style Toile quilts. Of the five guest rooms, three have en suites decorated in black and white tile, whilst the other two rooms share an authentic old style bathroom complete with a claw foot bath.

    Intercontinental Adelaide

    The InterContinental is situated in the heart of Adelaide, which is known for its food and wine, festivals, shopping and the Adelaide Casino. The hotel is an ideal base from which to explore, thanks to its location on the picturesque River Torrens, where it forms an integral part of Adelaide's spectacular new Riverbank promenade - and adjacent to the Adelaide Convention Centre, Adelaide Casino and the Festival Centre.

    The InterContinental's rooms and suites are spacious and beautifully appointed. All feature neutral décor, comfortable furniture and a marble en suite and afford lovely views from large windows.

    Luxury Bishops Garden

    The stunning Bishops Garden is one of Adelaide's finest properties and part of the North Adelaide Heritage Group's collection. An 1890s Adelaidean Villa, formerly the Bishop of Adelaide's Residence, it has been faithfully restored and furnished with Australian antiques and memorabilia, including a circa 1900 musical organ.

    A superb two bedroom apartment, it has an open plan living room, state of the art kitchen and dining room with glass French doors and windows overlooking the sunny private garden. A spectacular bathroom features a walk in dual person shower and the unique NecksageT King size Spa bath, whilst the Bishop's is meticulously decorated with fine heirloom antiques throughout and features an open log fire and brass beds.

    Majestic Roof Garden Hotel

    Opened in April 2004, Majestic Roof Garden Hotel is an inner city hotel that oozes personality and charm. Created with the discerning traveller in mind, the interiors of this 120 room hotel are an blend cosmopolitan class and a flow-through of space and light with soft earth tones, the sparkle of precious metal and ambient lighting.

    In the Majestic's rooms it's all about style and comfort. This means crisp white linen, opulent Japanese inspired bathrooms and tasteful modern furnishings. In-room features include 42" LCD TVs with cable and on-demand movies, access to broadband internet, a specially selected mini-bar range and king-size beds.

    Rawnsley Park

    Owned and managed by fourth generation locals Tony and Julieanne Smith, Rawnsley Park Station offers an authentic Outback experience.

    Its accredited luxury eco-villas are set on a private and secluded site with uninterrupted views of Wilpena Pound, yet within easy reach of the main facilities. Stylish and contemporary, the villas have been designed to complement the natural surroundings including rendered straw bale walls, polished timber floors and overhanging eaves with wide verandas to provide shaded walls. Retractable ceilings offer a unique opportunity to observe the stunning night sky from the comfort of your bed.

    Alternative accommodation includes holiday units, a caravan park and some of the best campsites you'll find in the Flinders, as well as the newly completed Arkapena Homestead, complete with two bedrooms, a private pool and an alfresco entertaining area.

    Southern Ocean Lodge

    The Southern Ocean Lodge is defined by superb contemporary design, astonishing location and first name service. Set back from a cliff above the pounding surf the sensitive design showcases a full frontal panorama and is within easy reach of all of Kangaroo Island's major attractions. Modern luxury and design integrity meld with Kangaroo Island vernacular and an amazing coastal colour palette, to create a unique eco chic style.

    The twenty-one superb suites command mesmerising views of the Southern Ocean as they cantilever along the coast. They are the ultimate fusion between a breathtaking wilderness setting and unbridled creature comforts. Interiors feature lavish king beds, sunken lounge, dramatic glass walled bathroom featuring Southern Spa amenities and an outdoor terrace with occasional seating and daybed.

    Signature design elements such as sandblasted limestone floors and recycled spotted gum feature walls, as well as contemporary bespoke furnishings and artworks commissioned from local Kangaroo Island artisans, harness the natural theatre of this dramatic landscape.

    The Louise Barossa Valley

    Food, wine, luxury accommodation and a warm welcome await at The Louise Barossa Valley. Ideally situated in the heart of the legendary Barossa wine country, The Louise offers absolute luxury and inspired design with private spaces, generous ensuites and breathtaking vineyard views.

    Just fifteen suites each have private terraces, soft contemporary furnishings and spacious en suites. Each suite is designed to cocoon and pamper, with outdoor rain showers, generous bathrooms and spa tubs amongst the luxurious touches. Private terrace are the ideal spots from which to enjoy glorious Barossa sunsets, whilst gas log fireplaces keep the large lounge rooms cosy as evening sets in. All have large comfortable beds, deluxe linens and spacious marbled en suites.

    Naturally, The Louise has an extensive wine list, showcasing the best of the Barossa Valley and beyond.
