Suggested Bahia accommodation

  • CASA DO AMARELINDO

    Casa do Amarelindo is a beautifully restored townhouse amidst the colourful cobbled streets of Salvador's historic Pelourinho district. Behind a colonial archway lie cool terracotta floors, whitewashed walls and tropical courtyards. This charming hotel offers a wonderful respite from the cheerful bustle of the town.

    The 10 quiet, simple yet pleasant rooms are decorated in a local Bahia style with a touch of the African influence so characteristic of the region. All have huge beds dressed with high-quality cotton linen, air conditioning and double-glazed French windows, plasma TVs, DVD and CD players. The colonial theme is represented in wrought-iron bedsteads and antique dark-wood furniture.

    The roof top terrace is an exceptional vantage point and a great spot for drinks or dinner whilst enjoying panoramic views of this UNESCO World Heritage town.

    CONVENTO DO CARMO

    Dating back to the 16th Century, this former monastery was originally commissioned by the First Order of Carmelite Friars. The building has been lovingly restored to create a superb, atmospheric hotel with two cloistered courtyards at its heart.

    The 80 or so rooms and suites, once monks' chambers, blend modern design and comfort with restored antiques and striking works of art to create comfortable, homely spaces. All are equipped with large, comfortable beds dressed in Egyptian cotton linen.

    The Convento is ideally located near the historical highlights of Pelourinho and, as such, is both a perfect base from which to explore and an ideal escape from the intensity of the city.

    KIAROA ECO LUXURY RESORT

    Named after the French Polynesian black pearl, Kiaroa Eco Resort stands in splendid isolation amongst 240,000 square metres of environmentally protected greenery - the resort is surrounded by the Atlantic Forest, one of the largest biodiversities in the planet.

    Kiaroa offers a selection of 28 rooms and bungalows with sea views. All are sympathetically designed to integrate with the surrounding environment, using local natural resources.

    In terms of design, they are inspired by French Polynesian and African motifs, as well as the surrounding landscapes to create a 'typically tropical' feel. Local handcrafts and thatched roofs abound, as do luxurious touches like Egyptian cotton bed linen, goose feather pillows, Natura's Ekos line amenities and fabulous outdoor spaces.

    TIVOLI ECO-RESORT PRAIA DO FORTE

    The Tivoli Eco Resort Praia do Forte is a 5-star Bahia hotel where simple architecture stands in perfect harmony with the stunning scenery and the biodiversity of the surrounding area.

    All 292 rooms offer ocean views from 'jungle lodge' style buildings, none of which stand above palm tree height. Inside, the room are beautifully appointed - hard wood panelling mixes with subtly coloured walls and the very highest quality furnishings. Double glass doors open onto lovely balconies and terraces hung with hammocks, which are perfect spots for reading and relaxation.

    The hotel's 'Experience Team' is always on hand to arrange trips and excursions, whether to local towns, into the 533 hectares of Atlantic forest just 6 km from the hotel, or to nearby waterparks and golf courses.

    TXAI

    Txai Resort is a fabulously beautiful eco-friendly estate. Rustic simplicity blends effortlessly with style and luxury on a breath-taking stretch of Bahia's undeveloped Cacoa Coast.

    The resort's 14 apartments and 26 bungalows are set on a hillside among coconut groves and within easy reach of the beach front and pool. All are rustic in design, yet very comfortable and spacious. Build from local woods and standing on stilts, they are quite unassuming on the outside, but beautifully calm spaces on the inside - where cool polished floors are punctuated by woven matting, and the large, comfortable beds are draped in pure white netting.

    Away from the rooms, the large, stylish sitting room is superb, and a testament to the owners' artistic flair.

    UXUA CASA HOTEL

    The only boutique hotel set within the historic 'village green' (the Quadrado) of Trancoso, UXUA Casa Hotel has become the stay of choice in this beautiful seaside village. Created by the designer Wilbert Das, UXUA consists of 10 casas, many restored fisherman homes, and each created at the hands of local artisans using traditional building methods and organic and reclaimed materials. Every casa offers a private garden, deck or patio, kitchen and dining area, air-conditioning, television, DVD and MP3 player audio system.

    There is an exquisite green crystal, lake-like swimming pool, spa and gym. Dining is Bahian cooking at its best and meals can be enjoyed at the poolside restaurant, beach lounge or at a rustic table on the Quadrado. The kitchen is open and guests are welcome to sit at the wooden counter and chat with the chefs to watch and learn the art of Bahian cooking. There is also beach bar selling locally inspired cocktails.

    Outdoor activities include surfing, river rafting, sea-kayaking, horse riding, fishing and exploring the nearby Pataxo Indian Reservation. Sports lovers can bike Trancoso's endless beaches, join in a game of beach volleyball or afternoon football match, enjoy a yoga or pilates session or have a go at capoeira, a traditional Bahian martial art. For those just looking to relax, the UXUA Beach Lounge offers reserved sofas and private areas exclusive to hotel guests. UXUA also features a gym and spa with both indoor and outdoor treatment rooms.

    VILLA BAHIA

    Villa Bahia is a hotel like no other in Salvador da Bahia. Incorporating two magnificent 17th and 18th century mansions, it is awash with colonial charm - exemplified by its quiet, atmospheric courtyards.

    The villa's 17 rooms have been carefully renovated to preserve their original spirit. Named after Portuguese colonies along the spice routes from Timor to Madagascar, the rooms are all individually decorated with dark woods, restored antiques and chandeliers as well as locally made fabrics and touches of décor relating to their namesake.

    The private terrace with fine views across the rooftops of colonial Salvador is a wonderful spot.
