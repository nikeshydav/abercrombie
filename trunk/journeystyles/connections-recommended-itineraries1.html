China: Life Along the Yangtze

Programme Overview

Embark on a four-night Yangtze cruise through the majestic Three Gorges. See pandas up close and view the Terra Cotta Warriors in Xian. Visit a local Beijing family and learn how to make dumplings.

  • Duration: 12 days/ 11 nights

    Highlights: Hong | Kong | Wuhan | Yichang| Fengdu | Xian | Beijing

    A&K Advantages

    • Cruise for four nights along the legendary Yangtze River aboard luxurious Sanctuary 'Yangzi Explorer'
    • Take a sampan ride through the local floating villages
    • Participate in traditional tai chi exercise, lead by a tai chi master
    • Visit a Yangtze River village to mingle with the locals
    • Enjoy a Chinese brush painting and calligraphy lesson
    • Learn about China's first emperor, Qin Shi Huang, entombed at the archaeological site of the stunning Terra Cotta Warriors
    • Explore an authentic hutong (neighborhood alleyway) and join a local family for a dumpling-making demonstration
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE HONG KONG

    On your Arrival in Hong Kong, the "City of Lights," you will be welcomed by an A&K Representative, who will meet and assist with transfer to the hotel.

    Overnight: InterContinental Grand Stanford Hong Kong

  • Day 2

    HONG KONG

    Begin to explore Hong Kong with a visit to the Dried Food and Herbal Medicine Market in the Western District. Further, visit the incense-filled Man Mo Temple and then continue to Victoria Peak to enjoy a beautiful scenic view. Later, step abroad for a sampan ride through the floating villages of Aberdeen Harbor, take view of the picturesque harbour, the seafood markets, sampan rides and the Jumbo Floating Restaurant

    This evening, join your fellow travelers for a welcome dinner.

    Overnight: InterContinental Grand Stanford Hong Kong
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 3

    WUHAN & YICHANG

    Today, depart for Wuhan and after arrival, enjoy lunch at a local restaurant. Later, embark on a high-speed train to reach Yichang Sanctuary. From here, begin your four-night scenic cruise along the Yangtze River on the 'Yangzi Explorer' cruise ship.

    The afternoon is at leisure, which you may choose to spend exploring the Van Gogh Museum on your own.

    Overnight: Sanctuary 'Yangzi Explorer'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    YANGZI EXPLORER

    This day, explore the Exhibition Center for the Three Gorges Dam Project, a massive engineering marvel more than 7,660 feet long.

    This evening, enjoy a warm welcome followed by a dinner onboard.

    Overnight: Sanctuary 'Yangzi Explorer'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    YANGZI EXPLORER

    Disembark the ‘Yangzi explorer’ in Badong and take a transfer by ferry to a traditional longboat rowed by a Tujia boatman, members of a local minority famed for their boat-building skills and knowledge of the river’s currents and eddies.

    Appreciate the scenic beauty as you travel through crystalline waters and towering rock formations.

    During the return journey, sail along the mountainous Wu Gorge, rugged Qutang Gorge and the majestic cliffs of Kui Men gate.

    Overnight: Sanctuary 'Yangzi Explorer'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    FENGDU

    Today, pause your cruise for a shore excursion in the beautiful village of Fengdu, admiring its elaborate Daoist temple and bustling market.

    Overnight: Sanctuary 'Yangzi Explorer'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    XIAN

    This morning, disembark in Chongqing for a guided city tour and Chongqing Zoo. Take view of China’s famous giant pandas, a beloved and endangered species of bear considered by many as the unofficial emblem of China.

    Enjoy your lunch before you depart for Xian, once the last stop on the Great Silk Road and today a melting pot of Eastern and Middle Eastern cultures.

    Upon your arrival, you will be assisted with transfer to your hotel.

    Overnight: The Westin Xian
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 8

    XIAN

    Set off to explore the Terra Cotta Warriors, one of the greatest archaeological finds of the 20th century, with a collection of terracotta sculptures depicting the armies of Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China. The unique facial features of the sculptures are a testimony to the remarkable craftsmanship of their highly-skilled sculptors.

    This afternoon, enjoy lunch along with a noodle-making demonstration. Further, explore the markets of the Old City quarter to observe the culture, people and architecture .Next, visit the famed Great Mosque.

    This evening, enjoy music and dance show inspired by the Tang Dynasty.

    Overnight: The Westin Xian
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 9

    XIAN

    Start your day with a visit to the Yangling Museum, the burial place of Emperor Jing, displaying a wealth of artifacts from their Han Dynasty tombs. Here, you will discover many intriguing archaeological finds.

    Later, Depart for Beijing, where you check into your hotel.

    Overnight: New World Beijing Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 10

    BEIJING

    This day, travel to view Tiananmen Square, which is flanked by an imposing complex of administrative buildings in the heart of China’s capital city. Rich in cultural and historical significance, the square is emblematic of the massive, centralized power of the Chinese government.

    Next, visit the Forbidden City, which was once barred to all but its emperors and their servants. Explore this largest ancient palatial structure in the world with its traditional Chinese architecture.

    This evening, relish a traditional Peking duck at a local restaurant.

    Overnight: New World Beijing Hotel
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 11

    BEIJING

    Start your day with an excursion to Mutianyu , where you visit one of the best-preserved sections of the Great Wall of China, a true wonder of the world. Admire the scenic surrounding countryside and later, enjoy a traditional lunch.

    Continue to travel back to Beijing, stop by to view the Bird’s Nest and Water Cube, fixtures of the 2008 Summer Olympics.

    Next, Hop into a pedicab to travel through a traditional Beijing hutong - a winding alleyway, once the lifeblood of old Beijing. Get an in-depth experience of the culture and people by interacting with a local family at their home, where you learn about their daily life and also participate in a dumpling-making lesson.

    Tonight, join your fellow travelers for cocktails and a farewell dinner.

    Overnight: New World Beijing Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 12

    DEPART BEIJING

    After breakfast you will be assisted with a transfer to the airport for your departure flight.

    Meal plan: B

Croatia: Jewel of the Coast

Programme Overview

Tour Zagreb, visit the Plitvice Lakes, meet an oyster fisherman who lets you sample his catch, privately tour the Mestrovic Gallery, visit Montenegro and hear from a survivor of the Balkan Wars.

  • Duration: 9 days/ 8 nights

    Highlights: Zagreb | Split | Brac | Split | Dubrovnik | Kotor

    A&K Advantages

    • Explore Split's Mestrovic Gallery, as its curator opens its doors to you for a private guided tour
    • Meet a local fisherman and sample his harvest of Croatia's famous oysters on our Culinary Connection
    • Make a poignant Neighborhood Connection, when you attend a private talk in Dubrovnik with a local resident who relates her experiences during the Balkan Wars
    • Meet some of the local traders and craftsmen of Dubrovnik
    • Immerse yourself in Croatia's musical heritage during a local folk band's performance and enjoy an authentic Musical Connection
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE ZAGREB

    Upon arrival at the airport, you will be welcomed and escorted by an A&K representative to the hotel.

    Tonight, Join your A&K representative and fellow guests for a gala welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Esplanade Zagreb
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    ZAGREB / THE STATELY CAPITAL

    Begin your exploration of Zagreb, the capital and the largest city of the Republic of Croatia, with an afternoon tour. Stroll through the famous parts of Zagreb, en-route visiting the National Theater. Continue to explore the Mimara Museum, the city’s largest art museum, also visit the Academy of Arts and Sciences.

    Later, take a tour of the Ban Jelacic Square, with Austro-Hungarian style architecture, is the true center of the city.

    Next from the Lower Town, board on an 1889 built funicular to reach the beautiful Upper Town quarter. The afternoon is at leisure to explore the capital at your own pace.

    Overnight: Esplanade Zagreb
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 3

    SPLIT/ PLITVICE LAKES

    This morning, take a beautiful drive to the coastal city of Split. Stop by to visit the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Plitvice Lakes, where emerald forests surround 16 shimmering turquoise lakes linked by a series of breathtaking waterfalls.

    Later , relish the lunch while you appreciate the mesmerizing beauty of the region.

    Next, set off for a travel to Split.

    Overnight: Radisson Blu Resort
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    BRAC / SOURCE OF STONE FOR THE WHITE HOUSE

    Today, embark on ferry ride to reach Brac, a Dalmatian island famous for its stone, used in Split’s Diocletian Palace and the White House in Washington, D.C.

    Continue the journey to visit Skrip, the oldest village on the island, and the Museum of Brac.

    The lunch for the day is specially arranged for you in a typical restaurant offering the local specialties of lamb, cheese, olives and wine

    Later, board a ferry for your return journey to Split.

    Overnight: Radisson Blu Resort
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 5

    SPLIT / THE EMPEROR’S PALACE

    This day, you get the opportunity to explore Split, starting with the Palace of the Emperor Diocletian at Peristil Square, where he made speeches. Also view the mausoleum, now a church.

    Later, visit the old town’s charming piazzas. Next is a private curator led tour of the Mestrovic Gallery to observe many of the masterpieces, carved in marble, bronze and wood.

    End your day with a visit to Riva, a coastal boardwalk lined with palm trees, to soak up the sun and views of the port and nearby islands.

    Overnight: Radisson Blu Resort
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 6

    DUBROVNIK / THE CULINARY ROAD

    Today, depart for Dubrovnik, an enchanting tourist destination. En-route visit Ston, world-renowned for its oysters. Here, get an in-depth culinary experience of the place as you get to sample one of the tasty delights from a local fisherman.

    Further, look forward to savor sumptuous seafood at a local restaurant followed by a wine tasting session at the Pelješac peninsula, renowned for its red wine.

    Set off for a travel to Dubrovnik.

    Overnight: Hilton Imperial Dubrovnik
    Meal plan:B, L, D

  • Day 7

    KOTOR, MONTENEGRO / Seaside Charms

    Start your day with a picturesque drive to marvelous Montenegro. Explore the walled city of Kotor, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Continue to visit its majestic 12th-century cathedral and Maritime Museum. Further, drive to arrive at the most southern fjord in Europe, Boka Kotorska, where precipitous mountains rise from azure waters. Here, aboard a boat to reach the Baroque Church of Our Lady of the Rocks.

    Later, Take a return travel to Dubrovnik.

    Overnight: Hilton Imperial Dubrovnik
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 8

    DUBROVNIK / The City Museum

    The morning is for a city tour of Dubrovnik. The Old Town, surrounded by impressive medieval stone walls and towers, is proclaimed a "city museum" by UNESCO. Here, you get an opportunity to interact with the markets local traders and craftspeople.

    Further, you get to make a very special neighborhood connection as you listen to emotional memories of the Balkan War by a war survivor. The remainder of afternoon is at leisure.

    Tonight, enjoy a traditional klapa performance as you savor the farewell dinner with your fellow guests.

    Overnight: Hilton Imperial Dubrovnik
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 9

    DEPART DUBROVNIK

    After breakfast, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

CRUISING THE MEKONG: INDOCHINA & ANGKOR WAT

Programme Overview

Sail for seven nights on the Mekong River aboard luxurious, all-suite 'Mekong Navigator.' Explore temple ruins, witness sunrise over Angkor Wat, and visit Phnom Penh and Ho Chi Minh City (Saigon).

  • Duration:14 days/ 13 nights

    Highlights: Bangkok | Siem Reap | Ho Chi Minh City | Kampong Cham | Phnom Penh | Kampong Chhnang | Chau Doc | Sa Dec

    A&K Advantages

    • Sail for seven nights on the mighty Mekong River on board the luxurious, all-suite 'Mekong Navigator'
    • Explore Angkor Wat it with your expert native guide
    • Visit vibrant Phnom Penh, Cambodia, and Saigon, Vietnam
    • Gain a deep insight of the Southeast Asian varied culture and traditions when you visit local villages and floating homes
    • Journey by Vietnamese sampan to Chau Doc and the Tay An Pagoda, with its Hindu and Middle Eastern influences
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive BANGKOK

    After your arrival in Bangkok, capital of Thailand, you will be met by an A&K representative and escorted to your hotel.

    Overnight: Novotel Suvarnabhumi Bangkok

  • Day 2

    SIEM REAP

    Today, depart for Siem Reap, Cambodia, near the timeless ruins of Angkor Wat. After arrival, you will be assisted with transfer to your hotel. Begin the exploration with a travel to Khmer Banteay Kdei, a Buddhist stone temple also known as the Citadel of Monks. Continue to view Srah Srang, a baray(artificial body of water) at Angkor, a popular architectural element of the Khmer Empire.

    Later, ride on a reumork (tuk-tuk) for an orientation tour of Siem Reap.

    This evening, enjoy the hospitality with a welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Sofitel Angkor
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 3

    SIEM REAP

    Begin your day early today to explore the splendid Ta Prohm, entangled in the surrounding jungle foliage. Climb over its crumbling stonework and marvel at the tree roots dripping down the temple's walls.

    Enjoy breakfast at your hotel before you set forth to discover the ancient fortified city of Angkor Thom and the spectacular Bayon Temple. Further view the Terrace of the Elephant, Covered in stunning bas-relief sculptures of elephants and Terrace of the Leper King to observe its detailed stone carvings.

    Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant. Then, continue to see the Angkor Centre for the Conservation of Biodiversity, known for its wildlife rescue, animal rehabilitation and endangered species breeding. Later this evening, enjoy watching the sun go down at Angkor Wat, with the outline of the buildings reflecting in the moat

    Overnight: Sofitel Angkor
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    SIEM REAP

    This day, drive to the vine-entangled temple of Beng Mealea, an incredible hidden jungle temple and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Continue to see Banteay Srei, a beautiful 10th-century Hindu temple complex, with intricate pink sandstone carvings.

    Relax over lunch before you visit the Cambodia Landmine Museum. Next is a tour of the craft center at Artisan d’Angkor for a deep insight into the remarkable skilled workmanship of traditional handicrafts in Cambodia.

    Overnight: Sofitel Angkor
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    'MEKONG NAVIGATOR'

    Continue the journey as you drive along the lush countryside to Kampong Cham on the mighty Mekong River. After arrival, embark your luxurious home for the next seven nights, the ‘Mekong Navigator’, designed in the French colonial style, 34-cabin ‘Mekong Navigator’ lends a sophisticated, yet comfortable ambience while cruising the Mekong

    After settling in your spacious cabin or suite, proceed for a special welcome dinner along with your fellow guests.

    Overnight: 'Mekong Navigator'
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 6

    'MEKONG NAVIGATOR’

    Pause the cruise for a visit to the local secondary school at Wat Hanchey, a hilltop pagoda with panoramic views of the Mekong, also receive a traditional Buddhist Water Blessing.

    Continue the cruise to Kampong Cham, where you visit Phnom Pros and Phnom Srei, also called Man Hill and Woman Hill.

    Proceed on the cruise to Phnom Penh, modern-day capital city of Cambodia and once called the Pearl of Asia for the beauty of its French-colonial architecture.

    Overnight: 'Mekong Navigator'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    'MEKONG NAVIGATOR'

    Enjoy your breakfast and set forth for a scenic cruise along the beautiful Tonle Sap River. Embark on a private excursion boat to arrive at the vibrant floating village of Kampong Chhnang, where you observe its local life.

    Later, travel back to Phnom Penh.

    Overnight: 'Mekong Navigator'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    'MEKONG NAVIGATOR’

    Begin this morning with a cyclo ride to tour the Phnom Penh’s spectacular Royal Palace and the National Museum, home to one of the world’s largest collections of Khmer art.

    The remaining of the afternoon is for you to explore the place at your own convenience.

    Overnight: 'Mekong Navigator'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 9

    'MEKONG NAVIGATOR’

    This day, experience one of the darkest periods in Cambodian history with a somber walk through the killing fields at Choeung Ek and the Tuol Sleng prison.

    Further, ride on a Vietnamese sampan to visit the picturesque river village of Chau Doc, in the heart of the Mekong delta. En-route visit the beautiful domed Tay An Pagoda.

    Overnight: 'Mekong Navigator'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 10

    'MEKONG NAVIGATOR’

    Cruise across the Mekong River to Vietnam, where you observe a change in the architectural style.

    The remaining of the afternoon is for you to explore the place at your own convenience.

    Overnight: 'Mekong Navigator'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 11

    'MEKONG NAVIGATOR’

    This day, ride through the Mekong Delta to Sa Dec on a sampan. Disembark for walk through a bustling, local market.

    Enjoy lunch on board before you disembark the cruise in Cai Be for tour of a rice factory, where rice paper, wine, and candies are made.

    Continue the voyage to My Tho.

    Overnight: 'Mekong Navigator'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 12

    HO CHI MINH CITY

    This morning, disembark the ‘Mekong Navigator’ and transfer to Ho Chi Minh City, formerly Saigon. Visit Quan Am Pagoda, a Chinese style Buddhist pagoda, with colorfully decorated garden, pond, courtyard and interior.

    Later, walk through the Binh Tay Market, located in an elegant old building. Enjoy lunch followed by an afternoon at leisure.

    Overnight: Caravelle Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 13

    HO CHI MINH CITY

    Today, travel into the countryside to discover the expansive Cu Chi Tunnel network, a key Vietcong resource during the Vietnam War, and meet a war veteran who recounts his experiences in the tunnels.

    Travel back to Ho Chi Minh City for lunch. Then, set on a city tour, soaking in the atmosphere on your last full day here. Tonight, gather for a farewell dinner to celebrate your journey.

    Overnight: Caravelle Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 14

    DEPART HO CHI MINH CITY

    After breakfast, transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

Chile & Argentina: Patagonia & Beyond

Programme Overview

Begin in Santiago, meet an oysterman in the Lake District to sample his catch, visit a Patagonian ranch, sail on Lago Argentino and enjoy a tasting of Argentine wines and stroll La Boca in Buenos Aires.

  • Duration: 13 days/ 12 nights

    Highlights: Santiago | Puerto Mont | Lake District | Punta Arenas | Puerto Natales | El Calafate | Buenos Aires

    A&K Advantages

    Discover your destination through these local connections.

    • Enjoy Chile's picturesque Lake District and meet a local fisherman to sample his harvest of oysters
    • Learn about life in the wilds with a visit to a Patagonian ranch
    • Explore the Patagonia national park of Torres del Paine - remote, rugged and windswept
    • Admire the Perito Moreno Glacier, hearing it crack and roar like an incredible living mass
    • Sail among icebergs and past glaciers on Lago Argentino
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE SANTIAGO

    After your arrival in Santiago, capital of Chile, you are met and assisted with transfer to your hotel. Visit the Palacio de La Moneda Presidential Palace, an architectural landmark and the Plaza de Armas, a beautifully landscaped plaza that includes a mixture of patterned trees and benches with its impressive Cathedral. After viewing the Central Post Office, embark on a funicular up San Cristobal Hill to admire the stunning statue of the Blessed Virgin Mary.

    This evening, enjoy a festive welcome dinner with Chilean wines at a local restaurant.

    Overnight: The Singular
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    PUERTO MONT / IN THE CHARMING LAKE DISTRICT

    Today, depart for Puerto Mont, a port city in the Chilean Lake District. Later, drive to Frutillara, known as the "City of Music" a charming village founded by German settlers.

    Walk past Teatro del Lago Concert Hall to take in the breath-taking views of Osorno Volcano. Continue to view the German Museum and later, enjoy lunch at a local restaurant.

    Overnight: Hotel Colonos del Sur
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 3

    LAKE DISTRICT / HUMBOLDTS & MAGELLANICS

    Begin with your ferry ride to Chiloé Island followed by a drive inland for an oyster tasting. Continue to explore Puñihuil, a small fishing village, sight the Magellanic and Humboldt penguins that live and nest there. This afternoon, relish a lunch of local cuisine.

    Overnight: Hotel Colonos del Sur
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    PUNTA ARENAS & PUERTO NATALES / RANCH LIFE

    This day, depart for Punta Arenas. Enjoy lunch after arrival. Set off on a tour of the incredibly beautiful Patagonian ranch. Continue with a drive to Puerto Natales, where you check into your hotel and later join your fellow guests for diner.

    Overnight: The Singular
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Days 5-7

    PUERTO NATALES / DISCOVERING PATAGONIA

    Enjoy the next three days exploring the spectacular Patagonia, led by your expert guides. You may choose from a wide range of exclusive expeditions and activities, such as horseback riding, trekking, kayaking, mountain biking and birding, all designed to reveal Patagonia at its most authentic.

    Experience the local culture and traditions by interacting with the local residents at their ranches. Learn about their history and customs while admiring the beautiful landscapes.

    Overnight: The Singular
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    EL CALAFATE, ARGENTINA / GATEWAY TO GLACIERS

    This day, depart for the national capital of glaciers- El Calafate in Argentina.

    After your arrival, you will be assisted with transfer to your hotel.

    Overnight: Imago Hotel & Spa
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 9

    EL CALAFATE / LOS GLACIARES NATIONAL PARK

    Begin your day to explore Los Glaciares National Park. Visit the magnificent Perito Moreno Glacier, one of the most beautiful glacier formations in the world and listen for its occasional crack and roar.

    Later, enjoy lunch and set off for your return journey to the hotel and an afternoon at leisure.

    Overnight: Imago Hotel & Spa
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 10

    EL CALAFATE / STUNNING LAGO ARGENTINO

    Cruise through the icebergs of Lago Argentino to view the magnificent glaciers.

    Next, set off for a drive to Estancia Cristina, where you visit the Costumbrista Museum. Later, drive through the mountain roads to view the stunning Upsala Glacier and Lake Guillermo.

    The lunch for the day will be served at Estancia.

    Overnight: Imago Hotel & Spa
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 11

    BUENOS AIRES / ARGENTINA’S CAPITAL

    Today, depart for Buenos Aires, the elegant capital of Argentina with European flair set. After arrival you will be assisted with transfer to your hotel. Continue for a walking orientation tour. Further, enjoy wine tasting at a local cellar/bar, where you learn about some of Argentina’s famed regional wines. End your day with dinner at a local restaurant.

    Overnight: Hotel Melia
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 12

    BUENOS AIRES / TOURING THE HISTORIC CITY

    This morning, take a guided tour of Buenos Aires. Begin with a visit to Plaza de Mayo, the main square. Continue through the San Telmo neighborhood to La Boca. Walk down the picture-perfect Caminito Street, a street museum and a traditional alley.

    Next is a tour of one of the best theaters in the world, the Teatro Colon, then visit La Recoleta Cemetery, a must visit in Buenos Aires.

    This evening, enjoy a gala farewell dinner along with your fellow guests at a local restaurant.

    Overnight: Hotel Melia
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 13

    DEPART BUENOS AIRES

    After breakfast, transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Overnight: Hotel Melia
    Meal plan: B
