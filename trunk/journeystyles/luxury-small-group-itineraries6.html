CHINA, TIBET & THE YANGTZE

Programme Overview

Explore remarkable cultures on an intriguing tour of Asia, from China's capital and industrial hub, Beijing to Lhasa, the heart of Tibet and highest city on earth.

  • Duration: 17 Days / 18 Nights

    Highlights: Beijing | Tibet | Yangtze

    A&K Advantages

    • Three-night cruise on the Yangtze River in a deluxe cabin aboard boutique, all-balcony Sanctuary 'Yangzi Explorer,' the finest five-star vessel sailing the river
    • A trip to Potala Palace, Lhasa, Tibet's spiritual capital
    • Private tour with an on-site archaeologist at the Terra Cotta Warriors exhibit
    • Excursion to well-preserved Mutianyu section of the Great Wall
    • Private boat in Leshan to see the largest stone-carved Buddha in the world
    • Visit to an artist's studio in Shanghai's up-and-coming M-50 Contemporary Art District
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE BEIJING, CHINA

    On arrival in Beijing, China’s capital and cultural hub, you will be received by an A&K representative who will assist you with transfer to your luxurious accommodation.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    BEIJING

    This morning, walk or ride up on a cable car to the well-preserved Mutianyu section of the Great Wall of China for sweeping views of the surrounding countryside.

    Your first night in China awaits a scrumptious welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    BEIJING

    Start your day with a tai chi class at the Temple of Heaven, one of the iconic sites of Beijing and ride like a local on a pedicab through a hutong.

    In the afternoon, take a brief stop for some lip-smacking local Chinese lunch. Explore Tiananmen Square and walk in the footsteps of emperors in the Forbidden City which was the seat of imperial power for over 600 years.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    XIAN

    This morning you will be assisted for a short flight to Xian. The last stop on the Great Silk Road at one time, Xian is now a melting pot of Asian and Middle Eastern influences. Take a guided tour of the Yangling Museum, site of Han Dynasty tombs and view its underground exhibition hall where you stand on a huge glass floor and watch the workers do their job without harming the relics. This visit proves to be quite an experience for all travelers.

    After a flavorful lunch, view the Great Mosque and Muslim Quarter on a fascinating market visit.

    Spend a calm and stress-free evening in the spa at your lavish hotel followed by a delicious local dinner.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    XIAN

    Start your morning with a heavenly finger licking breakfast. View the incomparable Terra Cotta Warriors. Lost to the world for two millennia until accidentally discovered by a farmer in 1974, you explore them with an archaeologist who will be your guide throughout the expedition.

    After lunch, enter the Ancient Gate of Xian’s City Wall- it is tall, long and thick! You may also cycle along its ramparts or even take a hiking tour which starts from the Yong Ning Gate, the most time-honored gate among the four main gates.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 6

    CHENGDU

    This morning, you will be escorted for your flight to Chengdu to visit the Chengdu Panda Breeding and Research Center. Here, you are sure to have a unique experience, where you come face to face with the National Animal of China, Panda, roaming free in its natural habitat.

    The evening is at leisure.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 7

    CHENGDU

    Today you travel off the beaten path to the relaxed riverside city of Leshan, where a private boat ride brings you before its 1,300-year-old, stone-carved Buddha. At 233 feet tall, it is the largest stone Buddha in the world. Behold this epic sculpture, learn how it was carved and why it was positioned to overlook the confluence of three rivers — the Minjiang, Dadu and Qingyi.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    LHASA

    This morning, take a short comfortable flight to Lhasa, the heart and soul of Tibet. Lhasa is one of the highest cities on earth at 11,450 feet and your home for the next three nights.

    Enjoy an afternoon at leisure to relax and acclimatize.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 9

    LHASA

    Rise early this morning and visit the vibrant Ani Tsangkung Nunnery and reveal the spiritual heart of Tibet at the sacred Jokhang, a 1,300-year old temple housing Tibet’s most revered golden Buddha.

    After a wonderful palatable Chinese lunch, take a stroll in Lhasa’s Barkhor Bazaar and visit renowned Sera Monastery to witness the world famous "monk debates."

    In the evening, discover Norbulingka, which served as the traditional summer residence of the successive Dalai Lamas from the 1780s up until the 14th Dalai Lama’s exile in 1959. A visit to this UNESCO World Heritage Site is an experience of a lifetime.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 10

    LHASA

    This day, visit Sanggye Dhunghu, where some 200 statues are carved into a dramatic rock face. Visualize Tibet’s 2000 yr old religious roots through the Potala Palace which is a 13-story architectural masterpiece replete with 1,000 rooms and 10,000 shrines. The beauty of the palace is further magnified with the spellbinding presence of Mt. Everest. The palace was also the former winter residence of the Dalai Lama.

    Indulge in some tangy flavorsome dinner at your hotel this evening.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 11

    CHONGQING

    This morning, fly to Chongqing to board your boutique luxury river cruiser for a three-night journey through the Three Gorges region. The evening awaits a documentary about the Three Gorges Dam Project in the ship’s Tang Theater followed by a delicious multi-cuisine dinner.

    Overnight: Yangzi Explorer
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 12

    After a fresh and crunchy breakfast, disembark for a shore excursion to Fengdu where you visit a village that was relocated due to construction of the Three Gorges Dam. Meet and interact with a local family, visit a kindergarten and take in a kaleidoscope of images at a farmers market.

    Call it a day with the Captain’s Reception, followed by a delicious dinner.

    Overnight: Yangzi Explorer
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 13

    THE THREE GORGES

    Early this morning, ‘Yangzi Explorer’ navigates the Qutang and Wu Gorges before anchoring at Badong, where you board a private ferry and travel up the Shennong Stream. Disembark to board local “pea pod” boats which are pulled by Tujia boatmen, members of a local minority culture famed for their boat-building skills and keen knowledge of the Yangtze River’s currents to witness the beautiful picturesque scenery of Bamboo Gorge.

    Enjoy the evening on the deck of Yangzi Explorer, observing stunning panoramic views at leisure.

    Overnight: Yangzi Explorer
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 14

    SHANGHAI

    This morning, you will be assisted for your flight to Shanghai where you will be met by an A&K representative for your transfer to your accommodation, located on the city’s historic Bund.

    Afternoon is at leisure.

    Enjoy a rooftop scenic sundowner while taking in the bird’s eye view of Shanghai’s Bund and tree lined Pudong districts

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 15

    SHANGHAI

    Explore China’s dynamic and lively Shanghai. Visit Zhujiajiao, a water village evoking China’s past. Stroll on its centuries-old lanes and board asampan (wooden boat) to glide along its main canal.

    Spend a phenomenal evening watching a fantastic Shanghai Acrobatic show.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 16

    SHANGHAI

    Wander through the streets of Old Town and visit the 16th-century Yu Garden where you may see the beautiful Pavilion of Listening to Billows with rockeries and serene ponds. Stroll the Bund, Shanghai’s most popular promenade and explore artist studios and galleries in the M-50 Contemporary Art District.

    This evening suit up and rejoice over a festive farewell dinner.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 17

    DEPART SHANGHAI

    After breakfast, you will be assisted for your flight back home or for the post-tour extension.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal plan: B

  • Mongolia Pre-Tour Extension

    Day 1

    ULAAN BAATAR

    Arrive in Ulaan Baatar, transfer to your hotel.

    This evening rejoice over a welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Kempinski Hotel Khan Palace
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    GOBI DESERT

    Fly to the vast Gobi Desert, visit scenic mountain gorges lined with glaciers.

    Overnight: Three Camel Lodge
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    GOBI DESERT

    Stop at Bayan Zag, where dinosaur egg fossils were found amid the Flaming Cliffs. Visit a camel-breeding family and ride a Bactrian camel.

    Overnight: Three Camel Lodge
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Return to Ulaan Baatar

    Tour Gandan Monastery and visit Zanabazar Fine Arts Museum with time to shop.

    Overnight: Kempinski Hotel Khan Palace
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    DEPART MONGOLIA

    After breakfast, you will be assisted with your return flight to Beijing and onward connections.

    Meal plan: B

    Available on the May 18, Jun 15, Aug 10, Sep 14, Sep 21 and Sep 28 departures

  • Guilin & Hong Kong Post-Tour Extension

    Day 1

    ARRIVE GUILIN

    Fly from Shanghai to Guilin where you will be received by an A&K representative who will help you with your transfer to your hotel.

    This day, visit Reed Flute Cave, Fubo Hill and Elephant Trunk Hill.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    HONGKONG

    Wake up to an early morning for an exciting bamboo rafting from Yangdi to Xingping after which you will be transferred to Yangshuo, a popular tourist destination with karst peaks, rivers, caves and laid back cafes. Ride a bicycle or golf cart to go around in the county.

    Later in the evening, fly to Hong Kong.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 3

    HONGKONG

    After breakfast, depart for Sheung Wan and visit a local herbal medicine market and Man Mo Temple. Ride a sampan to a floating village in Aberdeen.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    DEPART HONGKONG

    This morning you will be assisted with your transfer to the airport for your flight back home.

    Meal plan: B

    Available on the May 4, May 18, Jun 15, Sep 14, Sep 21, Sep 28, Oct 5, Oct 12 and Oct 19 departures

Splendors of Budapest, Vienna & Prague

Programme Overview

Discover the three jewels of Central Europe and celebrate their artistic and cultural traditions. Cruise the Danube through Budapest, visit Schönbrunn Palace in Vienna and meet an artist in Prague.

  • Duration: 9 Days/ 8 nights
    Highlights: Budapest | Vienna | Prague

    A&K Advantages

    • Discover the three jewels of Central Europe and experience their artistic and cultural traditions alongside local A&K experts
    • Embark on a private after-hours visit to the magnificent Schönbrunn Palace
    • Sample mouth-watering specialties and fine Austrian wines at a traditional heurigen (wine tavern), which dates back more than 200 years
    • Privately visit Budapest's State Opera House, as well as the library of the Strahov Monastery in Prague
    • Enjoy a private evening cruise on the Danube
    • Share lunch with a local chef, who offers insights into seasonal dishes, on a Chef's Table culinary experience
    • Ride Like a Local from Vienna to Prague aboard a highspeed train, a popular mode of travel in Europe
    • On an insider access Artisan Experience, visit the Manto Gallery in Prague and meet the artist
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE BUDAPEST

    Today, arrive in Budapest, whose broad avenues and turn-of-the-century architecture has earned it the name “Paris of Eastern Europe,” and like Paris, it is a cultural mecca for writers, artists and musicians.

    After arrival, you will be met by an A&K representative, who will assist with your transfer to the hotel.

    This evening, join your host and interact with your fellow travelers at a welcome dinner in the evening.

    Overnight: Dan Tel Aviv Hotel
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Budapest

    As you begin with your exploration of Budapest, visit Heroes’ Square and pause to view the Great Synagogue, the largest functioning synagogue in Europe. Then, visit St. Stephen’s Basilica, the city's largest church, showcasing the largest bell in all of Hungary. Moving further, privately explore the beautiful neo-Renaissance State Opera House.

    Afterward, take a leisure stroll through the streets bustling with merchants and local shoppers in the Central Market Hall. Enjoy lunch at Gundel, the city’s most famous restaurant, before you crossover the river to arrive in Buda.

    Experience the charm of Buda as you visit Matthias Church, the 13th-century coronation cathedral of a Hungarian king. Continue to visit Fisherman’s Bastion, a terrace built in neo-Gothic and neo- Romanesque styles, offering outstanding views of the city below.

    After dinner at leisure, look forward for an enjoyable and relaxing private cruise along the Danube.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Gresham Palace Budapest
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 3

    Vienna

    Today, depart from Budapest for Vienna. During this beautiful travel visit heurigen, a wine tavern more than 200 years old. Savor its sumptuous dishes and fine Austrian wine. The heurigen is a perfect place to experience Gemütlichkeit, a German word for a situation that induces a good mood.

    Embark on a guided walking tour to experience the fabulous imperial city of Vienna. Visit the Hofburg Palace, once center of the gigantic Habsburg Empire, where you admire the glittering collection of Imperial regalia in the Treasury. Currently the residence of the president of Austria, the Hofburg Palace has expanded over the years, adding wings designed by top architects and named for some of the great nations of the world.

    Further discover the Gothic St. Stephen's Cathedral, whose magnificent structure enshrines the heart of Vienna.

    End the day with an à la carte dinner at the hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel Sacher Wien
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Vienna

    This morning, begin with an enjoyable panoramic tour of Vienna. Visit the famous Hundertwasser House, one of Austria's architectural highlights .Continue to view the Upper Belvedere, showcasing exquisite sculptures, and the Secession Building, an architectural manifesto of the rebel artistic movement.

    This afternoon, enjoy lunch followed by time at leisure to shop and explore at your own pace.

    Later, set out for an A&K exclusive after-hours private tour of Schönbrunn Palace, where a six-year-old Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart once performed for the Viennese Royal Court.

    Overnight: Hotel Sacher Wien
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 5

    Vienna

    Travel to Bratislava, Slovakia’s capital, where you begin with a guided walking tour of its Old Town, once a jewel of the Austro- Hungarian Empire.

    This afternoon, experience the local gastronomy as you enjoy lunch at a local restaurant with its esteemed chef, who takes you through his life as a restaurateur and provides you with insights into the seasonal dishes.

    Return to Vienna and spend the remaining day at your convenience

    Overnight: Hotel Sacher Wien
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 6

    Prague

    Today, depart for Prague by an express train and enjoy a packed lunch on board. After arrival, explore the city with an introductory tour, led by your tour director.

    Later, enjoy a Scenic Sundowner at a popular restaurant, with spectacular views overlooking the Vltava River and Charles Bridge.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Prague
    Meal plan:B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Prague

    This morning, explore the scenic place with a walking tour of Josefov, the city’s Jewish Quarter, which remained a walled ghetto until 1848. Continue to view the Jewish Quarter, birthplace of Franz Kafka, known for his existentialist novel The Metamorphosis, and considered one of the most influential writers of the 20th century.

    Later, visit the Old Jewish Cemetery, site of 12,000 graves, and the Old-New Synagogue, Europe’s oldest active synagogue, completed in 1270.

    After lunch, set off for a private visit to the Estates Theater, one of the most beautiful theatres in Europe built in the Enlightenment era and world famous for some important personalities performing there, including the Mozart .Unwind here with refreshing glass of Champagne and a mini concert (subject to rehearsal schedules). Later, proceed for an unforgettable Artisan Visit as you meet a local Czech artist, Tony Manto, at his gallery and learn about the art of glassblowing.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Prague
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 8

    Prague

    Privately explore the Baroque library halls of the Strahov Monastery, with its glorious Baroque decorations and incredible frescoes, which reside amidst detailed stucco ceiling decorations. Admire the religious artifacts at the Loreto Chapel and visit the Gothic St. Vitus Cathedral, the largest and the most important temple in Prague.

    Afterward, walk past the magnificent Charles Bridge, Prague's most stunning bridge spanning 16 arches and lined with 30 Baroque statues of religious figures.

    Tonight, conclude your beautiful journey with a farewell dinner at a nearby local restaurant.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Prague
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 9

    Depart Prague

    After breakfast, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport to board your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

  • Warsaw & Krakow Pre-Tour Extension

    Day 1-2

    Arrive Warsaw

    After your arrival in Warsaw, Poland, you will be met by an A&K representative, who will assist with your transfer to the hotel.

    The next day, enjoy a guided tour of Warsaw highlights.

    Overnight: MaMaison Hotel Le Regina Warsaw
    Meal plan: D (Day 1); B (Day 2)

  • Day 3-4

    Krakow

    Today, drive to Krakow, en route exploring Czestochowa, world famous city for the Pauline monastery of Jasna Gora which is the home of the Black Madonna painting, a shrine of the Virgin Mary.

    The next day, explore the beautiful city of Krakow with a guided tour. Stroll through the Market Square and visit Oskar Schindler’s former factory.

    Overnight: Sheraton Krakow Hotel
    Meal plan: B (Day 3); B L (Day 4)

  • Day 5-6

    Depart Krakow

    This day, explore the infamous Auschwitz prison camp, once a network of German Nazi concentration camps and now a memorial.

    Later, return to Krakow. The next day, depart for Budapest.

    Overnight: Sheraton Krakow Hotel (Day 5)
    Meal plan: B (Days 5 & 6)

Treasures of Croatia

Programme Overview

  • Experience the best of Croatia and Montenegro, including two nights on the idyllic island of Hvar. Stop in Ston to sample its famous oysters, dine at a family-owned winery and tour scenic Dubrovnik.

    Duration: 8 Days/ 7 Nights

    Highlights: Split | Hvar| Dubrovnik

    A&K Advantages

    • Discover the best of Croatia and Montenegro including three nights in Dubrovnik and two in Hvar
    • Ride Like a Local when you board a public ferry to spend two nights on the idyllic island of Hvar, known for its sunny days and medieval buildings dating back to Venetian rule
    • Visit the fishing town of Ston and sample its famous oysters
    • Stroll Split's lively daily market on a true Market Experience
    • Tour a family-owned winery for a tasting and lunch with a family member on a Chef's Table culinary experience
    • Make an authentic Artisan Visit to a silk-weaving workshop
    • Take a boat out into the fjord of Kotor to visit the tiny Church of Our Lady
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Split

    Upon arrival at the airport,you will be welcomed by an A&K representative and assisted with transfer to the hotel.

    Tonight, join your A&K Tour Director and fellow guests for a gala welcome dinner at the hotel.

    Overnight: Le MéridienLav
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    Split

    Explore the coastal city of Split,beginning with the Palace of Diocletian,one of the most imposing Roman ruins in existence.Continue tovisit the nearby St. Domnius Cathedral,one of the emblems of the town of Split.

    Walk through Split’s Old Town and harbor-front promenade. Further, experience the local culture as you visit the daily market. Stop at Meštrovic Gallery,exhibiting many of the masterpieces, carved in marble, in bronze and wood.

    This evening, enjoy dinner with a klapa concert.

    Overnight: Le MéridienLav
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 3

    Hvar

    This day, board a public ferry and ride like a local along the splendid Dalmatian coastline to charmingHvar, the longest and sunniest Croatian island - one of the most beautiful islands in the world.

    In the afternoon stop for lunch at a local restaurant. Continue for a stroll through the town to view the Cathedral and Arsenal.

    Overnight: Adriana Hvar Spa Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    Hvar

    Today, get the opportunity to visit a family-owned winery, where you savor lunch followed by a winetasting session.

    Explore the town’s center and view the gleaming limestone Spanjol Fortress.

    Overnight: Adriana Hvar Spa Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 5

    Dubrovnik

    Today, depart for Dubrovnik, an enchanting tourist destination. En-route visit Ston, world-renowned for its oysters. Here, get an in-depth culinary experience as you sample fresh oysters and other delightsin a typical Dalmatian restaurant.

    Overnight: Hotel Excelsior Dubrovnik
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    Dubrovnik

    Begin the day with a guided walking tour of Dubrovnik. View the majestic Franciscan and Dominican monasteries, the Rector’s Palace and Dubrovnik Cathedral.

    Then, board a cable car to ascend Mount Srd, offering spectacular city views. Further, observe silk weavers at work on a fascinating Artisan Visit in Konavle. Later, return to Dubrovnik and unwind witha Scenic Sundowner at your hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel Excelsior Dubrovnik
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 7

    Dubrovnik

    Start your day with a picturesque drive to marvelous Montenegro.Explore the walled city of Kotor, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Continue with a drive to the Bay of Kotor fjord for lunch, and further board a boat to visit a tiny church.

    Later, take a return journey to Dubrovnik, where you enjoy your farewell dinner in a fine restaurant.

    Overnight: Hotel Excelsior Dubrovnik
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    Depart Dubrovnik

    After breakfast, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Return back to the ‘Eclipse,’ relax before gathering in the dining room for another wonderful meal. Explore the mysteries of the night sky with a fascinating astronomy lecture on deck; peruse the ship’s excellent library; or gather for cocktails in the lounge to cap a day of extraordinary discoveries.

    Meal plan: B

    • Lake Bled & Zagreb Pre-Tour Extension

  • Day 1

    Arrive Ljubljana

    Upon arrival in Ljubljana, Slovenia, you will be welcomed and escorted byan A&K representative to the hotel. Settle into your picturesque hotel, with breathtaking views of the cliff-top castle and the Julian Alps.

    Overnight: Grand Hotel Toplice
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    Ljubljana

    Today, visit Bled Castle to view the large collection of 16th- to 18th-century weaponry in its museum, offering an overview of the eventful history of Bled.

    Later, Travel to St. Mary Islandon a boat .Tour the island and further, join your local guide to walk Vintgar Gorge,one of the most popular natural features in Slovenia.

    Overnight: Grand Hotel Toplice
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 3

    Zagreb

    This day, travel to Ljubljana, capital of Slovenia. View its majestic 18th century cathedral, spectacular Baroque palace and vibrant frescoes.

    Later, drive to Zagreb, Croatia.

    Overnight: Esplanade Zagreb
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 4

    Zagreb

    Explore Zagreb,the capital and the largest city of the Republic of Croatia. View the National Theater and the Well of Life by Ivan Meštrovic.

    Later, visit the Meštrovic Atelier to observe the artist’s works.

    Overnight: Esplanade Zagreb
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 5

    Split

    This morning, travel to the coastal city of Split. Stop by to visit the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Plitvice Lakes, where emerald forests surround 16 shimmering turquoise lakes linked by a series of breathtaking waterfalls. Explore the National Park followed by lunch.

    Later, continue to Split.

    Meal plan: B, L

Myanmar & the Irrawadd

Programme Overview

Explore the untouched Myanmar, experience the tranquil Inle Lake, cruise through the Irrawaddy River on new Sanctuary 'Ananda' and further, enjoy a hot air balloon ride over Bagan and discover Myanmar’s countless pagodas and temples.

  • Duration: 10 Days/ 9 Nights

    Highlights: Yangon | Heho | Mandalay | Bagan

    A&K Advantages

    • Cruise the legendary Irrawaddy River in luxury on the newly launched, all-balcony Sanctuary 'Ananda'
    • Spend two nights on Inle Lake and enjoy a private viewing of a traditional Intha boat race
    • Capture the photographs of a lifetime on a magical hot air balloon ride, soaring above the 2,000 pagodas and temples of Bagan's plain
    • Travel off the beaten path on a tour of MusmeahYeshua, the last remaining Jewish synagogue in Myanmar
    • Ride Like a Local by horse cart in Bagan
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE YANGON

    After your arrival in Yangon, you are met by an A&K representative and escorted to your hotel.

    Overnight: Sule, Shangri-La, Yangon

  • Day 2

    YANGON

    Begin your journey of Myanmar, a profoundly Buddhist country with many prevailing ancient traditions.Take a walking tour of the vibrant Chinatown, its streets filled with woodenshuttered houses, shops and food stalls, with an non-crowded and authentic atmosphere.

    Further, viewthe MusmeahYeshua, a colonial relicand the last remaining Jewish synagogue in Myanmar, strategically located in the heart of city center. Experience the lively culture with a walk through the bazaar at Scott Market (Bogyoke Market) amid shops filled with antiques, old coins, Burmese jade and handicrafts.

    Later, enjoy lunch at a local restaurant beforeyou visit the Reclining Buddha at ChaukHtatGyi Pagoda, about the height of a five storey building with an expressive enamel face. Read the life story of the Buddha embossed on the soles of the statue’s staggered feet. Continue to explore the Buddhist culture with a visit to the 2,600 years oldShwedagon Pagoda, the most sacred Buddhist site in Myanmar. Visit this oldest historical pagoda in the world andadmire its round, gilded stupa capturing the rays of the warm afternoon sun, lending a crowning moment to your Burmese adventure.

    Conclude your day with a warm welcome dinner at Le Planteur Restaurant.

    Overnight: Sule, Shangri-La, Yangon
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    HEHO

    Today, take a flight to Heho and after arrival enjoy lunch at a local restaurant. Later, take a transfer to the scenic Inle Lake and further to your resort, by a private boat.
