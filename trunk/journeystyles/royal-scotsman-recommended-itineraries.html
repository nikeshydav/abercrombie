Classic Journey Aboard 'Belmond Royal Scotsman'

Programme Overview

  • On this quintessential train journey, follow the coast to Keith en route to Inverness, the capital of the Highlands. Visit the Glen Ord Distillery and stop in picturesque Plockton for a boat trip to the Isle of Skye. You are personally invited to visit magnificent Ballindalloch Castle in the Spey Valley. At Rothiemurchus Estate, take time to enjoy fishing, clay pigeon shooting or a guided walk though stunning landscapes.

    Duration: 5 days/ 4 nights

    Highlights: Keith | Kyle of Lochalsh | Boat of Garten | Dundee

  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE KEITH

    Early this afternoon, board the Belmond Royal Scotsman, the luxury train travelling through the Scottish Highlands. From Edinburgh's Waverley Station you travel northwards across the Firth of Forth passing the cantilever Forth Railway Bridge, a celebrated Scottish landmark. The Forth Railway Bridge is considered as a milestone in the development of railway civil engineering, and known as the eighth wonder of the world.

    Spend a leisurely afternoon, relaxing over a cup of tea as you pass through Arbroath, Montrose and Aberdeen. After your arrival in Keith, end your day with an informal dinner followed by an overnight stabling.

    Overnight: Belmond Royal Scotsman
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    KYLE OF LOCHALSH

    Begin the day with a leisurely breakfast aboard the train. Depart Keith and travel westwards to Inverness, the beautiful capital of the Highlands. Travel on to Muir of Ord, where you disembark to visit Glen Ord Distillery, the only remaining single malt Scotch whisky distillery on the Black Isle.

    Return to the train for lunch onboard. Travel along one of the most scenic routes in Britain en route for Kyle of Lochalsh, a village on the northwest coast of Scotland. The line passes Loch Luichart and the Torridon mountains, which are so old that they contain no fossils. Geologists believe that they were formed before any life began.

    Pass by Achnasheen, ascending towards Luib summit and Achnashellach forest, later descending to Strathcarron. Your train journey traces the edge of Loch Carron through Attadale, Stromeferry and Duncraig.

    Arriving at Plockton, you have the opportunity to self-explore this charming Scottish Highland village. Further, take a boat trip to observe the colony of wild seals, while you take in the splendid views across the Isle of Skye to the Applecross Mountains. Cross by a woodland walk, leading to a viewpoint overlooking the loch. Alternatively, you could visit the local art gallery or simply meander through this charming village. Later, rendezvous at the Plockton Hotel; where there is time to enjoy a dram or two before rejoining the motorcoach.

    Return to the train in Kyle of Lochalsh for your overnight stabling.

    This evening, enjoy a formal dinner and afterwards there is entertainment in the Observation Car. Marvel at the view from the train's verandah as you gaze over Loch Alsh to the Isle of Skye.

    Overnight: Belmond Royal Scotsman
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    BOAT OF GARTEN

    This day, after breakfast, you may choose to visit Eilean Donan Castle before the train heads north to Inverness. Further, travel southwards to Carrbridge, disembark here for your visit to Ballindalloch, one of Scotland's most romantic castles with magnificent gardens surrounded by the wonderful Spey valley.

    Later, return to the train in Boat of Garten on the private Strathspey Railway for overnight stabling.

    Begin the evening with an informal dinner followed by an interactive session with a Highlander, who brings to life the stories of Scottish heroes. Gain a true sense of the past and learn how life in the Highlands has changed over the centuries.

    Overnight: Belmond Royal Scotsman
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    DUNDE

    Enjoy your breakfast and then, debark the train at Boat of Garten. Take the motorcoach to Rothiemurchus Estate, where you could enjoy a variety of activities, including fishing, clay pigeon shooting, a guided walk or perhaps, you may simply prefer to unwind at leisure by the fire at Drumintoul Lodge surrounded by magnificent scenery.

    Rejoin the train in Kinggussie and savor lunch as you travel southwards through Blair Atholl and Pitlochry to Dunkeld. Disembark here for your visit to Glamis Castle, Scotland's most beautiful castle & childhood home of HM queen Elizabeth, The Queen Mother. Be intrigued by its history of legends and myths and marvel at its beautiful architecture and stunning gardens. Return back to the train in Perth, where you enjoy a formal dinner on the move before stabling for the evening at Dundee.

    Overnight: Belmond Royal Scotsman
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    DEPART DUNDE

    This morning, depart Dundee and travel through the former Kingdom of Fife. You cross the Firth of Forth passing the cantilever Forth Railway Bridge, a celebrated Scottish landmark. Conclude your luxurious train journey after your arrival in Edinburgh.

    Meal plan: B

Classic Journey Aboard 'Belmond Royal Scotsman'

Programme Overview

  • This program combines the favorite Classic and scenic Western journeys for seven memorable nights aboard Belmond Royal Scotsman. Travel toward Inverness, stopping in Plockton for a boat trip to the Isle of Skye. Visit Ballindalloch, the home of the Macpherson-Grant family since 1546, and enjoy time for leisure activities at Rothiemurchus Estate. Take a ferry from Wemyss Bay to the Isle of Bute to visit Mount Stuart, Britain's most spectacular Victorian Gothic house. All along the way feast on Scotland's spectacular scenery from the comfort of your elegant train.

  • Day 5

    SPEAN BRIDGE

    This morning, depart Dundee and travel east towards Edinburgh to explore this marvelous city at your pace. Visit the historic Royal Mile and view the Edinburgh Castle, which dominates the skyline of the city. Appreciate an art gallery or simply unwind with a cup of coffee amidst the beautiful surroundings. Re-board the train to begin with the second part of your journey.

    Early this afternoon, Belmond Royal Scotsman heads west through Falkirk, Clydebank and Dumbarton to skirt Glasgow to arrive at the northern bank of the Clyde. Near the mouth of the Clyde estuary lies Craigendoran, marking the commencement of the majestic West Highland Line.

    Travel along the edge of Gare Loch, while sipping a cup of tea. You can sight Loch Long on your left, and after a while, view Ben Arthur (The Cobbler) with its distinctive peak.

Enjoy an informal dinner before you meet and interact with a Highlander, who takes you through the life of Scottish heroes and gives you insights about life in the Highlands.

    Overnight: Belmond Royal Scotsman
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    BRIDGE OF ORCHY

    Breakfast is served as your journey continues west, passing through Fort William with views of Ben Nevis (Britain's highest mountain). Travelling along the Loch Eil you then pass Glenfinnan and Lochailort. Continue north along this stunning coastline to reach Arisaig. Disembark here for a romantic walk along the silver Morar Sands, a stunning string of white sandy beaches, with spectacular views of the Small Isles with its awe-inspiring Cuillin Mountains.

    Rejoin the train and relish lunch onboard. Your journey now retraces the route towards Fort William. Visit the Glenfinnan Monument, followed by tea at Inverlochy Castle. Re-board the train, where you will be served with an informal dinner. Head southwards to Bridge of Orchy for overnight stabling of the train.

    Overnight: Belmond Royal Scotsman
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    WEMYSS BAY

    Today, depart from Bridge of Orchy, travel south towards Glasgow and Gourock, with your breakfast served onboard. Disembark at Wemyss Bay, and set off for a ferry ride across to the Isle of Bute. The island embraces a variety of landscapes, seashore, moorland, farmland and forest. The natural history, geography, geology and ancient history of the island are revealed at every turn. From St. Blane's Chapel at the south of the island to the abandoned townships in Glen More at the north, every stone has its tale to tell.

    View Mount Stuart, Britain's remarkable Victorian Gothic house, adorned with stunning and mysterious interiors. Mount Stuart boasts of arguably the finest privately held collection of family portraits in Scotland.

    Relax over a cup of tea, with the afternoon to self-explore this magnificent house. Observe its tranquil Rock Garden, the Wee Garden containing rare plants from the southern hemisphere, the Kitchen Garden and the Victorian Pinetum. The Limetree Avenue leads you to a secluded stretch of coastline and one of the most beautiful beaches in Scotland.

    Return to the mainland and rejoin the train in Wemyss Bay, where you enjoy a formal dinner onboard, and the train stables for the night.

    Overnight: Belmond Royal Scotsman
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    DEPART WEMYSS BAY

    This morning, depart Wemyss Bay and enjoy breakfast aboard. Conclude your luxurious train journey after your arrival in Edinburgh.

    Overnight: Belmond Royal Scotsman
    Meal plan: B
