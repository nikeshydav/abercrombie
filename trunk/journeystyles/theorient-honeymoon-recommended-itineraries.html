LOVE IN INDONESIA: BALI & UBUD HONEYMOON

Programme Overview

Singaraja, Ubud and Uluwatu

  • Duration: 10 Days/9 Nights
    Highlights: Explore the Banjar hot springs
    Sightseeing of plantations, Buyan and Tamblingan lakes & Jatiluwih
    Penulisan Mountain top temple at sunrise
    Kintamani freewheel bike ride while sightseeing the small villages on the way
    Ubud insider to explore the Ubud culture and traditional dance performance
    Sailing cruise at Uluwatu
    Private Balinese Dance class

  • A&K Advantage

    Dinner on one of Bali’s best tables of the award winning Mosaic Restaurant
    Specially planned romantic dinner in a private & secluded area at Ubud
    Romantic sundowner at Uluwatu on the Impossible Beach
    Dining By Design at Anantara, Uluwatu
    Private arrival and departure airport transfers
    The services of an A&K Representative

    • * On arrival in each country, you are welcomed by your A&K Representative who conducts a briefing on your time in the country. An A&K Representative is always at hand to provide unparalleled 24/7 support, be it local travel tips or reconfirming arrangement details, to providing recommendations for restaurants and shopping. Should you ever need help or advice, dial your A&K Representative’s mobile phone for prompt and courteous service at any time.
    Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE DENPASAR–SINGARAJA–Day at Leisure

    Upon arrival at Ngurah Rai International Airport, Denpasar you will be greeted by your A&K Representative and transferred by a private vehicle to Singaraja, among the paddy fields and hills of this lush Indonesian island.

    Afternoon, relax at the resort which offers spectacular views across the northern coast valley to the Java Sea. Resort is set within a coffee plantation which is largely unexplored offering stunning view of the picturesque landscape, organic meals and tranquil surroundings. Celebrate the onset of your honeymoon with champagne by your private heated pool or sip on the famous coffee "kopi luak".

    A candlelit dinner tonight will be a choice of delectable dishes of authentic Indonesian cuisine served by a private butler in a private bale bengong with unhindered views of the skyline.

    Overnight: Munduk Moding Coffee Plantation

    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 2

    SINGARAJA–Banjar Hot Springs, organic lunch and spa

    After breakfast, you will leave in a private vehicle to the Banjar Hot Springs, located in Banjar countryside, close to the Wihara (Buda Temple). The hot wellspring source is probably hundreds years old and after the Japanese colonized Indonesia, it was developed into three pools to relocate the water for taking a bath. It is believed that having bath in the brimstone heated water is therapeutic and skin diseases heal. One may even enjoy the hot water fall, which has the ability to make the body feel revitalized and stress-free.

    Return to the plantation for a scrumptious organic authentic lunch.

    After lunch, take a relaxing private Balinese couple spa, surrounded by the plantation. Unwind your body and soul, with the intoxicating scent of flowers and spices. The tranquil sound of water as it trickles down a rock clad wall will soothe your mind.

    Dinner will be organised at the plantation.

    Overnight:Munduk Moding Coffee Plantation

    Meal Plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    SINGARAJA–UBUD–Sightseeing of plantations, Buyan and Tamblingan lakes & Jatiluwih

    Munduk is an ideal location for outdoor activities. This morning you’ll have the opportunity to explore the Coffee & Clove Plantations – learning about the plantation and processing methodologies. After this short trip, you will be transferred in a private vehicle along with a private guide south to a vantage point overlooking two superb volcanic lakes: Buyan and Tamblingan.

    Take a horse ride to explore the tropical rain forest, hillside area and volcanic lake along with your private guide. The short excursion includes a private boat trip with a traditional dugout at the serene Tamblingan Lake. Enjoy the picturesque backdrop while riding, en-route visit the village of Bedugul. Further ahead you will visit the major water temple of Pura Ulun Danu, founded in the 17th century; perhaps one of the most photographed temples in Bali. Dedicated to Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, featuring a beautiful thatched Hindu Meru which seems to float on the lake. This temple is also home to a Buddhist shrine and a small mosque.

    Continue through the vegetable farms before lunch overlooking verdant rice terraces.

    After lunch, continue to the UNESCO shortlisted rice terraces of Jatiluwih – the oldest in Bali for a stunning panoramic view of the beautiful landscape. Travel through the scenic area of Marga to your Resort in Ubud.

    Upon arrival at this intimate and rural hotel, you will be welcomed traditionally. Relax with champagne & strawberries on your private sundeck and infinity pool overlooking the Tjampuhan Valley of your COMO Villa.

    Take dinner on one of Bali’s best tables, enjoying the freshest of local ingredients fused with western techniques in the Discovery Menu of the award winning Mosaic Restaurant.

    Overnight : Uma by COMO

    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 4

    UBUD–Penulisan mountain temple, Kintamani freewheel, picnic & Ubud insider

    Rise early. You will be transferred in a private vehicle to COMO Shambhala Estate for a ride up to the top of Mount Batur, where the highest temple in all of Bali is situated. The views are spectacular at this sacred site, atop Penulisan Mountain. Watch the most stunning sunrise over Mt. Batur, Mt. Abang, and the majestic Mt. Agung. Take part in the morning temple ceremony.

    From the crater rim of Mount Batur volcano, you will now descend freewheeling on bikes to the resort. En-route, you will ride through rich Balinese countryside discovering colourful villages, plantations and rice terraces.

    Stop on a cleared patch with a romantic setup for a picnic lunch, before returning to the resort.

    Ubud, Bali was originally called “Ubad” which translates to medicine, and the reason why it began as a destination for healing. Today Ubud is a hub for arts, crafts, culture, wellness, food, shopping, spirituality, and Yoga. The special personalised Ubud Insider Tour will provide a native's perspective. The tour will be an outstanding opportunity to learn about the history of Ubud.

    No visit to Bali would be complete without witnessing traditional dance and this evening, in Ubud, you will be treated to a performance in a nearby village.

    On return to the hotel room you will be welcomed with candles and roses petals for a romantic ambience.

    Overnight:Uma by COMO

    Meal Plan: B, L

  • Day 5

    UBUD–Sightseeing

    Enjoy your Bubble bath with rose petals, aroma candles and lazy romantic breakfast in bed.

    After breakfast, you may choose to explore the small villages at your pace. View the picturesque landscapes, stopping for pictures with your private guide. You may visit the Besakih Temple, the ‘Mother Temple’ of Bali which is located on the western slopes of Mt Agung, Bali’s highest volcano (2 567 m), it is the largest and most important temple on the island. En-route stop by the village of Sibetan to observe traditional weaving and visit a salak plantation where you can taste Bali’s famed ‘snakeskin’ fruit.

    You may enjoy lunch at a local restaurant; with stunning terracing rice field views against a backdrop of Mt Agung.

    Relax and unwind with a special couple Balinese massage in your villa’s own private spa treatment area.

    Romance under the open starlit sky overlooking the Tjampuhan Valley with a romantic candlelit dinner sophistically setup in a secluded and private area especially for you.

    Overnight:Uma by COMO

    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 6

    UBUD–ULUWATU–At leisure & romantic sundowner

    Enjoy a leisurely breakfast in the privacy of your villa.

    After breakfast, you depart for Uluwatu in a private vehicle. It is an ideal quintessence of the island's natural beauty and spiritual charm. With its dramatic cliffs and steep drops into the Indian Ocean surf, the resort offers a unique experience like none other on the island.

    At noon, we suggest you sit and enjoy an exotic, authentic and delicious lunch as you look over the sapphire blue water stimulating your senses.

    Wander off to the nearby Impossible Beach and romance in the beautiful sandy beach. A chilled bottle of champagne and fruits will be set by a rug in the sand for you and your loved one to enjoy a romantic sundowner.

    Recommend you return to the resort for dinner.

    Overnight:Anantara Uluwatu Resort & Spa

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    ULUWATU–Shopping

    After breakfast, you are scheduled for some retail therapy today. You will visit the Nusa Dua Shopping Centre, which is Bali’s centre for world-class shopping. At offer is the island’s most widespread international shopping complex. It is a luxury shopper’s haven, with a perfect blend of the world’s famous brand names displayed along with famous local brands. The centre also features various private boutiques, galleries, art and craft shops, jewellery stores and a wide selection of trendy wining and dining venues.

    Shop at your pace and return at leisure to the hotel for dinner.

    Overnight:Anantara Uluwatu Resort & Spa

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    ULUWATU–Private sailing cruise

    Post breakfast you and your loved one will depart for a private sailing cruise. Sail across Badung strait to Lembongan Island by the most luxurious Catamaran and return to Benoa harbour by dusk. It is the perfect way to appreciate Bali's stunning beauty. Enjoy a village tour of seaweed farms and the famous underground house, snorkeling or reef viewing from glass bottom boat at Lembongan Island. Canoes are also available for your optional activities at the beach club.

    You may choose to go to Anantara Seminyak’s signature lounge and bar. Enjoy a sensational menu of grilled meat, seafood and Spanish tapas along with innovative cocktails and chill out beats from savvy DJs. It is a perfectly appropriate option, for both fine-dining and clubbing.

    Overnight:Anantara Uluwatu Resort & Spa

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    ULUWATU–Balinese dance class and Dining by Design

    Music and dance is integral and prevalent in Bali’s culture in abundance – from village streets to temple courtyards. Balinese dance forms are as varied as the island, but all feature precise hand movements and fluid motions. A special class with expert instructors in the privacy of the resort will give you and your loved one the chance to learn the moves. You may then dress in the traditional attire and create memories for a life time.

    Day at leisure or you may choose to relax and invigorate your senses with a special private couple massage in a Bale setting. Select your treatment from ancient wellness secrets from across Asia or advanced western practices.

    Evening, create unforgettable memories by bringing your own idea of the perfect romantic dinner to life through Anantara’s own “Dining by Design”. Design your menu and special setup according to your own mood, in consultation with their culinary team, and even choose your own venue from around the resort. Their team will transform the site you choose into the setting for a magical dinner made just for you.

    Overnight: Anantara Uluwatu Resort & Spa

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    DEPART ULUWATU

    This morning is free at leisure until you are transferred in a private vehicle to Denpasar International Airport for your flight.

    Meal Plan: B

LUXURY HONEYMOON IN CAMBODIA: TEMPLE & PRIVATE ISLAND

Programme Overview

Siem Reap and Phnom Penh, Sihanoukville

  • Duration: 8 Days/7 Nights

    Highlights:Sightseeing at Angkor Thom & Bayon Temple
    Private boat ride excursion of Tonle Sap Lake
    Visit to the jungle temple of Beng Mealea or Kompong Chhnang
    Visit Chantiers Ecoles (Artisans d’Angkor) and discover local art, crafts and silver handicrafts.
    Enjoy local adventure sports as you explore the island like snorkeling and kayak ride
    Bird watching perched on a platform on Koh Bong
    Lunch on a secluded island & Speed boat excursion

  • A&K Advantage

    Assistance with Visa on Arrival, Immigration and Custom clearance
    Sunrise at Angkor Wat with Champagne breakfast at Ta Lek gate
    Elephant ride from South gate to Bayon Temple and Phnom Bakheng
    Catered dinner at temple around Angkor (East gate of Bayon, Thommanon)
    Visit to Phnom Penh with a personal shopper
    Specially organised dinner with Apsara dance show
    Private arrival and departure airport transfers
    The services of an A&K Representative

    • * On arrival in each country, you are welcomed by your A&K Representative who conducts a briefing on your time in the country. An A&K Representative is always at hand to provide unparalleled 24/7 support, be it local travel tips or reconfirming arrangement details, to providing recommendations for restaurants and shopping. Should you ever need help or advice, dial your A&K Representative’s mobile phone for prompt and courteous service at any time.

    Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE SIEM REAP–At leisure

    Upon arrival you will be welcomed by an A&K representative in the restricted area. He will assist you with VISA ON ARRIVAL application, rapid transit through immigration to collect your checked luggage and custom clearance. A&K representative will complete all necessary forms on your behalf. You will then be transferred to the hotel in a private vehicle and assisted with check-in.

    Nestled between rice paddies and stretched along a river, this former sleepy riverside provincial town of Siem Reap serves as the entryway to the ancient magnificent Angkor and other ruins. This was formerly the seat of power for the once enormous and glorious Khmer empire through the 9th and 15th centuries AD. The architecture of this town is distinctly colonial and Chinese, which originated from a cluster of old villages, but today is alive with culture, restaurants and pubs.

    Amansara was originally the guesthouse of King Sihanouk, built by French architect is situated on the edge of Siem Reap. It is a short distance from the entrance to the Angkor UNESCO World Heritage Site. Set in a garden compound, the resort has the ambience of a private home and is the ideal base for travellers seeking to explore Khmer culture and the majestic ruins of Angkor. It is an award winning luxury Angkor Wat hotel.

    Relax at the resort and experience its luxurious amenities or soak in your suites private plunge pool with a glass of wine in the company of your loved one.

    Overnight: Amansara Resort

    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    SIEM REAP–Visit Angkor Thom, Bayon Temple & Tonle Sap Lake

    Enjoy a spectacular and a life time experience of viewing the sunrise at Angkor Wat with Champagne breakfast at Ta Lek gate. Angkor Thom, the heavily fortified city built in the 10th century by the greatest of the Khmer rulers, King Jayavarman VII (1181 - 1201). With a population in excess of one million inhabitants, Angkor Thom covered an area of 10 square kilometers and was larger than any city in Europe at the time.

    From Angkor Thom's southern gateway, continue to visit the Bayon Temple riding on an elephant back while you explore the temple along with your private guide. The structure is overwhelming in its construction and most Angkor specialists agree that it is one of the finest examples of classic Khmer architecture. This temple marks the exact centre of Angkor Thom, thus it is the most recognized of the city's temples and also the most enigmatic and mysterious.

    As you exit the Bayon Temple take a brief halt at the Royal Enclosures to see the Elephant Terrace. The terrace takes its name from the extraordinary representation of elephants and of an elephant hunt, which takes up the majority of the frieze. A little north of the main terrace is another, known as the Terrace of the Leper King after a statue found above it.

    You will also be visiting a unique jungle temple which is captivated by the large trees and vines that have grown over it, Ta Prohm. Ta Prohm is renowned as the most romantic experience of Angkor and even Historians have noted its mystical charm. Its photogenic and atmospheric combination of trees growing out of the ruins forms unique and exotic backdrop. It served as a backdrop for Hollywood blockbuster, “Tomb Raider”.

    This afternoon, explore the Tonle Sap Lake in a private boat. Tonle Sap Lake, also known as the ‘Great Lake’ or ‘sea of fresh water', lies south of Angkor links the Mekong River to the southern end of the Great Lake. An unusual phenomenon of this river is that its water flow changes direction to run in reverse; the source of this phenomenon is in the Himalayan Mountains of Central Asia. When the snow melts, the water flows southward along the course of the Mekong River and its tributaries, but the silted channels of the Mekong River system are inadequate to accommodate the amount of water received because of which the Mekong River flows back. The impact of this overflow causes the Tonle Sap River to reverse its course each year between July and October and feed into the Great Lake.

    On your private boat cruise pass floating houses, fish farms and view life on the water that dots the landscape. This is the best experience to explore rural Cambodia and gain a better understanding of the unique ecosystem rich with flora, fauna, fish and other wildlife.

    Enjoy a romantic private couple massage by a team of skilled masseuses providing a range of traditional Cambodian treatments in the evening. You may choose to enjoy the spa in treatment rooms, privacy of your suite, by the poolside or on the roof terrace.

    Overnight:Amansara Resort

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    SIEM REAP–Visit jungle temple of Beng Mealea or Kompong Chhnang and Artisans d’Angkor

    Enjoy your aroma bath with rose petals, candles and a romantic breakfast in bed.

    Today morning you may choose to continue exploring the temples of Cambodia and drive through local villages and paddy fields to a remote jungle temple of Beng Mealea, which is now utterly subsumed by the jungle. It has extensive carving of scenes from Hindu mythology, including the Churning of the Sea of Milk and Vishnu being borne by the bird god Garuda. Enjoy a short walk through the dense jungle and feel like an explorer when you discover the completely overgrown temple. The outstanding decoration dates from the first half of the 12th century.

    Or
    Visit the Kompong Chhnang, one of the largest fishing ports in the area, where you’ll take a trip round the local market, see old colonial mansions, visit a ceramics-making village and Artisans d’Angkor.

    At Chantiers Ecoles (Artisans d’Angkor) - Observe the students practicing age old carving and finishing techniques. It is a local woodworking and stone carving workshop/gallery which aims to reintegrate young Cambodians from rural areas to the creation of traditional cultural artworks.

    Later, sail up to the point where the Tonle Sap River joins the great Tonle Sap Lake - the largest freshwater and continue your private cruise to Kompong Luong, one of the biggest floating villages on the lake.

    Evening discover the local area at your pace.

    On return to the hotel room you will be welcomed with candles and roses petals for a romantic ambience.

    Overnight: Amansara Resort

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    SIEM REAP–Visit to the paddy fields or a silver workshop, elephant ride to Phnom Bakheng to watch sunset over Angkor Wat, Catering dinner at temple around Angkor ( East gate of Bayon, Thommanon...)

    After a leisurely breakfast you will be taken for a visit to the paddy fields. Learn about the village life and try your hand at rice planting. Season permitting, rice planting with your loved one can be a lot of fun.

    Alternately, you may visit few of the remaining silver workshops, as only a few families of silversmith in Cambodia propagate this practice now, which has been listed as Cambodia heritage by UNESCO in 2004. Artisans’ d’Angkor has set up a silver plated workshop in order to show case the process and encourage the revival of this Cambodian heritage.

    Afternoon at your pace

    Late afternoon, take an elephant ride up the steep, badly eroded, rock-hewn steps of Phnom Bakheng hill to watch the sun set over Angkor Wat. The view of the sun setting is stunning, mesmerising and should not be missed.

    Catering dinner specially planned at temple around Angkor (East gate of Bayon, Thommanon)

    Overnight:Amansara Resort

    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 5

    SIEM REAP–SIHANOUKVILLE–Song Saa Island at leisure

    After breakfast you will be transferred in a private vehicle for a chartered flight to Sihanoukville port.

    On arrival at Sihanoukville port you will be welcomed by your A&K Representative and you will be transferred to Song Saa Island on a private luxury speed boat and assisted with check-in.

    Afternoon is at your disposable to relax at your pace with champagne, chocolates and fruits on your private sundeck of your overwater villa. Romance with your loved one, enjoy a spectacular view of the Indian Ocean overlooking your private pool and watch the sun set in each other’s company.

    Relish a sumptuous authentic dinner made from fresh, local produce in the privacy of your villa, under the starlit sky and your toes dipped in your private pool.

    Overnight: Song Saa Island

    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 6

    SIHANOUKVILLE–Snorkeling, kayak ride, lunch on a secluded island and bird watching

    After a romantic breakfast served in the luxury of your private deck, you will depart for light marine safari.

    Discover Song Saa Islands by exploring the local reefs along with your private English speaking guide. Snorkel around the Song Saa’ coral garden and surrounding marine environment and get amazed by some colourful marine life. See vibrant corals and the fascinating creatures that live within them –you may even come across a seahorse, grouper or a parrotfish.

    Head out in one of the kayaks to explore the gorgeous untouched coves and bays of the surrounding archipelago at your own pace. Meander through Koh Bong’s blossoming mangrove forest. The winding roots of the mangroves provide habitation for fish and other marine creatures, while the branches are perfect for birds such as the resident kingfishers. While exploring the deserted islands, enjoy a private lunch at one the islands where a table would have been set sophisticated especially for you. Enjoy a mouth-watering lunch against a background of turquoise water and pure white sand.

    Return to the resort and perch up on one of the platforms on Koh Bong to watch the island’s vibrant birdlife. From these you can see hornbills and other colourful species, while sipping on some wine and enjoying the view of the sunset.

    Dinner will be organised privately on the beach in a romantic candlelit setup with your private butler.

    Overnight: Song Saa Island

    Meal Plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    SIHANOUKVILLE–Traditional blessing ceremony by local Buddhist monks, shopping, speed boating or spa, and special dinner with Apsara Dance Show

    Today morning after your breakfast get blessed in a traditional ceremony at a close by pagoda (temple) on Koh Rong by local Buddhist monks. Add a sacred touch to your honeymoon. You will be transferred to and from the local Buddhist pagoda in a private traditional long tail boat to add to the charm.

    You may choose to visit Phnom Penh for some retail therapy. You will travel by a luxury speed boat and be accompanied by a personal shopping guide who will take you to exclusive boutiques, galleries and other stores. You can buy traditional handicrafts, souvenirs, silk items amongst other goods that may fancy you. You may also wander around the streets of Phnom Penh and explore the local markets.

    On return if you still have the spark going in you, you could ride on a speed boat for sightseeing around neighbouring islands, or for water skiing and wakeboarding activities.

    Alternately, you can enjoy one of the bespoke rejuvenation treatments in the Song Saa Sanctuaries, which are set strategically in the rainforest and along the water’s edge, to provide a romantic ambience. This will help you soothe your sore muscles and relax on the last day of your honeymoon.

    A special dinner will be organized in a secluded hideaway spots for a private dining experience designed entirely for you along with traditional “Apsara Dance” show.

    Overnight: Song Saa Island

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    DEPART SIHANOUKVILLE

    After breakfast you will depart Sihanoukville by a private luxury speed boat and transfer by private charter to Phnom Penh for your flight.

    Meal Plan: B

Hatton, Kandy and Galle

    Hot air ballooning with a romantic champagne breakfast

    Visit to the Royal Botanical Gardens

  • A&K Advantage

    Personalised shopping experience with a private shopper

    Private arrival and departure airport transfers
    The services of an A&K Representative

    • * On arrival in each country, you are welcomed by your A&K Representative who conducts a briefing on your time in the country. An A&K Representative is always at hand to provide unparalleled 24/7 support, be it local travel tips or reconfirming arrangement details, to providing recommendations for restaurants and shopping. Should you ever need help or advice, dial your A&K Representative’s mobile phone for prompt and courteous service at any time.

    Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE COLOMBO–HATTON–At leisure

    Ayubowan!

    On arrival you will be welcomed by an A&K representative and transferred by a private vehicle to your resort. At the resort you will be assisted with check-in. En-route to the resort you will appreciate the beauty of the picturesque landscape.

    Ceylon Tea Trails, Sri Lanka's first Relais & Chateaux resort reflects the colonial era, connected by walking trails through stunningly beautiful Ceylon tea gardens.

    Day is at your leisure to take a walk or an adventure trek to experience the varied landscapes, exotic flora, abundant birdlife and the sweet rhythm of life in a tea garden at your own pace. Sip on a hot cup of local brew and enjoy the serene and tranquil surroundings.

    Overnight:Ceylon Tea Trails

    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    HATTON–Tour of tea plantation

    After breakfast you will have the opportunity for a “Tea Experience” where you will visit the tea plantation and the processing unit.

    Resident Tea Planter will introduce you to the age-old tea making process. Follow the trail of the planters of old Ceylon, and explore the slopes they identified for tea plantation. Watch the tea pluckers pick two leaves and a bud with deft hands. You will also be given a chance at plucking some tea. You will be provided with a basket in which to collect your leaves, you’ll find soon that it takes a bit of practice to make sure the leaves end up in your basket.

    Proceed to the Mini Tea Factory and understand the process, from the initial drying (withering) of the leaves, through rolling, fermenting and sifting, which ends up to produce the different types of tea. Tour ends with a tasting of different grades of this great brew.

    You will leave Tea Trails with a newly found appreciation of this wonder herb “Camellia Sinensis”

    After an exhaustive day, return to the hotel and relax rest of the day.

    You may choose to indulge in a special private couple tea based spa treatment.

    Overnight: Ceylon Tea Trails

    Meal Plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    HATTON–At leisure and local walk

    Enjoy a leisurely romantic breakfast in bed.

    Pamper yourselves with a special aromatic bath to begin your day. Choose from Cinnamon Spice, Green Tea, Black Pepper & Orange, Jasmine or a Bergamot & Geranium Bath.

    Day at leisure

    After lunch, you may take a stroll in the tea garden. Absorb vistas of mountain lakes, manicured tea gardens, forests and doll’s house villages. A private guide will accompany you and assist you in exploring the tea estate and introduce you to the local culture.

    On return, relax and enjoy your dinner in the privacy of your bungalow.

    Your hotel room would have been decorated with candles and roses petals for a romantic ambience.

    Overnight:Ceylon Tea Trails

    Meal Plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    HATTON–KANDY–Sightseeing and specially planned dinner with Kandy dance performance

    After breakfast depart for Kandy in your private vehicle.

    Kandy is one of the most beautiful places in Sri Lanka with its serene waterfalls, mountains and temples. The city is home to one of Sri Lanka’s seven UNESCO World Heritage Sites; the richly painted cave temple complex is the islands largest and best preserved. Though there are more than 80 documented caves in the area, the most significant are spread over 5 caves, which comprise of statues and paintings related to Lord Buddha and his life.

    After lunch, depart for a short excursion of the temples of the city in a private vehicle. Sri Lanka's residents follow four religions; Buddhism, Hinduism, Christianity and Islam, though Buddhism is the most widely practiced faith. Kandy has a large collection of places of worship, the most famous being the Temple of the Tooth.

    One of the most revered Temples by the Buddhists is the Sacred Temple of Tooth as it abodes the Sacred Tooth Relic of Lord Buddha. The Relic of the Tooth is retained in a two-story inner shrine fortified by two large elephant tusks. Visitors are not often allowed to enter this inner shrine; however you could offer flowers at the outer shrine area. The Paththirippuwa or the ‘Octagon’ which is a highlight was initially built as the part of the Royal Palace premises by the last King of Sri Lanka Sri Wickrama Rajasinghe (1797 - 1814) to address fellow countrymen.

    Wander to explore the Devales shrine. Apart from these temples, there are various other Buddhist temples of interest including Gadaladeniya, Lankatilaka and Embekke.

    Enjoy a specially planned candlelit dinner with Kandy Dance performance.

    Overnight: Kandy House

    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 5

    KANDY–Hot air ballooning, romantic breakfast and shopping experience

    Start your day early with a trip in a hot air balloon providing ultimate views over Kandalama Lake and the surrounding landscape. Drift gently across the sandstone and jungle below. There is no noisy engine so you can peacefully enjoy the stunning vistas as the world below wakes to another day. Watch your pilot gradually lower the balloon to inches above the evergreen trees as you pick leaves or touch the tree tops, spot a bird or an animal waking to the daybreak.

    On landing you will be served a romantic champagne breakfast.

    Day at leisure

    After lunch, treat yourself to some shopping with a personal shopper. Explore shops displaying precious and semi-precious stones, skillfully crafted jewellery, handicrafts, colourful batiks and silks.

    After hustle of the day, relax over a sumptuous dinner.

    Overnight: Kandy House

    Meal Plan: B

    KANDY–GALLE–At leisure

    After breakfast you will be transferred by a private vehicle to the beach side villas in a private vehicle.

    Appreciate the scenic beauty en-route to the resort.

    Enjoy a lazy lunch in the privacy of your villa.

    Relax and indulge in a romantic, intimate treatment enhancing your bond of love. Immerse yourselves in exotic aphrodisiac fragrances, warm honey, sensual massages, herbal body cocoons and aromatic flower baths.

    Romantic moonlit dinner will be organised in absolute privacy on a rock above the sea.

  • Day 7

    GALLE–Boat cruise and turtle farm visit

    After breakfast, you will be onboard a private boat for a boat cruise. The private boat journey begins near the ancient Athuruwella temple, a few minutes’ walk from the hotel and the journey ends at Katukoliha temple. The scenic boat journey is through the Old Dutch Canals passing through paddy fields and mangroves. You may have the opportunity of seeing a variety of birds and tropical vegetation beyond the river banks.

    After lunch, you will watch turtles in their natural habitat. If you are lucky, you may even have an opportunity to see turtles laying eggs in the evening hours. The Turtle Conservation Project is the only place where turtles are free and are not held in closed enclosures. Therefore we strongly recommend you visit this location instead of other hatcheries. You will be introduced to the five species of turtles found in Sri Lanka and the survival threats that turtles face.

    Overnight: Saman Villas

  • Day 8

    GALLE–Visit Mask Factory in Ambalangoda & picnic lunch

    After breakfast you will visit a mask factory in Ambalangoda. The town Ambalangoda is located in the south coastal region of Sri Lanka and is home of devil dancing, masks & puppets from the past. The traditional masks are carved from light Balsa like Kaduru wood. These trees grow in the marshy lands adjoining paddy fields. The wood is smoke dried for a week to cure before being used for carving out masks. The hand carved & painted masks in traditional dance dramas are vibrant & colorful. You will craft a miniature mask and can take it back as a souvenir.

    On your way back, you will stop at an identified spot for a picnic lunch.

    Experience an intimate candlelit dinner on a secluded golden sandy beach.

    Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    On arrival you will be welcomed by your A&K Representative and transferred to your hotel in a Vintage Rolls Royce and assisted with check-in.

    Tokyo is the world’s busiest, wildest and wackiest city.

    Day is at leisure.

    Afternoon, visit Tokyo's renowned Shinto shrine, wonderfully serene and austere. The 40-foot-high (12-meter) torii gate at the entrance to the 200-acre park is made of 1,500-year-old cypress, and there's another one closer to the shrine itself. Stop at the cleansing station where you may dip into a communal water tank to cleanse your hands and mouth before offering a prayer. You can write wishes on little pieces of paper and tie them onto the prayer wall, or do as the locals do - toss some yen into the offering box (it's near the enormous taiko drum), bow your head twice, clap twice, and bow once more.

    Explore the Akihabara Culture in the evening. We suggest you visit the Café Schatzkiste; Experience the subculture in this global mecca of manga, anime and gaming ‘otaku’ geeks. This is a quintessential Tokyo experience not to be missed.

    It is recommended, you dine at one of the world class restaurants located in your hotel this evening. You may choose to explore the night life of Tokyo.

    Overnight: Peninsula Hotel

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    TOKYO–Visit Meiji Jinja and shop at Omote-Sando

    After a romantic breakfast in bed, you will depart in a private vehicle for a short trip towards the old town.

    Visit Meiji Jinja; Tokyo's most famous Shrine, where you will have the opportunity to witness a traditional Shinto purification dance and receive a blessing from a Shinto Priest.

    After lunch, you will head to ‘Tokyo’s Champs Elysees’ Omote-Sando, along with your private shopping guide. Flagship designer shop & boutiques of worldwide brands line this tree-lined boulevard east of Meiji Shrine. You may go off-streets for more casual wear; side street Takeshita-dori is only 440 yards long (400 meters long) and overflows with young shoppers with low-priced boutiques—a mecca for fashionable teens.

    Evening is at leisure.

    We recommend you visit the Lovenet which is a unique experience of drinks and light meal in a private theme setting. Each suite is an image of different resort hotel from around the globe. You may select the suite room which suits you the best.

    Overnight: Peninsula Hotel

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    TOKYO–Visit Asakusa, Sensoji Temple, Hama-Rikyu Gardens, attend a tea ceremony & private sushi lessons

    Asakusa, part of Tokyo's Shitamachi (old town) and home to Sensoji Temple is also the Geisha district. In the company of a private guide explore the old town.

    Hama-Rikyu Gardens situated at the mouth of the Sumida River and known to be a feudal lord's haven during the Edo period. It consists of an old-style tea-house located on the tidal pond, a 300-year-old pine, pruned pine trees, plum trees grove and a peony field. The Tokugawa shoguns once used the duck hunting grounds and the rambling garden facing Tokyo Bay was the former villa of the shogun. Make time for ceremonial tea at the teahouse on a pond, experience the culture of tea, to immerse yourselves in the philosophical world of sado, or chanoyu, or the “way of tea.”

    Learn the art of some exceptional fish cutting, rice pressing and sushi serving skills at the capital’s premier sushi school in a private sushi lesson. Tokyo Sushi Academy a perfect souvenir from the city and an experience you will never forget.

    Overnight: Peninsula Hotel

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    TOKYO–KAMAKURA–HAKONE

    Travel with your experienced private guide in a private vehicle to Kamakura, the formal capital of Japan turned quaint seaside town. Explore the town while visiting the Large Bronze Buddha Statue, Kenchoji Temple and various other shrines of importance.

    After lunch continue to Fuji Hakone National Park where you will stay in a traditional inn. Hakone is the gateway to Mt. Fuji and is famous for its onsen or natural hot springs. Explore the national park.

    Nestled away in the mountains of Hakone, is the spa resort of Hakone Ginyu. 'Ginyu' translates to a person seeking inspiration for a poem by traveling around. It offers stunning views of the Haya-Kawa River as well as the Hakone Mountains. Typical Japanese Zen style is the core essence of the resort’s interiors, commanding view of a Japanese Garden, and an authentic style Onsen "Natural Hot Spring" is a perfect way to enjoy a romantic honeymoon.

    Evening is at leisure to relax and unwind in the beautifully crafted stone bath.

    We recommend you enjoy a delectable dinner of fresh seasonal authentic Japanese cuisine at the resorts restaurant.

    Overnight: Hakone Ginyu

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    HAKONE–Mt Fuji view, Visit to Hakone Open air museum, private ride on Lake Ashi and cable car ride

    After breakfast, visit the Hakone Open Air Museum and its intriguing number of outdoor sculptures. It also holds an extensive Picasso collection. Open daily, with Western and Japanese sculpture in a yawning cliff side setting.

    Proceed to an awe-inspiring Lake Ashi, the core of Hakone. Take a romantic ride on a private Spanish style vessel in the lake and soak in the mesmerizing vista.

    After lunch, you will ride the world's second longest cable car to the top of Mt Owakudani. You will visit Gora, which is a small town from where the cable car to Sounzan starts. Your heart may skip a beat as you crest a ridge that leaves you spell bound. You are hundreds of meters over the Great Boiling Valley (Owakudani Gorge). The visible cracks in the surface of the earth spit sulfurous fumes, setting the adrenaline flowing in your veins with each expulsion.

    Evening is at leisure.

    Overnight: Hakone Ginyu

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    HAKONE–KYOTO–Sightseeing- Heian shrine, Nijo Castle, Kinkakuji & Daitoku-ji Zen Temple

    After breakfast you will be onboard the Green Car of Shinkansen bullet train to Kyoto.

    Kyoto is home to an astonishing 2000 Shrines and Temples, including 17 UNESCO World Heritage Sights some of which you will explore by a private car.

    Amongst other things you will visit Heian Shrine, home to Japan's largest torii gate and strolling gardens. Explore Nijo Castle, famous for its Momoyama architecture, decorated sliding doors and ‘chirping’ nightingale floors. Continue on to Kinkakuji, originally constructed as a retirement villa for the Shogun. After his death and at his request it became a Buddhist Temple. The Daitoku-ji Zen Temple consists of a main temple and 22 sub-temples; one of which, Daisen-in, is home to one of Japan’s best Zen rock gardens, ideal for some Zen meditation practice.

    Overnight:Granvia Hotel

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    KYOTO–Traditional Japanese Wedding Kimono

    Enjoy a leisurely breakfast served in the privacy of your suite.

    Today will be a busy day as you start with having your hair styled, make up done and get dressed in traditional Japanese wedding kimonos.

    After lunch, you will be transferred by a luxury private car to Kamigamo Shrine, the oldest Shinto shrine in Kyoto and a registered World Heritage Site. Your hairdresser will be accompanying you. You will then perform a traditional wedding ceremony administered by a Shinto priest in this tranquil and atmospheric shrine.

    Dinner tonight will be a special Wedding Dinner in a private room at the Granvia's Rakusui Japanese restaurant.

    Overnight: Granvia Hotel

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    KYOTO–Sake tasting and Geisha performance at a kaiseki dinner

    Breakfast is at the hotel.

    Spend the morning on a guided tour of the sake brewery. You will be introduced to the traditional brewing processes and a history of the brewery. You will then sample a selection of their sake, followed by a visit to a liquor shop where the owner will educate you about the different types of sake on offer.

    Today evening, you will have a rare opportunity to experience the customary art and the world of the Geisha. The enticing, fascinating symbols of the art and tradition of Kyoto, Geisha, exist in a rare world that is usually not open to the general public let alone international visitors. The word Geisha literally translates to a person of art, and are indeed, finely schooled in a variety of traditional arts including classical dancing and playing of musical instruments.

    Opportunities to see maiko, apprentice geisha, perform are rare, as reservations can be very difficult to secure. With special privileges you will have the chance to watch an authentic maiko performance, also enjoy an exquisite and exotic Kyoto-style kaiseki dinner (a traditional, Japanese meal), at the open-air riverside terrace of Momijiya Restaurant.

    Overnight: Granvia Hotel

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    KYOTO–TOKYO

    After breakfast you will be transferred by a private vehicle for your private charter to Tokyo for your flight ahead.

    Meal Plan: B
