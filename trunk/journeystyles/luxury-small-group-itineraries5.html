IMAGES OF INDOCHINA & ANGKOR WAT

Programme Overview

From sailing over magical Ha Long Bay and watching the sun rise over Angkor Wat to exploring evocative Ho Chi Minh City to the hustle bustle of the lively city of Bangkok, experience all sights and sounds of Southeast Asia with Abercrombie And Kent.

  • Duration: 13 Days / 12 Nights

    Highlights: Vietnam | Cambodia | Bangkok

    A&K Advantages

    • Visit to Long Bay, studded with soaring limestone islands, on an overnight cruise
    • Cu Chi Tunnels
    • Travel like a local by cyclo through Hanoi and boat in Siem Reap
    • Gain a deeper understanding of the Khmer empire with a lecture by an expert in Angkorian history and culture
    • Stay at stunning Angkor Wat
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE HANOI, VIETNAM

    Arrive in Hanoi, the capital of Vietnam where you will be welcomed by an A&K representative who will assist you with your transfer to your French colonial style accommodation.

    Overnight: Sofitel Legend Metropole
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    HANOI

    Hanoi is the country’s cultural heart, bearing traces of centuries of changing dynasties.

    This morning, visit Ho Chi Minh’s house, a simple structure located among banyan and milkweed trees, which served as home to the revolutionary guerrilla leader who fought for the independence of Vietnam and later on went on to become the Prime Minister and President of this country.

    After a wonderful Vietnamese lunch, continue to the lotus-shaped One Pillar Pagoda, dating back to the first Vietnamese empire independent from China. Also, visit the Quan Thanh Temple, the Temple of Literature and the National Museum of Fine Arts.

    Later, travel like a local by cyclo through the Old Quarter.

    This evening awaits a traditional Vietnamese roi nuoc performance followed by an appetizing welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Sofitel Legend Metropole
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    HA LONG BAY

    Rise early this morning to travel aboard your built four-wheel-drive through the lush countryside to Ha Long Bay, Board a luxury junk and sail through a verdant setting where thousands of sculpted limestone islands rise from the water.

    Disembark for a trek to the nearby summit for panoramic views and a swim by a secluded beach. Later, visit Cua Van, the oldest fishing village in Ha Long Bay, where meet and interact with the residents. Observe the men fishing for squids and oysters, the children going to school and the women folk doing their laundry- definitely a milestone in your journey of discovery.

    Spend the night at Halong Jasmine Cruise where you can witness the magnificence of both dusk and dawn over this magical body of water.

    Overnight: Halong Jasmine
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    HA LONG BAY TO DANANG

    Greet the sunrise with a tai chi lesson on deck. After an exquisite breakfast, sail to explore an exciting limestone grotto.

    In the afternoon, return to Hanoi, and enjoy an artisan visit to Phu Lang Pottery Village to observe multi-generational artisans at work.

    In the evening you will be assisted for your fight to Danang where you will be escorted to your beachfront resort on Son Tra Peninsula near China Beach.

    Overnight: Intercontinental Danang Resort
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 5

    DANANG TO HOI AN

    After a morning at leisure, set out to discover lovely Hoi An, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that retains architectural styles of the many nations that used its port; it includes the Old House of Tan Ky, the Fujian Assembly Hall and the 400-year-old Japanese covered bridge. Visit a local home to see how silkworms are raised and how the silk is produced. Meet a lantern artist and learn how to make the silk lanterns for which Hoi An is acknowledged.

    The evening is at leisure

    Overnight: Intercontinental Danang Resort
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 6

    HO CHI MINH CITY

    This morning, you will be assisted for your flight to Ho Chi Minh City, formerly known as Saigon. Recently experiencing a resurgence of art and culture, this lively, evocative city is a hub of activity — a sharp contrast to the traditional ambience of Hanoi.

    Later in the afternoon, visit Quan Am Pagoda and Binh Tay Market for a remarkable market visit. Explore the surrounding neighborhood of Cholon, the city’s Chinatown.

    Call it a day with dinner and drinks at a culinary hot spot.

    Overnight: Intercontinental Asiana Saigon
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 7

    HO CHI MINH CITY

    Wake up to a scrumptious breakfast and set out in your private vehicle to see the city’s colonial legacy, including the Central Post Office, the holy Cathedral of Notre Dame and the gigantic Opera House.

    After a pleasant lunch, travel out of the city into the countryside to explore the expansive Cu Chi Tunnel network, a key Vietcong resource during the Vietnam War where you may choose to shoot a war-era rifle, and squeeze into and crawl through the small tunnels once traversed by Vietcong guerrillas, experiencing the life here as it once was.

    Overnight: Intercontinental Asiana Saigon
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 8

    SIEM REAP, CAMBODIA

    This morning take a flight to the charming Siem Reap, the town nearest to the ruins of Angkor Wat. Over the next few days, explore the region with your guide, born and raised in Cambodia.

    Set off to explore the temple and city of Angkor Wat, a breath-taking expression of Khmer architecture and Cambodia’s most prized national treasure. Visit the Bayon Temple, with its 172 large-scale faces bearing beguiling smiles, and the Elephant and Leper King Terraces. With each step, marvel at the imposing grandeur and remarkable detail of magnificent Angkor Wat.

    Overnight: Raffles Grand Hotel d’Angkor
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 9

    ANGKOR WAT

    Start your day with a talk by a noted authority on Khmer history and culture. Then, embark your journey to Ta Prohm temple as it rises in the golden light of early morning, exploring it at leisure.

    Before lunch, discover the Angkor National Museum, which houses the golden era Khmer Kingdom artifacts. With the local expertise of Abercrombie And Kent, access two less-visited areas of the city — Banteay Srei, also known as the “Citadel of Women,” and Banteay Samre, one of the most complete temple complexes of Angkor.

    In the evening, visit Sam’s Brothers Clean Water project, an A&K Philanthropy project.

    Overnight: Raffles Grand Hotel d’Angkor
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 10

    ANGKOR WAT

    Watch the sunrise over the main temple of Angkor Wat early this morning, an unforgettable experience. Walk amidst the moss-peppered pathways or climb the steep temple steps to wide courtyards and contemplate the temple’s spiritual power while walking its shaded hallways wafting with incense.

    After a palatable local lunch, visit Tonle Sap Lake to observe and explore the local life unfolding along its floating villages.

    Enjoy the panoramic views from the deck as you cruise through the waters of the lake, the perfect sundowner.

    Overnight: Raffles Grand Hotel d’Angkor
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 11

    BANGKOK

    This morning, you will be assisted with your flight to the lively Bangkok, the capital of Thailand.

    The remaining day is at leisure for you to revel in the amenities offered by your award-winning hotel.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 12

    BANGKOK

    Rise early to give alms to monks at a little-visited temple.

    After breakfast, travel by boat to experience life along the Chao Phraya River. Ride like a local on a tuk-tuk around the sprawling Grand Palace. Explore the ancient Siamese court, home to Thailand’s most important shrine, the green-jade Emerald Buddha. Discover Wat Pho temple, with its lovely gilded Reclining Buddha and the musical sound of coins dropping into brass offering bowls.

    Make your last night in Thailand a memorable one with a special farewell dinner hosted by Abercrombie & Kent.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 13

    DEPART BANGKOK

    This morning, after an exquisite breakfast, you will be assisted with your transfer to the airport for your homebound flight.

    Meal plan: B

  • Luang Prabang Pre-Tour Extension

    Day 1

    BANGKOK

    As you arrive in Bangkok, you will be welcomed by an A&K representative for your transfer to your accommodation.

    The day is at leisure.

    Overnight: Novotel Suvarnabhumi Hotel
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    LUANG PRABANG

    This day, you will be assisted with your flight to Luang Prabang, Laos, where you will be met by an A&K representative who will escort you to your luxurious hotel. See the monasteries of Wat Xiengthong, Wat Khili with its rare architecture and Wat Sene.

    You may join a village baci ceremony in the evening.

    Overnight: Luang Say Residence/ Belmond La Residence Phou Vao
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 3

    LUANG PRABANG

    After a delicious breakfast, head out in your private vehicle to witness the local people making food offerings to the monks.

    Later, visit a farm and the Kuangsi Falls followed by a mesmerising sunset cruise.

    Overnight: Luang Say Residence/ Belmond La Residence Phou Vao
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    DEPART LUANG PRABANG

    Today, enjoy tours of the Royal Palace, Phousi Hill, and the Traditional Art and Ethnology Center after which you will be escorted for your flight to Hanoi, Vietnam.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L


    Available on the Jan 18, Jan 29, Feb 8, Mar 1, Mar 22, Apr 26, Oct 18, Oct 25, Nov 12 and Dec 17 departures.

ISRAEL: CROSSROADS OF HISTORY

Programme Overview

Explore Biblical and historic sites in Yad Vashem, Bethlehem and Jerusalem; cruise the Sea of Galilee in a traditional boat; and share an authentic meal with a chef in her home.

  • Duration: 8 days/ 7 nights
    Highlights: Tel Aviv | Masada | Jerusalem | Nazareth | Tiberias

    A&K Advantages

    • Explore a vast array of biblical and historic sites with a local expert, including Masada, stronghold of the Zealots
    • Ride Like a Local when you cruise on the Sea of Galilee in a traditional wood boat of the kind used during Biblical times
    • Dine with a chef in her home and in select restaurants, where you savor the very best culinary dishes and traditions
    • Breeze through customs and immigration with VIP service, which greets you upon arrival and whisks you past long lines at Tel Aviv's Ben Gurion International Airport
    • Stay close to the action in the historic Scots Hotel in Tiberias and the iconic King David hotel in Jerusalem
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Tel Aviv

    Upon arrival in Tel Aviv, you will be met by an A&K representative and assisted with transfer to your hotel overlooking the Mediterranean.

    Overnight: Dan Tel Aviv Hotel
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Tiberias

    Begin the journey with a scenic coastal drive to the ancient Roman capital, Caesarea, where Herod laid out a classical city and constructed a revolutionary artificial harbor. Observe its amphitheater to view a partial inscription mentioning Pontius Pilate — the only secular reference to his existence.

    Further, discover the seaport city of Haifa, the country’s largest port, with an interesting mix of modern neighborhoods and older districts; churches and mosques; mountain and sea.

    Then, visit Acre, a UNESCO World Heritage Centre that dates back to Phoenician and Crusader times. Here, tour the Knights’ Halls, the El Jazaar Mosque and covered markets. Spend the evening with your fellow guests at the welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Scots Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Tiberias

    Today, cruise through the fabled Sea of Galilee by a private boat. Stop en-route at Capernaum, the site of the synagogue where Jesus taught. View St. Peter’s House, now a converted church; Tabgha, where the miracle of the loaves and fishes is said to have occurred; and the commemorative fifth-century mosaic in the Church of multiplication. Afterwards, proceed to the Mount of Beatitudes, where Jesus is believed to have delivered the Sermon.

    Overnight: Scots Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    Nazareth

    Travel to the Nazareth, where Jesus spent much of his life. Continue to the Basilica of the Annunciation, built on the traditional site of Mary and Joseph’s home.

    View the Church of St. Gabriel, one of the most beautiful and unique ones in Israel. Further see Mary’s Well, where an angel is said to have visited; and the basement of the Church of St. Joseph, where a cavern is said to have been the carpentry workshop of Joseph.

    Travel overland through the Jordan Valley to the Decapolis city of Beit Shean. Arrive in Jerusalem in the afternoon.

    Overnight: The King David
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Jerusalem

    Begin with the exploration of the fascinating, history-rich Old City of Jerusalem, built by King David in 1004 BC. Visit the Western Wall, one of Judaism’s most sacred sites. Explore the Temple Mount area (Al Haram el-Sharif) with its Dome of the Rock, El Aksa Mosque and the Davidson Centre, where artwork and archaeological finds take you back through the ages.

    In the Christian Quarter, stroll the Via Dolorosa, where Jesus carried the cross.Stop at the Convent of Zion Sisters (Antonia Fortress), where Pontius Pilate tried Jesus. Tracing the path of the Cross through bazaars of the Old City, arrive at the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, reputed site of Jesus’ crucifixion and resurrection.

    Overnight: The King David
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 6

    Jerusalem

    Travel to the Israel Museum, which houses a selection of the Dead Sea Scrolls, en-route passing the Israel’s Parliament- the Knesset.

    Afterwards, visit Yad Vashem, a Holocaust memorial and institution for Shoah research. Later, drive to Bethlehem and the Church of the Nativity, the birthplace of Jesus.

    Overnight: The King David
    Meal plan:B, L

  • Day 7

    Masada

    Today, take a beautiful drive along the Dead Sea to Masada. Then, ride a cable car to reach the last stronghold of the Jewish Zealots; here, Israelite fighters held out for three years against the invading Roman army before taking their own lives. This evening, conclude your journey with a special farewell dinner in the home of a local chef.

    Overnight: The King David
    Meal plan:B, D

  • Day 8

    Depart Tel Aviv

    This day, you will be assisted with transfer to Ben Gurion Airport for your onward flight .

    Meal plan: B

ITALIAN TREASURES

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 Days/ 9 nights

    Experience the best of Rome, Florence, Venice and more. Tour the Vatican City, view Renaissance art wonders with an A&K expert, taste wine in Tuscany and visit St. Mark's Basilica after hours.

    Highlights: Venice| Pisa | Florence | Rome

    A&K Advantages

    • Stop for a wine tasting in Tuscany on an Artisan Visit.
    • Dine at a historic restaurant in Florence for a Chef's Table culinary experience
    • View some of the greatest art treasures of the Renaissance with insight from an expert A&K guide
    • Enjoy special private access to the Vasari Corridor at the Uffizi Gallery, to walk in the footsteps of the Medici princes
    • Experience an unforgettable after-hours visit to St. Mark's Basilica, when it is illuminated just for you
    • Sample Venetian cuisine at a traditional bacari (wine bar)
    • Ride Like a Local aboard a traghetto (local gondola) to cross Venice's romantic Grand Canal
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Rome

    Upon your arrival in Italy’s ancient capital, Rome, you are greeted and escorted to your hotel.

    After enjoying the day at leisure, join your fellow guests in the evening for a festive welcome dinner.

    Overnight: The St. Regis Rome
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    Rome

    Today, explore the Imperial City, starting with the Palatine Hill. Visit the Colosseum, a majestic amphitheater where 50,000 spectators once watched gladiatorial battles.

    Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant before you begin with a tour of the Vatican City. Visit the Vatican Museum, showcasing masterpieces of painting, sculpture and other works of art collected by the popes through the centuries. Continue to marvel at the famous ceiling painted by Michelangelo at the Sistine Chapel. Afterward, view the great St. Peter’s Basilica, the world’s largest church. Later, observe the poignant Pietà of Michelangelo and the exuberant Baldaquin of Bernini.

    Overnight: The St. Regis Rome
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 3

    Rome

    Continue your city tour to visit the charming Campo de Fiori and delight your senses as you walk through its local food market. Further, explore the Piazza Navona, Rome's most celebrated square dominated by the Baroque Fountain and palazzo. Later, visit the Pantheon, most preserved and influential building of ancient Rome

    Later, stop at the famous Trevi Fountain to perhaps toss in a coin. Conclude your exploration at the foot of the famous Spanish Steps.

    Overnight: The St. Regis Rome
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 4

    Florence

    Begin the day with a drive to Florence, pausing at Orvieto, the small hillside town for lunch.

    Explore Florence with a guided walking tour.

    Arrive at the heart of the historic center to enter the splendid Piazza del Duomo followed by the cathedral housing Signorelli’s Cycle of the Apocalypse. Journey along the Tuscan countryside, amidst hill-towns surrounded by Etruscan walls and slender cypress trees.

    Enjoy a wine tasting session at a local winery on a lively Artisan Visit. Continue to Florence.

    Overnight: Westin Excelsior, Florence
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Florence

    This day, join a local art specialist for a guided tour of the Uffizi Gallery, one of the world's top art museums. The Uffizi Gallery is home to some of the most important works of the Renaissance, including works by Leonardo da Vinci.

    Proceed for an exclusive entry to the Vasari Corridor, an elevated enclosed passageway. Later, walk through the charming Boboli Gardens, home to a collection of sculptures.

    Overnight: Westin Excelsior, Florence
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 6

    Florence

    Take a beautiful drive to Pisa. Here, view the most remarkable architectural structures from medieval Europe, The Leaning Tower, famous for its dramatic tilt to one side.

    Later, visit the inspiring 11th-century Duomo, majestic Italian cathedral church and afterward, enjoy lunch.

    Return to Florence. dine at a restaurant with a fabulous history that involves Habsburg princes, which your chef explains.

    Overnight: Westin Excelsior, Florence
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Venice

    Continue to explore Florence on foot, visit the Galleria dell’Accademia, an art museum showcasing the splendid giant marble sculptures created by Michelangelo.

    Afterward, view Duomo, Italian cathedral church in the San Lorenzo district. Enjoy lunch before you travel to Venice. Privately tour St. Mark’s Basilica in the after-hours, led by an expert. Unwind later over a scenic Sundowner with spectacular views of Venice in the back drop.

    Overnight: Hotel Danieli
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    Venice

    This morning, embark on a walking tour to visit the Doge’s Palace, marvel at its lavish frescoes built in a Venetian Gothic style.

    Explore St. Mark’s Square and the Grand Canal. Continue to view La Fenice Theater. End the day witnessing one of the most spectacular views in Venice at the Accademia Bridge.

    Overnight: Hotel Danieli
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 9

    Venice

    Today, stroll through the market district near Rialto Bridge. Experience the local gastronomy as you sample authentic Venetian cuisine at a bacari. Further, visit Frari Church, one of the greatest churches of Venice.

    Bid farewell to Venice with a beautiful evening gondola ride followed by dinner at a fine restaurant.

    Overnight: Hotel Danieli
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 10

    Depart Venice

    After breakfast, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

CHINA & THE YANGTZE

Programme Overview

Discover China's past and present, from the ancient well preserved Terra Cotta Warriors in Xian to the modern skyline and busy lifestyle of Beijing.

  • Duration: 14 Days / 13 Nights

    Highlights: China | Yangtze

    A&K Advantages

    • Three-night cruise on the Yangtze River
    • Visit to the incredible Terra Cotta Warriors with an on-site archaeologist
    • Chef's Table experience with a private noodle-making demonstration
    • Up-close and personal encounter with giant pandas in their natural habitat
    • Trip to the less-visited and best-preserved Mutianyu section of the Great Wall
    • Walking tour of the Muslim Quarter in Xian
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE BEIJING, CHINA

    Arrive in the capital city of Beijing, China’s long-time seat of power, where you will be received by an A&K representative who will assist you for your transfer to your luxurious hotel.

    Overnight: Four Seasons
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    BEIJING

    This morning, walk or ride a cable car to the top of the well-preserved Mutianyu section of the Great Wall of China, enjoying sweeping views of the forested countryside. After experiencing this true wonder of the world, your day is at leisure.

    Your first evening in this city awaits a special welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Four Seasons
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Beijing

    Begin your day with a tai chi class by a master instructor at the ancient Temple of Heaven, the single most beautiful example of the classic Ming period architecture. Ride like a local on a pedicab through a historic hutong, a vast yet intimate neighborhood of small winding alleyways.

    After a delectable local lunch, spend the afternoon exploring the imperial grandeur of the Forbidden City as you indulge in the history of Tiananmen Square.

    Overnight: Four Seasons
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    XIAN

    This morning you will be assisted with your flight to Xian, a modern city cloaked in over 3,000 years of history. Last stop on the Great Silk Road once, Xian is now the melting pot of Middle Eastern and Asian influences. Have a good time at the special tour of the Yangling Museum to view its Restoration Lab and recent discoveries from Han Dynasty tombs.

    After a scrumptious lunch at an indigenous restaurant, explore the old city quarter with its teeming markets and a beautiful Great Mosque which is the 14th-century national heritage site. An intriguing blend of classic Chinese temple architecture and central Asian embellishments makes it one of the most fascinating sites in Xian.’

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    XIAN

    This morning, accompanied by an on-site archaeologist, visit the excavation site of the Terra Cotta Warriors and Horses, a life-size army which was created during the Qin Dynasty to protect Qin Shi Huang, China’s first emperor, in his afterlife. More than 8,000 soldiers, 130 chariots with 520 horses and 150 cavalry horses are spread through three pits, each revealing a unique face, giving evidence of the remarkable craftsmanship that went into making the warriors.

    Enjoy a chef’s table noodle-making demonstration followed by lunch.

    Late afternoon, visit the Ancient Gate of Xian’s impressive City Wall where you may choose to cycle along its ramparts.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 6

    CHENGDU

    This day you will be escorted for your flight to Chengdu, the charming capital of Sichuan Province.

    On arrival, visit the Chengdu Panda Breeding and Research Center. This A&K Philanthropy-supported project affords an unforgettable opportunity to come face to face with the pandas, China’s National Animal, roaming freely in their natural habitat.

    This evening is at leisure.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 7

    CHENGDU

    After a toothsome breakfast, explore the relaxed riverside city of Leshan, where you will be guided for a private boat ride to the 1,300-year-old, stone-carved Leshan Giant Buddha. At 233 feet tall, it’s the largest Buddha statue carved out of a cliff face, in the world. Behold this epic sculpture, learn how it was carved and why it was positioned to overlook the confluence of three rivers — the Minjiang, Dadu and Qingyi.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    CHONGQING

    Rise early this morning and travel to Chongqing in your private four-wheel-drive vehicle to board your boutique luxury river cruiser for a three-night journey to Yichang. You will be assisted to your cabin as the ship sets sail on its downstream passage to the stunning Three Gorges region.

    The evening awaits a documentary about the Three Gorges Dam Project in the ship’s Tang Theater followed by a delicious multi-cuisine dinner.

    Overnight: Yangzi Explorer
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 9

    YANGTZE EXPLORER

    Start your morning with traditional Chinese exercise and work up an appetite for the buffet breakfast. Discover the authentic method of preparing tea in China, and later, learn to make dumplings with the ship’s on-board chef.

    In the afternoon, disembark to enjoy a shore excursion to Fengdu. Explore the local life on a visit to a village which was relocated to make way for the Three Gorges Dam Project. Meet a local family and cherish the kaleidoscope of images in a local farmers market.

    Attend the Captain’s Reception in the evening, followed by a piquant welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Yangzi Explorer
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 10

    THE THREE GORGES

    This morning, ‘Yangzi Explorer’ navigates the Qutang and Wu Gorges before anchoring at Badong, where you will be guided to a private ferry up the Shennong Stream. You will then be transferred to local “pea pod” boats pulled by Tujia boatmen to view the picturesque Bamboo Gorge. The Tujia are members of a local minority culture famed for their boat-building skills and keen knowledge of the Yangtze River’s many currents and eddies. Later, you may try your hand at Chinese brush painting and calligraphy.

    In the evening, savor a “Taste of China” dinner complemented with engaging on-board entertainment.

    Overnight: Yangzi Explorer
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 11

    YICHANG TO SHANGHAI

    After breakfast, you will be guided to the Three Gorges Project Dam Site and Exhibition Center to reconnoiter the history and capacity of the world’s largest dam, including the story of resettlement of 1.2 million residents of the cities submerged behind the 600-foot-tall engineering marvel.

    Disembark in Yichang around midday for your flight to Shanghai.

    On arrival, you will be aided to check in to your elegant hotel located on the city’s historic Bund.

    Enjoy a relaxing rooftop Scenic Sundowner, gazing out at the spectacular display of colorful city lights that makes the Bund and Pudong districts a wonderland by night

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 12

    SHANGHAI

    Explore China’s dynamic and energetic Shanghai, where spectacular modern skylines meet with classic street markets and gardens. Contrast this experience with a visit to the charming rural village of Zhujiajiao, a traditional water village harkening back to China’s past, and glide through its main canal on a sampan.

    This evening, attend a performance by the Shanghai Acrobatics Troupe, which is sure to thrill and amaze you with dazzling features of strength, balance and athleticism.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 13

    SHANGHAI

    This morning, stroll on the streets of Old Town and visit the 16th-century Yu Garden with its pavilions and streams. Walk the Bund, Shanghai’s most popular promenade.

    In the evening, explore studios and galleries in the M-50 Contemporary Art District on an artisan visit followed by a festive farewell dinner.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 14

    DEPART SHANGHAI
