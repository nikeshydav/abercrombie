Botswana: Romance Under the Stars (2012)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 Nights

    Relive the ambience of a bygone era on this journey through the riverine forests, expansive delta and spectacular game-filled bush land of Botswana. Romance is in the air - literally - when you sleep under the stars on an elevated platform and share a lantern-lit "star bath" on your private deck. The euphoria continues on a private sundowner cruise as you admire enjoy your beautiful surroundings and the array of wildlife that comes to the water's edge.

  • Day 1

    India/Johannesburg, South Africa

    On Arrival at Johannesburg, where you are met by an Abercrombie & Kent representative and taken to your hotel, set in one of the city's most exclusive residential suburbs amid lush hillside terraces and sculpted gardens. The balance of the day is at your leisure.

    Overnight: The Westcliff Hotel
    Meal Plan None

  • Day 2

    Johannesburg/Maun, Botswana/Okavango Delta

    Fly to Maun and connect to your camp, located on the banks of the Boro River in the Okavango Delta. This labyrinth of waterways, wetlands and islands is an unspoiled paradise for some of Africa's greatest concentration of wildlife and birdlife. Your elegant African-themed suite is set on an elevated platform in the tree line, with a wooden deck that offers expansive views over the river and lagoon. Tonight, get close to nature - and each other - when you roll your bed onto the deck for the unique opportunity to sleep under the stars.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Baines' Camp
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Okavango Delta

    Game-viewing options flourish at Baines' Camp. Resting on 260,000 acres in a private concession neighboring the famed Moremi Game Reserve, the area is superb for viewing wildlife by land in open safari vehicles or on the water from a poled mokoro (dugout canoe). However, sometimes the best way to tune into the bush is on foot. Walking safaris are a specialty at your camp, and skilled guides introduce you to the area's natural wonders.

    Return from a night game drive for a relaxing "star bath," a freestanding traditional Botswana-style zinc bathtub set out on your private deck, surrounded by lanterns.

    Philanthropic Activities: During your time at Baines' you have the opportunity to participate in an educational experience with a group of semi-habituated African elephants. After a few safety tips from your mahout (trainer), you are encouraged to touch each of the elephants and lead one of them while it gently rests the tip of its trunk on your hand. You may even get a "kiss" on the cheek from one of your new friends. At a picnic spot under large shade trees, sit down and relax with cold refreshments and brunch. The elephants are fed pre-cut fodder, and you can observe them eating and playing together after their lunch.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Baines' Camp
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Okavango Delta

    Fly to the Moremi Game Reserve and your camp, situated on the previous hunting grounds of the Batawana Tribe on Chief's Island. The camp overlooks the seasonal Piajio Channel within the Moremi Game Reserve, an area renowned for its abundant wildlife and large variety of bird life. Sanctuary Chief's Camp is made up of 12 luxury bush pavilions - each with a spacious seating area and game-viewing deck with a hammock. While you are here, take a dip in Chief's pool, pamper yourself in the spa and choose from a variety of activities that include game drives and mokoro safaris (in season).

    Overnight: Sanctuary Baines' Camp
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5-6

    Okavango Delta

    Game viewing in the Moremi is nothing short of spectacular. In addition to 400 species of birds, ostrich, antelope, crocodile, wildebeest, kudu and hippo are common here. However, the area has been dubbed the "predator capital" of Botswana, boasting leopard, cheetah, painted wild dog, spotted hyena and a large resident pride of lion. Game drives are conducted in custom-built open vehicles, offering an ideal viewing vantage point. While you are here, you may wish to take an optional helicopter excursion for a different perspective on the wildlife. A specially prepared romantic dinner is also in store for you during one of your evenings at Chief's.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Baines' Camp
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Okavango Delta/Chobe National Park

    The name "Chilwero" means "place of high view" in the local Setswana language, and true to its namesake, Sanctuary Chobe Chilwero is positioned high above the Chobe River. Fly to Chobe National Park today and check in at your award-winning lodge, which offers unparalleled panoramic views across the islands and floodplains, stretching as far as Namibia. Your cottage features either a private garden or a sala (balcony) with a hammock, ideal for cuddling and casual game viewing.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Chobe Chilwero
    Meal Plan: Meals: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    Chobe National Park

    Chobe National Park covers a diversity of landscapes, ranging from riverine forests to floodplains and periodic marshlands. The area is home to one of the largest concentration of elephants in Africa, as well as an abundance of lion, hippopotamus, buffalo, impala, puku, and a large variety of bird species. Embark on your first game drive shortly after sunrise. You can also view water-loving game up close from a different perspective when you board a small boat. End your day with a romantic couple's spa treatment.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Chobe Chilwero
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 9

    Chobe National Park/Victoria Falls, Zimbabwe

    Travel overland this morning, crossing the border into Zimbabwe toward breathtaking Victoria Falls and your accommodations at the world-famous Victoria Falls Hotel. Built in 1904, the Victoria Falls Hotel's gracious colonial style epitomizes the romance of grand travel. Late this afternoon, set out on a private cruise on the Zambezi River aboard Ra-Ikane, a small luxury boat that reflects the ambience of a bygone era. Glide lazily down the river looking for hippo, elephant or buffalo coming to drink at the water's edge and absorb the stillness of the African bush as sunset brings the day to a close.

    Overnight: The Victoria Falls Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    Victoria Falls/Johannesburg/Depart

    The final day of your adventure begins with an exclusive "Flight of Angels" that offers the amazing opportunity to travel above the majestic falls in a helicopter for unrivaled views of the crashing waters. When you are back on the ground, set out on a walking tour of Victoria Falls. Notice the permanent rainbows that crisscross the gorge, producing images that are more like paintings than reality. The unique tropical rainforest that veils the edge of the falls, the rising spray and thundering roar as these waters break against the ragged rocks below make a sight and sound never to be forgotten. Later, transfer to the airport and fly to Johannesburg for your homebound flight.

    Meal Plan: B

Honeymoon Experience in Japan

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 14 Days
    Highlights: TOKYO - KAMAKURA - HAKONE/MT FUJI - KYOTO - ISHIGAKI – TOKYO.

    From beginning to end, your 14 day honeymoon experience in Japan will leave forever lasting fond memories. This itinerary offers City life, National park & World Heritage sites with airport transfer in a vintage Rolls Royce, Traditional wedding ceremony in a Japanese Kimono at the oldest shrine in Kyoto, Full day exploring Fuji Hakone National Park, Ride the world's second longest cable car to the top of Mt Owakudani & then Transfer to Kyoto by the Shinkansen bullet train.

    • ITINERARY

  • Day 1

    Arrival Tokyo

    Travel by vintage Rolls Royce to your hotel from the airport. Dine at some of the world class restaurants located in your hotel this evening.

    Overnight: in the Peninsula Hotel, Tokyo

  • Day 2

    Tokyo/Visit Asakusa & Sensoji Temple

    Visit Asakusa, part of Tokyo's Shitamachi (old town) and home to Sensoji Temple. You also visit Meiji Jinja; Tokyo's most famous Shrine, where you will witness a traditional Shinto purification dance and receive a blessing from a Shinto Priest.

    Overnight: in the Peninsula Hotel, Tokyo

  • Day 3

    Tokyo/Visit World Famous Tsukji Fish Market

    Take a private tour of Tokyo by car. Visit the world famous Tsukji Fish Market.

    Overnight: in the Peninsula Hotel, Tokyo

  • Day 4

    Tokyo - Kamakura - Hakone/Mt Fuji

    Travel with a knowledgeable guide to Kamakura, the formal capital of Japan turned quaint seaside town. From here continue to Fuji Hakone National Park where you will stay in a traditional inn.

    Overnight: at Hakone Ginyu, Hakone

  • Day 5

    Hakone/Mt Fuji

    Visit the Hakone Open Air Museum and its intriguing number of outdoor sculptures. It als holds an extensive Picasso collection. Ride the world's second longest cable car to the top of Mt Owakudani.

    Overnight: at Hakone Ginyu, Hakone

  • Day 6

    Hakone/Mt Fuji-Kyoto

    Continue on to Kyoto by the Shinkansen bullet train. Kyoto is home to an astonishing 2000 Shrines and Temples, including 17 UNESCO World Heritage Sights that you will explore by private car. Amongst other things you will visit Heian Shrine, home to Japan's largest torii gate and strolling gardens (featured in Lost in Translation).

    Overnight: at the Granvia Hotel, Kyoto

  • Day 7

    Kyoto/Visit Nijo Castle, Kinkakuji & Daitoku-ji Zen Temple

    Spend half a day sightseeing with local guide by private transport. Visit Nijo Castle, famous for its Momoyama architecture. Continue on to Kinkakuji, the Golden Pavilion before stopping at the Daitoku-ji Zen Temple for some Zen meditation practice.

    Overnight: at the Granvia Hotel, Kyoto

  • Day 8

    Kyoto/Traditional Japanese Wedding Kimono

    The big day has arrived! After breakfast, have your hair styled and then be dressed in traditional Japanese wedding kimono. Depart by private car to Kamigamo Shrine, the oldest Shinto shrine in Kyoto and a registered World Heritage Site. Your hairdresser will also accompany you. You will then have a traditional wedding ceremony administered by a Shinto priest in this tranquil and atmospheric shrine.

    Dinner tonight is a special Wedding Dinner in a private room at the Granvia's Rakusui Japanese restaurant.

    Overnight: at the Granvia Hotel, Kyoto

  • Day 9

    Kyoto/Ishigaki

    Take a flight from Kansai airport to Okinawa and discover the unique culture that prevails here. Head to Ishigaki, part of the Yaeyama Islands, famous for its clear waters and untouched beaches

    Overnight: in Intercontinental Ishigaki Resort

  • Day 10-12

    Ishigaki/Enjoy & relax on Ishigaki Sandy Beaches

    Enjoy 3 full days to relax on Ishigaki's sandy beaches, as you discover why the Okinawans live longer than anyone else in the world. For the adventurous, Ishigaki offers some of Japan's best snorkeling. Visit a perfectly preserved Ryuku village on nearby Taketomi Island, just 10 minutes away by boat.

    Overnight: in Ishigaki

  • Day 13

    Ishigaki/Tokyo

    This morning, transfer to Ishigaki airport for the 3h 30m flight to Tokyo's Haneda Airport. The rest of the day is free for any last minute sightseeing or shopping in Tokyo. Enjoy your last meal of the honeymoon at the New York Grill, located on the 52nd floor of the Park Hyatt hotel.

    Overnight: in Park Hyatt, Tokyo

  • Day 14

    Depart Tokyo

    A private transfer to Tokyo airport is included.

Honeymoon in Vietnam – Beaches & More

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 14 Days

    Highlights: Hanoi- Ho Chi Minh-Con Dao, itinerary covers Bustling commercial city & Southeast Asia nicest beach & offer UNESCO World Heritage site Halong Bay & Hoi An ancient town, Colonial city Hanoi,Southeast Asia nicest beach Hoi An & Con Dao & Bustling commercial city Ho Chi Minh .

  • Day 1

    Arrival Hanoi

    ***Note: Early check-in at hotel is NOT included.

    Arrive in Hanoi by Flight # TBA (Own ticket).

    Meet & transfer to hotel. Balance of the day is free at leisure.

    Lunch & Dinner at your own account

    Overnight Stay: Intercontinental Hanoi Westlake

  • Day 2

    Hanoi//Visit to Duong Lam Village, Temple of Literature

    After breakfast, start your day to visit Duong Lam Village. The ancient village has a history of about 1,200 years with many houses dating back up to 400 years. One special thing about the village is that most of the buildings here are made of laterite and mud, two materials that are abundant in the area. Laterite is used on house walls, gates, wells, temple walls, and so forth. The mud is taken from ponds. Apart from its historical and tourism values, Duong Lam ancient village is an important place for scientists to study resident communities in ancient agriculture. The village gate, banyan, well, communal house are important factors in classifying Duong Lam ancient village. Enjoy Lunch in the village before heading back to Hanoi.

    Next, you will visit the Temple of Literature, founded in 1070 and dedicated to Confucius, honors scholars and men of literary accomplishment.

    Later in the afternoon, you will take an hour cyclo ride into the Old Quarter area. The Old Quarter is characterized by numerous narrow streets, which date from the 15th century, each bearing a name that reflects the business once conducted there.

    Drop by Thang Long theatre to enjoy famous Water Puppet Show, the unique Vietnamese traditional art. This art originates from rural festivals in Vietnam. The artists-men and women-stand waist-deep in the water to manipulate the puppets, making them move about, talk, laugh and even dance on water surface.

    Dinner at your own account

    Overnight Stay: Intercontinental Hanoi Westlake

  • Day 3

    Hanoi/Halong (Overnight Cruise)

    After breakfast, departure transfer to Halong bay (around 3.5 – 4 hours driving).

    Noon: Check-in at Bhaya Café, Tuan Chau Island.

    Embark on Bhaya Legend and enjoy a welcome cocktail while slowly sailing to Bai Tu Long, passing the setting for the movie Indochina, Mat Quy and Am Tich Islets. Savor a delicious fresh seafood lunch on-board.

    Bhaya Legend moves on into Vung Vieng fishing village, nestled among the limestone peaks, unaffected by ocean waves or strong winds. Visit the village by kayak or small boat to see the daily lives of locals up close.

    Swim or kayak the turquoise waters off the pristine white sandy beaches. Watch the sunset over enchanting Halong Bay while cruising to the overnight anchorage.

    Candlelit set-dinner on board

    Retire to your cabin or join the fishing program.

    Overnight: Bhaya Legend I Cruise

  • Day 4

    Halong/Hanoi/Danang/Hoi An

    Enjoy the sunrise, the lapping waves, the fresh sea air, the gentle breeze or get up at your leisure. Complimentary tea & coffee are available on request.

    The crew will ready the vessel to sail to a destination of your choice. Choices include: sightseeing at Sung Sot Grotto; hiking up Titop Mountain; lagoon kayaking or swimming on a deserted beach.

    Brunch is served onboard. Bhaya Legend returns to Tuan Chau Island at 11 am. End of services.

    Transfer back to Hanoi for free shopping time & dinner (own arrangement) before transfer to Noi Bai Airport for flight to Da Nang (Own ticket)

    Upon arrival, private pick up and transfer to check in hotel for night.

    Overnight Stay: Victoria Hoi An Resort

  • Day 5

    Hoi An//Walking tour Hoi An

    After breakfast, free at leisure for your own activities.

    In the afternoon, take a walking tour of Hoi An, discover the historic town which used to be a prosperous seaport city during the 16th to 18th centuries. Visit Hoi An colorful local market, Tan Ky old house, Fukien Assembly Hall, and the 400-year-old Japanese covered bridge.
    Travelers can also see local residents raise silkworms and produce silk for Hoi An's burgeoning textile industry.

    Lunch & Dinner at your own account

    Overnight Stay: Victoria Hoi An Resort

  • Day 6

    Hoi An//Visit to Tra Que Village

    Pick guest up at hotel and transfer to Tra Que Village (bike can be arrange somewhere in the middle route with additional charge), At the home of your host, Mr Tuan , a welcome drink of freshly picked fruits is waiting, before we take a walk around the village and learn about the history and customs of this charming village. We walk through the market gardens where hundreds of local farmers work their fields using the traditional methods of hundreds of years ago - no electrical machinery here.

    We return to the home of Mr Tuan, and this is where the real fun begins. Everyone is given farming instruments, and we set about joining the farmers in preparing the land for sewing. Our guide describes the traditional farming methods while we help fertilize the garden using seaweed from the local river, and then water it using huge tin cans strapped to our back. Time is then spent picking vegetables and herbs, and learning how to wrap them to prevent damage.
    Many of the local herbs are used by the farmers for traditional village medicines.

    It's now time to try are hands at making the village speciality named “Tam Huu” spring rolls - using the local herbs and vegetables we have just picked or Xeo Cake ( Banh Xeo). We then sit down and enjoy a lovely local meal.

    Rest of the day is free at leisure.

    Dinner at your own account

    Overnight Stay: Victoria Hoi An Resort

  • Day 7

    Hoi An//Danang//Ho Chi Minh City visit Cu Chi

    After late breakfast, transfer to the airport for your noon flight to Ho Chi Minh City (Own ticket)

    Arrive in Ho Chi Minh City, meet your guide and start to visit Cu Chi, a small community located in the northwest of Ho Chi Minh City, which gained notoriety for its network of deep tunnels that provided shelter and an effective source of infiltration for the Viet Cong during the later years of the Vietnam War. The tunnels, which reached hundreds of miles in total length, housed storage rooms, make-shift hospitals and sleeping quarters that accommodated rebel fighters for up to two weeks at a time. Heavy bombing by U.S. forces did not halt movement through the tunnels, making this an unusually successful defense against the superior weaponry of the American troops.

    Back to Saigon

    Overnight Stay: Intercontinental Asiana Saigon

  • Day 8

    Ho Chi Minh City// Insider Saigon by Vintage Vespa

    Breakfast at hotel

    This is the best way to get around and explore real Saigon bustle local life, drivers to take you on a tour: past the historic Reunification Hall, down Dong Khoi Street – passing the red-brick Notre Dame Cathedral, elegant Post Office and Opera House – before cruising down central Le Loi and out to Ben Thanh Market, squeezing through backpacker Pham Ngu Lao, out to the Chinese Cholon district and back to the city. Our Vespa tour of modern-day Ho Chi Minh City will give you a view of greater Saigon unlike anything you'll see from the hot, crowded interior of a tour bus, and much more of the city than you could see on your own. (Note: tour will not take guest inside the buildings).
    Tour includes: Bird club (morning tour), Thich Quang Duc Monument, Flower market, Funeral house, Ong Bon Pagoda, Tailor’s accessories market, Chinese medicine market in Chinatown, driving through tunnel to District 2, visit a local market, People’s Committee building, Rex Reunification Palace, Notre Dame Cathedral and the Old Central Post Office, CIA building on Dong Khoi, Opera House. Guests ride pillion behind our drivers.
    Return to hotel.

    Lunch & Dinner at your own account

    Overnight Stay: Intercontinental Asiana Saigon

  • Day 9

    Ho Chi Minh City//Con Dao

    After breakfast, transfer the airport for the airport for the noon flight to Con Dao (Own ticket)

    Arrive in Con Dao, PRIVATE transfer to Six Senses Con Dao (arranged by hotel).

    Free at leisure rest of the day.

    Overnight Stay: Six Senses Con Dao Resort

  • Day 10-13

    Con Dao// Enjoy the Resort at Leisure

    Breakfast at hotel & Full day at leisure

    Overnight Stay: Six Senses Con Dao Resort

  • Day 14

    Con Dao// Ho Chi Minh City// Departure

    Breakfast at hotel

    Transfer to airport for the flight to Ho Chi Minh to connect with your departure.(Own ticket)

    ***************************************End of A&K services**************************************

Seventh Heaven – Honeymoon in Myanmar

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 Days

    This itinerary offers the finest hotels, enjoy the best restaurants and experience a level of service you could only dream about.
    Travel to Myanmar and celebrate your new life together on this luxurious and romantic journey into one of Asia's most fascinatingly mysterious countries & Discover an impressive colonial and spiritual heritage in one of the most fascinating cities in Southeast Asia i.e. Yangon, See the dreamscape temples of Bagan and the picturesque Inle Lake, Take romantic horse carriage tours, experience special sunset cocktails in private romantic settings and exclusive boat rides, Visit a Burmese monastery and receive a special blessing traditionally bestowed upon newly wedded honeymooners to wish you good luck and eternal happiness.

  • Day 1

    Arrival in Yangon

    On arrival at the Yangon airport, you will be welcomed your tour guide, who will assist you through all the immigration procedures.

    We’ll stop on the way into town for a foot massage at Yangon’s best spa. This is the perfect way to relax and unwind after a long international flight.

    Check in to your hotel where a special Burmese wedding present is waiting.

    Depending on your arrival time, we recommend having lunch at Monsoon restaurant to enjoy freshly prepared Myanmar cuisine.

    This afternoon, enjoy a tour of Yangon's colonial city centre, with its many cultural delights. Yangon, formerly known as Rangoon, is a relatively quiet and charming city with an impressive colonial and spiritual heritage. Travel past faded colonial buildings, busy street markets, and glittering temples. If you wish, we can take as short walking tour for a different perspective of the city. The route covers around 2 kilometres and passes sites such as the Strand Hotel, the old Post Office, other historic buildings as well as parks and markets.

    End your tour at the magnificent Shwedagon Pagoda the most revered Buddhist temple in Myanmar. Although the origins of the pagoda are unclear, the local legend states that the original structure was built 2500 years ago then renovated several times until taking its current shape in the 15th century. The 8-sided central stupa is 99 meters tall and gilded with gold leaf and is surrounded by 64 smaller stupas and your guide will show you around the massive complex, explaining why this temple is so revered. Enjoy breathtaking views of the golden stupa from this vantage point.

    Return to your hotel where a bottle of champagne and special welcome present awaits you back in your room to toast your arrival to Myanmar.

    Overnight: at The Strand, Yangon

  • Day 2

    Yangon//Bagan//Visit to an Elevated Temple, Myinkaba village & Gubyaukgyi Temple

    This morning, transfer to the airport for your domestic flight to Nyaung Oo. Upon arrival, transfer to your hotel in Bagan. A special wedding present is waiting in your room upon arrival.

    After freshening up, the journey begins with a visit to an elevated temple with spectacular views over the surrounding plains. This is the perfect introduction to the grandeur and scope of Bagan’s architecture. Next continue to Ananda Temple, one of the most revered temples in Bagan and which is quiet in the morning, before all the tour buses arrive.

    Afterwards we will go to Myinkaba Village and visit Gubyaukgyi Temple which is noted for its beautiful mural paintings on its inner walls and well-preserved plaster carvings on its exterior. Nearby is Manuha Temple and Nan Paya Temple, two smaller temples with nice Buddha statues and stone work.

    Transfer to hotel for a break to escape the intense afternoon heat of Bagan.

    Later this afternoon, resume your journey with a visit to two workshops producing Bagan’s most famous products: lacquer ware and wood crafts. Watch as the skilled craftsmen use techniques passed down through generations to create beautiful items.

    Then travel by horse carriage among the temples starting at Ananda and passing many of Bagan’s famous monuments and traversing the plains.

    This evening enjoy dinner at your hotel.

    Overnight: at Aureum Palace, Bagan

  • Day 3

    Bagan//Visit the Colorful Nyaung Oo Market

    Begin today with a visit to the colorful Nyaung Oo Market where dozens of vendors come to sell their fresh produce and products. The market is at its busiest early in the morning as the locals come to do their shopping. This is a great opportunity to observe the daily lifestyle of the friendly locals.

    Continue to the neighboring villages where you will learn about their traditional livelihood. Learn the interesting process of creating poneyay gyi (pea powder paste) and see how local plum jam is made and packed.
    Then resume your temple touring with a visit to the glittering Shwezigon Pagoda, a prototype of later Myanmar stupas.

    Transfer to the Taungbi Monastery where you will participate in a special traditional Buddhist ceremony. Afterwards you will receive a special blessing from the monks to ensure good luck and happiness. This auspicious occasion is sure to be an unforgettable part of your honeymoon in Myanmar.

    Return to your hotel for a break from the midday heat and lunch at leisure.
    Late this afternoon, the romance and beauty of Myanmar's Irrawaddy River awaits you. Board a luxurious private boat for a cruise down the River, soaking in the scenery and enjoying sunset cocktails and canapés along the way. Return to shore and dinner is at your leisure.

    Overnight: at Aureum Palace, Bagan

  • Day 4

    Bagan/Heho/Inle Lake

    Transfer to Nyaung Oo Airport in Bagan for your domestic flight to Heho. Here you will enjoy a one-hour scenic drive to Nyaung Shwe, the gateway village to the lovely Inle Lake.

    Start with a visit to the Nyaung Shwe morning market where locals gather every morning to buy and sell fresh produce from the lake and its surroundings.

    Continue by local trishaw down to the bustling canal which is a hive of activity in the morning as boats from the lake come in to unload tomatoes and other vegetables for distribution to the markets around the country. Watch as baskets laden with produce are transferred from boats to trucks and bikes.

    Hop back on the trishaw and wind through quiet streets. Make a stop at the ‘tomato house’, a warehouse-like structure where the tomatoes are sorted and priced for sale. Then continue past some of the many monasteries and nunneries in the town, stopping for photos as you go.

    Then venture on to the lake by your private boat. First we’ll stop at a stilted restaurant to enjoy lunch with a view! (Client’s account) Then we resume our touring with a visit to Phaung Daw Oo Pagoda, the lake's main sanctuary, which houses five sacred Buddha statues.

    Proceed to the village of Inpawkhone to observe the traditional silk weaving techniques of Inthar people. Inpawkhone is also known for its weaving from the stems of lotus flowers, a time consuming process that results in high quality materials famous throughout the country. We also visit a cheroot factory where local Burmese cigars are rolled by hand.

    Then continue to your hotel for check-in. A fruit basket and welcome gift will be waiting in your room. Your romantic experience continues with a wine tasting at the Inle Princess Resort, before enjoying a private terrace dinner.

    Overnight:at Inle Princess Resort, Inle Lake

  • Day 5

    Inle Lake // In Dein// Explore area on Foot & Strolling around the village

    This morning, enjoy a relaxing boat trip along a small canal to the Pa-oh village of Indein. Explore the area on foot, strolling around the village, visiting a local school, and wander through the beautiful Alaung Sitthou area. Alaung Sitthou is filled with picturesque ancient stupas and has magnificent views of the lake below.

    On the return journey, stop at a stilted village for a hands-on cooking class with a local family. This is a great way to learn about the local ingredients, traditions and cuisine of the Inthar people. Enjoy a lunch of your creations as you sit on cushions around a round table.

    Return by boat to the hotel for a relaxing afternoon before enjoying dinner at the hotel. Should you wish to really indulge, head to the Resort’s spa for a luxurious massage or body treatment (client’s account).

    Overnight: at Inle Princess Resort, Inle Lake

  • Day 6

    Inle//Heho//Yangon

    This morning, transfer to the airport for your domestic flight back to Yangon.

    This afternoon you'll head to Bogyoke Market (also known as Scott Market), known for its colonial architecture and inner cobblestone streets. Browse the aisles of handmade goods, looking for the perfect souvenir of your Myanmar holiday.

    OPTION: Before leaving the market, have a chance to hear what the future holds by visiting a famous Myanmar fortune teller. San Zar Ni Bo will read your palm and discuss your future. (Client’s account) As your honeymoon in Myanmar comes to a close, enjoy a romantic candle lit dinner at Le Planteur, one of the country’s best restaurants.
