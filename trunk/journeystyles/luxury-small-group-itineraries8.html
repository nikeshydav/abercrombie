Turkey: A Journey Through History

Programme Overview

From the Hagia Sophia and Topkapi Palace in Istanbul and the volcanic rock "fairy chimneys" of Cappadocia, to a private cruise on the Bosphorus, journey through a land rich in history and tradition.

  • Duration: 11 Days/ 10 Nights

    Highlights: Istanbul | Cappadocia | Bodrum | Izmir

    A&K Advantages

    • Enjoy a private VIP meet and greet on arrival in Turkey
    • Explore the Basilica Cistern, Hagia Sophia and Topkapi Palace in peace when they open exclusively for your visit
    • Privately visit the Chora Church Museum to see the Byzantine mosaics and frescoes
    • Meet the chef who prepares your lunch at an Aegean courtyard restaurant, a Chef's Table culinary experience
    • Privately cruise the Bosphorus, serenely passing palaces, mansions and gardens along the shore
    • Experience the Cappadocian culture firsthand when you join a local family for tea in their home
    • Learn about the traditional art of weaving on an engaging Artisan Visit to the Göreme Weavers Association
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Istanbul

    Upon arrival at the Istanbul Airport, you receive VIP assistance through immigration and customs. Later, you will be assisted with transfer to the hotel by an A&K representative. Tonight, enjoy a festive welcome dinner.

    Overnight:Four Seasons Hotel Istanbul at Sultanahmet
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    Istanbul

    Begin your exploration in Istanbul with a private, before-hours opening of the magnificent Hagia Sophia, agreatarchitectural beauty. Further, enjoy an exclusive private opening of Topkapi Palace,largest and oldest palace in the world to survive till date.

    Continue to the Istanbul Archaeological Museum. Later, take aleisure stroll in Pera to view its historic iconic buildings. In the evening, experience the culture as you visit a local coffeehouse and learn about the history of coffee.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Istanbul at Sultanahmet
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 3

    Cappadocia

    Today, explore the historically significant Basilica Cistern with a private before-opening hours visit. Further, walk through the city to appreciatethe impressive city walls. Later, enjoy a private tour(subject to renovations) of Chora Church, now a museum.

    Afterward, fly to Cappadocia to view volcanic outcroppings known as fairy chimneys and observe the frescoes at the rock-carved churches of Göreme. Then, discover the stunningly restored Dark Church.

    Overnight: The Museum Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Cappadocia

    Rise early today for an optional early morning trip in a hot air balloon. Afterward, visit the intriguing underground city of Kaymakli. Enjoy lunch with a local family. Afterward, get an opportunity to interact with the Goreme Village Weavers Association. Later, unwind with a Scenic Sundowner with the city’s mesmerizing views in the backdrop.

    Overnight: The Museum Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 5

    Bodrum

    Depart forBodrum via Istanbul. Then, transfer to the Aegean and further to the ancient port of Iassoson a boat ride.

    Later, return to your hotel with stunning views of a nearby 15th-century castle

    Overnight: The Marmara
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    Bodrum

    This day, visit the Bodrum Underwater Archaeology Museum, showcasing the oldest shipwreck of all time.

    Later, meet and interact with the chef, who prepares your lunch on a Chef’s Table culinary experience in an Aegean courtyard restaurant.

    Overnight: The Marmara
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 7

    Izmir

    Travel to Izmir and explore ancient Ionian ruins, including temples of Zeus and Apollo.

    Further, discover the well-preserved ruins of Priene, on the Meander River delta, across from the remnants of once-mighty Miletus.

    Overnight: Swissôtel Grand Efes
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    Ephesus

    Continue your excursion with a visit to the archaeological site of ancient Ephesus, also visit the Temple of Diana.

    Further, make a visit to the recently excavated Terrace Houses to appreciate their mosaic floors and frescoed walls.

    Overnight: Swissôtel Grand Efes
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 9

    Istanbul

    This day, Travel back to Istanbul and visit the Ottoman Baroque Dolmabahçe Palace, one of the most glamorous palaces in the world.

    Enjoylunch in the Nisantasi district followed by a private cruiseacross the Bosphorus to enjoy stunning panoramic views.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Bosphorus
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 10

    Istanbul

    Ride like a Local on a public ferry and explore the magnificent Suleymaniye Mosque, an imperial mosque. Continue to the Grand Bazaar, where you enjoy lunch at a restaurant.

    This evening, gather for a farewell dinner.

    Overnight: Shangri-La Bosphorus
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 11

    Depart Istanbul

    After breakfast, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

  • Dardanelles & Troy Pre-Tour Extension

    Day 1

    Arrive Istanbul

    Upon arrival at the Istanbul Airport, you will be assisted by an A&K representative with transfer to the hotel.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Istanbul – MaçkaPalas
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    Troy

    Today, drive through the Thracian peninsula, crossing the Dardanelles by ferry and proceed to Troy. Explore site of the legendary Trojan War recounted in Homer's epic, The Iliad.

    Further, learn about the history of Troy and experience the culture as you meet and interact with the locals.

    Overnight: Akol Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 3

    Istanbul

    Begin the day with a travel to the Gallipoli Peninsula, where you tour sites of the massive battle fought here in 1915.

    Later, continue your journeyto Istanbul.

    Meal plan: B, L

Wonders of Chile, Argentina & Brazil

Programme Overview

A Journey to experience South America's amazing diversity.Indulge in a private wine sampling in Chile, discover Argentina's Alpine "Little Switzerland", view the awe-inspiringIguazu Falls and explore the vibrant Rio de Janeiro in Brazil.

  • Duration: 12 Days/ 11 Nights
    Highlights: Riode Janeiro| Iguazu Falls | Buenos Aires | Bariloche| Frutillar| Santiago

    A&K Advantages

    • Rest easy with pre-booked arrival night accommodations
    • Ride Like a Local when you board Santiago's metro at the Plaza de Armas en route to the Fine Arts Museum
    • Savor Chile's exquisite wines and learn more about Chilean cuisine in a Chef's Table culinary experience during a private visit to De Martino vineyard
    • Save time and enjoy a stress-free, single-day border crossing with the A&K Andes Crossing, travelling in spacious,private vehicles with clear views of the scenery
    • Catch the spirit of Buenos Aires, when you receive a private tango lesson from a pair of tango professionals
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Santiago

    After your arrival in Santiago, capital of Chile, you receivespecial VIP assistance through customs. Afterward, you are assisted by an A&K representative with transfer to your hoteland guaranteed early check-in. Unwind at leisure or embark on your choice of optional city tours — biking, shopping, visiting art galleries or exploring the home of Pablo Neruda.

    Tonight, gather for a festive welcome dinner at Restaurant Emilio, where Chef Emilio Peschiera personally greets you and shows you how to make a Pisco Sour cocktail his way.

    Overnight: Ritz-Carlton, Santiago
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    Santiago

    This morning, ride like the local to unveil Santiago in the most authentic way.

    Further, board their famed metro subway from the Plaza de Armas to the Fine Arts Museum.

    Visit the De Martino wineryfor anexclusive sampling of Chilean wine. Also learn about Chilean cuisine from a local chef on a Chef’s Table experience.

    Overnight: Ritz-Carlton, Santiago
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 3

    Frutillar

    Today, depart for Puerto Mont, a port city in the Chilean Lake District. Later, drive to Frutillara, known as the "City of Music" a charming village founded by German settlers.

    Walk past TeatrodelLago Concert Hall to take in the breath-taking views of Osorno Volcano. Continue to view the German Museum and later, enjoy lunch at a local restaurant.

    Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant before you tour the German Museum, exhibiting a settler’s house and possessions dating to the 1850s.

    Overnight: Cumbres Puerto Varas
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Bariloche

    Begin the day with a scenic drive through the Chilean Lake District. Journey along the scenic deep blue mountain lakes, snow-capped volcanoes and ancient forests,cross the Puyehue National Park and continue to Argentina.

    After arrival, enjoy lunch at Puerto Bahia Manzano. Continue with a boat ride to Victoria Island andstroll through the Patagonian paradise. Conclude your day in charmingBariloche.

    Overnight: LlaoLlao Hotel & Resort
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Bariloche

    Kick start the day as you hike the picturesque woodlands surrounding Argentina’s “Little Switzerland.” Proceedto the beach surrounding your hotel for a Scenic Sundowner, admiring the serene, twilight skies.

    Overnight: LlaoLlao Hotel & Resort
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 6-8

    Buenos Aires

    Today, depart for Buenos Aires, the elegant capital of Argentina with European flair set. After arrival you will be assisted with transfer to your hotel located in the fashionable Recoleta neighborhood.

    The next day, visit the Teatro Colón, considered one of the five best venues in the world for opera. View Eva Perón’s memorial in Recoleta Cemetery, followed by a private tango lesson in the evening, led by a pair of professionals.

    Tonight, dine at elegant Cabaña Las Lilas. Your final day here is at leisure.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Buenos Aires
    Meal plan:B D (Day 6), B L D (Day 7), B (Day 8)

  • Day 9

    Iguazu Falls

    Board a flight to the astoundingIguazu Falls, to view its awe-inspiring cascades. After exploring the Argentine side of the park, board the train to illustrious Devil’s Throat. Stroll through a scenic walkway that stretches 3,600 feet over the tropical basin.

    Overnight: Hotel Das Cataratas
    Meal plan:B, D

  • Day 10

    Rio de Janeiro

    Set off to explore Iguazu’s rumbling cascades. After lunch, fly to Rio de Janeiro.

    After arrival, settleinto your comfortable lodgings, strategically located in the heart of Copacabana Beach.

    Overnight: Copacabana Palace
    Meal plan:B, L

  • Day 11

    Rio de Janeiro

    Today, view the splendid Christ the Redeemer statue, high atop Corcovado Mountain. The afternoon is at leisure to relax or explore on your own.

    This evening, join your fellow travelers at famous Marius Crustaceos& Carnes for a festive farewell dinner featuring the finest seafood and meats

    Overnight: Copacabana Palace
    Meal plan:B, D

  • Day 12

    Depart Riode Janeiro

    Embark on a cable car up Sugar Loaf, where you enjoy lunch at local restaurant.

    Later, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B, L

Wonders of Peru & the Galápagos

Programme Overview

  • Walk the spectacular ruins of Machu Picchu with an expert guide and cruise for seven nights aboard 'Eclipse' in the Galápagos, going ashore to stroll among some of the world's most diverse wildlife.

    Duration: 16 Days/ 15 Nights

    Highlights: Lima | Sacred Valley | Machu Picchu | Guayaquil | Galápagos

    A&K Advantages

    • Cruise the Galápagos for five nights on board 'Eclipse,' a Condé Nast Traveler Readers' Choice
    • Ride Like a Local aboard a Vistadome train to explore the ruins of Machu Picchu, with an overnight stay at the only hotel on the site
    • Enjoy a Chef's Table experience when you prepare Peruvian cuisine and savor the mouthwatering results
    • Absorb Peru's rich musical traditions during a private performance by an expert in Andean music
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1-2

    Arrive Lima

    Arrive in Lima, where an A&K representative will welcome you to the capital of Peru and assist with your transfer to the hotel. The “City of Kings”, Lima, is a mélange of colonial structures, pre-Columbian sites and modern conveniences.

    The next day, Begin your exploration with a city tour; visit the Plaza Mayor, which is the birthplace of the city of Lima. View the historic Cathedral to appreciate its architectural intricacies. Then,tour the fascinating Larco Herrera Museum to view an extensive private collection of pre-Inca ceramics, one of the largest collections of its kind in the world!

    This evening, dine at the extraordinary archaeological site of HuacaPucllana.

    Overnight: Belmond Miraflores Park
    Meal plan: B, D (Day 2)

  • Day 3

    Sacred Valley

    This morning, fly to Cusco, an Andean city located at over 11,000 feet above sea level. After arrival, continue with a drive to the sacred valley of the Incas, the vast green valley that sprawls out far below Machu Picchu, and where an ancient way of life lives on.

    En route, you get the opportunity to visit a camelid breeding center.

    This evening, experience a Chef’s Table excursion, where the chef takes you through the preparation of a variety of Peruvian specialties.

    Overnight: Sol Y Luna Lodge & Spa
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Sacred Valley

    Today, visit to the imposing fortress of Ollantaytambo, a formidable stone structure of massive terraces that hug the mountain slopes.

    Delve deeper into the rich local culture as you visit the Kuychi Center, supported by A&K Philanthropy.

    Overnight: Sol Y Luna Lodge & Spa
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Machu Picchu

    Travel aboard Vistadome train into the Andes towards Machu Picchu, viewing the spectacular Peruvian countryside through the train’s glass dome.Explore the breathtaking Incan cityfrom sunrise to sunset, led by your expert guide through this Inca citadel.

    Overnight: Belmond Sanctuary Lodge
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6-7

    Machu Picchu

    Walk the ruins in the early morning mist and then choose from two hiking options to the surrounding area. Later, travel back to Cusco.

    The next day, tour Cusco, and exploreSacsayhuaman, one of the most amazing Incan constructions for tourists. Marvel at its monumental stones, precisely cut and fitted together without mortar.

    Experience the cultural fusion of the Spanish and the Incan as you visit the San Pedro market. Enjoy an Andean music presentation, where you learn about the history of a wide array of musical instruments by regional expert Tito Leon. Continue for diner at one of the city’s finest local restaurant, MAP Café.

    Overnight: Belmond Hotel Monasterio
    Meal plan: B, L (Day 6); B, D (Day 7)

  • Day 8

    Lima

    Travel back to Lima followed by an evening at leisure. Perhaps get in some last-minute shopping in a local crafts market or sample some of Peru’s culinary offerings.

    Overnight: Belmond Miraflores Park
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 9

    Guayaquil

    This day, board a flight to Guayaquil, Ecuador, to begin your Galápagos adventure.

    After arrival, you will be assisted with transfer to your hotel.

    Overnight: Hilton Colón Guayaquil
    Meal plan: B

  • Day 10-14

    The ‘Eclipse’

    Enjoy a private welcome breakfast and board a flight to the Galápagos. After your arrival, you will be escorted by a naturalist guide to embark on ‘Eclipse’, dazzling with the essence of luxury, for a five-night cruise. The ‘Eclipse’ is a vessel built for adventure, offering a stylish and relaxed atmosphere. ‘Eclipse’ has been awarded the “Smart Voyager” seal of approval by the Rainforest Alliance, certifying it as eco-friendly and socially responsible.

    Explore the Galápagos Islands, haven for some of the world’s most varied wildlife and also called a unique ‘living museum and showcase of evolution’. Here, you may view some of the unique and unusual animal life – such as the land iguana, the giant tortoise and many types of finch – that inspired Charles Darwin’s theory of evolution by natural selection following his visit in 1835.

    Because of constant change in the weather, water and wildlife conditions, your islands itinerary is not fixed, but you will embark on two daily excursions each day to the main sites and islands. Explore landmarks such as Punta Espinoza on Fernandina Island, El Barranco on Genovesa Island, Pinnacle Rock on Bartolome Island and the Charles Darwin Research Station on Santa Cruz Island.

    Begin each of your cruise days with an early breakfast followed by a morning shore excursion along a naturalist guide on a sturdy rubber raft- Panga. View sunbathing sea lions; skittering Sally Lightfoot crabs; tangles of marine iguanas; and amusing blue-footed boobies, as well as penguins, finches, and terns; even a century-old tortoise diligently trudging over the sand. Return to ‘Eclipse’ for lunch. Keep your binoculars close by for a possible sighting of whales or seabirds. In the afternoon, embark on your second excursion of the day, perhaps with an opportunity to swim, kayak or snorkel.

    Return back to the ‘Eclipse,’ relax before gathering in the dining room for another wonderful meal. Explore the mysteries of the night sky with a fascinating astronomy lecture on deck; peruse the ship’s excellent library; or gather for cocktails in the lounge to cap a day of extraordinary discoveries.

    Overnight: 'Eclipse'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 15-16

    Guayaquil

    Today, disembark The ‘Eclipse’ and depart for Guayaquil. Tonight, share your experiences with fellow guests overthe farewell dinner.

    The next day, after breakfast, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Overnight: Hilton Colón Guayaquil
    Meal plan: B, D (Day 15); B (Day 16)

Wonders of the Galápagos

Programme Overview

Cruise for seven nights aboard 'Eclipse' to the Galápagos, where you discover pristine beaches among giant tortoises, marine iguanas and blue-footed boobies, and snorkel and kayak with rays and sea lions.

  • Duration: 10 Days/9 Nights

    Highlights: Guayaquil| Galápagos Islands

    A&K Advantages

    • Cruise for seven nights in the Galápagos on board 'Eclipse,' a Readers' Choice for Condé Nast Traveler readers, enjoying its fully air-conditioned staterooms and common areas
    • Discover the Galápagos on twice-daily island excursions that include nature walks limited to 12 guests for more interaction with your naturalist guide
    • Kayak wildlife-rich waters or snorkel amid sea lions and fish
    • Learn how the seafood appetizer ceviche is prepared and sample the result during a fun Chef's Table experience
    • Enjoy a wide range of on-board activities, including stargazing, whale watching and an equator-crossing celebration
    • Ride Like a Local when you board a panga to go ashore and explore island coastlines
    • On A&K Family departures, a Children's Activity Coordinator and child-friendly programming are included throughout the journey
    • Enjoy VIP lounge access in Baltra and Guayaquil for maximum comfort prior to your departure flights
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Guayaquil

    On yourArrival in Guayaquil, Ecuador´s largest city and main port, you will be welcomed by an A&K Representative, who will assist with transfer to your hotel.

    Overnight: Hilton Colón Guayaquil

  • Day 2

    ‘Eclipse’

    Enjoy a private breakfast before you transfer to the airport for your flight to Galápagos. After arrival, you will be escorted by a naturalist guide to embark on ‘Eclipse’, dazzling with the essence of luxury, for a seven-night cruise. The ‘Eclipse’ is a vessel built for adventure, offering a stylish and relaxed atmosphere. ‘Eclipse’ has been awarded the “Smart Voyager” seal of approval by the Rainforest Alliance, certifying it as eco-friendly and socially responsible.

    After settling in, attend an introductory briefing and a fitting for your wetsuit and snorkeling gear.

    Overnight: 'Eclipse'

  • Day 3-8

    ‘Eclipse’

    Today, begin to explore the Galápagos Islands, haven for some of the world’s most varied wildlife and also called a unique ‘living museum and showcase of evolution’. Here, you may get the opportunity to view some of the unique and unusual animal life – such as the land iguana, the giant tortoise and many types of finch – that inspired Charles Darwin’s theory of evolution by natural selection following his visit in 1835.

    Because of constant change in the weather, water and wildlife conditions, your islands itinerary is not fixed, but you will embark on two daily excursions to the main sites and islands. Explore landmarks such as Punta Espinoza on Fernandina Island, El Barranco on Genovesa Island, Pinnacle Rock on Bartolome Island and the Charles Darwin Research Station on Santa Cruz Island.

As soon as your feet touch terra firma, expect to spot flora and fauna (all different depending on the island), take photographs and confer with your naturalist guide. View sunbathing sea lions; skittering Sally Lightfoot crabs; tangles of marine iguanas; and amusing blue-footed boobies, as well as penguins, finches, and terns; even a century-old tortoise diligently trudging over the sand.

In the afternoon, return to the pangasfor the second excursion of the day, perhaps with an opportunity to swim, kayak or snorkel, where you may meet face to face a water-dwelling inhabitant, such as a curious dolphin or a sea lion.

    Further, return to ‘Eclipse’ in the evening. After some time at leisure to unwind,gather in the dining room for a lavish sumptuous meal. Spend the evening at your own pace.You may opt to attend a lecture on board by one of your affable guides, recap the day’s highlightsor gain a glimpse of the upcoming adventures. Explore the mysteries of the night sky with a fascinating astronomy lecture on deck; peruse the ship’s excellent library; or gather for cocktails in the lounge to cap a day of extraordinary discoveries. Drift asleep in your air-conditioned cabin to the rise and fall of gentle ocean swells, awakening refreshed the next morning ready for a new day of adventure.

    Overnight: 'Eclipse'
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 9

    ‘Eclipse’

    Today, disembark The ‘Eclipse’ and depart for Guayaquil. After arrival, enjoy your lunch at leisure.Later, visit the Ecua-Andino Panama Hat Factory, where you are welcomed by a refreshing cocktail. Here,observe the fine craftsmanship involved in making this iconic headgear.

    Tonight, share your experiences with fellow guests overthe farewell dinner at your hotel’s elegant Portofino Restaurant.

    Meal plan: B, D
