Suggested Tanzania Accommodation

  • BARAZA ZANZIBAR

    In Zanzibari culture, the Baraza or bench has for centuries been a focal point of community life. Saunter on the award winning Bwejuu fine sandy beach, or be amazed by the coral reefs along the eastern shore. Start your day with a yoga session or perhaps a relaxing treatment in the spa.

    A fusion of design with beautiful arches, intricate hand-carved décor, hand made furniture and intricate brass lanterns adorn the spacious villas. Relax on your expansive terrace with private plunge pool whilst sipping your favourite cocktail. Mouth-watering cuisine awaits in the dining room and round off the evening with a digestif in the Sheesha Bar.

    BEHO BEHO

    In its privileged location, this is one of the most magical places it's possible to visit in East Africa. Set in the cooler highlands of the Selous Game Reserve, Beho Beho offers the aficionado the true essence of a safari refuge. From this luxurious base camp, you can explore on foot, by boat or in open safari cruiser the untold splendours of one of the world's largest game preserves.

    Eight stone-built, palm-thatched en suite cottages with open-sided lounges and dining areas allow panoramic views over the Rufiji River flood plain. Designed for comfort, this sophisticated setting for post-safari pampering includes a cocktail bar, billiards room and swimming pool.

    BREEZES BEACH CLUB & SPA

    Situated along the pristine beach of Bwejuu amid landscaped gardens of whispering palms, you will find your terraced room or suite tastefully decorated in ivory tones with teak wood finishes and Zanzibar carvings. All rooms are fully aircondiioned with ensuite bathrooms, refrigerator and a personal electric safe box and hairdryer. Experience the Sultan's Bath in the Frangipani Spa, soak in scented oils and water strewn with exotic flowers before a pampering massage. Try a fusion of Indian and Swahili spices at The Sultan's Table. Stroll for miles on a white sandy beach and enjoy an excellent variety of watersports.

    There is a wonderful opportunity to meet the local people of the Island in the nearby village of Bwejuu, take the Swahili day trip organized as part of a community project called "Breezes for Bwejuu!" Visit the homes of the local villagers, see the clinic and meet the children in their school and nursery. This is a unique perspective into the true culture and way of life of Zanzibari people. The proceeds of the tour go directly to a fund, which Breezes have set up to benefit the village.

    There is a large swimming pool at the resort as the sea at Bwejuu is tidal. A family owned and run business a personalised service and warm hospitality awaits.

    CHADA KATAVI

    Katavi National Park is as dramatic as anywhere you'll ever visit in one lifetime - one of the greatest wildlife experiences of Africa. Miles from anywhere, it has an almost mythical status and a colossal density of mammals, from elephant and cheetah to crocodile and hippo.

    Almost everywhere you look, there's something grazing, bellowing, fighting, mating or hunting. Holing up at Chada Katavi, the only camp in a million wild acres, is a rather civilised event, the perfect retreat after a day of thrilling safari adventure and discovery on wheels or on foot. Creature comforts abound in this untamed world. Hidden in the acacia and tamarind trees are just six romantic roomy tents with fine wooden furniture, large shade net windows that let in light and air, and private de luxe bathrooms. Fabulous bush cuisine is served in your remote hideaway at dinnertime.

    Escarpment Luxury Lodge

    Nestling into the edge of the escarpment which towers over Lake Manyara in Northern Tanzania is Escarpment Luxury Lodge.

    Whether from the seclusion of the private deck of your suite - or even from the huge bath in your bathroom, or from the wide and spacious deck of the main lodge, you will be afforded this incredible view as well as the possibility of sighting some of the wild life that inhabits Lake Manyara National Park.

    The main area is spacious with split level architecture and a wrap around deck to maximise the views. Inside is a lovely colourful bar area boasting comfortable sofas and private spaces for that quiet moment. There is also a computer area tucked away out of view with internet access.

    The dining area is ideally located at the front of the building with views across the lake. The verandah has tables and chairs that are set for meals in good weather and also overlook the lake and camp fire terrace.

    FUNDU LAGOON

    Only accessible by boat and set on a beautiful island beach, Fundu Lagoon operates with palpable love for the natural environment. Wooden walkways and sandy paths lead to 16 thatched tented rooms, all large and discreetly private with fantastic ocean views. The sound of the waves will surely lull you to sleep.

    This resort offers true escapism based on complete comfort and high levels of service, including an open-sided restaurant presenting outstanding international cuisine, a wonderful infinity pool and a calming spa. Snorkelling, diving, explorations by canoe and sunset cruises on a catamaran or dhow are just some of the many pleasures in store for you.

    HYATT REGENCY DAR ES SALAAM

    This large hotel - known fondly as 'The Kili' - is a popular landmark in the city, a vivid reminder of the region's elegant colonial heritage. The hotel has 180 elegant rooms and suites with luxurious interiors that offer stunning views overlooking the bay and vibrant city.

    The in-room amenities are everything you would expect, while the views over the picturesque lagoon and city islands are incredible. A choice of two outstanding restaurants, three bars, a ravishing infinity pool and an exceptional spa deliver a philosophy of hospitality founded on well-being and generous service. A convenient location to Dar es Salaam International Airport.

    Kiba Point

    Kiba Point is a very special lodge in the Selous, the sights and sounds of the river and the great variety of adventures to be had along its banks, the surrounding woodlands and open sand rivers give the client an incredible safari experience.

    Sitting by the river with fabulous sightings of the river's residents and great birding as well as lots of big mammal. Other activities include visits to Stieglers Gorge, Kidai Island, Hot Springs and Lake Tagalala.

    The accommodation is four open fronted rooms all with private plunge pools set in the forest line, all have an en suite bathroom with indoor and outdoor showers with hot and cold running water and flush toliets. The main mess area of the camp has a swimming pool with sun loungers round to cool off from the midday sun.

    There is a lounge and dining area with beautifully handmade wooden furniture, dining under the African stars is a highlight of the day.

    Open year round except for 26 March to 31 May 2012 when the camp is closed for maintenance.

    KICHECHE VALLEY

    The camp is set on the Eastern side of the 50,000 hectare Naboisho conservancy in a beautiful secluded valley dotted with Acacia trees. The valleys to the west provide a scenic backdrop whilst game driving and are ideal for game walks. Kicheche Valley Camp is unfenced and offers 6 beautiful spacious tents on raised decks. Bathrooms are en-suite and are equipped with a dresser, flushing toilet and hot and cold running water.

    There is a family suite on request. The Naboisho Conservancy is home to a variety of wildlife, this area holds the largest number of Giraffe in the Masai Mara, elephants frequently walk through the concession and researchers have recorded 3 different prides of lions totalling 35 at the latest count. Kicheche Valley Camp is an hours flight from Nairobi airport and a 40 minute game drive from the airstrip to the camp.

    KIRAWIRA CAMP

    Kirawira is the epitome of luxurious colonially-styled safari camps. Commanding panoramic views over the endlessly rolling reaches and volcanic reefs of Serengeti National Park, it recalls a bygone era of explorers and pioneers. Personal valet service and superb cuisine make for the most satisfying of experiences.

    The 25 deluxe individual tents are set on wooden platforms, each graced with a large, opulently decorated double bedroom and bathroom. Best of all a private veranda affords spectacular views over the shimmering plains. A great variety of large game can be viewed in the lush vegetation around the camp.

    LEMALA NDUTU

    Perched on the edge of a permanent marsh, Lemala Ndutu is the ultimate amphitheatre for the wildebeest migration between December and April when it's nine luxury tents are relocated from the Mara area to Ndutu region.

    The camp is situated inside the Ngorongoro Conservation Area along the border of Southern Serengeti. Tucked away under huge umbrella thorn acacias, Lemala Ndutu offers fantastic views of the marsh which attracts a large number of plains game and predators alike.

    The tents are appointed with large beds with warm duvets, wooden flooring and rugs, 24-hr solar lighting, en-suite bathrooms with a dressing area, flush toilet and a safari shower; all the tents are spacious and very comfortable.

    The mess tent offers a separate living and dining area contemporary in design with a central bar area and is beautifully decorated with comfortable leather sofas, chandeliers, side cabinets, rugs and chests reminiscent of a bygone era.

    LEMALA NGORONGORO

    Lemala Ngorongoro has eight luxury canvas tents and 1 luxury family tent. The style of the camp is classic East African Hemingway's each tent having two large single beds or a super king double bed, 24 hour solar powered lighting, a flush loo, 40 litre safari shower, running water for the basin, wooden flooring and tea & coffee.

    Each luxury tent can accommodate a triple while the family tent can accommodate 4 to 5 guests. Lemala Camp offers private lunches on the Ngorongoro crater floor and breakfast with the Masai, these are optional (additional charge).

    The location of the camp is ideal for the early morning crater trips with a team of staff and guides to look after your ever need this truly is an incredible camp to stay at to experience the Ngoronogoro Crater.

    NDUARA LOLIONDO

    A small and exclusive camp with 6 double safari "yurts", each with en-suite bathrooms, low flush toilets, bucket showers and washstands. Hot and cold water available on demand. Meals served under the stars or in dining yurt. There is also a separate library/lounge yurt. Each set of clients has their own private guide and 4WD safari vehicle.

    The emphasis is on Maasai cultural interaction, walking, night game drives as well as day game drive games, picnics & sundowners in this community area. The camp is seasonal, with stunning private campsites close to the Serengeti and Ngorongoro park boundaries. Clients staying in Loliondo have full access to the northern Serengeti (Jun to Nov) & Ngorongoro (Dec to May) for safari activities. The landscape and game viewing is spectacular, with lots of resident game and stunning private campsites which highlight the beauty and amazing diversity of this area. Children welcome over the age of 8 years old, only children over the age of 12 can participate in the walking activities.

    NGORONGORO CRATER LODGE

    Perched on the rim of the awe-inspiring Ngorongoro Crater at the eastern boundary of the Serengeti, this is one of the most architecturally spectacular safari lodges in Africa. It lies within the Ngorongoro Conservation Area - more than 3,000 square miles of pristine African wilderness.

    The lodge is made up of three adjacent intimate safari camps with breathtaking views, providing a total of 30 romantic Maasai-inspired suites sitting on stilts by the edge of the crater. Each stone and thatch suite has magnificent views of the crater from large glass windows in the bedroom, bathroom and lounge, yet is almost invisible from the crater floor. Glamorous colonial-style interiors with sumptuous touches, such as a chandelier-lit bath tub and shower, are enhanced by discreet personal service from your butler, who will bring you tea, fuel your fireplace and draw your bath. The domed dining room, with its crystal, silver and massive wooden platters, makes every meal a distinctive occasion.

    PLANTATION LODGE

    Plantation Lodge emerges like a green oasis in the soft, red-brown hills amid the vastness and silence of East Africa. To stay here is to sink thankfully into a world of rest and relaxation. The 16 individually styled rooms melt harmoniously into the garden bursting with bougainvillea, frangipani and jacaranda, with furnishings selected with great attention to detail.

    A collection of wonderfully striking sepia photographs adorn many of the walls. The bathrooms are just as beautiful and the views from the balcony simply enchanting. Fresh variety is the motto in the restaurant, where guests are pampered with culinary delights using the lodge's own vegetables, salads, herbs and fruit. Being on the doorstep of the Ngorongoro Conservation Area, the Serengeti and the Tarangire and Lake Manyara National Parks. Plantation Lodge offers a variety of safari options.

    RAS NUNGWI

    On the northernmost peninsula of Zanzibar, Ras Nungwi Beach Hotel is a cluster of whitewashed, palm-thatched cottages in rich tropical gardens overlooking the sparkling Indian Ocean. This is the place to relax and enjoy the beauty of the natural surroundings without forsaking first-class comforts.

    All 32 rooms are tastefully furnished, with hand-crafted four-poster beds and indigenous decor, balconies, air-conditioning and de luxe bathrooms. One of the most unspoilt beaches on the island, fringed by a wide and thriving coral reef, is ideal for walking, swimming and all forms of watersport. The sounds of the ocean round off your relaxing day in paradise.

    SANCTUARY KUSINI LUXURY CAMP

    Sanctuary Kusini sits in exclusivity in a remote area of these "endless plains" of legend, affording a rare opportunity for secluded game drives well away from the normal tourist trail. Relaxed and welcoming in atmosphere, here you can make your days and nights as lively or tranquil as you wish, with or without children.

    Stay in a permanent camp that blends seamlessly into the delicate environment of the predator-rich plains of the Serengeti. One of the oldest ecosystems on earth, this is where early Man appeared. Today, witness the thundering and awe-inspiring migration of over a million wildebeest and some 200,000 zebra - and the majestic big cats that prey on them. Expert guides enhance your visit by sharing their knowledge and experience acquired during a lifetime in the bush. Luscious banquets in the wild and sundowner cocktails add even more memories. Exclusive in location, luxurious in setting and rich in experience, Kusini offers a dreamy encounter with the real Africa.

    Few holiday locations pay homage to the surrounding environment with the same astonishing effect. Your temporary home in the bush will be one of only 12 well-appointed tents with highly polished floors and elevated wooden terraces overlooking the dramatic panorama of short grass plains. Attended by a personal butler and positioned in amongst the rocky outcrops to ensure your privacy, these first-rate pavilions feature an en suite bathroom with shower and comfortable furnishings throughout. Gather for elegant candlelit meals and safari tales in the dining tent, and perhaps take a book from the library for a quiet read on the veranda - or back on your own personal deck.

    SANCTUARY SWALA

    Sanctuary Swala is to be found in a remote valley within Tarangire National Park, recently rebuilt the camp offers private game viewing, wilderness indulgence and a privileged glimpse of an Africa undisturbed by mass tourism. Culture is interspersed with extraordinary sights and sounds, comfort mixed with the excitement of the chase on morning and evening expeditions. The friendly and relaxed pace is the ideal backdrop to a thoroughly enjoyable safari holiday.

    Nestling under a canopy of acacia trees, this is an exceptionally beautiful setting for a refreshing break from the western world. Large family herds of elephants and concentrations of lions and even the occasional leopard - along with wonderfully prolific birdlife - congregate around a nearby waterhole to create a sensation of majesty and purity. Being unaccustomed to vehicles, the game is unusually "wild". Accomplished hosts and safari guides enhance your stay with their own personalised African affability, professionalism and knowledge. The low volume of visitors to the park offers a truly unique and private experience.

    Each tent has en suite bathrooms with showers are complemented by fixtures and fittings including dressing tables crafted from local materials, designed to mirror the understated refinement of classic safari style. Private balconies with cosy furnishings overlook the bush and the river basin. This allows you to gaze at your leisure on resident wildlife including waterbuck and impala, after which the camp is named. Intimate meals are hosted in the comfortable dining tent, with full silver service, Pan-African cuisine and fine wines adding to the rustic charm.

    SERENGETI SAFARI CAMP

    A small and exclusive traditional safari camp with 6 double safari tents, each with en-suite bathrooms, eco flush toilets and bucket showers and washstands. Hot and cold water available on demand. Meals served under the stars or in separate dining tent.

    There is a main mess tent that includes a lounge area and a small library. Each client has their own private guide and 4wd safari vehicle. Location varies throughout the Serengeti ecosystem according to the seasons. The camp movements are pre-planned in an effort to be within reach of the wildebeest migration but cannot always be guaranteed given the unpredictability of nature. Children welcome over the age of 8 years old, only children over the age of 12 can participate in the walking activities.

    SINGITA SABORA TENTED CAMP

    Singita Sabora Tented Camp is situated on the Serengeti plains of the Grumeti Reserves. The area encompasses 400,000 acres of unrivalled wilderness and forms part of the famous migratory route, which is traversed by hundreds of thousands of animals every year. There is an Equestrian Centre situated adjacent to Sasakwa Lodge (10 kms from Sabora) and is home to sixteen horses. Experienced riders can enjoy rides on the Serengeti Plains.

    Nine luxury air-conditioned oversized tents each feature an en suite bathroom with a bath and outdoor shower, dressing area, reading area, viewing deck with Swarovski spotting scope and a library lounge tent. The main area of the camp offers a dining room, bar lounge with satellite television, DVD player and library, elevated viewing deck and a swimming pool. There is wireless internet access if needed.
