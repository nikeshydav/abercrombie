Europe Honeymoon Exclusive

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 23 Days

    Highlights : Tour at Patek Philippe museum, Excursion to Lausanne and Montreux, Private chocolate tasting at Max Chocolatier, Lunch cruise on Lake Lucerne, Excursion in to the Tuscany countryside, INSIDER ACCESS: Rome by Night, INSIDER ACCESS: Learn to the secrets of authentic pizza making, Dinner at Solo per due Restaurant(only for two), Paris: Exclusive Artists Jewellery Tour, Paris: Invitation to the World Of Fashion, Paris: Versailles with Helicopter, Paris: Create Your Own Fragrance, Paris: Exclusive Private Yacht hire with dinner.

  • ITINERARY

    Day 1

    Arrival Geneva

    Arrival at Geneva airport, flight tba
    Private transfer from Geneva airport to Geneva hotel

    Geneva – Capital of Peace is one of its names - the European headquarters of the United Nations is located here. Another well-deserved name for this city with a humanitarian tradition, international organisations and cosmopolitan flair, is The World's Smallest Metropolis. Quays, lakeside promenades, parks, elegant stores and lively streets and alleyways in the old part of town wait to be discovered. Boat trips beckon on the lake and the Rhone River. The Alps seem close enough to touch. Geneva - small metropolis surrounded by unspoiled nature and breath-taking scenery.

    Overnight: Hotel Mandarin Oriental

  • Day 2

    Geneva

    Private transfer from Geneva hotel to Patek Philippe museum
    Private guided tour at Patek Philippe museum (2 hours)
    Private transfer from Geneva Patek Philippe museum to hotel

    The Patek Philippe Museum hosts an extraordinary collection of watchmaking masterpieces dating from the 16th to the 20th century. This private tour highlights the historical significance of the art of watchmaking and enamelling.

    Overnight: Hotel Mandarin Oriental

  • Day 3

    Full Day to Lausanne

    Full day excursion to Lausanne and Montreux by private transfer (max. 8 hours)
    Private guided tour at Chillon Castle (1 hour)
    Private guide for walking tour of Lausanne (2 hours)

    On your way to Lausanne, you will take the scenic road along the shores of this sparkling lake, the largest in Europe, and through the vineyards and picturesque villages and towns of this major wine-producing area of Switzerland.

    Built in three tiers, Lausanne has the sparkling blue waters of Lake Geneva at its feet, deep green forests at its back and snow-capped Alps in the distance. It is a city to explore with your senses, offering everything from quaint country markets to cosmopolitan shops and shows. A climb up to the Old City will take you to Lausanne's superb gothic cathedral, to the ancient castle of Saint Maire and to the university with its fine museums. Down by the waterside is the picturesque resort of Ouchy, where you can linger in one of the lakeside restaurants or visit the famous Olympic Museum. Continue to Montreux by coach. Montreux is the most frequented resort on Lac Leman (Lake of Geneva), thanks to its beautiful location and pleasant surrounding countryside. The city stretches gently along the shore of a wide, south-facing bay and rises above the lake like a huge amphitheatre amidst the wooded and vine-clad slopes. Montreux has attracted many visitors since the 18th century. Its palm trees give sample proof of its mild climate. The main features of the city tour is the Vieux Montreux (the old parts of the township) and, of course, the visit of the Castle of Chillon - one of the most impressive, medieval strongholds of Switzerland, immortalised by many famous poets.

    Overnight: Hotel Mandarin Oriental

  • Day 4

    Geneva to Interlaken

    Today you will pick up your self-drive car MERCEDES ML 63 AMG from hotel and you will have this car from 4th till 19 Dec 13.
    Self-drive from Geneva to Interlaken via scenic Gruyeres, travel time is approx.. 2 hours and 30 minutes.

    This scenic transfer takes you to the Swiss Cheese & Chocolate country. Drive along picturesque vineyards to Chexbres and Chatel St. Denis, typical wine growing villages. Visit the Cheese Factory where you can watch the cheese production and even taste some of it. Next highlight is the visit of the famous Chocolate Factory Cailler in Broc. Perched on a hill in a landscape full of grace and harmony is the picture-book, medieval town of Gruyeres with its quaint houses dating from the 16th and 17th centuries, a real paradise for photographers. Continue to Interlaken with you rental car.

    Interlaken is one of the best known and most popular summer holiday resorts in Switzerland, outstandingly well-equipped to cater to the needs of visitors. The Hohematte, 700 yards long, is a pedestrian paradise of shops, hotels and sidewalk cafes, interrupted only by inviting gardens. For a comfortable excursion through this quaint town, take a horse-carriage ride. You will view the mountains, the lakes, the endless beauty of Switzerland; all are at your fingertips in this comfortable, inviting, and elegant town.

    Overnight: Hotel Victoria Jungfrau

  • Day 5

    Visit of Mount Jungfraujoch

    Full day excursion to Mount Jungfraujoch (unescorted, without guide & vehicle),

    This excursion takes you to the very heart of the Bernese Alps - the mighty "Eiger" (13'026 ft/ 3970m), "Monch" (13'449 ft/ 4099m) and "Jungfrau" (13'643 ft/ 4158m), which form an imposing nucleus of the Alpine Region. Walk to Interlaken Ost station and take the train into the Waterfall Valley of Lauterbrunnen where you change and board the train to Jungfraujoch. The travelling time from Interlaken to the Jungfraujoch is approx. 2 1/2 hours each way. At Jungfraujoch, the highest railway station in Europe, you can visit the Ice Palace and the Sphinx Terraces - reached by lift affording breathtaking view of the Aletsch Glacier, the largest river of ice in Europe. Refreshment or a meal can be obtained at the Jungfraujoch Restaurant "Top of Europe". After approx. 21/2 hours you will return to the mountaineering village of Grindelwald, the winter sports and mountaineering centre. Enjoy the view on the mighty mountains, before returning to Interlaken.

    Overnight: Hotel Victoria Jungfrau

  • Day 6

    Interlaken to Lucerne

    Self-drive from Interlaken to Lucerne via scenic Brunig pass
    Travel time is approx.1 hour.
    Private chocolate tasting at Max Chocolatier (1 hour)

    After a scenic drive along the Lake of Brienz have a short stop at the quaint typical Swiss Village of Brienz, the center of Switzerland's wood-carving industry. Before leaving you should not forget to take one or two pictures of these beautiful wood chalets with bright splashes of the geraniums in the window sills. Cross the Brunig Pass and drive along the Lakes of Lungern, Sarnen and Lucerne.

    The world-famous, historical city of Lucerne lies in Switzerland in the heart of Europe. Because of its central location right on Lake Lucerne, which is surrounded by grand mountains like Mount Titlis at 3000 meters high or the impressive Mount Pilatus, Lucerne is considered to be the "Switzerland in Switzerland". You will find everything here: the lake, the mountains, the city a distinctive consonance of nature and work of man, which has been preserved and carefully developed for centuries.

    What do you want to see while in Lucerne?
    The Chapel Bridge with its gabled paintings of old battles and the adjacent quaint quarters with little streets ranging and changing from silence to abundant liveliness? The curiosities of the museums, the treasures of the many churches? Take a leisurely walk through a maze of small streets, bridges, promenades and plazas dominated by countless historical towers, fountains and frescoed buildings.

    Overnight: Palace Luzern

  • Day 7

    Excursion to Mount Pilatus

    Excursion to Mount Pilatus (Self-drive) unescorted, without guide & vehicle
    After a drive through Lucerne and Kriens by rental car ascend to Frakmuntegg by gondolas which sweep you over pine forests and mountain meadows and change to aerial cable car to Pilatus Kulm. On top you have time to walk up to the vista point. There will be time to admire the breathing panorama over the Swiss Alps and Lucerne and also to enjoy a delicious meal in the restaurant. Descend to Kriens by cable car and gondola again and drive back to Lucerne.

    Overnight: Palace Luzern

  • Day 8

    Cruise on Lake Lucerne

    Lunch cruise on Lake Lucerne (2 hours)

    Note:

    Lunch will be chosen and paid on spot

    To see Central Switzerland at its best, you need only step aboard any one of the five "old-timer" paddle steamers for a journey into time. If you prefer however, there are 15 elegant saloon cruisers also available for a tour of the lake that encompasses 38 kilometres of typically Swiss scenic splendour. You will drift by peaceful meadows and idyllic bays, see dramatic inland fjords stretching to the bottom of vertical cliffs that rise majestically to the first Alpine summits. Enjoy your lunch break on the lake. The chef on board has prepared a number of delightful culinary surprises for you.

    Overnight: Palace Luzern

  • Day 9

    Lucerne to Zurich Airport

    You will drop your self- drive car at hotel and you will be privately transferred to Zurich Airport.
    Departure Zurich airport.
    Upon arrival at Florence airport you will be met and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel in Florence. The remainder of the day is at leisure to relax after your flight.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Florence

  • Day 10

    Florence

    Today you will pick-up your self-drive car MASERATI GRAN CABRIO from hotel which will be with you from 20th till 23rd Dec.

    This morning you will be met in the lobby of your hotel by your Guardian Angel who will accompany you on foot to your cooking school in the centre of Florence. Experience the vibrant sights and sounds of a local food market during a guided tour (1hour) with your English-speaking tutor. Then enjoy a private three-hour hands-on lesson which can be tailored to your level of ability, your taste and interests, and will teach you some of the secrets of the local and national cuisine.

    This afternoon you will enjoy a half-day walking tour (4 hours) of Florence with your local English-speaking guide. Your tour will include visits to the Uffizi Gallery, housing some of the world's most important art treasures, and the Church of Santa Croce, where many famous Florentines such as Michelangelo and Galileo are buried.

    Vasari Corridor: Your tour will include a visit to the Uffizi Gallery, housing some of the world's most important art treasures. The Uffizi was built in 1560-80 as a suite of offices (uffici) for Duke Cosimo I's new Tuscan administration. Here you will enjoy a very special private entry to the Vasari Corridor*, the private corridor built in 1565 by Giorgio Vasari, court architect to the Medici family. Having walked through this private 'Princes' route' to the Pitti palace on the south side of the River, enjoy some time taking in the Boboli Gardens, built as a private garden to the impressive Medici residence.

    Inclusions: Inclusions: Services of A&K Guardian Angel to accompany by foot to cooking school;
    Cooking lesson (approx. 3 hours); Services of a local English-speaking Guide up to 4 hrs; Entrance to the Santa Croce Church and Leather school; Entrance to the Uffizi Gallery; Entrance fund
    Exclusions: Any meals not specified; Tips

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Florence

  • Day 11

    Day at Leisure

    Today if you want you can visit chianti region on your own using self drive (unescorted, no guide & vehicle)

    Renowned for its art, wine, history and evocative landscapes, Tuscany is a region where the past and present merge in pleasant harmony. Today's full-day tour takes in the Chianti wine region; often considered the most beautiful area in Tuscany. Hill-towns gaze across the countryside from high, many encircled by Etruscan walls and slender cypress trees. As you travel along winding roads, soak up the rich panorama comprising beautiful rolling hills, vineyards and olive groves. Admire the charming hamlets and farmhouses juxtaposed with fortified villas and castles, architectural symbols of the violence and inter-communal strife that tore Tuscany apart in the Middle Ages. Heading south of Florence towards Siena, enjoy a leisurely espresso or gelato in the historic main square of Greve, considered the principal town in Chianti. This afternoon, sit back and enjoy a stunning drive through the Chianti countryside until you reach the hilltop town of Castellina, whose noble origins date back to the thirteen century. Here you will have time to soak up the lively atmosphere as you wander through its charming streets, prior to visiting two local vineyards to sample the world-famous Chianti Classico.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Florence

  • Day 12

    Day at Leisure

    Today if you want you can visit Tuscany for shopping using self-drive. unescorted, no guide & vehicle)

    Excursion in to the Tuscany countryside to visit the various outlet stores, the ideal opportunity to be immersed into Italian haute couture. Visit the best outlets, where you can shop with savings of up to 70%. At The Mall, you can find famous names such as Gucci, Bottega Veneta, Emanuel Ungaro, Ermenegildo Zegna, Giorgio Armani, Hogan, I Pinco Pallino, La Perla, Loro Piana, Marni, Salvatore Ferragamo, Sergio Rossi, Tod's Valentino and Yves Saint Laurent. Only 5km's from The Mall is located the Fendi and Dolce & Gabbana outlets' then on to the Prada village "SPACE", where you'll find designers Helmut Lang, Miu Miu and Jil Sander. Lunch can be enjoyed in a local restaurant (payable directly).

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Florence

  • Day 13

    Florence to Rome

    Car pick up from Rome hotel (you will drive from Florence hotel to Rome hotel on today).
    Check-in at hotel.

    INSIDER ACCESS: Rome by Night
    This evening you will be met at your hotel by your English-speaking guide and private vehicle for a 2-hour drive around the eternal city at dusk. As the evening progresses and the sun goes down Rome’ s city lights superbly highlight the monuments and sights of the city. Explore the most famous piazzas and fountains of Rome aglow, each of which takes on a new perspective when lit. Proceed to the Gianicolo, the eighth hill of Rome for a breathtaking view of the entire illuminated city.

    Overnight: St. Regis, Rome

  • Day 14

    Rome

    Enjoy a half-day tour (4 hours) which will no doubt leave you in awe as your guide shows you the heart of the Roman Empire. Visit the imposing Colosseum, commissioned by Emperor Vespasian, the stadium could accommodate up to 50,000 spectators who came to view the gladiatorial battles that lasted up to 100 days! Adjacent is the Arc of Constantine, dating from AD 315 it is a forerunner to many other Triumphal Arches around the world. Possibly even more impressive are the excavations of the vast Roman Forum, where the remains of temples, streetscapes, palaces and homes have been uncovered and can now be visited up close. Your guide will bring to life the atmosphere of Rome's pulsating heart, crowded with people from across the Roman Empire. Your guide will also explain the other nearby Emperor's forums; Traiana, Augusto and Cesare. Your visit today also includes the Palatine Hill, inhabited since the 9th century BC, archaeologists believe this to be the place where Romulus, founder of Rome, lived.

    Inclusions: Transport in private, air-conditioned vehicle with English speaking driver (1-2 pax = Mercedes E Class/ 3-4 pax = Mercedes Viano or similar/ 5-6 pax= Mercedes 8 seater minibus); Services of an English-speaking local Guide; Entrance to the Colosseum and Roman Forum & Palatine; Entrance fund;
    Exclusions: Any meals not specified; Tips;

    Overnight: St. Regis, Rome

  • Day 15

    Dinner at SOLO PER DUE

    INSIDER ACCESS: Learn to the secrets of authentic pizza making


    This morning, transfer to a popular Roman Pizzeria for a private cooking class, starting at 10:00am. You will prepare the dough, learning of the various aspects as you go from recommend flour and dough rising times, to the toppings and cooking process required for the perfect pizza! Your pizzaiolo speaks English and can give you some further insight into what it is like running a restaurant business in Rome. After your lesson (up to 3 hours), enjoy the fruits of your labour for lunch.
    Later in the evening we have arranged a special dinner for you.


    Solo per due Restaurant


    Today you will be transferred by private vehicle to the smallest restaurant in the world with a table just for you spend a special moment in superb atmosphere enjoying a divine dinner. SOLO PER DUE - or "Just for Two" - is the smallest restaurant in the world: it has only one table and it takes just two people at a time. So there are no queues, no turns, and no waiting; all the attention is dedicated to the two people who have booked. The menu is entirely based on the use of fresh ingredients, chosen each day according to the season and to the clients wishes (the menu can be based on meat or fish accordingly).
    After your relaxed Dinner you will be returned to your hotel in Rome.
    Transfer for dinner is by Mercedes E class and driver at disposal until the end of thedinner aperitivo, 4 courses dinner, exclusively Italian cuisine, accompanied by wines from only the best cellars.

    Overnight: St. Regis, Rome

  • Day 17

    Half day Vatican sightseeing

    With your guide and driver, cross the Tiber River into the awe inspiring Vatican City (4 hours). The Vatican was created by the Lateran Treaty in 1929 and is the World's Smallest Independent State. Visit the Vatican Museums, part of the Papal Residence which is estimated to contain 1400 rooms, chapels and galleries. The Museums contain an extraordinarily rich collection of precious arts, maps, sculptures and antiquities however the most breath-taking masterpiece is Michelangelo's frescoed ceiling in the Sistine Chapel. At the centre of the Vatican lies the vast Basilica of St. Peter, you pass by the colourfully dressed Swiss Guard into the world's largest church. The present Cathedral is a beautiful mix of Renaissance and Baroque architecture, formed under the hands of masters such as Michelangelo. The stunning interior is an elaborate and detailed display of marble, mosaic, gilt and bronze. Your tour includes express entry to the Vatican Museums, past the regular lines. The Vatican requires visitors to cover upper arms and legs to below the knee
    Inclusions: Transport in private, air-conditioned vehicle with English speaking driver (1-2 pax = Mercedes E Class/ 3-4 pax = Mercedes Viano or similar/ 5-6 pax= Mercedes 8 seater minibus); Services of an English-speaking local Guide; Entrance to Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel, and St Peters Basilica; Entrance fund;

  • Day 2

    Paris

    Today you will enjoy a full day (8 hours) sightseeing tour in Paris with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. This morning an overview tour will include famous Parisian sights such as the Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Champs Elysées and the Ile de la Cité, where you will visit the beautiful Notre Dame Cathedral and the Sainte Chapelle, a pearl of Gothic architecture. This afternoon visit the world-famous Louvre Museum. Originally the Royal residence of the Kings of France, the sprawling Louvre palace contains one of the most important art collections in the Western world, spanning from remotest antiquity to the mid 19th cent, with the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo as perhaps its two most famous exhibits, and the upturned glass pyramid by I.M. Pei at the entrance adding a contemporary touch.

  • Day 3

    Paris/ Versailles/ Paris

    Enjoy a half-day (up to 5 hours) sightseeing tour to Versailles with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. Situated to the west of Paris, Versailles is the location of the huge palace that was constructed in the 17th century for Louis XIV, the 'Sun King'. It became the model for grand houses of royalty all over Europe. Take a tour of the opulent State Apartments that culminate in the magnificent Gallery of Mirrors. You may like to take a stroll in the formal ornamental gardens that are embellished with many fountains, and see the Grand Canal that stretches into the distance across the park. Return to Paris following your visit.

    Inclusions: Transfer in a private, air-conditioned vehicle with English-speaking driver (1-2 pax Citroen C6 or similar, Services of an English-speaking local guide, Entrance fees to Versailles Palace Gardens and State Apartments

    Exclusions: Any meals or entrance fees not specified & Tips

    Overnight: Sofitel Paris Le Faubourg
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Paris/ Dijon

    Morning after breakfast, check out and you will be transferred to the station to board train to Dijon.

Italy: Flavors of Venice, Tuscany & Rome (2012)

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 Nights

    Italy captures classic romance like no other place in the world. This journey is a toast to your love as you traverse the canals of Venice in your own gondola, steal a kiss on Juliet's balcony in Verona, savor a private picnic in the heart of an Umbrian vineyard and explore Rome, a city so beautiful it gave romance its name. Fall in love all over again as you live the dolce vita of this sensual, beguiling country.

  • Day 1

    India/Venice

    Begin your romantic adventure by boarding an flight to Venice, one of the world's loveliest cities. On arrival, you are met by an Abercrombie & Kent representative and transferred to your hotel. Speed across the lagoon to your palace hotel, with a convenient location right on the city's Grand Canal. Arrive at the hotel - a 14th-century Venetian palace adorned with masterpieces by the likes of Tiepolo - by private jetty. This evening, take to Venice's famous canals as you cruise past the Santa Maraidella Salute Church and into the lagoon to toast the sunset and your romantic holiday with a glass of Veneto Prosecco. Continue to the beautiful island of Torcello, where a table is reserved for you in the delightful gardens at the LocandaCipriani. Savor one of their specialties: Adriatic fresh oysters.

    Overnight: Ca' Sagredo
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    Venice

    Explore the less-visited side of "La Serenissima" (a fitting nickname for this most serene of Italian cities) alongside your expert local guide today. Lose yourselves in the charming labyrinth of narrow streets and passageways in the old artisans' quarter, where postcard-perfect scenes await around every corner. Wander through the Frari Church, which houses the breathtaking masterpieces of Titian, Bellini and Canova. This evening, your private gondolier rows and serenades you through the Grand Canal and under the Rialto Bridge on to your restaurant of choice.

    Overnight: Ca' Sagredo
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 3

    Venice/Verona

    Travel overland to the Roman city of Verona, the setting for Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet. The family homes of the famous fictitious lovers are still standing, and today you have the chance for a private moment together on Juliet's balcony, perhaps even the opportunity to renew your wedding vows in this truly special location. Cap a lovely day with front-row seats at an epic opera performance al fresco in the Roman Arena, such as La Traviata (summer only). Tonight, overnight in the luxurious surroundings of a 13th-century palace.

    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 4

    Verona/Assisi

    Travel through central Italy and the stunning Tuscan countryside, a land of rolling vineyards and charming villas. Explore the region around Cortona, synonymous with the romantic novel and film Under the Tuscan Sun. Gaze at the tranquil beauty of Tuscany's Val d'Orcia, a spectacular sight to behold with your beloved. Enjoy a leisurely afternoon in medieval Assisi. You may wish to spend time in your hotel's spa, which sits within the 2,000-year-old Roman structure of the building. Your accommodations this evening are within a beautifully renovated convent, with incredible views over the dramatic Umbrian countryside.

    Overnight: Nun Assisi Relais & Spa Museum
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Assisi

    This morning, you are at leisure to explore the fascinating town of Assisi on your own. In the afternoon, visit a family-run Umbrian vineyard, where a gourmet picnic awaits you amid the serene countryside setting of the vineyard. Dine under the shade of a tree with the medieval city of Assisi as your backdrop. Take your time - your personal waiter will continue to serve estate wines as long as you wish to linger.

    Overnight: Nun Assisi Relais & Spa Museum
    Meal Plan: B

    South Georgia

    Today, travel deeper into the heart of the Umbrian countryside for one of the most memorable meals of your life. Dubbed the world's smallest restaurant, the one-of-a-kind Solo Per Due is dedicated to hosting and serving just you and your partner a dining experience tailored around your tastes and preferences. Your waiter serves on the call of your bell; otherwise, the restaurant and terrace are just for you, offering stunning views through Umbria's olive-tree clad hills. Continue to legendary Rome. Many say that the "Eternal City" is even more stunning as the sun goes down and the cityscape lights up. Alongside your guide and chauffeur, experience Italy's capital at night, touring the beautifully lit Trevi Fountain, Baths of Caracala and ancient Roman Forum before heading up to a lovely villa in the Borghese Gardens for the finale. At the top of the villa, champagne and canapés await you and your loved one to share in private as you gaze from your terrace out into the city that gave romance its name. Your romantic boutique hotel enjoys one of Rome's premier addresses, just across from the stunning Roman Colosseum.
