Splendors of Morocco

Programme Overview

Delve into Morocco, from its historic cities to its mysterious desert, staying in an A&K Exclusive Desert Tented Camp by the Erg Chebbi dunes and spending three nights in Marrakech.

  • Duration: 11 days/ 10 nights

    Highlights: Casablanca | Fez | Erfoud | Merzouga | Ouarzazate | Marrakech

    A&K Advantages

    • Experience the Sahara in style when you sleep under the stars at an A&K Exclusive Desert Tented Camp alongside the stunning Erg Chebbi dunes
    • Experience life like a local with a ride on camelback and by horse and carriage; you also learn the secrets of Moroccan cuisine at an exclusive Chef's Table experience
    • Stay in the "Red City" at the luxurious Four Seasons Resort Marrakech, your home for three nights
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Casablanca

    On your Arrival in Casablanca, you will be welcomed by an A&K Representative, who will assist with your transfer to the hotel. After settling in, spend the rest of the day at leisure in this romantic city.

    Gather tonight for a cocktail reception and welcome dinner.

    Overnight:Hyatt Regency Casablanca

  • Day 2

    Fez

    Begin the day with a visit to the Hassan II Mosque, the largest mosque in Morocco and Africa and the 7th largest in the world. Proceed to the Moorish city of Rabat and the Mohammed V Mausoleum, a landmark of the Moroccan capital. Admire the magnificentMauresque-style Hassan Tower and Oudaya Kasbah, along with its scenic Andalusian garden.

    Conclude your day as you savor a special welcome dinnerat a traditional riadin Fez.

    Overnight: PalaisFaraj
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Fez

    Today, experience the Middle Ages era in the largest living medieval city in the worldFez el-Bali (“Old Fez”), the cultural heart of Morocco. Stroll through the 9,000 lanes, alleys and souks that comprise its old quarter, where locals conduct their day-to-day business.

    Later, visit the 14th-century Karaouine, the Hispano-Moorish AttarineMedersa (Koranic College)and the Nejjarine Square, en-route admire the beauty of the elaborate mosaic-tiled fountain.

    Continue toexplore the 13th century Fez el-Jadid (“New Fez”), which boasts of traditional Jewish architecture of wrought iron-decorated windows and carved wood balconies.

    Overnight: PalaisFaraj
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    Fez

    Travel overland to the bustling modern city of Meknes, built in the 17th century. Discover the architectural legacy as you view the monumental Bab Mansour gateway, considered one of the finest gates of Morocco.Continue to the Granaries and the Royal Stables, with the capacity to accommodate around 12,000 horses.

    Later, viewMoulay Ismail’s Mausoleum, one of the few shrines in Morocco that may be visited by non-Muslims. Continue to the Roman ruins of Volubilis, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to Morocco’s largest and best-preserved Roman ruins with excellently preserved mosaics. With triumphal arches andbasilicas, the Volubilis skyline brims with iconic examples of Roman architecture.

    End the day with a return journey to Fez.

    Overnight: PalaisFaraj
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 5

    Erfoud

    This day, travel to the small, desert-fringed Erfoud. Stop for lunch in Midelt en route and observethe everyday life of the localresidents as you cross Berber villages, endless palm plantations and the majestic Atlas Mountains.

    Settle into your hotel, set amid dunes and lush vegetation, with the remaining day at leisure.

    This evening,join your fellow guests for dinner.

    Overnight: Kasbah Hotel Chergui
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    Merzouga

    Kick start your morning to discover the fortified desert town of Rissani and explore the quarry, site of fossilized fish. Continueto the desert dunes of Erg Chebbi by four-wheel-drive vehicle and be welcomed at your nomadic tented campwith mint tea and pastries. ExploreErg Chebbilike a local on camelback, viewing its stunning dunes that can reach up to 500 feet high, creating a splendid landscape.

    Later, Return to your camp for a traditional meal before a campfire, accompanied with local entertainment.

    This evening, unwind as the sounds of the Sahara lull you to sleep.

    Overnight: A&K Exclusive Desert Tented Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Ouarzazate

    Today, travel to Ouarzazate, once a stopping point for African traders travelling to the cities of Morocco and Europe. En route, take in the scenic views of the High Atlas’ southern slopes, intersecting the Dades Valley’s flourishing crops, irrigation canals and palm trees. Travel along the “Road of a Thousand Kasbahs” and view the 17th-century Amerhidl Kasbah, one of the grandest in the regions, its facade printed on Morocco’s 50-dirham note.

    Overnight: Le Berbère Palace
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    Marrakech

    ExploreOuarzazate, referred to as “The Door of the Desert”. Today, the city is a regional trade center, known for its pottery and carpets. View the Taourirt Kasbah, which now consists of simple clay houses and crenellated towers that are beautifully decorated with geometric motifs.

    Further, see the spellbindingAïtBenhaddou, a UNESCO World Heritage, with the region’s most exotic kasbahs. The structures here defy the laws of gravity as they perch upon the steep slope. (Some of these buildings have been featured in films, including Lawrence of Arabia and Gladiator.)

    Next,explorethe fascinating “Red City” of Marrakech. This evening, indulge in dinner at your luxurious hotel, where you interact with chef and learn about the nuances and history of Moroccan cuisine.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Marrakech or Selman Marrakech
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 9

    Marrakech

    Today, tour the Marrakech’s ancient medina, the old walled section of the city. Travel to the Dar Si Said, once a palace and now the Museum of Moroccan Arts.

    Further, visit the 12th-century Koutoubia Minaret, one of the finest examples of Islamic religious structure in North Africa. Walk through the vibrant Djemaa el- Fna square where fire-eaters, mime artists and street musicians perform.Proceed to the Bahia Palace, an exemplary specimen of privileged life in the 19th century.

    In your leisure time, perhaps explore the city’s fabled, labyrinthine souks, their bustling alleyways brimming with all manner of wares.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Marrakech or Selman Marrakech
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 10

    Marrakech

    Today, appreciate the beauty of the 12th-century Menara Gardens,surrounded by the Atlas Mountains, olive groves, orchards and reflecting pools. Then, ridea horse-drawn carriage to JardinMajorelle, a scenic garden created by French painter Jacques Majorelle. The remaining day is at leisure to explore at your own pace. You may also take theopportunity to interact with Moroccan children at Al Karam, anorganizationsupported by A&K Philanthropy dedicated to assist homeless children.

    Tonight, attend a special farewell dinner with entertainment by a local gnaoua band.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Marrakech or Selman Marrakech
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 11

    Depart Marrakech

    After breakfast, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

  • Essaouira Post-Tour Extension

    Day 1

    Arrive Essaouira

    On your Arrival in Essaouira, you will be welcomed by an A&K Representative, who will assist with your transfer to the hotel

    Overnight: L’HeureBleuePalais
    Meal plan: L

  • Day 2

    Essaouira

    Begin to explore with a guided tour of the fortified town of Essaouira, observing its medina (old town) filled with artisan’s workshops and boutiques.

    Tonight, gather with your fellow travelers for a special farewell dinner.

    Overnight: L’HeureBleuePalais
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 3

    Casablanca

    Today, take a scenic drive along stunning coastline past striking cliffs and ocean waves to Casablanca. Settle into your hotel and spend the remaining day to relax or explore at leisure.

    Overnight: Hyatt Regency Casablanca
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 4

    Depart Casablanca

    After breakfast, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B
