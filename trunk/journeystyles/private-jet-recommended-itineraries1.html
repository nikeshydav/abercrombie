Africa: Across a Continent by Private Jet

Programme Overview

Immerse yourself in the wonders of a continent on an inspiring journey on a privately chartered jet equipped with fully lie-flat seats. Africa: Across a Continent by Private Jet takes you to seven countries in 19 days, revealing each destination at its most authentic.

  • Duration: 19days/ 18 nights

    Highlights: Rome | Ethiopia | Uganda | Tanzania | Zambia | Botswana | Namibia | South Africa

    A&K advantages

    • Come aboard our exclusively chartered Boeing 757-200ER jet and stretch out on a seat that extends into a fully lie-flat bed
    • Meet with local dignitaries at a private reception in Addis Ababa for a unique insight into modern-day Ethiopia
    • Come face-to-face with mountain gorillas in Uganda, and encounter the local Batwa tribe and learn their ways of living
    • Spot wildlife in the Serengeti, accompanied by the finest safari guides on the continent
    • See Victoria Falls from the air on a private helicopter flight
    • Enjoy the exclusive hospitality of five-star luxury camps in Botswana and Zambia
    • Experience a private feast in the Namibian desert, complete with fire spinners, local music and stunning vistas
    • Take in the views from atop South Africa's Table Mountain at a sundowner cocktail reception
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Rome

    Upon your arrival in Italy’s ancient capital, Rome, you are greeted by an A&K representative and assisted with a private transfer to your hotel.

    This evening, join your fellow guests for a cocktail reception followed by a festive dinner in a private venue at your hotel, the St. Regis Rome.

    Overnight: The St. Regis Rome
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2-3

    Ethiopia

    Stretch out on A&K’s private jet and savor the finest amenities of any plane in the sky as you travel southwards to Ethiopia, among the first regions on earth to embrace Christianity. Begin with your experience of this ancient desert seat of the Queen of Sheba, with your first night featuring a welcome dinner accompanied with traditional music.

    The next day board a local charter flight to Lalibela, where you spend a day to explore this isolated town famous for its churches carved directly from the pink granite earth. Learn more about the Ethiopian Orthodox Church by meeting a local priest followed by a presentation of traditional religious songs. Later, return to Addis Ababa, to view a replica of “Lucy”, 3.2 million-year old Ethiopian fossilized skeleton. Here, a curator takes you through the discovery and significance of one of the world’s most famous early human fossils, at the National Museum. Tonight, attend a cocktail reception followed by dinner at the hotel, accompanied by special guests and local dignitaries.

    Overnight: Sheraton Addis
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4-6

    Uganda

    Depart from the stark landscape of Ethiopia for the mountains, crater lakes, waterfalls and lush rainforests of Uganda. Arrive at your destination, Bwindi Impenetrable Forest, one of Africa's most ancient and biologically diverse habitats. Your excursions for the first day include a visit to the Victory Primary School to meet the students, who entertain you with song.

    The major attraction of your stay here is the gorilla tracking adventure, a once-in-lifetime opportunity to meet one of our closest biological relatives up close in its natural habitat. Head off into the jungle with an experienced forest guide, who leads you in your search of a gorilla family. Enjoy this challenging hike, as get the chance to spend an hour observing these magnificent creatures, watching them forage, play and interact. Attend a special lecture by the Head Ranger to learn more about these incredible species.

    You may also choose to visit the village of Buhoma to meet with a medicine man and visit a local school supported by Abercrombie & Kent Philanthropy. The Batwa Experience provides a unique encounter with living history, a chance to interact, hike and indulge in a mock hunt with this renowned African tribe.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Gorilla Forest Camp or Volcanoes Lodge or Mahogany Springs Lodge
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7-9

    Tanzania

    Today, your private jet whisks you to the sweeping, hill-dotted plains of Tanzania’s Serengeti National Park, a true safari country and a World Heritage Site. In Serengeti, expect to witness the “Big Five” (lions, leopards, Cape buffalo, rhinos, and elephants), along with giraffes, gazelles, zebras and much more. Make the best of your visit as you arrive during the Serengeti seasonal migration period, an annual wonder in which hundreds of thousands of wildebeest, gazelles and zebras cross the plains in search of grazing land, closely watched by the animals that prey on them. Settle in your private safari camp set in the serene surroundings.

    Explore the arid, wildly beautiful Serengeti plains for the next three days with extraordinary game drives. Observe the Serengeti’s priceless living bounty in the company of the continent’s best driver-guides. Unwind at the end of the day with a gourmet meal and a complimentary treatment in the massage tent.

    Between game drives, enjoy the varied experiences on offer in the Serengeti including, an unforgettable morning hot-air balloon flight to observe life on the plains, followed by a champagne breakfast in the bush. Later, meet members of the local Maasai tribe, who stage a performance right in your camp. Know more about two of the Serengeti’s top predators, lions and cheetahs, from conservation experts working to preserve these animals.

    Overnight: A&K Mobile Tented Camp
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 10-12

    Zambia

    As you fly to Zambia in your private jet, observe the landscape gradually transforming from dry scrubland into verdant plains carved by flowing waters. After arrival, enjoy a formal lunch, which is a complete feast for the senses, as you overlook the mighty Victoria Falls, and listen to the gentle sounds of an African choir. The Zambezi River flows evenly over a bed of basalt before tumbling down a ravine more than a mile across and 350 feet high, sending a cloud of spray hundreds of feet into the air. Embark on a helicopter to experience unparalleled views and staggering scale of the falls. Proceed to your luxurious tree-house accommodation, built in the living canopy of giant ebony trees lining the riverbank. Later, embark on a sunset safari cruise on the Zambezi, stopping for pre-dinner cocktails and snacks.

    Set off for an authentic neighborhood connection at Nakatindi Village. Receive a warm welcome by the locals and learn how they live. Continue to the Elephant Wallow for a memorable afternoon with these gentle giants. This afternoon, enjoy lunch on the banks of the river, interact with the elephants and experience their intelligence and majesty face to face. Conclude the day, with refreshing sundowner cocktails amid Zambia’s scenic splendor.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Sussi & Chuma
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Days 13-15

    Botswana

    Arrive at your destination Botswana, whose abundant waters mirror the boundless African skies and provide life-giving sustenance to a variety of wildlife. Settle in your exquisite lodge, set above the Chobe River, with sprawling views across the islands. The lounge and dining room connect to spectacular outdoor viewing patios, where you may glimpse Chobe’s renowned elephants: the park is home to a staggering 60,000, one of the largest concentrations in Africa.

    Discover the flora and fauna of this wildlife-rich area during daily game viewing activities. Search for elephants, buffalos, leopards, lions, giraffes, kudus and hippos, all of which you may sight during your guided game drives through Chobe National Park. Return to the lodge, with time at leisure to unwind. Cap every evening with cocktails on a lazy cruise down the Chobe River.

    Please note that in order to provide you with exclusive accommodations at your luxury safari camps, your group will be split up during your visit to Zambia and Botswana, with half of the group visiting Botswana from Days 10-12, followed by Zambia from Days 13-15.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Chobe Chilwero
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 16-17

    Namibia

    This day, arrive in Namibia, which boasts of some of the most remarkable scenery in Africa, exhibiting striking desert landscapes and ocean shoreline. Embark on a private cruise on the picturesque Walvis Bay, keeping an eye out for the rich population of birds, seals and dolphins abundantly found in these waters. Further, visit Sandwich Harbor to observe its resident flamingoes. Then, explore the desert dunes by an off-road vehicle, cresting the 200-foot-high roaring dune for outstanding views of the surrounding landscape.

    Gather for the evening’s sundowner cocktail in the dramatic setting of lunar-like dunes. Tonight’s dinner is set amid a backdrop of high cliffs in the desert, glowing in the candlelight and accompanied by fire spinners and a marimba band. End the night with a stargazing session under the African skies.

    Overnight: Swakopmund Hotel & Entertainment Centre
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 18-19

    South Africa

    In the last leg of your journey discover South Africa, exhibiting a beautiful landscape with modern cities and tended fields nestled alongside mountains and scrubland. After your arrival in Cape Town proceed to iconic Table Mountain, where (weather permitting) you ascend by an aerial cable car to the summit for panoramic views of the sea and city. Further, pause at Signal Hill overlooking some of Cape Town’s stunning coastline for canapés and sundowner drinks followed by a spectacular performance by the Cape Minstrels.

    This evening, conclude your unforgettable African adventures at the gala farewell dinner at Cape Town’s Two Oceans Aquarium. Savor dinner and drinks accompanied by a spectacular pageant of sand tiger sharks, loggerhead turtles, rays and other ocean dwellers — an astonishing capstone to an already extraordinary journey.

    The next morning, you are assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Overnight: Cape Grace
    Meal plan: B,L,D (Day 19: B)

Around the World by Private Jet: Islands, Savannas & the Amazon (2015)

Programme Overview

Come aboard a private jet equipped with fully lie-flat seats for a journey specially designed by Geoffrey Kent to explore some of his favorite destinations. Cruise the Amazon in style, view some of the world's most remote and intriguing islands and conclude with a special breakfast hosted by Geoffrey Kent in his adopted home of Monaco.

  • Duration: 26 days/ 25 nights

    Highlights: Miami | Amazon River | Easter Island | Samoa | Papua New Guinea | Bali & Komodo | Sri Lanka | Madagascar | Kenya | Monaco

    A&K Advantages

    • Come aboard our exclusively chartered Boeing 757-200ER jet and stretch out on a seat that extends into a fully lie-flat bed
    • Enjoy a true feast for the senses with a special dinner on board your luxury Amazon River cruiser, prepared by rising culinary star Pedro Miguel Schiaffino
    • Experience an intimate side of Easter Island with a celebratory feast at the home of a noted islander, accompanied by a party of local dignitaries
    • Indulge in some mid-journey relaxation and pampering in tropical Samoa
    • Learn the ways of a genuine pre-modern civilization at an encounter with Papua New Guinea's Huli tribe, accompanied by local experts
    • Discover Madagascar's rich folkloric tradition when a local storyteller regales you with tales of the island's mythic history
    • Search for wildlife in Kenya's Masai Mara with the company that pioneered the luxury safari, A&K
    • Celebrate the end of your journey in Geoffrey Kent's adopted home of Monaco at an exclusive breakfast with friends of Geoffrey in attendance
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Miami

    Upon your arrival in Miami, you will be welcomed by an A&K representative and assisted with transfer to your accommodations at the Four Seasons.

    Tonight, meet your tour leadership team and fellow guests over cocktails and dinner, sharing the anticipation of the incredible adventures ahead.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Miami
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2-4

    Amazon River

    Begin with your adventures today as you board your private jet to Peru, where you board your twin river cruisers, ‘Aqua’ and ‘Aria,’ for three nights to sail the mighty Amazon. The world’s largest river, the Amazon and its rainforest ecosystem harbor one-third of every species found on earth. Begin to explore with shore excursions alongside your expert naturalist guide, who reveals to you the secrets of the region’s stunning biological diversity, ranging from brilliant-hued tropical birds to rare pink river dolphins. Your voyage includes a special dinner prepared and hosted by Pedro Miguel Schiaffino, one of Latin America’s most innovative chefs, who builds his culinary creations around traditional Amazonian techniques and ingredients.

    Overnight: 'Aqua' / 'Aria'
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5-6

    Easter Island

    Fly to your next destination, one of the most remote — and enticing — destinations on earth: Easter Island. After a travel of nearly 3,000 miles over the Pacific arrive at this grandeur of monolithic moai statues inexplicably gazing out to sea. Historically intriguing, culturally compelling and scenically magical, Easter Island is like nowhere else on earth. Be welcomed with flowers and warm smiles from your Rapa Nui hosts. Savor a sunset cocktail and proceed to attend the Hotumatua ritual, a celebration of the island’s mythic founding.

    The next day, visit the Orongo ceremonial village, site of the island’s “birdman” rituals, and observe a demonstration of Haka Pei, a thrilling form of downhill sledding. Continue to the beach and savor a barbecue. This afternoon, explore the island’s unique heritage. You may choose from a variety of options, including a cave tour; archaeological trek by vehicle or on horseback; or a hike along “the way of the moai,” also a chance to view many of Easter Island’s iconic statues in one excursion.Tonight, celebrate with a special umu (earth-cooked) feast in a Rapa Nui home, attended by local dignitaries.

    Overnight: Explora Rapa Nui
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7-9

    Samoa

    Continue with your Pacific voyage, crossing the International Dateline. Pause in Samoa and unwind with the day at leisure. Samoa is as close to paradise on earth as you will find, with balmy tropical breezes blowing in from an emerald-blue sea fringed by pristine beaches. On arrival, you will be greeted by a colorful local dance troupe, followed by a welcome drink at your resort.

    The next morning, tour the island, including the village of Vailima, last residence of Robert Louis Stevenson. This afternoon, relish a barbecue lunch on the beach and spend the remaining afternoon at leisure or enjoy the resort’s optional activities. You may choose from a variety of water activities in the island’s stunning reefs, a game of tennis or a rejuvenating spa treatment. Conclude your stay in Samoa with a celebratory fia fia, a feast of Polynesian dishes accompanied by dancers and fire-spinners.

    Overnight: Aggie Grey's Lagoon, Beach Resort & Spa
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 10-12

    Papua New Guinea

    Today, fly to your next spectacular destination, Papua New Guinea, an island chain of undisturbed indigenous tribes and sweeping natural beauty. After arrival at the capital city, Port Moresby, you visit the National Museum. Settle in your accommodation for the first night at the Airways Hotel.

    The next day, fly to your lodge in the Southern Highlands or East Sepik Province for an immersion into the lush countryside. Here, you are guided by expert local guides as you walk along the rainforest paths, viewing the area’s stunning flora and fauna. Unexplored till the 1930s, the so-called Huli wigmen are renowned for the elaborate wigs worn by the men, and for the traditional lifestyle they maintain in the face of a rapidly modernizing country. Explore the culture in-depth as you meet the members of this 150,000-strong tribe and witness demonstrations of bilum (string bag) making, umbrella making and archery.

    Later, join your local expert, who gives you insights on Huli social and family customs. Unfold the climax of your visit as you witness an unforgettable spectacle of traditional song and dance, a sing sing performed by the Huli.

    Please note that the travelling party will be split into two groups, enjoying similar activities in either the Tari or Karawari regions of Papua New Guinea.

    Overnight: Serengeti Serena Safari Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 13-15

    Bali & Komodo

    Today, be welcomed in Bali by locals bearing flower leis, a prelude to the next two adventure-packed days. On one day, a charter flight and boat ride takes you to Komodo National Park, home of the Komodo dragon, the largest lizard in the world and the island’s preeminent predator. This afternoon, enjoy lunch on the Kenawa Island beach.

    The next day, visit Tirta Empul Temple, a place of Hindu devotion visited by thousands of pilgrims drawn by the natural spring waters. Observe a traditional Balinese religious ceremony and processional, put on exclusively for you. Later, visit the nearby Presidential Palace and pause for an afternoon refreshment. Capping off a spiritually minded day, a Balinese priest performs a blessing, followed by a peek into the future with a one-on-one consultation with an astrologer.

    Mark your final evening here with a lavish celebration at which you are the honored guests. A party of local villagers leads you to the venue. Arrive in the torch lit room and be welcomed by showering flower petals. Proceed for a sumptuous dinner accompanied by demonstrations of Balinese dance forms, including the thrilling kecak fire-twirling dance.

    Please note that the travelling party will be split into two groups in Bali, visiting Komodo National Park and Tirta Empul Temple on alternate days.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Resort Bali at Jimbaran Bay
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 16-18

    Sri Lanka

    Fly to the island of Sri Lanka, formerly known as Ceylon. Begin your first day with a visit to the Tea Virgins tea factory, where the owner takes you through the process of tea manufacturing and sampling of freshly brewed tea. This afternoon, lunch is served at Kahanda Kanda, a stunning boutique hotel set amid the lush tea plantations overlooking the Koggala Lake. Further, visit Galle Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a former European colonial outpost occupying the southwestern tip of the island.

    The next day, travel by seaplane to the Cultural Triangle and cluster of ancient cities and temples, including the most iconic Buddhist site in Sri Lanka, Sigiriya. Explore Sigiriya led by a local expert, who reveals to you the significance behind its iconography. Later, enjoy shopping with your specially arranged personal shopper, helping you find the best pieces and pieces of Sri Lanka’s famous rich blue sapphires. Tonight, conclude your journey with a celebratory dinner accompanied by renowned local musicians.

    Please note that the travelling party will be split into two groups in Sri Lanka, visiting the tea plantation/Galle Fort and the Cultural Triangle on alternate days.

    Overnight: Amangalla
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 22-24

    Kenya

    Arrive in Nairobi and board your private charter flight to the Masai Mara. After arrival in this outstanding game reserve, you are welcomed by a party of tribal dancers. Initiate your safari adventures with a game drive. As you search for the Africa’s “Big Five” — buffalo, elephants, rhinos, lions and leopards, arrive at exclusive tented camp.

    Spend the next three days observing this incredible living bounty on game drives, a guided bush walk and even an early-morning balloon safari complete with breakfast served in the bush. Retire at the end of each day to your private camp, reliving the day’s adventures over a gourmet dinner.

    You also have the opportunity to visit a Maasai camp, where you meet the nomadic herders, who have acted as stewards of this land for hundreds of years. Visit a local school and observe how A&K Philanthropy is working to make lives better in the Mara. Complete your journey in Kenya with an informal talk from a wildlife conservation expert.

    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 25-26

    Monaco

    Conclude your unforgettable around-the-world journey as your private jet takes you to the final destination, Nice. On your arrival in Nice, gateway to the spectacular Côte d’Azur, a fleet of chartered helicopters takes you to neighboring Monaco, playground of the rich and famous — and Geoffrey Kent’s adopted home.

    The next morning, join your fellow guests for a farewell breakfast accompanied by Geoffrey Kent, who shares reminiscences from his extraordinary lifetime of travel. Later, return to Nice and rejoin your private jet and depart for the U.S. Enjoy one last opportunity to relax and share the treasured memories of your journey with your fellow guests, and bid a fond farewell to your dedicated cabin crew and tour leadership team on arriving in New York.

    Overnight: Hotel Metropole Monte Carlo
    Meal plan: B (L,D in flight)

Wonders of the World by Private Jet with Special Guest Geoffrey Kent

Programme Overview

Experience the world's most iconic destinations - from Machu Picchu and Easter Island to iconic cities Sydney and Istanbul - on a journey aboard a plane equipped with fully lie-flat seats, and travel with A&K founder Geoffrey Kent as you camp in the deserts of Morocco and explore Marrakech.

  • Duration: 24 days/ 23 nights

    Highlights: : Miami | Peru | Easter Island | Samoa | Australia | Cambodia | India | Turkey Morocco

    A&K Advantages

    • Come aboard our exclusively chartered Boeing 757-200ER jet and stretch out on a seat that extends into a fully lie-flat bed
    • Visit the Incan citadel of Machu Picchu, arriving by private luxury train
    • Discover an intimate side of Easter Island with a celebratory feast at the home of a noted islander, accompanied by a party of local dignitaries
    • Indulge in some mid-journey relaxation and pampering in tropical Samoa
    • Experience true-blue Australia with an afternoon on a beautiful private island
    • Visit stunning Angkor Wat and the timeless Taj Mahal
    • Explore Istanbul's best-known sites before they open to the public
    • Camp in a luxurious desert tent in Morocco, hosted by A&K founder Geoffrey Kent
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Miami

    Upon your arrival in Miami, you will be welcomed by an A&K representative and assisted with transfer to your accommodations at the Four Seasons.

    Tonight, meet your tour leadership team and fellow guests over cocktails and dinner, sharing the anticipation of the incredible adventures ahead.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Miami
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2-4

    Peru

    Begin with your adventures today as you board your flight to Peru, seat of the ancient Inca Empire.

    Celebrate your first night with a private tour and dinner at the Larco Herrera Museum, showcasing a remarkable collection of pre-Columbian artifacts.

    The next day, fly to Cusco. Begin your exploration with a guided city tour and experience the cultural fusion of the Spanish and the Incan. This evening, enjoy a private dinner at the Convent of San Francisco, accompanied by a musical performance.

    Set out for your final day here. Begin with a ride on board the luxurious Hiram Bingham train — reserved exclusively for your party — to Machu Picchu, one of the most beautiful and enigmatic ancient sites in the world. Arrive at this mysterious site after the morning crowds have departed. Explore this breathtaking Incan citadel as you stroll through its ghostly terraces and absorb the mesmerizing surrounding views. Further this evening, drink the famous Pisco Sours followed by a private dinner accompanied by entertainment, on the train back to Cusco.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel / Palacio Nazarenas Hotel
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5-6

    Easter Island

    Fly to your next destination, one of the most remote — and enticing — destinations on earth: Easter Island. After a travel of nearly 3,000 miles over the Pacific arrive at this grandeur of monolithic moai statues inexplicably gazing out to sea. Historically intriguing, culturally compelling and scenically magical, Easter Island is like nowhere else on earth. Be welcomed with flowers and warm smiles from your Rapa Nui hosts. Savor a sunset cocktail and proceed to attend the Hotumatua ritual, a celebration of the island’s mythic founding.

    The next day, visit the Orongo ceremonial village, site of the island’s “birdman” rituals, and observe a demonstration of Haka Pei, a thrilling form of downhill sledding. Continue to the beach and savor a barbecue. Later, explore the town of Hangaroa, visit Father Sebastian Englert Museum, where the history of the island is brought to life. It houses the only known moai fragment that retains its original white eyes. Continue to explore the Hanga Roa market, view the locally made craft items and interact with local merchants. Tonight, celebrate with a special umu (earth-cooked) feast in a Rapa Nui home, attended by local dignitaries.

    Overnight: Explora Rapa Nui
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7-9

    Samoa

    Continue with your Pacific voyage, crossing the International Dateline. Pause in Samoa and unwind with the day at leisure.

    Samoa is as close to paradise on earth as you will find, with balmy tropical breezes blowing in from an emerald-blue sea fringed by pristine beaches. On arrival, you will be greeted by a colorful local dance troupe, followed by a welcome drink at your resort.

    The next morning, tour the island, including the village of Vailima, last residence of Robert Louis Stevenson. This afternoon, relish a barbecue lunch on the beach and spend the remaining afternoon at leisure or enjoy the resort’s optional activities. You may choose from a variety of water activities in the island’s stunning reefs, a game of tennis or a rejuvenating spa treatment. Conclude your stay in Samoa with a celebratory fia fia, a feast of Polynesian dishes accompanied by dancers and fire-spinners.

    Overnight: Aggie Grey's Lagoon, Beach Resort & Spa
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 10-12

    Australia

    Journey across the Southern Pacific to Australia. Celebrate your arrival in Sydney and proceed for dinner overlooking the spectacular views of Sydney Harbour.

    The next day, embark on a harbor cruise aboard a privately chartered yacht to arrive at the beautiful Shark Island. This afternoon, experience an authentic Australia at leisure on this private island, reserved exclusively for your group. Here, you may learn the art of didgeridoo playing and boomerang throwing, taste the local wines and cheeses, while listening to authentic Outback songs from a true-blue troubadour.

    On your third day, aboard a chartered helicopter flight taking you over the Greater Blue Mountains to arrive at the Emirates Wolgan Valley Resort and Spa, 4,000- acre wildlife and conservation reserve offering an exemplary Aussie bush experience. You may select from a wide variety of optional activities, including a trail ride on horseback, a painting lesson with a local artist, wine-tasting, cooking class, a guided nature tour to see kangaroos, wallabies and wallaroos or a rejuvenating spa visit. Later, return to Sydney for a private performance and dinner at the renowned Sydney Opera House, overlooking the splendid city skyline.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Sydney
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 13-15

    Cambodia

    This day, board your private jet and journey across the Equator to Siem Reap, Cambodia, seat of the former Khmer Empire. After arrival, you get the opportunity to meet and interact with a local archaeologist and Angkor Wat expert, a prelude for your journey back in time tomorrow. This evening, enjoy welcome dinner at a traditional Khmer house.

    The next day, rise early to break the dawn with a sunrise view of Angkor Wat. Arrive at the site by a tuk-tuk followed by a Champagne breakfast. Explore the place by walking through the moss-covered cobbled pathways and marveling at the intricately carved bas-reliefs. Enjoy the romantic Ta Prohm, entangled in the surrounding jungle foliage. Climb over its crumbling stonework and marvel at the tree roots dripping down the temple's walls. Further, explore the fortified city of Angkor Thom and Bayon Temple, with its beguiling 172 large-scale faces. Pause for a private lunch behind one of the temples.

    Catch a glimpse of the local life with a sunset cocktail cruise on Tonle Sap Lake. Tonight, enjoy an exclusive dinner at your hotel, accompanied by a poolside Cambodian market.

    Begin the next day offering alms at a Buddhist monastery. Visit Banteay Srei (the Pink Temple) and continue to Sam’s Brothers Clean Water Project, an A&K Philanthropy-supported project. This afternoon, enjoy an impressive scenic helicopter flight over the temple site and floating village. Tonight, enjoy your farewell dinner followed by an entertaining shadow puppet show at the elegant Thommeanon Temple.

    Overnight: Raffles Grand Hotel d'Angkor
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 16-17

    India

    Today, fly to your next destination, Agra, India. After arrival, you will be escorted to your hotel, where you are greeted to a festive welcome befitting a maharajah. As you settle in your room, take in the spectacular views of Taj Mahal from your balcony. This evening, gain a new perspective on the famous monument as you attend a performance depicting the story of Shah Jahan, builder of the Taj, and his beloved Mumtaz Mahal, the empress who was entombed within it.

    The next day, visit the Taj Mahal itself. No photograph does the monument justice — it looks as if it was painted onto the sky and is both strikingly simple and graced with fine details, particularly the pietra dura (inlaid stonework) of the cenotaph. Witness for yourself the way its luminous marble skin takes on the pink and orange hues of the sunrise. Further, visit the Agra Fort and Itmad-Ud-Daulah, also referred to as the “Baby Taj.” Spend the afternoon at leisure and return to the Taj Mahal, its marble now tinted gold with the fading daylight. Later, attend an exclusive Mughal-themed dinner at your hotel, accompanied with a performance of Kathak dance, a pantomime-like art with roots back to the nomadic bards of ancient India.

    Overnight: The Oberoi Amarvilás
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 18-20

    Turkey

    Set off to board your private jet for a flight to Istanbul, Turkey. After arrival, begin to experience the city where East meets West and where cultures have collided and connected for centuries. Privately explore (before opening hours) both the dark vaults of the Basilica Cistern and the timeless Hagia Sophia, a former Greek Orthodox patriarchal basilica, later an imperial mosque, and now a museum, mirroring Istanbul’s own journey through history. Continue to explore Istanbul’s history as you visit the Hippodrome, once a location for chariot races. Conclude your day with another exclusive visit to the spectacular Turkish and Islamic Arts Museum.

    The next morning, take a private tour of Topkapi Palace, enjoying the elegant gardens before the crowds arrive. Stop at the Grand Bazaar and experience the contemporary life, continue for a stroll through the Pera District and Galata, the old Jewish Quarter. One evening includes a sunset cruise and dinner at a rooftop restaurant, to enjoy the spectacular cityscape of domes and minarets; the next night, Bid farewell to Istanbul with dinner in a private hall of an Ottoman palace and be entertained with a melodious performance by the Ottoman Court musicians.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Istanbul at Sultanahmet
    Meal plan: B,L,D

  • Day 21-24

    Morocco

    This day, on arrival at Morocco’s Er Rachidia airport, you will be welcomed by the A&K desert team. Set off for your journey across the dunes on a four-wheel-drive vehicle through the city of Rissani and into the desert. Then, embark on a camel ride to travel up the famous Erg Chebbi sand dunes to your private tented camp, where A&K founder Geoffrey Kent is waiting to welcome you. Further, join Geoffrey for cocktails while overlooking a beautiful sunset over the dunes. Enjoy an unforgettable night in the Sahara, complete with a traditional performance by Gnaoua tribesmen, along with stargazing in the gigantic desert skies.

    The next day, fly over the Atlas Mountains to Marrakech in the company of Geoffrey. Arrive in time for lunch, followed by an exclusive demonstration of Arabian horsemanship. Proceed with a drive to Souk Smarine, a maze of stalls bursting with textiles and handcrafts for a shopping extravaganza and a chance to take in the city sights.

    Begin the final day of your unforgettable journey with a visit to the Ben Youssef madrasa and the Bahia Palace, followed by vibrant and lively Djemaa el-Fna square, a kaleidoscope of activity. Continue for an enjoyable carriage ride to the Majorelle Gardens, famous for their vivid cobalt-blue buildings.

    This evening, share the experiences of your unforgettable world-spanning adventure with Geoffrey and fellow guests at A&K’s exclusive lavish Berber feast at Palm Grove camp.

    The next day, board your private jet for Miami.

    Overnight: La Mamounia
    Meal plan: B,L,D(Day 24: B)
