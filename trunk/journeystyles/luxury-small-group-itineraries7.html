Costa Rica: Nature's Riches from Coast to Coast

Programme Overview

Discover Costa Rica's ecological wonders, as you walk above the treetop canopy on suspended bridges, learn coffee growing techniques on a working plantation and ride a zipline through the rainforest

  • Duration: 9 Days/ 8 Nights

    Highlights: San Jose | Monteverde| La Fortuna | Tortuguero

  • A&K advantages

    • See both the Pacific and Caribbean coasts and all the remarkable habitats in between, and travel comfortably between Arenal and Tortuguero with a private air charter
    • Enjoy a Chef's Table culinary experience, learn to prepare a traditional Costa Rican dessert, and then savor the results
    • Gain firsthand knowledge of Costa Rica's ecological wonders and habitats as you explore with your expert naturalist guides, keeping an eye out for caimans, monkeys, river otters, toucans and turtles
    • Explore the creeks and lagoons of Tortuguero National Park on board a motorized boat in search of a wide range of indigenous wildlife

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE San Jose

    Upon your arrival in San José, Costa Rica, you will be met by an A&K representative and assisted with transfer to your hotel

    Spend the remaining day at leisure.

    Overnight: Studio Hotel or similar
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Monteverde

    Kick start your day with a drive to the Central Pacific coast. Embark on a boat excursion,ledby an expert naturalist guide on the Tarcoles River, home to wild crocodiles and the rare scarlet macaw. Observe the mangrove ecosystem and continue to Monteverde, a verdant hamlet in the cloud forest.

    Proceed to settle in your exquisitelodge and enjoy welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Monteverde Lodge & Gardens
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Monteverde

    Explore the cloud forest, spread across 25,000-acre, including theMonteverde Cloud Forest Reserve and the Monteverde Bat Jungle.

    Afterward, meet and interact one of the area’s original Quaker settlers or another local conservationist for insights into the forest’s history.

    Overnight: Monteverde Lodge & Gardens
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    La Fortuna

    Today, onthe first two stops, travellike a local in a four-wheeldrive taxi. View the beautiful coffee plantation of Don Juan Cruz and learn about the traditional coffee-growing techniques as well as the chocolate-making process.

    Taste the fruit of the cacao pod and chocolate in many forms. Enjoy an authentic Costa Rican lunch before you travel along picturesque gravel roads to Rio Chiquito.

    Cruise across Lake Arenal to arrive at your hotel, surrounded byscenic views of the Arenal Volcano.

    Overnight: ArenalNayara Hotel & Garden
    Meal plan: B,L, D

  • Day 5

    La Fortuna

    Explore the Arenal region walking through its numerous fixed and hanging bridges positioned at or above the tree line.

    On ground level, walk along beautiful trails and, weather permitting, enjoy views of Arenal Volcano. Visit the Hidalgo family’s hot springs for a swim or a soak. Dine this evening in traditional Costa Rican style.

    Overnight: ArenalNayara Hotel & Garden
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 6

    Tortuguero

    This morning, you travel to Costa Rica’s Caribbean coast toexperience a true paradise at Tortuguero.Continue through its narrow, tranquil creeks by boat in search of the abundant wildlife. Visit Tortuguero National Park, home to monkeys, three-toed sloths, river otters, caimans, lizards and the strawberry poison dart frog. In the afternoon, explore via a boat excursion.

    Overnight: Tortuga Lodgeor similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Tortuguero

    Begin your day early for a guided birding walk to sight a multitude of avian species. Continue to journey through the slow-moving tropical rivers that define Tortuguero National Park, an absolute wildlife haven. En-route view a spectacular array of wildlife in the thick flora, perhaps monkeys, sloths, river otters, caimans, iguanas or poison dart frogs

    Enjoy your lunch at leisure followed by traditional Costa Rican dessert, prepared before you by a local chef.

    Proceed for an authentic neighborhood connection at theTortuguero village. Interact with the locals and get a glimpse of how they lead their lives. Continue to the Sea Turtle Conservation Museum and know about the Atlantic green sea turtles that thrive here.

    This evening, enjoy a farewell dinner with your fellow travelers.

    Overnight: Tortuga Lodgeor similar
    Meal plan: B, L,D

  • Day 8

    San Jose

    Begin early today for a boat ride to Caño Blanco, across lushBraulio Carrillo National Park.

    Stop at the Atlantic Forest Zipline Adventure to fly across the wonderfulMolinete River enjoying a 360-degree bird’s-eye view of the beautiful surrounding canopy. Enjoy yourlunch and proceed to San Jose.

    Overnight: Studio Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 9

    Depart San José

    Today, you are assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B,

Peru: Machu Picchu & the Sacred Valley

Programme Overview

This fascinating journey through Peru, from Lima to Cusco, takes you to the Sacred Valley and Machu Picchu, where you stay at the only hotel on site. You also visit massive, mysterious Sacsayhuaman.

  • Duration: 9 Days/ 8 Nights

    Highlights: Anchorage | Talkeetna

  • A&K Advantages

    • Explore Machu Picchu in the company of an expert with an overnight stay in the only hotel on the site
    • Gain a deeper understanding of Peru's fascinating and rich pre-Columbian cultures as you explore the Larco Herrera Museum
    • Enjoy a Chef's Table experience in the preparation of Peruvian cuisine and savor the mouth-watering results
    • Ride Like a Local through the stunning Peruvian countryside aboard a glass-topped Vistadome train
    • Absorb Peru's rich musical traditions during a private performance by an expert in Andean music

  • Day 1

    Arrive Lima

    Arrive in Lima this evening, where an A&K representative will welcome you to the capital of Peru and assist with transfer to the hotel.The “City of Kings”, Lima, is a mélange of colonial structures, pre-Columbian sites and modern conveniences.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Lima

    Begin your exploration with a city tour;visit the Plaza Mayor, which is the birthplace of the city of Lima. View the historic Cathedral to appreciate its architectural intricacies

    Journey into the past, as you explore Casa Aliaga, an incredible relic of colonial splendor and Lima’s oldest colonial mansion. Observe the grace of the wood and marble staircase leading up to a fine entrance patio. Appreciate its beautiful rooms, the family chapel, the Golden Salon, with incredible golden mirrors and a beautiful upper gallery, which is home to some important paintings from the Cusco School.

    After the historic exploration, experience the modern Lima.Tour the fascinating Larco Herrera Museum to view an extensive private collection of pre-Inca ceramics, one of the largest collections of its kind in the world!

    This evening, discover the extraordinary archaeological site ofHuacaPucllana. Later, enjoy a welcome dinner comprising of the contemporary Peruvian cuisine at the on-site restaurant.

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 3

    Sacred Valley

    This morning, after breakfast fly to Cusco, an Andean city located at over 11,000 feet above sea level. After arrival, continue with a drive to the sacred valley of the Incas,the vast green valley that sprawls out far below Machu Picchu, and where an ancient way of life lives on.

    En route, you get an opportunity to visit Awanakancha, a center where South American camelids, such as llamas and alpacas, are bred. Here, learn about the popular fibers created from their coats and the ancient weaving techniques. Spend the remaining day acclimatizing to the altitude.

    This evening, embark on a Chef’s Table culinary excursion as you join the chef in the kitchen of Wayra Restaurant. He takes you through the step-by-step preparation of a variety of Peruvian specialties, such as quinoa soup and lomosaltado de alpaca.Later,relish the mouthwatering results.

    Overnight: Sol Y Luna Lodge & Spa
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Sacred Valley

    Tour the Sacred Valley of the Incas,beginning with a visit to the imposing fortress of Ollantaytambo, a formidable stone structure of massive terraces that hug the mountain slopes. Imagine the scene as your guide paints a vivid picture of the Spanish and Incas who fought here, the site of the Incas’ greatest victory over their invaders.

    This afternoon, attend a private lecture and interactive session with a dedicated expert on the Inca ruins. Delve deeper into the rich local culture when you visit the Kuychi Center, to visit, play a game and share a barbecue lunch with the underprivileged children at Niños del Arco Iris (Children of the Rainbow), a project supported by A&K Philanthropy

    Overnight: Sol Y Luna Lodge & Spa
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Machu Picchu

    Travel aboard Vistadome train into the Andes towards Machu Picchu, viewingthe spectacular Peruvian countryside through the train’s glass dome. After arrival, enjoy anexciting overnight stay at the only hotel at Machu Picchu from where you can view the breathtaking Incan city from sunrise to sunset

    Continue for a guided tour of the ruins,followed by time to self-explore. You may opt to see the Temple of the Sun or Intihuatana, a ritual stone that may have been a kind of astronomical clock or calendar. As you wander, sense the majesty of the age-old stone walls and picture life as it might have been for the leaders, warriors, priests and craftsman who once inhabited this seemingly uninhabitable Incan citadel about 8,500 feet above sea level

    In the evening, unwind in your luxurious accommodations amidst the serene environment

    Overnight: Machu Picchu Sanctuary Lodge
    Meal plan: B, L,D

  • Day 6

    Cusco

    Awaken to sunrise and take a stroll through Machu Picchu to witness the beautiful sunrise view.

    Afterward, you may choose to hike IntiPunku(Sun Gate), the entry point to the ruins from the Inca Trail,ortrek up to Huayna Picchu,the towering mountain behind the actual site of Machu Picchu to experience the stunning panoramic, led by your guide.

    Later, Board the Vistadome train for a travel to Cusco, enjoying astounding views of the mountain scenery in the back drop.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 7

    Cusco

    This day, tour Cusco, visiting the Cathedral on the Plaza de Armas and the Santo Domingo Temple,a Dominican church built on the foundations of the Koricancha, the Incas’ principal religious building.

    Afterward, travel to Sacsayhuaman, one of the most amazing Incan constructions for tourists. Marvel its monumental stones precisely cut and fitted together without mortar.

    Spend the afternoon at leisure to experience the cultural fusion of the Spanish and the Incan by interacting with the locals at the market of San Pedro.

    This evening, enjoy an Andean music presentation, where you learn about the history of a wide array of musical instruments by regional expert Tito Leon. Continue for diner at one of the city’s finest local restaurant, MAP Café.

    Overnight: Hotel Monasterio
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 8

    Lima

    Travel back to Lima followed by an evening at leisure. Perhaps get in some last-minute shopping in a local crafts market or sample some of Peru’s culinary offerings. You may also stroll through the streetsand view the city’s buildings to appreciate its architectural intricacies and mix of colonial and Baroque styles architectural designs

    Overnight: Miraflores Park Hotel

    Meal plan: B

  • Day 9

    Depart Lima

    After breakfast, transfer to the airport for your onward flight or begin the Galápagos Post-Tour Extension.

    Meal plan: B

  • Galapagos Post-Tour Extension

    Begin this inspiring journey of discovery in Guayaquil, Ecuador, enjoying an evening to rest after your inbound flight. The next morning, fly to Baltra in the Galápagos Islands.

    After your arrival, you will be escorted by a naturalist guide to embark on ‘Eclipse’, dazzling with the essence of luxury, for a five-night cruise. The ‘Eclipse’ is a vessel built for adventure, offering a stylish and relaxed atmosphere. ‘Eclipse’ has been awarded the “Smart Voyager” seal of approval by the Rainforest Alliance, certifying it as eco-friendly and socially responsible.

    Explore the Galápagos Islands, haven for some of the world’s most varied wildlife and also called a unique ‘living museum and showcase of evolution’. Here, you may get the opportunity to view some of the unique and unusual animal life – such as the land iguana, the giant tortoise and many types of finch – that inspired Charles Darwin’s theory of evolution by natural selection following his visit in 1835

    Embark on twice-daily excursions to the main sites and islands. Explore landmarks such as Punta Espinoza on Fernandina Island, El Barranco on Genovesa Island, Pinnacle Rock on Bartolome Island and the Charles Darwin Research Station on Santa Cruz Island

    Begin each of your cruise day with an early breakfast, perhaps taken al fresco on ‘Eclipse’s’ spacious deck. Enjoy the morning with a shore excursion along a naturalist guide on a sturdy rubber raft- Panga. View sunbathing sea lions; skittering Sally Lightfoot crabs; tangles of marine iguanas; and amusing blue-footed boobies, as well as penguins, finches, and terns; even a century-old tortoise diligently trudging over the sand.

    Spend the evening at your own pace. Explore the mysteries of the night sky with a fascinating astronomy lecture on deck; peruse the ship’s excellent library; or gather for cocktails in the lounge to cap a day of extraordinary discoveries. Drift asleep in your air-conditioned cabin to the rise and fall of gentle ocean swells, awakening refreshed the next morning ready for a new day of adventure

    Return to Guayaquil for a day at leisure, departing the next day

The Snows of Kilimanjaro

Programme Overview

Ascend soaring Mount Kilimanjaro in comfort with A&K's dedicated team, skirting highland montane and dense rubber tree forests, haunting volcanic lava landscapes and breathtaking canyons en route.

  • Duration: 9days/ 8 nights
    Highlights:

    A&K Advantages

    • Climb in confidence with A&K, the company with a successful Kilimanjaro summit rate of 97% - far above the industry average of 60-70%
    • Trust in A&K's deep experience, which spans more than 50 years in Tanzania and 30 years trekking Mount Kilimanjaro
    • A&K ensures the quality of every trek by directly employing all of our chefs, porters and guides, the last of which are certified Wilderness First Responders
    • Benefit from more time to acclimate - a key component to a successful summit - with three more days on the itinerary than most other companies provide
    • Utilize top-of-the-line camping gear and safety equipment, owned and maintained by A&K
    • Stay in the largest and most comfortable sleeping tents on the mountain, while also enjoying private toilet facilities
    • Porters transport your luggage and equipment; you carry only a daypack
  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    Arrive Arusha

    After your arrival in Arusha, you will be welcomedby an A&K representative and escorted to your lodge.

    Overnight: Lake Duluti Serena Hotelor similar
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Arusha

    Prepare yourself for the upcoming climb as you walk through the spectacular Arusha National Park. This scenic park issituated between the peaks of Mount Kilimanjaro and Mount Meru, a prominent volcano and the second highest peak in Tanzania.

    It is home to a wide range of habitats, from its string of crater lakes populated by waterfowl, to its highland montane forest and imposing summit views.

    The forest harbors a wealth of wildlife, including frolicking black-and-white colobus monkeys and regal bushbucks, sometimes glimpsed in the glades between the ancient cedar trees.

    Tonight, enjoy a welcome dinner with fellow guests.

    Overnight: Lake Duluti Serena Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Mount Kilimanjaro

    Today, drive from Arusha to the Machame village on the slopes of Mount Kilimanjaro to begin your climb.

    As you make your way up through dense rubber tree and giant fig forests,keep an eye out forelephants.

    After a six hour hike, arrive at your camp,poised at an altitude of 10,000 feet,near huts once used for shelter on the mountain

    Overnight: Machame Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Mount Kilimanjaro

    Rise earlytoday;sip a piping-hot cup of coffee or tea and set off to explore the commanding views of Uhuru Peak. Trek out of the forest up to the Hagenia zone, through a savanna of tall grasses and haunting volcanic lava landscapes. After a six-hour hike, possibly the most scenic route to the summit, arrive at the Shira Plateau and your camp. At 12,500 feet, it boasts sweeping views across the Masai Steppe to distant Mount Meru and the Shira Needles.

    Overnight: Shira Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Mount Kilimanjaro

    This day, embark on a six-hour hike; skirt the Shira Plateau toward the Kibo Massif, continuing upward to the pass at Lava Tower (15,000 feet).

    Descend toward Barranco Camp through the immense, waterfall-punctuated Grand Barranco canyon, which is fed by streams from the Umbwe River.

    The Barranco Camp is located at an elevation of 13,000 feet, amid a stunning location, right underneath the great Barranco Wall and with spectacular views all around.

    Overnight: Barranco Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    Mount Kilimanjaro

    Today, hike for about four hours along the Barranco Cliff with no increase in elevation. This allows you time to rest and adjust to the increasingly higher altitudes.

    This afternoon, you may traverse over scree and ridges to thetheKaranga Valley set beneath the icefalls of the Heim, Decken and Kersten glaciers.

    Overnight: Karanga Camp
    Meal plan:B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Mount Kilimanjaro

    Begin the day with another short, four- to five-hour trek;acclimatizeyourself as you near the mountain’s peak.Arrive at the BarafuCamp (15,200 feet), located on a moraine. Appreciate thepanoramic views of the majestic Mawenzi Peak to the right and looming Kibo Peak overhead. The surrounding, scenic alpine desert terrain is largely mineral and features some lichens and moss. Return early this evening and rest as you have an early start tomorrow.

    Overnight: Barafu Camp
    Meal plan:B, L, D

  • Day 8

    Mount Kilimanjaro

    Start early with a hot cup of coffee or tea. Begin with the final ascent to reach Stella Point, at 18,630 feet on the rim of Kibo Crater. Journey through a winding path flanked by the towering Ratzel and Rebmann glaciers.

    View the sun riseas you continue around the rim to Uhuru Peak (19,341 feet), where the air is rarefied. Should all go as planned, you will stand at the highest point in Africa in about seven hours’ time. Celebrate your achievement and later, return back to your last campsite (10,200 feet). Trek for about 13 hours today

    Overnight: Mweka Camp
    Meal plan:B, L, D

  • Day 9

    Depart Arusha

    Today, travel back to the base of the mountain, where your A&K driver greets and transports you back to Arusha.

    This afternoon, enjoy the farewell lunch as you bask in your accomplishments with fellow guests. Relax in your dayrooms until your transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Overnight: Lake Duluti Serena Hotel (dayroom)
    Meal plan:B, L

    • Tanzania Safari Post-Tour Extension


    Available on all departures

  • Day 1

    Ngorongoro Crater

    Begin the day with a drive to Ngorongoro Crater,a UNESCO World Heritage site, an extinct volcano and the largest caldera in the world. The crater today is a bucolic sanctuary supporting a wide array of wildlife including lions, giraffes and the elusive black rhino.

    Proceed to settle in your lodge perched on the rim of the caldera.

    Overnight: Ngorongoro Serena Safari Lodge
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    Ngorongoro Crater

    Today, descend into the crater to discover a sanctuary for lions, hyenas, elephants, wildebeests and endangered black rhinos.

    Later, visit a Maasai village to interact with a village elder and knowin-depth the village culture

    Overnight: Ngorongoro Serena Safari Lodge
    Meal plan:B, L, D

  • Day 3-4

    Serengeti National Park

    Drive to Serengeti National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.During the journey take a tour of the Olduvai Gorge, home to many significant archaeological discoveries.

    Proceed to settle in your comfortable lodge, set high on a hill with stunning views of the plains and arranged in series of rondavels (round huts).

    Spend in the remaining day and the next day toexplore the aridwith game drives through the rambling park, site of the Great Migration. Here, you may witness wildebeests and zebras in abundance, searching for fresh grass during the migration periods.

    Overnight: Serengeti Serena Safari Lodge
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 5

    This day, depart for Arusha and proceed to relax in your dayroom before boarding your onward flight.

    Overnight: Lake Duluti Serena Hotel
    Meal plan: B

NEW ZEALAND & AUSTRALIA

Programme Overview

Explore two inspiring destinations in a single journey, New Zealand and Australia, an experience galore.

  • Duration: 18Days /17Nights

    Highlights: New Zealand | Australia

    A&K Advantages

    • Wine tasting in Queenstown
    • Search for shooting stars in the dark southern night sky near Mount Cook, New Zealand's scenic "Southern Alps"
    • A Chef's Table experience overlooking the Daintree Rainforest
    • A full day visit on a catamaran to experience the wonders of Australia's Great Barrier Reef
    • An exclusive afternoon tea experience at the Botanical Ark, home to more than 3,000 tropical plants
    • Day trip toPhillip Island

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE AUCKLAND, NEW ZEALAND

    On arrival in Auckland you will be welcomed by an A&K Representative who will escort you to your accommodation

    This evening awaits a scrumptious welcome local dinner.

    Overnight: Sofitel Auckland Viaduct Harbour
    Meal plan: D

  • Day 2

    QUEENSTOWN

    Start this morning with a scenic drive along Auckland’s sparkling WaitemataHarbour Waterfront. Visit the Auckland Museum to view its world-renowned collection of Polynesian artifacts.

    This evening, you will be assisted with your flight to Queenstown, a city founded by Maori jade hunters and treasured today for its magnificent scenery.

    Overnight: Sofitel Auckland Viaduct Harbour
    Meal plan: B,D

  • Day 3

    QUEENSTOWN

    This day, begin early with an idiosyncratic tour of Queenstown aboard a custom-built four-wheel-drive. View the scenic Skippers Road, Coronet Lookout and Lighthouse Rock. Continue to Arrowtown to visit the site of the Lord of the Rings movie location. Enroute stop for some delicious refreshments and witness the gold mining demonstration.

    In the afternoon, proceed to theWaituri Creek Winery for some toothsome lunch along with a tasting of their fine vintages.

    In the evening,while returning to Queenstown, take a brief stop en route at the bridge made famous by Bungee Jumpers where you may enjoy the magnificent landscape view from a skyline gondola

    Overnight: Sofitel Auckland Viaduct Harbour
    Meal plan: B, L,

  • Day 4

    QUEENSTOWN

    Thismorning is at leisure.

    Laterin the afternoon, experience some of New Zealand’s rural life on a visit to Walter Peak High Country Farm, located beside the picturesque Lake Wakatipu. Cross through the lake on a boat to the farm for a walking tour with the farm’s owner with an opportunity to go up close to the livestock.

    Enjoya gourmet BBQ dinner before returning to your boat.

    Overnight: Sofitel Auckland Viaduct Harbour
    Meal plan: B,D

  • Day 5

    MOUNT COOK

    This morning, embark on a journey to the amazing Aoraki/Mount Cook National Park, home to New Zealand’s highest mountain, for a breathtaking hike. Later, feast onatraditional New Zealand Pie.

    In the evening, you will take a tour of the Milky Way for a stargazing experience at Theatre Planetarium that brings to light unique features of the southern sky.

    Overnight: TheHermitage
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    CHRISTCHURCH

    Ona scenic drive past Lake Pukaki, head towards Christchurch. En-route, visit the solitary Church of the Good Shepherd and appreciate the sights of the spectacular Southern Alps as you continue through the Canterbury Plains.

    You will be assisted with check in at your hotel in the center of this vibrant, ever-changing city.

    Enjoy this evening at your pace.

    Overnight: The George
    Meal plan: B,D

  • Day 7

    CHRISTCHURCH

    After a scrumptious breakfast, proceed for a guided city tour to Christchurch. Begin your drive along the scenic River Avon and the beautiful Botanical Gardensoffering spectacular views. You may enjoy a leisurely punt on the Avon River before returning to your hotel for an afternoon at leisure.

    In the evening, you gather for a New Zealander farewell dinner at the Curator’s House, located in the beautiful Christchurch Botanic Gardens, a perfect sundowner.

    Overnight: The George
    Meal plan: B,D

  • Day 8

    MELBOURNE, AUSTRALIA

    After breakfast, you will proceed for a breathtaking audiovisual presentation at the International Antarctic Center. Later, you will be assisted for your flight to Melbourne, where you will be received by an A&K representative and transferred to your hotel. Your hotel is located at the prestigious “Paris End” of Collins Street, with sweeping views of Melbourne’s skyline.

    In the evening, enjoy a delightful Australian welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Sofitel Melbourne
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 9

    MELBOURNE

    This day, take a tour of Melbourne’s landmarks as you walk through the Royal Botanic Gardens up to the Shrine of Remembrance. Visit Federation Square and take a stroll on the boulevard to the Arts Precinct.

    Enjoy alocal lunch at a restaurant before you continue to the Block Arcade where yousight glass covered shops. Later ride like alocal on one of the city’s famed trams to visit the iconic Queen Victoria Market, which has been the heart and soul of Melbourne for over a century.

    Visit Phillip Island and spot the little penguins swimming ashore and koalas dozing in the sun.

    In the evening, enjoy an exotic seafood dinner before returning to Melbourne.

    Overnight: Sofitel Melbourne
    Meal plan: B, L, D

    • Day 10

    ULURU

    Fly via Alice Springs to Uluru, which was formerly known as Ayers Rock. You will be met and assisted with transfer to your luxurious hotel.
