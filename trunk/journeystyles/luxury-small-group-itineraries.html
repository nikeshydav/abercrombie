Ancient Wonders: Jordan & Israel

Programme Overview

Explore Petra; traverse Wadi Rum; bathe in the buoyant Dead Sea; cruise the Sea of Galilee in a traditional boat; and explore ancient and modern Israel, from Jerusalem to Tel Aviv.

  • Duration: 12 Days / 11 Nights

    Highlights: Amman | Jerash | Petra | Wadi Rum | Masada | Jerusalem | Nazareth | Tel Aviv

  • A&K advantages

    • Explore Petra, the city "half as old as time," and take a four-wheel-drive excursion through Jordan's Wadi Rum, a desert of otherworldly proportions
    • Ride Like a Local when you cruise the Sea of Galilee in a boat reminiscent of those used during the time of Jesus
    • Prepare an evening meal at the Chef's Table, working alongside local women under the supervision of an experienced chef
    • Enjoy expedited customs and immigration formalities with VIP service at Ben Gurion Airport and the Allenby Bridge

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE AMMAN

    On arrival you will be greeted by an A&K Representative, who will assist with your transfer to the hotel.

    Spend the remaining day at leisure.

    Overnight: InterContinental Amman Hotel
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    JERASH

    Travel to Jerash, the 3rd-century “Pompeii of the Middle East,” with its well-preserved Roman ruins.

    Walk along the Roman Cardo, observing its chariot wheel grooves. Further, discover Amman’s Citadel, Great Temple of Amman and the Roman Theater.

    Later this evening, gather for a special welcome dinner.

    Overnight: InterContinental Amman Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    PETRA

    This morning, journey along the scenic broad avenue to Mount Nebo, One of the most revered holy sites of Jordan.

    Continue to Madaba — the beautiful “City of Mosaics, one of the oldest preserved mosaic map, composed of two million pieces of colored stone, a serving guide to Jerusalem’s layout after its destruction and rebuilding in AD 70.

    Take a dip in the mineralrich waters of the Dead Sea.

    Overnight: Movenpick Petra
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    PETRA

    Today, discover the mysteriously charming city of Petra. Admire the rock-carved rose-red city with rock-cut structures sculpted from mountains by the Nabataeans.

    Walk through the siq to the majestic Treasury. Continue to visit the temple of the Winged Lions, where you observe the sphinxes belonging to the first-century. Continue to the Palace Tomb, an exemplary of the craftsmanship of rock caved buildings.

    Explore the local gastronomy with an exclusive Chef’s Table experience, in which you cook an authentic evening meal with a local chef.

    Overnight: Movenpick Petra
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 5

    WADI RUM

    Travel to Wadi Rum, the Valley of the Moon, offering an astounding desert scenery view. Admire the views of rose-tinted mountains and desert vistas.

    Overnight: InterContinental Amman Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 6

    MASADA

    Today, travel across the Allenby Bridge into Israel. Reduce the travel time with special VIP transfers. Arrive in Masada, where Jewish Zealots made their last stand against the Romans.

    Then, ride a cable car to reach the last stronghold of the Jewish Zealots; here, Israelite fighters held out for three years against the invading Roman army before taking their own lives.

    Later, stop at Qumran Caves, where the Dead Sea Scrolls were found in 1947. Continue to Jerusalem.

    Overnight: The King David
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    JERUSALEM

    Begin with the exploration of the fascinating, history-rich Old City of Jerusalem, built by King David in 1004 BC. Visit the Garden of Gethsemane where, according to the Bible, Jesus prayed with his disciples before his crucifixion. Explore the Temple Mount area (Al Haram el-Sharif) with its Dome of the Rock, El Aksa Mosque and the Western Wall

    In the Christian Quarter, stroll the Via Dolorosa, where Jesus carried the cross. Tracing the path of the Cross through bazaars of the Old City, arrive at the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, reputed site of Jesus’ crucifixion and resurrection.

    Overnight: The King David
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 8

    JERUSALEM

    Visit Yad Vashem, a Holocaust memorial and institution for Shoah research. Afterwards, travel to the Israel Museum, which houses a selection of the Dead Sea Scrolls.

    Later, drive to Bethlehem and the Church of the Nativity, the birthplace of Jesus.

    Overnight: The King David
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 9

    NAZARETH

    Today, drive to the old seaport of Acre. Enjoy lunch in Sephoris (or Tsipori) before you continue to Nazareth, where Jesus spent much of his life.

    Proceed to Tiberias and settle into your hotel.

    Overnight: Scots Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 10

    NAZARETH

    Begin the day with a private boat ride on the Sea of Galilee. En-route stop at Capernaum, view St. Peter’s house and the synagogue where Jesus taught. Further, drive to Golan Heights for panoramic views of the Sea of Galilee, continuing to Banias.

    Tonight, bid farewell with a special gala dinner.

    Overnight: Scots Hotel
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 11

    TEL AVIV

    Travel to Caesarea, from where St. Paul was taken to Rome as a prisoner.

    Further, explore Tel Aviv, and pause at the winding, colorful artist colony of Jaffa.

    Overnight: DAN TEL AVIV HOTEL
    Meal plan: B, L

  • Day 12

    DEPART TEL AVIV

    This day, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal plan: B

Kenya & Tanzania Wildlife Safari

Programme Overview

Witness the abundant wildlife of Kenya and Tanzania on game drives through East Africa's greatest parks and reserves, pausing to visit a Maasai village and learn of mankind's origins at Olduvai Gorge.

  • Duration: 12 days/11 nights

    Highlights: Nairobi Amboseli National Park | Lake Manyara National Park| Ngorongoro Crater| Serengeti National Park| Masai Mara Game Reserve

  • A&K Advantages

    • Experience the best of Kenya and Tanzania with 10 days in the region's best national parks and game reserves, while staying at ideally located lodges with stunning views, and an exquisite permanent tented camp in the Masai Mara
    • Embark on daily game drives with A&K's expert driver-guides in exclusive custom-built safari vehicles
    • Visit a Maasai village to learn about the customs and traditions of these nomadic herders
    • Make your stay more meaningful by visiting a local school and interacting with the children
    • Explore Olduvai Gorge on a private visit, where the Leakey family discovered fossils linking man to his distant ancestors
    • Sip sundowner cocktails during a Masai Mara game drive

  • Day 1

    Arrive Nairobi

    Upon arrival in Nairobi, you are met by your A&K representative and assisted with transfer to your hotel.

    Journey this capital city, your gateway to a dramatic procession of natural spectacles and wildlife in Kenya and Tanzania, among the most thrilling, exotic destinations in the world.

    Overnight: Nairobi Serena Hotel or similar
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Amboseli National Park

    Begin your exploration with an overland journey to Kenya’s Amboseli National Park at the foot of snow-capped Mount Kilimanjaro, Africa’s highest mountain and home of the nomadic, vibrantly dressed Maasai tribe.

    Join a safari briefing about the days ahead before you begin to explore the acacia woodland, freshwater swamps and open plains in search of wildlife.

    This evening, join your fellow guests for a welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Amboseli Serena Safari Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Amboseli National Park

    The morning and afternoon game drives today, give you an opportunity to closely observe the lions, elephants, leopards, cheetahs and a prolific bird population.

    View the beautiful mirages formed in the dried lakebed at the reserve. Prepare for predator sightings, as opportunistic animals begin roaming the plains in search of prey.

    Overnight: Amboseli Serena Safari Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Lake Manyara National Park

    Travel by road to Arusha, crossing the border of Tanzania and stopping at the colorful Mto wa Mbu market. Continue to your next lodge, positioned 2,000 feet above the atmospheric Great Rift Valley and freshwater Lake Manyara.

    Your lodge is a gateway to high-adventure sports, such as mountain biking, as well as a tranquil respite for guests who want nothing more than to unwind.

    Overnight: Lake Manyara Serena Safari Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Ngorongoro Crater

    This morning, embark on a game drive in Lake Manyara, a scenic gem, on a lookout for nonchalant baboons, large herds of buffalo and zebra, who gather on the grassy, sun-drenched plains. You may also choose to simply relax by the outdoor pool under African skies

    This afternoon, enjoy your farm fresh lunch and proceed to explore the magnificent Ngorongoro Crater, a UNESCO World Heritage site, an extinct volcano and the largest caldera in the world. The crater today is a bucolic sanctuary supporting a wide array of wildlife including lions, giraffes and the elusive black rhino.

    Further, embark on a culinary excursion in the hotel kitchen with a Chef’s Table demonstration, at which you prepare and sample local dishes. During the rest of the day experience one of the most inspiring outdoor arenas in all of Africa, boasting of its magnificent landscapes.

    Overnight: Talkeetna Alaskan Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6

    Ngorongoro Crater

    Today, continue with your exploration of this vast land of animals, traversing the prairies for elephants while flamingos paint the shimmering water. You may even see the rare black rhino. Following a picnic near a pool where hippos gather

    Afterward, visit a Maasai village for insights on the daily life of the nomadic cattle-herders’. Also get the opportunity to experience their culture and witness a demonstration of traditional bead work.

    Overnight: Ngorongoro Serena Safari Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Serengeti National Park

    Begin to explore the Serengeti National Park, recently proclaimed a 7th world wide wonder. During the journey take a privately guided tour of the Olduvai Gorge, home to many significant archaeological discoveries. En-route view a spectacular mix of savanna grassland and volcanic hills, before dropping steeply downward into the gorge.

    Continue with a visit to the small museum, absorb views from the lookout point and examine the excavations, which revealed 3.7-million-year-old footsteps and the skull of a 1.8 million-year-old man.

    Settle down in your accommodation, which offers commanding views of a true safari country. The park is also the setting of the Great Migration.

    Overnight: Serengeti Serena Safari Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8

    Serengeti National Park

    This day, begin your search for the “Big Five” — lions, leopards, Cape buffalo, elephants and rhinos. Here you may witness wildebeests and zebras in abundance in search of fresh grass during the migration periods.

    Also get the chance to view the lesser-known “Little Five” — including leopard tortoises and rhino beetles — along the rivers, plains and rocky outcroppings.

    Overnight: Serengeti Serena Safari Lodge or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 9

    Masai Mara Game Reserve

    Today, fly to the vast Masai Mara Game Reserve. Arrive at one of the best known and most popular reserves in the whole of Africa, rich in plains game and houses a prolific population of predators.

    The unending landscape hosts some 400-500 bird species, 95 animal species and all of the great predators, including the country’s largest lion population.

    After arrival, settle in your luxurious tented accommodations, ideally situated at the confluence of the Mara’s four game-viewing regions. Later, drive through the famed reserve to experience the wilderness.

    Overnight: Mara Intrepids or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 10-11

    Masai Mara Game Reserve

    The next two days are for you to explore this wonderful region, bestowed with over 500 square miles of savannas, woodlands and rivers. Observe the wildlife as they amble out with wide, purposeful eyes as the sun begins to set.

    Catch sight of crocodiles as they bask in the sun and ever-alert hippos wallow in muddy, cooling water. Enjoy viewing the antics of mischievous monkeys overhead. Take note of the massive African elephants rustling along the forest edge and graceful giraffes snatching leaves from branches high above.

    You may also pay a visit to a local primary school, which is supported by A&K Philanthropy.

    One evening, enjoy the sunset with a Scenic Sundowner experience. Raise a toast with traditional cocktails, while marveling at the incredible atmosphere just before dusk. Celebrate the last night with a special farewell dinner.

    Overnight: Mara Intrepids or similar
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 12

    Depart Nairobi

    Today, you travel back to Nairobi, where you may relax in your dayroom before you transfer to the airport for your flight home.

    Overnight: Nairobi Serena Hotel (dayroom) or similar
    Meal plan: B

  • Mount Kenya Post-Tour Extension

  • Day 1

    Overnight: Fairmont Mount Kenya Safari Club
    Meal plan: L, D

  • Day 2

    Fairmont Mount Kenya Safari Club

    This day, relax and enjoy the hospitality of your luxurious hotel.

    You may also participate in optional activities at an extra cost, such as a horseback riding on the slopes of Mount Kenya, guided forest walks, trout fishing, golf or treatments at the spa.

    Overnight: Fairmont Mount Kenya Safari Club
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 3

    Depart Nairobi

    Today, depart for Nairobi and relax in your dayroom before your onward flight.

    Overnight: Nairobi Serena Hotel (dayroom)
    Meal plan: B

Kenya Safari in Style

Programme Overview

Experience Kenya's finest game viewing while enjoying five-star hospitality in the bush. Privately visiting a Maasai village, elephant orphanage and learning about local cuisine during a cooking demonstration.

  • Duration: 10 days/ 9 nights

    Highlights: Kenya

    A&K Advantages

    • Stay in the heart of wildlife action, sleeping under African skies in luxury, permanent tented camps
    • Embark on private game drives in A&K 4x4 vehicles in Amboseli National Park, Lewa Wildlife Conservancy and the Masai Mara Game Reserve
    • Meet the Maasai people during a private village visit
    • Privately visit Daphne Sheldrick's Orphanage, seeing young elephants lovingly interact with handlers before being reintroduced to the wild
    • Set out on a walking safari, or discover wildlife up close on horseback or camelback (optional) at the private Lewa Wildlife Conservancy • Partake in a Chef's Table experience, during which your camp chef prepares a local dish using just-plucked, organic herbs and regional spices
    • Add meaning to your stay by participating in AKP projects, such as tree planting or a wetlands initiative

  • Day 1

    Arrive Nairobi

    Upon arrival in Nairobi, you are met by an A&K representative and assisted with transfer to your luxurious boutique hotel.

    Overnight: Hemingways Nairobi
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Nairobi

    Begin your journey through the wilds of Kenya. Visit the Karen Blixen Museum, former African home of Danish author Karen Blixen. View many of the author’s original belongings and the bucolic setting where "Out of Africa” was filmed.

    Visit the Giraffe Center, a sanctuary for the rare Rothschilds giraffe. Feed them while perched upon a platform, watching as they gently snatch it from your hand with their long, blue tongues.

    Stop for an Artisan visit at Kazuri, a jewelry workshop created by and for women with the mission of employing disadvantaged members of Kenyan society. Proceed to Daphne Sheldrick’s Orphanage, home to small elephants, who are reintroduced to the Tsavo National Park. Enjoy the views of elephant calves bathing in the mud hole and being bottle-fed by human surrogate mothers.

    This evening, join your fellow guests for a gala welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Hemingways Nairobi
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 3

    Amboseli National Park

    Begin your exploration with an overland journey to Kenya’s Amboseli National Park at the foot of snow-capped Mount Kilimanjaro, Africa’s highest mountain and home of the nomadic, vibrantly dressed Maasai tribe.

    The elephant population is prolific here. Amid acacia woodland, scrub brush, freshwater swamps and open plains, watch for buffalo, hippos and waterfowl.

    Overnight: Tortilis Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Amboseli National Park

    The morning and afternoon game drives today, give you an opportunity to closely observe the lions, elephants, leopards, cheetahs and a prolific bird population.

    Then, from atop the perch of prehistoric Observation Hill, observe the grasslands and mighty mountain transform colors at sunset.

    View the beautiful mirages formed in the dried lakebed at the reserve. Prepare for predator sightings, as opportunistic animals begin roaming the plains in search of prey.

    Overnight: Tortilis Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5-6

    Lewa Wildlife Conservancy

    Today, depart for the Lewa Wildlife Conservancy, a harbor for endangered black rhinos, big cats and elephants located in northern Kenya. After arrival, drive to your tented camp, set amidst the picturesque Northern Frontier District, within a private wildlife conservancy and rhino sanctuary.

    Explore this afternoon and tomorrow, in search of northern species, such as the reticulated giraffe, Somali ostrich and the long-necked gerenuk, which stands on its hind legs to feed. Further, you perhaps explore through exquisite facilities like a walking safari, optional horseback ride or camel ride (additional cost).

    Overnight: Lewa Safari Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 7

    Masai Mara Game Reserve | Abundant Animals

    This day, take a flight to Masai Mara, an outstanding game reserve. After arrival, settle in your award-winning luxury camp. Its 14 elegant tents feature four-poster beds and verandas overlooking a private stretch of the Mara River, where hippos wallow.

    In the late afternoon, embark on a game drive in search of Africa’s “Big Five” — buffalo, elephants, rhinos, lions and leopards. You may also view A&K Philanthropy supported camp’s wetland project or plant your own tree at a native tree nursery.

    Later, return to camp for dinner.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Olonana
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 8-9

    Masai Mara Game Reserve

    For the next two days, explore the vast plains of Masai Mara, 583 square miles of rambling, acacia-dotted savanna, woodlands and tree-fringed rivers with game drives. Masai Mara showcases a large diversity of ecosystem, including a vast mammal population, lions, elephants, giraffes, a variety of gazelle species and zebras. Cheetahs, rhinos and leopards are also sighted often.

    Experience the local culture and traditions in-depth as you may privately visit a Maasai village and interact with the locals to learn about local customs and daily life. One special sunset on the Mara is celebrated with a Scenic Sundowner.

    Conclude your journey with a farewell dinner accompanied by the fellow travelers.

    Overnight: Sanctuary Olonana
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 10

    Depart Nairobi

    Today, travel back to Nairobi. After arrival, you will be escorted to your hotel for the day.

    Later, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Overnight: Fairmont The Norfolk (dayroom)
    Meal plan: B

Kenya Wildlife Safari

Programme Overview

Witness Kenya's denizens - endangered black rhinos, lions, giraffes and leopards - as well as visit an elephant orphanage and chimpanzee sanctuary and meet the nomadic Samburu tribe.

  • Duration: 9 Days/ 8 Nights

    Highlights: Nairobi | Nanyuki | Samburu Game Reserve | Masai Mara Game Reserve

    A&K Advantages

    • Visit Daphne Sheldrick's Orphanage, where young elephants interact with handlers before returning to the wild
    • Visit Kenya's only chimpanzee sanctuary and one of the largest rhino sanctuaries in East Africa
    • Acquaint yourself with the local Samburu people, who share their knowledge of fire-starting, spear-throwing and animal tracking, as well as beadwork and leatherwork
    • Attend a Chef's Table experience and cooking demonstration of Swahili-influenced dishes

  • Day 1

    Arrive Nairobi

    Upon arrival in Nairobi, you are met by your A&K representative and assisted with transfer to your elegant hotel, an oasis in the heart of this vibrant city.

    Overnight: Nairobi Serena Hotel
    Meal plan: None

  • Day 2

    Nairobi

    Begin your journey through the wilds of Kenya. Visit the Giraffe Center, a sanctuary for the rare Rothschilds giraffe. Feed them while perched upon a platform, watching as they gently snatch it from your hand with their long, blue tongues.

    Proceed to Daphne Sheldrick’s Orphanage, home to small elephants, who are reintroduced to the Tsavo National Park. Enjoy the views of elephant calves bathing in the mud hole and being bottle-fed by human surrogate mothers. Stop for an Artisan visit at Kazuri, a jewelry workshop created by and for women with the mission of employing disadvantaged members of Kenyan society.

    This evening, join your fellow guests for a gala welcome dinner.

    Overnight: Nairobi Serena Hotel
    Meal plan: B, D

  • Day 3

    Nanyuki

    Take a drive to the Sweetwaters Reserve within the Ol Pejeta Conservancy at the foot of Mount Kenya. Begin to explore with a safari game drives, or, at an additional cost, take a guided walking tour or a night game drive.

    You may even view game from your tent’s private veranda, which overlooks a frequented, floodlit waterhole and salt lick. Sweetwaters is an excellent place to see two of Africa’s most endangered species: black rhinos and chimpanzees. The species are provided with appropriate environment for survival through special reserves.

    Overnight: Sweetwaters Serena Camp
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 4

    Samburu Game Reserve

    Begin the day with a tour of the bush of Samburu Game Reserve, where you settle in a comfortable tented camp perched along the banks of the Ewaso Nyiro River. This river is the lifeline of the reserve and animals congregate along its banks.

    Overnight: Samburu Intrepids
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 5

    Samburu Game Reserve

    Start early today for a game drive followed by a hot breakfast and time to relax.

    This afternoon, another game drive reveals a different atmosphere as the reserve’s more nocturnal animals emerge in search of food.

    Arrive at the Samburu village, home to the nomadic Samburu people who live a pastoral, herding life. Here, experience the tribe’s unique culture and traditions.

    Overnight: Samburu Intrepids
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 6-8

    Masai Mara Game Reserve

    This day, take a flight to Masai Mara, an outstanding game reserve. After arrival, settle in your luxury tented camp.

    For the next three days enjoy your stay in the magnificent savanna country. Explore the game reserve with morning and afternoon game drives in search of Africa’s “Big Five” — buffalo, elephants, rhinos, lions and leopards. Moving further, you may embark on an optional hot air balloon safari for a memorable experience.

    Later, visit a primary school supported by A&K Philanthropy, where your family may interact with students when school is in session.

    One evening, toast to a well-timed sunset right on the vast Mara with a classic Sundowner cocktail. Tonight, share in a memorable farewell dinner with your new friends, swapping tales of adventure from across this magnificent country.

    Overnight: Mara Intrepids
    Meal plan: B, L, D

  • Day 9

    Depart Nairobi

    Today, travel back to Nairobi. After arrival, you will be escorted to your hotel for the day.

    Later, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Overnight: Nairobi Serena Hotel (dayroom)
    Meal plan: B
