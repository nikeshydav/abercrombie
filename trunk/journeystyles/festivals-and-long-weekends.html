A NEW YEAR CELEBRATION IN LONDON

  • Duration: 6 Days/ 5 Nights

    Celebrate New Year at this fabulously festivecity, fun-filled, sparkling with fireworks, gourmet delights and amazing night life options.

    Programme Overview

    London | Stonehenge
  • DETAILED ITINERARY

    Day 1

    ARRIVE LONDON

    Upon arrival at London meet your personal Guardian Angel. You will be assisted with private transfer and check-in to your hotel.

    Rest of your day is at leisure.

    Overnight: The Kensington or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    LONDON

    This morning visit Westminster in your private vehicle with your expert local guide who is keen to share his knowledge and local insight with you for a half day tour. Explore the Westminster Abbey and the National Church of England that has been the scene of many state occasions, including the Coronation and more recently the wedding of Prince William. Proceed to Trafalgar Square and laterhead down the historic Whitehall past Downing Street, to the Houses of Parliament. On return to your hotel you pass Buckingham Palace.

    Rest of the day is at leisure.

    This evening you may wish to attend the spectacular fireworks display at the London Eye by the River Thames. You could perhaps enjoy an exceptional dinner at one the exclusive restaurants that offer stunningviews over the Thames and promise a wonderful spot from which to view the fireworks.

    Alternatively, you may view the festivities from a distinctive spot while on a superb dinner cruise as you are treated to gourmet cuisine, entertainment, and of course, lots of champagne!

    Overnight: The Kensington or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    LONDON

    Day is at leisure to embrace some local traditions, watch the fantastic spectacle of the New Year’s Day Parade through the city, or simply relax after the previous night’s rejoicing.

    Overnight: The Kensington or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    BATH AND STONEHENGE

    Depart early this morning with your private driver-guide for a full day excursion across the charming and picturesque English countryside. You will go past the hill of Old Sarum on your way to Salisbury Plain and visit Stonehenge, arguably the most famous prehistoric site in Europe, a spectacular sight of the enormous stones erected in circles.

    Later, explore the beautiful Georgian city of Bath, which displays the Roman Baths that were discovered in an excellent state of preservation near to Bath Abbey church,alongside appreciate magnificent Palladian architecture for which the city is renowned.

    Evening is at leisure.

    Overnight: The Kensington or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    LONDON

    Your driver-guide in a private vehicle will assist you with a half-day excursion to Windsor. Enjoy a tour of Windsor Castle, the largest inhabited castle in the world, and the Queen's favourite residence. Subject to opening restrictions, you may see the State Apartments, St. George's Chapel and Queen Mary's Doll's House. Stunning views of Eton College can be viewed from the castle ramparts. Later visit the charming old town to stroll around some of the boutiques and cafes.

    Afternoon is at leisure to explore at London at your own pace.

    Overnight: The Kensington or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    DEPART LONDON

    You will be assisted with a transfer to the airport by your Guardian Angel for your departure flight.

    Meal Plan: B

    Programme Overview

    Madrid | Toledo
  • DETAILED ITINERARY

    Day 1

    ARRIVE MADRID

    On arrival at Madrid meet your Guardian Angel who will assist you with the transfer and check in to your hotel.

    Day is at leisure or you may explore Madrid at your own pace.

    Overnight: The Ritz Madrid or similar

  • Day 2

    MADRID

    This morning hop onto a bike for a ride to the historic centre of Madrid known as the "Madrid de los Austrias" because it was built in the era of the Habsburg monarchs. Cycling through the narrow streets and squares is the perfect way to discover the city’s charm. Visit the arcaded Plaza Mayor which in the 17th century was once famedfor bullfights and the burning of heretics at the stake; today it is the scene of the delightful Christmas Market where Madrileños go to buy the figures to fill their traditonal "Belenes" the Nativity scenes which decorate every traditional home. Many churches in the area have beautiful Belenesall decorated and set which you can stop to see, perhaps before enjoying some delicious hot chocolate and crisp "churros" in the marvelous Chocolatería de San Gines.

    Afternoon is at leisure.

    Overnight: The Ritz Madrid or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    MADRID–TOLEDO–MADRID

    This morning departin your private vehicle and English-speaking guide to Toledo, a fortified city dating from Roman times, when it was the capital of Visigothic Spain. The Cathedral, a blend of 13th, 14th and 15th century architecture, the Church of Santo Tomé, housing El Greco's most famous painting 'Burial of Count Orgaz' and the city's oldest and largest synagogue - the 12th century Santa Maria la Blanca Synagogue are the center of attraction.

    Alternately, we recommend a cooking class comfortably in the home of an expert local chef. The menu prepared and then, of course eaten, is chosen by you from a selection of many kinds of traditional Spanish dishes - from paella to tapas, or perhaps something typical of the festive season.

    Overnight: The Ritz Madrid or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    MADRID

    This morning, visit the Prado with an expert local guide. The Prado Museum is one of Madrid's main attractions - here you can inspect works by Spain's three great masters: Goya, Velazquez and El Greco amongst many other famous works.

    Rest of the day is at leisure.

    Overnight: The Ritz Madrid or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    MADRID

    Madrid awaits you to begin this day in your own way as the city slowly comes to life. We strongly recommend a stroll in the beautiful Retiro Park, before making your way

    Rest of the day is at leisure.

    Overnight: The Ritz Madrid or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    DEPART MADRID

    Today you will be transferred to the airport in a private vehicle with an English-speaking driver to board your flight.

    Meal Plan: B

NEW YEAR IN PARIS

  • Duration: 6 Days/ 5 Nights

    Rejoice is one of the most stylish cities of Europe. Parisian delightful celebrations on a week long holiday for the special you.

    Programme Overview

    Paris | Versailles

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE PARIS

    On arrival at Paris, you will be met by a Guardian Angel and will be transferred by a private vehicle to your hotel and assisted with check-in.

    Overnight: Relais Christine or similar

  • Day 2

    PARIS – VERSAILLES – PARIS

    This morning, enjoy a half day tour of some of the most famous and magnificent Parisian sights from the comfort of your own private vehicle. Allow your expert local guide to show you the Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Champs Elysées and the Place de la Concorde, imparting his fascinating knowledge and insight along the way. Complete the tour on the Ile de la Cité, the island on which the medieval city developed, and visit the beautiful Notre Dame Cathedral with its colourful rose windows. Also stop by at the Conciergerie, the prison where Marie Antoinette was held captive during the French Revolution.

    This afternoon along with your guide proceed for a private tour to Versailles. Explore the opulent state apartments of the huge palace that was built to magnify the glory of the great Sun King Louis XIV. The most magnificent of these rooms is the Gallery of Mirrors, from where a panoramic view of the formal gardens that stretch from the palace down to the ornamental canal is available.

    Overnight: Relais Christine or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    PARIS

    This day’s focus is on the Impressionist painters, the visits start with the lesser known, but exceptionally interesting, Musée Marmottan. In this museum you are able to appreciate 60 odd paintings which Claude Monet owned, both the one’s he had painted himself and particularly treasured, and also those that he collected from his friends Renoir, Sisley, Pissarro and others. Your private guide will be able to offer detailed explanations.

    Later this afternoon visit the Musée de l'Orangerie, which is home to Monet's magnificent "Water Lilies" paintings. Travel up to the hill of Montmartre, the Bohemian quarter where many of the Impressionists lived and worked, and an area where artists still gather to this day. The famed landmark of the hill is the Sacré Coeur Basilica, from where you get an excellent view over the city.

    Overnight: Relais Christine or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    PARIS

    This morning you visit the world-renowned Louvre Museum along with your private guide. Originally the Royal Residence of the Kings of France, the sprawling Louvre palace holds one of the most important art collections in the Western world, spanning from remotest antiquity to the mid-19th century, with the Mona Lisa, the Venus de Milo and the glass pyramid by I. M. Pei at the entrance adding a contemporary touch.

    The afternoon is at leisure.

    This evening be treated to a gala event; select from two of the classic Parisian ways to celebrate.

    Enjoy an exceptional dinner cruise on the River Seine, gourmet dinner followed by lively entertainment for an exciting party atmosphere. At midnight, cruise in front of the spectacularly illuminated Eiffel Tower which forms the centre piece of the celebrations.

    Alternatively, you may prefer to attend a legendary Parisian cabaret show. The show offers some of the most spectacular displays of costume and dance on any stage with an exquisite dinner, where special menus and programs are created in order to provide the finest entertainment.

    We recommend the Lido de Paris and the Moulin Rouge as the two most celebrated of all the clubs. Dinner is served accompanied by the orchestra, and then the cabaret show leads you up to midnight in style. Revel in the exciting and colourful atmosphere long into the night.

    Overnight: Relais Christine or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    PARIS

    The day is at leisure for you to explore Paris at your own pace. You may like to visit the most iconic site in the city, the Eiffel Tower. Ascend to the third level that sits at the very top of the structure and be treated to a bird’s eye view of the innumerable buildings of Paris below, a truly special sight.

    Overnight: Relais Christine or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    DEPART PARIS

    You will be assisted with your transfer in a private vehicle to the airport for your departure flight.

    Meal Plan: B

FESTIVE CHEER: VIENNA & SALZBUR

  • Duration: 10 Days/ 9 Nights

    Explore the European Christmas markets all brightly set and cheerful with the festivities in the air.

    Programme Overview

    Vienna | Danube Valley | Schloss Fuschl | Bavarian Alps | Salzburg

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE VIENNA

    On arrival you will be welcomed by your local A&K Guardian Angel who will assist with transfer to your hotel and check-in.

    Day is at leisure.

    The hotel is ideally located in the heart of this fabulous city and you are just a few steps away from the best of Viennese shopping and cultural attractions.

    Overnight: Hotel Sacher Wien or similar
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    VIENNA

    You will be met by your local guide at the reception for exploring the beautiful Christmas Markets of Vienna. Visit the ‘Christkindlmarkt’ to start with, located on the square in front of the Town Hall. It is Vienna’s famed classic market, dating back over 700 years. The ice rink which is built every year for the winter period is its prime attraction. It is a pleasure to ice skate whilst surrounded by Christmas decorations, lights, the lovely smell of mulled wine and freshly baked cookies. The market is surrounded by a park which is full of trees, brightly decorated with lights that are shaped like hearts or gingerbread men.

    Later visit the ‘Spittelberg market’, which unusually winds its way through narrow streets, alleyways and courtyards; quite unlike other Christmas markets. This market is dedicated to an eclectic and extensive collection of arts and craft - from exotic wares from the Far East to locally-produced beeswax candles. Located next to the Fine Arts Museum you may use the chance to visit the museum where you can see many pictures that show scenes of the Nativity.

    Overnight: Hotel Sacher Wien or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    VIENNA

    Schönnbrunn Palace provides the perfect setting for an idealistic and a romantic Christmas market and to date is one of the most important cultural monuments. Take a short tour around the Palace and then return to the magnificent Advent village. You will find crafted Austrian artifacts ranging from wooden Christmas tree decorations, felt, glass and stone, to handmade pottery, tin toys, clay products and hand-carved mangers. Additionally you can savour wines from all over Austria, varied punches and tea specialties. Christmas music is on by national and international choirs, brass bands and groups.

    At some point today you may experience a delicious Apple Strudel demonstration at the Palace. Appreciate the skills of a professional sugar baker as he or she showcases the creation of a world famous Viennese Apple Strudel and lets you into some insider secrets, prior to sampling this delicious delicacy.

    Later in the evening you may attend a concert at one of the local churches, or you may attend a larger concert in one of the concert halls.

    Overnight: Hotel Sacher Wien or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    DANUBE VALLEY & SCHLOSS FUSCHL

    Begin your day with a journey to Schloss Fuschl visiting the beautiful Danube Valley. Enroute visit Krems, with its 14th century former cloister, and the wine town of Dürnstein, arguably the most beautiful of the region’s villages and home to the ruins of a castle fortress. Continue to Melk to visit the Abbey, one of the finest Baroque buildings in the world. From the library take a short walk for a spectacular view of the Danube and Wachau Valley before continuing your journey to Schloss Fuschl.

    When touring by private car with a driver-guide, we use an experienced driver-guide who will give commentary en-route and maybe do some guiding although generally they are not permitted to guide within museums and certain historical sites of interest.

    This fairy-tale chateau is situated on a peninsula in Lake Fuschl at the gateway to the Salzkammergut, just 20 minutes from Salzburg. It was built in 1450 as a hunting lodge and used as the summer residence of the archbishops of Salzburg. In the 1950s the chateau became world-famous, as its romantic situation on the banks of Lake Fuschl provided the ideal setting for the 'Sissi' films with Romy Schneider. There are 110 elegant and stylishly furnished rooms and suites: each of which is its own unique piece of art. There are many activities such as swimming in the lake, mountain biking, Nordic walking and skiing in winter. Fuschl is also a paradise for golfers with the hotel's own 9-hole GC Fuschl next to the hotel and the 18-hole championship course GC Eugendorf. Hotel Schloss Fuschl has the sole fishing rights on the Fuschl lake. The hotel features two restaurants, a bar, vinothek & wine cellar and light refreshments are also served on the hotel's 'Jetty'.

    Overnight: Schloss Fuschl or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    SCHLOSS FUSCHL

    Day is at leisure. You could take a walk around a part of the lake, or you could stay indoors and indulge in a luxurious spa service at the hotel with a stunning lake view.

    Cosy up next to the log fire in the lounge and enjoy a Christmas drink whilst listening to the relaxing melodies being played by the pianist. Or you could follow the lounge outside wrapped up under heated fur covers and enjoy fairy-tale views, all accompanied by the chef’s wonderful hot punch.

    Overnight: Schloss Fuschl or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    BAVARIAN ALPS & SLEIGH RIDE

    At this time of year, the south of Germany turns into a Winter Wonderland. Explore Germany today, starting with the legendary and mythical Untersberg Mountain and to the old border crossing into Germany. As you go alongside the King’s Lake River on your drive through the striking Bavarian alpine valley with panoramic views of its farms and villages. On reaching Bischofswiesen enjoy a sleigh ride through the winter paradise. Cuddle up under warm blankets and appreciate the picturesque scenery. Mulled wine can be savoured during your 1 hour long ride.

    Time permitting you can either visit the beautiful village of Berchtesgaden or drive to the King’s Lake (Königssee) located among the steep and imposing mountains of the Bavarian Alps for a stroll.

    Overnight: Schloss Fuschl or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    SCHLOSS FUSCHL & SALZBURG

    Meet your driver and private vehicle for your transfer to Salzburg.

    In the baroque centre of Salzburg, City of Mozart, in the famous Getreidegasse, stands the Hotel Goldener Hirsch, retaining the character of an elegant private home.

    Day is at leisure.

    Overnight: Hotel Goldener Hirsch or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    SALZBURG

    Discover the most exemplary sights in this 'Mozart City' such as the Getreidegasse with Mozart's Birthplace, the Pferdeschwemme (Royal Horse Trough), St Peter's Abbey and cemetery. Take a ride up the funicular railway to visit the Hohensalzburg Fortress, the largest, fully preserved fortress in central Europe. The lavish staterooms such as the Golden Room provide marvelous views of Salzburg and the surrounding countryside.

    Visit the Mirabell Palace and Gardens, best known for being part of the background in 'The Sound of Music'.

    Overnight: Hotel Goldener Hirsch or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    SALZBURG

    Day is at leisure in Salzburg.

    Overnight: Hotel Goldener Hirsch or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    DEPART SALZBURG

    Your driver will privately transfer you to the airport for your departure flight.

    Meal Plan: B

GINGERBREAD AND GLÜHWEIN: WARMING WINTER DELIGHTS IN BELGIUM

  • Duration: 4 Days/3 Nights

    A short escape to some of Belgium's best Christmas markets as well as the highlights of Brussels, Bruges and Ghent.

    Programme Overview

    Brussels Old Town | Grand Place Christmas Market | Ghent | Bruges

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE BRUSSELS

    On arrival you will be met by your Guardian Angel and transferred by a private vehicle to your hotel in Brussels, where you will be assisted with check-in.

    Overnight: Hotel Amigo or similar
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    BRUSSELS

    Begin your Belgian experience with a walking tour of Brussels’ beautiful old town. Appreciate the wonderful ornate architecture around you as you stand in the middle of Grand Place, the magnificent square in the heart of the old quarter, considered to be one of the most elaborate in Europe.

    Meander through the many wooden chalets that are a part of this fantastic Christmas market at this famous square. Delightful handmade gifts, unusual stocking fillers and authentic food are available in abundance at this market to make this Christmas extra special.

    Next you visit the Brussels' most famous resident, the Manneken Pis, a bronze statue of a small boy watering the fountain. Proceed further afield to Horta House and, finally, to the Atomium, from the top of which you will be rewarded with a stunning panoramic view of Brussels.

    Overnight: Hotel Amigo or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    GHENT & BRUGES

    A full day excursion is scheduled to the picturesque cities of Ghent and Bruges with an English-speaking driver-guide. Ghent is the principal seat of the county of Flanders and its medieval quarters and its history is marked by splendid castles. Visit one of the greatest masterpieces of Flemish painting: the Adoration of the Mystic Lamb by Jan van Eyck.

    Later continue to Bruges, a beautiful medieval city of canals which can be much appreciated as you go along the most attractive waterways and streets towards the Groeninge Museum. This splendid city is often referred to as the "Venice of the North”. Explore another of Belgium’s great Christmas markets. Bruges has two markets both show casing handmade gifts, clothing, food, drink and other knick knacks. We definitely recommend savouring a fresh hot waffle and Glühwein as you stroll around the stalls – it’s a tradition that’s well worth following!

    Overnight: Hotel Amigo or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    DEPART BRUSSELS

    Today you will be assisted with transfers to the airport for your departure flight.

    Meal Plan: B

THREE WINTER WONDERLANDS: BERLIN, PRAGUE & MUNICH

  • Duration: 10 Days/ 9 Nights

    A festive journey across three amazingly beautiful cities, those are renowned for their spectacular markets and all year round appeal.

    Programme Overview

    Berlin | Prague | Munich

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    BERLIN

    On arrival at Berlin you will be welcomed by your local representative, and transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with your check-in.

    Overnight: Hotel de Rome or similar
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    BERLIN

    Start your day this morning with a half day overview tour of Berlin in the comfort of your private vehicle. Visit the recently created Potsdamer Platz, dubbed 'Europe's largest construction site' during the mid 1990s and now a bustling square. The Square comes alive at this time of year with lights, music, handicraft, food stalls and a festive ice rink to get you in the magical festive spirit.

    Proceed to Checkpoint Charlie, the famed junction between East and West Berlin and the impressive headquarters of the DZ Bank, located on Pariser Platz 3 close to the Brandenburg Gate. The interior courtyard is home to Gehry's towering, curvaceous fish that houses a central conference space and captures motion and movement in the building.

    Fascinating facts about Berlin's turbulent past will be shared with you and you'll hopefully get a real feel of the city. Stop by at Kaiser Wilhelm Memorial Church whose ruins are a reminder of World War II. The church is set against the festive backdrop of one of the city's most popular Christmas markets around the elaborately decorated Breitscheidplatz.

    Later this afternoon you may enjoy Afternoon Tea (2.00 pm – 6.00 pm) with a Christmas theme at the Opera Court Lounge back at your hotel (not included and subject to availability and the hotel continuing its yearly theme).

    Evening is at leisure.

    Overnight: Hotel de Rome or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    BERLIN

    This morning is at leisure.

    Later this afternoon you will be taken around the city for an orientation to some more of the amazing Christmas markets this city has to offer. There are in fact more than 50 markets, some of which are magical and traditional and others more trendy and lively - feel free to tell your guide your preferences.

    We would strongly recommend you visit the WeihnachtsZauber on Gendarmenmarkt, where you will be greeted with the smells of roasted almonds, hot cocoa and Glühwein. As well as the market stalls offering traditional handicrafts and culinary temptations, enjoy the entertainment and atmosphere created by the many jugglers, acrobats and musical ensembles in the square.

    Overnight: Hotel de Rome or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    BERLIN - PRAGUE

    You will be privately transferred to Berlin Railway Station for your Concierge Rail journey to Prague. First class seating will be pre-arranged for you and our luggage handlers will look after your bags - allowing you to concentrate on settling into your seats for the journey.

    On arrival in Prague you will be met directly off the train and privately transferred to your hotel. We will assist you with your check-in.

    Later take a walking tour of Prague’s Old Town along with an English speaking guide. A walk on the quaint cobbled streets will take you to the Old Town Square where you will see the Gothic Town Hall and famous astronomical clock. At this time of the year this square is home to a cheerful market with brightly decorated wooden huts selling Czech handicrafts, hot food and warm drinks. The Christmas tree is the most impressive, shipped from the Krkonose (Giant) mountains in the North of the Czech Republic decorated in a blaze of lights. It is a spectacular sight with the presence of carol singers and bands playing festive tunes.

    Overnight: The Augustine Hotel or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    PRAGUE

    Visit the Prague's Castle District. Begin with Hradcany Castle which was the seat of Bohemian kings and Holy Roman emperors. The Cathedral of St. Vitus and the Basilica of St. George which was founded in 905 will be next. Complete your tour with a visit to the Sanctuary of Our Lady of Loreto, an important place of pilgrimage, and the Strahov Monastery, with its glorious Baroque decorations, you will enjoy a private tour of the ornate library.

    The rest of the day is at your leisure. We recommend you sample some home-made Glühwine, hot apple cider, gingerbread, grilled chestnuts, and sausages.

    Overnight: The Augustine Hotel or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    PRAGUE

    Explore more of Prague’s Christmas markets at your own pace. Christmas market at Namesti Miru is more intimate, where you will find many handmade goods such as soaps, jewellery, wooden toys, ceramic decorations and much more. The market is overlooked by the neo-Gothic church of St Ludmila and a traditional nativity scene.

    Overnight: The Augustine Hotel or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    PRAGUE - MUNICH

    You will be privately transferred to Prague Railway Station for a Concierge Rail journey to Munich. On arrival in Munich you will be met directly off the train and assisted with your luggage and proceed for your private transfer to your hotel.

    At the hotel be welcomed with a festive and warming glass of Glühwein on the terrace.

    Explore the streets along with an English speaking guide as you pass the beautiful lit and decorated window displays on the Maximilianstrasse and then up to Maffeistrasse where the Loden-Frey fashion house is located. Your guide will then accompany you to the Dallmayr Delikatessen - a great place to indulge in some shopping for gifts or just to spoil yourself.

    Overnight: Hotel Goldener Hirsch or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    MUNICH

    Discover Munich by private vehicle on a full day orientation tour along with an English-speaking guide. The tour will include a visit to the Olympia Stadium and Nymphenburg Palace with its extravagant Rococo Amalienborg Pavillion.

    Later continue to the Alte Pinotek, one of the world's most famous art galleries who star exhibits include Maximilan's Four Apostles. Bring the tour to an end with a visit of the Residenz, former fortress of the Bavarian Kings.

    The evening is at your leisure.

    Overnight: Charles Hotel or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    MUNICH

    Once again you have the opportunity to explore more of the city along with your local English-speaking guide on a short walking tour through the compact Old Town centre with its multitude of fantastic shops, markets and always busy beer halls such as the "Hofbrauhaus" that Munich is so famous for. See the Karlsplatz fountain, the Frauenkirche - Munich's Cathedral - as well as the Marienplatz with the New Rathaus and its chiming clock, playing daily at 11.00am and noon.

    The market at the Marienplatz dates back to the 14th century and is the oldest Christmas market in the city. Meander through the many stall selling Bavarian wood carvings and gingerbread amongst other treats. The festive scene is complete with a 100ft tall Christmas tree in the square, adorned with 2,500 glittering lights and an Alpine choir performing on the balcony of the town hall. After dinner the ball begins with music from the Wiener Opera Orchestra and Big Band Gerhard Aflenzer in the Grand Festival Hall.Mark the midnight New Year stroke with the ringing of the Pummerin, the largest bells in the history of St Stephen’s Cathedral in Vienna.

    Overnight: Grand Hotel Europe
    Meal Plan: B

    ST PETERSBURG

    This afternoon, enjoy lunch at the lively country restaurant Podvorie Dacha, where President Putin has celebrated his birthday. Travel back to St. Petersburg and after some time at leisure, set off to enjoy a memorable night at the Tsar’s Ball at Catherine Palace.

Further, view a bell concert and a beautiful display of fireworks as the clock strikes twelve.

    After midnight, enjoy a Russian folklore show, Cossack show, while the Jazz Orchestras plays until early hours making for a truly special and greatly memorable New Year celebration.

CHRISTMAS AND NEW YEAR’S EVE IN MOROCCO

  • Programme Overview

    Explore Morocco, discover its main attractions and celebrate Christmas or welcome in 2015 in thrilling Marrakech!

    Duration: 9 days/ 8 nights

    Highlights: Casablanca | Fes Rabat | Marrakech | Atlas Mountains

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE CASABLANCA

    Upon arrival at the Casablanca airport, you will be greeted by an A&K representative. The name Casablanca evokes thoughts of good living, romance and adventure in a tropical setting. Casablanca’s fairly recent growth has made it the bustling, French-influenced business centre of Morocco it is today.

    Depending on your arrival time, you will visit the Hassan II Grand Mosque, one of the largest in the world and can house as many as 25,000 worshipers. The mosque’s minaret is the tallest religious structure in the world. Later, drive to the historical city of Fes.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    FES

    Today, explore the Middle Ages as you visit Fes El Bali, the largest living medieval city in the world and cultural heart of Morocco. Your sightseeing for the day includes a visit to the Nejjarine Square, Fondouq (Caravanserai), Dar Batha and the Museum of Moroccan Arts.

    Continue to visit the Fes El Jedid (New Fes), a UNESCO World Heritage site built by the Merinid Dynasty in the 13th Century, providing an interesting insight into Jewish architecture and culture. Here you view the Royal Palace, mosques, medersas (schools), souks and the city wall.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    RABAT

    This day, explore Volubilis, the site of the largest and most well preserved Roman ruins in Morocco. Further, visit Meknes, the formal Imperial city built in the 17th Century.

    Later, admire Bab Mansour gateway, the grandest of all imperial Moroccan gateways. Also view the Moulay Ismail’s Mausoleum.

    This afternoon, drive to Rabat, the capital of the Kingdom.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    RABAT

    Start the day with a visit to Citadel of Challah, the oldest part of the city. Enjoy your lunch and proceed for a visit to La Tour Hassan II Mosque, the city's most famous site is the massive minaret of Hassan. Continue to see the Mohammed V Mausoleum, one of the greatest monuments of modern Morocco, inaugurated in 1967.Here, the deceased king lies entombed in white onyx, surrounded by royal guards.

    Later, visit the Kasbah des Oudayas, Stroll through the sights as you appreciate the scenery. The Kasbah was the Almohad citadel of medieval Rabat, and is guarded by an impressive arched gate built around 1195.This afternoon, depart for the bustling Marrakech, the pearl of the south, enchanting you with its historical monuments.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    MARRAKECH

    Today, discover the mysteries of Marrakech. Begin with a visit to the Bahia Palace, a small piece of folly ordered in 1880 by the great vizir Sidi Moussa.

    Further, visit Si Said Museum to appreciate its beautifully decorated weapons, fabulously decorative clothing, beaten copper articles and captivating traditional Berber jewels.Enjoy your lunch and proceed to visit the Ben Youssef Medersa, one of the jewels of Marrakech. The current structure of this Koranic school was built around 1570.

    Later, discover the labyrinth of souks and get ready to shop. This maze of vibrant alleys and small squares are home to a bewildering array of stalls and ateliers devoted to specific crafts. Then, visit the Djemâa el Fna Square, where a multitude of street-artists and stalls of dried fruit and orange juice can be found.

    Conclude the day with a visit to the Koutoubia Mosque, an emblem of Marrakech. Built in the 12th century, the Koutoubia minaret is the most perfect Islamic religious structure in North Africa.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    MARRAKECH

    This morning, travel to visit a little farm located in the surroundings of Marrakech for a cookery class. Enjoy a cozy and casual atmosphere and be welcomed by Chef Tarek, under a Berber tent adorned with carpets and cushions, nestled in organic gardens. Embark on a culinary excursion as you mix all the ingredients to cook a tasteful Moroccan salad and sweet and sour tajine. The chef also shares with you his knowledge and tip on Moroccan cooking. While the dish is cooking on the fire, interact with the locals of the Berber village before returning to the farm for your freshly cooked meal.

    This afternoon, enjoy a horse-drawn carriage ride, starting in the Menara Gardens, where tranquility and comparative coolness makes it one of the most pleasant and romantic places for taking a stroll in Marrakech. Also view a harmonious Saadian style pavilion, built at the end of the 19th century.

    Later, Continue to the Majorelle Gardens, created in the 1920's by the French painter Jacques Majorelle. Majorelle is a very unique and special blue and is now a trademark colour name.

    Whether you are celebrating Christmas or welcoming in the 2015 New Year, if you prefer an elegant and refined atmosphere or something more wild, we will be pleased to arrange a special evening which will remain in your memories forever.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    MARRAKECH

    Spend the day at leisure relaxing by the pool of the hotel or strolling in the medina.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    ATLAS MOUNTAINS

    This day, unveil another side of Morocco, the gorgeous Atlas Mountains and North Africa’s greatest mountain range. En-route enjoy stunning views of the snow-capped mountains and the little Berber villages nestled in the hills. Further, explore the village of Asni and Imlil on the foothills of the Djebel Toubkal, the highest mountain in the Atlas.

    Arrive in Imlil and exchange your car for a mule, the traditional method of transportation in this region. Travel to explore the fascinating and authentic Berber culture. Reach the village located on a small hill and get the opportunity to experience their culture and family. Visit the home of a village local and interact with his family, view the traditional oven where they cook the delightful Berber bread and savor mint tea.

    Later, depart for the valley du Kik and the rocky Agafay desert. This will be a complete change of scenery with dry landscape replacing lush vegetation. Then visit the tranquil Kasbah Beldi, located in the heart of the countryside, far from the hustle and bustle of Marrakech. This beautiful and charming place is perfect for a rustic lunch in a cozy and comfortable atmosphere. Appreciate the spectacular views on Lake Lalla Takarkoust as you enjoy the smell of roses with a sumptuous lunch. Return to Marrakech and spend the rest of the day at leisure.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    DEPART MOROCCO

    This day, bid farewell to Morocco and transfer to the airport for your onward flight.

    Meal Plan: B

View the magnificent staterooms of the palace (if no member of the Royal Family is staying here at the time).

    This afternoon, travel to discover the Rosslyn Chapel, recently seen in the Da Vinci Code. Explore this exquisitely carved church building with an expert guide, who will take you through some of the mystical masonic symbolism that is present on almost every surface.

    Overnight: The Balmoral
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    EDINBURGH

    This morning, discover the Scottish countryside as you drive into the soft fruit-growing region called Strathmore and visit the turreted medieval Glamis Castle, the childhood home of Queen Elizabeth and legendary setting of Shakespeare's 'Macbeth'.

    Proceed southwards over the Sidlaw Hills to arrive at St. Andrews, the charming and historic town famous as the home of golf. Later, Visit the ruins of the cathedral, destroyed during the reformation also view the historic university buildings where Prince William first met Kate Middleton.

    Travel back to Edinburgh and get ready to watch the torchlight procession that winds its way from the Royal Mile along to Carlton Hill. Toward the end of the procession a firework display marks the start of the Hogmanay celebrations.

    Overnight: The Balmoral
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    EDINBURGH

    Begin the day with a drive down to the city docks at Leith, where you will board the former Royal Yacht Britannia, once used by the Royal Family, now permanently moored here. Take a guided tour of the Yacht, which has played host to some of the world's most famous people. View the staterooms in which the Queen entertained, and the private quarters where the Royal Family relaxed.

    Celebrate the New Year’s Eve at the glamorous and stylish Hogmanay Gala Ball at the Balmoral Hotel. Begin the evening with a Champagne and Canapé reception in the Holyrood Suite. Be serenaded by the delightful trio ‘Le Swing’ singing Rat Pack and Michael Buble classics.

    Later, Proceed for a stupendous dinner in the Sir Walter Scott Suite. Witness an amazing traditional Scottish bagpipes performance followed by a dance performance by some of the world’s top highland dancers. Continue the night’s celebration as you take part in a Scottish ceilidh, a form of Scottish dancing, accompanied by one of the best ceilidh bands in the country. Mark the midnight stroke with an outstanding view of the city’s extraordinary fireworks. Drink champagne, take part in the customary Auld Land Syne song and let the DJ entertain you into the early hours of the morning.

    Overnight: The Balmoral
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    EDINBURGH

    This day, relax after the celebrations of the previous night. Enjoy the tranquility of the streets and explore the city with a short walk as you climb up Calton Hill to absorb stunning views of the city. You may opt for a longer adventure walk down to Holyrood Park. Continue to climb up Arthur’s Seat, the extinct volcano that provides one of the most spectacular backdrops to any European City.

    Overnight: The Balmoral
    Meal Plan: B

    DEPART EDINBURGH

    After breakfast, you will be assisted with transfer to the airport by a private vehicle for your onward flight.
