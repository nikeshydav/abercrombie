Experiential Travel with A&K:

Experiential Travel is designed to excite, educate, entertain, intrigue, surprise and delight.

Today, travel is about participating in life enriching experiences and returning home transformed. It is about fulfilling the next and very final level of Maslow's hierarchy of needs, self-actualisation. Self actualisation is characterised not by a willingness to consume prestige goods or services for the sake of upgrading oneself socially but, by a quest to find one's own true happiness through connections with loved ones or with the world. This is the essence of a true travel experience.

But simply seeing the sights is no longer enough. Experience seekers want to stray from those beaten paths, dig deeper, get a read on how the locals live, work and play. This can include eating at a restaurant favored by locals instead of tourists, participating in an outdoor adventure or visiting sites not found in most guidebooks. This is called "experiential travel" - travel we live through instead of look at and it's never been more popular.

Many tourists tend to zip through a place, adding a notch to their "been there, seen that" list of cities and countries. They may even boast that they made it through seven countries in seven days after a week in Europe. This is travel just for the sake of traveling and not to experience destination insights to cherish the sights for a lifetime.

Experiential travelers tend to experience a place from the inside rather than view it as an outsider. They prefer to linger in one place and savor the local culture, knowing that a closer encounter with a location allows people to learn more about cuisine, customs and history than a one-day jaunt through a city can provide.

Some of the authentic destination experiences offered by Abercrombie & Kent on its inspiring journeys:

KENYA
  • Discover the Maasai people firsthand with a visit to a local village and school.
TANZANIA
  • Join the Serengeti Cheetah Project as a temporary, honorary researcher, tracking and identifying cheetah in the wild.
  • Experience the time-honored Maasai culture in person during a visit to a local village.
  • Set out on a bush walk, which provides a unique, on-the ground vantage point from which to view wildlife.
EAST - AFRICA
  • Sip sundowner cocktails in secluded repose, while savoring vibrant African sunsets.
  • Gaze across the Masai Mara plains from atop the Siria Escarpment, where Out of Africa was filmed, and visit the Karen Blixen house and museum, where the story was Penned.
NAMIBIA
  • Learn your way around the African night sky during a talk with an astronomer.
  • Take to the spectacular dunes and rocky outcrops on a guided ATV ride in Sossusvlei.
BOTSWANA / ZIMBABWE
  • Sleep under the stars on a plush, fully mobile bed, elevated amid groves of trees and surrounded by papyrus beds.
  • Take advantage of a rare privilege: interacting with a group of elephant habituated to human visitors.
  • Set out to discover the habits of nocturnal animals during a nighttime game viewing adventure.
EGYPT
  • Sail away from the crowds to the Temple of Hathor at Denderah, one of Egypt's best-preserved and less frequented Temples.
ISRAEL
  • Take a scenic sail on the Sea of Galilee, viewing sights from a wood boat similar to ones used during Jesus' time.
  • Visit the rarely seen city of Safed, a center of Jewish mysticism and one of Judaism's Four Holy Cities.
JORDAN

MOROCCO

CHINA

THAILAND

  • Travel as locals do throughout Thailand for a closer look at traditional life: by boat along canals and by rickshaw.
INDO-CHINA

MYANMAR
