Travel to Brazil


Brazil immediately paints a canvas of white sand beaches, bikini clad bodies, carnivals, samba, however this vibrant land has a lot more on offer. With Abercrombie and Kent, holidays in Brazil are synonym to experiencing the life to fullest itself given the effervescence of its culture and way of life of this country.

Rio de Janeiro is a lively second largest city of Brazil with the famous Cristo Redentor looking over the city's skyline from Corcovado Mountain. Visitors to Rio can also soak in the stunning views of the city from the Sugarloaf Mountain, which is also a point of entrance to it. While Ipanema and Copacabana are the easily the most sought after beaches, one has a plethora of gorgeous sun kissed coastlines to choose from during your luxury holiday to Brazil. In the north coast, the Ilheus, Natal and Salvador da Bahia are studded with glamorous pristine beaches, while south of Rio is lined with the lush white beaches running along the Atlantic rainforest, making this South American nation a popular honeymoon destination!

Modern Brazil showcases a 16th century colonial Portuguese heritage in its buzzing contemporary cities in the north. Visitors can embrace the colorful and vivid past of Brazil through its prominent diamond and gold mining towns of Minas Gerais to the slave trade ports of Bahia state, fine colonial structures everywhere in the nation.

Brazil is also an important eco-destination of the world because of its natural wonders. The Amazon River is not only the longest river on earth, but also have a vast basin that covers nearly half of the country. Southwards, one can witness the largest wetland area on the globe - Pantanal, which borders Paraguay and Bolivia. An exploratory Travel to Brazil can offer an exclusive peek into the wildlife of predators such as caiman, capybara, jaguars, and anacondas as well as the bright hued parrots and hyacinth macaw. Have an up-close and personal with the majestic Iguazú Falls on the Argentinean border from the specially constructed boardwalks or through a boat trip from one of the nearby lakes.
