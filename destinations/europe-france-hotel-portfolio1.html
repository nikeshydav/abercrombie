SUGGESTED COTE D'AZUR ACCOMMODATION

  • CARLTON CANNES

    The palatial Intercontinental Carlton Cannes stands as a monument to a golden age of European architecture. From the moment you enter the lobby area, the combination of classical styles with unmistakable chic is striking - and the theme continues throughout the hotel.

    Located close to the Cannes Palais des Festivals, the Carlton is a great base for exploration - the Old Town, nearby shopping on Rue d'Antibes or La Croisette and the mysterious Lérins Islands, which is easily accessible as a day trip.

    Read more

    CHATEAU DE LA CHEVRE D'OR

    Utterly enchanting, the Château de La Chèvre D'Or is steeped in history. This romantic property is scattered throughout the heart of the mediaeval hamlet with its rough-hewn stone walls and meandering cobbled pathways. Entering this hotel feels like a taking a step back in time.

    We think this hotel is genuinely unique and a magical experience in its own right.

    Read more

    CHATEAU ST MARTIN & SPA

    This enchanting hotel is a lovingly restored Roman fortified structure steeped in the history of crusaders and the Knights Templar. The property stands in a 35 acre estate peppered with more than 300 olive trees and providing sweeping views of the Mediterranean coast.

    The restoration was carried out by the celebrated contemporary architect Luc Svetchine and it shows. No detail has been overlooked in creating a superb property that effortlessly brings together historical features with modern design to create a hotel that really is one of a kind.

    The suites are decorated in Louis XV & XVI period style, with shimmering fabrics, harmonious tones, and wall hangings which echo a French style art of living.

    Read more

    HOTEL METROPOLE

    A member of Leading Hotels of the World, the Hotel Metropole is a monument to the Belle époque architectural style, and is the last word in luxury and comfort.

    The 141 rooms and suites have recently been redesigned by Jacques Garcia in a Mediterranean style that captures the atmosphere and romance of a bygone age, whilst remaining resolutely contemporary. The very epitome of classical elegance, they eschew the stripped back minimalism found in so many top hotels, instead combining warm colours and beautiful furnishings to create a genuinely welcoming, homely feel.

    We were particularly impressed with the Carré D'Or suite, with its huge terrace looking out to the Mediterranean beyond the rooftops of Monte Carlo.

    Read more

    HOTEL ROYAL RIVIERA

    The Hotel Royal Riviera retains the intimacy of a private Mediterranean villa, whilst radiating the glamour of a palace built for aristocrats on the 'Grand Tour'. Completely renovated, it is one of the best addresses on this fashionable stretch of coast.

    With 77 bedrooms and suites, the hotel displays a neo Hellenic style in the main building, whilst 14 guest rooms in L'Orangerie are decorated in Provençal tones. The stylish Bar le Cap offers the ideal relaxed atmosphere in which to enjoy pre-dinner drinks, both inside and out, allowing guests to savour the easy pace of life in the French Riviera.

    Read more

    LA PEROUSE

    Designed by a leading Provençal architect and a member of Leading Small Hotels of the World, this opulent but intimate hotel is the perfect base from which to explore the fantastic restaurants, boutiques and cafés of this region.

    The whole place is designed with style and comfort in mind, from the well-chosen, understated furnishings and décor to the cool, marble bathrooms. A delightful sun terrace enjoys superb views across Nice and the Mediterranean from its elevated position.

    La Perouse is the very definition of a boutique, luxury hotel. From the rooms to the fragrant gardens, it is a perfect hideaway in the centre of Nice.

    Read more

    LE MAS CANDILLE

    Nestling quietly in the hills above Cannes, Le Mas Candille is surrounded by mature cypress trees and old olive groves. An imaginatively transformed 18th century 'Mas' or farmhouse, the hotel comprises of 40 rooms, each individually styled and sympathetic to the hotel's Provençal heritage, yet with a nod to contemporary living.

    Guest rooms are split between Le Mas and La Bastide, whilst six new suites are housed in a double storey extension built into the hillside with stunning views towards Mougins. A partially covered walkway links the suites in La Villa Candille to the main hotel.

    The private balconies are a real treat. When you witness the dramatic sunrises and sunsets, with their vibrant, vivid palettes of colour and light, you will instantly understand why this part of the world has inspired so many artists.
