Travel To Greenland


Vast swathes of beautiful unfenced wilderness cover Greenland, the world's largest non-continental island. With over 84% of its surface area swaddled in ice, holidays in Greenland are like going on a vacation on top of an ice cap. Although it is the largest island in the world, it is home to just 60,000 people. Dozens of colorful little towns dot the western coast while the south boasts of emerald green farms liberally sprinkled with sheep. Largely unspoiled, it is blessed with abundant wildlife which is often up close.

Northern lights illuminating and dancing in the night sky are a sight to behold. The magnificence of ice and the deafening silence are memories you will cherish throughout life as you walk, sail or roam around on the Greenlandic ice sheet. Spotting a herd of musk oxen is another unique sight.

A trip to this chilly, frosty place is an adventure in itself with less number of tourist's and lesser infrastructure. A&K trips include traversing the warmer coastal regions and heading inland past the glacier cap. Travel is usually by helicopter or snow mobile.
