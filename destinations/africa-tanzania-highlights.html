Tanzania Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Serengeti National Park

    Serengeti National park is a UNESCO World Heritage site that houses Africa's big cats, elephant, buffalo, gazelle and giraffe. It is the oldest national park in Tanzania and sees the homecoming of over two million wildebeest which migrate along with more than 200,000 zebra and 300,000 Thompson's gazelle.

    These animals flood the park's land to graze over fresh grass making it an ideal place for predators to catch their prey. It is the longest overland migration in the world and you can imagine what these migrating animals would look like in the expansive spread of Serengeti (Largest National Park in Tanzania) which spreads over a land area of 14,763 sq km.

    Tarangire National Park

    A great place to start a Tanzanian safari, Tarangire National Park is not as famous as Serengeti but surely adds to the charm of the list of worth-visiting national parks in Tanzania.

    Lined with ancient baobabs and majestic acacia trees, a relaxed luxurious holiday awaits at Tarangire. Lie down in the luxury tented camp and listen to the whistling of the nearby waterhole where elephants come to drink water. Best time to spot the mammals is between the dry months of June to September.

    Ngorongoro Crater

    The Ngorongoro crater is a mesmerising sight to watch. It is the largest unbroken caldera in the world where it's fertile slopes and forest cover tracks house 300,000 animals including the rare black rhino.

    Its 260 sq km crater area is home to various ecosystems where large numbers of buffalo, elephants, wildebeest, zebras, elands, gazelles, hippos, various kinds of birds and high concentration of predators live.

    Mount Kilimanjaro

    Mount Kilimanjaro is Africa's highest mountain and is also the largest free standing mountain in the world. It's height of 5895m contrasts with the scrub land you see around it. It is made up of an extinct volcano that attracts crowd in major numbers making it one of the most deserving of all tourist places in Tanzania.     Selous Game Reserve

    The Selous Game Reserve is largest in the world. It has some of the finest and most pure virgin bushland in Africa where you could set your eyes on big-tusked elephants and large maned lions.

    If its privacy you want then Selous is the ideal choice as not many travellers come to this end. Get a motorised boat safari in the river or go on a walking safari to experience what it feels like when they say you could own a piece of Africa when you are here.

    Zanzibar

    Zanzibar is home to UNESCO's Stone Town that features some of the most unique architecture with Arab forts, markets and its famous inland spices.

    A key trading post at the time of Sultan rule, Zanzibar is one place you would want to be if you like being near the coastline. It evokes an image of white sandy beaches lined with palms where warm water of the Indian Ocean washes ashore any feeling of going back home.
