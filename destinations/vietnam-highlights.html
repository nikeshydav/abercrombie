Vietnam Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Hanoi, Halong Bay And sapa Holidays

    A city where the medieval and the modern co-exist, Hanoi is where imagination meets and blends with reality. An eclectic mix of Asian and European influences with wide tree-lined avenues, beautiful lakes and over 600 temples, Hanoi has a lot to offer to the visitor. Located on the banks of the Red River with the serene Hoan Kiem Lake at its heart, Hanoi is where you will find men in conical hats plying their wares beneath modern high-rises. Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum and the 11th century Quanh Tanh Temple before exploring the bustling street life of the Old Quarter by the traditional cyclo are places to watch out for. Fine Arts Museum, Old Quarters, Temple of Literature and Tran Quoc Pagoda are some of the places to look out for. There is no better place to untangle the paradox that is modern Vietnam than a trip to Hanoi.

    A UNESCO World Heritage Site due to its priceless, unfinished and natural limestone sculptures, Halong Bay, translates as 'the bay of the descending dragon' and is one of the most impressive tourist places in Vietnam. An assortment of 3000 or more incredible islands rising from the emerald waters of the Gulf of Tonkin makes for an incredible sight.

    Ho Chi Minh City Holidays

    This metropolis is always in a hurry - bustling, noisy, singing and entertaining, its people an eclectic mix of warmth, happiness and vigor. Always on the move, Ho Chi Minh City breathes life and vitality into one and all that choose to settle here. Formerly known as Saigon, it seamlessly and effortlessly blends the old and the new into an urban collage. The Reunification Palace is one of the most important buildings in the city. From the city you can visit the Cu Chi Tunnels, an underground hiding place for the Viet Cong during the 60's and the 70's. Vietnam Sightseeing is truly an incredible experience once you enter this magical city.

    Hoi An and Danang Holidays

    As you stroll around the narrow, cobbled streets of Hoi An, you take a flight back in time. A common halt for backpackers, it is fast gaining popularity with tourists too. A trading port in the 17th century, it is a pedestrian friendly place, the Old Town being closed for cars and hiring a bicycle is the next best thing other than taking a leisurely stroll. The Japanese Bridge and the Quan Cong Temple are sights to discover. To reach Hoi An, drive along one of the most spectacular roads in Vietnam which winds along the coast with magnificent views of the beaches to one side and lush hills on the other. This half day journey is often the highlight of a Vietnam tour for photographers, with plenty of opportunities to stop at rustic fishing villages and to meet with the locals and capture some amazing images of the scenery.

    Perhaps more than any other place in Vietnam, Hoi An preserves traces of history that swathes you as you set to explore it. Abercrombie & Kent's Hoi an and Danang holidays are the perfect way to explore this magnificent region.

    HUE HOLIDAYS

    Ancient splendor and elegance are hard to put a value on. If exotic art and exquisite architecture rank much above beaches and beer on your holiday list, then a trip to Hue is a must. Standing on the banks of the beautifully named Perfume River, this ancient town is any historian's dream come true. 19th century ruins, royal tombs and museums dot this wonderful city.

    Hue can be easily visited on a day trip from Danang. Explore the architectural wonders of this city in the Tu Duc Temple, The World Heritage Citedel Site, Hue Imperial City (The Citadel), Thien Mu Temple complex dominated by the Phuoc Duyen Tower, which has seven floors each dedicated to a different Buddha.     Mekong Delta Cruises & Holidays

    Stretching along 39,000 square kilometers and at its heart is the city of Can Tho lies the Mekong Delta. A watery landscape of green paddy fields and sleepy villages, it yields enough rice to feed the country with a sizable surplus.

    Rightly called as Vietnam's Rice Bowl, explore its bustling floating markets by boat and see the locals buying and exchanging goods. Abercrombie and Kent's Mekong Delta cruises and holidays are the ideal way to explore this charming place.

    Nha Trang Holidays

    Swimming, scuba diving, snorkeling, wind surfing and kite boarding - the beach! Clearly Vietnam's beach resort town, Nha Trang offers boat trips to nearby islands other than the fun option of sun bathing on its white sands.

    The capital of Khanh Hoa province, it is located on the South Central coast of Vietnam having the South China Sea on the east side, the south has Cam Ranh Town, the north borders the Ninh Hoa district and the west borders the Dien Khanh district.
