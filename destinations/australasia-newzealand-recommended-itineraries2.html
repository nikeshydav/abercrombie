NORTH'S SCENIC GOLFING'

  • Duration: 6 Days / 5 Nights

    Challenge your golfing skills on specially designed courses with stunning landscape and the most spectacular panoramic views.

    Programme Overview

    Auckland – Kauri Cliffs, Bay of Islands

    A&K Advantage

    Play a round of golf at the iconic Grange Golf Club at Auckland.
    Tee off at Kauri Cliffs Golf Course.
    Test your golfing skills at Waitangi Golf Club.
    Utilize the contours of the Keri Keri Golf Club
    Enjoy an evening cruise along with Waitamata harbor
    Take a cruise around the Bay of Islands and watch the dolphins play
    Whisk off for a fishing experience on a fully equipped boat

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE AUCKLAND

    Auckland is an amazing spot for a round of golf with a comfortable climate and fresh air all around you.The region boasts of more golf courses than anywhere else in NZ. Enjoy your stay with a self-drive experience alongside.

    On arrival you will be assisted to your self-drive vehicle and assisted with check-in at your hotel

    Post lunch tee off at Auckland’s iconic golf course, The Grange Golf Club. The Grange, an 18 Hole Par 70 Course is broadly known as one of New Zealand’s iconic golf courses which offers a brilliant assessment of golf skills and golfers are welcome from around the globe. Be it Amateur or Professional, this course has hosted every major Golf Tournament in New Zealand, including the New Zealand Open Championship on 8 occasions.

    A spectacular way to spend an evening is Auckland is aboard the yacht cruising along the Waitamata harbor. Relax on this dinner cruise enjoying the panoramic view of the city all lit up and beautiful. You may also lend a hand to the captain.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel or similar
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    AUCKLAND

    This morning enjoy a game of golf at Royal Auckland Golf Club. This is a member’s only golf course and a special entry may be arranged for you at this 18 Hole Par 72 Course (subject to confirmation).

    OR

    You may be delighted with a game of golf at the Gulf Harbour Country Club, an 18 Hole Par 72 Course, located in the Whangaparoa Peninsula at a distance of approximately an hour from main Auckland. The drive to country club is scenic and nothing short of spectacular. The beauty of the location, challenge and teeing off enjoyment are sure to awe you. This world-class golf course with challenging tees and greens and an area of 6,400 metre, par 72 meanders through native grasses and vegetation and has a natural look and feel from start to finish. The magnificent topography makes Gulf Harbour special and delights you with an amazing experience.

    Evening, relax in a luxurious spa treatment at your accommodation or visit a vineyard. Taste the amazing New Zealand wines produced at the vineyard along with dinner and you will also receive priceless information on wine pairing.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofite or similar
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 3

    AUCKLAND –BAY OF ISLANDS

    Driving Distance: 280 kms approx.
    Duration: 4.5 hrs approx.

    This morning take a scenic drive to the Kauri Cliff region for the most spectacular golfing experience. You will be assisted with check in to the hotel.

    Afternoon, tee off at Kauri Cliffs. The relaxed and refined luxury lodge, Kauri Cliffs which is listed in the world’s top 100 resorts offers an 18 Hole Par 72 Championship Course. Kauri Cliffs golf course was designed and built by David Harman. Area of the course is 6,510 metres with five sets of tees to challenge every skill level. While golfing you enjoy stunning views since 15 holes view the Pacific Ocean, out of which 6 holes are played alongside cliffs plunging in to the sea. The inland holes wind through marsh, forest and farmland. It is ranked among the 100 Greatest Golf Courses in the World by Golf Magazines World Top 100 Course Ranking panel. Callaway ladies and gent’s clubs are available on rent.

    OR

    Take a break to unwind and relax over a luxurious spa session.

    Overnight: Kauri Cliffs or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    BAY OF ISLANDS

    Today the golf course for you to test your skill can be Waitangi Golf Club. This is an 18 Hole Par 70 Course, which is an exciting and ever changing challenge to the golfer and offers the most marvelous views of the sea and the island over the Bay of Islands, Russell. The stunning panoramic view stretches over the river and estuary over Waitangi and Opua extending further to the rolling bush and forest views and if that is not enough to please you the you can view ahead to the inland over Mount Bledisloe and Puketona.

    OR

    Carrington offers an 18 Hole Par 72 Championship Course and offers a variety of vistas to the golfer. The course delights you with sweeping ocean views to the north and east, and hill side vineyards of Karikari Estate to the south. The clubhouse and lounge complex provide outstanding views of the golf course, wetlands, beach and the ocean beyond owing to its location on a high ridge in the middle of the golf course. The championship standard course was designed by American golf course designer Matt Dye.

    Later take a cruise around the Bay of Islands and watch the dolphins play around and spot some whales too. For an up-close experience you can dive in for a swim with wild dolphins.

    Overnight: Eagle’s Nest or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    BAY OF ISLANDS

    Drive to the Keri Keri Golf Club this morning and tee off at this 18 Hole Par 73 Championship Course and measuring 6101 metre in the beautiful Bay of Islands. The course has been designed to utilize all the amazing contours naturally sculpted by the Puketotara Stream and the Kerikeri River, which delivers beautiful views of the New Zealand native bush and birds.

    This afternoon, enjoy some time at leisure or whisk off for a fishing experience on a fully equipped boat including fishing equipment of the highest quality. Your catch will be prepared to your liking ready to cook, or it can be smoked by our local professional smoker.

    Overnight: Eagle’s Nest or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    DEPART BAY OF ISLANDS – AUCKLAND

    Return to Auckland and handover your self-drive vehicle at the airport to the A&K Representative.

    Meal Plan: B

    • Possible Extensions:
    3 Days HAWKE’S BAY

  • Day 1

    AUCKLAND – HAWKE’S BAY

    Take a short flight of 1 hr duration to Napier Airport and on arrival you will be assisted to your self-drive vehicle.

    You will be assisted with check-in to your lodge. Enjoy this amazing experience of playing on New Zealand’s top golf course. This stunning course was designed by the legendary golf architect Tom Doak offering par 71 golf course with an area of 7,119 yards (6,510 meters). This course provides challenge to golfers of all skill levels. Cape Kidnappers currently holds the rank of the 41st best golf course in the world by Golf Magazine and is considered as one of the great modern wonders in the world of golf.

    Overnight: The Farm at Cape Kidnappers or similar
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    HAWKE’S BAY

    Be adventurous and depart for Hot Air Ballooning early this morning. Float over farmland, orchards and vineyards and appreciate the backdrop of distant mountain ranges. The flight takes place during the first few hours of daylight and is dependent on weather conditions.

    Just one game of golf at this amazing Cape Kidnappers golf course will never be enough. Enjoy another game of golf and challenge your golfing skills again.

    Afternoon can be well spent on a fishing session, since Hawke’s Bay offers a wide range of fishing opportunities. The bay's major rivers, the Mohaka, Tutkuir, Ngaruroro, Waipawa and Tukituki, are well populated with famed wild brown and rainbow trout.

    OR

    You may kayak on the stretch of one of the Cape Kidnappers’ river which are suitable for kayakers of varying skill levels. Enjoy an easy drift down a picturesque river with warm water, great swimming holes, abundance of bird life with a rural backdrop. Dock at a riverbank for a scenic sundowner.

    Overnight: The Farm at Cape Kidnappers or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    DEPART CAPE KIDNAPPERS – AUCKLAND

    Return to Auckland on a short flight and proceed for your connecting international leg of the return journey.


    Meal Plan: B

SOUTHERN GOLF SPLENDOUR

  • Duration: 7 Days / 6 Nights

    Tee off at golf courses with varied topography and scenic views that will catch you off guard and encourage you to explore other activities on offer.

    Programme Overview

    Christchurch – Lake Wanaka – Queenstown

    A&K Advantage
    Play a round of golf at the Clearwater Golf Course.
    Tee off at Terrace Downs Golf Course.
    Make the most of the picturesque Wanaka Golf Course.
    Enjoy the stunning surroundings at the Millbrook Resort.
    Enjoy a hot air balloon ride over Christchurch
    Enroute to Lake Wanaka, enjoy lunch at a Vineyard in the South Canterbury countryside
    Relax over a spa session

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE CHRISTCHURCH

    On arrival you will be privately transferred to your hotel and assisted with check-in.

    Post lunch depart for a game of golf at Clearwater Golf Course. The 18 hole Par 72 championship course is home to the NZ PGA Championship and is suitable for golfers of all skill levels. It was designed by John Darby in consultation with New Zealand golfing legend, Sir Bob Charles and is an amalgamation of the links golf inspired by the great Scottish courses along with parkland golf, which is more suggestive of Florida. The course is a scenic pleasure as it winds around a number of spring-fed lakes and trout-filled streams offering uninterrupted views of the enormous Southern Alps.

    Overnight: Peppers Clearwater resort or similar

    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    CHRISTCHURCH

    Early this morning take a hot air balloon ride over Christchurch. The views will surely mesmerize you and become beautiful remembrances. Cheer over a champagne breakfast on touch down.

    Tee off at Terrace Downs Golf Course, which offers a par 72, 18 hole championship golf course and is rated as one of the top courses and golfing destinations in New Zealand. Owing to its location at the foothills of the Southern Alps, it is one of the very few courses to offer such amazing views of the alpine region. Designed by Sid Pudicombe and Associates it is a unique blend of Parklands and Links style which has been elusively unified with the natural environment. The charming views of the Rakaia Gorge and mountain vistas are presumably the biggest distractions while playing a challenging game of golf at the course.

    This afternoon enjoy a scenic drive along the River Avon to the beautiful Botanical Gardens. If you desire for more scenic beauty, admire the wonderful vista of Christchurch and the quaint Lyttleton Harbour as you drive to the top of the Port Hills.

    Overnight: Peppers Clearwater resort or similar

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    CHRISTCHURCH – LAKE WANAKA

    Head out for a scenic drive to Lake Wanaka region.

    Driving Distance: 430 kms approx.
    Duration: 5 hrs approx. (excluding stop overs)

    Enroute enjoy lunch at a vineyard in the South Canterbury countryside set in rural scenic landscape, with a wine tasting session of award winning wines and hearty dishes to complement the accompanying wines.

    Relax over a spa session later in the evening.

    Overnight: Whare Kea Lodge or similar

    Meal Plan: B, L

  • Day 4

    LAKE WANAKA

    Head out for the famed Flight of Kea. The experience includes an exciting walk in the wilderness, a gorgeous scenic helicopter flight to Milford Sound, offering spectacular views over Mount Aspiring, referred to as the Matterhorn of the south and topped with a gourmet picnic at the Whare Kea Chalet.

    Wanaka Golf Course awaits you this afternoon. It is a picturesque 18 hole Par 70 course surrounded by mountains and overlooks Lake Wanaka. It is located above Wanaka town, in the heart of the Queenstown Lakes region.

    Overnight: Whare Kea Lodge or similar

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    LAKE WANAKA – ARROWTOWN

    Depart towards Arrowtown located 15km from Queenstown airport.

    Driving Distance: 70 kms approx.
    Duration: 1 hr approx.

    On arrival you will be assisted with check-in and you could directly head out for a round of golf. The Millbrook Golf Course, located in stunning surroundings, the natural arena of the alpine against the backdrop of the Remarkables mountain range exploits the dramatic terrain to deliver world-class golfing experience at its 18 hole, Par 72 championship course, designed by renowned professional and master golfer, Sir Bob Charles. A complete experience for golfers of all calibers.

    Overnight: Millbrook Resort or similar

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    ARROWTOWN

    Day at leisure to either play another round of golf at this amazing course.

    OR

    Head for a NZONE Skydive to Queenstown, which is popularly known as the adrenaline capital of New Zealand. Get harnessed to an experienced qualified jumpmaster and jump out of an aircraft into thin air for an experience that will change you forever! Leave with some great pictures and some amazing memories. Follow it up with a parachute jump over the spectacular Remarkables mountain range or Lake Wakatipu

    OR

    Later, experience an incredible scenic experience over TSS Earnslaw. It is much more than a lake cruise, you get to visit a working sheep’s farm and explore for a true farm experience. Hop on to a cycle for a private guided tour to the Lord of the Rings location.

    Overnight: Millbrook Resort or similar

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    DEPART ARROWTOWN – QUEENSTOWN

    Meet your representative and depart for your onward connections.

    Meal Plan: B

LUXURIOUS LODGES – NORTH ISLAND ON SELF DRIVE

  • Duration: 9 Days / 8 Nights

    Explore the scenic North Island of New Zealand on a self-drive or a chauffeur driven route with comfortable stays at luxurious lodges!

    Programme Overview

    Auckland – Tauranga – Rotorua – Lake Taupo

    A&K Advantage
    - Stay at some stunning luxury lodges located at spectacular scenic locations offering excellent hospitality and world class cuisine for a completely lavish experience.
    - Private luxury self-drive or private chauffeured vehicles to take you on the scenic routes.
    - Private meals and vineyard visits.
    - Exclusive spa arrangements at Te Aroha
    - Visit Rainbow Springs and take a tour through Kiwi Encounter
    - Helicopter ride over the Tauranga region & the varied geothermal areas of Rotorua towards White Island, the only active volcano.
    - Enjoy a cultural evening at Te Po.
    - Visit the famed Huka Falls, the most visited natural attraction in New Zealand.
    - Witness the Maori Rock Carvings around Lake Taupo on a boat cruise.
    - Head to Tongariro National Park for the legendary Tongariro Alpine Crossing.
    - Visit Bulli Point for an exhilarating Cliff diving experience, Lake Taupo style.
    - SKYCITY for 2 ultimate experiences of Sky Walk and Sky Jump.
    - Enjoy an evening dinner cruise along the Wickemata harbor.

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE AUCKLAND

    On arrival you will be met by a representative and transferred privately to your accommodation and be assisted with check-in.

    Unwind and relax from your lengthy flight. While at Auckland, you could visit the Auckland Museum that houses world-renowned collection of Polynesian artifacts.

    Explore the glittery pulsating, country’s largest city and commercial hub, Auckland

    The Gulf of Hauraki offers amazing Whale and Dolphin Safari. You may also visit the bustling seaside village of Devon Port with its beautiful small beaches and amazing views of the two volcanic cones.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel or similar
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    AUCKLAND – TAURANGA

    If you choose to self-drive, a vehicle will be provided at the time of check-out from the hotel; alternately you may proceed with your chauffer. Start your day early as you enjoy your drive towards Tauranga.

    Enroute visit two world-famous visitor attractions; the famous Waitomo Glowworm Caves and Hobbiton Movie Set, made famous by the movie trilogies “The Lord of the Rings” and “The Hobbit”.

    Driving Distance: 170 kms
    Duration: 3 hrs approx.

    At Waitomo, you will experience the famous Glowworm Caves on a 45-minute tour. After lunch in Waitomo, we continue through the small country town of Cambridge to “The Shire’s Rest”, so you can have the wonderful opportunity to see the original Hobbiton Movie Set, which was the setting for Peter Jackson's "The Lord of the Rings" and "The Hobbit" movie trilogies.

    At the end of the tours, head towards Tauranga

    Driving Distance: 60 kms approx.
    Duration: 1 hr approx.

    The French Country House

    A luxurious farmhouse in the Bay of Plenty, The French Country House will enchant! Its living areas are spacious, light and airy with a mixture of luxury antique furnishings and comfortable modern facilities. The views from the balcony over the Bay of Plenty countryside to the sea are spectacular with excellent access to activities in Mt. Maunganui and Tauranga.

    Overnight: The French Country House or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    TAURANGA

    The Bay of Plenty has an abundance of marine life. Step onboard a custom built boat for the sea safari that starts at 8am daily and the tour takes approximately 5 hours to complete. Look out for dolphins, whales, orcas and more. You could either sit back and watch the marine life, or take a plunge in to swim with them in their natural habitat. Light snacks and refreshments will also be available during the tour.

    Mt Maunganui is located at a short distance and offers the most stunning and spectacular views as you explore it on foot

    Overnight: The French Country House or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    TAURANGA - ROTORUA

    This morning depart for Rotorua.

    Driving Distance: 75 kms approx.
    Duration: 1.2 hr approx.

    On arrival you will be assisted with check-in.

    Take a scenic flight over the region to the White Island, the only active volcano. Disembark for a private guided tour of the islands crater floor and you will view active steam vents “fumaroles”, hot streams, vibrant sulphur formations and the steaming crater. You could choose either a helicopter or a floatplane to view some of the most dramatic geothermal scenery and the ever changing landscapes of this island.

    Rotorua is also the heartland of New Zealand's Maori culture, and you can head to Te Po for a cultural evening experience. Enjoy a performance of song and dance, followed by food cooked in traditional hangi style (an earth oven). Hear stories of the past & experience an authentic Maori welcome ceremony followed for a truly traditional cultural experience.

    Peppers on the Point

    Surrounded by the sparkling waters of Lake Rotorua, Peppers on the Point is an intimate luxury retreat featuring spectacular views to the historic Mokoia Island. Rotorua is the heartland of New Zealand’s Maori culture and is set amidst a landscape of erupting geysers, hot thermal springs and bubbling mud pools. With so many sights to see and cultural offerings within easy reach, we will have you immersed in relaxation upon your return.

    Overnight: Peppers on the Point or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    ROTORUA

    Head out for an exhilarating River Jet ride experience. Be it scenic or a thermal safari down the Waikato River through the Tutukau gorge. Also, experience the legendary Hamilton spins.

    OR

    Visit the Rainbow Springs Kiwi Wildlife Park for a guided tour of Kiwi Encounter. It is a hatchery and nursery for the unique and treasured 'Kiwi' birds. Later you may enjoy a stroll through the magnificent Redwood Forest.

    Overnight: Peppers on the Point or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    ROTORUA – LAKE TAUPO

    Early morning depart for a scenic drive to Lake Taupo across the unusual volcanic topography and watch the pine forest plantations as you drive past.

    Driving Distance: 90 kms
    Duration: 1.5 hrs approx.

    On arrival you will be assisted with check-in to the lodge.

    Depart to visit the famed Huka Falls, the most natural attraction in New Zealand. For an adventurous twist you could explore kayaking or canoeing at the falls.

    Another sought after attraction is the Maori Rock Carvings around Lake Taupo. Take a boat cruise and appreciate the spectacular views of the carvings.

    Before the end of your day visit ‘Craters of the Moon’ – an active Geothermal Wonderland.

    Lake Taupo Lodge
    Relax, unwind and soak up the atmosphere in this lakeside retreat in the volcanic and fly fishing heartland of New Zealand.

    This award winning, luxury bed & breakfast lodge is a designer's delight. Inspired by Frank Lloyd Wright, it is set within magnificent landscaped gardens overlooking beautiful Lake Taupo and the mountains.

    Huka Lodge

    Huka Lodge, located on Lake Taupo, is extremely well located and offers amazing views and hospitality. Its garden is of great regional importance and is ranked as a 'Garden of National Significance'.

    Overnight: Lake Taupo Lodge / Huka Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    LAKE TAUPO

    Take a walk amidst the volcanoes of Tongariro in Tongariro National Park for the legendary Alpine Crossing which is renowned for an unforgettable experience. It is one of the most popular activities of the region.

    OR

    If you are passionate about motor sport, you may visit Taupo’s Motorsport Park. This park is a state of the art race track, an international FIA category 2 race circuit and NZDRA certified drag strip. The facility which hosts a driver formula challenge also provides an opportunity to do the laps in a race car.

    On return visit Bulli Point for an exhilarating Cliff diving experience, Lake Taupo style! The Bulli Point offers three ledges, the highest being seven metres offering an amazing plunge into the fresh blue water.

    Overnight: Lake Taupo Lodge / Huka Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    LAKE TAUPO – AUCKLAND

    This morning enjoy fishing over a picnic lunch before you depart to Auckland.

    Driving Distance: 282 kms
    Duration: 3.5 hrs approx.

    OR

    Start your day early and depart to Auckland. On arrival settle in your hotels and head out to explore Ponsonby and Jervois Roads boutique stores at your pace. For an ultimate adrenaline rush visit the SKYCITY for 2 ultimate experiences of SkyWalk and SkyJump.

    Later you may, unwind at the hotels spa for a relaxing and luxurious experience.

    OR

    A spectacular way to spend an evening in Auckland is aboard the yacht cruising along the Waitamata harbor. Relax on this dinner cruise enjoying the panoramic view of the city all lit up and beautiful.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    AUCKLAND

    Depart for the Airport and in case you were self-driving, hand over the vehicle to the Representative.

    Meal Plan: B

MAGNIFICENT SOUTH ISLAND LODGES

  • Duration: 14 Days / 13 Nights

    Travel the South Island circuit of New Zealand and be amazed with its scenic drives, beautiful landscapes and splendid lodges.

    Programme Overview

    Oamaru – Dunedin – TeAnau – Manapouri – Queenstown – Lake Wanaka – Franz Josef – Christchurch

    A&K Advantage
    - Stay at luxury lodges with magnificent views offering excellent hospitality and world class cuisine for a completely lavish experience.
    - Go for private guided wildlife tour to observe the rare penguins and Albatross at Dunedin.
    - Visit TeAnau Glowworm Caves.
    - Enjoy an overnight cruise over Doubtful Sound to Manapouri.
    - For an adrenalin rush stop at the Kawarau River Bridge and take the bungee jump.
    - Visit an award winning vineyard in the Gibbston area for wine tasting and sumptuous lunch.
    - Enjoy jet boating across the Matukituki River.
    - Experience the amazing heli hike over the Franz Josef glacier.
    - Hop on to a quad bike for a fun and challenging ride on the rocky beds of the glacier.
    - Enjoy a punt on the Avon River at Christchurch.

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE CHRISTCHURCH – OAMARU

    On arrival you will be met by an A&K representative who will assist you to your self-drive vehicle, if you choose to self-drive; alternately meet your driver for your journey ahead to Oamaru.

    Driving Distance: 245 kms

    Duration: 3 hrsapprox

    As you cross across the Southern Alps, you can spectacle outstanding scenic beauty. You will cross the large town of Ashburton which sits between the Rakaia and Rangitata rivers, where fly fishing is the local obsession. You may take the loop road to Geraldine and pay a visit to the gourmet cheese factory and the vintage car museum.

    On arrival you will be assisted with check-in and could fish for salmon and/or trout in the world-famous Waitaki River.

    OR

    View the famed penguin parade, as Oamaru is home to two colonies of penguins, Blue Penguins (also known as Fairy Penguins, the smallest of the penguins) and Yellow-Eyed Penguins, which are among the rarest.

    Pen-y-Bryn Lodge
    Pen-y-Bryn is Welsh for “Top of the Hill” and indeed the house sits atop South Hill, one of Oamaru’s most sought-after districts. Reputed to be the largest single-storied timber dwelling in Australasia, the house has kept its Victorian heritage and offers guests the perfect combination of old world elegance and modern amenities.

    Overnight: Pen-y-Bryn Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    OAMARU

    Early morning drive to the renowned Vanished World Heritage Trail. The Vanished World Heritage Trail is a self-guided trail allowing you to see numerous fossils and geological formations that express a feeling of “deep time”. A drive along this trail will take you from seaside to mountains providing a unique outlook on the area’s changing landscape through the Waitaki District.

    OR

    Take a full day trip to Danseys Pass which is the most popular amongst the many gorgeous drives in the area. On a drive along Danseys Pass you will cross the old Central Otago towns of Naseby and Ranfurly, as well as to Elephant Rocks and some Maori rock paintings.

    Overnight: Pen-y-Bryn Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    OAMARU – DUNEDIN

    Depart Oamaru to drive towards Dunedin.

    Driving Distance: 137 kms
    Duration: 2 hrs approx.

    The drive further down south is across historical landmarks like Totara Estate Centennial Park, a spectacle of restored farm buildings of the 1860s, an old limestone flour mill, Clark’s mill and also the beach of Hampden where you could find interesting, ancient stones on the beach.

    On arrival you will be assisted with check-in to the lodge and you could then directly head out for adventurous water sports. You could choose from kayaking, canoeing to stand up paddle boat lessons for an exhilarating time.

    Dunedin region also offers a choice of tracks for mountain biking providing scenic scapes of the Otago Peninsula.

    Relax in the luxury of an in room spa session before dinner.

    Kaimata Retreat
    Kaimata Retreat is a timber built luxury lodge on the Otago Peninsula that spans a 350 ha estuary and offers sweeping water views of coastal birds and wildlife.

    Overnight: Kaimata Retreat or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    DUNEDIN

    Depart for a 3 hour private guided wildlife tour to a private beach and experience a discreet uninterrupted observation of the rare Yellow-eyed penguin and Hooker's sea lion. You can further experience a short 1 hour duration Albatross private guided tour, where you will be educated about Northern Royal Albatross, followed by a visit to well-populated and frequented viewing areas on Taiaroa Head.

    This afternoon, take a scenic heli flight over the Otago Peninsula or explore the Otago Peninsula on a mountain bike.

    Overnight: Kaimata Retreat or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    DUNEDIN – TE ANAU

    The drive from Dunedin to TeAnau will be across fresh green farmlands. You will cross the historic mill town of Milton and Balclutha which is distinctive owing to its arched concrete bridge.

    Driving Distance: 317 kms
    Duration: 4 hrs approx.

    On arrival you will be assisted with check-in at the lodge.

    This evening visit TeAnau Glowworm Caves on a guided tour. The journey begins with a scenic cruise across the lake. Once inside the caves you can view the still developing cave formations, with the sound of flowing water in the background as you drift through caverns inhabited by glowworms making it a beautiful adventurous experience.

    Fiordland Lodge
