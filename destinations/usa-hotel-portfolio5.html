Suggested New England accommodation.

  • ARABELLA

    The Arabella is a classic windjammer yacht which cruises along the New England coastline during the Summer and Autumn. She offers the ultimate in small-ship crewed sailing. All of the staterooms can be booked individually and feature private bathrooms, satellite TV, and individual climate control. The beautiful teak decks boast a hot tub and shaded cabana seating. The tall ship presence pulls in the crowds as you dock in some of the most glamourous spots in New England.

    Evenings are your own free time in port, so you don't miss out on the many gourmet dining options in each of the places you visit. Lunches and breakfasts are served aboard. There are snorkels and kayaks aboard and with so much to do its a great alternative to fly-driving this part of America. Perfect for the family.

    BOSTON, FAIRMONT BATTERY WHARF

    The Fairmont Battery Wharf is in a great location from which to explore this historic city. Boston's Italian North End, bustling financial district, Freedom Trail, Faneuil Hall Marketplace and TD Garden are all very close by.

    The hotel is set across 4 buildings all right on the waterfront and connected by an underground walkway. The look and feel of the hotel is fresh and contemporary. This is a great spot to begin or end your New England adventure.

    BOSTON, FOUR SEASONS

    The Four Seasons Boston is characterised by elegant simplicity and a warm New England welcome. The hotel boasts a fantastic location and is close to almost all the major attractions of Boston.

    You can easily wander this Historical city on foot.

    BOSTON, TAJ BOSTON

    The Taj Boston has the ambience of a Boston townhouse. Overlooking the public gardens, there are many little touches that make this hotel unique, such as the 'fireplace butler' who comes to each of the suites with fireplaces offering a choice of firewood.

    MAINE, BLACK POINT INN

    The Black Point Inn epitomises the genteel luxury you'll experience time and again along the New England coastline. Here the pace is slower, unhurried and gracious.

    Guests have been visiting since 1878 to enjoy the views and the opportunities to relax - that ethic hasn't changed since.

    MAINE, SOUTH CASCO, MIGIS LODGE

    The Migis Lodge is in a gorgeous spot, nestled on Sebago Lake, Maine, amidst beautiful, fragrant tall Northern Pines and is a perfect place for a family adventure holiday. If you are seeking lakes, forests, great seafood, crisp fresh air and quiet serenity, then this place is for you and your family. There is so much to do including golf, tennis, motorboating, nearby there are beautiful villages and miles of hikes and trails.

    Migis Lodge offers a Summertime Children's Activity Program, with activities changing daily and including games, crafts, sports, picnics, swimming, field trips, storytelling and treasure hunts.

    MASSACHUSETTS, CAPE COD, CHATHAM BARS INN

    Chatham Bars Inn is an idyllic retreat on Cape Cod noted for its excellent service, fine cuisine and beautiful surroundings. The Inn is nestled near the heart of the village of Chatham and overlooks Pleasant Bay and the Atlantic Ocean.

    If you're looking for boat charters whilst on Cape Cod, then the Inn offers its own top-notch fleet. A variety of luxury boats offer unforgettable Cape Cod experiences. Unique boating excursions offered by Chatham Bars Inn include Cape Cod sunset cruises on Pleasant Bay, big game sport fishing, sailing tours, family activities, and private charters to Nantucket and Martha's Vineyard.

    Chatham, one of the oldest villages on Cape Cod, is just a brief stroll away, where quaint streets are lined with historic homes, unique shops, art galleries, and glorious gardens. The village is anchored by the Chatham lighthouse and is a wonderful spot to stroll around.

    MASSACHUSETTS, CAPE COD, WEQUASSETT

    Wequassett, or "cresent on the water", was originally named by the Wampanoag Indians who made this area their home. Set close to the charming village of Chatham on Cape Cod, the resort has stood since 1925 and has always been at the forefront of New England luxury.

    Wequassett has that typical look and feel of the area - white-washed buildings and clapboard cottages. This is a luxurious spot to base yourself for day trips along the Cap or out to the islands of Nantucket and Martha's Vineyard. The resort's golf course, tennis centre and sailing school allow guests who don't wish to travel far to keep the days active and the evenings relaxed.

    MASSACHUSETTS, LENOX, WHEATLEIGH

    Wheatleigh is a grand 19th-Century country house set in a beautiful area of New England. The Berkshires is a verdant region of low hills and mountains dotted with pretty villages. Wheatleigh is located in the village of Lenox, a pretty spot with upmarket shops and restaurants. In recent years The Berkshires have become something of a mecca for those seeking cultural attractions. Nearby to Wheatleigh is 'Tanglewood' - the summer home of the Boston Symphony Orchestra.

    The understated elegance of Wheatleigh together with their attention to detail is what makes this a hotel that should always be included in a New England driving trip.

    MASSACHUSETTS, NANTUCKET ISLAND, WHITE ELEPHANT HOTEL

    Nantucket Island is a perfect little seaside getaway in New England. A haven for boaters, and more recently for people seeking great restaurants, shops and places to hike and bike to. The island is a great spot for families with plenty to do. The White Elephant Hotel like to keep things simple and unfussy. Guests are encouraged to relax and take in the ocean air.

    Many people just visit Nantucket for the day so the atmosphere slows down in the evenings when people disappear back to the mainland. The island makes for a great alternative or in addition to Cape Cod. The hotel is quietly elegant, refreshingly simple, and comfortable.
