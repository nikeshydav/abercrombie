ADVENTUREOUS ESCAPE IN NORTH KIWI

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 Days / 6 Nights

    Highlights: Auckland - Bay Of Plenty: Rotorua, Tauranga - Lake Taupo

    The zeal to explore the extremes will attract you for an activity filled time at the North Island of New Zealand.

    A&K Advantages
    Get an adrenalin rush Jet boating at Auckland Harbour
    Spend the day on a kayak and walking tour of the famed Rangitoto Island
    Do the exhilarating Auckland Bridge climb
    Take a ride on the world’s only Shweeb - a human powered monorail racetrack
    Step onboard a custom built boat for an encounter with the dolphins
    Enjoy a scenic flight over the Tauranga region & the varied geothermal areas of the Rotorua
    Enjoy a cultural evening at Te Po
    Visit the famed Huka Falls, the most visited natural attraction in New Zealand
    Take a boat cruise to witness the Maori Rock Carvings around Lake Taupo
    Head to Tongariro National Park for the legendary Tongariro Alpine Crossing
    Stop at Bulli Point for some Cliff diving

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE AUCKLAND

    On arrival at Auckland, you will be met by a representative and privately transferred to your accommodation. Relax & unwind from the flight and post lunch be met by a guide for your private sightseeing of Auckland.

    The sightseeing tour will include a scenic drive along the waterfront, the quaint Victorian village of Parnell and panoramic views from the extinct volcano - Mount Eden. At the Auckland Museum see a world-renowned collection of Polynesian artifacts including a 25-metre long war canoe built in 1836. On conclusion you will return to your accommodation.

    Overnight: The Hilton or similar
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    AUCKLAND

    Enjoy breakfast and a late start to the morning. Head out towards the harbour for an adrenalin rush!

    Jet boating was invented in New Zealand so make the most of this with the experts! Step on board a jet boat & take a 35 minutes adrenalin pumping tour of the Auckland harbor with high speed spins and tricks! The supercharged jet boats are built to carry up to 24 passengers and depart hourly from the waterfront. Most trips pass under the harbour bridge and explore the harbour and is definitely a must do while in Auckland.

    Some time at your own pace to explore the city or you can drive to one of the amazing award winning winery for a wine tasting session followed with dinner.

    Overnight: The Hilton or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    AUCKLAND

    Come explore the landmark of the Auckland region and see Auckland city from another perspective. Spend the day on a kayak and walking tour of the famed Rangitoto Island.

    After your safety briefing and paddling instruction you will kayak across the Waitemata harbour, translates to 'sparkling waters', where you may see wildlife such as Little Blue Penguins and Cooks Petrels.

    On arrival at Rangitoto Island, which is the largest and youngest of Auckland’s volcanoes, you will start the leisurely walk to the summit lasting for about an hour. Upon reaching the top you will be treated to the finest 360 degree views that the Auckland region has to offer, making the trek totally worthwhile.

    At the return to the water’s edge, a healthy hearty kiwi lunch will be awaiting you, with cold and hot drinks to match! Enjoy the meal and area before heading back to the sheltered waters of St Helier's Bay.

    Overnight: The Hilton or similar
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 4

    AUCKLAND – BAY OF PLENTY (ROTORUA)

    Start your day at the crack of dawn & leave for another truly unique experience. Head out for the Auckland Bridge climb – the only way to truly appreciate the stunning & iconic Harbour Bridge! You could climb under; go over & around Auckland's most iconic landmarks. The climb takes around an hour and a half & is a personally guided tour. Get on top & enjoy the unbelievable 360 degree view of the city.

    At the end of the climb, if you still need more adrenaline rush, head for the Bungy Jump experience off the bridge! Located at a 10minute walk, meet the experts who will provide you with all the gear for you to make the most of this amazing experience!

    Enjoy breakfast and depart to Rotorua either on a private chauffeured vehicle or on a self-drive. The self-drive vehicle will be handed over to you at the hotel.

    Driving Distance: 74 kms approx.
    Duration: 1.5 hrs approx.

    You could either carry on with the chauffer guide or enjoy your self-drive to Rotorua and beyond. Check into your hotel & head for some more exciting activities!

    Agroventures Adventure Park offers five truly unique New Zealand adventures suitable for all ages and comfort levels .They have the world’s only Shweeb - a human powered monorail racetrack. Swing from 40m towards the earth at 130kmph on NZ’s one and only Swoop and the Southern Hemispheres’ only wind tunnel the Freefall Xtreme.

    Spend an hour or the entire day – guaranteed to give you an unforgettable experience and a sense of accomplishment that will remain for long after you have waved farewell.

    Overnight: Koura Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    ROTORUA – TAURANGA

    You have another early start to the day. Wake up early & drive for approximately an hour to the town of Tauranga for another experience of a lifetime – an encounter with the dolphins!

    Driving Distance: 64 kms approx.
    Duration: 1 hrs approx.

    The Bay of Plenty has an abundance of marine life. Step onboard a custom built boat for the sea safari that starts at 8am daily and the tour takes approximately 5hours to complete. Look out for dolphins, whales, orcas and more. You could either sit back & watch the marine life, or take a plunge in & swim with them in their natural habitat. Light snacks & refreshments will also be available during the tour.

    After this exhilarating experience, enjoy your lunch and later depart for a scenic flight over the region. Enjoy the views of the Tauranga region before heading over vast expanses of flourishing native and pine forests to the many and varied geothermal areas of the Rotorua. You could choose either a helicopter or a floatplane to view some of the most dramatic geothermal scenery & activity the destination has on offer.

    Rotorua is also the heartland of New Zealand's Maori culture, and you can head back to Rotorua visiting Te Po for a cultural evening experience. Enjoy a performance of song and dance, followed by food cooked in traditional hangi style (an earth oven). Hear stories of the past & experience an authentic Maori welcome ceremony followed for a truly traditional cultural experience.

    Overnight: Trinity Wharf Hotel or similar
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 5

    ROTORUA - LAKE TAUPO

    Check out of your hotel this morning & enjoy about an hour and a half’s scenic drive towards Lake Taupo.

    Driving Distance: 85 kms approx.
    Duration: 1.5 hrs approx.

    Check into your hotel at Lake Taupo & depart to visit the famed Huka Falls, the most visited natural attraction in New Zealand. The Waikato is New Zealand's longest river & it moves gracefully from Lake Taupo. Just before the Huka Falls it enters a shallow ravine of hard volcanic rock and the effect is simply magical! A foot bridge right at the top of the falls puts you in a prime position to get up close and witness the spectacular display of more than 220,000 liters of water blasting by every second.

    Post lunch; take a boat cruise to witness the Maori Rock Carvings around Lake Taupo. In the late 1970s master carver Matahi Whakataka-Brightwell had completed his 10-year training with Maori elders & came to Lake Taupo to mark the occasion with a significant carving. He took inspiration from Ngatoroirangi, a visionary Maori navigator who guided the Tuwharetoa and Te Arawa tribes to the Taupo area over a thousand years ago. Get up & close to some of these amazing rock art – the main carving being over 10 metres high and was completed over four long summers!

    This evening, perhaps enjoy a pre-dinner drink at the lodge & later enjoy a meal prepared with the freshest of ingredients organically grown around the lodge; overlooking the stunning lake & mountains.

    Overnight: Lake Taupo Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    LAKE TAUPO

    Head to Tongariro National Park for the legendary Tongariro Alpine Crossing! It is rated as ‘the best full day trek in New Zealand’ and listed by many as one of the 'top 10 day treks in the world'. Lonely Planet even said that a walk amidst the volcanoes of Tongariro is a life-changing experience not to be missed. The diverse and dramatic scenery and unique land forms are spectacular and you will experience some of the most scenic and volcanically active areas of the Tongariro National Park.

    Stop by Bulli Point for some Cliff diving, Lake Taupo style! Take the plunge into the fresh blue water -there are three ledges at Bulli Point with the highest being seven metres.

    Later this evening, maybe you could stop by the town center and enjoy some eclectic shopping or visit one of the quaint cafes that line the friendly streets.

    Overnight: Lake Taupo Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    LAKE TAUPO – AUCKLAND DEPARTURE

    Head to the airport at Lake Taupo for your short 1hr duration flight to Auckland to connect to for international departure or extend your stay & visit the South Island starting from Queenstown. The self-drive vehicle if opted for can be handed over at the airport to the representative.

    Meal Plan: B

BREATHTAKING GOLFING EXPERIENCE IN NORTH ISLAND

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 6 Days / 5 Nights

    Specially designed courses with stunning landscape and the most spectacular panoramic views will surely challenge your golfing skills.

    Highlights: Auckland – Kauri Cliffs, Bay of Islands

    A&K Advantage

    Enjoy an evening cruise along with Waitamata harbor
    Take a cruise around the Bay of Islands and watch the dolphins play
    Whisk off for a fishing experience on a fully equipped boat

    • Detailed Itinerary

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE AUCKLAND

    Auckland is an amazing spot for a round of golf with a comfortable climate and fresh air all around you.The region boasts of more golf courses than anywhere else in NZ. Enjoy your stay with a self-drive experience alongside.

    On arrival you will be assisted to your self-drive vehicle and assisted with check-in at your hotel

    Post lunch tee off at Auckland’s iconic golf course, The Grange Golf Club. The Grange is broadly known as one of New Zealand’s iconic golf courses which offers a brilliant assessment of golf skills and golfers are welcome from around the globe. Be it Amateur or Professional, this course has hosted every major Golf Tournament in New Zealand, including the New Zealand Open Championship on 8 occasions.

    A spectacular way to spend an evening is Auckland is aboard the yacht cruising along the Waitamata harbor. Relax on this dinner cruise enjoying the panoramic view of the city all lit up and beautiful. You may also lend a hand to the captain.

    Overnight: Mollies or similar
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    AUCKLAND

    This morning enjoy a game of golf at Royal Auckland Golf Club. This is a member’s only golf course and a special entry will be arranged for you (subject to confirmation).

    OR

    You may be delighted with a game of golf at the Gulf Harbour Country Club located in the Whangaparoa Peninsula at a distance of approximately an hour from main Auckland. The drive to country club is scenic and nothing short of spectacular. The beauty of the location, challenge and teeing off enjoyment are sure to awe you. This world-class golf course with challenging tees and greens and an area of 6,400 metre, par 72 meanders through native grasses and vegetation and has a natural look and feel from start to finish. The magnificent topography makes Gulf Harbour special and delight you with an amazing experience.

    Evening, relax in a luxurious spa treatment at your accommodation or visit a vineyard. Taste the amazing New Zealand wines produced at the vineyard along with dinner and you will also receive priceless information on wine pairing.

    Overnight: Mollies or similar
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 3

    AUCKLAND –BAY OF ISLANDS

    Driving Distance: 280 kms approx.
    Duration: 4.5 hrs approx.

    This morning take a scenic drive to the Kauri Cliff region for the most spectacular golfing experience. You will be assisted with check in to the hotel.

    Afternoon, head off for a teeing session at Kauri Cliffs. The relaxed and refined luxury lodge, Kauri Cliffs which is listed in the world’s top 100 resorts offers an 18 Hole Par 72 Championship Course. Kauri Cliffs golf course was designed and built by David Harman. Area of the course is 6,510 metres with five sets of tees to challenge every skill level. While golfing you enjoy stunning views since 15 holes view the Pacific Ocean, out of which 6 holes are played alongside cliffs plunging in to the sea. The inland holes wind through marsh, forest and farmland. It is ranked among the 100 Greatest Golf Courses in the World by Golf Magazines World Top 100 Course Ranking panel. Callaway ladies and gent’s clubs are available on rent.

    OR

    Take a break to unwind and relax over a luxurious spa session.

    Overnight: Kauri Cliffs or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    BAY OF ISLANDS

    Today the golf course for you to test your skill can be Waitangi Golf Club. This is an 18 Hole Par 70 Course, which is an exciting and ever changing challenge to the golfer and offers the most marvelous views of the sea and the island over the Bay of Islands, Russell. The stunning panoramic view stretches over the river and estuary over Waitangi and Opua extending further to the rolling bush and forest views and if that is not enough to please you the you can view ahead to the inland over Mount Bledisloe and Puketona.

    OR

    Carrington offers an 18 Hole Par 72 Championship Course and offers a variety of vistas to the golfer. The course delights you with sweeping ocean views to the north and east, and hill side vineyards of Karikari Estate to the south. The clubhouse and lounge complex provide outstanding views of the golf course, wetlands, beach and the ocean beyond owing to its location on a high ridge in the middle of the golf course. The championship standard course was designed by American golf course designer Matt Dye.

    Take a cruise around the Bay of Islands and watch the dolphins play around and spot some whales too. For an up-close experience you can dive in for a swim with wild dolphins.

    Overnight: Eagle’s Nest or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    BAY OF ISLANDS

    Drive to the Keri Keri Golf Club this morning and tee off at this 18 Hole Par 73 Championship Course and measuring 6101 metre in the beautiful Bay of Islands. The course has been designed to utilize all the amazing contours naturally sculpted by the Puketotara Stream and the Kerikeri River, which delivers beautiful views of the New Zealand native bush and birds.

    This afternoon, whisk off for a fishing experience on a fully equipped boat including fishing equipment of the highest quality. Your catch will be prepared to your liking ready to cook, or it can be smoked by our local professional smoker.

    Overnight: Eagle’s Nest or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    DEPART BAY OF ISLANDS – AUCKLAND

    Return to Auckland and handover your self-drive vehicle at the airport to the A&K Representative.

    Meal Plan: B

    • Possible Extensions:
    3 Days HAWKE’S BAY

  • Day 1

    AUCKLAND – HAWKE’S BAY

    Take a short flight of 1 hr duration to Napier Airport and on arrival you will be assisted to your self-drive vehicle.

    You will be assisted with check-in to your lodge. Enjoy this amazing experience of playing on New Zealand’s top golf course. This stunning course was designed by the legendary golf architect Tom Doak offering par 71 golf course with an area of 7,119 yards (6,510 meters). This course provides challenge to golfers of all skill levels. Cape Kidnappers currently holds the rank of the 41st best golf course in the world by Golf Magazine and is considered as one of the great modern wonders in the world of golf.

    Overnight: The Farm at Cape Kidnappers or similar
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    HAWKE’S BAY

    Be adventurous and depart for Hot Air Ballooning early this morning. Float over farmland, orchards and vineyards and appreciate the backdrop of distant mountain ranges. The flight takes place during the first few hours of daylight and is dependent on weather conditions.

    Just one game of golf at this amazing Cape Kidnappers golf course will never be enough. Enjoy another game of golf and challenge your golfing skills again.

    Afternoon can be well spent on a fishing session, since Hawke’s Bay offers a wide range of fishing opportunities. The bay's major rivers, the Mohaka, Tutkuir, Ngaruroro, Waipawa and Tukituki, are well populated with famed wild brown and rainbow trout.

    OR

    You may kayak on the stretch of one of the Cape Kidnappers’ river which are suitable for kayakers of varying skill levels. Enjoy an easy drift down a picturesque river with warm water, great swimming holes, abundance of bird life with a rural backdrop. Dock at a riverbank for a scenic sundowner.

    Overnight: The Farm at Cape Kidnappers or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    DEPART CAPE KIDNAPPERS – AUCKLAND

    Return to Auckland on a short flight and proceed for your connecting international leg of the return journey.


    Meal Plan: B

LUXURIOUS LODGES – NORTH ISLAND ON SELF DRIVE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 Days / 8 Nights

    Explore the scenic North Island of New Zealand on a self-drive or a chauffeur driven route and comfortable stays at luxurious lodges!

    Highlights:Auckland – Tauranga – Rotorua – Lake Taupo

    A&K Advantage
    - Private luxury self-drive vehicles or private chauffeured vehicle to take you on the scenic route.
    - Stay at luxury lodges
    - Private meals and vineyard visits
    - Special spots located for unforgettable memories
    - Exclusive spa arrangements at Te Aroha
    - Visit Rainbow Springs & take a tour through Kiwi Encounter
    - Helicopter ride the Tauranga region & the varied geothermal areas of the Rotorua towards White Island, the only active volcano
    - Enjoy a cultural evening at Te Po
    - Visit the famed Huka Falls, the most visited natural attraction in New Zealand
    - Take a boat cruise to witness the Maori Rock Carvings around Lake Taupo
    - Head to Tongariro National Park for the legendary Tongariro Alpine Crossing
    - Visit Bulli Point for an exhilarating Cliff diving experience, Lake Taupo style
    - Unwind at the Mollies lodge’s spa for a relaxing and luxurious experience
    - Sky city for 2 ultimate experiences of sky walk and sky jump
    - The Gulf of Hauraki offers amazing Whale and Dolphin Safari
    - Enjoy an evening dinner cruise along the Wickemata harbor

  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE AUCKLAND – Auckland Museum and Whale & Dolphin Safari

    On arrival you will be met by a representative and transferred privately to the hotel and be assisted with check-in.

    Unwind and relax from your lengthy flight. While at Auckland, you could visit the Auckland Museum that houses world-renowned collection of Polynesian artifacts.

    Explore the glittery pulsating, country’s largest city and commercial hub, Auckland

    The Gulf of Hauraki offers amazing Whale and Dolphin Safari. You may also visit the bustling seaside village of Devon Port with its beautiful small beaches and amazing views of the two volcanic cones.

    Mollies
    Mollies luxury boutique hotel is located just moments from Auckland's CBD in the gracious heritage suburb of St Mary's Bay.

    Private, spacious and welcoming, Mollies offers elegant accommodation, fine dining and impeccable service in a relaxed and intimate 5-star setting.

    Blending Victorian charm with bold contemporary art and bespoke furnishings, Mollies has earned a reputation as one of New Zealand's top places to stay. Perfect for that romantic retreat, honeymoon or luxury escape, it also makes a unique alternative to serviced apartments for extended breaks away.

    Each suite is peaceful and beautifully appointed, with sea, garden or city views. Guests can indulge in the day spa, relax in the lounge or cocktail bar, and stroll on the leafy grounds. For a change of pace, the designer boutiques and cafes of Ponsonby and Herne Bay are a short walk away.

    Overnight: Mollies
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    AUCKLAND – TAURANGA – Self-drive and visit Waitomo Glowworm Caves and Hobbiton

    You will receive your self-drive vehicle at the hotel on check-out. Start your day early as you enjoy your drive towards Tauranga.

    Enroute visit two world-famous visitor attractions; the famous Waitomo Glowworm Caves and Hobbiton Movie Set, made famous by the movie trilogies “The Lord of the Rings” and “The Hobbit”.

    Driving Distance: 170 kms
    Duration: 3 hrs approx.

    At Waitomo, you will experience the famous Glowworm Caves on a 45-minute tour. After lunch in Waitomo, we continue via the small country town of Cambridge to “The Shire’s Rest”, so you can have the wonderful opportunity to see the original Hobbiton Movie Set, which was the setting for Peter Jackson's "The Lord of the Rings" and "The Hobbit" movie trilogies.

    At the end of the tours, head towards Tauranga

    Driving Distance: 60 kms approx.
    Duration: 1 hr approx.

    The French Country House
    It is a luxurious farmhouse in the Bay of Plenty, New Zealand.

    If you are looking for boutique accommodation, a luxury weekend escape, or a romantic getaway, The French Country House in Tauranga the Bay of Plenty, New Zealand will enchant.

    The French Country House living areas are spacious, light and airy with a mixture of luxury antique furnishings and comfortable modern facilities. The views from the balcony over the Bay of Plenty countryside to the sea are spectacular. The French Country House is designed to offer individual privacy for romantic getaway weekends or corporate retreats with excellent access to activities in Mt. Maunganui and Tauranga.

    Overnight: The French Country House or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    TAURANGA

    The Bay of Plenty has an abundance of marine life. Step onboard a custom built boat for the sea safari that starts at 8am daily and the tour takes approximately 5 hours to complete. Look out for dolphins, whales, orcas and more. You could either sit back & watch the marine life, or take a plunge in & swim with them in their natural habitat. Light snacks & refreshments will also be available during the tour.

    Mt Maunganui is located at a short distance and offers the most stunning and spectacular views as you explore it on foot

    Overnight: The French Country House or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    TAURANGA - ROTORUA

    This morning depart for Rotorua.

    Driving Distance: 75 kms approx.
    Duration: 1.2 hr approx.

    On arrival you will be assisted with check-in.

    Take a scenic flight over the region to the White Island, the only active volcano. Disembark for a private guided tour of the islands crater floor and you will view active steam vents “fumaroles”, hot streams, vibrant sulphur formations and the steaming crater. You could choose either a helicopter or a floatplane to view some of the most dramatic geothermal scenery and the ever changing landscapes of this island.

    Rotorua is also the heartland of New Zealand's Maori culture, and you can head to Te Po for a cultural evening experience. Enjoy a performance of song and dance, followed by food cooked in traditional hangi style (an earth oven). Hear stories of the past & experience an authentic Maori welcome ceremony followed for a truly traditional cultural experience.

    Peppers on the Point
    Surrounded by the sparkling waters of Lake Rotorua, Peppers on the Point is an intimate luxury retreat featuring spectacular views to the historic Mokoia Island. This historic property was a family home right until 2004 and is perfect for a serene and tranquil escape.

    This stunning retreat offers the perfect base for experiencing New Zealand’s rich culture or you can simply relax and enjoy the tranquility of this stunning location from the comfort of our inviting entertainment rooms with open fireplaces.

    Peppers on the Point, Rotorua is the heartland of New Zealand’s Maori culture and is set amidst a landscape of erupting geysers, hot thermal springs and bubbling mud pools. With so many sights to see and cultural offerings within easy reach, we will have you immersed in relaxation upon your return.

    Overnight: Peppers on the Point or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    ROTORUA

    Head out for an exhilarating experience of river jet rides. Be it scenic or a thermal safari down the Waikato River through the Tutukau gorge. Also, experience the legendary Hamilton spins.

    Agroventures Adventure Park offers five truly unique New Zealand adventures suitable for all ages and comfort levels .They have the world’s only Shweeb - a human powered monorail racetrack. Swing from 40m towards the earth at 130kmph on NZ’s one and only Swoop and the Southern Hemispheres’ only wind tunnel the Freefall Xtreme.

    Spend an hour or the entire day – guaranteed to give you an unforgettable experience and a sense of accomplishment that will remain for long after you have waved farewell.

    OR

    Visit the Rainbow Springs Kiwi Wildlife Park for a guided tour with a Kiwi Encounter. It is a hatchery and nursery for the unique and treasured 'Kiwi' birds. Later you may enjoy a stroll through the magnificent Redwood Forest.

    Overnight: Peppers on the Point or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    ROTORUA – LAKE TAUPO

    Early morning depart for a scenic drive to Lake Taupo across the unusual volcanic topography and watch the pine forest plantations as you drive past.

    Driving Distance: 90 kms
    Duration: 1.5 hrs approx.

    On arrival you will be assisted with check-in to the lodge.

    Depart to visit the famed Huka Falls, the most natural attraction in New Zealand. For an adventurous twist you could explore kayaking or canoeing at the falls.

    Another sought after attraction is the Maori Rock Carvings around Lake Taupo. Take a boat cruise and appreciate the spectacular views of the carvings.

    Before the end of your day visit ‘Craters of the Moon’ – an active Geothermal Wonderland.

    Lake Taupo Lodge
    Relax, unwind and soak up the atmosphere in this lakeside retreat in the volcanic and fly fishing heartland of New Zealand.

    This award winning, luxury bed & breakfast lodge is a designer's delight. Inspired by Frank Lloyd Wright, it is set within magnificent landscaped gardens overlooking beautiful Lake Taupo and the mountains.

    Huka Lodge
    a world where elegant and generous hospitality meets sophistication and style - creating a welcoming haven, whose reputation and awards are richly deserved.

    Founded in the 1920s, Huka Lodge is considered to be the iconic New Zealand retreat. Sought by the world’s most discerning travellers – enjoying an unrivalled riverside location, offering superb hospitality and attentiveness as palpable as the force of the Waikato River itself.

    Overnight: Lake Taupo Lodge / Huka Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    LAKE TAUPO

    Take a walk amidst the volcanoes of Tongariro in Tongariro National Park for the legendary Alpine Crossing which is renowned for an unforgettable experience. It is one of the most popular activities of the region.

    OR

    If you are passionate about motor sport, you may visit Taupo’s Motorsport Park. This park is a state of the art race track, an international FIA category 2 race circuit and NZDRA certified drag strip. The facility which hosts a driver formula challenge also provides an opportunity to do the laps in a race car.

    On return visit Bulli Point for an exhilarating Cliff diving experience, Lake Taupo style! The Bulli Point offers three ledges, the highest being seven metres offering an amazing plunge into the fresh blue water.

    Overnight: Lake Taupo Lodge / Huka Lodge or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    LAKE TAUPO – AUCKLAND

    This morning enjoy fishing over a picnic lunch before you depart to Auckland.

    Driving Distance: 282 kms
    Duration: 3.5 hrs approx.

    OR

    Start your day early and depart to Auckland. On arrival settle in your hotels and head out to explore Ponsonby and Jervois Roads boutique stores at your pace. For an ultimate adrenaline rush visit the sky city for 2 ultimate experiences of sky walk and sky jump.

    On arrival at the hotel, unwind at the hotels spa for a relaxing and luxurious experience.

    A spectacular way to spend an evening is Auckland is aboard the yacht cruising along the Waitamata harbor. Relax on this dinner cruise enjoying the panoramic view of the city all lit up and beautiful. You may also lend a hand to the captain.

    Overnight: Mollies or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    AUCKLAND

    Depart for the Airport and hand over the self-drive vehicle to the A&K representative.

    Meal Plan: B

NEW ZEALAND – FOOD & WINE LOVER’S PARADISE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 13 Days / 12 Nights

    New Zealand’s award winning wines, delectable fresh food made with the freshest produce and enticing farmer’s markets will melt you in your knees.

    Highlights:Pegasus Bay – Waipara – Marlborough - Bay of Many Coves – Wellington – Hawkes’ Bay – Auckland

    A&K Advantage
    - Enjoy a gourmet lunch at this family-run winery
    - Visit the Waipara farmer’s market for an authentic feel of local produce like olive oils, venison, breads, vegetables, nuts and more
    - Taste the award-winning wines in a visit to the Mud House Wine Company located at the Waipara Springs
    - Head to Herzog for cooking Master class and kitchen confidential introduction
    - Visit the Moruara Island & spend a day cruising through the Queen Charlotte Sound eco-marine haven
    - Sail from Picton to Wellington across the Cook Strait
    - Appreciate tea in a Gong Fu Cha Private Tea Appreciation Workshop at Divine Kapitea
    - Ride the rapids of the scenic Otaki River under the careful eye of qualified guides
    - Explore the best local produce with a private guided food tour
    - Take a scenic helicopter ride over the spectacular Mount Hector
    - Visit the Te Awa Winery with a personal tour of the cellars
    - Visit the sky city for 2 ultimate experiences of sky walk and sky jump.
    - Enjoy an evening cruise along the Wickemata harbor

  • Detailed Itinerary

    Day 1

    ARRIVE CHRISTCHURCH – PEGASUS BAY, WAIPARA

    On arrival you will be met by an A&K representative who will assist you to your self-drive vehicle.

    Proceed to Pegasus Bay in the Canterbury region

    Driving Distance: 60 kms aprrox.
    Duration: 45 mins approx.

    Visit Pegasus Bay, the fine Waipara winery; a family-run winery with a reputation of region’s best wine and food. Relish a gourmet meal at this winery, which is amongst the top five wine producers nationally as per Robert Parker's buyer's guide and the restaurant was named Best Winery Restaurant in NZ by Cuisine magazine for the second consecutive year in 2009. The spread of wines available include award winning Rieslings, chardonnay, and pinot noir. Taste the wines while you admire the floor-to-ceiling stacks of oak aging casks.

    You will be introduced to local foods and wines to pair along. Later meet the wine makers, viticulturists and the owners of this spectacular vineyard.

    Overnight: Dry Paddocks Country Retreat or similar
    Meal Plan: L

  • Day 2

    WAIPARA

    Waipara Springs produce a range of wines that have consistently won national and international recognition. From Sauvignon blanc, Riesling and rich Chardonnay to intensely concentrated Pinot noir, Waipara Springs vineyards have been setting high benchmarks for New Zealand wineries.

    This morning, visit the Waipara farmer’s market. It is stocked with local produce like olive oils, venison, breads, vegetables, nuts and more.

    Later visit the Mud House Wine Company located at the Waipara Springs. Taste the award-winning wines as you enjoy the scenic views.

    Overnight: Dry Paddocks Country Retreat or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    WAIPARA – MARLBOROUGH

    Depart for the renowned wine producing region of Marlborough.

    Driving Distance: 264 kms approx.
    Duration: 3 hrs approx.

    Marlborough is New Zealand’s flagship wine region. Marlborough and Sauvignon Blanc are greatly responsible for placing New Zealand on the International wine scene. It is often referred to “Kei puta te Wairau” by the locals, which translates to “The place with a hole in the cloud” and is one of the country’s driest region. The right combination of cool yet a heavily sunny climate, with low rainfall, moderately fertile soil and free draining contribute to variety of the available wines.

    You will be met by an A&K Representative and assisted with check-in.

    A representative will further accompany you for the vineyard visits in this region to introduce you to the famed and award winning wineries.

    Hunters Wine
    An original family owned winery established more 3 decades ago, it is an award winner in the field of wines, its owner’s entrepreneurship and for it’s beautiful landscape.

    Neudorf Vineyards
    You will be introduced to the different wines matured at the vineyard at the cellar door experience at this vineyard. This winery is a result of a persistent effort by its owners for over 3 decades and produces award winning wines

    Herzog
    Herzog is another family owned New Zealand winery and restaurant. The passion for wine and food along with a commitment to excellence and quality has been the driving force behind this amazing winery.

    Continue the wine tasting into the evening and pair your favoured wines with local delicious food items in an award winning restaurant.

    Overnight: Lake Timara, Vineyard Cottage or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    MARLBOROUGH

    This morning you will be assisted by an A&K representative to wine estates. Highfield Estates being one of them.

    Return to Herzog for a Master cooking class and an introduction to the kitchen confidential. You will also be educated on wine pairings with the meal you learn.

    Overnight: Lake Timara, Vineyard Cottage or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    MARLBOROUGH – BAY OF MANY COVES

    Drive to the region of Bay of Many Coves and hand over the vehicle to the representative.

    Driving Distance: 33 kms approx.
    Duration: 30 mins approx.

    Hop on a private chartered yacht for a short ride (30 mins) to Bay of Many Coves, which offers some exciting water based activities and experiences, like swimming with the dolphins, kayaking and motor yacht cruising.

    Visit the Moruara Island to spend a day cruising through the Queen Charlotte Sound eco-marine haven. It is abundant in sea animals like seals, dolphins, penguins, gannets and shearwaters. The surrounding Sound also has a resident pod of Hector’s Dolphins, which are thought to be world’s rarest marine dolphin.

    Savour the delights of fresh local seafood and produce and enjoy the local delicacies (fresh seafood, wild meats, home-crafted and creative cuisine).

    Overnight: On board Private Yacht
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    BAY OF MANY COVES – WELLINGTON

    Sail from Picton to Wellington across the Cook Strait. The Cook Strait journey is amazing and the most unforgettable, as you come across some of the world’s most breathtaking scenery and wildlife.

    On arrival you will be assisted to your self-drive vehicle and later for check-in to the hotel.

    Appreciate tea in a Gong Fu Cha Private Tea Appreciation Workshop at Divine Kapitea, followed with an oriental High Tea indulgence. “Gong Fu Cha” is the ancient art of Chinese tea brewing experience, which has highly therapeutic effects in a relaxing ambience taking tea appreciation to a whole new level.

    Ride the rapids of the scenic Otaki River under the careful eye of qualified guides. There is enough for all to experience adventure at different levels, from taking control of a raft, swimming the rapids, jumping off a rock, experiencing the waterfall in all its glory, to simply relaxing and enjoying the view. To add to a twist to this experience you could opt for night rafting, where you will be guided by the glow worms that line the banks and the sounds of the approaching rapids.

    Overnight: Greenmantle Estate or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    WELLINGTON

    You will spend the day on a private guided food tour. Explore the best local produce, visiting well known coffee makers, chocolate tasting and relish lunch at one of the renowned restaurants.

    Take a scenic helicopter ride over the spectacular Mount Hector. During the winter season it’s often snow capped at an altitude of 5000ft. Listen to informative lores of the Tararua Ranges of how skiers would tramp from the Otaki Forks to ski the slopes near Kime Hut during the 1920s & 1930s.

    Visit the Te Papa Museum and the Weta Workshop. Weta workshop is a conceptual design and physical manufacturing facility for the creative and the entertainment world. It is an award winning company famed for the costumes of Lord of the Rings.

    Overnight: Greenmantle Estate or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    WELLINGTON – HAWKES BAY

    This morning you will take a short 55 mins flight to Napier Airport. On arrival you will be privately transferred to your hotel and assisted with check-in.

    The Hawke's Bay region offers a wealth of exciting Food & Wine excursions. It is New Zealand’s second largest wine growing region and home to over 70 wineries. Attend wine tasting, wine appreciate, food and wine pairing sessions to increase your knowledge on this field.

    Hawke’s Bay is privileged with two of the most popular markets in the country. Black Barn Grower’s Market which is organized every Saturday morning over summer and the Hawke’s Bay Farmer’s Market where you could find the freshest produce every Sunday morning.

    Visit the Te Awa Winery with a personal tour of the cellars, private tasting and followed by a three course lunch.

    Hawke’s Bay region is rich with bounty from the land, sea and rivers owing to the Mediterranean climate. On a food tour you have the opportunity to meet the local producers of cheese, olive oils and orchard fruit and taste their products.

    Overnight: Farm at Cape Kidnappers or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    HAWKES BAY

    This morning head off for the vineyard visits.

    Te Mata Estate, is one of New Zealand's top wineries producing the classic Hawke's Bay red. Since it is made in small quantities, a chance to taste it should never be missed.

    Mission Estate Winery is located in the Taradale hills overlooking Napier. It is the country's oldest winery, dating back to 1851 and produces award-winning wines, including the Mission Jewelstone range which can be bought or tasted at the cellar door. You could enjoy a meal with a seat on the terrace which provides a spectacular view of the vineyard and Napier.

    Back at the Farm at Cape Kidnappers, you could experience a quad bike tour of the 6,000 acre working sheep and cattle farm.

    Overnight: Farm at Cape Kidnappers or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    HAWKES BAY

    Locate the Kiwi’s habitat this morning as you walk along with an experienced guide from Environmental Services. It is a fun experience to identify and explore the shy bird’s habitat.

    Craggy Range Winery, is located beside a small lake with the towering heights of Te Mata Peak beyond. This vineyard offers an amazing milieu for wine making and tasting.

    Afternoon can be well spent on a fishing session, since Hawke’s Bay offers a wide range of fishing opportunities. The bay's major rivers, the Mohaka, Tutkuir, Ngaruroro, Waipawa and Tukituki, are well populated with famed wild brown and rainbow trout.

    Overnight: Farm at Cape Kidnappers or Similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    HAWKES BAY – AUCKLAND

    Depart for a short flight of 1 hr 5 mins from Napier Bay to Auckland.

    Auckland is home to about 100 vineyards and wineries, known for rich, Bordeaux style reds, as well as the boutique vineyards on Waiheke Island.

    Artisan Wines is the producer of high-quality wine using grapes sourced from around New Zealand. Appreciate excellent wine and food in the serene beauty of Oratia Valley as you taste award-winning Auckland wines in the boutique winery. Saturday is a good day because a farmers' market is organized on the grounds of this vineyard. You can try a wide range of Artisan wines alongside local produce, much of it being organic.

    Babich Wines, is a family owned vineyard, which been making wine in New Zealand for nearly 100 years beginning first in the far north where Josip Babich joined his brothers from Croatia and planted grapes near the gum fields. The Henderson cellar site has a range of tastings and snacks. The 72-acre site is unique; years ago it was amid farmland but now is almost surrounded by houses as the population in west Auckland has grown.

    Overnight: Hotel De Brett or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 12

    AUCKLAND

    Explore Ponsonby and Jervois Roads at your pace or for an ultimate rush visit the sky city for 2 ultimate experiences of sky walk and sky jump.

    This afternoon, visit the Villa Maria Winery, which has grown from a small company founded by the Fistonich family to one of the country's biggest producers. It is renowned for its consistency, and depending on your time of travel you could enjoy one of the outdoor music concerts hosted during summer. The tasting room offers selections from most of the company's vineyards.

    A spectacular way to spend an evening is Auckland is aboard the yacht cruising along with Wickemata harbor. Relax on this dinner cruise enjoying the panoramic view of the city all lit up and beautiful. You may also lend a hand to the captain.

    Overnight: Hotel De Brett or similar
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 13

    DEPART AUCKLAND

    You will be privately transferred to the Airport to board your international flight.

    Meal Plan: B
