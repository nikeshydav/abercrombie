New Zealand Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Auckland

    Auckland brims with natural beauty and sightseeing attractions making it one of the most visited cities in New Zealand. An amazing city boasting of an urban environment where everyone lives within half an hour of beautiful beaches, hiking trails and a dozen enchanted holiday islands. New Zealand's most diverse city features a vibrant Polynesian culture, sun drenched sidewalks & parks, buzzing shopping centers and a range of fine dining restaurants and bars.

    Auckland is surrounded by volcanic hills and is home to twin harbors that render it the title of "The City of Sails"; Waitemata on the Pacific Ocean and Manukau by the Tasman Sea. And, Auckland lives upto its name having the highest number of boats per capita in the world. As you're never too far from water in Auckland you can take a relaxing harbor cruise, a fishing charter, whale and dolphin spotting, kayaking or surfing. Also take a walk through the Aukland Harbour Bridge to observe the country with a different view. Built in 1959 as a four-lane highway, heavy traffic took its charm away. However, a Japanese firm has brought it back to life with renovations which is why it is now jokingly referred to as the 'Nippon Clip-on' because of this extension.

    When not in water, you can pay a visit to the prolific Auckland War Memorial Museum, stroll along the Auckland Harbour Bridge and the Sky Tower, the tallest tower in the Southern Hemisphere standing at an astounding 328 meters.

    Rotorua

    The perfect place for geologists, Rotorua meaning "the second great lake of Kahumatamomoe" in Maori is nestled along the shore of the lake with the same name. Its natural geothermal reserves such as the Whakarewarewa are the main attraction for tourists in the region along with its 17 lakes, collectively known as Lakes of Rotorua. The region also showcases the unique and different culture of the Maoris. The White Island, dating back to 150,000 to 200,000 years, is the oldest, most active and live volcanoes to be found in the country.

    Because of the high presence of natural geysers, hot bubbling mud pools, hot thermal springs and the Buried Village the city has been dubbed as the Sulphur City and certainly makes up for one of the best sightseeing in New Zealand.

    Queenstown

    Queenstown is one of New Zealand's most visited towns and also a known haven for adrenalin junkies. Often called the 'adventure capital' of the world, its lake and mountain landscape make it a hub for adventure sports such as hiking, bungee jumping, sky diving, canyon swinging, jet boating, horse trekking and river rafting all year round. Located on the South Island, it features the great Four Season Alpine and lake resorts for travelers.

    Stunning natural landscapes of the Remarkables Mountains, Lake Wakatipu and the charming Arrowtown characterize Queensland. If you're more of lounging kind then there are options galore with luxurious lake resorts, where one can indulge with spa treatments and boutique shopping and excellent food and wine. During winters you can ski or go snowboarding while other adventure activities include caving, rafting, sledging, bungy-jumping, jetboating, skydiving and hand gliding. While you are there do not miss out on the historical beauty of Arrowtown!

    The Bay of Islands

    One of the most exciting Tourist places in New Zealand is The Bay of Islands, a stunning maritime park comprising of about 144 islands and bays rich with marine life. You can spot aquatic mammals such as whales, dolphins, and seals. While you are there visit KeriKeri which features the old and famous KeriKeri Mission Station and Stone Store built in the early 1800's which makes it New Zealand's oldest European style architecture.

    Get up and close with the dolphins in Paihia or look into the fames 'Hole in the Rock' i.e. Piercy Island! Close by is Cape Brett where you can admire the 360 m high lighthouse. Also make a stopover in Russell, a former capital of the country and a sea port which saw one of the earliest European settlements. It was notorious as the 'Hell Hole of the Pacific' as well as was an active whaling station during the colonial times.

    Cape Reinga at the northernmost tip of the country overlooks the dissection between Pacific Ocean and the Tasman Sea. A Maori legend is that it is from this fissure between the east and the west that the dead spirits embark upon their afterlife journey.

    Napier

    Napier is a city that had to rebuild itself from the scratch after a destructive earthquake measuring 7.9 on Richter scale in 1931. Rejuvenated with the skillful Art Deco architecture, Napier transformed itself completely after the two and a half minutes of total annihilation!

    Today the city is decked with a marvelous collection of lovely structures and every February Napier celebrates Art Deco weekend. During your visit do not forget to savor the finest wines from the local vineyards along with the local delicious cuisine.

    Christchurch

    Christchurch is the largest city in New Zealand's southern island and one of those cities where the new age cosmopolitanism rubs shoulders with the colonial charms. Sometime in 1850, English settlers arrived and made this beautiful 'Garden City' their home. The city is dotted with various gardens, horticulture displays, walking trails offering much peace and tranquility.

    Christchurch is brimming with art and culture with theatres, art galleries, live concerts, and festivals galore. Christchurch Cathedral, is a significant landmark here and one of the must visit places along with the Christchurch Gandola, Botanical Gardens, International Antarctic Centre and Neo- Gothic Arts Centre. Christchurch tramway system has been a major tourist attraction although has been out of operation since the earthquake in February 2011.

    Nelson & Abel Tasman National Park

    Nelson, situated at the eastern shores of Tasman Bay is the second oldest settled city of New Zealand. Proclaimed a city by the royal charter in 1858, it is named in the honor of the British Admiral Lord Nelson. Teeming with natural beauty, this city is often regarded as a paradise on earth making it a must visit. The region is a haven for ecotourism as well as adventure tourism thanks to its natural landscapes as also a gateway to the Abel Tasman National Park.

    Abel Tasman National Park has abundant foliage, hilly countryside, valleys streaming with rivers, and scenic coastlines. Visitors here have plenty of natural wonders to devour in a year round pleasant temperature. One can lounge around on the beaches enjoying the glistening sun, or camp around, go kayaking, or simply enjoy sightseeing.
