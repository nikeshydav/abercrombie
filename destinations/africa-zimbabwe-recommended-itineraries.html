Wings over Zambia & Zimbabwe

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 Nights

    A safari in wild Africa. Prolific wildlife. Up close with nature on walking safaris. Mana Pools National Park in the heart of the Zambezi Valley. Water-based safari activities.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Johannesburg

    An A&K greeting on arrival into the safari gateway of Southern Africa, and assistance to your airport hotel.

    Overnight: InterContinental Johannesburg O.R. Tambo (Standard Room)

  • Day 2

    Johannesburg - Lusaka - South Luangwa National Park

    This morning, return independently to the international terminal for your flight* north to Lusaka, Zambia. A connecting light aircraft flight takes you further north to Mfuwe and the spectacular South Luangwa National Park. The next part of your adventure begins with an afternoon game drive

    Overnight: Sanctuary Puku Ridge Tented Camp (Safari Tent)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3 - 4

    South Luangwa

    You now have two days to explore the South Luangwa. Rise before dawn for a morning game drive: your safari guide will introduce you to this superb park and deliver some exciting photographic opportunities. Elephant, lion, wild dog, buffalo and a wide variety of antelope reside in this area, as does the regal leopard. Keep a lookout for the South Luangwa's endemic species, Thornicroft's giraffe and Cookson's wildebeest, and drive along the Luangwa River to see the vibrant carmine bee-eaters nesting in the riverbank walls; take an afternoon safari drive with sundowners, and a thrilling night drive; or, as the South Luangwa is 'home' of the walking safari, head out on foot with your armed guide to see the bush from a different perspective

    Overnight: Sanctuary Puku Ridge Tented Camp (Safari Tent)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    South Luangwa - Lusaka - Mana Pools National Park

    A light aircraft flight takes you from Mfuwe via Lusaka to Mana Pools National Park in Zimbabwe. On the middle reaches of the Zambezi River, a hundred kilometres below Lake Kariba is the Mana Pools National Park situated in the heart of the Zambezi Valley. Ringed by the far distant blue hills of the escarpment, this area is bursting with a profusion of birds and animals, especially during dry season from June to October, and it is Zimbabwe's second world heritage site

    Overnight: Ruckomechi Camp (Tented Unit)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6 - 7

    Mana Pools National Park

    Ruckomechi Camp lies on the banks of the Zambezi, shaded by a large grove of acacia and mahogany trees and with a superb view of the mountains of Africa's Great Rift Valley across the river in Zambia. Two full days at the camp to discover the fascinating surrounds. The area is renowned for large numbers of elephant, buffalo, hippo and eland, especially in the winter when they concentrate along the river. Predators such as lion, leopard and wild dog are also found. Birdlife is superb, particularly for both mopane woodland and riverine species, with numerous local species like Collared Palm-Thrush, Racket-tailed Roller, Purple-banded Sunbird and Black-throated Wattle-Eyes. Activities include wildlife viewing in open 4WD vehicles on motorised pontoon boats, on foot and in canoes

    Overnight: Ruckomechi Camp (Tented Unit)
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    Mana Pools National Park - Lusaka

    A good-bye from A&K as you end your safari with a flight to Lusaka airport

    Meal Plan: B

Classic Zimbabwe Safari

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 days
    Highlights:Victoria Falls, Hwange National Park, Lake Kariba

    Experience the awesome power of nature in comfort with this eight day itinerary giving you the best Zimbabwe has to offer.

  • Day 1

    Johannesburg / Victoria Falls

    Arrive Johannesburg.

    Connecting flight to Victoria Falls.

    Meet and greet transfer to your hotel.

    Experience luxury and hospitality that only the Stanley & Livingstone at Victoria Falls can offer. This is a hotel for the discerning traveller seeking true grace and style. Accommodation is in exquisitely decorated suites overlooking a waterhole frequented by a variety of game. The hotel is located on the 6,000 acre Nakavango private reserve on the outskirts of Victoria Falls – a stone’s throw from “Mosi – oa- Tunya” the smoke that thunders. This small luxury hotel is a grand experience fit for royalty.

    Evening sundowner cruise.

    Unwind at the end of the day on board a cruise boat on the mighty Zambezi River. The sundowner cruise takes you through some of the channels of the river, offering an opportunity to see animals such as hippopotamus, elephant, crocodile as well as a variety of birdlife that inhabit the riverine vegetation.

    A selection of drinks and snacks are served.

    Overnight: Stanley & Livingstone Hotel
    Meal Plan:D

  • Day 2

    Victoria Falls

    Guided Tour of the mighty Victoria Falls.

    Wander through the rainforest and view the largest curtain of falling water from a number of panoramic viewing points. The evergreen rainforest is home to monkeys, baboons and other small animals such as the bushbuck. The Falls themselves vary in height from the Devil’s Cataract being the lowest at 70 metres high to the Rainbow Falls at 108 metres. The Falls are split into five sections namely the Devil’s Cataract, Main Falls, Horseshoe Falls, Rainbow Falls and the Eastern Cataract which make up this world wonder which is also a World Heritage Site.

    Afternoon at leisure for optional activities.

    There are a variety of activities to choose from our tour menu ranging from soft to hard adventure options.

    Suggestions include Flight of Angels (scenic flight over the Falls by helicopter), white water rafting, bunji jumping, canoeing, elephant back safaris, lion walks, traditional village tours among others.

    Overnight: Stanley & Livingstone Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 3

    Victoria Falls / Hwange National Park

    Charter flight or road transfer to Hwange National Park.

    The charter flight will take approximately 30 minutes, while a road transfer takes approximately two and a half hours.

    The largest of Zimbabwe's eleven national parks, Hwange National Park offers unrivalled game viewing opportunities. Home to the Presidential herd of elephant, plains game, buffalo, and big cats this park is a wildlife enthusiast's haven.

    Situated in one of Hwange National Park’s most outstanding locations, bordering ancient elephant paths and a vast grassland corridor, is The Hide. Herds of elephant, buffalo and a myriad of other game are drawn to the constant waters of the Hide’s waterhole, and the spectacle of great predators making a sudden appearance is not uncommon.

    Overnight: The Hide
    Meal Plan: L,D

  • Day 4

    Hwange National Park

    Morning and afternoon game drives.

    Take a game drive through the park where you will come face to face with lions, elephants, giraffes and many other game species. Other activities include walking safaris escorted by a professional guide, and night game drives on the private concession which offers a thrilling chance to see leopard and other nocturnal species.

    Overnight: The Hide
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Hwange National Park

    Morning and afternoon game activities.

    Overnight:The Hide
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Hwange / Lake Kariba

    Charter flight to Bumi Hills airstrip at Lake Kariba.

    The duration of the flight is approximately 45 minutes.

    Bumi Hills Safari Lodge - the gem of Lake Kariba is set in a unique wilderness area overlooking the waters of the lake. Great game viewing opportunities from both land and water and a chance to catch the illusive tiger fish for those who are up to the challenge.

    Overnight:Bumi Hills Safari Lodge
    Meal Plan: L,D

  • Day 7

    Lake Kariba

    Morning and afternoon game activities.

    Game viewing options include game drives along the shoreline, fishing
    and game viewing by boat. In addition to game viewing activities, the
    lodge also offers cultural tours in the form of visits to a nearby village in the Omay Communal lands.

    Overnight:Bumi Hills Safari Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    Kariba / Victoria Falls / Johannesburg

    Morning charter flight to Victoria Falls airport.

    The flying time is approximately one and a half hours.

    Connecting flight to Johannesburg.

    Optional - South African extension.

    Connecting flight to outbound international destinations.

    Meal Plan: B

Ultimate Zimbabwe

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 days
    Highlights:Harare, The Great Zimbabwe Ruins, Matobo Hills National Park, Hwange National Park

    A diverse itinerary of nature, culture and history combined into 10 days for an epic Zimbabwe experience.

  • Day 1

    Johannesburg to Harare

    Meet & greet then transfer to your hotel.

    One of the Leading Hotels of the world, this hotel offers five star luxury and comfort in Harare. Located in the central business and shopping districts of the city, overlooking the Africa Unity Square and colourful flower markets the hotel is a great choice for the discerning traveller. Facilities at the hotel include a beauty centre with sauna, swimming pool, gym, a number of restaurants including the award winning La Fontaine Grill Room and is less than 5km from three top golf courses.

    Afternoon city tour.

    Harare is a city of contrasts – combining the old and the new. The new section of the city is characterized by high rise buildings, tree-lined avenues while the older section of the town boasts buildings dating back to the early 1900s. Drive through the city and see the various landmarks such as the Parliament Buildings, the Supreme Court, Africa Unity Square (the historic place where the Union Jack Flag was raised by the first settlers), the Kopjie and finally a traditional African market.

    Overnight:Meikles Hotel
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    Harare to Great Zimbabwe

    After breakfast depart by coach for a three hour drive to the south of the capital Harare to Masvingo.

    Arrive at your hotel.

    Situated 5 minutes drive from the Great Zimbabwe Ruins is the Lodge at the Ancient City. You will be accommodated in spectacular stone and thatch lodges set on granite outcrops with huge boulders forming an integral part of the overall architecture. The lounge area offers views across a valley to the Great Enclosure of the Ruins.

    Afternoon visit to the Great Zimbabwe Ruins - evidence of a bygone dynasty that lived in this area. Wander through the mystical ruins and view artefacts in the museum which depict the way of life of the inhabitants of this National Monument. The ruins have been dated back to 1200 AD.

    Overnight:Lodge at Ancient city
    Meal Plan: L,D

  • Day 3

    Great Zimbabwe to Bulawayo

    Continue to Bulawayo, the capital of Matabeleland Province, and the second largest city in Zimbabwe. The drive from Great Zimbabwe is approximately three and a half hours.

    Some of the most majestic granite scenery in the world is found in the Matobo Hills. Nestled in this unique range of hills is Camp Amalinda. This lodge has gained an excellent reputation for its African hospitality and bushveld charm. The lodge consists of individually thatched suites carved into the huge boulders.

    Facilities include a swimming pool, safari spa, library, boma and a natural cave wine cellar for the wine enthusiasts.

    After lunch visit the Matobo Hills National Park, home to large granite boulder outcrops (balancing rocks), historic bushman paintings, the grave of Cecil John Rhodes - the founder of Rhodesia (now Zimbabwe). The park is also home to the elusive rhinoceros as well as other plains' game.

    Other activities that are available include tracking Black and White Rhino, horse-riding, walking and hiking and night game drives.

    Overnight:Camp Amalinda
    Meal Plan: L,D

  • Day 4

    Bulawayo to Hwange National Park

    After breakfast, set off north through the rural areas towards Hwange National Park a driving time of approximately 3 hours.

    The largest of Zimbabwe's eleven national parks, Hwange National Park offers unrivalled game viewing opportunities. Home to the Presidential herd of elephant, plains game, buffalo, and big cats this park is a wildlife enthusiast's haven.

    Situated in one of Hwange National Park’s most outstanding locations, bordering ancient elephant paths and a vast grassland corridor, is The Hide.

    Herds of elephant, buffalo and a myriad of other game are drawn to the constant waters of The Hide’s waterhole, and the spectacle of great predators making a sudden appearance is not uncommon.

    Overnight:The Hide
    Meal Plan: L,D

  • Day 5

    Hwange National Park

    Morning and afternoon game drives.

    Take a game drive through the park where you will come face to face with lions, elephants, giraffes and many other game species. Other activities include walking safaris escorted by a professional guide, and night game drives on the private concession which offers a thrilling chance to see leopard and other nocturnal species.

    Overnight:The Hide
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Hwange National Park

    Enjoy morning and afternoon game activities.

    Overnight:The Hide
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Hwange National Park to Victoria Falls

    Depart for Victoria Falls - a drive of approximately two and a half hours from Hwange National park.

    Evening sundowner cruise on the mighty Zambezi River.

    Unwind at the end of the day on board a cruise boat on the mighty Zambezi River. The sundowner cruise takes you through some of the channels of the river, offering an opportunity to see animals such as ippopotamus, elephant, crocodile as well as a variety of birdlife that inhabit the riverine vegetation.

    A selection of drinks & snacks are served.

    Overnight:Stanley & Livingstone
    Meal Plan: L,D

  • Day 8

    Victoria Falls

    Guided Tour of the mighty Victoria Falls. Wander through the rainforest and view the largest curtain of falling water from a number of panoramic viewing points. The evergreen rainforest is home to monkeys, baboons and other small animals such as the bushbuck. The Falls themselves vary in height from the Devil’s Cataract being the lowest at 70 metres high to the Rainbow Falls at 108 metres. The Falls are split into 5 sections namely the Devil’s Cataract, Main Falls, Horseshoe Falls, Rainbow Falls and the Eastern Cataract which make up this world wonder which is also a World Heritage Site.

    Afternoon at leisure for optional activities.

    There are a variety of activities to choose from our tour menu ranging from soft to hard adventure options.

    Suggestions include Flight of Angels (scenic flight over the Falls by helicopter), white water rafting, bunji jumping, canoeing, elephant back safaris, lion walks and traditional village tours among others.

    Overnight:Stanley & Livingstone
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 9

    Victoria Falls

    Village tour - visit an authentic traditional village on the outskirts of Victoria Falls and have an opportunity to see firsthand the way of life of some of the ethnic groups and learn about their customs & culture. You will also have the opportunity to buy some local handicrafts made in the village.

    Afternoon at leisure for optional activities.

    Overnight:Stanley & Livingstone
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 10

    Victoria Falls to Johannesburg

    Transfer to Victoria Falls airport.

    Depart Victoria Falls and fly to Johannesburg.

    Connect to outbound international flights.

    Meal Plan: B
