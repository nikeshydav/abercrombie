SUGGESTED BRITISH COLUMBIA ACCOMMODATION

  • CLAYOQUOT WILDERNESS RESORT

    Clayoquot Wilderness Resort offers a picture-postcard slice of outdoors adventure Canada. Located on Clayoquot Sound on the western side of Vancouver Island, the resort can only be accessed by chartered seaplane. Clayoquot mirrors the luxury you would expect from a safari lodge in Africa. 20 opulent tents built on stilts overlooking the water provide the ultimate in exclusivity. This year stylish new bathrooms have been added to each tent, creating the ultimate in 'glamping' whilst maintaining the strict environmental standards to which the resort adheres.

    The resort best suits families and couples looking for activities and adventure. The area is a haven for wildlife lovers. Clayoquot Sound is home to black bears, wolves, cougars, grey whales, orcas, porpoises, seals, river otters, elk and bald eagles. The guides will introduce families to the animals and explain their feeding and migratory patterns. Each evening guests arrange their activities for the next day with John the General Manager - kayaking, horse riding, deep sea fishing, archery, zip lining, cooking lessons, and many more, are all included.

    Clayoquot also specialises in adult pampering with a full spa (our guests receive a complimentary treatment) and sensational fine dining, with unique wine pairings. The tents are nestled in temperate rainforest offering romantic privacy and most activities can be arranged exclusively.

    FAIRMONT CHATEAU LAKE LOUISE

    The Fairmont Chateau Whistler is located at the base of Blackcomb Mountain in Whistler, British Columbia. Classicly elegant rooms and suites have amazing mountain or village views and are created to blend with the natural alpine setting.

    FAIRMONT PACIFIC RIM, VANCOUVER

    The Fairmont Pacific Rim is the new luxury hotel to greet Vancouver. The hotel forms part of a giant glass tower - part residential, part gleaming hotel. Upon our visit we found a vibrant bar scene located in the open plan area beside the lobby and its giant fireplace. Its easy to see why locals come to the Pacific Rim for an evening drink, the hotel is right on the water and offers sensational views over Vancouver's bustling harbour and Stanley Park.

    - Make use of the outdoor hot tubs after a massage at the spa, very unusual for a major city.
    - Take a jog from the hotel along the waterfront and around Stanley Park
    - Watch the seaplanes landing and departing right in front of your room

    HASTINGS HOUSE

    Whilst you might you have thought you weren't looking for a little piece of England when heading off on a long-haul holiday, Hastings House offers effortless Canadian laid-back friendly luxury in the style of an English 'Sussex' home. This country resort is on Salt Spring Island - which is sandwiched between Vancouver Island and the mainland.

    The focus is on fine dining, wellness and relaxation all with views of the stunning Ganges Harbour. This is a sublime, kick back sort of place designed for you to take things in, enjoy a book and a glass of wine.

    KING PACIFIC LODGE

    From the moment you take off on your amazing float plane journey through the inside passage you'll know you are going somewhere special. The Great Bear Rainforest on the west coast of British Columbia is true wilderness. Here, in a remote location, King Pacific Lodge sits on a giant floating barge - 17 spectacular rooms and suites. This has to be the most luxurious floating hotel in the wilderness anywhere.

    Guests come to King Pacific to experience one of the few genuine untouched corners of the world, to experience the outdoors and to experience all of the wildlife viewing opportunities in the area. Your package includes all meals, transportation and activities, making planning easy and fun. Try your hand at ocean fishing, guided fly-fishing, luxury hiking and even heli-hiking, wilderness kayaking and wildlife viewing trips. For meals you generally sit at group tables. Part of the charm is getting to know the other guests sitting at mealtimes. Everything is based around local ingredients and is truly amazing.

    NIMMO BAY

    Nimmo Bay is unique. It must be one of only a few places where each day you experience the wonders of Canada with your own helicopter and pilot for you to explore. Departing from the stunning lodge, the guides will take you to glaciers, mountain tops, old growth forests, white sand beaches, waterfalls and pristine lakes. Each day is completely different as you discover remote islands, hot springs, isolated rivers, and cultural museums.

    The opportunities to view the wildlife are constant and varied. A ride in your own private helicopter is truly the most exciting way to experience British Columbia's central interior. Families will love the amazing 'magic carpet rides' that will take you to a world of adventure in just minutes.

    The resort is a unique retreat offering wildlife, fishing, kayaking, rock climbing and hiking. Massage and yoga are available. Evenings are spent around the evening fire pit. Each day away in the helicopter comes complete with a deluxe gourmet spread. The helicopter stays with you all day to ensure a secure, secluded and serene wilderness experience. The chalets are homely and the meals hearty.

    PACIFIC YELLOWFIN CRUISE

    The remote inlets and miles of undisturbed coastline make British Columbia the perfect place for small-ship cruising. We offer the most luxurious luxury boat cruises in British Columbia on the Pacific Yellowfin - a fully restored coastal yacht originally built during the Second World War.

    Exquisite food, fine wine and a wonderful décor are all hallmarks of Pacific Yellowfin. The staterooms are magnificent and the ship comes equipped with the best in equipment to make the most of the outdoors.

    The routes are flexible and tailor made, but itineraries specialise in Desolation Sound, the Gulf islands and Great Bear Rainforest - all stunning places to explore. For something longer a trip from Vancouver up to Alaska can be arranged. The crew are adept at dealing with children and bringing this fascinating part of the world to life.

    ROCKY MOUNTAINEER

    The Rocky Mountaineer is undoubtedly the most spectacular train trip in North America. Departing from Jasper, Alberta you'll travel to Kamloops through the Rockies enjoying the dramatic landscape as you tuck into a fabulous lunch. Abercrombie & Kent only recommends the Gold Leaf dome car service which will provide much better views of the Continental Divide, the river valleys of the Monashee and Cariboo Mountains, and Mount Robson the highest peak in the Rockies. The night is spent in Kamloops a former gold mining town.

    Continuing on the luxury train, you'll head west through the desert-like interior. Here you'll see winding river canyons which are surrounded by the peaks of the Coast and also Cascade Mountains. The journey continues into the lush green fields of the Fraser River Valley. Again, a great lunch aboard will be served as you travel along the Thompson River. The journey concludes in Vancouver, where we will transfer you to your choice of luxury hotel.

    The Wickaninnish Inn sits on Chesterman Beach near Tofino, Vancouver Island - a stunning area of inlets, coves, beaches and forests. Every one of the 75 guest rooms offers either a view of the pounding ocean or the beach.

    Guests choose from two buildings 'At-the-Pointe' or 'On-the-Beach'. The Pointe rooms sit perched on a rocky outcrop - a dramatic and photogenic setting. Here the sounds are of the waves crashing into the cliffs below.
