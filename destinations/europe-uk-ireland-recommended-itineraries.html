Pride and Prejudice Tour - In the Footsteps of Jane Austen

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 6 days

    A special Pride and Prejudice themed tour leading you into the world of Elizabeth Bennet and Mr Darcy, whose story has captured the imaginations of so many.

    Highlights: Chawton House, Pride & Prejudice costume exhibition, village of Lacock used as the setting for Meryton, the Georgian city of Bath, the real "Longbourn Court"

  • Day 1

    Arrive London

    After clearing customs at the airport, meet your Guardian Angel and transfer by private vehicle to your hotel. After check in, the remainder of your day is at leisure to settle in to this historic city on the River Thames.

    Overnight: The Milestone

  • Day 2

    Chawton & Winchester

    Embark on a full-day excursion to the south west of London. Firstly drive into the rural countryside of Hampshire where Jane Austen spent much of her life, stopping in the village of Chawton. Visit the house where the author lived in the latter years of her life and where she penned the majority of her mature writing (including Pride and Prejudice and Sense and Sensibility which were both revised in the house as well as Emma and Persuasion which were entirely written here).

    If you are visiting between October and December 2013 you may like to visit the special exhibition "She Had Dressed With More Than Usual Care" in which costumes worn in the 1995 BBC Production of Pride and Prejudice will be displayed..

    Also of interest may be St. Nicholas Church where the Austens worshipped. You can enter the church and also see the graves where Jane’s mother and sister are buried. Just a short walk away is the Chawton House Library; a beautiful building where the landscaping around the house has been re-planted to reflect what it looked like in Austen's day.

    Afterwards head to Winchester where Jane Austen was buried in the cathedral in 1817 at the age of 41. Winchester Cathedral was a building she greatly admired and her modest funeral was attended by just four people. There is an illustrated exhibition detailing Jane Austen’s life, work and death in Hampshire displayed beside her grave. Jane Austen only lived a few weeks in the house (now a private residence) in College Street but continued to write there.

    Optional Excursion: Travel north onto Salisbury Plain to visit Stonehenge, arguably the most famous prehistoric site in Europe. Visitors cannot fail to wonder at the effort it must have taken to drag the enormous stones over the land and erect them in circles. From here return to London past Old Sarum hill.

    Overnight: The Milestone
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Basildon Park - Lacock - Bath

    On your way to Bath today, stop at Basildon Park near the town of Reading. This impressive Georgian House took a starring role in the 2005 production of Pride & Prejudice, starring Keira Knightley as Lizzy. The mansion played Mr Bingley's house, Netherfield, a suitably grand residence for a 'young man of four or five thousand a year'.

    Continue your journey west and visit Lacock; a timeless, traditional English village. Besides being used as a movie set for Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone, Lacock appeared as Meryton in the 1995 BBC television adaptation of Pride & Prejudice. Here the Bennet girls shopped for bonnets, sought the latest gossip from their Aunt Phillips, and hoped to attract the attentions of the officers.

    Overnight: The Royal Crescent
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Bath

    Explore the city of Bath which is so familiar throughout Jane Austen’s novels as a prosperous spa resort, popular with fashionable society. The city’s impeccably preserved Georgian architecture remains unchanged from the streets described in Northanger Abbey and Persuasion.

    Visit the magnificent Assembly Rooms and admire the Ball Room, Octagon, Tea Room and Card Room that were used in the eighteenth century for dancing, music and card playing, and of course, visit the Jane Austen Centre which offers a snapshot of life during Regency times and provides an authentic period atmosphere. If you wish, take time to enjoy a pot of tea and some home-made cake before continuing your tour.

    See Number 4 Sydney Place, an 18th-century Georgian apartment where Jane lived with her family from 1801 to 1806. Jane also walked along the Royal Crescent on Sundays after church; at the time, it was a very fashionable thing to do in order to be seen by high society.

    To complete the day, you will have the opportunity to discover Bath’s other highlights such as the Pump Rooms.

    Overnight: The Royal Crescent
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Bath - Luckington Court - London

    Today is a special day for Pride and Prejudice fans and an opportunity to immerse yourself further in the world of Jane Austen as you travel with your driver-guide to Luckington Court, the site where the BBC production of Pride and Prejudice starring Colin Firth, was filmed. Receive a warm greeting from your specialist Pride and Prejudice tour guide who will take you on an engaging private tour of Luckington Court (known as Longbourn Court in the novel) and its extensive gardens. You are sure to be captivated by the church where Lizzie marries Darcy and the charming rooms within the house itself as you listen to anecdotes about the production. Your tour of the house will conclude with a full English afternoon tea.

    Continue your journey back to London with the option of stopping in one of the most beautiful areas of England. The Cotswolds are famous for thatched cottages and hundreds of honey-colour limestone villages in beautiful rural settings. There are a number of charming villages to wander through and great, cosy country pubs to dine in.

    Overnight: The Milestone
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    Depart London

    Make your way to the airport for your homebound flight.

    Meal Plan: B

London Fashion Tour

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 days

    A 4 day stylish tour of London. See the main sights of the capital then induldge in a spot of shopping and even get to meet the designers in their fashion houses. Shopping just doesn't get better!

    Highlights: London, Tower of London, St. Paul's Cathedral, Tower Bridge, Westminster Abbey, the Houses of Parliament, Buckingham Palace and Trafalgar Square, Net A Porter, Burberry Head Office, Meeting with the Royal Designer Gina Foster

  • Day 1

    Arrive London

    Upon arrival at London airport you will be met in the arrival hall by your driver then privately transferred to your hotel, where you will be welcomed to England by your local Guardian Angel and assisted with check-in procedures.

    After nearly three years of extensive 100$ million dollar restoration, the Savoy Hotel takes its place again on the banks of the River Thames in the heart of London. Situated between Knightsbridge and Mayfair the hotel is only steps away from the world's finest theatres, museums and opera houses. The hotel combines the Edwardian and Art Deco style which is reflected in all 268 guestrooms and suites.

    Overnight: The Savoy

  • Day 2

    London

    This morning meet your local guide and private vehicle for a half-day (4hrs) orientation tour of London to see the beautiful famous sights of the city. You will begin by touring past the Tower of London, St. Paul's Cathedral, Tower Bridge, Westminster Abbey, the Houses of Parliament, Buckingham Palace and Trafalgar Square; among many other interesting sites for which London is renowned. Finally you will finish your orientation tour with a flight on the London Eye for a panoramic view of London.

    This afternoon you will meet your local fashion expert guide and spend a couple of hours at Net A Porter (subject to availability), meeting the buyers and enjoy a shop in the personal shopping suites. NET-A-PORTER is an online, London, United Kingdom-based luxury clothing and accessories retailer. The website, presented in the style of a fashion magazine, was launched in 2000 and now stocks over 300 international brands such as Jimmy Choo, Christian Louboutin, Alexander McQueen, Stella McCartney & many more.

    Tonight you may like to dine at Hakkasan (Payable Locally).

    Overnight: The Savoy
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    LONDON

    This morning embark on a unique experience where you can enjoy a tour of Burberry head offices and meet the famous Christopher Bailey (subject to availability) and/or the head designers that work in Christopher's team. After lunch at leisure (payable locally) meet with the head designers of Reiss (subject to availability) and see the head offices. You may like to end you London fashion experience meeting with Gina Foster, and see how it all works behind the scenes; her recent designs have been worn by the Duchess of Cambridge on many occasions.

    Tonight you may like to dine at Nobu Berkeley (Payable Locally).

    Time permitting (during one of the two days) you may get to meet with the designers and a tour of the head office of Pringle of Scotland.

    Tonight you may like to dine at L'Atelier de Joel Robuchon (Payable Locally).

    Overnight: The Savoy
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Depart London

    Today you will be transferred by private vehicle to the London Gatwick airport to board your flight.

    Meal Plan: B

LONDON

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 days

    A four-day itinerary of London's highlights.

    Highlights: The Tower of London, St. Paul's Cathedral, Trafalgar Square, the Houses of Parliament, Westminster Abbey, the National Church of England, Buckingham Palace

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE LONDON

    Upon arrival at London Heathrow airport you will be met by your driver and privately transferred to your hotel. Your representative will meet you upon arrival, welcome you to England and assist you with check-in procedures.

    The remainder of the day will be at your leisure.

    Overnight: The Kensington Hotel

  • Day 2

    LONDON

    An expert local guide and your chauffeur will be waiting to meet you for a full day sightseeing tour (8 hours) of the City of London and Westminster. You will visit the Tower of London, where the Beefeaters guard the dazzling Crown Jewels, and where you will gain excellent views of Tower Bridge from the Medieval Palace. Proceed afterwards to St. Paul's Cathedral, rebuilt by Sir Christopher Wren after the Great Fire of London in 1666. Continue on to Trafalgar Square, where the National Gallery of Art is overshadowed by Nelson's Column. You will then head down Whitehall and past Downing Street, to the Houses of Parliament. You will also visit Westminster Abbey, the National Church of England that has been the scene of many state occasions including the Coronation. Finally return to your hotel past Buckingham Palace, the Queen's official residence in London.

    Overnight: The Kensington Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    LONDON

    Today is at your leisure to explore London further on your own.

    Overnight: The Kensington Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    LONDON

    Today you will be privately transferred to the train station / London Heathrow Airport.

    Meal Plan: B

A PRIVATE GOLF TOUR TO SCOTLAND

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 days

    An 8-day tour designed especially for the golfing enthusiast with opportunities to play on some of Scotland's most renowned courses including the famous Gleneagles, home of the 2014 Ryder Cup.

    Take the unique opportunity as an added extra, to learn the true art of golf from a real golfing Master. Having won tournaments against some of the best players in the world, your private tutor will show you how their skills can be applied to your game so you can reach your full potential.

    Highlights: Edinburgh, Gleneagles, Carnoustie, St Andrews, Dalmahoy Golf and Country Club, Rosslyn Chapel

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE EDINBURGH - GLENEAGLES

    Welcome to Scotland! Upon arrival at Edinburgh Airport, meet your Guardian Angel and transfer by private vehicle to your hotel in Perthshire, where you will be assisted with check-in. Enjoy the remainder of your day at leisure.

    Overnight: Gleneagles Hotel

  • Day 2

    GOLF AT GLENEAGLES

    This morning, enjoy an early morning tee time to play a round on the 6,471 yard King's Course. James Braid's plan for the King's Course was to test even the best players' shot-making skills over the eighteen holes making it both challenging and exhilarating for any golfer. Gleneagles is certainly one of the most beautiful places to play golf in the world, with the springy moorland turf underfoot, the un-spoilt views from the tees, the rock-faced mountains to the north, the green hills to the south, and the peaks of the Trossachs and Ben Vorlich on the western horizon.

    Handicap: No handicap certificate required.

    This afternoon is yours to enjoy the many activities available on site. You may wish to add one of the following: Gleneagles boasts many activities to choose from including the world famous Shooting and Fishing School, the Equestrian School which is one of the most impressive riding centres in Europe, the Gundog School - the first of its kind in the world and also off-road 4x4 driving on two specially developed courses. The Club, with two swimming pools, outdoor hot pool, tennis courts, gymnasium, cycle hire and walking/running tracks.


    Overnight: Gleneagles Hotel Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    GOLF AT CARNOUSTIE

    This morning, meet your driver-guide and transfer eastwards for a round of Golf at Carnoustie. Dating as far back as the 1500s, golf has been played over the links at Carnoustie but the present Championship course came into play in 1850, designed by Alan Robertson from St. Andrews and was then extended and improved in 1926 by the famous architect James Braid. Not only does it offer the ultimate challenge to every golfer but it provides a playing quality second to none.

    Tee times can be limited on the Championship Course so there is also the option to play the 5303 yards Buddon Links Course.

    Handicap: Max 28 for Men, max 36 for ladies

    On your return journey to Gleneagles you may take a scenic drive along Strathmore, the area of Scotland that is famed for the growing of soft fruit, notably raspberries. Stop in the picturesque village of Dunkeld that has an idyllic situation overlooking the River Tay.

    Overnight: Gleneagles Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    GOLF AT ST. ANDREWS

    Today your driver-guide will transfer you to St Andrews for another spectacular round of golf. St Andrews is the home of golf and has been for some 600 years. Enjoy a round on the New Course (6604 yds), opened in 1895, having been laid out by Old Tom Morris.

    Those wishing to play the famous Old Course, will be entered into the ballot, which is drawn 2 days prior to the day of play. There is no guarantee that you will be successful in obtaining a tee time.

    Handicap: No handicap certificate required on the New or Jubilee Course

    Overnight: Gleneagles Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    GOLF AT GLENEAGLES - EDINBURGH

    This morning you will experience the second of the three famous Gleneagles courses, the PGA Centenary Course. Designed by Jack Nicklaus, this course has an American-Scottish layout, many water hazards and raised contoured greens. Nicklaus himself described the location as "The finest parcel of land in the world I have ever been given to work with". From the beginning you will enjoy spectacular scenery and then as you progress over the course you are distracted by the lush panorama of the rich Perthshire straths, the rugged Grampians, Ben Vorlich and the mountains above the Trossachs.

    After your round of golf you can enjoy lunch at leisure before you are transferred to your Hotel in Edinburgh.

    The Balmoral Hotel is located at Number 1 Princes Street in the heart of Edinburgh, a minute's walk from the city's main shopping area, and is the top hotel in the city. The preserved grand public areas reflect the origins as a major railway terminus hotel, and the current more contemporary décor reflects the development into a modern top class venue. There are 2 restaurants, The Number One restaurant and the less formal Hadrian's, where breakfast is served. There is also a tea lounge, The Palm Bar. Other Hotel facilities include a Health Club, which is open from 7.00am - 9.00pm, with swimming pool, fully equipped gymnasium, solarium and steam room.

    Overnight: Balmoral Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    EDINBURGH SIGHTSEEING

    This morning, meet an expert local guide and private vehicle for a half day sightseeing tour (4 hours) of Scotland's capital city. Head up to Edinburgh Castle, see the 'Honours of Scotland', or the Scottish Crown Jewels, and obtain stunning views of the city. Continue down the Royal Mile, past St. Giles High Kirk, to Holyrood Palace, the Queen's Official Residence in Scotland. (You will tour the staterooms of the palace if no member of the Royal Family is staying here at the time.)

    This afternoon is yours to spend at leisure enjoying the sights and stores of this most dramatically located city.

    Overnight: Balmoral Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    GOLF AT THE DALMAHOY GOLF AND COUNTRY CLUB - ROSSLYN CHAPEL

    For your final round, accompany your driver-guide just outside the city to tackle the prestigious East Course at the Marriott Dalmahoy Golf & Country Club. The East Course has recently completed a £1 million redevelopment and is now a true Championship venue. The course has hosted major championships such as the Solheim Cup in 1992 and the Scottish PGA Championship on numerous occasions. It measures in at over 7000 yards, making it the longest course in Scotland and leaving you with truly special memories of your Scottish Golf experience.

    Handicap: No handicap certificate required

    Following your round return you to Edinburgh via Rosslyn Chapel. This intricately carved church is one of the most remarkable medieval buildings in Scotland, and preserves a huge amount of carvings and masonic symbols. Its appearance in 'The Da Vinci Code' catapulted the chapel to fame in recent years.

    Overnight: Balmoral Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    DEPART EDINBURGH

    Today your golf adventure comes to an end as you are transferred to the airport where you will board your flight home.

    Meal Plan: B
