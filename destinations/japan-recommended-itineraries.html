ESSENCE OF JAPAN

Programme Overview

Explore the culturally rich country of Japan with Abercrombie & Kent. Acquaint yourself with the colorful and rich heritage of this perpetually blossoming country, including wonderful kabuki (Japanese performing arts) and traditional geisha.

  • Duration: 7 Days / 6 Nights
    A&K Advantages: -Visit to the colorful temples and shrines
    -An excursion to the magnificent Mount Fuji
    -Travel by Shinkansen (bullet train) to Kyoto
    -VIP assistance with immediate check-ins in all hotels

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE TOKYO, JAPAN

    This morning, on arrival in Tokyo, you will be welcomed by an A&K representative who will drive you to your accommodation.

    After settling in, enjoy an afternoon visit to the Tokyo Tower, continuing to the country's largest and most important museum in Ueno Park, the Tokyo National Museum.

    Overnight:Shangri-La
    None

  • Day 2

    Tokyo

    Today, an early start sees you trying a breakfast of the freshest sushi at the Tsukiji Fish Market.

    After breakfast, continue on to the Senso-ji Temple (also known as the Asakusa Kannon Temple) which enshrines a golden image of the Buddhist Goddess of Mercy - not on public display. You may shop souvenirs at the colorful shopping street of Nakamise-dori, which leads to the temple.

    In the afternoon, visit Edo-Tokyo Museum and the gardens of the Imperial Palace, home of the Emperor and Imperial family.

    Make sure you find time for a stroll through the neon-lit streets of Shinjuku, Ginza or Shibuya this evening as Tokyo comes alive at night with its bustling crowds, bars and restaurants.

    Overnight: Shangri-La
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    HAKONE

    This morning, you will be driven to the spectacular Hakone, taking a brief stop en route, to visit Kamakura, the former capital. Here, see a giant bronze Buddha statue (the second largest in Japan) at Kotoku-in Temple and Tsurugaoka Hachiman-gu Shrine. Continue along the Shonan coast and spend two nights at the simple but traditional Gora Kadan Ryokan.

    Overnight: Aura Tachibana
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    HAKONE

    This day, embark on a full-day sightseeing tour of Hakone and the majestic Mount Fuji, Japan's tallest and best known mountain. You may also visit Owakudani Valley, where steam and sulphur fumes rise from crevices in the rocks. If brave enough, you can buy a boiled egg which has turned black in the sulphur waters.

    Weather permitting, take the ropeway to the top of Mount Komagatake for breathtaking views of Mount Fuji, Lake Ashi, Mount Futago and the mountains of the distant Izu Peninsula.

    Overnight: Aura Tachibana)
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    KYOTO

    This morning, you will take a short train ride to Kyoto aboard the Shinkansen train. At Kyoto, you will be received by an A&K representative who will help you with an immediate check-in to your palatial accommodation. Enjoy a visit to the striking Golden Temple, Kinkaku-ji, a national treasure. Also see Kiyomizudera or Pure Water Temple. Founded in 780, the temple remains associated with the oldest sect within Japanese Buddhism - the Hosso.

    Overnight: Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    KYOTO

    This day, after a toothsome local breakfast, head out to visit Arashiyama, once an excursion place for Emperors of the Heian Period. Today, Kyotoites make annual excursions here to see the gorgeous cherry blossoms in spring and the maples in their autumn colors.

    Enjoy the afternoon at leisure. You may wish to explore the Southern Higashiyama District which is full of temples, shrines, gardens and traditional neighborhoods.

    This evening, perhaps take a stroll through the historic Geisha district of Gion, where you may catch a glimpse of a ‘Geiko’ hurrying between teahouses to attend their guests.

    Overnight: Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    DEPARTURE JAPAN

    This morning, after breakfast, you will be assisted with your transfer to Osaka International airport to connect with your flight back home.

    Meal Plan: B

CLASSIC JAPAN

Programme Overview

Gain an in-depth perspective on Japanese culture with lengthy stays in Tokyo and Kyoto. Participate in the traditional Tea Ceremony, travel in a bullet train and shop at the effervescent morning markets for all kinds of handicrafts and souvenirs.

  • Duration: 14 Days / 13 Nights

    A&K Advantages: -Stay at elegant and luxurious properties
    -Private transfers
    -An excursion to the Hakone area which boasts terrific views of Mt. Fuji
    -Witness the renowned traditional Tea Ceremony

  • Day 1

    Tokyo

    This day, as you arrive at Tokyo's Narita Airport, you will be met by an A&K representative who will drive you to your luxurious accommodation in a private vehicle. After a chance to freshen up, you will receive an in depth Orientation Session from our Tokyo Staff in the lobby of the hotel. Spend the rest of the day at leisure.

    Overnight: Shangri-La / Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 1

    Tokyo

    This day, as you arrive at Tokyo's Narita Airport, you will be met by an A&K representative who will drive you to your luxurious accommodation in a private vehicle. After a chance to freshen up, you will receive an in depth Orientation Session from our Tokyo Staff in the lobby of the hotel. Spend the rest of the day at leisure.

    Overnight: Shangri-La / Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 3

    Tokyo

    Spend the morning at the Meiji Jingu, Tokyo's most important shrine. Head on to the fashionable modern areas of Harajuku, Omotesando & Shibuya, then explore the electronics paradise of Akihabara before ending the day with a visit to the Imperial Palace Gardens.

    Overnight: Shangri-La / Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Tokyo

    Start the day with a guided tour of the fascinating Tsukiji Fish Market, which will end with lunch of the freshest sushi & sashimi imaginable.

    In the afternoon, take a boat trip along the Sumida River to Asakusa for the Senso-ji, Tokyo's oldest temple. The streets around the temple are home to a fascinating mix of souvenir shops and traditional stores.

    Overnight: Shangri-La / Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    ODAWARA

    After breakfast, you will be transferred to Tokyo station, from where you board the local train to Kamakura by the sea for a visit to the giant Buddha and the town's impressive local temples.

    From Kamakura, take a train to Odawara, where you will be received by a representative, who will drive you to your accommodation.

    In the evening, invigorate in a luxuriating soak in your private hot spring bath before a delicious kaiseki dinner.

    Overnight: Hilton Resort & Spa
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    HAKONE

    This day, take a circular tour of the Hakone area, by a mountain train, funicular railway, cable car, and boat. Weather permitting, there are great views of Mount Fuji from the fascinating geothermic Great Boiling Valley and from Lake Ashi.

    If you're feeling energetic, the old cobbled Tokaido Road to Hakone Yumoto can be walked in full on an afternoon, or you can choose to return across the lake and relax.

    Overnight: Aura Tachibana / Gora Kadan
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    TAKAYAMA

    After breakfast, you will be taken to Odawara Station, from where you take the first bullet train and then an Express Train to Takayama, perfectly located in the foothills of the Japan Alps.

    Spend your afternoon at Takayama Jinya, the Edo Period Administrative offices, before visiting the San-machi Suji area of wooden merchant houses, many of which are now museums. The old town is full of sake breweries, which can be identified by the cedar balls hanging outside.

    The evening is at leisure to relax at your Ryokan.

    Overnight: Wanosato / Oyado Koto No Yume
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    HIROSHIMA

    Start the day early at the vibrant morning markets, which have an interesting mix of crafts and produce for sale, and make a great place to try local foods, people-watch, and shop for souvenirs.

    Later, you will be transferred to the station where you board the train to Hiroshima where you will be received by an A&K representative, who will escort you to your hotel in private vehicle.

    Spend rest of the day visiting the moving Atomic Peace Park and Memorial Dome.

    Overnight: Rihga Royal Hotel / Sheraton Hiroshima Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    MIYAJIMA

    This day, take a day trip to the sacred island of Miyajima, where you visit Itsukushima Shrine and its famous 'Floating' Torii gate, the 5 Tier Pagoda and Daisho-in Temple. Tide a cable car or hike to the peak of Mt Misen for stunning views across the island studded Inland Sea. Wildlife abounds the island; the deer which wander freely are traditionally believed to be messengers of gods, and Japanese macaques - the most northerly of all monkeys- inhabit the wooded peak of Mount Misen.

    You’re sure to have a great time here!

    Overnight: Miyajima Grand Hotel Arimoto / Hotel Miya Rikyu
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    KYOTO

    After breakfast you will be transferred back to Hiroshima Station, from where you will board the Bullet Train on to Kyoto, where you'll be met and transferred to your cosmopolitan hotel, your home for the next four nights.

    In the afternoon, visit the iconic Golden Pavilion and Ryoanji Zen Garden, and then head to nearby Ninnaji Temple for one of Kyoto's most beautiful gardens.

    Overnight: Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    NARA

    Rise early this morning for a day trip to Nara and witness the impressive giant bronze Buddha - Japan's largest - housed in the famous all-wood Todai-ji temple.

    Stroll through Nara-koen Park, and visit Kasuga Shrine and the lovely Isuien Gardens.

    In the evening, return to Kyoto, taking a brief stop at Fushimi Inari, one of Kyoto's most famous shrines and notable for its stunning tunnels of thousands of red torii gates.

    Overnight: Nara Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 12

    KYOTO

    Start your day with a stroll around Gion, the Geisha district then head on to visit a traditional Kyoto machiya townhouse where you tour the machiya before enjoying the classic elegance of a traditional Tea Ceremony followed by a light lunch.

    In the afternoon visit Nijo Castle, the former home of the Shoguns then end the day with a trip to Kiyomizu Temple.

    Overnight: Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 13

    KYOTO

    This day is at leisure for sightseeing and shopping at your own pace.

    Overnight: Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 14

    DEPART JAPAN

    After a scrumptious breakfast, you will be assisted with your transfer to Kansai International Airport for your homebound flight.

    Meal Plan: B

ANCIENT AND MODERN

Programme Overview

Take a holiday in the ultra-modern Japan. Travel to the royal Imperial Palace & the Ginza, see the World Heritage shrines of Nikko National Park and stay in a traditional Ryokan with stunning views of Mt. Fuji; a perfect getaway!

  • Duration:10 Days / 9 Nights

    A&K Advantages: -Visit the Imperial Palace & the Ginza
    -Explore the World Heritage shrines of Nikko National Park
    -Stay in a traditional ryokan
    -Catch a glimpse of the volcanic hot springs
    -Appreciate sights of the Shinto shrines in Kyoto
    -Attend the traditional Tea Ceremony at a local family’s home

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE TOKYO

    On Arrival into the fast moving, neon-lit metropolis of Tokyo, you will be welcomed by an A&K representative who will drive you to your accommodation.

    Overnight:The Peninsula Tokyo (Deluxe Room) / Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: None

  • Day 2

    Tokyo

    Rise early this morning as you hit the road to visit the amazing Tsukiji fish market, biggest in the world.

    Other highlights of the day include the Imperial Palace, the Meiji shrine, the vast Asakusa Kannon (Buddhist temple) and the fascinating Edo Tokyo Museum, shops and galleries of the Ginza district and Omotesando Street.

    Return to your luxurious hotel for a relaxed evening.

    Overnight:The Peninsula Tokyo (Deluxe Room) / Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Tokyo

    This day, venture out of the city a few hours north to the mountainous Nikko National Park, which is the host to a fine collection of World Heritage such as gilded shrines and temples, set amidst lush woodland. Look out in particular for the fabulous Toshogu Shrine, mausoleum to the first and greatest Tokugawa Shogun.

    On your way back to Tokyo stopover at a Bonsai Village to acquaint yourself with the traditional art of miniaturizing trees.

    Overnight:The Peninsula Tokyo (Deluxe Room) / Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    HAKONE

    This morning, an overland journey to Hakone takes you through the seaside town of Kamakura, the 12th century capital, famous for its hidden Buddhist temples and Shinto shrines, walking trails and volcanic hot springs.

    Embrace yourself for a treat this afternoon as you arrive at your ryokan, a traditional Japanese inn. Get ready to immerse yourself in the culture: wear the yukata (traditional robe), sample Japanese cuisine, sleep on a futon on tatami mats and soak in onsen (hot spring bath)!

    Overnight:Ryuguden Ryokan / Gora Kadan
    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 5

    HAKONE

    Revel in marvelous views of Mt. Fuji from your ryokan and gear up to spend the day enjoying beautiful sceneries and outdoor activities. Take a ropeway ride at Mt. Komagatake, a cruise on Lake Achi; visit the Owakudani crater, volcanic hot water springs and the informative Samurai Art Museum.

    Overnight:Ryuguden Ryokan / Gora Kadan
    Meal Plan: B, D

  • Day 6

    KYOTO

    Today, you will be helped out to board a bullet train for a ride from Odawara Station to Kyoto.

    This afternoon is at leisure for you to discover and explore this charming city, often called Japan's cultural capital.

    Overnight:Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    KYOTO

    Kyoto is a city full of treasures with all classic Japanese characteristics: raked pebble gardens, red shrine gates, golden temples and chance for a glimpse of the geisha culture.

    This day, tour one of the country's most famous sights, the Golden Pavilion and an icon of traditional Japanese landscape architecture, the rock garden of Ryoanji Temple, dating back to the late 1400s. Also, visit the 17th century Nijo, a castle of the first local shogun and two more of the city's significant temples.

    Overnight:Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    KYOTO

    This morning, grab the chance to experience Japanese customs first hand when you visit a private home for the traditional tea ceremony. In time-honored fashion, wear a kimono and obi as you join your gracious hostess for the unique ritual.

    Later, join in for a calligraphy demonstration before visiting the Uzumasa Movie Studio where a collection of Japanese buildings are used as sets for historical movies and TV dramas providing an interesting glimpse into elements of Japan's architectural history.

    Call it a day at the Kiyomizu Pottery Sannenzaka Museum which specializes in Japanese fine arts since the late 15th to early 20th century.

    Overnight:Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    OSAKA

    Travel a short distance this morning to Nara, Japan's capital in the 8th century.

    This day, visit a lovely compact collection of interesting historic sites and cultural attractions. See the Todaiji Temple which manifests the country's largest bronze Buddha and surrounding deer park. Continue to the Kasuga Shrine, founded in the 8th century with a charming woodland setting and resident herds of sacred deer.

    In the evening, see the World Heritage listed Horyuji Temple, Japan's oldest, which houses some of the country's rarest treasures.

    In the evening, continue to Osaka, where you spend your last night in Japan.

    Overnight:Ritz Carlton
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    DEPART OSAKA

    This day, say 'sayonara' to Japan as you are driven to the airport for your flight back home.

    Meal Plan: B
