Turkey Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Bodrum

    The Anatolian peninsula comprising most of modern Turkey is one of the oldest continually inhabited regions in the world due to its location at the intersection of Asia and Europe, bordering the Black Sea, Aegean Sea and Mediterranean Sea.

    The Mediterranean coastline is dotted with well-preserved Greco-Roman cities such as Pergamom and Ephesus, while the austere and rugged Anatolian plateau has cave churches hidden away in the improbable fairytale landscape of Cappadocia. Istanbul, quintessentially Eastern is at the heart of the nation, with historic Roman aqueducts, Byzantine churches, glittering Ottoman mosques and palaces on offer, contrasting with the modern glamorous bars, restaurants and the new Istanbul Modern Gallery.

    Luxury holidays in Turkey with Abercrombie & Kent will uncover the rich history set in a varied and beautiful landscape with historic sites and archaeological wonders.

    All Turkey holidays should include a trip up the Bosphorus which takes you past yalis the wooden summer residences on the waterfront that are the stately homes of Turkey. Experience the folk culture with a visit to see whirling dervishes or a Turkish shadow play. A visit to a hammam, an Ottoman bath ceremony is not to be missed on your Turkey holiday. Taste delicious cuisine - start with shared meze dishes including sarma, rice in vine leaves, humous and for mains pirzola, grilled mutton. The meal is completed with a sweet desert such as the puff pastry speciality baklava. Allow us to share our knowledge and take you on your own personal luxury holiday in Turkey.

    CAPPADOCIA

    Ancient Anatolia, one of the few landlocked regions of Turkey, is home to one of its most remarkable sights, the Goreme National Park - Cappadocia. The area is renowned now for its Fairy chimneys, rock carved churches and underground cities, where inhabitants would shelter from invading hoards coming across the Anatolian plains.

    Discovered in 1964, Kaymakli is one of the largest of their underground cities, believed to have housed thousands in the 6th to 9th centuries. Used to shelter from the winter cold, the ventilation systems alone are impressive and vast.

    This unique landscape was created by volcanic, geological and erosive activity. The terrain is made of such a soft material that its inhabitants hollowed out their homes from the rock, and religious orders such as the Cappadocian Fathers took root in the 4th Century, one of the most ancient and influential monastic movements. Today you can see scores of hidden chapels, adorned with frescoes and wall paintings depicting images from the bible and holy scriptures. The Goreme Open Air Museum displays the greatest concentration of churches, it having been recognised by UNESCO as a world heritage site.

    It's the haunting and dreamlike landscape that really captivates you, never more so than at dawn when tens of hot air balloons take off from the valley floor, providing the passengers with a unique view of this unforgettable area. Any visit to Turkey should include at least two days here, there simply is no place comparable.

    ISTANBUL

    Beside the waters of the Bosphorous, East truly meets West. The great mosques, colourful bazaars and tangled streets of Istanbul will guarantee you an excitingly different experience.

    You may visit for the monuments and museums, to see the sultan's jewels or the gold of the Byzantine mosaics, or simply to watch the sunset over the skyline of minarets and domes. But take time to watch the street life, for this is the real Istanbul, for which the mosques and palaces are merely decoration, and the people of the city have had centuries to cultivate this life of Istanbul into an art form.

    IZMIR

    Izmir is Turkey's 3rd largest city, but one with charm of an Aegean settlement. The town is set on the so-called bay, facing west. It is a very ancient city, going back at least 5000 years, said to be the hometown of Homer. Life has a somewhat laid back pace here; the townspeople make sure to come down to the waterside promenade ("Kordon") and stroll against the sunset over the Aegean.

    Cafes, bars and restaurants line up on the side. Explore the ancient Roman Agora and stroll thru the lively markets of Kemeralti to the Konak Square.

    Izmir is known for its seafood and we recommend highly Deniz Restaurant: let the waiter bring you a variety of mezes, go inside the kitchen to look at the fish stand and pick out the ones you want. Linger along with some raki and cheese. Catch that spirit that makes this an Aegean city.

    THE COAST

    From Antalya we visit Aspendos, home to what is considered the best-preserved antique theatre in Turkey. Continue along the Turkish Riviera to explore the ancient site of Perge and the Antalya Museum, one of Turkey's best regional museums.

    Travel along the Lycian Coast to Myra, known for its rock carved Cliffside tombs before we journey by boat to Kekova Island and the site of ancient Simena. Visit the charming fishing village of Kaleköy; then continue to Kalkan. Explore Xanthos, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and Patara, a major naval and trading port of Lycia. Continue to the picturesque fishing village of Goçek and then onto Dalyan.
