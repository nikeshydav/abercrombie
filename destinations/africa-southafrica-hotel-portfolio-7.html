Suggested KwaZulu Natal accommodation

  • Cleopatra Mountain Farmhouse

    This charming little getaway is found high in the Natal Drakensberg. The Farmhouse is directly adjacent to the Highmoor Nature Reserve, famous for its hikes, and near to Giant's Castle. It is easily combined with a visit to the Battlefields.

    Cleopatra Mountain Farmhouse offers six very individual bedrooms and a delightful restaurant set around a small lake. The spacious guest rooms, converted from the stables, are each differently themed. Constants are snug down duvets and pillows, fresh, crisp cotton sheets, freshly cut flowers, tea, coffee and cakes, as well as large claw foot bathtubs.

    A day in the open air is ideal preparation for a truly exceptional dinner. Richard Poynton, who has worked and studied extensively throughout Europe under the continent's leading chefs, prepares five-course feasts, new each day, served in a stunningly situated dining room. The warm and welcoming atmosphere, the fantastic setting, wild décor and superb food makes this a great base to explore the Drakensberg.

    Fugitives' Drift Lodge

    The Rattray family continue to pioneer Heritage Tourism in South Africa, having created the ideal setting for visitors to experience the events surrounding the great battles of Isandlwana and Rorke's Drift. Clients have two accommodation options, Fugitives' Drift Lodge and the adjacent Guesthouse. Thrilling battlefield lectures are headed up by Rob Caskie and delivered in situ with him and his knowledgeable team.

    Let the history of Isandlwana and Rorke's Drift unfold as the superb storytellers lead you onto the battlefields to recount the events from both the Anglo and Zulu perspectives. At Isandlwana you will learn how the Zulu's defeated the British army and how at sunset the story of Rorke's Drift unfolded to reveal why the most Victoria Crosses in one act of battle were awarded. Aside from the battlefield tours, the lodge and guesthouse provide a quiet haven to relax. Guided walks, horseback rides and fishing are all available.

    Hotel Izulu

    Hotel Izulu is set on a hillside with beautiful panoramic views of the coast of Northern KwaZulu Natal. It is a real haven in a secluded tropical setting just north of Durban. Five freestanding Bali-style villas are set around the main hotel building, all reflecting contemporary Tuscan architecture. Exceptionally spacious luxury suites feature large lounge, gorgeous bathroom, elegant furnishings and superb facilities. Enjoy the range of holistic treatments and indulge all your senses at the Impilo Beauty Spa.

    The chef personally selects the finest local ingredients, creating unimagined delights that encompass the big five of gastronomy - taste, aroma, texture, succulence and presentation. Dine a la carte at the elegant contemporary Gigis Restaurant, in the intimacy of your suite, or at a table in the charming wine cellar where many rare and sought after South African wines can be enjoyed by the bottle or the glass. Or enjoy a light lunch by the pool, courtyard or outdoor terrace.

    Phinda Forest Lodge

    Refurbished in November 2008 and nestled within 15,000 hectares of Phinda Private Game Reserve, exquisite architectural design meets conservation in these luxurious hand-crafted suites.

    Set deep in the heart of a rare dry sand forest, the lodge comprises of 16 glass, air-conditioned private stilted suites that appear to float between the torchwood trees. Other facilities include an stylish infinity swimming pool, luxurious dining and sitting areas and an expansive viewing deck with panoramic views of the game-filled plains. The suites are positioned to ensure absolute privacy and each en suite bathroom features twin hand basins, bath and separate shower. A nice touch, all suites feature a personal bar on their private veranda.

    Phinda Mountain Lodge

    Phinda is not only a luxury destination; it is also a dynamic working model in land management and creative wildlife conservation. Phinda was restocked with lion, rhino, buffalo, elephant, leopard, giraffe and other big game in an operation dubbed "phinda izilwane", return of the wild animals. The reserve today provides an exceptional game and marine experience, freshly refurbished, Mountain Lodge is a great family friendly base from which to explore.

    Set within 23,000 hectares of Phinda Private Game Reserve, Mountain Lodge has 25 air-conditioned suites, each with en suite bathrooms, sitting areas with private bar and spectacular viewing decks with plunge pool. Mountain Lodge provides the perfect setting for a family safari; a shaded swimming pool allows a wonderful place to relax during the heat of the day. The guest dining and sitting areas, shaded terrace and bar open onto decks with spectacular views of the Lebombo Mountains and bushveld.

    During your stay experience the bush on foot and opt to take an exciting bush walk. Guided by expert rangers, watch out for some of the 400 species of bird life recorded on the reserve. One of the most unspoilt areas of KwaZulu Natal, Maputaland is sparsely populated. Standing just above sea level there are a large number of freshwater and brackish lakes and pans, filled with many species of fish, water birds, crocodiles and hippo. The local inhabitants have developed ingenious traps across the lakes to trap the fish.

    Phinda Vlei Lodge

    Nestled below the Lebombo Mountains of Northern KwaZulu-Natal, Phinda is a Zulu word meaning "the return". One of the most exciting safari destinations in South Africa, Phinda's 25,000 hectares of private game reserve spans seven unique eco-systems. Situated in the rare Dry Sand Forest, Phinda Vlei is an intimate hideaway in the African bush.

    Fringing Phinda's unique vlei (wetland) system, this stunning safari lodge is a tribute of intimacy and integration with the African bush. Relax on your private deck or in your personal plunge pool and absorb the beauty of nature. Each of the six stilted glass and thatch suites are decorated using a subtle blend of North and West African styles with a hint of Balinese influence. Views over the vlei ensure fabulous game viewing of a variety of wildlife including herds of zebra and grazing antelope. The atmosphere is exclusive and personal, a mixture of understated sophistication and sheer luxury. Delicious cuisine is served to you in a variety of locations; Vleis' bush banquets are renowned.

    The main areas at Phinda Vlei are inviting and stylishly designed. Sitting and dining areas benefit from fireplaces and expansive viewing decks. Guests are welcome to visit the craft shop at Forest Lodge a short distance away. Due to the diversity of eco-systems at Phinda, it is possible to dive in the warm Indian Ocean - an experience to rival Australia's Great Barrier Reef, partake in game drives and walks in search of the 'Big Five', and explore the unique Sand Forest.

    Teremok

    Set on the tropical Indian Ocean coastline of KwaZulu Natal amongst palm and giant milkwood trees, Teremok Marine is a luxurious five star boutique lodge.

    Just eight individually decorated suites are elegantly furnished, light and airy. The concept behind the communal areas is that of an uncluttered one, sumptuous African chic furnishings in the living room ensure a comfortable haven for relaxation as you enjoy the sea and garden views. Enjoy renowned Teremok breakfasts in the simple dining room, delicious food is prepared in the open-kitchen using the freshest of ingredients. The perfect base from which to explore the KwaZulu Natal coastline.

    Three Trees at Spioenkop

    Surrounded by the Drakensberg Mountains and overlooking a secluded valley of the Spionkop Game Reserve lies Three Tree Hill Lodge. Specialising in the Second Anglo-Boer War (1899-1902) and in particular the Battle of Spionkop, narrators bring to life the world-changing events of this great moment of history.

    Six twin cottages, each with their own shower and bath, have been appointed in a colonial design. The décor is homely and comfortable with stable doors opening onto a private deck, each with fabulous valley views.

    Large verandas create an ideal environment in which to wind down and absorb the Spioenkop atmosphere and meals are often served outside to take advantage of the amazing views. There are numerous opportunities for horse riding around the lodge. The nearby Spioenkop Game Reserve is also well worth a visit. The main attraction is, of course, the battlefield tours. Expert guides take you on a journey re-living the dramatic events of one of the most famous battles between the British and the Boers.

    Zimbali Forest Suites

    The ten Forest Suites at Zimbali offer refined luxury accommodation. Suites are decorated to complement the natural forest surroundings, each with luxurious bathrooms complete with Victorian baths and a choice of indoor and outdoor showers.

    King sized beds, separate dining room and lounge area and 24hr butler service is on hand to attend to your every need. For golfers, each suite benefits from its own private golf cart, useful on the par 72 championship course. In addition the suites have access to two all-weather tennis courts, and a relaxing spa. Take walks through the forest and watch out for a variety of bird species or perhaps one of the resident families of bush buck.
