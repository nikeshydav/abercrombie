  • BARGE CRUISE

    A barge cruise across the French countryside river is one of the most unique ways to discover European countryside. An all inclusive leisurely holiday on calm waters with every luxury accompanied with great food & wine make these cruises a complete holiday experience. Enjoy a laid back vacation on vessel hotel away from the usual tourists' circuits and watch the beautiful French countryside unfold before your eyes. These bespoke itineraries also include excursions and sightseeing to historic châteaux, medieval villages, and world-famous French vineyards.

    Our fleet comprise of luxurious and comfortable vessels, outfitted to present an unsurpassed cruise experience while maintaining the highest standards of quality and safety. Each saloon is fully air conditioned, offering space for up to two guests to share comfortably. For a complete stay experience each room is also equipped with essential amenities. Canopied decks with sun loungers offer guests a chance to unwind with a glass of French wine while soaking in the beautiful French countryside scenery. Many have a small library, games and a music centre, while some carry bicycles for use on towpaths. Hotel barges are also available for exclusive charter by larger parties. It must be noted that children under 12 years of age are not accepted unless it is a charter booking or a special family departure.

    During the cruise, guests enjoy the world class, gourmet cuisine that France is globally renowned for. On board as well during the excursions guests will savor some of the finest regional wines, often straight from a Grand Cru or Premier Cru vineyards. Farm-produced cheeses, patés, charcuterie, prime meats and fish, and garden-fresh fruits and vegetables, fresh baguettes are only a highlight of the food offered. Four or five course dinners are served each evening. Throughout the cruise you will sample fine wines, often from regional vineyards at both lunch and dinner.

    COTE D'AZUR

    Côte d'Azur or French Riviera brings luminous images of lofty cliffs overlooking deep blue waters, sun kissed white beaches, opulent estates, exclusive shopping hubs and famous who's who rubbing shoulders with each other. This wealthiest stretch of the world is home to gorgeous towns such as St. Tropez, Nice, Monte Carlo and Cannes as well the lesser known but equally charming Grasse, Valbonne, Vence, La Garde Freinet and The Var, flaunting beautiful French countryside and vineyards.

    Côte d'Azur's colorful landscape features most famous lavish holiday retreats famous for their Mediterranean splendor lined with some of the prettiest marinas. This coupled with year round mild weather has made the French Riviera a famous holiday destination. An array of present day favorites have their origins here, such as soupe au pistou, aioli and the world-famous bouillabaisse containing five to twelve kinds of Mediterranean fish and flavored with saffron.

    Abercrombie & Kent offers some of the most luxurious villas to complete your holiday which have been handpicked to offer you the best of the Côte d'Azur holiday experience.

    Take a leisurely stroll by one of its Mediterranean beach hugging coastline in Nice, party among the St-Tropez's A-listed celebrities, gamble away in one of the casinos in Monaco, discover the artistic surface of Côte d'Azur that inspired painters such as Pablo Picasso, Marc Chagall, Auguste Renoir, Claude Monet to Henri Matisse.

    LAKE GENEVA

    Lake Geneva (Lac Lèman in French) forms a natural border between Switzerland and France and, being over 70 km long, 14 km wide and 310 m deep, is one of the largest freshwater lakes in Western Europe. This crescent shaped river is originated from a retreating glacier and lies on the course of river Rhone.

    Situated at the foot of the mighty Alps, Lake Geneva is often described as world's most spectacular water bodies. Chablais and Bernese Alps are must regions when at Lake Geneva and so are the high summits if Grand Combin and Mont Blanc.

    PARIS

    Paris, the capital of France, is one of the world's most visited cities. The City of Lights, with its grandeur, legendary heritage, celebrated arts & culture makes it rather tricky to paint a picture with mere words or superlatives. It is best described as an experience in itself that one needs to live up once in their lifetime.

    Paris is a comprehensive tourist destination with its historically renowned architecture, vibrant social scene, delightful food and drink culture as well as prominent shopping hubs. It is home to Eifel Tower, the most recognizable structure of modern history, and The Louvre, acclaimed as the world's most well known art museum as well as the famous Notre Dame cathedral. Kids from all over the world await the day they visit world famous Disneyland Paris! Here, one can while away sitting in a roadside boutique café gorging on croissant with Café Au Lait, visit the Mona Lisa at the Louvre, spend a fun filled day at Disneyland or take a cruise in River Seine watching the city come alive with nightlights in the evening!

    Home to some of the world's leading fashion houses, the city offers fabulous shopping both in department stores and small boutiques. For the gastronomes amongst us there is a buzzing café society and a plethora of fashionable restaurants offering fine dining and exquisite wines.

    Paris is easily accessible via high speed Eurostar from London and getting around is a walk in the park with well connected Metro or RER intercity train service.

    Provence

    Sightseeing in France is incomplete without an exploration of Provence. Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur is the sun drenched region of South West France, rubbing coastline by the Mediterranean Sea. This region because of its spectacular natural beauty and abundant sunshine has been an inspiration for artists such as Cezanne, Giono, Van Gogh and Chagall. The landscape of Provence is lined with gently sloping vineyards and lavender fields and medieval villages. The local people (the Midi) have a soft singing accent which evokes their relaxed lifestyle.

    Resplendent with shimmering meadows, vineyards, bustling farmer markets are few of the features along with the beautiful backdrop that draws travelers and well heeled city dwellers to the region for a much needed countryside retreat. Abercrombie & Kent offers a vast selection of villas that exude luxury and comfort in the middle of tiny villages that are brimming with sunny loveliness. Just as described by Peter Mayle, in A Year in Provence, your Provence experience would be as remarkable and one to cherish.

    Southwest France

    What we know as Southwest France is one of the most visited tourist places in France! It includes the famous regions such as Gascony, Dorgonge and Bordeaux rub shoulders with the Atlantic Ocean, stretching from Gironde Estuary in the north to Spanish Border in the south.

    Gascony and Dorgone showcase their long gone affair with the medieval eras where travelers can admire the prehistoric fortresses or explore the charming old villages of Rocamadour, Perigueux, Sarlat and Cahors. Its mesmerizing natural scenery entwined with lush meadows, fluid rivers, and sprawling vineyards across the rolling hills create a magical visual treat.

    Dordogne is a gourmet's paradise with some of the best and freshest farm produced French specialties including foie gras, magret de canard and entrecote bordelaise. Moreover to ensure a complete French dining experience, one is spoilt for choices with the finest Bordeaux wines from the region itself. This ritual is often wrapped up with a balmy digestive of Armagnacs or Sauternes sweet white wine, also another famous native coming from the Bordeaux region.
