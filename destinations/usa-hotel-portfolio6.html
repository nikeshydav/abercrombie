Suggested New York accommodation.

  • MANDARIN ORIENTAL

    Situated high above Columbus Circle, the hotel's central position means you are never far from the eclectic mix of culture and shopping that New York is so famous for, including the Empire State Building, Lincoln Centre and Times Square. Take advantage of the onsite concierge who can help organise tickets for the most coveted sporting events, museum visits or even a round of golf in neighbouring new Jersey.

    The Mandarin Oriental is home to one of Manhattan's favourite restaurants, Asiate, a gourmet restaurant that will elevates your dining experiences to new heights, with gracious service, inventive cuisine, an award-winning wine collection and stylish design.

    Read more

    ST REGIS

    Excellently located in the heart of midtown Manhattan, and within walking distance of Central Park, Fifth Avenue shopping, excellent restaurants and exciting theatre, The St Regis hotel offers a feeling of regality from the moment you step over its threshold.

    The story of St. Regis begins at the forefront of New York's Gilded Age, where an elite group of supremely wealthy families rose to form the country's first aristocracy when it opened in 1904.

    Read more

    THE LOWELL

    The Lowell is located on East 63rd Street in New York City's fashionable Upper East Side, one of the most exclusive parts of the city. It has the very best of both worlds - complete privacy on a quiet tree-lined street but with the vibrancy and convenience of Midtown which is just 5 blocks away.

    Most of the 49 delightfully appointed suites have wood burning fireplaces and two of the junior suites offer terraces - a rarity in New York City. Amongst the many thoughtful touches are complimentary drinks upon arrival, Voss water at turndown and a selection of Bulgari amenities. The Lowell is relatively small by New York standards and its dedication to ensuring guests have complete privacy means it has quite a low profile - but it is easily one of Manhattan's very finest hotels.

    Read more

    THE MARK

    Surrounded by New York's premier museums, shops and restaurants, Central Park and located in the infamous Upper East Side, The Mark places you at the epicentre of Manhattan's social and cultural riches.

    It expertly combines the elements of grandeur, luxury, comfort, artistry and intimacy ensuring its place as one of New Yorks most sophisticated establishments.

    Read more

    THE PENINSULA

    Characterised by a blend of classic design, intimate ambiance and personal service, The Peninsula perfectly combines old school charm with state of the art technology.

    THE PIERRE

    As the iconic U.S. flagship of Taj Hotels, and with an unrivalled position on New York's Fifth Avenue overlooking Central Park, The Pierre continues to set the standard for sophistication and unsurpassed hospitality.

    THE PLAZA

    The Plaza has a great location on the corner of Fifth Avenue and Central Park. It is a New York City landmark that has been indulging guests since 1907. We recommend The Terrace Suite on the 21st floor which has its own outdoor entertaining space, a butler's pantry and amazing views of the Big Apple. Special touches include 24 carat gold finished taps, afternoon tea at Palm Court, or the use of a vintage Rolls Royce whilst in Manhattan.

    A new touch for children is the unique Eloise Suite, inspired by The Plaza's most famous resident. Fashion designer Betsey Johnson has recreated the spirit of Eloise with fabulous touches including a signature Plaza chandelier, gold leaf molding, a white zebra-print carpet, custom-designed bedding depicting images of Eloise, a sparkly padded pink headboard and Eloise's name scrawled in neon lights on the wall.

    THE SETAI

    This hotel enables you to experience the best of both worlds. Situated on Fifth Avenue, the enviable position of the Setai ensures that you are never far from the buzz and excitement of New York City, while the inside provides a welcoming sanctuary where you can regale in its luxurious simplicity after a hard day of exploring the Big Apple.
