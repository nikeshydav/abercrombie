Suggested Buenos Aires accommodation

  • Alvear Palace

    The Alvear Palace combines Louis XVI elegance with imposing architecture to create a truly palatial feel. Built in 1932, it sits in the heart of La Recoleta, the most refined quarter of Buenos Aires, and offers a wide choice of individually and lavishly furnished suites, salons and Palace Rooms

    The Alvear Palace's Louis XVI elegance and imposing architecture create a truly palatial feel. Built in 1932, it has been extensively updated to create superb hotel in the heart of Buenos Aires.

    Throughout the 197 rooms and suites, an exquisite combination European elegance and modern technology prevails. Décor is Empire, Louis XV and Louis XVI style, using objets d'art to create a sophisticated air and give each room its own personality. The works of well-known Argentine artists are on display in the exclusive suites.

    Estancia los Patos

    Celebrating the time-honoured way of life on an estancia, Estancia los Patos is part of a property first established by the Videla Dorna family 200 years ago. It comprises five simple suites and offers an authentic experience of life on the pampas.

    The rooms are simply furnished, but very comfortable - with their eclectic furnishings and décor, they feel very much like those of a residential home. In fact, the estancia is so welcoming and inclusive, the whole place barely feels like a hotel at all. It is more akin to staying with friends, though with the amenities and facilities you'd expect of a hotel.

    Four Seasons Buenos Aires

    Located in the heart of the city in stylish La Recoleta, the Four Seasons combines a marble tower with an adjacent French-style mansion and is an ideal base from which to explore. Four Seasons offers 116 spacious guest rooms and 49 suites, including seven one-of-a-kind suites in the adjacent Belle Époque-style mansion

    Four Seasons service resonates in this hotel - with its contemporary tower and early 20th-century, French-style mansion. In the tower, warm, comfortable rooms feature leather upholstered headboards and dark mahogany furnishings. Suites in La Mansión offer either French country décor, with high ceilings, antiques and Toile de Jouy wallpaper, or a more contemporary look.

    Four Seasons Carmelo, Uruguay

    Set at one of the widest rivers in the world at the Rio de la Plata the Four Seasons Resort Carmelo is an optimal place for an inspiring experience of Uruguay in a mild climate all year around. The hotel is one hour from the charming town of Colonia, a UNESCO World Heritage site.

    Its spacious bungalows and bi-level suites are surrounded by fragrant pine and eucalyptus trees and are lovely, private spaces.

    In total, the Four Seasons has 20 bungalows - including six overlooking the river - and 24 bi-level suites. Each exudes a cosy ambience, with polished, hand-finished floors and open-beamed ceilings with ceiling fans. The addition of mesh drapes over hand-carved beds, wall hangings and individual, local furnishings create a rustic feel that is both relaxed and atmospheric.

    Jardin Escondido

    With its extensive gardens, a solar-heated pool, an outdoor 'parilla' barbeque and a beautiful terrace surrounded by plants of all kinds, Jardin Escondido is its own hidden paradise. This property is ideal for exclusive hire, though rooms may be booked individually

    A genuine sanctuary, Jardin Escondido is awash with wonderful design and décor touches - locally made cushions, rugs and wall hangings, and a colour scheme that mixes russet reds, earthy hues, pinks and mustard yellows. Groups or families may rent the property in its entirety, or couples might take one of two principle sections on either side of the garden.

    Legado Mitico Buenos Aires

    This hotel is a wonderful combination of old and contemporary, ideal for those who seek the authentic and sophisticated. Located in the trendy district of Palermo Viejo district. The hotel does not have its own restaurant, but there are a wide variety in the surrounding streets

    This hotel is a wonderful combination of old and new, ideal for those with an eye for the urbane. The Legado is hidden behind mahogany-paneled doors off the cobbled streets of Palermo and is a haven of sophistication where every detail has clearly been pored over in creation of superb interiors. Despite all the refinement, the hotel still manages to create a friendly, homely atmosphere.

    Mansion Algodon

    Those looking for a truly exceptional boutique hotel experience in Buenos Aires will be excited by the opening of Mansion Algodon, the latest in a plethora of high end boutique hotels to open in the city. Mansion Algodon is a supremely grand building dating from 1912.

    Fashioned in French Classical design it has been impeccably restored, retaining a host of original features, whilst creating beautifully stylish, contemporary interiors.

    Its 10 large suites have been designed to the very highest standard, to create living spaces that feel more like upscale apartments than hotel accommodation. No detail has been neglected in pursuit of the very height of style and sophistication.

    Park Hyatt Buenos Aires

    Ideally situated on Avenida Alvear, Palacio Duhau is a superb city hotel and admirable member of the legendary Park Hyatt portfolio. Its stylish, spacious rooms and exclusive suites are all immaculately presented, with modern décor offering a backdrop for the latest technology and luxurious furnishings.

    All have plush king sized beds, oversized work desks and majestic bathrooms with tub and rain shower. Luxurious touches include Celedonio Lohidoy-designed spa products, 30-inch flat-screen TVs and butler service. This hotel is the ideal base from which to explore this beautiful, exciting and endlessly fascinating city.

    Sofitel

    Hotel Sofitel Buenos Aires is an ideally located and stunning property where renowned interior designer Pierre-Yves Rochon has seamlessly blended French and Art Deco design with Argentine culture and Buenos Aires tradition. Gleaming chandeliers reflect in black and white marble floors and the scent of fresh flowers welcomes guests in from the bustling streets.

    The hotel has just over 100 light and airy rooms, gently coloured and with Argentine leather furnishings, and with ample bathrooms lined in Boticcino marble. Superb in every detail, this hotel was voted as one of the 'World's Best Places to Stay' in the Condé Nast Traveller's Gold List 2009.
