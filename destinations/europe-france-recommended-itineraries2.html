A WINE INFUSED TOUR OF BORDEAUX

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 6 days

    A six day itinerary exploring the beautiful wine region of Bordeaux.

    Highlights: Bordeaux, Martillac, St Emilion, Medoc

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE BORDEAUX - MARTILLAC

    Upon arrival at Bordeaux airport you will be welcomed to France by your local Guardian Angel as you exit customs into the arrivals hall, and transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in procedures.

    Overnight: Les Sources de Caudalie

  • Day 2

    MARTILLAC - BORDEAUX - MARTILLAC

    Today you will be met by your local English speaking guide and private vehicle for a morning tour of Bordeaux (4hrs).

    After your morning sightseeing our local Bordeaux host will then invite you into her/his typically Bordelaise home to share a south western French meal accompanied by Bordeaux wine and talk about the French way of life, the Bordeaux region, etc (subject to availability). After lunch you will be transferred back to you hotel.

  • Day 3

    MARTILLAC - ST EMILION WINE REGION - MARTILLAC

    Meet your private vehicle and English-speaking guide for a full day (8-hour) tour in and around St. Emilion. Spend the morning in the medieval town of Saint Émilion, overlooking the Dordogne valley and surrounded by its famous vineyards. See the Monolithic Church, the nave of which is supported by ten rough, ill-aligned pillars, as well as a number of remaining reliefs depicting winged angels and the signs of the zodiac. Also visit the Ermitage Grotto, where Émilion lived. Émilion was the Benedictine hermit who first came to the area. His piety attracted a number of companions and he started a community on the site now recognized as Saint Émilion. The Ermitage Grotto is a cave that over the centuries has evolved into a little chapel in the form of a Latin cross. St. Emilion is also famed for its fine Bordeaux Wines, and you will have the opportunity to enjoy a tasting and visit to some of the local vineyards such as Chateau Pétrus or Cheval Blanc.

    Overnight: Les Sources de Caudalie
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    MARTILLAC - MÉDOC WINE REGION - MARTILLAC

    Spend the morning with your English-speaking guide and private vehicle in the Médoc region (6hrs), home to the most famous crus of red wines. Today's excursion, in the company of a knowledgeable local guide, will introduce you to a traditional wine-growing land where some world renowned wines are produced with more than 800 crus and 8 appellationsof controlled origin wines. Discover the magnificent landscape when driving around parts of the "Route du Médoc". You will appreciate exploring the skillful cultivation of Médoc wines during a cellar tour followed by a wine tasting at a one of the estates. There will also be photo stops at Chateau Margaux & Chateau Lafite Rothschild or Latour before returning back to your hotel.

    The remainder of your day is at leisure.

    Overnight: Les Sources de Caudalie
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    MARTILLAC - BORDEAUX GRAND CRU CLASSÉS - MARTILLAC

    Today is an opportunity to enjoy a full day (10hrs) in the World of Bordeaux Grand Cru Classés with a knowledgeable local guide and wine dealer (subject to availability) who will accompany and guide you to three special chateaux where exceptional Grand Cru classes are produced.

    You will be able to visit Chateau Brane Cantenac (second Grand Cru classé de Margaux). Founded in the 18th century by the Gorce family, who gave it its original name, this great wine estate was producing one of the most highly regarded wines of the Médoc well before the 1855 classification. The high price of its wines gave the estate its rank at the top of the second classed growths. Following your first chateau visit, transfer to the Graves wine regionfor lunch in a typical Bordelais region restaurant (to be paid locally). This afternoon visit Chateau Bouscaut.The Bordeaux wine produced in this chateau is ranked among the Premiers Crus for red and dry white wine in the Classification of Graves wine of 1953 and 1959. End your day with a visit to Chateau Climens,the leading property in Barsac and producing one of the greatest sweet wines in Bordeaux. It can trace its history back to the 16th century and has been managed by Berenice Lurton since 1992. The wines from the best vintages can last for up to 50 years. Climens is classified as a Sauternes 1er Cru Classé.

    Overnight: Les Sources de Caudalie
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    DEPART MARTILLAC

    Today you will be privately transferred with the assistance of your Guardian Angel to Bordeaux airport.

    Meal Plan: B

PARIS, PROVENCE AND THE RIVIERA

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 14 days

    A bespoke FIT tour of Paris, Provence and the French Riviera.

    Highlights: Paris, Loire Valley, Versailles, Giverny, Avignon, St Remy, Les Baux, Luberon, Aix en Provence, Mougins, Grasse, Cannes, St Jean Cap Ferrat

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE PARIS

    Upon arrival at Paris airport you will be met by your driver and privately transferred to your hotel, where you will be welcomed to France by a local representative and assisted with check-in procedures.

    Overnight: Hotel Meurice

  • Day 2

    PARIS

    After enjoying breakfast at your hotel, your English-speaking guide will meet you for a morning walking tour of Paris. This overview tour will include famous Parisian sights such as the Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Champs Elysées and the Ile de la Cité, where you will visit the beautiful Notre Dame Cathedral and the Sainte Chapelle, a pearl of Gothic architecture.

    You will then have a choice of afternoon activity:

    A behind the scenes tour can be organised at the Eiffel Tower. View the internal machinery room located under the structure and from which the elevators are controlled and see the fascinating hydraulics and intricate mechanics on the first floor of this historic monument. You will also be able to enter the old secret military bunkers sited under the Champs des Mars, previously dedicated to radio and telegraph equipment.

    Enjoy the gourmet world of Paris, visiting a small private chocolate boutique and tastings with a master chocolatier and a visit to his atelier. You will also visit a wonderful patisserie and boulangerie, going into the kitchens and watching the teams make their exquisite cakes, pastries and breads. The tour continues from gourmet shop to gourmet shop, and finishes downstairs at a wonderful bakery which uses 17th century ovens to watch how their unique bread is made.

    Explore the best of Paris's vintage boutiques with your personal shopping guide who will show you true hidden gems in one of the fashion capitals of the world. Meet specialist sellers and receive private access to boutiques to find something truly unique to remind you of your trip to Paris.

    Overnight: Hotel Meurice

  • Day 3

    PARIS

    Today you will enjoy a half day sightseeing tour of Paris which includes the world-famous Musée du Louvre and the Musée d'Orsay.

    The Louvre is housed in a magnificent building, originally built for use as a royal palace for King Francis I. The main entrance is now covered by a large glass, steel rod and cable pyramid designed by the American architect I. M. Pei. It is home to one of the most important art collections in the Western world, spanning the period from remotest antiquity, through to about 1850.

    The Musée d'Orsay is housed in a converted railway station. The collection's main attraction is its Impressionist works: Renoir, Sisley, Pissarro and Monet are all well-represented. The Post Impressionist collection includes works by Van Gogh and Cézanne.

    Overnight: Hotel Meurice

  • Day 4

    PARIS - LOIRE VALLEY - PARIS

    This morning you will be met at your hotel and transferred to Paris Montparnasse railway station where you will board a TGV train - you will be confirmed in first class seats. Upon arrival at St Pierre des Corps you will meet your English-speaking driver-guide for a sightseeing tour of the Loire Valley by private vehicle.

    You will visit the Chateau de Chenonceau that straddles the river and is one of the best-known chateaux of the Loire region. Continuing on to Amboise you will visit the Clos Lucé, a handsome Renaissance manor where Leonardo da Vinci spent the last four years of his life. The museum contains working models of some of da Vinci's inventions. Return to Paris by TGV where you will be met by your driver and privately transferred back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel Meurice

  • Day 5

    PARIS - VERSAILLES - GIVERNY - PARIS

    Today you will enjoy a full day sightseeing with your private vehicle to Versailles and Giverny. You will drive out to Versailles to admire the huge palace of the great Sun King, Louis XIV, visiting the state apartments and decorative gardens.

    After lunch at leisure continue on to Normandy to see the house and gardens that inspired Monet in Giverny. The house is decorated in the original colour schemes admired by Monet and in the gardens you will see the famous Japanese bridge and water lily pond.

    Overnight: Hotel Meurice

  • Day 6

    PARIS - AVIGNON

    Today you will be met at your hotel and privately transferred to the railway station. Here you will be shown to your reserved first class seating and your luggage will be handled onboard for you. Upon arrival at Avignon railway station you will be met from the door of the train and your luggage will be handled for you. You will then be privately transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in.

    This afternoon meet your English-speaking local guide for a half day walking tour in the magnificent papal stronghold of Avignon. Visit the Clock Square before entering the huge Palais des Papes, where seven exiled popes lived between 1309 and 1377 and where the main rooms are decorated with excellent 14th century frescoes.

    This afternoon, discover Provence in a Citroen 2CV with your own private English-speaking driver guide. Experience the small winding streets of Avignon in a traditional French car and receive a unique view of Provencal daily life.

    Overnight: Hotel de la Mirande

  • Day 7

    AVIGNON - ST REMY - LES BAUX - AVIGNON

    This morning is at leisure. This afternoon you may wish to drive out to the countryside to have a rustic lunch of fresh local specialities with a true Provencal peasant and visit the medieval chapel and cloisters of the asylum into which Van Gogh voluntarily admitted himself.

    See the ruined medieval citadel of Les Baux de Provence, perched atop a rock dominating the surrounding countryside, and the truly astonishing "Cathedral of Images" where overblown coloured photographs depicting the life and masterpieces of Picasso are projected onto the walls of a huge cave, to the accompaniment of atmospheric music.

    Overnight: Hotel de la Mirande

  • Day 8

    AVIGNON - LUBERON - AIX EN PROVENCE

    The Lubéron, inspiration for the book A Year in Provence by Peter Mayle, is a region of rolling hills and verdant nature dotted with churches and rural communities. Drive to the great 12th century Cistercian Abbey of Sénanque, isolated in a ravine deep in the heart of the countryside, and to Gordes whose cobbled lanes and sweeping views over the surrounding countryside are truly enchanting.

    Stop en route in Cavaillon for a Melon Discovery Tour (available June to September only - this is a public tour). The tour includes a visit to a melon farm where full explanations behind the famous melon de Cavaillon will be given. Tasting of the local delicacies is a must before heading towards Aix en Provence where you will check in to your hotel.

    Overnight: Villa Gallici

  • Day 9

    AIX EN PROVENCE

    This morning your local English-speaking guide will meet you for a half day walking tour of Aix, once the capital of Provence. Enjoy the bustling Cours Mirabeau, the heart of the town, and a visit to Cézanne's studio. It was here that Cézanne painted some of his most well-known works. There is also a striking collection of simple objects which once featured in his still life paintings.

    This afternoon, enjoy a private painting lesson where you will create your own masterpiece which your local representative will mail home to await your return.

    Overnight: Villa Gallici

  • Day 10

    AIX - MOUGINS

    Today you will travel to the Côte D'Azur.

    En route, visit Mont Sainte Victoire, a local landmark made famous by Cézanne, and the renowned Chateau de Vauvenargues, Picasso's final home and resting place. Public visits to Picasso's burial site will only be possible for four months during summer 2009, the first time since his death.

    You may also like to visit the "village in the sky" of Tourtour, officially one of the most beautiful villages in France. Along the way you will enjoy lunch with a local basket weaver and his wife who crafts pottery. All of the food for the meal is produced on their farm.

    Driving through the Var region, with a stop at the simple medieval abbey of Thoronet, before making your way on to the medieval hilltop village of Mougins.

    Overnight: Le Mas Candille

  • Day 11

    MOUGINS - GRASSE - MOUGINS

    Visit the International Perfume Museum of Grasse where you will be given the unique opportunity to step into the shoes of a professional Perfumer and create your own signature blend. You will even decide on the bottle you wish to use and the packaging so that you can take your bespoke fragrance home with you. Afterwards your driver will meet you for your return journey back to Mougins.

    Overnight: Le Mas Candille

  • Day 12

    MOUGINS - CANNES - MOUGINS

    Your day starts with a trip to the famous local market in Cannes, Marché De Forville, where you will select the ingredients for your chosen recipe.

    Le Mas Candille is renowned for its cuisine and cookery classes and it is here that you will spend the rest of the morning in the company of their Michelin-starred chef, Serge Gouloumés learning the art of Provençal cuisine.

    This afternoon is free to explore Mougins at leisure.

    Overnight: Le Mas Candille

  • Day 13

    MOUGINS - NICE - ST JEAN CAP FERRAT - MOUGINS

    Today meet your local English-speaking guide and private vehicle for a full day sightseeing tour to Nice, St Jean-Cap Ferrat and Eze. This morning you will have the opportunity to explore the historic atmosphere of Nice's Old Town and colourful market. You will also visit the Matisse Museum which houses a large collection of the artist's work.

    Continue on to the port of St Jean Cap Ferrat and the beautiful Villa Ephrussi de Rothschild, follow by Eze and its jaw-dropping views over the Mediterranean sea.

    Overnight: Le Mas Candille

  • Day 14

    DEPART MOUGINS

    Transfer to Nice airport for your return flight home.

French Honeymoon

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 20 Days

    Highlights: • Sightseeing of Paris city including major highlights and evening enjoy romantic 4 Course menu dinner including live musical animation.
    • Sightseeing tour to Versailles with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. Situated to the west of Paris, Versailles is the location of the huge palace that was constructed in the 17th century for Louis XIV, the 'Sun King'.
    • Full day (8 hour) sightseeing tour including Beaune and Nuits Saint Georges, well known as the wine capital of Burgundy.
    • Opportunity to visit Aiguille du Midi by Cable Car (highest point of Europe) or visit the Mer de Glace (famous glacier which is about to disappear slowly and gradually in coming years)

  • Day 1

    India/Paris

    Arrival Paris
    Upon arrival at the Paris airport, you will be met and transferred by your Guardian Angel and private vehicle with English-speaking driver to your hotel in Paris, where you will be assisted with check-in.

    Day at leisure.
    This evening discover Paris, the city of lights and enjoy a dinner cruise on the Seine.
    8:30PM board an entirely glass-encased boat for your romantic dinner including live musical animation. Your 4-course menu will include some wine (1 bottle for 4 guests) and a glass of champagne with dessert.
    Special Offer - Value Added Honeymoon Couples - Bouquet of Flowers and Bottle of Champagne

    Overnight: Sofitel Paris Le Faubourg
    Luxury Double/Twin
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    Paris

    Today you will enjoy a full day (8 hours) sightseeing tour in Paris with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. This morning an overview tour will include famous Parisian sights such as the Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Champs Elysées and the Ile de la Cité, where you will visit the beautiful Notre Dame Cathedral and the Sainte Chapelle, a pearl of Gothic architecture. This afternoon visit the world-famous Louvre Museum. Originally the Royal residence of the Kings of France, the sprawling Louvre palace contains one of the most important art collections in the Western world, spanning from remotest antiquity to the mid 19th cent, with the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo as perhaps its two most famous exhibits, and the upturned glass pyramid by I.M. Pei at the entrance adding a contemporary touch.

    Optional/ alternative tour & NOT included in the itinerary

    This afternoon your guide will take you to a renowned "Parfumeur" for a unique private workshop (4hrs). You will step behind the scenes and learn about the key stages in the composition of a fragrance. You will discover the techniques and the ingredients before becoming a "nose" yourself! You will work with a selection of high quality ingredients and design your own personal blend of notes. You will name and label your 17.5 fl oz bottle of personal fragrance and take it away with you as a souvenir of this olfactory experience.

    9.00 PM - enjoy the cabaret theatre show at the Moulin Rouge (1/2 bottle of Champagne included). See the world-renowned showgirls and French Cancan dancers on the Moulin Rouge's historic stage.

    Make your own way back to the hotel after the show.

    Overnight: Sofitel Paris Le Faubourg
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Paris/ Versailles/ Paris

    Enjoy a half-day (up to 5 hours) sightseeing tour to Versailles with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. Situated to the west of Paris, Versailles is the location of the huge palace that was constructed in the 17th century for Louis XIV, the 'Sun King'. It became the model for grand houses of royalty all over Europe. Take a tour of the opulent State Apartments that culminate in the magnificent Gallery of Mirrors. You may like to take a stroll in the formal ornamental gardens that are embellished with many fountains, and see the Grand Canal that stretches into the distance across the park. Return to Paris following your visit.

    Inclusions: Transfer in a private, air-conditioned vehicle with English-speaking driver (1-2 pax Citroen C6 or similar, Services of an English-speaking local guide, Entrance fees to Versailles Palace Gardens and State Apartments

    Exclusions: Any meals or entrance fees not specified & Tips

    Overnight: Sofitel Paris Le Faubourg
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Paris/ Dijon

    Morning after breakfast, check out and you will be transferred to the station to board train to Dijon.

    Concierge Rail service from Paris to Dijon.

    After lunch at leisure, meet your local guide for a 3-hr walking tour of the city. Once the capital city of the mighty Dukes of Burgundy before coming under the control of the French crown, Dijon is still the capital of the Burgundy region and its wonderful old quarters are among the most interesting in France. Stroll through a network of narrow streets and small squares with fine 15th-16th century mansions and the 13th century gothic church of Notre-Dame before coming up behind the Ducal Palace, which houses a huge museum of French and Flemish art. Full of fascinating shops, Dijon's famous mustard is sold everywhere as well as the blackcurrant liqueur.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel La Cloche
    Superior Double
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Burgundy Region

    This morning you will meet your private vehicle and English-speaking driver-guide for a 3-hr walking tour of the city First, visit Beaune, known as the wine capital of Burgundy, where will visit the Hospices de Beaune (also known as the Hotel Dieu) a former hospital founded in 1443 to provide free care for men who had fought in the Hundred Years War. Today Beaune is famous for its tiled roofs and Flemish architecture which have become icons of Burgundy. Continue on to the wine country between Beaune and Dijon and visit the Clos de Vougeot famous for its wine-making barn. Also see the famous villages such as Gevrey-Chambertin or Marsannay-la-Côte before returning to Dijon.

    Overnight: Hotel Sofitel La Cloche
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    Dijon/ Lyon

    Today you will be met at your hotel and privately transferred with your Guardian Angel to the Dijon railway station. Here you will be shown to your pre-reserved 1st class seating.

    Upon arrival at the Lyon railway station an E/S assistant will meet you and accompany you for your transfer to the hotel.

    This afternoon embark on a HD (up to 5hrs) city tour with your guide. The capital of the Rhone-Alps region, Lyon is the second largest conurbation in France. Two broad rivers, the Saône and the Rhône run majestically through the heart of the city, south of which they merge. The long narrow strip between the two rivers is thecenter of Lyon: Place Carnot,
    Place Bellecour and Place des Terreaux are linked by fashionable shopping streets and surrounded by fine Renaissance buildings. Across the Saône, Le Vieux Lyon, is a picturesque and well-restored district with numerous impressive Gothic and Renaissance buildings. Visit the The Maison des Canuts (silk workers of the 19th century) is a living museum that reveals the richness and quality of the silk industry in Lyon and it is the only place where manual looms, invented by Jacquard, are in use. Your tour will give you the opportunity to learn about the history of silk, the life of the silk workers.

    Overnight: Sofitel Lyon Bellecour
    Superior Queen Room
    Meal Plan: Meals: B

  • Day 7

    Lyon at leisure

    Full day at leisure in Lyon.
    You can choose from Plenty of activities from Cruise on river or to take tram ride to visit the highest point and famous church of Lyon or may be have a leisure walk in the city to understand local people and their culture

    Overnight: Sofitel Lyon Bellecour
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    Lyon / Annecy

    Private transfer from Lyon to Annecy with English speaking driver. (Approx 3 hours drive)

It is known as "Europe's cleanest lake" because of strict environmental regulations introduced in the 1960s. It is a popular tourist destination known for its swimming and water sports. The lake was formed about 18,000 years ago, at the time the large alpine glaciers melted. It is fed by many small rivers from the surrounding mountains (Ire, Eau morte, Laudon, Bornette and Biolon), and from a powerful underwater source, the Boubioz, which enters at 82 m depth.

    Rest of the day at leisure to explore the place on your own.

    Overnight: Hôtel Impérial, Annecy
    Deluxe with park view
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Annecy/ Chamonix

    Private transfer from Annecy to Chamonix with English speaking driver.

    Rest of the day at leisure to enjoy the many activities the resort has to offer!

    Cham' is a destination popular the world over. In the streets of this high-mountain town you'll hear languages from every corner of the globe. But the people who come to this 'summit meeting' share the same way of thinking, the same love for mountaineering, its legends and its ethics. When you're out shopping in the town centre, wherever you are look up and you'll see 'it' there. 4,810 metres (15,781 ft) of rock, snow, ice and dreams of things to come.

    Overnight: Hameau Albert 1
    Classic Double/Twin
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    Chamonix

    At leisure to go up the Aiguille du Midi by Cable Car or visit the Mer de Glace (Glacier)

    Overnight: Hameau Albert 1
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    Chamonix

    At leisure on your own.

    You may choose from ample activities available from River rafting to Luge ride, spa to fishing etc.

    Overnight: Hameau Albert 1
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 12

    Chamonix/ Geneva/ Nice

    Private transfer from Chamonix to Geneva airport to board flight to Nice (NOT included in tour cost)

    Upon arrival in Nice you will be met by your local guardian angel and transferred to your hotel in Nice.

    Rest of the day is free on your own. Overnight stay at the hotel.

    Overnight: Le Méridien Nice
    Deluxe Double/twin
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 13

    Nice

    Today meet your local English-speaking guide for half-day sightseeing (4hrs) in Nice. You will explore the beautiful Old Town, and colorful open-air market and take the lift up to the Château Hill (Colline du Château) area for unrivalled views of the city.

    Inclusions: Services of an English-speaking local guide, Lift to the Château Hill Exclusions: Any meals or entrance fees not specified & Tips

    After lunch at leisure, set off to spend the afternoon exploring the Principality of Monaco with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide (4hrs). This exceptional city-state is just east of Nice, a land steeped in history and also a Mediterranean mini Las Vegas. The capital, Monaco, is a fairy tale city standing on a rocky headland almost surrounded by sea, and contains the palace complete with guards, the tropical gardens offering excellent views, and the museum containing Roman antiquities and prehistory collections.

    Look around the Place du Casino, possibly the most famous casino in the world, in the center of Monte Carlo. Go on to the well-known Rock, crowned by the Palais Princier (open April through October) , where the royal family lives, seeing the extravagance of the 16th and 17th century frescoes, as well as tapestries, gilt furniture and paintings.

    Overnight:Le Méridien Nice
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 14

    Nice/ Carcassonne

    Private transfer from Nice to Carcassonne with English Speaking driver. Stop en route (about 1 hr) for lunch at leisure.

    Transfer time = approx. 5 hours

    As there is not direct train between the 2 cities, we have based the transfer by private vehicle.

    Overnight: Hotel de la Cite
    Deluxe Double/Twin Classic Room with city view
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 15

    Carcassonne/Bordeaux

    Morning enjoy 2,5hrs hour walking tour of the medieval walled-city with a local guide.

    Evening transfer to Carcassonne station to board a direct train to Bordeaux.

    Upon arrival in Bordeaux, meet your local guardian angel and driver for your transfer to the Grand Hotel .Evening at leisure.

    Overnight: Grand Hotel de Bordeaux & Spa
    Superior Double
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 16

    Bordeaux

    Today you experience historic Bordeaux with your guide on a walking tour (3hrs) to see some of the citys famous monuments and buildings. See the 18th-century Place de la Bourse and the 13th-century Cathedral of St. Andre, now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Admire the neoclassical facades of the Bordeaux Law Courts, St. Andre Hospital and the 18th-century Palais Rohan, now the City Hall. When you explore Old Bordeaux, walk past the Cailhau Gate and the Grosse Cloche, the former belfry of the town hall in the Middle Ages.

    Afternoon at leisure to do some shopping or enjoy the hotels spa.

    Optional - 6hr excursion to St-Emilion with English Speaking driver-guide

    Only a short distance from Bordeaux, the village of St. Emilion was built into a limestone hill. The cobbled streets of this medieval town are lined with stores selling the wines the region is known for. In the eighth century, Emilion, a hermit, lived in a cave with an underground spring. Over the next 500 years, his cave was enlarged to create Eglise Monolithe, one of Frances largest underground rock churches. Visit the cave and then take time to stop at some of the local vineyards, like the famous Château Franc Mayne or Chateau La Tour du Pin Figeac

    Overnight: Grand Hotel de Bordeaux & Spa
    Superior Double
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 17

    Bordeaux/St Pierre des Corps

    Your GA and driver will accompany you to Bordeaux station for your direct high speed train (TGV) to St Pierre des Corps.

    Upon arrival in St Pierre des Corps you will have a short transfer to Tours city center. Balance of the day at leisure.

    You may decide to look around Tours independently. Tours is a city that grew around the tomb of St Martin, its 3rd bishop at the end of the 4th century. It has now become the largest town in Touraine. Rich merchants built its Renaissance and neo-classic mansions and it is home to the Musée des Beaux Arts, a remarkable series of salons decorated in silk and wood panelling. See the Cathedral of St. Gatien with its Renaissance lanterns and 13th century stained glass as well as the Place Plumereau and Rue Briconnet, center of the old medieval quarter.
