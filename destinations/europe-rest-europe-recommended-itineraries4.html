AUSTRIA AND SOUTHERN BAVARIA

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 12 days

    A 12-day FIT itinerary taking in the highlights of Austria and Southern Bavaria.

    Highlights: Vienna, Salzburg, Innsbruck, Munich.

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE VIENNA

    Upon arrival at Vienna Airport you will be met by your local representative and transferred to your hotel. The remainder of the day will be at leisure to relax after your flight.

    Located on the Ringstrasse, the Hotel Imperial's central location offers easy access to the nearby Opera House, concert halls, museums and shopping district. The grand marble staircase, crystal chandeliers and splendid public rooms all help to create an air of sophistication and elegance. Facilities include the Imperial Restaurant, the Imperial Café (famed for its torte) and piano bar.

    Overnight: Hotel Imperial

  • Day 2

    VIENNA

    Today you will be met by your private English-speaking guide for a half day walking tour of the imperial city of Vienna. Your tour will commence at the Hofburg complex, the residence of the Habsburg family who ruled the Austro-Hungarian Empire until 1918. You may be able to see a training session of the famous Spanish Riding School (timetable and ticket availability permitting). You will also visit St Stephen's Cathedral whose soaring structure enshrines the heart of Vienna.

    The afternoon will be at leisure for you to enjoy. Why not visit the new Museums Quarter? A late baroque palace, it has recently been converted to house a variety of interesting museums, including the Museum of Modern Art, as well as contemporary exhibitions. Or perhaps enjoy a shopping spree on Vienna's famous Kärtnerstrasse.

    Overnight: Hotel Imperial
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    VIENNA & SCHÖNBRUNN PALACE

    This morning your private vehicle and English-speaking guide will take you away from the centre of Vienna to Schönbrunn Palace, which was the vast summer residence of the Habsburgs. Schönbrunn Palace takes its name from a beautiful spring that was found here. The spectacular setting of the palace and the gardens is a prime example of Baroque planning. The beauty of the strict architectural symmetry is further enhanced by its gardens with their fountains, trees and alleyways.

    The afternoon will be at leisure for shopping or some independent sightseeing.

    Overnight: Hotel Imperial
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    VIENNA & VIENNA WOODS

    Leave the city of Vienna behind this morning with your English-speaking driver-guide and private vehicle for a sightseeing excursion to the charming Vienna Woods. Visit Mayerling, scene of the suicide pact between Crown Prince Rudolph and Baroness Maria Vetsera. Then visit a hunting lodge which was replaced by a convent by the bereaved Emperor. Continue to nearby Heiligenkreuz, a magnificent Cistercian Abbey before returning back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel Imperial
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    VIENNA-DANUBE VALLEY-SALZBURG

    This morning you will transfer with your driver-guide and private vehicle towards Salzburg along the beautiful Danube Valley. En route sightseeing will include the town of Krems where you will visit a 14th century former cloister, then on to Durnstein which is arguably the most beautiful of the region's villages and finally to Melk where you will see the magnificent abbey.

    The Goldener Hirsch is a personally run hotel, rich in tradition. The hotel consists of four townhouses, one of which is across the street from the others. Guestrooms are stylishly furnished with antiques and enjoy all modern services. Other facilities at the hotel include a gourmet restaurant, a traditional tavern and a bar.

    Overnight: Hotel Goldener Hirsch
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    SALZBURG

    This morning you will enjoy a walking tour of the city. Start from your hotel with an English-speaking local guide who will show you the most important sights in this "Mozart City" such as the Getreidegasse with Mozart's birthplace, the "Pferdeschwemme" (Royal Horse Trough), St Peter's Abbey and cemetery. You will also visit the Hohensalzburg Fortress, which is the largest of its kind in Europe with lavish staterooms such as the Golden Room and marvellous views of Salzburg and the surrounding countryside. Your walking tour will end at the Mirabell Palace and Gardens, best known for being part of the background to "The Sound of Music".

    After lunch at leisure your private vehicle and English-speaking guide will take you to Hellbrunn Palace (4 hrs). You will have plenty of time to see this palace in detail, as well as the beautiful gardens and park - sixty acres in total - as well as the aquatic gardens with their magical world of trick fountains, grottos and unpredictable water jets. You will also visit the Sound of Music Pavilion and the "Roman Theatre". Time permitting your driver will also take you past Schloss Leopoldskron (not open to the public) with a stop for taking some lovely photos across the lake.

    Overnight: Hotel Goldener Hirsch
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    SALZBURG-SALZKAMMERGUT-SALZBURG

    You will enjoy a full day of sightseeing (8 hours) with your driver-guide and private vehicle to the Salzkammergut Lake District, located outside Salzburg. Begin in Fuschl on Lake Fuschl, which is renowned for its pure blue deep waters. Then travel on to St. Wolfgang, which is set against a dramatic backdrop of mountains and home to the White Horse Inn where you may wish to savour a coffee and applestrudel with cream on the terrace. The last port of call today is the town of Bad Ischl, best known for its fine tortes (pastries). You will visit the yellow Kaiservilla, coloured as a sign of the wealth and power of Emperor Franz Joseph, who spent so many summers here.

    Overnight: Hotel Goldener Hirsch
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    SALZBURG-KITZBUHEL-IGLS

    You will travel today with your driver-guide and private vehicle into the Tirol region, where you will stop at Kitzbuhel, Austria's most fashionable ski resort, for a stroll through its picturesque town centre and a visit to the rococo chapel of St. Andrew, before continuing your journey to Igls situated just outside Innsbruck.

    The privately owned Schlosshotel Igls meets the demands of the most discerning guests. Leisure facilities include both an indoor and an outdoor swimming pool, sauna, steam bath, solarium and massage room.

    Overnight: Schlosshotel Igls
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    IGLS-INNSBRUCK-IGLS

    This morning you will be transferred by private car to Innsbruck for a three hour walking tour of the city with a local English-speaking guide. The most impressive feature is the Golden Roof which is made of gilded tiles of gold and covers the house built in 1420 for Duke Friedrich. Historically the most important site that you will visit will be the Hofburg Imperial Palace. Built by Maximilian, this rococo palace has been added to by generations of the family until the 1800s. Close by lies the Hofkirch (Court Church), built as a mausoleum for Maximilian I, and the 16th century Silver Chapel with its elaborate altar and silver Madonna.

    You will have some time at leisure in Innsbruck for lunch and some independent sightseeing, before you are privately transferred back to your hotel in Igls.

    Overnight: Schlosshotel Igls
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    IGLS-NEUSCHWANSTEIN CASTLE-MUNICH

    After breakfast set off with your driver-guide and private vehicle to cross the mountains into Germany, where you will stop at Neuschwanstein Castle. Due to its magical beauty it attracts many visitors from near and far, and it is best visited early before it gets too busy. Looking more like a stage set than a real palace, it is no wonder Walt Disney used it as the model for his sleeping beauty castle and later Disneyland castle itself. Its construction almost brought Bavaria to bankruptcy and Ludwig's early death in the lake is rumoured to be closely connected with this. Do try to find time to walk to the Marienbrucke (Mary's Bridge) crossing a deep, narrow gorge. From this vantage point you have the best and probably most photographed views of this stunning fairytale castle. (Please note this visit is NOT suitable for anyone with walking problems.)

    After some free time for souvenir hunting you will continue your journey to Munich.

    The boutique-style Mandarin Oriental is a beautifully renovated neo-Renaissance building and all the bedrooms are individually furnished. It has a rooftop outdoor heated freshwater pool (open in the summer) as well as an in-house fitness room.

    Overnight: Mandarin Oriental
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    MUNICH

    This morning your local English-speaking guide will accompany you on a three hour walking tour through the compact Old Town centre with its multitude of fantastic shops, markets and always busy beer halls such as the "Hofbrauhaus" that Munich is so famous for. See the Karlsplatz fountain, the Frauenkirche - Munich's Cathedral - as well as the Marienplatz with the New Rathaus and its chiming clock, playing daily at 11.00am and noon.

    In the afternoon enjoy an orientation tour (3 hours) of Munich by private vehicle with your English-speaking local guide, passing the Olympia stadium and including a visit to Nymphenburg Palace.

    Overnight: Mandarin Oriental
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 12

    DEPART MUNICH

    This morning you will be privately transferred to Munich airport for your departure flight.

    Meal Plan: B

HIGHLIGHTS OF BUDAPEST, VIENNA & PRAGUE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 days

    A tour of some of the best sights and sites in Budapest, Vienna and Prague.

    Highlights: Budapest, Vienna, Prague

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE BUDAPEST

    Upon arrival at the Budapest airport you will be met by your local representative, welcomed to Hungary and shown to your waiting car. You will then be privately transferred to your hotel, located in the center of Budapest, where you will be assisted with check-in procedures.

    The remainder of the day will be at your leisure.

    You will be confirmed for three nights at the Budapest Hilton.

    Overnight: Budapest Hilton Hotel

  • Day 2

    BUDAPEST

    Enjoy a half-day tour (4 hours) of the Buda side of the Danube river with you English-speaking guide for a walking tour. In this ancient Royal district you will visit the Matthias Church, which has witnessed the coronation of many Hungarian Kings, and enjoy stunning panoramas of the city and river from the Fishermen's Bastion. The Bastion affords fantastic views across the whole city. Also visit the former Royal Palace which houses the Hungarian National Gallery, Budapest History Museum and the National Széchenyi Library. Your guide will escort you back to your hotel on foot.

    Overnight: Budapest Hilton Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    BUDAPEST

    Today meet your English-speaking local guide and private vehicle for a half day sightseeing tour (4hrs) on the Pest side of the Danube. Beginning at the market hall (only open in the morning Monday to Saturday) you will see the vast array of provisions and craftwares on sale. Continuing on your tour you will visit the ornate Synagogue which is the largest in Europe and also its Jewish Museum. See the vast Heroes' Square with its impressive Millennial Monument, and St. Stephen's Basilica, the city's largest church. You will also enjoy a private tour (approx. 1 hour and subject to theater scheduling) of the palatial Opera House, built between 1875 and 1884 and painstakingly restored after being severely damaged during the siege of the city in 1944-5 alternatively you may like to enjoy a viewing of the Crown Jewels in the Parliament Building.

    Overnight: Budapest Hilton Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    BUDAPEST - VIENNA

    You will be privately transferred to the station and your local representative will show you to your pre-reserved seating in the First Class carriage of the train bound for Vienna. The journey takes approximately three hours.

    Upon arrival into Vienna you will be met from the train door by your local representative and privately transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in.

    In the afternoon meet your English-speaking local guide today for a half-day walking tour (4 hours) of the Imperial city of Vienna. Walk along the famous 'Ringstrasse' with its 19th century buildings and visit the Hofburg Palace complex, the residence of the Habsburg family who ruled the Austro-Hungarian Empire until 1918, and the gothic St Stephen's Cathedral, whose soaring structure enshrines the heart of Vienna.

    Overnight: Sacher Wien
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    VIENNA

    Meet your English-speaking local guide today and travel outside the city center by private vehicle for a half-day excursion (up to 4 hours) to the magnificent Schönbrunn Palace with its beautiful grounds. Schönbrunn Palace was the vast summer residence of the Habsburg's and takes its name from a beautiful spring found on the estate. The beauty of the strict architectural symmetry is further enhanced by its gardens with fountains, trees and alleyways. Your guide will accompany you through many of the palace staterooms including the 'Hall of Mirrors' in which Mozart performed for the Empress Maria Theresa when he was only six years old. Return to Vienna for a further half-day tour (4 hours) into Vienna's musical past. Your guide will show you the places where the great composers lived, worked and played. Your guide will also tell you some history about the composers and their music.

    Overnight: Sacher Wien
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    VIENNA - PRAGUE

    You will be privately transferred to the station and your local representative will show you to your pre-reserved seating in the First Class carriage of the train bound for Prague. The journey takes approximately four and a half hours.

    Upon arrival into Prague you will be met from the train door by your local representative and privately transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in.

    You will be confirmed at Alchymist Grand Hotel & Spa.

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    PRAGUE

    Meet your English-speaking local guide and enjoy a half-day walking tour (4 hours) of the Old Town, with its cobbled streets, and the Jewish Quarter, birthplace of Franz Kafka. See the Gothic Town Hall in Old Town Square, famous for its astronomical clock. Visit the Jewish Museum which houses the largest collection of Judaica in Europe, the Old New Synagogue and the Jewish Cemetery. You may also enjoy a walk across the 14th century Charles Bridge.

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    PRAGUE

    Today you will enjoy a full day sightseeing tour (8 hours) of Prague's Castle District and art museums with your English-speaking local guide and private vehicle. Hradcany Castle was the seat of Bohemian kings and Holy Roman emperors. Visit the Cathedral of St. Vitus, one of the finest and most richly endowed in central Europe and the Basilica of St. George, which was founded in 905. Stroll along the Golden Lane, a row of tiny houses dating from the 16th century. Continue to the Sanctuary of Our Lady of Loreto, an important place of pilgrimage, and the Strahov Monastery with its glorious Baroque decorations.

    After lunch at leisure you will visit the Troja Palace. Troja was built in the late 17th century by Jean-Baptiste Mathey for Count Sternberg, a member of leading Bohemian aristocratic family. Situated at the foot of the Vltava Heights, the exterior of the palace was modeled on a Classical Italian villa, while its garden was laid out in formal French style.

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    DEPART PRAGUE

    You will be privately transferred to Prague Airport.

    Meal Plan: B

DISCOVER BUDAPEST, VIENNA AND PRAGUE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 13 days

    Enjoy this 13-day journey through the most beautiful capitals in Central Europe.

    Highlights: Budapest, Danube Valley, Godollo, Holloko, Bratislava, Vienna, Mauthausen, Cesky Krumlov, Terezin, Nelahozeves, Prague.

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE BUDAPEST

    Upon arrival at Budapest Airport you will be welcomed to Hungary in the arrivals hall by your local representative, and transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in procedures.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Gresham Palace

  • Day 2

    BUDAPEST

    Today meet your English-speaking local guide and private vehicle for a full day sightseeing tour. This morning start on the Pest side of the Danube. Beginning at the market hall (only open in the morning) you will see the vast array of provisions and craftwares on sale. Continuing on your tour you will visit the ornate synagogue which is the largest in Europe and is also home to the Jewish Museum. See the vast Heroes' Square with its impressive Millennial Monument, and St. Stephen's Basilica, the city's largest church. You will also enjoy a viewing of the Crown Jewels in the Parliament Building and a private tour (approx. one hour and subject to theatre scheduling) of the palatial Opera House, built between 1875 and 1884 and painstakingly restored after being severely damaged during the siege of the city in 1944-5. In addition we can include a private opera recital where two arias will be performed on the sweeping stairs of the opera house.

    This afternoon cross the graceful Chain Bridge over the Danube to Buda and ascend by funicular to Castle Hill where the former Royal Palace gracefully towers over the Danube. Explore the cobbled and colourful streets of this ancient royal district and visit the Matthias Church, which has witnessed the coronation of many Hungarian Kings. Enjoy stunning panoramas of the city and river from the Fishermen's Bastion and the nearby Gellért Hill before returning to your hotel.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Gresham Palace
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    BUDAPEST - DANUBE VALLEY - BUDAPEST

    Meet your English-speaking local guide and private vehicle this morning for a full day sightseeing excursion to the picturesque Danube Bend area of Hungary, the centre of culture during the Hungarian Renaissance. Visit the preserved Baroque town of Szentendre, a flourishing artist's community with a profusion of cobbled streets, church spires and colourful houses. Visit the Margit Kovács Museum which displays many of the artists' best pieces from fine ceramics to life-size sculptures, and the Blagovestenska Church, a red roccoco masterpiece. Continue to Visegrád, the royal seat in medieval times, which offers an imposing fortress and the partially restored palace of the Anjou kings. Here you will also visit the ancient citadel. Visit Esztergom, the seat of the Hungarian Catholic Church, and visit the Basilica, Crypt and Treasury containing an impressive collection of priceless treasures. Return to your hotel in Budapest at the end of the afternoon.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Gresham Palace
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    BUDAPEST - GODOLLO - HOLLOKO - BUDAPEST

    This morning enjoy a full day excursion into the Hungarian Great Plain. Stop in Godollo to visit Grassalkovich Palace, once the summer residence of Hungary's Queen Elisabeth (Sissy) and the world's second largest Baroque castle. The castle served as a Soviet army base during the Communist era, however it has now been transformed back to its former glory. Continue your sightseeing to Holloko, a traditional village featured in the UNESCO World Heritage list, where 50 traditional Hungarian houses have been declared historic monuments. The handmade wooden houses are clustered around the ruins of a 13th century castle. After your visit you will be transferred back to your hotel in Budapest.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Gresham Palace
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    BUDAPEST - VIENNA

    This morning transfer by private vehicle to the Austrian capital of Vienna. Before entering Austria enjoy a stop at Pannonhalma where your guide will accompany you on a tour of the 1000-year-old Benedictine Abbey which is still inhabited by monks to this day. Upon arrival at your hotel in Vienna, you will be met by your Guardian Angel and assisted with check-in.

    Overnight: Hotel Imperial
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    VIENNA - BRATISLAVA - VIENNA

    Today enjoy exclusive Captain's lounge seating on a catamaran from Vienna to Bratislava and back.

    Upon arrival in Bratislava port meet a local English-speaking guide for your tour. Bratislava is one of the youngest capitals in Europe and few people know that during the time it was called Pressburg or Pozsony it was one of the most important cities in the Austro-Hungarian Empire. You can start your sightseeing tour in the city centre in Primate's Square where the most striking building is the Primate's Palace. The palace houses the world's most comprehensive collection of tapestries, manufactured in an English royal factory from the 17th century. Continue to the Old Town Hall. Located on the left side of the Town Hall's tower you will see the Jesuit Church, boasting its spectacular Baroque inventory. Also see Bratislava Castle (closed till end of 2010), a national cultural monument standing on a strategic site, settled as early as the Celtic Era and the Great Moravian Era. Today, it houses exhibitions of the Slovak National Museum.

    Overnight: Hotel Imperial
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    VIENNA - VIENNA WOODS - VIENNA

    Meet your English-speaking local guide today for a half-day tour of the Imperial city of Vienna. Walk along the famous 'Ringstrasse' with its 19th century buildings and visit the Hofburg Palace complex, the residence of the Habsburg family who ruled the Austro-Hungarian Empire until 1918 (closed on Saturdays and Sundays). You will attend the masses in the Chapel of the Hofburg Palace, performed by the Hofmusikkapelle comprising the Vienna's Boys Choir. and members of the chorus and orchestra of the Vienna State Opera (Sundays only, starting at 9.15 am). After the performance continue your tour of the centre of Vienna by horse carriage and also visit the gothic St Stephen's Cathedral, whose soaring structure enshrines the heart of Vienna. You will be taken back to your hotel by horse carriage.

    This afternoon visit the charming Vienna Woods. Visit Mayerling, scene of the suicide pact between Crown Prince Rudolph and Baroness Maria Vetsera. Then a hunting lodge, it was replaced by a convent by the bereaved Emperor. Continue to nearby Heiligenkreuz, a magnificent Cistercian abbey. On the return trip to Vienna stop at the Seegrotte, a former mine and now a fascinating underground lake, where you can take a motor boat trip for the best view of the reflections before returning back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel Imperial
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    VIENNA - SCHONBRUNN & DANCING LESSON - VIENNA

    This morning you will travel outside the city centre with your driver-guide for an excursion to the magnificent Schönbrunn Palace with its beautiful grounds. Schönbrunn Palace was the vast summer residence of the Habsburgs and takes its name from a beautiful spring found on the estate. The spectacular setting and symmetry of the palace and the gardens are a prime example of Baroque planning. Your guide will accompany you through many of the palace staterooms including the Hall of Mirrors in which Mozart performed for the Empress Maria Theresa when he was only six years old.

    Vienna is the world's capital of music and one of its most sought-after "export goods" is the Viennese Waltz, the dance in three quarter time that waltz king Johann Strauss triumphantly took across the globe. This afternoon you will have the opportunity to take an individual Viennese Waltz lesson (in English) in an exclusive baroque ballroom. After your introductory lesson you will have a traditional Viennese "pause" with coffee or sparkling wine and cake. At the end of the lesson you will receive a certificate authenticating your efforts.

    Overnight: Hotel Imperial
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    VIENNA -TELC - PRAGUE

    Depart Vienna this morning with your English-speaking driver and transfer by private vehicle to Prague. Stop en route (up to 2 hours) at the medieval Czech town of Telc, famous for its fine Gothic, Renaissance and Baroque buildings, where you can visit the Abbey at your leisure. Upon arrival at your hotel in Prague you will be met by your Guardian Angel and assisted with check-in.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Prague
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    PRAGUE

    Meet your English-speaking local guide and enjoy a half-day walking tour of the Old Town with its cobbled streets, and the Jewish Quarter, birthplace of Franz Kafka. See the Gothic Town Hall in Old Town Square, famous for its astronomical clock. Visit the Jewish Museum which houses the largest collection of Judaica in Europe, the Old New Synagogue and the Jewish Cemetery. You will also visit the Estate Theatre for a private musical walk through the theatre lasting about one hour with explanation about the renovation, history and work of W.A. Mozart in this theatre. This visit will also include a concert with the work of Mozart and his colleagues from the same time.

    This afternoon enjoy a half-day sightseeing tour of Prague's Castle District with your English-speaking local guide. You will travel by private vintage tram from a tram stop near your hotel to a stop near Prague Castle. Hradcany Castle was the seat of Bohemian kings and Holy Roman emperors. Visit the Cathedral of St. Vitus, one of the finest and most richly endowed in central Europe, and the Basilica of St. George which was founded in 905. Stroll along the Golden Lane, a row of tiny houses dating from the 16th century. Complete your tour with a visit to the Sanctuary of Our Lady of Loreto, an important place of pilgrimage, and the Strahov Monastery, with its glorious Baroque decorations, where you will enjoy a private tour of the ornate library.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Prague
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    PRAGUE - KONOPISTE - KUTNA HORA - PRAGUE

    Enjoy a full day excursion outside Prague with your English-speaking local guide and private vehicle. Visit Konopiste Castle, originally built in the 13th century, but rebuilt and restored many times. Stop in Kutna Hora, a charming small town where rich deposits of silver were discovered, eventually making the king the richest ruler in Central Europe in the 14th century. Here you will visit the Italian Court and Mint, and the Church of St. Barbara whose three huge Gothic tent shaped spires dominate the town. You will also visit the Ossuary (Bone Church), a small church that is decorated with the bones of 40,000 people, before returning to your hotel in Prague.

    The reminder of the day is at your leisure.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Prague
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 12

    PRAGUE - TEREZIN - NELAHOZEVES - PRAGUE

    Today you will travel outside of Prague with your English-speaking local guide and private vehicle, first stopping at the town of Terezin where the former Nazi Theresienstadt Concentration Camp is located. Here you will see the camp, a prison for 150,000 Jews during World War II, as the Nazi's left it in 1944. See the Lesser Fortress with its tiny cells that served as the prison of the Prague Gestapo, and the Ghetto Museum whose exhibitions detail the town's tragic story.

    After your excursion travel back to Prague, via Nelahozeves. The Czech Republic's greatest composer, Anton Dvorak, was born in the village of Nelahozeves. Visit his birthplace and memorial museum located in a small house in the village (subject to opening times). You will also stop at the Renaissance Chateau, once a residence of powerful nobility, which now houses a permanent exhibition entitled Private Spaces. This completely new exhibition, opened in summer 2007, presents object which span five centuries, including hundreds of pieces displayed for the first time. After your visit you will be transferred back to your hotel in Prague

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Prague
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 13

    DEPART PRAGUE

    Today you will be privately transferred to Prague Airport for your flight home.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Prague
    Meal Plan: B
