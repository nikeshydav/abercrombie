CELEBRATE IN LISBON

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 days

    Ideal for a group of family & friends wishing to celebrate a special occasion in one of Europe's smallest and most charming capital cities (suitable for parties exceeding 40 persons).

    Highlights: Solar dos Presuntos , Museu de Arte Antiga, Museo de Oriente, Arrábida Natural Park, Palacio dos Marqueses da Fronteira, Sintra, Cascais

  • Day 1

    ARRIVAL LISBON

    Upon arrival, transfer privately to your hotel and enjoy the remainder of the day at leisure.

    Just a short walk from your hotel is the traditional restaurant Solar dos Presuntos where you will enjoy this evening's dinner. Specialties include ham, seafood and grills and is popular with the locals. The walls are hung with photographs and a fine collection of watercolors drawings and paintings. The atmosphere is relaxed and informal with charming service.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Ritz

  • Day 2

    LISBON

    Spend the morning discovering the best known sights of Portugal's capital. The enchanting hilltop district of the Alfama, little changed since Moorish times and crowned by the castle of St George and with superb views over the city and Tagus Estuary. Then through the 18th century Baijo which was rebuilt after the catastrophic earthquake of 1755 on to the riverside Jeronimos Monastery, an amazing example of the exuberant "Manueline" gothic architecture that is unique to Portugal and containing the tomb of the great explorer Vasco da Gama. Close by is the fascinating little Torre de Belem fortress, surrounded by water; and the Bandeirantes Memorial to the Portuguese seafarers who over the centuries created the furthest flung and longest lasting of the great European empires.

    This afternoon there is time to relax and soak up the atmosphere of this remarkable city, perhaps stroll and maybe shop in the fascinating Bairro Alto district; or join your expert guides on an optional sightseeing tour to discover more of the treasures Lisbon has to offer.

    OPTION 1: Museums of Lisbon
    Time to discover some of the most interesting museums which Lisbon has to offer. First and foremost the superb museum created by the eccentric Armenian oil millionaire Calouste Gulbenkian who gave to Lisbon his amazing collections of art ranging from early Babylonian pottery to Rembrandt to Lalique glass. Or the enchanting Museu de Arte Antiga, filled with paintings by medieval and Renaissance Portuguese artists. Also not be missed by art lovers is the recently created Museo de Oriente, which not only chronicles Portugal's nearly 600 years' presence in the Far East but is quite possibly the most brilliant display of Oriental art anywhere in Europe. And perhaps combine all this culture with a ride on a 100 year tram?

    OPTION 2: Arrábida Natural Park
    This afternoon travel to the nearby Arrábida Natural Park for time exploring independently. You may wish to take a short hike through the Serra da Arrabida, which stretches up along the coast between Setúbal and Sesimbra. It sits on the northern slope of the Sado River estuary in Portugal. The park was founded in 1976 and covers 41.7 square miles. The highest point is marked by the Serra da Arrábida, 1637 ft high. These chalky hills have the only surviving example of primitive Mediterranean vegetation. Tucked away in the heart of Arrábida's Natural Park, the formation goes back 180 million years. The soil and mild climatic features, considered the best in Portugal, have produced a lush vegetation rich in species. Two of the park's beaches are popular among the inhabitants of Lisbon and Setúbal. Those beaches are Portinho da Arrábida and Figueirinha. The first one, on a small bay, and with a little island off the shore, is especially famous, being a regular feature on Portuguese TV commercials.

    This evening enjoy a memorable private dinner at the Palacio dos Marqueses da Fronteira, once a country manor house and dating in its entirety from the 17th century. It is surrounded by the finest formal garden in Portugal, and the terrace is perfect for pre-dinner cocktails. Inside there are two principal rooms; the family dining room which can be used for cocktails in the unlikely event of wet weather and music, has a magnificent chandelier and fine paintings; the other, the grand "Sala de Batallas" with a superb plasterwork ceiling, fine chandeliers and azulejos depicting the Portuguese victories over the Spaniards in the 17th century makes for a memorable setting for dinner.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Ritz

  • Day 3

    LISBON

    Today you travel out of Lisbon to discover the delightful town of Sintra, long the residence of Kings and aristocrats not just from Portugal but in the 18th century the home of English "milords" like the poet Byron. The Palacio Nacional in the center of the town was for centuries the royal palace. It is a fascinating combination of medieval fortifications, Royal apartments and gardens decorated with tiles and fountains. Nearby is another extraordinary royal residence, the hilltop Palacio de Pena built in the late 19th century by the German consort of the Portuguese queen in a style reminiscent of the castles of "mad" King Ludwig of Bavaria.

    After lunch and time to shop in some of Sintra's boutiques, handicraft and antiques stores, the road takes you down to the coast to the delightful resort of Cascais. Once just a fishing port and then the preferred summer refuge of Lisbon society, Cascais retains a charm all of its own. Return to your hotel at the end of the afternoon.

    This evening drive out to the beautiful family owned estate half an hour northwest of Lisbon, Quinta Sant'Ana. The estate mainly produces wine but also livestock. Dinner here, which can either be in the old wine cellar, in another part of the cellar or in the main dining room, might include the local specialty of a whole roast pig. There is considerable space for a really fun party, entertainment of all kinds, including country dancing by the local villagers and perhaps ending with pulsating Brazilian music from one of the best bands in Lisbon.

    We can also offer a selection of alternative dinner venues such as 'A Trevessa' in the Old District of the city or the 'SILK - Club', offering an informal atmosphere in the Bairro Alto area and enjoying spectacular views.

    Overnight: Four Seasons Hotel Ritz

  • Day 4

    DEPARTURE LISBON

    Today clients will be transferred privately to Lisbon airport.

Gastronomic Spain

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 12 days

    A 12-day itinerary with particular focus on the gastronomic highlights of Spain.

    Highlights: Madrid, Toledo, Caceres, Cazalla de la Sierra, Sanlucar la Mayor, Seville, Cordoba, Lucena, Osuna, Sanlucar de Barrameda, Jerez de la Fontera

  • Day 1

    ARRIVAL MADRID

    On arrival in Madrid transfer to your hotel in the city centre and have time at leisure before meeting Simon Butler-Madden and fellow travellers for a unique introduction to the wines of Spain in a private tasting followed by dinner, which involves a tasting of Spanish "tapas".

    Overnight: Hotel Ritz

  • Day 2

    MADRID - TOLEDO - MADRID

    This morning you travel out of Madrid to the province of Toledo, gateway to La Mancha, land of Don Quixote and the finest of all Spanish cheeses. You visit a farm where the sheep are milked twice daily to produce this unique cheese before going to another property where saffron, the greatest of all Spanish spices is produced exactly as it was hundreds of years ago.

    Later, proceed to Toledo itself, city of El Greco, and where the most delicious marzipan made from fresh almonds and honey has been made since Moorish times. The evening is free to dine where you please.

    Overnight: Hotel Ritz
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    MADRID - CACERES

    Today head south west from Madrid, first to Malpica del Tajo to visit one of the most prestigious names in Spanish wine, Carlos Falcó Marques de Griñón, producing quite outstanding varietal wines in a region hitherto unknown for quality.

    After a light lunch in a local inn you continue into the land which was the birthplace of Conquistadores such as Cortés and Pizarro, visiting the delightful hill town of Trujillo, where the local speciality is a liqueur made from acorns! Continue to the UNESCO World Heritage city of Cáceres, famed for one of the most outstanding restaurants in all Spain, "Atrio" presided over by the star chef Toño Perez, with 2 Michelin stars and absolute top rating in Spain's most important gourmet guide. Here you dine tonight.

    Overnight: NH Palacio de Oquendo
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    CACERES - CAZALLA DE LA SIERRA

    The road runs south through countryside filled with the evergreen oaks that produce the acorns which are the secret to the flavor of the exquisite "pata negra" hams appreciated by every Spaniard. In the hilltop village of Montanchez visit one of the most prestigious establishments to learn how this unique delicacy is produced. Then on through the rice paddies of Almendralejo to Mérida, once one of the most important Roman cities in Spain, where you have time to visit the superb Roman theatre.

    During the afternoon you enter the hill country of the Sierra Morena where your goal is the enchanting, centuries-old manor house of "Trasierra" which will be your home for the next two nights. You dine this evening in the elegant dining room of this unique property.

    Overnight: Trasierra
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    CAZALLA DE LA SIERRA

    A day to relax and enjoy the experience of staying in some of the most attractive surroundings in all Spain. There will be time to walk in the woods, stroll in the village of Cazalla de la Sierra, visit the delightful parish church and the former Carthusian monastery before sampling a simple lunch of real local cuisine in the shape of the tapas for which the Bar Los Mellis is renowned and accompanied of course by the local wine. Returning to Trasierra in the afternoon it is time to rest before taking part in a cooking class presided over by television chef Gioconda Scott, daughter of your hostess Charlotte Scott. Charlotte has lived in Spain for many years and created this extraordinary oasis combining the charm of a whitewashed Andalusian farmhouse with exquisite interior decoration, which has featured in the world's top design magazines.

    Overnight: Trasierra
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    CAZALLA DE LA SIERRA - SANLUCAR LA MAYOR

    After a leisurely start, today the road leads south towards Seville, capital of Andalucía. Your next port of call is the Cortijo El Esparragal, one of the most exquisitely beautiful country houses in Andalucía and home of one of the most prestigious studs breeding the superb Spanish horses. Here you will have an opportunity to sample delicious wines from nearby Jerez while watching a unique equestrian display of riders and horses from the stud on the lawns in front of the house. This will be followed by a sampling of perhaps the most famous of all Spanish dishes, paella (but not too much, since appetites should be prepared for this evening's feast!).

    A short drive now brings you to the enchanting Hacienda Benazuza El Bulli Hotel, one of the finest in Andalucía, run, as its name betokens, under the auspices of Spain's most renowned gastronomic gurus, Ferrán Adriá. Its starred restaurant, where you dine tonight, is "la Alquería', presided over by star chef Rafael Morales, serves an ever changing selection of dishes created by Adriá at his renowned restaurant "El Bulli" in Catalonia.

    Overnight: Hacienda Benazuza el Bulli
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    SANLUCAR LA MAYOR - SEVILLE - SANLUCAR LA MAYOR

    After enjoying a breakfast at the Benazuza which aspires to the same perfection as the evening meal, you make the short drive to Seville to spend the day exploring the never ending wonders of this unique city, including the mighty cathedral, the second largest in Christendom, the Real Alcázares Royal Palace and the enchanting barrio Santa Cruz district. At lunchtime you are free to stroll and perhaps enjoy an itinerant lunch sampling the classic tapas for which Seviille is famed.

    Afterwards you ride in a horse and carriage first through the María Luisa Park and then on to the Casa de Pilatos, the grandest private mansion in all Seville dating back to the early 16th century. Then it is time to return to enjoy the surroundings of the Hacienda Benazuza before another unique gastronomic experience this evening.

    A few minutes from the hotel is the Restaurante Alhucemas. Small and unpretentious in appearance this is nevertheless renowned as one of the finest places in all Andalucía to enjoy supremely fresh fish and sea food. The ultimate accolade is that, as the photographs on the wall testify, the great Ferrán Adriá himself is a fan of the superb but simple cuisine of the owner Miguel.

    Overnight: Hacienda Benazuza el Bulli
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 8

    SANLUCAR LA MAYOR - CORDOBA - LUCENA

    Today the road leads eastwards to Córdoba, 1000 years ago capital of the Caliphate and where today the tradition of Moorish cuisine lives on which you will be able to experience in a lunch of specialties that have their origins in the Moorish and Sephardic cuisines that go back beyond the Christian Reconquest in 1285.

    You will also visit the Mezquita, today a Cathedral, and once the largest mosque in the world. Later continue into the superb landscape of the Natural Park of the Sierra Subbética to your hotel. Tonight you dine in the simple but excellent hotel restaurant.

    Overnight: MS Convento Santo Domingo
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 9

    THE LAND OF OLIVE OIL

    You are now in the heart of the most intensive production of olive oil in the world and today is spent learning about and tasting the liquid gold that Spain exports to every corner of the globe. You will hear a lecture on the secrets of making the finest extra virgin varieties and taste the different varieties of oil and olives before touring the olive orchards to learn how the trees are grown and see one which is over 1000 years old.

    After lunch in an ancient inn in the enchanting hilltop village of Priego de Córdoba you continue to the wine town of Montilla home of the uniquely exciting sherry style of the Montilla-Moriles denomination. Here you will visit one of the most renowned wineries to learn about their wine and taste it. This evening you are free to explore Lucena and dine at leisure in a local restaurant.

    Overnight: MS Convento Santo Domingo
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 10

    LUCENA - OSUNA - SANLUCAR DE BARRAMEDA

    And now the road leads again southwards, across the heartland of Andalucía, over hills and rivers and past white villages that do not seem to have changed since Moorish times. Through the fascinating World Heritage town of Osuna where you stop for a light lunch of tapas and on to the province of Cadiz, to the Hacienda San Rafael, one of the most beautiful country hotels in all Andalucía.

    This evening you will enjoy an exceptional dinner prepared by the brilliant chef of San Rafael and followed by a pulsating and authentic performance of flamenco dancing and singing performed by local villagers.

    Overnight: Hacienda de San Rafael
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 11

    SANLUCAR DE BARRAMEDA - JEREZ DE LA FRONTERA - SANLUCAR DE BARRAMEDA

    You are only a few miles from Jerez de la Frontera capital of the sherry region and one of Andalucía's most atmospheric cities. You begin a visit here by going to the market where unbelievable quantities of fresh fish from the nearby sea are sold in a hubbub of excitement and perhaps sampling a few of the crispest and most delectable "churros" in all Spain sold at one of the stalls outside the market. And then for a private visit to one of the most prestigious smaller makers of Jerez, the centuries old prize winning bodega of Lustau where you will learn the secrets of this most famous of Spanish wines and taste them from bone dry finos to the most luscious Pedro Ximenez.

    Later, drive through the rolling vineyard country where every inch of the soil is covered with vines, to the town of Sanlúcar de Barrameda on the banks of the Guadalquivir estuary, home of the "manzanilla" variety of sherry and also famed for the most delectable shrimp in all Spain. Return to San Rafael, to relax, enjoy farewell drinks and prepare to return home.

    Overnight: Hacienda de San Rafael
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 12

    SANLUCAR DE BARRAMEDA - SEVILLE

    Transfer this morning for your return flight home.

    Meal Plan: B
