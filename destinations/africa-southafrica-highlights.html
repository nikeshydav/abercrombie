South Africa Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Johannesburg

    Johannesburg is often called the gateway to Southern Africa. Growing as a commercial hub, it has one of the world's best infrastructure facilities making it a young major city. Soweto in the city is a great crowd-puller while its Apartheid Museum brings to light the era of South Africa's previous regime.

    For those who love history, you must see buildings from the gold rush era from Carlton Centre, Africa's tallest building visit and the Fort, an infamous prison which now houses the Constitutional Court opened in 2004. At the City Centre go through the Market Theatre precinct, Museum of Africa and Newtown, Sterkfontein Cavesm, Hector Peterson Memorial, Cradle of Humankind and the Nelson Mandela Bridge. A walk around the Newtown will get you impersonal with the rich and diverse culture of the city. Try the gold mines for a little adventure or go for the Lesedi Cultural Village if you want to emulate traditional routine-dance and food from Zulu, Sotho, Pedi, Xhosa and Ndebele villages.

    Cape Town

    Known as the chic city of South Africa; Cape Town is famous world-wide for its Table Mountain, vast lands of Cape Winelands and beautifully-decorated coastline. The Winelands of Cape have a distinct flavour of their ancestral cultural heritage, natural beauty and wines to die-for.

    Hospitable and warm places like Stellenbosch, Pearl, Wellington, Franschhoek and Robertson in the Breede River Valley are a must on the itinerary. Also visit the World Heritage Site of Robben Island and the Cape of Good Hope on a Peninsula Tour to learn more about its history.

    Kruger National Park

    With an acronym like 'Big Game Country' to its credit, the Kruger National Park offers a luxurious escapade into wildlife with private resorts both within and on the border. There is a luxurious sense to the park with affordable and luxury accommodations available for all travellers in the midst of rich and dense wildlife adventures.

    You can imagine yourself dining in open air, having traditional African cuisine in the middle of amazing wildlife game views. There is the Sabi Sand Private Game Reserve, Timbavati Private Game Reserve, Thornybush Private Game Reserve and Kapama Private Game Reserve for you to witness and enjoy a true South Africa Safari.

    Garden Route

    Stretching from Heidelberg in the West to the mouth of the Storms River in the East, over an area of two hundred miles lies one of the most beautiful and scenic areas of Garden Route. Known as South Africa's Garden of Eden, it's one of those areas which have been bestowed with spellbinding landscapes. While enjoying a journey in the vast lands, you can drop off at the popular spots of George, Wilderness, Sedgefield, Knysna, Plettenberg Bay, Nature's Valley, Storms River and the Tsitsikamma National Park.

    Delightfully balanced between mountains and beaches, the Garden Route has flowers and bays that will hold you to them. Between Heidelberg and Storms River, the area runs parallel to a land that is not less than a paradise filled with lakes, mountains, forests, rivers and golden-sand beaches. Hikers can tread the forests for some adventure while those who want to feel the breeze can simply enjoy a long drive amidst clear rivers and lakes which allow boating and fishing. You can be rest assured of a leisure-filled holiday amidst the mild climate of the Garden Route.

    KwaZulu Natal

    Think of the warm waters of the Indian Ocean and you would be able to imagine KwaZulu-Natal with its sub-tropical climate that gives it the amazing landscape and rich locale. The mega Drakensberg mountain range in the west and savannahs in the east give Natal a distinct flavour.

    A mixture of African, European and Indian culture; KwaZulu-Natal is the kingdom of the Zulu. It houses two World Heritage Sites- the Greater St Lucia Wetland Park and the Ukhahlamba Drakensberg Park. Travellers can get to see a wide array of natural habitat of the St Lucia Wetland while enjoying the wildlife at the Hluhluwe-Imfolozi, Itala, Mkuze and the Tembe Elephant Park.
