China's Silk Road

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 days

    Explore China's Silk Road, historically a very important trading route that spanned across the Asian continent, with this exclusive itinerary.

    Highlights: Urumqi, Kashgar, Turfan, Dunhuang.

  • Day 1

    URUMQI

    This morning, you will be transferred to the airport for your flight to Urumqi, where you will be welcomed by your guide and transferred to the hotel.

    Overnight: Sheraton Hotel
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    URUMQI - KASHGAR

    This morning, you will be driven to Tianshan Heavenly Lake. Standing at an elevation of 1900 metres, this world famous mountain lake was formed after its river courses were blocked up by the mud-rock flow of glaciers in ancient times. After lunch at a local restaurant, enjoy a boat cruise on the lake.

    This evening, you will be transferred to the airport for your flight to Kashgar, where you will be met by your guide and transferred to the hotel.

    Overnight: Baroni Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    KASHGAR

    This morning, you will visit the site of the great Sunday Market that remains open throughout the week and is a popular market for the local Uighur population.

    Next, you will visit the Id Kah Mosque, the largest mosque in China. Dating back to the 15th century, it is the centre of religious life in Kashgar and its open courtyard and prayer hall can accommodate over 3000 during festivals. Continue on to visit the Tomb of Abjak Hoja, a Holy site to the Muslims of Xinjiang.

    This afternoon, take a walk through a typical Uighur neighbourhood and visit a local family. The Uighur, who are a Turkic-speaking people, originated in Mongolia as part of the vast sea of steppe tribes that covered the Altai and grasslands of Mongolia and Siberia.

    Conclude your sightseeing today with a look at the Old City district. Kashgar has a history of more than 2000 years and originally belonged to the Zhuangpu prefectural General's Office of the HunIn in Han Dynasty. Surrounded by mountains, this famous city has many Islamic and Buddhist constructions and tombs which hint at its glorious history.

    Overnight: Baroni Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    KASHGAR - URUMQI - TURFAN

    This morning, you will be transferred to the airport for your flight to Urumqi.

    After lunch at a local restaurant, visit the Xinjiang Archeological Institute to view the remarkable collection of ancient mummies, some more than 5000 years old.

    Next, visit the Er Da Qiao Bazaar, a traditional covered market in Xinjiang where food, clothes, souvenirs and animals are sold. This is an opportunity to witness basic everyday Uighur life.

    You will then be transferred overland to Turfan and your hotel.

    After dinner, enjoy a Uygur Dance Performance.

    Overnight: Tuha Petroleum Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    TURFAN

    This morning, you will be transferred by donkey cart to visit the impressive ruins of the ancient city of Gaochang that lies 47 kilometres southeast of Turfan. It became the capital of the kingdom of Gaochang under the Han house of Qu. The practice of Buddhism led to the establishment of many monasteries, temples and large religious communities and the monk Xuan Zang taught in this city for several months.

    Continue on to visit the Bezelik Buddist Caves, Astana Tombs, and the Flaming Mountains.

    In the afternoon, visit more interesting sites such as the Kerez Irrigation System, a series of wells and underground tunnels that use gravity to bring ground water to the surface, and Jiaohe, a city that evokes the past glories of the Silk Road.

    Conclude your sightseeing today at Suliman's Minaret. The minaret is constructed mainly of bricks in the style of the pre-Savafid architecture of Iran and is a lovely example of decorated brickwork.

    Overnight: Tuha Petroleum Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    TURFAN - URUMQI

    This morning, you will be transferred overland to Urumqi, with sightseeing en route.

    Visit Red Hill Park for a panoramic view of the city. Locally known as " Hong Shan", the hill has small viewing pavilions including the Zhenglong Pagoda which is the symbol of the city.

    Overnight: Sheraton Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    URUMQI - DUNHUANG

    This morning, you will be transferred to the airport for your flight to Dunhuang.

    Upon arrival, commence sightseeing by visiting the Buddhist Yulin Caves and West Jin Dynasty Underground Tomb.

    Conclude your sightseeing today with a tour of the ancient city of Dunhuang. Known as Shazhou (the realm of sand), Dunhuang became an important garrison on the caravan routes linking China to central Asia and the Middle East. Its reputation grew even more after monks and faithful pilgrims began work here on the Mogao Caves in 366. In the midst of forbidding desert, the greatest gallery of Buddhist sculpture and art were created.

    Overnight: Dunhuang Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    DUNHUANG

    This morning, you will visit the Mogao Grottoes. These are one of the most famous Buddhist grottoes in the world with many beautiful statues and wall art preserved for future generations.

    After dinner, visit the night bazaar, which specialises in antiques, tea and local products.

    Overnight: Dunhuang Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 9

    DUNHUANG

    This morning you will be driven to the Yumen Guam Pass, situated 75 kilometres northwest of Dunhuang. The Jade Gate Pass was a strategic pass on the ancient Silk Road, a gateway from central China to the western regions. Continue to an early remnant of the Great Wall from the Han Period.

    In the afternoon, visit the White Horse Pagoda that is located on the outskirts on Dunhuang city, and finally the Dunhuang Museum.

    Overnight: Dunhuang Hotel
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 10

    DEPART DUNHUANG

    This morning you will be transferred to the airport for your flight home.

    Meal Plan: B

DISCOVER BEIJING AND XIAN

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 days

    A special interest itinerary exploring China's fascinating capital, the excavated terracotta army at Xian and a portion of the Great Wall of China.

    Highlights: Beijing, the Terracotta Warriors and Horses excavation at Xian, and the Great Wall of China.

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE BEIJING

    This evening, you will arrive at Beijing Airport where you will be met and transferred to your hotel.

    Overnight: The Peninsula

  • Day 2

    BEIJING

    Begin your morning with a lesson in Tai Chi, which is held in one of Beijing's largest public parks. Tai Chi is one of China's most popular forms of exercise and is practiced by both young and old.

    In the afternoon, you will meet one of Beijing's leading art critics/curators. You will spend the afternoon together at 798 and Coachangdi to learn about the burgeoning world of contemporary Chinese art.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    BEIJING

    This morning, see the Chinese flag being raised in Tiananmen Square. Following the flag raising, take in a tour of Tiananmen Square with an expert guide.

    You will spend the rest of the morning on the campus of Peking University, regarded as China's finest university.

    In the evening, enjoy a sumptuous dinner in one of Beijing's notable restaurants.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    XIAN

    You will fly to Xian this morning. Upon arrival you will be transferred directly by vehicle to the single greatest archaeological find of the 20th century: The Terracotta Warriors and Horses Excavation.

    After a dumpling lunch at a local restaurant, you will continue your exploration of Xian. See the Great Mosque of Xian, one of the most beautiful and unusual structures in Xian. The area around the mosque is generally considered to be the "Muslim quarter".

    Transfer back to Xian Airport and return to Beijing.

    Overnight: The Peninsula Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 5

    BEIJING

    Today begins with an early morning bicycle ride through an older Beijing neighbourhood.

    After your scenic bike ride, you will visit some of the Beijing's famous buildings: the Urban Planning Centre, the National Theatre, the CCTV Building, and the Olympic Stadium.

    This afternoon, visit a master class to learn a bit about the timely traditions of tea and its importance to Chinese culture. Then you will meet a Beijing historian for a walking tour of one of Beijing's most celebrated neighborhoods. The tour will take you through the hutongs, the network of residential lanes that once criss-crossed much of pre-1911 China.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    BEIJING

    This morning, you will join a local fitness instructor for an early morning power walk that will take you through one of the city's more historic districts. Then spend the rest of the morning learning about the world of Chinese cuisine as you take part in a class run by the Beijing Culinary Institute.

    This afternoon, you will be getting on the mat for a session in Chinese Wushu, a type of martial arts.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    BEIJING

    Start the day off at the Panjiayuan weekend market, the biggest and best market experience in Beijing. Then visit the extraordinary Forbidden City. Centre of the Chinese world for nearly 500 years, the imperial palace remains the most complete and best preserved collection of ancient buildings in China.

    After a wonderful lunch at the famous Fang Shan, you can enjoy one of the following walking tours to learn more about China's tradition of arts:

    &bull   Artists on the edge &bull   Mind for meditation &bull   Along the alleyways of history

    Following dinner in a local restaurant enjoy a performance by one of the city's finest acrobatics troupes.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    BEIJING

    Start the day early with a visit to the Mutianyu site of the Great Wall. Enjoy time to walk around and explore the ramparts and battlement towers that once stood at the vanguard of Chinese civilisation.

    Enjoy dinner at a beautifully restored courtyard house situated in the former imperial city.

    Overnight: The Peninsula
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 9

    DEPART BEIJING

    Today you will be transferred to the airport for your departure flight.
