Zimbabwe Travel Tips

Time Zone

  • GMT + 2 hours

Language

  • English is the official language of Zimbabwe. The two main ethnic languages spoken are Shona (mostly in the Eastern, Northern and Southern parts of the country) and Ndebele (mostly in the Matabeleland region to the west of the country).

In 2009 Zimbabwe adopted a multi-currency system. The major trading currencies are United States Dollars and South African Rand. ATMs dispense cash in US Dollars while other currencies are available inside the banking halls. While the following South African Rand coins are accepted R5, R2, R1, 50c (smaller denominations are not accepted in some areas), US Dollar coins are not accepted.

• Currently only visa cards are widely accepted in Zimbabwe. A limited number of establishments may accept Mastercards but it is advisable to check beforehand.

• Credit cards are not accepted in the markets.

• Due to the delays in the clearing of funds, most establishments no longer accept traveller's cheques. Local banks are currently not cashing traveller's cheques as they are treated as normal cheques with a 21 day clearing period.

• Tips to airport and hotel porters are included on private airport transfers.

• Tips for drivers and guides are not included. We feel this is a very personal matter, but the following suggestions may be used as a guideline.

For full details on climate, please see Best Time to Go.

• Clothing should be mostly lightweight for the summer months. It is advisable to take a jacket or sweater for the cooler evenings.

• We recommend that you bring a camera, camcorder, batteries and film, insect repellant, sun block, binoculars, torch, sunglasses and a hat.

• Anti-malarial precautions are an absolute necessity when visiting the game parks in the region. Professional medical advice should be sought on this.

• Zimbabwe has limited medical facilities of international standards and most major cases of illness are evacuated to South Africa which is the preferred country for treatment in the region. We do advise you to take out travel insurance to cover any possible medical treatment, and if necessary emergency evacuation. All hospitals charge tourists as private patients.

• The sun in the southern hemisphere is extremely strong - do wear an effective sunscreen and hat during summer. It is advisable to avoid the midday sun. Recommended SPF is 40.

• Throughout Southern Africa it is safe to eat all foods and drink tap water in cities and towns. Purified water in flasks or bottled water is provided at game lodges throughout the region.

• For up to date information on latest health and vaccination recommendations, please contact your doctor.

Zimbabwe's electrical system operates on 220 V (a.c)

All visitors to Zimbabwe require a passport that must have at least two blank pages to accommodate the visa which takes up a whole page. On arrival, tourists will receive a single or double entry visa upon payment of the statutory visa fee. Multiple entry visas are not available at port of entry and must be applied for in advance. For visitors leaving the country and coming back into Zimbabwe, a re-entry visa is now required.

• Zimbabwe is renowned for it's stone and wood sculptures which can be purchased in the market areas. Other craftwork such as bowls, carvings, batiks, crochet work, beadwork, handmade local jewellery, chest sets are also available in the shops and market areas.

• Ivory products are available and it is important to get a CITES certificate for any ivory purchased. It is also important to check the regulations for the country you will be travelling to as some countries have banned the importation of ivory products even if you have the CITES certificate.
