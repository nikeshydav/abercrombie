Zimbabwe safaris & luxury holidays


Close your eyes and imagine: A vast African river happily and peacefully gurgling through the infinite expanse of pristine tropical rainforest only to be suddenly met by an edge that sends it plummeting down one hundred and twenty eight meters into an endless gorge! This heart stopping fall is the famed Vitoria Falls and the drama unfolds right in front of your eyes, here at Zimbabwe!

A world heritage site and a major attraction, the falls, however, are not the only reasons that beckon you to the wonderful land. A striking and diverse landscape, cosmopolitan cities brimming with arts and culture, exotic wildlife teeming in its adventurous game parks and some very warm and friendly folk - that's Zimbabwe for you!

A high plateau with the central region liberally dusted with hills, Zimbabwe is nestled in the southern part of Africa, flanked by Mozambique in the east and the north-east, Zambia and Botswana in the west and South Africa in the south. The eastern parts are dotted with high mountain ranges called the Eastern Highlands, which grows most of the country's coffee!

Sightseeing in Zimbabwe is a pleasure to say the least. Learn of the history and culture of this wonderful country from the Great Zimbabwe Ruins and indulge in the scenic beauty of the Eastern Highlands. With a total of eleven national parks in the area, game viewing is a common activity. Revel your senses in the free flowing Victoria Falls, make friends with elephants at the Hwange National Park, home to one of the highest concentration of game especially elephants, enjoy the warm sun and soak in the pleasure of man-made wonderland of Lake Kariba and stroke the adventurous streak in you at the Mana Pools, showcasing the last remains of true wilderness with great views of the Zambezi Escarpment.

Victoria Falls Hotel is one such place that takes prides at being a stalwart in the line of luxury and comfort. Also, multiple ranges of safari lodges impart the feel of nature's delight with some being- Singita Pamushana in the lowveld of Zimbabwe, Bumi Hills Safari Lodge at the foot of Lake Kariba and The Hide amongst the wilds of Hwange National Park. Best Time To Go Zimbabwe

Best time to go to Zimbabwe

High season: July to October
Shoulder season: May to June

Zimbabwe is exceptional for game viewing and scenery all year round. It offers excellent special interest safaris at certain times of the year. Birding season is from November to March when the waterways of the Zambezi and Lake Kariba are home to the many migratory birds during their breeding season. Botanical season is from December to May when the vegetation is lush and green, and also when most plants are in flower.

Photography is excellent year-round, but depending on what your main interests are the scenery is most spectacular from September to February during the first rains. For wildlife the winter months from May to September are best, as the vegetation is less dense and game viewing is generally more prolific.

The Victoria Falls are spectacular in April and May after the rainy season, although the thick spray may obscure your view (a flight above by helicopter or micro-light is an amazing way of viewing the falls). At the end of the dry season, October to December, the water levels are low and one can see the magnificent rock formation of the falls. River rafting is most exciting during low water from August to December and is limited at high water.

In 2009 Zimbabwe adopted a multi-currency system. The major trading currencies are United States Dollars and South African Rand. ATMs dispense cash in US Dollars while other currencies are available inside the banking halls. While the following South African Rand coins are accepted R5, R2, R1, 50c (smaller denominations are not accepted in some areas), US Dollar coins are not accepted.

• Currently only visa cards are widely accepted in Zimbabwe. A limited number of establishments may accept Mastercards but it is advisable to check beforehand.

• Credit cards are not accepted in the markets.

• Due to the delays in the clearing of funds, most establishments no longer accept traveller's cheques. Local banks are currently not cashing traveller's cheques as they are treated as normal cheques with a 21 day clearing period.

• Tips to airport and hotel porters are included on private airport transfers.

• Tips for drivers and guides are not included. We feel this is a very personal matter, but the following suggestions may be used as a guideline.

For full details on climate, please see Best Time to Go.

• Clothing should be mostly lightweight for the summer months. It is advisable to take a jacket or sweater for the cooler evenings.

• We recommend that you bring a camera, camcorder, batteries and film, insect repellant, sun block, binoculars, torch, sunglasses and a hat.

• Anti-malarial precautions are an absolute necessity when visiting the game parks in the region. Professional medical advice should be sought on this.

• Zimbabwe has limited medical facilities of international standards and most major cases of illness are evacuated to South Africa which is the preferred country for treatment in the region. We do advise you to take out travel insurance to cover any possible medical treatment, and if necessary emergency evacuation. All hospitals charge tourists as private patients.

• The sun in the southern hemisphere is extremely strong - do wear an effective sunscreen and hat during summer. It is advisable to avoid the midday sun. Recommended SPF is 40.

• Throughout Southern Africa it is safe to eat all foods and drink tap water in cities and towns. Purified water in flasks or bottled water is provided at game lodges throughout the region.

• For up to date information on latest health and vaccination recommendations, please contact your doctor.

Zimbabwe's electrical system operates on 220 V (a.c)

All visitors to Zimbabwe require a passport that must have at least two blank pages to accommodate the visa which takes up a whole page. On arrival, tourists will receive a single or double entry visa upon payment of the statutory visa fee. Multiple entry visas are not available at port of entry and must be applied for in advance. For visitors leaving the country and coming back into Zimbabwe, a re-entry visa is now required.

• Zimbabwe is renowned for it's stone and wood sculptures which can be purchased in the market areas. Other craftwork such as bowls, carvings, batiks, crochet work, beadwork, handmade local jewellery, chest sets are also available in the shops and market areas.

• Ivory products are available and it is important to get a CITES certificate for any ivory purchased. It is also important to check the regulations for the country you will be travelling to as some countries have banned the importation of ivory products even if you have the CITES certificate.
