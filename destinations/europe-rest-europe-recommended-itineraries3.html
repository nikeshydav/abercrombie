PRAGUE FAIRYTALE CITY

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 4 Days

    A fantastic 4 day visit to the romantic, fairy-tale city of Prague. Meander through the cobbled streets flanked with beautiful architecture and take in the magic of Prague's history and culture.

    Highlights: Explore Prague's charming Old Town, stroll along the Charles Bridge and discover the wonderful Baroque architecture of the Little Quarter

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE IN PRAGUE

    Upon arrival at Prague airport you will be met in the arrival hall by your driver and privately transferred to your hotel. The hotel Augustine exudes history and elegance and has been created from 7 different buildings including the 13th century Augustinian monastery after which the hotel has been named.

    Within short walking distance of the character and enchanting history of Prague's old town, you may enjoy a romantic evening stroll, or a quiet drink in the hotel's intimate St Thomas brewery bar, housed in the cellar.

    For your honeymoon enjoy a complimentary rose petal bath and breakfast in bed on your first morning. You will also receive 50% off couples massage treatments to ensure you can fully relax and feel pampered during your stay.*

    *honeymoon promotion valid throughout 2013

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel

  • Day 2

    PRAGUE

    Meet your English-speaking local guide and enjoy a half-day walking tour (4 hours) of the Old Town with its winding cobbled streets and fairy-tale charm and the fascinating Jewish Quarter, birthplace of Franz Kafka. See the striking Gothic Town Hall in Old Town Square, famous for its unique astronomical clock, and visit the Jewish Museum which houses the largest collection of Judaica in Europe, the Old-New Synagogue and the very moving Jewish Cemetery. You may also enjoy a walk across the romantically picturesque 14th century Charles Bridge. This afternoon is at your leisure for you to relax and enjoy the facilities of your hotel. Or, for those keen to explore this delightful city further, why not venture to Prague's Castle District. Hradcany Castle was the seat of Bohemian kings and Holy Roman emperors. See the Cathedral of St. Vitus, one of the finest and most richly endowed in central Europe, and the Basilica of St. George which was founded in 905. Stroll along the Golden Lane, a row of tiny and colourful houses dating from the 16th century.

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    PRAGUE

    Meet your local guide and enjoy a walking tour (3 hours) of the picturesque Lesser Town (Mala Strana). Also known as The Pearl of Baroque Architecture, the Lesser Town is one of Prague's oldest districts and full of captivating history and hidden gems which can't fail to enchant you. The most extensive and unforgettable building of this era is Albrecht von Wallenstein's Palace with five courtyards and a magnificent garden set as a French Park (please note that the palace is only open on Saturdays & Sundays and garden is opened daily from April till October).

    You may also like to see the Lesser Town square with the beautiful baroque St Nicholas Church and perhaps visit the Carmelitan church, The Church of Our Lady Victorious, with the famous statue of the Holy Infant Jesus of Prague. Or take a visit to the lovely Vrtba's Gardens, near the Church of Our Lady Victorious (open daily from April till October).

    If music is the food of love, what better way to spend the afternoon that with a private musical tour of Prague. The location of the Czech Museum of Music is within the former Baroque church of St. Mary Magdalene in the Little Quarter, the National Museum has taken the unique opportunity to update this space - once decorated with Baroque masterpieces and resounding with supposedly one of the biggest organs of 17th-century Prague. Also enjoy a visit to the Museum of Antonin Dvorak, located in a baroque Vila 'Amerika', since 1932 in an important early 18th century building of architect Kili?n Ign?c Dienzenhofer. End your Musical tour of Prague by a private walk-through the Estate Theatre, lasting about 60 minutes with explanation about the theatre renovation, history and work of W.A. Mozart in this theatre.

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    DEPART PRAGUE

    Today you will be transferred to Prague airport by private vehicle to board your flight.

    Overnight: Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

OKTOBERFEST & CULTURAL HIGHLIGHTS OF MUNICH AND BERLIN

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 days

    A 9-day itinerary based around the annual Oktoberfest Festival in Germany, as well as exploring the cultural and historical highlights of Munich and Berlin

    Highlights: Munich, Hofbrauhaus, Oktoberfest, Dachau, Neuschwanstein Castle, Berlin, Potsdam

  • Day 1

    ARRIVAL MUNICH

    Upon arrival at Munich airport you will be welcomed to Germany by your local Guardian Angel as you exit customs into the arrivals hall, and transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in procedures.

    This afternoon your local English-speaking guide will accompany you on a 4-hour walking tour through the compact Old Town centre with its multitude of fantastic shops, markets and always busy beer halls such as the "Hofbrauhaus" that Munich is so famous for. See the Karlsplatz fountain, the Frauenkirche - Munich's Cathedral - as well as the Marienplatz with the New Rathaus and its chiming clock, playing daily at 11.00am and noon (and also 5:00pm between March - October). Your tour will end at the Beer and Oktoberfest Museum, which occupies one of Munich's oldest houses, built in 1327, and still features some of the original wooden beams and the traditional Heaven's chair. Wander through exhibits of historic beer mugs, Oktoberfest memorabilia as well as Munich's brewing history. At the end of the museum visit enjoy beer tasting.

    You will be confirmed for 5 nights at the Charles Hotel. The Charles Hotel is about 15 min walk from the Old Town.

    Overnight: The Charles Hotel

  • Day 2

    OKTOBERFEST MUNICH

    Today you will be met by your English-speaking guide and privately transferred to the Oktoberfest events area to enjoy today's festivities (drinks/food payable locally). Your guide will stay with you throughout the day to ensure you not missing anything from today's festivities. You will be privately transferred back to your hotel at the end of the day.

    Overnight: The Charles Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    MUNICH

    Today enjoy a full day (8 hours) of Munich outskirts by private vehicle with driver and your English-speaking guide. This morning visit the Olympia Stadium, home of the 1972 Olympic Games and a travel to Nymphenburg Palace with its extravagant Rococo Amalienborg Pavillion. This afternoon see the spectacular BMW World. For over 90 years the headquarters of this famous car manufacturer has been situated in Munich and gives you the opportunity to experience the brand BMW in all its facets. The BMW museum consists of temporary exhibitions which are displayed in a rotunda that is called "Bowl" and the permanent exhibition. In the BMW factory approximately 900 vehicles are manufactured by 9,000 employees from more than 50 different countries per day. During the tour you will have exciting insights into the press shop, body shop, paint shop, engine production and assembly lines work. Start and finish the tour in the welcome centre where a comprehensive display of BMW automobiles and motorcycles is offered.

    Overnight: The Charles Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    DACHAU & THIRD REICH TOUR

    Today enjoy another full day (8 hours) sightseeing with your English-speaking guide and private vehicle with driver. Your Third Reich tour will include the place where Hitler started his 'career', where Goerg Elser tried to kill Hitler, an original piece of the Berlin Wall, the place of the White Rose, Konigsplatz, the Place of the Victims of Nacionalism and many more. This afternoon travel to the Dachau Memorial Site, built as a concentration camp by the Nazi's in 1933, 32000 prisoners died here and since 1965 the entire site has been designated as a memorial to those people. Dachau town itself is also worth a visit with many historic buildings and a palace. After your tour you will be privately transferred back to your hotel in Munich.

    Overnight: The Charles Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    NEUSCHWANSTEIN & LINDERHOF

    After breakfast you will set off with your English-speaking guide and private vehicle with driver into the Bavarian countryside (9 hours), beginning your tour at Neuschwanstein Castle. Due to its magical beauty it attracts many visitors from near and far, and it is best visited early, before it gets too busy. Looking more like a stage set than a real palace, it is no wonder Walt Disney used it as the model for his sleeping beauty castle and later Disneyland castle itself. Its construction almost brought Bavaria to bankruptcy and Ludwig's early death in the lake is rumoured to be closely connected with this. Do try to find time to walk to the Marienbrucke (Mary's Bridge) crossing a deep, narrow gorge. From this vantage point you have the best and probably most photographed views of this stunning fairy tale castle. (Please note this visit is NOT suitable for anyone with walking problems). You will also see Ludwig II's country retreat at Linderhof. The Rococo styling of the Schloss Linderhof is said to have been styled on Versailles, Paris. After sightseeing, return to your hotel in Munich.

    Overnight: The Charles Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    MUNICH - BERLIN

    Today you will be privately transferred by your driver to the airport. Upon arrival at Berlin airport you will be welcomed by your local Guardian Angel in the arrivals hall, and transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in procedures.

    You will be confirmed for 3 nights at the Rocco Forte Hotel de Rome, inclusive of Buffet Breakfast.

    Overnight: Rocco Forte Hotel de Rome
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    BERLIN

    Today you will enjoy a full-day sightseeing tour (8 hours) with your local English-speaking guide and private vehicle. Begin your tour with a visit to the Reichstag, new home of the German Parliament with its stunning Norman Foster designed dome. Then carry on to the newly created Potsdamer Platz, dubbed "Europe's largest construction site" during the mid 1990s. Visit the nearby Checkpoint Charlie, the famous crossing point between East and West Berlin, you will also visit the Museum dedicated to the checkpoint. After lunch at leisure, travel to the Museum Island, where your English-speaking guide will accompany you around one of the world's greatest museums, the Pergamon Museum (4 hours). The Pergamon Museum houses a vast collection of ancient artifacts and antiquities. You will also visit Berlin Cathedral with its vast dome. After sightseeing you will be transferred back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Rocco Forte Hotel de Rome
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    POTSDAM

    Depart Berlin today with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide to the city of Potsdam (8 hours). Your first stop will be Cecilienhof Palace. Built in the early part of the 20th century, the palace served as an important place in world history when the Potsdam Agreement was signed here in 1945 by Truman, Stalin and Churchill. It is said that each party had a private entrance to the castle so that no one party would know the arrival time of the others. After lunch at leisure, you will continue to the Sanssouci Gardens, the royal residence of Frederick the Great. Strolling through the gardens you come upon the Chinese teahouse, housing a small but elaborate porcelain collection; a Dutch mill; a Greek temple and even Roman ruins, erected on a hill opposite the dream palace of Sanssouci. The rococo-style Sanssouci is the best-known palace in the complex; it rests on a hilltop of vineyards and the inside is a display of original furnishings.

    Overnight: Rocco Forte Hotel de Rome
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    DEPARTURE BERLIN

    Today you will be privately transferred by your driver to the airport.

THREE WINTER WONDERLANDS: BERLIN, PRAGUE & MUNICH

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 days

    A Christmas-themed 10-day itinerary for the month of December, featuring three beautiful and festive cities, all of which are renowned for their spectacular Christmas markets and all year round appeal.

    Highlights: Berlin, Prague, Munich

  • Day 1

    BERLIN

    Upon arrival at Berlin Airport you will be welcomed to Germany in the arrivals hall by your local representative, and transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with your check-in.

    Overnight: Hotel de Rome

  • Day 2

    BERLIN

    Start your first morning in Berlin with a 4 hour overview sightseeing tour, with the expert guidance of your local English-speaking guide and in the comfort of your private vehicle. Begin your tour at the recently created Potsdamer Platz, dubbed 'Europe's largest construction site' during the mid 1990s but now a bustling square. The Square comes alive at this time of year with a winter wonderland of lights, music, handicraft and food stalls and a festive ice rink to get you in the magical Christmas spirit.

    Continue along your route where you will see Checkpoint Charlie, the famous crossing point between East and West Berlin and the spectacular headquarters of the DZ Bank, located on Pariser Platz 3 close to the Brandenburg Gate. It was designed by the American architect Frank Gehry, best known for the fantastic Guggenheim building in Bilbao. The interior courtyard is home to Gehry's towering, curvaceous fish that houses a central conference space and captures motion and movement in the building. (Since this is a bank, it is only possible to enter inside the inner lobby which has clear glass doors). Your guide will share fascinating facts about Berlin's turbulent past and you'll hopefully gain a real feel for the city.

    Also visit the Kaiser Wilhelm Memorial Church whose ruins stand as a dramatic reminder of World War II. The church will be set against the atmospheric backdrop of one of the city's most popular Christmas markets, dominated by the 20m high Christmas tree and dazzling lights around the elaborately decorated Breitscheidplatz.

    After your lunch at leisure your guide will once again be on hand for up to 4 hours to give you an orientation tour on foot of some more of the best Christmas markets this city has to offer. There are in fact in excess of 50 markets, some of which are magical and traditional and others more trendy and lively - feel free to tell your guide your preferences so they can adapt the tour for you. However, we would strongly recommend you include the WeihnachtsZauber on Gendarmenmarkt, located in one of the most beautiful squares in the city where you will be greeted with the smells of roasted almonds, hot cocoa and mulled wine. As well as the market stalls offering traditional handicrafts and culinary temptations, enjoy the entertainment and atmosphere created by the many jugglers, acrobats and musical ensembles in the square.

    The evening is at your leisure, so why not spend the evening exploring the markets further, which look especially festive when all lit-up. Or take advantage of one of the many cafes with outdoor heaters and blankets to keep you warm whilst enjoying a glass of Gl?hwein.

    Overnight: Hotel de Rome
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    BERLIN

    After breakfast your private vehicle and English-speaking guide will whisk you away to Charlottenburg Palace for a half-day of sightseeing (6 hours). Not only will you spend time visiting the Palace, which dates back to 1695 and boasts extravagant interiors and richly decorated artifacts, you will also spend time at the Christmas market of festively decorated cabins and glass pagodas doused in romantic light in front of the palace.

    Between 2pm and 6pm enjoy Afternoon Tea with a Christmas theme at the Opera Court living room back at your hotel. Originally a lobby of a former bank the room offers a stunning and cozy atmosphere. Choose from a wonderful selection of cakes, sandwiches and an impressive 23 tea options!

    Overnight: Hotel de Rome
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    BERLIN - PRAGUE

    Your local representative will collect you from your hotel and will be privately transferred to Berlin Railway Station to begin your Concierge Rail journey to Prague. First class seating has been pre-arranged for you and our luggage handlers will look after your bags - allowing you to concentrate on settling into your seats for the journey. When you arrive in Prague you will be met directly off the train and once again our luggage handler will look after your bags in readiness for your private transfer to your hotel. We will assist you with you check-in. On arrival at the hotel you will receive a surprise seasonal welcome gift to put you in the festive mood for your stay in Prague.

    Once you have settled in your English-speaking local guide will collect you and take you on a leisurely 3 hour walking tour of Prague's Old Town. Heading down the quaint cobbled streets, your first stop will be the Old Town Square where you will see the Gothic Town Hall and famous astronomical clock. At Christmas time this square becomes home to a festive market consisting of brightly decorated wooden huts, selling Czech handicrafts, hot food (corn on the cob, sausages and other local specialties) and warm drinks. Most impressive of all is the Christmas tree, shipped from the Krkonose (Giant) mountains in the North of the Czech Republic. The tree is decorated in a blaze of lights, which are turned on every night round 5pm. Set against a dark gothic skyline, this is a spectacular sight topped by the presence of daily carol singers and bands playing festive tunes. Snow is also probable underfoot at this time of year completing the magical scene in front of you. At the end of your tour your guide will accompany back on foot to your hotel.

    Overnight: The Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    PRAGUE

    This morning travel by private vehicle to Prague's Castle District. Hradcany Castle was the seat of Bohemian kings and Holy Roman emperors. Visit the Cathedral of St. Vitus and the Basilica of St. George which was founded in 905. Complete your tour with a visit to the Sanctuary of Our Lady of Loreto, an important place of pilgrimage, and the Strahov Monastery, with its glorious Baroque decorations, where you will enjoy a private tour of the ornate library before returning back to your hotel.

    The rest of the day is at your leisure. Why not take the opportunity to visit some of the City's smaller Christmas markets such as those in Wenceslas Square or Havelske Trziste.

    Overnight: The Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    PRAGUE

    Meet your English-speaking local guide for a half day tour (4hrs) tour of Prague today to explore the Jewish Quarter, birthplace of Franz Kafka. Visit the Jewish Museum which houses the largest collection of Judaica in Europe, the Old New Synagogue and the Jewish Cemetery. End your tour of Prague with a privately guided tour through the Estate Theatre, lasting about 60 minutes with explanation about the theatre renovation, history and work of W.A. Mozart in this theatre, culminating in a short private concert.

    This afternoon your guide will take you the more intimate Christmas market at Namesti Miru where you will find many handmade goods such as soaps, jewellery, wooden toys, ceramic decorations and much more. The market is overlooked by the neo-Gothic church of St Ludmila and a traditional nativity scene.

    Overnight: The Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    PRAGUE - MUNICH

    Your local representative will collect you from your hotel and will be privately transferred to Prague Railway Station to begin your 6 hour Concierge Rail journey to Munich. First class seating has been pre-arranged for you and our luggage handlers will look after your bags - allowing you to concentrate on settling into your seats for the journey. When you arrive in Munich you will be met directly off the train and once again our luggage handler will look after your bags, in readiness for your private transfer to your hotel. We will assist you with you check-in.

    On arrival at The Charles Hotel you will be enjoy a festive and warming glass of Gl?hwein on the terrace.

    You will then be met by your guide who will be at your disposal for 2 hours. As the streets will already be dark you will pass the beautiful lit and decorated window displays on the Maximilianstrasse (a city centre street with luxurious shopping opportunities) and then up to Maffeistrasse where the Loden-Frey fashion house is located, having been established in 1842. Your guide will then accompany you to the Dallmayr Delikatessen - a great place to pick up some gifts or just thoroughly spoil yourself. The extensive and authentic store has designated departments for delicacies including wine, spirits, coffee, honey & jam, confectionary, caviar & salmon, meat, tobacco, fish and much more.

    Overnight: Charles Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    MUNICH

    Today, enjoy an orientation tour (8 hours) of Munich by private vehicle and with your English-speaking guide, your tour will include the Olympia Stadium, home of the 1972 Olympic Games and a visit to Nymphenburg Palace with its extravagant Rococo Amalienborg Pavillion. Continue this afternoon to the Alte Pinotek, one of the world's most famous art galleries who star exhibits include Maximilan's Four Apostles. Your tour will finish today with a visit of the Residenz, former fortress of the Bavarian Kings.

    The evening is at your leisure; alternatively why not attend a performance at the Munich Bayerische Staatsopera House? The daily events for December include ballet, opera and some family friendly options - we would be more than happy to make some suggestions, arrange tickets and book return transfers for you (please note these costs are not included in the tour price).

    Overnight: Charles Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    MUNICH

    Once again today you get the opportunity to explore more of the city centre. Your local English-speaking guide will accompany you on a 3-hour walking tour through the compact Old Town centre with its multitude of fantastic shops, markets and always busy beer halls such as the "Hofbrauhaus" that Munich is so famous for. See the Karlsplatz fountain, the Frauenkirche - Munich's Cathedral - as well as the Marienplatz with the New Rathaus and its chiming clock, playing daily at 11.00am and noon.

    The market at the Marienplatz dates back to the 14th century and is the oldest Christmas market in the city. Meander through the many stall selling Bavarian wood carvings and gingerbread amongst other treats. The festive scene is completed with a 100ft Christmas tree in the square, adorned with 2,500 glittering lights and an Alpine choir performing on the balcony of the town hall.

    You may also like to visit the country's largest manger market. Christmas mangers have had a long tradition in Munich, and the market sells everything you might need to create an authentic nativity scene.

    After your lunch at leisure you will be transferred to Theresienwiese where you will spend the afternoon at the Tollwood Winterfestival. Not only is there a wonderful Christmas market selling crafts and cuisine from around the world, you will also be entertained by music and theatre performances (the majority of activities are ticket-free events, however we can of course provide information and tickets for the other available events - extra charges will apply).

    Your return transfer will drop you back to your hotel so that you can enjoy your final evening at leisure.

    Overnight: Charles Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    DEPARTURE

    Today you will be privately transferred to the airport with the assistance of your local representative.

    Meal Plan: B

BERLIN, PRAGUE & ST. PETERSBURG

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 days

    A 10-day journey to Berlin, Prague, and St. Petersburg, reliving the majestic historical times of these great cultures.

    Highlights: Berlin, Potsdam, Prague, Cesky Krumlov, St Petersburg

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE BERLIN

    Upon arrival at Berlin airport you will be welcomed to Germany by your local representative as you exit customs into the arrivals hall, and transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in procedures.

    Overnight: Hotel de Rome

  • Day 2

    BERLIN

    Today you will enjoy a full-day sightseeing tour (8 hours) with your local English-speaking guide and private vehicle. Begin your tour with a visit to the Reichstag, new home of the German Parliament with its stunning Norman Foster designed dome. Then carry on to the newly created Potsdamer Platz, dubbed `Europe's largest construction site' during the mid 1990s. Visit the nearby Checkpoint Charlie, the famous crossing point between East and West Berlin, you will also visit the Museum dedicated to the checkpoint. After lunch at leisure, travel to the Museum Island, where your English-speaking guide will accompany you around one of the world's greatest museums, the Pergamon Museum (4 hours). The Pergamon Museum houses a vast collection of ancient artifacts and antiquities. You will also visit Berlin Cathedral with its vast dome. After sightseeing you will be transferred back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel de Rome
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    BERLIN - POTSDAM - BERLIN

    Depart Berlin today with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide to the city of Potsdam (8 hours). Your first stop will be Cecilienhof Palace. Built in the early part of the 20th century, the palace served as an important place in world history when the Potsdam Agreement was signed here in 1945 by Truman, Stalin and Churchill. It is said that each party had a private entrance to the castle so that no one party would know the arrival time of the others. After lunch at leisure, you will continue to the Sanssouci Gardens, the royal residence of Frederick the Great. Strolling through the gardens you will discover the Chinese teahouse, housing a small but elaborate porcelain collection, a Dutch mill, a Greek temple and even Roman ruins, erected on a hill opposite the dream palace of Sanssouci. The rococo-style Sanssouci is the best-known palace in the complex; it rests on a hilltop of vineyards and the inside is a display of original furnishings. You will enjoy the benefit of a tour conducted by your own specialist guide of the palace.

    Overnight: Hotel de Rome
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    BERLIN - PRAGUE

    Today you will be met at your hotel and privately transferred with your representative to the Berlin railway station to begin your rail journey. Here you will be shown to your pre-reserved 1st class seating and your luggage will be handled onboard for you. Upon arrival at the Prague railway station you will be met from the door of the train and your luggage will be handled for you. You will then be privately transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in.

    Overnight: The Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    PRAGUE

    Meet your English-speaking local guide for a full day (8hrs) tour of Prague today. First enjoy a walking tour of the Old Town, with its cobbled streets, and the Jewish Quarter, birthplace of Franz Kafka. See the Gothic Town Hall in Old Town Square, famous for its astronomical clock. Visit the Jewish Museum which houses the largest collection of Judaica in Europe, the Old New Synagogue and the Jewish Cemetery. You may also enjoy a walk across the 14th century Charles Bridge. In the afternoon, meet your driver and enjoy a tour to Prague's Castle District. Hradcany Castle was the seat of Bohemian kings and Holy Roman emperors. Visit the Cathedral of St. Vitus, one of the finest and most richly endowed in central Europe, and the Basilica of St. George which was founded in 905. Stroll along the Golden Lane, a row of tiny houses dating from the 16th century. Complete your tour with a visit to the Sanctuary of Our Lady of Loreto, an important place of pilgrimage, and the Strahov Monastery, with its glorious Baroque decorations, where you will enjoy a private tour of the ornate library.

    Overnight: The Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    PRAGUE - CESKY KRUMLOV - PRAGUE

    Today you will enjoy a full day excursion (10 hours) into the Czech countryside with your English-speaking local guide and private vehicle. Visit Cesky Krumlov, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, considered one of the prettiest towns in Bohemia. The highlight of the tour is a visit to Krumlov Castle, which dates back as far as the 12th century. Lunch and afternoon at leisure.

    Overnight: The Augustine Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    PRAGUE - ST PETERSBURG

    Today you will be privately transferred to St Petersburg airport with assistance of your representative. Upon arrival at St Petersburg airport you will be met in the Customs hall by an airport representative holding your name sign and assisted through immigration formalities and customs. After clearing Customs, proceed through the doors to the Airport Hall. At this point, your guide will be waiting for you and you are transferred to your hotel where you will be welcomed to Russia by your local representative and assisted with check-in procedures.

    The balance of the day is free to relax after your journey, or you may choose to explore on your own and get a first impression of this magnificent city.

    Overnight: Hotel Astoria
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    ST PETERSBURG

    This morning meet your English-speaking guide for a visit (4 hours) to one of the most famous museums in the world, the Hermitage. Originally commissioned by Catherine the Great as a winter palace, the museum now houses over three million exhibits. Some of the interior highlights include the Ambassador's Staircase, the Throne Room and the Ballroom. You will also see the Scythian gold room or the Diamond room by special arrangement.

    This afternoon's sightseeing (4 hours) with your private vehicle and English speaking guide will take you out of the city to Tsarskoe Selo ('the Tsar's village'), more commonly known as Pushkin.
