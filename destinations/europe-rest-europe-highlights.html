Rest of Europe Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Austria

    Whether you want to stretch your legs and unwind in the amazingly beautiful Alps, or take a weekend break and enjoy some city sophistication, Austria is the place to discover, unearth and explore. A land of naturally beautiful landscapes, mighty mountains, gurgling lakes and bubbling rivers comes as a standard in a country that runs from the elegance of Vienna in the North to the warm Carinthian Mountains in the South.

    The special concoction of tradition and innovation runs in the very veins of this wonderful European land. Tune up in Salzburg, the birthplace of Mozart and the setting for Sound of Music. Other popular visitor attractions include Mirabell Palace and the Gardens, Nonnberg Convent and Schloss Hellbrunn.

    Soak up the sun, relax over coffee and cakes and browse through museums and art galleries, you can never have enough of the compact yet delightful capital city of Austria, Vienna. Best explored on foot, it houses the majestic Vienna Opera House, the most celebrated opera house the world over and home to the Philharmonic Orchestra. It is also the centre of the old Habsburg Empire, steeped in imperial heritage, music and art and famous for the great romance of its 'cafe society.' Also take out time for a visit to the Schonbrunn Palace, the summer residence of the imperial family demonstrating the magnificence of the opulent 18th century Europe.

    Berlin

    Happy, exuberant, a bit cheeky at times, tolerant, laidback and easygoing, that is how Berlin would've been if it were a person. Let history surround you in Berlin, the city of art, artists and museums. Posing a stark contrast between historical traditions and modern day architecture, Germany's vibrant capital city is home to some major government buildings. The Brandenburg Gate and Checkpoint Charlie stand as a visual reminder of the city's turbulent past, blended with modern architecture such as Daniel Libeskind's spectacular Jewish Museum and the glass dome of the Reichstag designed by Sir Norman Foster.

    Putting the treasures of the world on public display, Berlin is home to impressive museums too numerous to mention. The 'Island of Museums', contains a unique complex of world-class institutions including the Pergamon Museum with its renowned collection of antiquities, and the Old National Gallery which contains an outstanding collection of 18th and 19th century paintings and sculptures.

    The nearby city of Potsdam is of interest too. Schloss Sanssouci is an enchanting Rococo palace with extravagant interiors and an extensive park, while Cecilienhof Palace served as the venue for the Potsdam Conference in 1945 where the main Allied powers established the political balance of Europe.

    Reinventing itself constantly, Berlin is an honest city with honest people. And, whatever you're planning, in Berlin you are sure to get your money's worth.

    Munich

    A modern, cosmopolitan city with a big heart and bigger traditions, the most important thing to take on a holiday to Munich is plenty of time. The land of beer gardens and BMW, Munich is a city with its own style. All contrasts but no contradictions, this 800 year old Bavarian capital has so many attractions that it's practically hard to decide where to pin a start!

    Best explored on foot, Munich is an architectural delight and home to the famous Oktoberfest. Surrounded by chestnut trees, the Hofbrauhaus is the country's most famous beer hall. The Church of the Asam Brothers exemplifies one of the finest examples of Baroque architecture in Europe. The Neo-Gothic town hall features an 80m high clock tower where mechanical knights fight a daily tournament. The Black Forest is one of Germany's most picturesque regions, apart from Munich. The university town of Freiburg and some of Germany's medieval villages are other places of interest.

    The Netherlands

    Sitting north of Belgium and east of Germany, The Netherlands, often referred to as Holland, conjures up images of psychedelic coffee shops in Amsterdam and quaint windmills dotting up the countryside. Exuding diversity and culture, The Netherlands is a fun place to visit. Visit the many famous museums including Rijksmuseum, Van Gogh Museum and Anne Frank's House.

    Look out for the museum dedicated to Frans Hals, the famous Dutch artist in nearby Haarlem. The Hague (Den Haag) is home of the European Court of Justice. Delft is known far and wide for its traditional pottery whilst Leiden is one of the oldest towns and home to the Rijksmuseum van Oudheden (National Museum of Antiquities) and the Stedelijk Museum. And, of course, do not forget to mark a visit to the famous tulip fields in spring and the Keukenhof Gardens.

    Switzerland

    What comes to your mind when you think of Switzerland? Is it the dramatic landscape and the quaint countryside? Or is it the French, German and Italian cultural influences? Or maybe, it is the historical castles and heritage.

    Best known for its financial institutions, cheese, chocolate and the watch making industry, Switzerland as a holiday destination is equally loved the world over. Whether your idea of a perfect vacation is hiking in a world of breathtaking vistas or indulging yourself in the lap of luxury, the tourist places in Switzerland will never, ever disappoint you. The Swiss lakes and mountainous regions comprise of what legends are made of and tales of human endeavor scaling the peaks of the Eiger, Jungfrau and Matterhorn. Marvel at the beautiful landscape from onboard the world famous Glacier Express train that cuts through the mountains, the Jungfraujoch cog wheel train from Interlaken, the paddle-steamer of Lake Lucern and the cable car ride on Mount Pilates.

    Enjoy the forest expanse blanketed in a cover of snow during winters or drive up cattle while you inhale the raw, fresh fragrance of the Alpine meadow during summers or simply unwind with friends over some warm, melting cheese fondue.

    The Czech Republic

    Made of the two provinces of Bohemia and Moravia, the Czech Republic is dotted with quaint little towns, castles and chateaux. Magical Prague is one of the main cities, the wealth of which isn't confined to its mystical palaces, gardens, museums and churches alone.

    Uncover a world of prehistoric finds, magnificent statues and 19th century technical marvels. Moravia is a traditional province located to the east of Bohemia covered with lush, rolling hills and colorful little villages.

    Hungary

    Brimming with culture and natural beauty, Hungary is uniquely diversified. The capital city of Budapest is one of the finest capitals in the world. Seeking to revel your senses in some rejuvenation? Then turn to one of the many thermal spas here, famous for their natural healing spring water.

    One of the most popular holiday destinations in Eastern Europe, the Hungarian landscape lies on a majestic scale featuring the Lake Balaton, the largest lake in the world, the Hortobágy National Park and Puszta (the Great Hungarian Plain), a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Moreover, the area known as the "Danube Bend" is fascinating as one of the cultural centres of the Hungarian Renaissance.

    Poland

    A treasure trove of cultural richness and bountiful gardens of nature, that is how the world pictures Poland, and rightly so. Located bang in the middle of Europe, the country is inhabited by warm, friendly and hospitable people steadily building a modern economy, though holding on dearly to their roots and cultural heritage.

    Overloaded with history and culture (though they are not the only options in Poland), the northern part of the country includes white, sandy beaches of the Baltic coast and lake lands known for forested rolling hills and thousands of bubbling, flowing lakes. The exact age of Warsaw, the capital city is not known since none of its founding documents or its municipal charter has survived invasions, terror and genocide. The city grew alongside the duke's residence, with its location defined by the limits of the Old Town. One of the oldest and most beautiful cities of Eastern Europe, Krakow has been listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

    Greece

    A crossroad of culture, color and civilizations, Greece, though statistically small, is a land big in diversity, culture and heritage.

    A country of beautiful contradictions, Greece takes you back in time. Stretching across many kilometers, Greek shores are famous the world over for their crystal clear waters and unique diversity. Here, you can enjoy an array of water sports apart from swimming. Stroll down olive groves, or take a walk through ancient sites to behold, roam from beaches to mighty, rocky mountains and clusters of sparsely inhabited islands. Explore the breathtaking scenery of the capital city of Athens, a city dating back to some 3,500 years and housing about a third of the population. The city is dominated by the famous Acropolis with the Parthenon, which has stood for 2,000 years along with the nearby Agora which formed the political heart of ancient Athens. Artifacts from excavations of Ancient Greece form the priceless collections of the National Archeological Museum, the Museum of Cycladic Art and the Benáki Museum. Nearby, the Temple of Poseidon towers over steep cliffs which tumble into the Aegean Sea at Cape Sounion, whilst at Delphi Ancient Greeks consulted the oracle at the Temple of Apollo.

    On the Peloponnese, Corinth is home to the Odeion and the Temples of Apollo and Octavia. Epidaurus is renowned for its magnificent 4th century BC theatre, whilst the Sanctuary of Asklepios was an extensive therapeutic and religious centre. Nafplio was shaped by the many struggles between the Turks and Venetians. and houses Venetian castles alongside ancient mosques. Ancient Olympia is home to the original Olympia Games. The site includes the Temple of Zeus, the Palaestra - an ancient training centre for athletes, and also the Archeological Museum with a room dedicated to the Olympic Games.

    The Greek islands, with names that conjure up images of crystal blue sea, fine beaches and white and blue villages, make the rest of the world seem grey and cold. Here, and in the rural backwaters of the mainland, life runs in the slow lane, much as it has done for centuries. Mykonos is a beautiful, cosmopolitan island which has embraced tourism. The whitewashed cube-shaped houses of Mykonos town border narrow winding streets built to withstand wind and pirate raids. Most visitors stay here to visit the tiny, uninhabited island of Delos, now a vast open air museum. Truly the picture postcard destination, Santorini was given its distinctive shape when a volcanic island erupted in 1450 BC. The island includes the Minoan town of Akrotiri, the ancient site of Thira, the monastery of Profitis Ilias, and the village of Pyrgos. The old town of Rhodes is dominated by the Palace of the Grand Masters which houses priceless mosaics from the island of Kos. Encased by ancient walls, the town contains the Mosque of Süleyman, a synagogue and Jewish quarter, and the Street of the Knights. Crete is an island of rugged mountains, crystal blue water, relaxed in nature and idyllic in setting. The island includes Heraklion Archeological Museum, the Palace of Knossos and the charming town of Réthymno.

    Greece is truly a landscape that has given us plenty of postcard images, but still remains incredibly vibrant and impossible to capture.
