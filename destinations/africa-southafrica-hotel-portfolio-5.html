  • 1933 at Lion Sands

    Situated on the perennial Sabie River on the Lion Sands property, 1933 is a fabulous exclusive use bush home, perfect for the ultimate family safari. Carefully constructed to blend in with nature the lodge is by no means out of touch with modernity.

    The lodge's large windows, sliding doors and muted colours mirror the surrounding landscape and create a seamless, indoor-outdoor flow. Timber, thatch and screed flooring add earthy charm to the interior, where the decor is classic-contemporary. Accommodation comprises of four spacious suites, lavish bathrooms, a childrens dormitory that can sleep up to eight and an interleading room for an au-pair.

    Camp Jabulani

    Camp Jabulani, a proud member of the Relais & Châteaux Association, is situated within the Kapama Private Game Reserve in South Africa - a 12 000 hectare Big Five game reserve. Thirteen thousand hectares of undisturbed bushveld forms the backdrop.

    The 6 elegantly furnished canvas suites at Camp Jabulani's luxury accommodation, each sleeping 2 guests, are set on stilts in the upper reaches of tall trees, overlooking a seasonal river and are interconnected to one another by wooden walkways high above the sandy river bed. Each of the six luxury suites are remote and private, with wooden sliding doors present interiors which are stylish and comfortable. Massive stone tubs, an outdoor glass-enclosed shower, a fire-place with separate lounge area, and a private plunge pool complement each of the rooms. During the day the "walls" of the suites are rolled away to bring the bush just that bit closer. A thatched bar, dining room and campfire area in the indigenous garden, contribute to the intimate ambience of the camp.

    The Zindoga Villa is Camp Jabulani's most recent addition and is a palatial self-contained facility. It has been designed for guests who prefer the more exclusive touch, for smaller groups, or for families travelling with younger children. Two individual units are connected by a lounge and dining area. Each unit has a spacious bedroom with en-suite bathroom, expansive wooden deck and a private heated plunge pool. One of the units also has a charming twin room, with private bathroom for two children sharing.

    • Take an early morning hot air balloon trip to view game from a unique angle (additional cost)

    Chitwa Chitwa

    Chitwa Chitwa is situated in the remote northern wilderness on the Sabie Sand Game Reserve. The lodge borders the Kruger National Park to the east with Mala Mala to the south. Each of the 8 suites, all embedded in the African savanna, has been devotedly furnished by Maria Brink.

    Her passion for nature and artistic talent are evoked in the magical atmosphere at Chitwa Chitwa. Together with her husband Charl, she has created a jewel of South African hospitality, an emblem of the savannas most authentic refined luxury. Originally a private retreat of the Brink family, Chitwa Chitwa was transformed by Charl into an oasis of conservation and harmony. His dedicated holistic approach and wise land management are aimed at the handing of an uncontaminated African paradise to the future generations. All suites have a private swimming pool framed by spacious timber decks that overlook the lake.

    • Enjoy a private dinner in a African river bed
    • The Chitwa children eco-experience is designed to encourage younger guests to learn Africa's many secrets and get a great awareness of the environment in general.
    • Visit Dixie a small cultural village situated close to the Gowrie Gate
    • Relax on our shaded deck at sunset and view the game

    Cybele Forest Lodge & Spa

    The first Relais & Chateaux property in South Africa, this exquisite sanctuary of relaxation is nestled amongst 300 acres of forest and mountains. Here lies Cybele Forest Lodge and Health Spa. A perfect addition to any South African adventure, Cybele has so much to offer.

    Cybele is situated 420km east of Johannesburg in the heart of Mpumalanga between White River and Hazyview. It is 40km from the Phabeni Gate into the Kruger National Park and close to many of the exclusive private game reserves in the Sabi Sands. Limited to a maximum of 28 guests, housed in a variety of individual suites and cottages, privacy and fine service is assured. Original works of art adorn the walls, underfloor heating and open fires ensure that the atmosphere is cosy, particularly during the cooler winter months.

    Cybele is all about relaxation. There are superb walks on the property down through the forest to the river and along to the waterfall. With their own horses, enjoy some breathtaking rides through the forest, where experienced grooms are on hand to lead you on an adventure of discovery. The fully-equipped gym overlooks the garden and forest whilst the heated swimming pool is just a short walk away. On entering the spa you will find yourself in a completely private and peaceful zen courtyard, where lemon trees and mint bushes gently scent the air. A wide range of treatments have been designed to rejuvenate the body and soul. The cuisine at Cybele is created from the freshest of locally grown organic produce and is complemented by an award-winning wine list.

    Garonga Safari Camp

    Garonga Safari Camp is an exclusive 12 bed camp in the greater Makalali Conservancy situated to the west of Kruger National Park. Garonga's holistic philosophy is to offer a safari for the soul, an unhurried and intimate wildlife encounter - a time to reflect and find inspiration in nature.

    Activities include game drives, wilderness walks, sleep-outs, outdoor bush-baths, aromatherapy and reflexology sessions. Tracking game on foot is an experience not to be missed. The tented rooms, which reflect the bush surroundings, are spacious and are tempting places to lie and meditate for hours. Excellent cuisine is complemented with a wide variety of South African wines.

    Leopard Hills Private Game Reserve

    Perched high in the forest canopy on a hill overlooking a natural waterhole, Leopard Hills commands fantastic views over the park and to this day is owned by the Kruger family.

    Each of the eight luxurious glass-fronted suites have stilted sundecks and heated rock plunge pools offering superb views of the African bushveld beyond. The traditional Shangaan boma provides a unique evening dining experience evening out under the stars. Other facilities at Leopard Hills include a gym and sala, where a large range of bush treatments are available. The library offers a setting of peace and tranquillity, where you are able to browse through a large selection of literature and African journals.
