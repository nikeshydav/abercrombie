  • Rocco Forte, The Augustine, Prague

    Conveniently located in Prague's picturesque Malá Strana district, stands The Rocco Forte Augustine Hotel. The quintessence of Prague's history, character and beauty is close to the majestic Prague Castle, the Wallenstein Garden and the famous Charles Bridge. The design of the interiors was accomplished by the Rocco Forte Collection's Director of Design.

    The property been created from seven buildings, many of which have historical significance, including the 13th century Augustinian St. Thomas Monastery, after which the hotel is named. The elegantly designed bedrooms all boast stunning views over the courtyards, the monastery chapel or Prague Castle. The suites include an impressive 150sqm The Charles Suite and The Tower Suite, located in the monastery's astronomical tower. All rooms feature a range of decorative accessories inspired by early 20th century Czech cubism, many of which are reproductions from Czech design specialist Modernista, including timber chairs by Pavel Janak and chaise-lounges by Adolf Loos in some of the larger rooms. The Hotel houses the Monastery Restaurant, which overlooks a large courtyard with outside seating area during the summer months. Hotel features two bars; Tom's Bar, located in the monastery's former barrel-vaulted double-height refectory and The Brewery Bar, housed in the cellar, in the former Monastery brewery building. For relaxation, the hotel's spa features four treatment rooms, a hammam offering special heat treatments, sauna, steam room, relaxation area and an 80sqm fully-equipped gym.

    Mandarin Oriental Prague

    Mandarin Oriental, Prague is an intimate hotel housed in a former monastery with roots dating back to the 14th Century. Located in Malá Strana, Prague's oldest quarter, the hotel is just minutes away from many major sights.

    Its 99 rooms and suites combine understated elegance with subtle Oriental touches and the latest technology. Essensia restaurant offers a modern Asian/European menu; guests can also enjoy holistic treatments at the stunning Spa, housed in a former Renaissance chapel.

    Four Seasons Hotel, Prague

    In the lively Stare Mesto quarter, the Four Seasons is in one of Prague's finest locations - beside the banks of the Vltava River close to the St. Charles Bridge and overlooking the historic Hradcany Castle. Formed from three superbly restored historic buildings, the Four Seasons embodies gracious design and detail that celebrate five centuries of Czech culture.

    Inside, cool white marble and pale wood in the reception areas give way to darker, toned down Biedermeier-esque furnishings in the rooms. Naturally, the rooms and suites are beautifully appointed and very well equipped.

    Away from the hotel, wander the historic streets, squares, museums and galleries of vibrant Prague, or further afield, explore the region's gothic palaces, imposing castles and ancient limestone caves.

    Aria Hotel Prague

    The Hotel is located in Mala Strana, on the left-bank of river Vltava close to Charles Bridge and Prague Castle overlooking Vrtbovska Garden (entrance to these gardens is free of charge to hotel guests).

    There are four floors in this hotel and each is dedicated to one of the main categories of music: Jazz, Opera, Classical and Contemporary. All the 52 guest rooms and suites include original art, books and a flat screen computer featuring the biography and music of one of the most influential artists or composers in their category. The hotel offers all of the usual 5* amenities as well as a private screening room, Music Box Entertainment Chamber, Fitness Center, Turkish Steam Bath, Massage room, fine dinning room, Roof Garden
