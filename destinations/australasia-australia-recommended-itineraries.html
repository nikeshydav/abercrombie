Family Australia

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 days

    Explore Australia on a thrilling family itinerary, visiting Sydney, the Australian Outback and sacred Uluru. Tour the Taronga Zoo, learn to surf at Bondi Beach and snorkel amid the Great Barrier Reef.

    Highlights: • Refresh after your overseas flight with immediate hotel check-in to your Hyde Park View room at the Sheraton on the Park
    • See Australian wildlife up close during a private walkabout tour of Taronga Zoo
    • Learn to surf at famous Bondi Beach with a true-blue Aussie surfer
    • Attend an authentic, Aussie-style barbecue in a natural bush setting under the stars
    • Ride a camel in the Outback while taking in views of Uluru (Ayers Rock) and Kata Tjuta
    • Gather food, throw spears and learn about traditional hunting at Cooya Beach with your private Aboriginal guide

  • Day 1

    Arrive Sydney, Australia

    Upon arrival, your hotel room is ready for you. Take time to settle in and rest before exploring Sydney - a former penal colony dating back to the 18th century and now a vibrant, multicultural city - at leisure. Your explorations might take you to wander around the historic Rocks area, the original settlement of the city. Later this afternoon, take a private tea-time cruise of Sydney Harbour, and look out onto the lush surrounding parklands, reserves and gardens.

    Overnight: Sheraton on the Park

  • Day 2

    Sydney

    This morning, depart by ferry for a privately guided visit to Taronga Zoo, home to an aquarium, rainforest aviary, nocturnal animal abode and a koala house. The zoo is located next to the water, and you enjoy magnificent harbor views as you zigzag the paths leading to animal enclosures. You also come face to face with local "residents," including emus, kangaroos, wombats, koalas and kookaburras. Afterward, head to famous Bondi Beach and learn to ride the waves during a surfing lesson with an Aussie surfer (minimum age is seven). Later tonight, attend a welcome dinner, and meet other families sharing your adventure.

    Overnight: Sheraton on the Park
    Meal Plan: B L D

  • Day 3

    Sydney/Alice Springs

    Today you board a flight to Alice Springs, the second-largest city in the Northern Territory of the country's fabled Outback. In this vast land of deserts and semi-arid plains, you and your family witness a mixture of light and color seen nowhere else on earth. The sky here is a bright blue, and the clear air causes the sun to sparkle as it reflects on the red rocks below. Embark on a thrilling ATV ride on a private cattle ranch, where you and your young explorers traverse bush tracks, dry riverbeds and the base of mountain ranges. Tonight, attend a traditional Aussie-style barbecue amid the bush and beneath the endless stars for an unforgettable, quintessential experience.

    Overnight: Chifley Alice Springs Resort
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 4

    Alice Springs/Uluru

    Today is full of educational activities, beginning with a visit to the School of the Air, a radio instruction program that brings homeschooling to isolated children living in the vast Outback. Continue on to The Royal Flying Doctor Service - a service providing medical care to those residing in Australia's furthest reaches - and the Alice Springs Telegraph Station. Later this afternoon, board a flight to Uluru (Ayers Rock), and visit this sacred Aboriginal site. Walk along its base, viewing hallowed locales and paintings of ancient Aboriginal tribal myths. End a day of new discoveries and welcome the evening over canapés and beverages at sunset.

    Overnight: Sails in the Desert
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Uluru/Cairns/Port Douglas

    This morning, catch the Camel Express and embark on a 45-minute ride on a good-natured camel on a caravan through the classic Central Australian desert landscape. Enjoy views of Uluru (formerly known as Ayers Rock) and Kata Tjuta (the Olgas), another set of rock formations that are equally stunning. Later this afternoon fly to Cairns, continuing to your luxurious resort in the seaside town of Port Douglas.

    Overnight: Pullman Port Douglas Sea Temple Resort & Spa
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 6

    Great Barrier Reef

    Experience one of your journey's highlights today: A swim and snorkel excursion among the colorful fish in the crystal-blue waters of the unforgettable Great Barrier Reef. Enjoy a full day of sun and fun as you explore various coral sites that make up this breathtaking natural wonder of the world. The fragile, stunning reef - a UNESCO World Heritage Site - is composed of nearly 3,000 individual reefs and can actually be seen from outer space. The ecosystem supports a rich kaleidoscope of marine life, including dolphins, sea turtles, whales, coral, dugongs, sea snakes, marine birds and as many as 2,000 species of fish.

    Overnight: Pullman Port Douglas Sea Temple Resort & Spa
    Meal Plan: B L

  • Day 7

    Port Douglas/Cooya Beach/Port Douglas

    This morning, travel to Cooya Beach, meandering through primordial habitats with your Aboriginal guide while unearthing the ancient history of the area. Learn about an ages-old way of life as you gather food, throw spears and learn about traditional hunting in the mudflats, mangroves and beaches. Later this evening, attend a special farewell dinner with your new friends.

    Overnight: Pullman Port Douglas Sea Temple Resort & Spa
    Meal Plan: B D

  • Day 8

    Depart Port Douglas

    Relax before your departure flight, or extend your stay and discover another region of this unique part of the world.

    Meal Plan: B

CLASSIC AUSTRALIA

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 11 days

    An ideal itinerary for the first time visitor to Australia, showcasing the best of the country: cosmopolitan Melbourne, iconic harbour city Sydney, the Central Australian monolith Uluru and the Great Barrier Reef.

    Highlights: Melbourne, Coliban Park, Uluru, Palm Cove, Lizard Island and Sydney.

  • Day 1

    Arrive Melbourne

    On arrival into Melbourne you will be met by your guide and chauffeur and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel. Your accommodation is available for immediate occupancy and the remainder of the morning is at leisure.

    After meeting your guide visit Cook's Cottage before taking a walk through the unique Royal Botanic Gardens laid out over 150 years ago and reputed to be the finest in the Southern Hemisphere. Delight in the over 10,000 species and 50,000 individual plants in the 38 hectare gardens, including trees and plants of great cultural value. Many of these plants are irreplaceable, endangered or even extinct in the wild and have become a natural sanctuary for native wildlife including black swans, bell birds, cockatoos and kookaburras that fill the air with their distinctive song.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne, Club Lounge King Room

  • Day 2

    Melbourne - Coliban Park - Melbourne

    This morning drive into the countryside travelling to one of Victoria's oldest and most delightful country properties, Coliban Park. At this private sheep station, established in 1917, watch sheep being shorn and learn first hand about one of Australia's most important industries. Enjoy lunch with the Barber family in their traditional family homestead. A highlight will be a tour of the property, perhaps seeing kangaroos in the wild.

    This evening enjoy drinks and canap?s in the Club lounge.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne, Club Lounge King Room
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Melbourne - Uluru

    Fly to the rich, red Outback. Explore the beautiful valleys at Kata Tjuta (the Olgas), sacred to the Aborigines. Gaze up at Uluru (Ayers Rock), one of the oldest rock formations on Earth.

    On arrival in Uluru you will be met and transferred to your accommodation at Ayers Rock Resort.

    Drive into the desert this afternoon to visit the Kata Tjuta (which means many heads), a series of 36 massive, vividly coloured sandstone domes rising from the plains. In ancient times these domes may have been a single formation bigger than Uluru itself. Walking through the valleys and gorges of this spiritual place is a haunting experience and gives the opportunity to learn a little about the unique desert flora and fauna of Central Australia. Enjoy a champagne toast as the sun sets behind you.

    Overnight: Sails in the Desert, Standard Room
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Uluru - cairns - Palm Cove

    On today's early morning tour drive out to view the magnificent colours of Uluru at sunrise. If you are feeling energetic perhaps attempt the 1,144 foot climb to the summit of the Rock for a spectacular panorama of the surrounding desert. With a circumference of eight kilometres, the Rock is sacred to the local aboriginal tribes, and during a walk around the base see hallowed sites and view paintings of ancient Aboriginal tribal myths before returning to the hotel.

    Later today, fly to yet another Australian treasure, Palm Cove beach in the tropics of Far North Queensland.

    Overnight: Sea Temple, 1 Bedroom Apartment
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Palm Cove - cairns - Lizard Island

    After a sumptuous breakfast at Sea Temple meet your chauffeur for transfer to Cairns Airport. Board your charter flight for Lizard Island, located on Australia's Great Barrier Reef, the world's largest coral reef system. During the flight you will see the amazing Great Barrier Reef from the air, a sight not to be missed.

    Overnight: Lizard Island Lodge, Anchor Bay Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Lizard Island

    Explore the wonders of Lizard Island - one of just two Barrier Reef islands completely surrounded by the reef itself - including white sand beaches, luxurious accommodation and fine food.

    Sample an array of activities, such as scuba diving at the famous "Cod Hole" or try reef or blue water fishing (not included). Leave time to pamper yourself with an island spa treatment (not included) or simply relax under one of the swaying palms.

    During your time at Lizard Island you will enjoy a truly unique and exclusive experience with a visit to the Lizard Island Research Centre. This Research Centre attracts marine scientists from around the globe in their quest to conserve and manage the world's delicate coral reef systems. The environmental source for some 800 research publications, the Research Station is the only field station operated by the Australian Museum and is internationally recognised as a leading marine research facility.

    Led by either Lyle or Ann, the Directors of the Island's Research Station, watch Australian and international marine scientists at work and have a first hand look at a range of marine research projects, many of which utilise the state of the art seawater aquarium system enabling controlled experiments with living reef organisms.

    Overnight: Lizard Island, Anchor Bay Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    Lizard Island

    Relax on Lizard Island or choose one of the optional activities available, see Day 6.

    Overnight: Lizard Island Lodge, Anchor Bay Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 8

    Lizard Island - Cairns - Sydney

    After breakfast on Lizard Island farewell the Great Barrier Reef as your charter flight takes you back to Cairns to connect with a scheduled service to the picturesque city of Sydney.

    On arrival in Sydney meet your personal guide and transfer by private vehicle to the Four Seasons. Superbly situated right on Sydney Harbour in the city's historic Rocks area, the Four Seasons Sydney Hotel has wonderful views of the Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge. It is only a short walk from Sydney's major shopping and business districts. Enjoy Executive Club access including evening drinks and canapés daily.

    Your guide can suggest an array of fine harbour side restaurants for dining this evening (not included) and assist with reservations should you require them.

    Overnight: Four Seasons, Opera View Junior Suite
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Sydney

    You are a guest this morning on a private tour of the famous Sydney Opera House, the busiest performing arts centre in the world. Since opening in 1973, it has brought countless hours of entertainment to millions of people and continues to attract the best in world-class talent year after year. There are nearly 1000 rooms in the Opera House including the five main auditoria, a reception hall, five rehearsal studios, four restaurants, six theatre bars, extensive foyer and lounge areas, sixty dressing rooms and suites, a library, an artists' lounge known as the "Green Room" as well as administrative offices and other services.

    Later enjoy a leisurely privately chartered harbour cruise. Your captain will point out the sights of this beautiful harbour and its icons, the Opera House and the Sydney Harbour Bridge. Enjoy a leisurely light lunch on board while cruising past homes built around this magnificent harbour.

    This afternoon is at leisure for a visit to Taronga Zoo, the Museum of Contemporary Art or another of Sydney's myriad attractions (not included).

    Overnight: Four Seasons, Opera View Junior Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L>

  • Day 10

    Sydney

    Today is at leisure. Perhaps spend time exploring the Rocks, the original settlement of Sydney. Wander through the narrow streets built with convict labour and browse through the dozens of specialty shops. Or perhaps take a Sydney ferry as it delivers the locals to stops around the harbour or take the hydrofoil to Manly and return on the ferry - the choice is yours!

    You may even choose to climb the Harbour Bridge, go sea kayaking or travel to the magnificent Blue Mountains. (Optional, not included).

    Overnight: Four Seasons, Opera View Junior Suite
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 11

    Depart Sydney

    Transfer by private car to Sydney Airport, depart for your flight home.

    Meal Plan: B

Australian Tour of Wonders

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 12 days

    Highlights: This luxury itinerary has been designed to include the best accommodation in each region and tours that we feel are essential for the region:

  • Day 1

    Arrive Melbourne

    On arrival into Melbourne and after clearing customs, you will be met by an Abercrombie & Kent guide and transferred by private vehicle to your accommodation.

    Melbourne, the capital of the state of Victoria, is known as the Garden, Arts and Sports capital of Australia. Its beautiful tree-lined boulevards, many parks and gardens and beautiful Victorian terrace houses, great restaurants, wonderful theatre and art galleries together with international sporting events all combine to make this elegant city one of the world's most livable.

    Afternoon at leisure.

    Park Hyatt Melbourne

    ophisticated and contemporary, the Park Hyatt offers an exclusive sanctuary in the heart of Melbourne. It is the perfect base from which to explore this beautiful city and surrounding areas.

    The rooms are havens of luxury amidst the bustle of historic Melbourne, featuring Madrona paneled walls and rich furnishings with a hint of art deco. Room amenities reflect the hotel's exceptional standards, and include plush beds, beautiful furniture and Italian marble bathrooms with deep soaking tub and walk-in shower. Spacious rooms overlook a district awash with Victorian architecture. Conveniently situated in one of Melbourne's most historic areas, Park Hyatt is at the top of Collins Street, opposite St Patrick's Cathedral and bordered by the delightful Fitzroy Gardens. The sleek Radii Restaurant is a dining experience unlike any other, offering creative, contemporary cuisine Park Club Health and Day Spa offers the ultimate escape with a signature Elemis massage The hotel has an exquisite 25m indoor pool. Other facilities include bars and lounges, a fitness centre and an outdoor tennis court. Babysitting is available

    Club Deluxe room includes club access inclusions of complimentary continental breakfast, all day refreshments and evening canapés and aperitifs.

    overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne - Club Deluxe room

  • Day 2

    Melbourne

    Why is sport such an integral part of Melbourne life?
    Understand how a horse race brings our city to a standstill and why the question on everyone's lips in Melbourne is "Who do you 'barrack' for?".

    Local hosts will take you through Melbourne's sporting obsession and by the end of it you too will understand how, in this city, sport is a way of life.

    A unique Melbourne sightseeing tour.
    • Explore Flemington Racecourse, home of the Melbourne Cup, the horserace that stops the nation.
    • Drive though the Commonwealth Games Athletes Village
    • Tour of Melbourne Cricket Ground
    • Drive around Albert Park, on the Melbourne F1 Albert Park Grand Prix Circuit alongside Melbourne Sports & Aquatic Centre.
    • Enjoy lunch overlooking the Etihad Stadium Arena (formerly Telstra Dome).

    This afternoon is at leisure.
    overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne - Club Deluxe room
    MealPlan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Melbourne to Cairns to Lizard Island

    Meet the A&K chauffeur and transfer to Melbourne airport. Fly Melbourne to Cairns (flight cost not included).

    On arrival into Cairns, transfer to the General Aviation terminal to meet your scheduled flight to Lizard Island - a journey of one hour, flying over the Great Barrier Reef.

    Lizard Island
    Imagine an island so pristine, so untouched, that when you walk one of the twenty-four secluded beaches there are no other footprints. Lizard Island is the most northerly island resort located directly on the Great Barrier Reef, north of Cairns. Repeatedly voted one of the top ten island resorts in the world it is renowned for spectacular fishing, particularly in marlin season (September-December) and world famous diving opportunities such as the "Cod Hole".

    Choose from a wide choice of activities including guided nature walks, kayaking, private beach picnics or pamper yourself with a spa treatment at Azure Spa. Whatever your choice you'll appreciate the peace and tranquility of this natural environment.

    Most recent a ccolades include featuring in Condé Nast Traveller's 2010 Gold List as only one of two Australian hotels named for Best Hotels for Leisure Facilities and ranking 8th place in the category 'Top 20 Resorts Oceania' in the US Condé Nast Traveler Readers' Choice Awards

    Insider Access - Lizard Island Research Station

    Lizard Island Research Centre attracts marine scientists from around the globe in their quest to conserve and manage the world's coral reef systems. The environmental source to some 800 research publications, the Research Station is the only field station operated by the Australian Museum and is internationally recognised as a leading marine research facility. Led by one of the Directors of the Island's Research Station, watch Australian and international marine scientists at work and have a first hand look at a range of marine research projects, many of which utilise the state of the art seawater aquarium system enabling controlled experiments with living reef organisms. This first ever behind the scenes tour is an A&K exclusive.

    overnight: Lizard Island Anchor Bay Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Lizard Island

    Today enjoy a complimentary Half day snorkeling tour of the Great Barrier Reef.

    Under the supervision of a qualified guide, you will experience amazing fish, coral and other marine creatures just metres from the surface. Suitable for both beginners & experienced snorkelers.

    Remainder of day at leisure

    overnight: Lizard Island-Anchor Bay Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Lizard Island

    Day at leisure

    overnight: Lizard IslandAnchor Bay Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Lizard Island to Surfers Paradise

    Today depart Lizard Island onboard a scheduled Hinterland Air service. Transfer to your next domestic flight to Brisbane (flight cost not included).

    On arrival, meet the A&K chauffeur and transfer to the Gold Coast.

    You will receive a complimentary pass to visit the Skypoint Observation Deck. SkyPoint located on top of the iconic Q1 Building, one of the world's tallest residential towers takes you to the highest point above the Gold Coast and offers spectacular 360 degree views from the surf to the hinterland and beyond. Rising 230m into the sky, SkyPoint is located on Level 77 & 78 of the iconic Q1 Resort in southern Surfers Paradise. There are 9,463 curtain wall panels in the tower from level 3 to level 85(top of crown) which cover 32,685m2 and have a combined weight of 1,303 tonnes of glass and aluminium.

    The Sea Temple Surfers Paradise is centre stage and beachfront on Surfers Paradise Esplanade brings a new dimension to contemporary sophistication and luxury. Choose from elegantly designed and fully self-contained 2 and 3 bedroom beach view apartments with spectacular uninterrupted ocean and hinterland views. The Seaduction Restaurant and Bar offers floor-to-ceiling windows and breathtaking views from every table. This stunning east facing restaurant is one of the most sought after dining experience on the Gold Coast.

    overnight: Sea Temple Surfers Paradise - One bedroom beach view apartment
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Surfers Paradise to Australia Zoo and beyond

    This is a genuine tour to Australia onboard a private, luxury 4WD with a private guide for the whole day. Your tour includes lunch, drinks and snacks; your choice of length of stay; AND options to visit the Glasshouse Mountains, Sunshine Coast beaches, rainforest or gorgeous Monteville village. Zoo entry, in-vehicle video and pick-up from your hotel door included.

    Tour also includes: . Entry to Australia Zoo
    . Pick-up / drop-off from your accommodation
    . Guided commentary of the region, Glasshouse Mountains and the zoo
    . In-vehicle video featuring Australia Zoo and the Irwin family
    . Great seats for the Wildlife Warrior show in the Crocoseum
    . Hand feed Asian elephants
    . Pat Koalas and hand feed Kangaroos
    . Guided tour of the new state of the art Australia Zoo Wildlife Hospital
    . Option to include either a fully or part-guided luxury "Caddy" tour
    . Option to meet the animals on an 'Animal Encounter'
    . Option to stop at a fruit stall.

    overnight: Sea Temple Surfers Paradise - One Bedroom Beach View Apartment

  • Day 8

    Day at leisure Surfers Paradise

    Day at leisure.

    overnight: Sea Temple Surfers Paradise -One Bedroom Beach View Apartment

  • Day 9

    Surfers Paradise to Sydney

    Meet the A&K chauffeur for a private transfer to Gold Coast Airport (Coolangatta). Fly to Sydney (flight cost not included).

    On arrival into Sydney's Domestic airport you will be met by your A&K guide and transferred to your accommodation.

    Sydney, the capital of the state of New South Wales, is Australia's most well known city. With its extraordinary icons both natural and man-made this is a city that never fails to please the eye. It's magnificent natural harbour spanned by the 'coat hanger', the Sydney Harbour Bridge, and the man-made sails of the Sydney Opera House are just some of its most widely photographed views.

    The Park Hyatt Hotel Sydney offers guests intimate, residential-style hotel with architecture, design and art that reflects the Australian landscape. Starting from spacious 40 square metres (410 square feet), most of the hotel's 155 luxurious guest rooms are designed to celebrate Park Hyatt's unique indoor-outdoor experience. Floor to ceiling glass doors open onto private balconies that honour the hotel's exclusive waterfront location. The hotel's dedicated team of butlers provide for an unparalleled guest experience with around the clock discreet, personalized service for all guests. Park Hyatt Sydney's premier restaurant, The Dining room, offers quintessential Sydney dining experience, whilst The Living Room provides guests with comfortable and elegant setting to enjoy refreshments throughout the day. There is also a spa, relaxation room, gym and panoramic rooftop pool with cabanas.

    overnight: Park Hyatt Sydney - Opera Deluxe room with complimentary breakfast

  • Day 10

    Sydney

    Morning at leisure.

    Cruise like a local departs at 12.30 from outside the hotel.

    This afternoon you will be orientated with Sydney from the most enviable vantage point - on the water! Cruising on Sydney Harbour is a classic Australian experience, and today you will board 'Calypso' - a 52 foot motor cruiser with a minimum of two and a maximum of twelve guests onboard.

    Whilst cruising this iconic harbour you will have the opportunity to swim, fish or kayak. However most choose to sit back with a glass of Australian beer or wine, relax on the sunbeds and take in the amazing sights - the Harbour Bridge, the Opera House, Luna Park, Fort Denison and the waterfront homes of the rich and famous. A barbeque lunch is served on board, while anchored in a secluded cove. Your 4 hour experience on the water ends at Campbell's Cove pontoon, just a short walk from your hotel.

    overnight: Park Hyatt Sydney - Opera Deluxe room with complimentary breakfast
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 11

    Sydney - at leisure

    Full day to enjoy the sights of Sydney at leisure.

    overnight: Park Hyatt Sydney - Opera Deluxe room with complimentary breakfast
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 12

    Sydney departure

    Meet the A&K chauffeur in the hotel lobby and transfer by private vehicle to Sydney airport.

    Meal Plan: B

SOUTH AUSTRALIAN SPLENDOURS

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 6 days

    This itinerary is ideal for an outdoor lovers escape, taking in all the attractions of South Australia.

    Highlights: Privately guided touring through the Barossa, Flinders Ranges and Clare Valley. Truly luxurious accommodation throughout. Kangaroo Island - wildlife and scenery in abundance.

  • Day 1

    Adelaide - Barossa Valley

    Meet your guide and drive north to the picturesque Barossa Valley, try your hand at wine making, meet local characters such as Diana Laidlaw and enjoy a relaxing lunch at a local restaurant.

    At Pancake Estate Wines in the Barossa Valley meet Diana Laidlaw, a retired Member of the South Australian Parliament (1982-2002) and a gifted ceramic artist. In 2005, Diana was rewarded with a Member of the Order of Australia for services to the parliament, the arts and improved transport systems. Today she takes an active role in the family business that has been in operation since 1968 and develops her craft with a studio and gallery on site. Taste estate grown wines and tour the winery, studio and gallery with Diana.

    Overnight: The Louise, Marananga Suite
    Meal Plan: L,D

  • Day 2

    Barossa Valley - Flinders Ranges

    This morning depart for the rich and rugged landscape of the Flinders Ranges. Stay at Rawnsley Park Station, a working sheep station offering secluded luxury eco-villa accommodation. The remainder of the day is free to enjoy the stunning surroundings.

    Overnight: Rawnsley Park Lodge, Luxury Eco Villa
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 3

    Flinders Ranges - Clare Valley - Jamestown

    This morning head south to the Clare Valley, renowned for its historic towns, fertile soils and elegant wines. Spend the evening at North Bundaleer, one of the last grand pastoral homesteads built in South Australia. Enjoy a sense of occasion with dinner in the grand dining room then retire to the elegant drawing room or the comfortable library with its eclectic selection of books.

    Overnight: North Bundaleer Homestead, Red Room Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Jamestown - Kangaroo Island

    After breakfast visit Sevenhill Cellars, the oldest vineyard in the Clare Valley before continuing on to Adelaide for your flight to Kangaroo Island. Stay at the stunning Southern Ocean Lodge, located at Hanson Bay and commanding spectacular ocean, beach and wilderness views. The 21-suite lodge offers the ultimate fusion of nature, luxury and exquisite dining. After dinner take a Koala Night Walk tour or a Southern Star Gaze.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge, Flinders Suite

  • Day 5

    Kangaroo Island

    Discover the magnificent coastal scenery and abundant wildlife for which the island is renowned. Southern Ocean Lodge is Australia's first true luxury lodge, offering a unique and exclusive travel experience on Kangaroo Island in South Australia. Floating atop a secluded cliff on a rugged stretch of coast, the lodge commands peerless views of the wild Southern Ocean and pristine Kangaroo Island wilderness. Sensitive, intimate and sophisticated, Southern Ocean Lodge is a sanctuary of refined comfort and luxe. Activity options, exclusive to guests include: walking among the sea lions at Seal Bay Conservation Park or viewing koalas, penguins and kangaroos all in their natural habitat.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge, Flinders Suite
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    Kangaroo Island - Depart

    Explore Kangaroo Island further today, try a spot beach fishing or relax with a spa treatment before your return flight to Adelaide.

    Meal Plan: B
