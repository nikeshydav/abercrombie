Suggested Kruger & Mpumalanga accommodation

  • Rattray's on Mala Mala

    Spacious suites at Rattray's offer the discerning traveller private plunge pools, enormous living areas and two bathrooms! Inside the main lodge there is a beautifully air-conditioned library well stocked for the avid reader, an attractive bar and an adjoining wine cellar with a wide range of South Africa's best wines.

    Meals are either served in the elegant dining room, on the deck or in the traditional reed boma under the magnificent jackalberry tree. The game drives here reflect Rattray's desire to minimalise impact on the area and the result is that an impressive density and diversity of wildlife is to be seen.

    Royal Malewane

    Hidden on a 30,000 acre private reserve adjacent to the Kruger National Park, Royal Malewane is an exclusive getaway for those who value privacy, personalised service and some of the best game viewing South Africa has to offer.

    Set high amongst a canopy of indigenous acacia and lanternbush trees and linked via elevated walkways, each of the six Royal Malewane suites benefit from rich deck wood terraces, thatched gazebos and infinity pools. Window-side Victorian baths add to the glamour of these opulent suites. In addition, the stunning Royal Suite and Malewane Suite comfortably accommodates up to four guests and affords the complimentary services of a private chef, butler and game drives.

    Royal Malewane provides an escape from the constraints of modern life to a synthesis of wildest Africa and age-old colonial splendour. As the lodge is set deep in the African bush, this is ideal for the traveller who wants to experience true Africa. Lavish meals are prepared by award-winning chef, John Jackson and include sumptuous bush breakfasts and dinners served in a Bedouin tent lit by Moroccan lanterns. Game viewing, by open vehicle or on foot, is tailored. Royal Malewane is privileged to have the only surviving Master Tracker currently working in Southern Africa. This guiding excellence combined with a staff to guest ratio of four to one ensures a supremely personal service.

    Sabi Sabi Little Bush Camp

    Situated within the Sabi Sabi Private Game Reserve, an unspoilt part of Africa, ecologically and geographically integrated with the adjacent Kruger National Park, Little Bush Camp has just six suites positioned along the seasonal Msuthlu River.

    Each with en suite bathrooms, indoor and outdoor showers and wooden decks, this really is a fabulous private luxury camp. Delicious dinners are served outside under gently lit trees and often in the candle lit boma - a stunning location to share stories of the day's game viewing, whilst marvelling at the African night sky. Families are more than welcome at Little Bush Camp and a lovely swimming pool provides a welcome break from the heat of the day.

    Singita Boulders Lodge

    Boulders Lodge takes its inspiration from a huge boulder that could not be removed without disturbing the environment during construction of this exquisite lodge. Therefore the entire camp has been carefully positioned so as to tread lightly on the surrounding environment.

    An innovative design organically incorporates these granite masses into its structure using native woods and textured fabrics. Interiors are truly stunning: the height of contemporary minimalism blending seamlessly with the space and temperament of the bush. Identical in size only to Ebony Lodge, Boulders Lodge accommodates 24 clients in 12 glass fronted suites, two of which have two bedrooms and can be converted for families.

    If you can tear yourself away from the beauty of Boulders Lodge suites, there are many activities available. Aside from thrilling morning and evening game drives, experienced guides can lead you into the bush on foot for a close-up experience of this precious eco-system. If you would prefer not to walk, mountain bikes are also available and rangers are only too pleased to guide you on a trail through the bush. The African Trading store stocks a wide range of locally crafted curios and this combined with a tour of one of the local communities will really add to your understanding of the local area and Singita's involvement.

    Singita Ebony Lodge

    Singita Private Game Reserve is an exclusive 18,000 hectare sanctuary adjoining the Kruger National Park. Well known for its prolific wildlife, especially its concentration of big game and relaxed cats, in particular the often elusive leopard. The reserve allows for both game driving and walking safaris. Here stand three Singita properties, Ebony Lodge, Boulders Lodge and Castleton Camp.

    Ebony Lodge is a charming tribute to the opulence of its colonial heritage. Gloriously appointed high on the banks of the Sand River, and richly adorned with the scarlet fabrics so loved in African culture, this camp has a cosy, immediately intimate feel. Reminiscent of a bohemian past, an expansive library, heavy wooden furniture and banqueting table are all in evidence. Twelve luxurious suites, two of which have two bedrooms and can convert to family suites benefit from private plunge pools and private bush settings. Suites are large and divided into separate living, bedroom and bathroom/dressing areas. Decadent bathrooms house large free-standing Victorian baths, each with separate powerful indoor and outdoor showers.

    Game drives may be enjoyed in the morning and evening and are led by knowledgeable and experienced rangers. Cuisine is of the highest standard and complemented by an extensive cellar showcases a premium selection of wines, including some of South Africa's most sought-after private reserves and limited release wines. Between game drives, why not indulge yourself with a visit to the new and highly acclaimed Premier Wine Boutique, or perhaps relax in the spa with an African head massage.

    Singita Lebombo & Singita Sweni

    Situated in one of the most beautiful and game rich areas of the Greater Kruger National Park and in a 15,000 hectare private concession on the remote eastern boundary of the parkstand two amazing lodges: Singita Lebombo and Singita Sweni. Here, amidst the Lebombo Mountains at the confluence of the Nwanetsi and Sweni Rivers, a rare wilderness experience can be enjoyed with spectacular scenery boasting the highest concentration of wildlife in the entire park.

    Lebombo Lodge, an architectural icon of the safari world, is cantilevered out over rocks with the Sweni River far below. The 15 suites cutting-edge contemporary style fuses glass, slatted wood and bleached branches with natural, textured fabrics and luxury amenities. Immediately below Lebombo and set on the banks of the Sweni River, Sweni Lodge is the smallest of the Singita Lodges. Six luxurious suites, identical to Lebombo's are tucked away amongst the trees. The central area is decorated in a sea of chocolate and avocado fabrics.

    The Village Spa offers a range of massages, skin care and body treatments, all performed by highly qualified therapists. A relaxation area is available between treatments and after exercising in the fully-equipped gym. Outstanding cuisine combines African flavours with western culinary traditions. Guides are skilled at providing informative and entertaining game drives. Conservation is key at Singita and the staff are only too happy to discuss their latest initiatives.

    Tanda Tula Safari Camp Timbavati

    Comprising of twelve luxurious thatched east African style tents each benefit from riverbed views, private wooden decks, Victorian baths and outdoor showers.

    The Tandala suite is set apart, with its own pool, personal cook, ranger and game-viewing vehicle. Aside from twice daily game drives, bush walks and bird hide viewing with picnic breakfast or lunch can also be arranged. The rangers at Tanda Tula have specialist knowledge of wildlife conservation. For the past five years, Tanda Tula has served as the main sponsor for the Save The Elephants Transboundary Elephant Research Project. The study tracks the movements of elephants in order to enable the reserve to make informed management decisions.

    Londolozi Tree Camp

    The Manor house is situated within the Tintswalo Concession, on the private Manyeleti Reserve, within the Greater Kruger National Park. The large thatched Manor house boasts three downstairs en suite air-conditioned bedrooms that can be configured as either twins or doubles, one of the rooms has an optional child's room which leads off it.

    The main double en suite bedroom is positioned upstairs with beautiful views of the bushveld. Dining can be enjoyed on the patio or in the open air boma under the stunning night sky. Guests of the Manor house are also welcome to use the facilities of the Élan Vital spa at the Tintswalo Main Safari lodge.

    Tintswalo Safari Lodge

    Tintswalo Safari Lodge nestles alongside a riverbed within Manyeleti - a pristine reserve that opens into the vastness of Kruger National Park. Main Lodge overlooks a waterhole frequented by the 'Big Five' and smaller game.

    Six exquisitely appointed en suite Explorer Suites and the sumptuous Presidential Suite all have their own plunge pools. Incomparable dining in unique indoor and outdoor venues and indulgence in complete relaxation and rejuvenation in the Élan Vital Spa, enhance the Tintswalo experience. Game viewing at Tintswalo takes place on foot, or in individually seated Land Rovers. A special children's programme is available for young visitors. Tintswalo sponsors and runs an effective community education project in the area.
