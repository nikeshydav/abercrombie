TUSCANY ON FOOT

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 days

    A great way to experience the true Tuscany is to go off the beaten path guided through the countryside on foot. These family-friendly walking/gentle hiking ideas can be completed in one tour or alternatively clients can take their pick and we can build an itinerary to suit them - maybe mixing it with sightseeing or other family activities.

    Highlights: San Donat, Monsanto, Greve, Montefioralle, Castel Petraia, Monteriggioni, Volterra, Balze, Pienza, Lajatico Region, Vinci, Montalbano, Montecatini, Massa, Cozzile

  • Day 1

    THE CHIANTI BLACK ROOSTER ROUTE: SAN DONATO TO MONSANTO WITH CHIANTI TASTING

    A leisurely walk through the heart of the Chianti Classico producing vineyards, south of Florence. Chianti Classico is type of wine easily identified by the black rooster on the bottle. Your walk beings at San Donato and end Monsanto both famous for their Sangiovese rich wines. After working up an appetite, a welcome snack awaits in a farmhouse with an abundant reward including tasting of three wines (Vernaccia, Chianti Classico, Chianti Reserve) with a selection of local sheep cheese, sorbet and coffee.

    Walking level - easy

    Overnight: Please contact us for options

  • Day 2

    THE CHIANTI CLASSICO ROUTE: GREVE TO MONTEFIORALLE TO BADIA A PASSIGNANO

    The walk begins at the lively market town of Greve-in-Chianti before an uphill walk to the delightful village of Montefioralle, an important stronghold during the wars between Florence and Siena and birthplace of Amerigo Vespucci. The walk continues past vineyards and stunning Tuscan vistas to Badia di Passignano, a medieval hamlet and abbey founded in 395 A.C by the Archbishop of Florence. Monks of the Vallombrosian Order still live in the abbey and are the keepers of its cultural and historical treasures. Here its possible to celebrate your accomplishment with wine tastings at the winery of the noble Antinori Family.

    Walking level - easy

    Overnight: Please contact us for options

  • Day 3

    CASTEL PETRAIA TO MONTERIGGIONI ALONG THE VIA FRANCIGENA

    The starting point of the walk is Villa Petraia, a former castle which was transformed into a beautiful villa by the Medici family in the 14th century. Walk along the ancient via Francigena, a pilgrimmage route that stretched from England through France leading Pilgrims to Rome. Your destination today is not so far, stopping at Badia a Isola for a rest and to see the 1000 year old Abbey. Onwards from here walk to the village of Monteriggioni, famous throughout Tuscany because its vast defensive walls are all still intact and is mentioned in Dante's "Divine Comedy".

    Walking level - easy

    Overnight: Please contact us for options

  • Day 4

    VOLTERRA TO THE BALZE AND LUNCH ON A SHEPHERD'S FARM

    Before the walk begins, your nature guide will be pleased to show you the town of Volterra, which dates from Neolitic times however evidence of Etruscan and Roman times are still visible today. It is also famous for its white sculpture workshops which work with white alabaster. More recently as a location in the "Twilight" novels (although it was not filmed here). Your walk brings you through one of Tuscany's most unusual geological phenomenon, the Balze. The Balze is a deep, eroded limestone ravine whose continued growth has claimed several churches and villages since the Middle Ages as the buildings tumbled into its deep gullies. Today it is an ancient monastery which looks towards the edge! Your walk ends at a rustic sheep farm where you will have the opportunity to try local organic specialities such as caprese, ricotta cheese with honey, salumi and a welcome glass of Sangiovese wine. You can also see the processes of making cheese with sheeps milk and (if you wish) meet the flock!

    Walking level - easy

    Overnight: Please contact us for options

  • Day 5

    PIENZA

    A simple walk admiring the picture-postcard views of the Southern Tuscany region of the Val d'Orcia. The majority of the walk is along a white (gravel) road through a long avenue of cypress trees until you arrive at Pienza. Pienza itself is a UNESCO listed town, often referred to as a utopia of Renaissance planning. The concepts were first put into practice after Pope Pius II decided, in 1459, to transform the look of his birthplace and gave it its name Pienza ("Pio's Town"). Pienza offers a variety of beautiful spots for lunch and shopping for local craftwares.

    Walking level - easy

    Overnight: Please contact us for options

  • Day 6

    THE LAJATICO REGION, AND ANDREA BOCELLI'S WINERY

    Walk in the Western part of Tuscany, close to Pisa , in the Lajatico Region. Your walk takes you through gentle Tuscan hills and cypress lined byways until you arrive at the Theater of Silence. The Theatre is a natural theatre with a lake at its center framed in the hills of Lajatico where Andrea Bocelli, its honorary president, performs at the theatre in July of every year. The rest of the year it remains silent. You walk takes you onwards, past an old windmill to Bocelli's winery - the family vineyard of the great singer where you will have the opportunity to try Chianti Boccelli in the cantine.

    Walking level - easy

    Overnight: Please contact us for options

  • Day 7

    LEONARDO'S VINCI AND MONTALBANO WITH FARMHOUSE LUNCH

    The great renaissance artist, scientist and genius Leonardo da Vinci, came from the town of Vinci which is close to Florence. The walk begins in Vinci at Leonardo's Museum, housed in the imposing Castello Guidi, the museum plays host to a collection of the Leonardo's designs, and models of his various machines. From Vinci it is an uphill and downhill hike past the vineyards to Montalbano. The landscape is dominated by hills with grapevine cultivation occurring in the plains or on terraced fields, olive trees spreading over the higher areas with chestnut trees at the summit. Your walk ends at a farmhouse where a typical Tuscan lunch of local produce awaits.

    Walking level - moderate

    Overnight: Please contact us for options

  • Day 8

    MONTECATINI AND THE MEDIEVAL VILLAGES OF MASSA AND COZZILE WITH PICNIC LUNCH

    A circular walk starting and ending in the natural spa town of Montecatini Terme brings you into the region known as Valdinievole in the province of Pistoia. Your walk brings you to the genuine medieval villages of Massa and Cozzile, where your driver will be waiting with a sumptuous picnic lunch, followed by gelato from one of the very best artisans in the area. On the walk back to Montecatini, weary legs can let the funicular take the strain back to the art nouveau town and avail themselves of one of the many natural spas.

    Walking level - easy /moderate

    Description of Walk Ratings:

    1) Easy: Suitable for most people with a basic level of fitness and in good health. The walk involves mostly light ascent and descent.

    2) Moderate: Suitable for hill walkers with a reasonable fitness level. The walk may involve more ascent than descent

    Your Walk Leader:

    Your walk leader is experienced in the routes on offer and will be a fully qualified nature and hiking guide of the Tuscany Region. Your guide will know many off the beaten path stops and can give full commentary on the history, geography and nature of the areas you walk through. Your safety is always his/her main concern - they will be qualified to administer primary First Aid and will carry a First Aid kit.

    Guided walks available in English, French, German and Portuguese

    Practicalities:
    Walking times and pace are flexible and according to clients' desires and abilities.

    Guests may walk on different types of terrain, even or uneven, gravel or dirt roads, country roads or paths, it is suggested for clients to bring adequate walking/hiking shoes with ankle support as well as clothing fit for the weather, to make the hike as comfortable and as safe as possible.

    Guests will be transported from their hotel to the start point of the walk. It can be arranged for the vehicle to meet guests at strategic points along the way to offer a resting place, refreshments or change of course to another location.

    Bottled water will be provided.

The Real Italian Job

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 days

    A 9 day Italian tour from North to South for the real motoring aficionado.

    Highlights: Turin, Pininfarina, Chianti, Bologna, Galleria Ferrari museum, Paletti Autodroma at Varano de' Melegari, Rome, Tuscany

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE TURIN, ITALY'S MOTOR CITY

    Private chauffeur transfer to your hotel in the heart of Turin.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    TURIN, THE ART OF THE AUTOMOBILE

    Full-day guided tour focusing on the long heritage of the Italian automobile industry. Begin at the Lingotto, Fiat's original production facility in Turin, whose rooftop testing track featured in The Italian Job movie. The iconic structure churned out cars from the 1930s until 1982, and today, has been miraculously transformed into a hotel and entertainment venue. Within the Lingotto is the Pinacoteca Agnelli, which houses a permanent collection of the Angelli dynasty's private artworks including Canaletto, Matisse and Canova. The Agnelli family founded Fiat, and are, to this day, the most revered family in the country.

    You also have a pre-scheduled appointment at the gallery of one of the world's foremost styling houses, Pininfarina. For more than 80 years, Pininfarina has pushed the envelope to produce the most aesthetically beautiful cars, including Ferrari, Maserati, and Alfa Romeo. The collection houses design icons from the past as well as examples of future prototypes.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    MASERATI TRACK DRIVING

    Your English-speaking driver will bring you to the Paletti Autodroma at Varano de' Melegari, in the heart of Italian luxury sports car territory. Enjoy a two day intensive course with an ex-professional racing driver, learning how to drive and safely control a Maserati GT super-car on track conditions.

    Experiences include learning to drive at high-speed and control slides without the aid of electronic devices.

    Spend the night in the nearby city of Parma.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    MASERATI TRACK DRIVING

    Continue your 2 day Maserati Track Driving course and spend a second night in Parma.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    CAVALINO ROSSO, FERRARI DAY

    Travel with your English-speaking driver to Maranello, the home of Ferrari. Here you will have a privately guided tour (1 hour) of the Galleria Ferrari museum which exhibits a bevy of Grand Tourers, Formula 1 racing cars and development prototypes from throughout the illustrious history of Italy's most famous marque.

    After the visit it is time to experience Ferrari for real with a 60 minute awesome experience test driving a Ferrari in and around the mountains surrounding Maranello. A 'co-driver' will also join you.

    Select (in advance) from a F548 Italia, a Scuderia or a California. The California has 2 (tight) back seats should all the family want to enjoy the ride. Please remember your driving license.

    Your day ends in the heart of the medieval city of Bologna, overnight base of this year's Mille Miglia race.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    THE ULTIMATE ITALIAN SUPERCAR, LAMBORGHINI

    In nearby Sant'Agata Bolognese is the home of Italy's most expensive car manufacturer, Lamborghini. Enjoy privileged access into the spotless factory area and follow the whole process of making a Lamborghini Gallardo. See how the mechanical precision parts are hand-made from single blocks of metal. Witness the engine mounting, bodywork welding and paintwork, all the way through to the experts hand-finishing the interiors.

    Also, experience the exquisite collection of cars in the museum before returning to Bologna.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    THE SUN HIGHWAY TO TUSCANY

    This morning your gleaming red Ferrari will be delivered to your hotel and, after being shown the controls, the open road is yours. Pick up the Mille Miglia route again, maybe taking the Autostrade del Sole (Sun Highway) into Tuscany, turn some heads in Florence's via Tournabuoni or enjoy the sweeping roads of Chianti before checking into your hotel in the heart of the wine district of Southern Tuscany.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    THE FINAL STRETCH, TUSCANY TO ROME

    The final day with your gleaming Ferrari is yours to make your way through Umbria and Lazio. Follow the classic route of the race, passing by Etruscan Vitterbo before arriving into the great city of Rome, to the admiring glances of many Italians as you pull up outside your luxury hotel.

    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    DEPART ROME

    Private Chauffeur transfer to the airport

    Meal Plan: B

THE ADRIATIC COAST

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 days

    Wander off the beaten path and explore Italy's dramatic Adriatic coast.

    Highlights: Venice, Rimini, San Marino, Emilia Romagna Coast, Le Marche, Ripatransone, Bay of Gargano, Salvalletri and Lecce.

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE VENICE

    Upon arrival in Venice, meet your local representative and speed across the Lagoon by private motor-launch to your hotel, right on the banks of the Grand Canal. The balance of your day is free to begin your explorations of Venice.

    Venice is a magical, romantic city. Its beautiful Gothic architecture and narrow winding alleyways and canals make Venice a unique and atmospheric city that you won't want to leave. One of the highlights of Italy, Venice's unmatched position on the north end of the Adriatic Sea gave the city economic and defensive advantages over its trading rivals. Its amassed wealth was invested in building monuments, churches, bridges and squares, many of which have not only graced the artist's canvas but also created a city that is itself a work of art.

    Overnight: Ca' Sagredo

  • Day 2

    VENICE - PO DELTA - RAVENNA - RIMINI

    This morning you are transferred to Piazzale Roma where you meet your experienced English-speaking driver/guide for the overland journey down the Adriatic Coast.

    Travel from Venice to Rimini via the Po River Delta and the Comacchio Valley. En route you also visit Ravenna, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that is famous for its remarkable mosaics. Visit the Cathedral of San Vitale with its elaborate Byzantine mosaics, dating from the 6th century AD. Also visit the exquisite Basilica of Sant' Apollinare in the nearby village of Classe.

    Spend the evening in the vibrant city of Rimini.

    Overnight: Il Grand Hotel di Rimini
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    THE REPUBLIC OF SAN MARINO - EMILIA ROMAGNA COAST

    Enjoy a full day exploration of the Republic of San Marino and the villages of the Emilia-Romagna coastline, including Riccione, Gabicce and Misano Adriatico.

    San Marino is marvellously situated on Monte Titano, its three castles crowning the triple peaks of the hill and its picturesque streets and old houses meandering downward from the top. Legend has it that the town was founded by St. Marinus, a stone-mason from Dalmatia who fled here escaping persecution in approximately 300 AD. It remains today the world's oldest sovereign state, independent but protected by the Italian state, and its stamps and coins are highly valued by collectors.

    There are some very attractive places of interest here: the Church of San Francesco dating from the 15th century, the Basilica di San Marino with its richly decorated interior, and the Palazzo del Governo, a sumptuously appointed government centre from whose roof the views are magnificent. The three castles, Rocca, Cesta, and Montale are all accessible by road and the commanding views from the hilltop are impressive indeed.

    Overnight: Il Grand Hotel di Rimini
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    LE MARCHE

    Today you depart Rimini and head inland to the heart of Le Marche to visit the renaissance town of Urbino, famous birthplace of the artist Raphael. Begin your visit with a trip to Ducal Palace which boasts world class Italian renaissance art, including two of Piero della Francesca's finest pictures and a portrait by Raphael. The palace also houses a fascinating archaeological collection mostly of Roman tablets.

    Continue to the coast where you may wish to stop for a sea food lunch in Senigallia, one of the popular seaside towns on the Adriatic close to the famous Velvet Beach.

    Pass through Ancona and around the Conero Peninsula before visiting the seaside villages of Porto Recanati and Civitanova Marche. You may wish to visit the nearby medieval hilltop borgo of Civitanova Marche Alta, still partly enclosed by its old walls. Among the fine ancient palaces and churches is an unusually good modern Italian art collection in the civic Galleria d'Arte Moderna.

    Overnight: Hotel I-Canalichi
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    RIPATRANSONE

    Today you will visit the beautiful town of Ascoli Piceno, the centre of the southern Marche. The breathtaking main square, Piazza del Popolo, is one of the most elegant provincial squares in all of Italy. To one side of the square stands the Palazzo del Popolo, a splendid 13th century building guarded by a monumental statue of Pope Paul III.

    During the afternoon, continue inland to visit the medieval thermal spa town of Aquasanta, perched on mountain slopes at the southwestern tip of the Marche region. Visitors have come here over the centuries to seek treatment in the mud pools or the warm sulphurous waters of the thermal baths which are claimed to be excellent for your health.

    Overnight: Country Hotel I-Canalichi
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    RIPATRANSONE - BAY OF GARGANO

    Today you are transferred overland to the Bay of Gargano in the Apulia region of southern Italy. En route, you may wish to stop in the seaside town of Termoli in the Molise region. It is situated along the coastline and along an ancient borough up on a rocky promontory. You can also see the massive 12th century Swabian Castle which, from its privileged position, controlled the Adriatic and hosted a military garrison to protect the population from the attacks of Saracen pirates and Venetian merchants.

    The evening is spent at leisure at your luxurious hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel Baia dei Faraglioni
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    THE BAY OF GARGANO

    Today explore the stunning coastal area that is part of the Gargano National Park. Enjoy the breathtaking views of the coast en route to the coastal towns of Vieste and Peschici.

    The medieval historic centre of Vieste rises up on rocks, dominated by the castle built by Federico II. The narrow streets and whitewashed stone buildings are typical of Apulia fishing villages. Among the natural beauty spots you can visit is the great white rock known as Pizzomunno or Faraglione di Vieste.

    Peschici is located at the northernmost tip of the peninsula and is famous for its trabucchi, traditional fishing net structures. Here you can enjoy a tasty 'fritto misto' plate of fried fish along with a local wine of the region.

    Overnight: Hotel Baia dei Faraglioni
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    GARGAGNO - ALBEROBELLO - MATERA - SALVALLETRI

    Today you have the opportunity to visit some of the most unique places in the southern heel of Italy as you transfer to Salvalletri.

    Alberobello has been named a UNESCO World Heritage Site for the more than 1,000 "trulli", beehive shaped white houses, that date back to the 13th century. The distinctive style is unique to this region.

    Afterwards drive to the extraordinary town of Matera whose poverty and isolation in former times saw it cast as the setting for Carlo Levi's book Christ Stopped at Eboli. Today the old town is a fascinating reminder of the hardships of former times, with 120 churches and houses carved out of the rock side, and a guided visit brings those days vividly to life.

    Overnight: Masseria San Domenico
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    LECCE - MARTINA FRANCA

    Enjoy an excursion to nearby Lecce, considered the most beautiful town in southern Italy. Visit the honey-coloured Cathedral of the Holy Cross and the Palazzo della Prefettura, as well as the gardens, courtyards and old streets of this beautiful baroque town. There will be time to purchase some of the local handicrafts for which the town is famous.

    Continue onto Martina Franca, a fascinating mix of Moorish and Baroque influences, famous for its wine and olive oil production. Here you will visit one of the area's most renowned olive oil presses and have the chance to taste the fine olive oil.

    Overnight: Masseria San Domenico
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    DEPART ITALY

    Transfer today to Bari or Brindisi airport.

    Meal Plan: B

A WEEK IN VENETO AND EMILIA ROMAGNA

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 days

    A week privately touring some of the highlights of northeastern Italy.

    Highlights: Venice, Bologna, Florence, Verona and Padua.

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE VENICE

    Arrive in true style into Venice with a private motor-launch which will speed across the Lagoon to your 5* hotel which is just a two minute walk from St. Mark's Square. Your local representative will be waiting to welcome you to Venice.

    Your local representative is a part of a unique network of over 20 representatives throughout Italy who make sure your vacation operates smoothly and to your absolute satisfaction. Your local representative is there on the ground to ensure that your travel arrangements run perfectly. He/she is on call should you have any queries and is there to assist you.

    You will be confirmed for two nights at the Bauer Hotel in a superior, inclusive of buffet breakfast.

    Overnight: Bauer Hotel

  • Day 2

    THE SPLENDOUR OF VENICE

    Today you will be met at your hotel by your local English-speaking guide for a half-day walking tour (3 hours) of Venice. Your tour will include visits to St. Mark's Basilica, with its rich decoration of marbles and golden mosaics, and the Doge's Palace, once the seat of government and residence of the doges.

    The rest of the day is yours to explore this truly fascinating city. You may be interested in an evening performance at La Fenice Opera House which is just a short walk away.

    Overnight: Bauer Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    VENICE TO 'THE FAT ONE'

    Leave Venice in the style you arrived with a private boat to the point where your chauffeur will be waiting for you to take you inland to the Emilia Romagna - Italy's breadbasket and its medieval capital city of Bologna.

    Having settled in to your hotel, this afternoon you will be met by your local English-speaking guide for a half-day walking tour (3 hours) of the historical centre to include a visit to the Basilica of San Petronius. Construction on this cathedral began in the 14th century and work was still in progress on this vast building some 300 years later. It still stands unfinished with the wings of the transept missing and the facade only partially decorated, lacking most of the marble face the architects planned several hundreds of years ago. You may also visit the complex of four churches known collectively as Santo Stefano and the intricate medieval streets around the piazzas. The region and the city is known to be the gastronomic heart of Italy, earning it the nickname of 'The Fat One'

    You will be confirmed at the Grand Hotel Baglioni in a classic room inclusive of buffet breakfast.

    Overnight: Grand Hotel Baglioni
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    HOME OF THE RENAISSANCE - FLORENCE

    Your local representative will escort you on foot to the railway station where you will be shown to your First Class pre-reserved seats on the express train bound for Florence. The journey is less than 60 minutes through lush mountainous countryside.

    Upon arrival you will be met from the train door by your expert guide for a walking tour of this great city. Begin your walk in the bustling San Lorenzo district, past the Medici Chapels to Brunelleschi's vast Duomo. Continue through the city's most important Piazza's to the Uffizi Gallery, housing some of the world's most important art treasures. Continue on to see the Ponte Vecchio and the newly restored Piazza Santa Maria Novella before taking your return train journey back to Bologna where your local will be waiting to take you back to your hotel. Today's excursion involves a lot of walking on level surfaces with cobblestones.

    Overnight: Grand Hotel Baglioni
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    BOLOGNA TO VERONA

    Your English-speaking chauffeur awaits you in the hotel lobby to drive you straight to your next destination, the historic city of Verona.

    On arrival the day is yours to relax, take in the atmosphere or maybe catch up on some shopping.

    You will be confirmed at the Due Torri Baglioni Hotel in a classic room inclusive of buffet breakfast.

    Overnight: Due Torri
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    HISTORIC VERONA

    Today you will be met at your hotel by your local English-speaking guide for a 2 hour walking tour of Verona. The city owes its romantic reputation to Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet, which was set here. Your tour will include Piazza delle Erbe, Juliet's House and the Roman Arena, one of the largest and finest preserved amphitheatres which is still used today as one of Italy's most important musical venues.

    Overnight: Due Torri
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    RETURN TO 'LA SERENISSIMA' VIA PADUA

    Your driver awaits you once again to take you back to Venice for your final night. Stop on the way in the illustrious city of Padua where you have time at leisure to discover the wonderful art and architecture such as the Capella degli Scrovegni Return to your Venetian hotel once again by private boat to celebrate the final evening of your Italian adventure.

    You will be confirmed for a night at the Bauer Hotel in a superior, inclusive of buffet breakfast.

    Overnight: Bauer Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    DEPARTURE FROM VENICE

    Transfer by private motorboat once again back to Venice Airport.

    Meal Plan: B
