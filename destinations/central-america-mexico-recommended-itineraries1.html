WHALES OF BAJA

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 5 Nights

    Experience the longest mammal migration on earth as the Pacific gray whales migrate from Alaska's Bering Sea to the warm waters of San Ignacio Lagoon on Mexico's Baja Peninsula. Please note you will be joining travellers from all parts of the world. The maximum group size will be 20 guests.

    Departures: 21 & 24th February 2013

    Highlights: Witness the gray whale migration, See the whales and their calves, Stay in cabanas right on the beach and overlooking the lagoon

  • Day 1

    Loreto, Mexico

    Arrive in Loreto. Founded in 1697, Loreto was the capital of California when it was under Mexico's rule. In the evening there will be a welcome dinner and an orientation on the gray whales of Baja.

  • Day 2

    Loreto/San Ignacio Lagoon

    Drive across the desert to the Pacific Ocean. Accommodation for the next few nights will be in beach cabanas. Overlooking the bay, they are simply furnished and are clean and comfortable. Environmental bathroom facilities are shared. The cabanas are located right on the San Ignacio Lagoon and are the best remote accommodation available to experience the Vizcaino Desert, a UN Biosphere Reserve.

  • Day 3-4

    Whale watching

    Two full days to see the magnificent whales. There will be two excursions by boat of up to two hours each day. San Ignacio Lagoon is the traditional breeding and birthing grounds for the gray whales after their 5,000 mile migration from the Bering Sea of Alaska. Babies at birth can measure up to 16 feet in length. The whales are often intrigued with humans making for close encounters with the boats. Local guides will interpret their behaviour and will maximise the encounters without disturbing them. Between boat excursions, other activities available include bird watching and hiking with naturalists.

  • Day 5

    San Ignacio Lagoon/Loreto

    There will be a final chance to visit the whales before returning to Loreto.

  • Day 6

    Depart Loreto

    Transfer to the airport for your onward flight or extend to explore more of Mexico.

SWIM WITH WHALE SHARKS - JUNE TO SEPT

Programme Overview

  • Available June through September

    The Riviera Maya is one of the hottest spots to enjoy the amazing experience of swimming with the whale sharks. Get up close and personal with these gentle giants. Every year the destination welcomes visitors as well as celebrities to take part in this unique experience across the summer until the beginning of September. As many as 1,400 whale sharks may make their way through the waters north of Isla Mujeres every summer.

    Whale sharks are rare species and they are the largest fish in the world measuring up to 59 feet and weighing 15 tons. Its ample mouth extends to almost 5 feet when opened. In the 80's there had been less than 350 confirmed in the entire world. Since then, consistent sightings have been recorded and its migration is now a magical experience that you can enjoy in few places throughout the world. The waters surrounding Isla Mujeres and Contoy Island boast one of the densest populations of migrating whale sharks in the world. The presence of these creatures in the warm waters of the Yucatan Peninsula is thanks to the nutrient filled water, which generates a large quantity of available food.

    Those choosing to experience the wonder of swimming with them are accompanied by experts who provide all the necessary information to better understand their behavior. Once familiarized with the animal, you can jump off the boat accompanied by a guide and snorkel to see it up close. There is no need to fear, since this shark feeds exclusively on tiny organisms known as plankton; therefore they are completely harmless towards humans.
