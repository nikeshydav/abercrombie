    Troms? was developed in the late 18th century when its harbor, noted for being ice-free year round due to the Gulf Stream, was a major center for fishing and trade. The town is situated on an island linked to the mainland by the striking Troms? Bridge. Upon arrival you will have a city tour that will include the Domkirke cathedral and the statue of polar Explorer Roald Amundsen, then to the southern tip of the island to visit the Troms? museum with it?s fascinating displays of both ancient and modern life of the Sami, as well as archeology and geology of northern Norway.     Barents Sea Norway

    Today, Le Boreal heads north through the Barents Sea. This morning enjoy breakfast and spend time on deck with your expedition naturalists as you keep an eye out for whales and the many sea-birds common in these waters.

    This afternoon, Le Boreal reaches Bj?rn?ya (Bear Island) which is the southernmost island in the Svalbard Archipelego. We plan to board Zodiacs to cruise along the southern tip of the island and its massive bird cliffs, home to kittiwakes, guillemots and the occasional puffin.   • Friday- Thursday August 01-07, 2014

    Svalbard Island

    The Le Boreal serves as your maritime passport to adventure in this remote Arctic Archipelago that very few people in the world will ever have the opportunity to view. We will have 7 full days of expedition cruising as we circumnavigate Spitsbergen Island. The Captain and Expedition team are on the lookout for the day?s best expeditionary options. Visits may include:

    Hornsund is a stunning fjord is on the Southwest corner of Spitsbergen with rugged rock walls surmounting blue hued glaciers criss-crossed by crevasses, with birds perching in the high cliffs. The ?glacier coast? to the south provides magnificent views of slow moving glaciers that travel toward the sea. Here we often will see Beluga whales feeding at the front of the glacier as well as ringed seals.

    Halvmaneoya also referred to as ?half moon island? and was used as a hunting ground for over-wintering Russians and Norwegians in the late 17th century. Polar Bears and arctic foxes were hunted for their meat and fur, and walrus for their blubber. Today, the island is a haven for birds including eider ducks, arctic terns, snow bunting and grey phalaropes. We also visit an old hunting hut ?Bjorneborg? (the bear castle) which was used by hunters and trappers.

    Barents?ya and Edge?ya: These islands in Southern Svalbard off the southern tip of Spitsbergen provide excellent opportunities for Tundra hikes to enjoy the scenic beauty that is Svalbard. The colors of the tundra are alive with blooming flowers, varied bird and duck species and grazing reindeer.

    Torrelnesset is located in far southwest coast of the island of Nordaustlandet which is the highest arctic land mass in the Svalbard archipelago and predominantly ice-covered. We go ashore at the barren coast to view the walrus colonies that are common here.

    Hinlopen Strait: Le Boreal will navigate this passageway between the Main island of Spitsbergen and the island of Nordaustlandet. Here we will visit alkefejellet or ?auk cliffs? which are sheer and stunning cliffs that drop directly into the sea and are home to colonies of auks.

    Woodfjorden and Leifdefjorden are known for their unique and intriguing geological distinctions. Jagged peaks and dormant volcano?s surround red sand-stone hills and still active geothermal springs. These geological features create an ideal habitat for birds and other wildlife including Polar Bears, fox and reindeer.

    Magdalenefjordenis located on Spitsbergen?s Northwest corner and one of the most scenic and iconic fjords in all of Svalbard.

    NyAlesund has a history stretching back to the early Arctic explorers. Today, a polar research station occupies the site where a Norwegian coal mind once operated. Here Scientists study meteorology, seismology, atmospheric sciences and marine biology. Conditions permitting, you go ashore to visit the small community, including the northernmost post office in the world and the small museum and art gallery.

    Throughout our landings and days of cruising in Spitsbergen, our primarily objective will be to view the Polar Bears and other wildlife that reside in this remote Arctic wilderness. Each landing is under the close supervision of our Bear Guardians, and our time at sea will be primarily in search of ice, which is where we have the optimal chance to find the Ice Bears in their natural habitat.

    Overnight: MV Le Boreal
    Meals: Breakfast, Lunch and Dinner
