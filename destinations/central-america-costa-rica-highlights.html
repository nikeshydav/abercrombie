Costa Rica Highlights & Sightseeing

  • CORCOVADO AND THE OSA PENINSULA

    Corcovado National Park is tucked away in the southwest corner of Costa Rica's Osa Peninsula. Thanks to its spellbinding natural beauty, the area is deemed a crown jewel in the already stunning landscape of the country.

    In fact, Corcovado has been described as "the most biologically intense place on Earth in terms of biodiversity" by National Geographic and has delighted a number of tropical ecologists for a long time. It is reachable via a short scenic flight from San Jose. Visitors here can expect to witness abundant wildlife in a diverse form including a number of rare endangered species. One can stay in lodges within the rainforest that offer the best of raw natural wealth just footsteps from the door. Travelers can also relish the splendor of Corcovado through arranged guided tours which range from sunset bird watching, to boat trips out on the Golfo Dulce and greet the prying dolphins. Once here they can also dive & snorkel in turquoise blue water or hike the Osa Trail in the rainforest. Mammals like ocelots, tapir and even - on occasion - jaguars and the birdwatching here is first-rate including hummingbirds, macaws, toucans and hundreds of other species.

    Manuel Antonio

    Manuel Antonio National park is a compact national park located in Costa Rica's Central Pacific Conservation Area just off the Pacific Coast. This picturesque park famous for its extensive hiking trails and spotless beaches attracts nearly 150,000 visitors annually, both from abroad and local areas.

    The biodiversity here has enabled various life forms to thrive and species such as raccoon, coatamundi, agouti, two-toed sloth, the white-faced and squirrel monkey.

    Visitors can indulge themselves in the beauty of its serene beaches including Espadilla Sur, Puerto Escondido and Manuel Antonio and stroll beside their never ending coastlines. Along with stunning accommodation possibilities on offer, anyone striving for some adrenaline rush can also partake in sports such as rafting, canopy tours, kayaking, sport fishing or simply go hiking or bird watching in its flourishing forests.

    MONTEVERDE

    Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve is a significant ecotourism hub in Costa Rica and named after the town it is based in. This astounding reserve consists of six ecological zones with nearly 90% of area recognized as unspoiled forests.

    Nestled up in Tilaran Mountain range, this reserve boasts of a rich biodiversity having over 2500 species of flora and fauna. Although sightings of large cats and tapir here are extremely rare, you should spot monkeys and even possibly the quetzal bird in the summer months. Here visitors can also find the largest number of orchids in the world, having over 500 species of this beautiful flower. One can hike leisurely through the labyrinth of marked walking trails within the reserve in a serene setting of pure nature.

    The cloud forest and Santa Elena, a nearby region is accessible through road, however during wetter season i.e. May - September transit is less frequent. Traveler may combine a Monteverde trip with a visit to the famous Arenal volcano and lake before arriving at the numinous plains of this cloud forest.

    NICOYA PENINSULA

    Among the astounding string of awe-inspiring Costa Rica tourist places, Nicoya Peninsula easily stands out because of its separated location from mainland Costa Rica by the Gulf of Nicoya and Tempisque Estuary. Nicoya Peninsula flaunts powdery white sand beaches along its extensive coastline which exhibits dramatic scenery.

    This beach-fringed, sun-kissed strip of land is swarmed with veiled coves and bays and a scenic countryside where one can enjoy ecotourism. Its rural area is characterized by the friendly locals, cattle rearing farms, sparsely populated villages and a relaxed pace of life allowing one to indulge and unwind!

    TORTUGUERO

    Tortuguero literally means Land of Turtles, is a scenic little village on the North Caribbean coast of Costa Rica which also embraces within itself a national park with the same name. Take your Sightseeing in Costa Rica at a whole new level by witnessing the rare communities of Green Sea and Leatherback turtles nestling on its golden beaches where they usually retreat to lay or hatch their eggs. The Tortuguero National Park is also a protected natural habitat for these turtles bestowing them with a safe haven to thrive and flourish in. To minimize interference and human impact on their fragile natural environment the observation tours are watchfully arranged at night.

    In the meantime, daytime at the national park is usually marked by journeying around the contrasting landscape of Tortuguero encompassing the lush green mangroves, wetlands, golden beaches and misty rainforest teeming with wildlife such as alligators, iguanas, monkeys and a myriad of bird life. As there are no roads leading to this coastal town, travelers commute via picturesque boat rides from the town of Moin found close of the port of Limon.
