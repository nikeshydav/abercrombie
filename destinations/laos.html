Travel To Laos

Landlocked and laidback, Laos is blessed with possibly the most warm, hospitable and chilled-out people on earth! Bordered by China, Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia and Myanmar, South East Asia's most pristine environment and culturally rich country is fast earning the status of a holiday hub.

Abercrombie and Kent offer holidays in Laos for travelers looking to discover an unspoiled country with enchanting villages and superb scenery.

Travelers come to Laos to experience undulating mountains, remote villages, tribal arts and crafts and the magic of the Mekong and leave with so much more; inimitable memories of the ever-smiling locals, saffron clad monks moving silently along ancient monasteries and markets awash with fresh, local produce. If you are hunting for the authenticity of Asia, Laos surely doesn't disappoint.

Visiting Laos is like going back in time when high-rises, traffic jams and the hustle and bustle of city life didn't exist. Nestled between Mekong and Khan Rivers, it is a beautiful place to say the least. Visitors will be awed by the verdant landscape and dazzling scenery which is unlike any other in the whole wide world. Here Buddhism permeates every facet of life, change comes slowly and life winds down early. The country's varied topography is home to the flat plains of the remote Bolovens Plateau, the Khong Pha Pheng Falls, the mysterious Plain of Jars and hill tribes in the forests of the North.

Experience the refreshingly simple village life in the capital city of Vientiane. Get lost as you stroll down the roads of the historical and royal city of Luang Prabang where watching saffron robed monks asking for alms and moving silently is perhaps the most beguiling scene you will experience in the whole of Asia.

Travel to Laos with Abercrombie and Kent and get a first-hand experience into the country that speaks of serenity and love.

Best Time To Go Laos

Best time to go to Laos

The cooler part of the dry season, November to February is when Laos is at its best, avoiding the torrential rains of the summer months, and the sweltering heat of April and May.
