The Australian Red Heart

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 days

    See in 7 days the Australian Red Heart

    Highlights:

    . privately guided experiences, self-driving adventure

    . Incredible geological formations

    . Spectacular Kings Canyon

    . Aboriginal dot painting, camel riding and cattle station quad biking

    . Self Drive the Mereenie Loop Road

  • Day 1

    Alice Springs

    On arrival meet your local guide and take a journey of discovery around the Outback's most famous town. Visit the Royal Flying Doctor Service base, the Alice Springs School of the Air and the Alice Springs Telegraph Station (circa 1872). This evening enjoy a genuine 'Outback Aussie BBQ', see native plants and wildlife and hear about Aboriginal culture. Learn to throw a boomerang, watch damper being cooked in the coals of the fire, enjoy billy tea and marvel at the expanse of the country while dining under the stars.

    Overnight: Chifley Alice Springs Resort, Deluxe Rivergum Room
    Meal Plan: D

  • Day 2

    Alice Springs

    This morning you will be transferred to Alice Springs Desert Park, which naturalist Sir David Attenborough considers to be the best wildlife park in the world. Experience the flora, fauna, history and people of Australia's desert culture. Later this afternoon embark on an exhilarating two-hour Quad Bike adventure at the oldest working cattle station in NT, Undoolya Station. Experience the vastness of the property aboard your fully-automatic and extremely safe quad bike, frequently encountering kangaroos, goannas, emus and station cattle. Your evening is at leisure: you might choose to dine at the resort or in town.

    Overnight: Chifley Alice Springs Resort, Deluxe Rivergum Room
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    Alice Springs - Kings Canyon

    After breakfast at Alice Springs Resort, you'll collect your 4WD Land-cruiser and embark on a self-driving adventure to the spectacular and rugged Western MacDonnell Ranges via Larapinta Drive and the former mission station of Hermannsburg, then on the Mereenie Loop Road through to Kings Canyon.

    Overnight: Kings Canyon Resort, Deluxe Room
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    Kings Canyon

    Today visit stunning Kings Canyon with its towering vertical walls and pockets of lush vegetation. The famous Rim Walk takes around 3 hours and is best experienced early in the morning or in the later afternoon. The view to the valley floor is breathtaking, and a shorter walk traces the bottom of the gorge to a lookout which offers a view of the spectacular cliff face at the end of the canyon.

    Overnight: Kings Canyon Resort, Deluxe Room
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    Kings Canyon - Uluru

    Driving to Ayers Rock today you pass the spectacular Mt Connor Lookout and then make a stop at the legendary outback cattle station of Curtin Springs before driving into Yulara. The World Heritage protected areas of Uluru (Ayers Rock) and Kata Tjuta (The Olgas) are often considered the most fascinating natural wonders of the world. This afternoon meet your driver/guide for a private tour of the massive rock domes of the Olgas. As you walk through the Gorge, learn how the Aboriginal people used the plants of the area in their daily life. This evening enjoy the 'Sounds of Silence' Dinner in the desert, a gourmet Barbeque dinner lit by lanterns and candlelight. After dinner an astronomer takes you on a tour of the glittering southern night skies.

    Overnight: Sails in the Desert, Standard Room
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 6

    Uluru

    This morning witness the magic of sunrise at Ayers Rock, now known as Uluru, then perhaps take the opportunity to climb (weather permitting), or walk around the sandstone Rock, the world's largest monolith. During this morning's comprehensive tour you will also visit the Cultural Centre. This afternoon, return to the Cultural Centre and join Aboriginal artists for a Dot Painting Workshop, and learn how Aboriginal culture is expressed in artwork. Then board your 'ship of the desert', riding high atop a camel through the dunes as the colours of the late afternoon settle onto the surrounding scenery.

    Overnight: Sails in the Desert, Standard Room
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Uluru

    Today is at leisure to relax by the pool before returning your vehicle to the Ayers Rock Airport and departing for home.

    Meal Plan: B

The Kimberley Far Away

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 7 days

    The Kimberley far away, Iconic landscapes Western Australia, 7 days

    Highlights: . Faraway Bay Bush Camp
    . Scenic flight by fixed wing and helicopter over the Bungle Bungles
    . Fly over the famous King George Falls
    . Outstanding fishing opportunities
    . Exclusive El Questro Homestead
    . Champagne viewing of Argyle Pink Diamonds

  • Day 1

    Arrive Kununurra

    Arrive and privately tour Kununurra, the gateway to the stunning East Kimberley Region. Take in the highlights of the town and learn about and visit the Ord Irrigation Scheme, and its importance in the farming of watermelons, honeydew melons, star fruits and mangos, as well as the new superfood chia (an ancient Aztec grain recently redeveloped in Kununurra) and sandalwood plantations. Then stop at Kimberley Fine Diamonds for a champagne viewing of the stunning Argyle Pink Diamonds, before settling into your accommodation for the evening.

    Overnight: The Kimberley Grande, Kings Pool View Room

  • Day 2

    Bungle Bungles

    Take a scenic journey by fixed-wing aeroplane over the Kununurra region and waterways viewing expansive sheep and cattle stations such as Lissadell and Texas Downs. Touch down at Purnululu National Park's Bellburn Airfield and walk into Cathedral Gorge and Piccaninny Creek. Enjoy a picnic lunch then board your helicopter for an exhilarating 30-minute low-level flight over the Bungle Bungles and its many rugged gorges. Re-joining the fixed wing aircraft once more, fly over the Osmand Range and Argyle Diamond Mine en route back to Kununurra.

    Overnight: The Kimberley Grande, Kings Pool View Room
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    Kununurra - Faraway Bay

    Take to the skies again this morning to fly over the Ord and Berkeley Rivers, then follow the turquoise Kimberley coastline looking down on dugongs, sea turtles, sharks and salt water crocodiles. You'll circle the famous King George Falls then land on the private airstrip of Faraway Bay Bush Camp. Transfer the short distance to the Camp by open 4WD. Settle in and slow down to the pace of a land far, far away.

    Overnight: Faraway Bay Bush Camp, Ensuite Cabin
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 4

    Faraway Bay

    Select from an array of guided and independent activities today. Fish from a dinghy in Faraway Bay, or simply observe the stunning marine and birdlife. Take a refreshing dip in the freshwater clifftop pool, enjoy a bushwalk, or embark on a day out on a 13-metre ocean-going cruiser to the mighty King George River.

    Overnight: Faraway Bay Bush Camp, Ensuite Cabin
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 5

    Faraway Bay - El Questro

    Depart Faraway Bay this morning by light aircraft bound for El Questro Homestead, in the Durack Ranges. After an elegant lunch looking over the lush oasis of gardens, cruise on the Chamberlain Gorge. Spot Rock Wallabies, Johnston Crocodiles and abundant birdlife as you are guided by an El Questro Ranger. Returning on dusk, marvel at the changing colours on the towering cliffs of the gorge. Dine under the stars in superior style this evening.

    Overnight: El Questro Homestead, Homestead Room
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 6

    El Questro

    Today choose from a variety of activities: learn bush tucker history, soak in the Zebedee Thermal Springs, try horseriding, take a guided fishing, bushwalking or bird-watching tour, or simply relax by the pool. Dining this evening is again an event in itself.

    Overnight: El Questro Homestead, Homestead Room
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

  • Day 7

    El Questro - Kununurra

    After breakfast, transfer privately by road to Kununurra for your departing flight (flight not included).

    Meal Plan: B

An Aussie Family Holiday

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 11 days

    Family holiday through Australia.

    Highlights: Melbourne, Alice Springs, Ayers Rock, Port Douglas, Sydney

  • Day 1

    Arrive Melbourne

    On arrival into Melbourne and after clearing customs, you will be met by an guide and transferred by private vehicle to your accommodation. Your room will have been pre-registered for immediate occupancy.

    Melbourne, the capital of the state of Victoria, is known as the Garden, Arts and Sports capital of Australia. Its beautiful tree-lined boulevards, many parks and gardens and beautiful Victorian terrace houses, great restaurants, wonderful theatre and art galleries together with international sporting events all combine to make this elegant city one of the world's most liveable.

    After some time to check-in, perhaps have a late breakfast, relax & refresh - meet your representative in the hotel lobby for your unique afternoon orientation of Melbourne.

    With your entertaining local guide take a leisurely ride through Melbourne's character filled districts like Fitzroy, Carlton, Southbank and Docklands. Visit major sites like the Queen Victoria Markets, St. Patrick's Cathedral, Victoria Parliament, Melbourne Cricket Ground and Federation Square. Stop to take in a funky Fitzroy café for a quick drink! Over 3.5-4 hours riding around 17 kilometres at a very easy pace with plenty of stops you'll take in all the iconic sights and get a feel for the living city. You will return to your hotel in the late afternoon.

    Park Hyatt Melbourne The Park Hyatt Melbourne is the city's most deluxe hotel. It is located at the 'top end' of Collins Street, directly opposite Melbourne's grandest cathedral and a few steps from Parliament House, the central business district and the beautiful Fitzroy Gardens. The luxurious oversized rooms are a haven for relaxation and comfort.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 2

    Melbourne

    After breakfast this morning you will be met by your driver and transfer by private vehicle an hour & a half out of the city to rural Victoria. Today is a real treat - an exclusive experience - where you are welcomed into the home of the Barber family, to learn a little of what it is like to be an Aussie family 'living off the land'. This is a genuine operating sheep property which has been in the Barber Family name since 1917. The magnificent 6500 acre property is located approx 90 minutes from Melbourne. Meet Duncan Barber a 4th generation farmer of this land and enjoy an exclusive visit to his sheep farm.

    On arrival at the property you are treated to a delicious home-baked morning tea in the delightful surroundings of the Homestead, built in 1859. The history of the property and the passion the family have for it will become apparent as you enjoy your time getting to know your hosts Duncan, his wife Jess and Duncan's mother Rita.

    After morning tea, it is time for you to watch as Duncan shears a sheep. Within the confines of this historic woolshed built in 1944, you will learn more about the wool industry and the rich 150 year history which can be traced back to the Elector of Saxony. The finer details of preparing and classing a fleece and the process a fleece goes through will be detailed, and this beautiful product that ends up as fabric made into Italy's finest men's suits (Zegna) is sure to impress and enthral. This is also a chance to watch Duncan work his Australian Sheep Dogs & to plant an Australian Native Tree to commemorate your visit to the property before returning to the homestead for a three course lunch with the Barber family. Your lunch will include Australian wine, beer and water.

    To conclude your day, Duncan will take you for a drive around the property to spot kangaroos in their natural environment before you re-join the vehicle and journey back to Melbourne. You will return to the city in the late afternoon with a sense of having broken through the tourist crust & seen the real Australia.

    Overnight: Park Hyatt Melbourne
    Meal Plan: B,LD

  • Day 3

    Melbourne - Alice Springs

    After breakfast this morning meet your driver in the hotel lobby for your private vehicle transfer to the airport for your flight to Alice Springs. On arrival into Alice Springs you will be met by your driver & transferred to your hotel.

    After some time to check in & perhaps enjoy some lunch at the resort you will be met by your representative for your half day, private sightseeing tour of 'Alice'.

    The vastness of the Australian outback is highlighted on this tour. Visit the School of Air and the Royal Flying Doctor Service to see how people living in remote areas have access to essential services. See spectacular views of 'the Alice' and surrounding MacDonnell Ranges from Anzac Hill, stop at the Old Telegraph Station - site of the original communications post and visit an Aboriginal Art Gallery where you have the opportunity to purchase yourself a painting to take home.

    The Alice Springs School of the Air was the first of its kind established in Australia. Here you will discover how this unique educational service provides for 140 children living in and around the one million square kilometres of Central Australia.

    The Royal Flying Doctor Service is the first, largest and most comprehensive aero medical organisation in the world. A visit to the base allows for an interesting insight into how people living in the outback have access to essential medical services.

    Located on the banks of the Todd River the Alice Springs Telegraph Station was established in 1872 to relay messages from Darwin to Adelaide. The protected Historical Reserve is the site of the first European settlement in Alice Springs and includes a museum and restored original stone buildings. A diverse range of wildlife can be enjoyed and guided tours are available.

    You will return to your hotel in the late afternoon. After some time to refresh you will be met for a unique evening enjoying an Aussie Bush BBQ. On this fun night enjoy a genuine "Aussie Barbeque" in the 'great Australian bush'. See native plants and wildlife and hear about the Aboriginal culture. Learn to throw a boomerang - let's hope it comes back! Watch damper (bush bread) being prepared and cooked in the coals of the fire, taste "Billy Tea" and marvel at the expanse of the universe while enjoying dinner under the stars.

    Chifley Alice Springs Resort Situated on the banks of the Todd River with its magnificent river red gums, Alice Springs Resort combines efficient and friendly outback service with modern, comfortable facilities. The Resort is a five minute walk to the town's cultural, shopping and business centre with lush green lawns and rich red earth combining to contribute to a unique Australian experience.

    Overnight: Chifley Alice Springs Resort
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 4

    Alice Springs - Ayers Rock

    After a light breakfast this morning meet your representative in the resort lobby for your exhilarating morning on a quad rush experience.

    This morning tour includes a short transfer from all accommodation in Alice Springs to Undoolya Station, only 17km east of the town centre. This is the oldest working cattle station in NT, and Quad Bikes are the sole operators on the station. Enjoy two hours riding a quad bike around the station, with commentary across the station, over bush tracks, dry riverbeds and beside mountain ranges. Wildlife is viewed at close proximity and a cooked BBQ breakfast is included. Please note that you will be joined by other non guests on this excursion and the transfers are via coach.

    You will return to the resort mid-morning to collect your luggage, and then transfer to Alice Springs airport for your short flight to Ayers Rock. On arrival into Ayers Rock you will be met & transferred by private vehicle to the resort.

    This afternoon, you will enjoy a private tour of The Olgas (Kata Tjuta). This word means (many heads) and is a collection of 36 sandstone domes that in ancient times may have been a single formation bigger than Uluru itself. Walking through the valleys and gorges is a haunting experience in this spiritual place.

    This evening enjoy the 'Sounds of Silence' Dinner the desert. Travel out to a private dining setting in the desert. As the sun dips behind the many domes of Kata Tjuta (Olgas) and Uluru (Ayers Rock) sip champagne while listening to the haunting sounds of the didgeridoo. After a gourmet Barbeque dinner the lanterns will be dimmed. Hear the sounds of the great Outback while an astronomer takes you on a tour of the southern night skies. View the stars through an onsite telescope before returning to the hotel. The resort provides return coach transfers.

    Sails in the Desert provides comfort and luxury right in the heart of Central Australia. Relax in spacious, airy and exquisitely furnished rooms, enjoy a cocktail in the award winning park-like grounds, or sit back and relax under the magnificent white sails that gives the hotel its name.

    Overnight: Sails in the Desert
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 5

    Ayers Rock

    A pre-dawn start this morning for an Uluru sunrise experience. Meet your representative in the hotel lobby and depart on your private sightseeing tour to view Ayers Rock at sunrise. Walk around the base see hallowed sites and view paintings of ancient Aboriginal tribal myths. You will also visit the Cultural Centre to explore at your leisure, before a unique, indigenous experience. Join an Aboriginal artist at the Uluru Cultural Centre for a fun introduction to Aboriginal art. An Aboriginal guide will provide an introduction to their culture and how this is expressed in their artworks. You can then complete your own painting which you can take home as a memento of your experience.

    Your will return to your accommodation around midday, with the afternoon - the heat of the day - at your leisure.

    Overnight: Sails in the Desert
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    Ayers Rock - Port Douglas

    Today you will be transferred to the airport by private vehicle for your flight to Cairns. On arrival into Cairns you will be met by your guide & transferred 1.5 hours along the picturesque Pacific Highway to your accommodation in the seaside township of Port Douglas.

    Sea Temple Resort and Spa Port Douglas Located on the secluded beachfront of Four Mile Beach, Sea Temple Resort & Spa Port Douglas has been designed with the utmost luxury and sophisticated relaxation in mind. This resort blends seamlessly with the surrounding landscape echoing the natural features of this tropical oasis and centering around an expansive lagoon and island bar. The resort comprises of 194 rooms plus Day Spa, Restaurant, bars and Signature 18 Hole Links style Golf Course in one of the most internationally famous locations in Australia. Sea Temple Port Douglas offers tropical elegance in a stunningly beautiful location, sophisticated and stylish, perfectly positioned for your rejuvenation.

    Your villa boasts a private plunge pool with outdoor BBQ and dining area, full kitchen & laundry and a boardwalk pathway directly to Four Mile Beach.

    Overnight: Sea Temple Port Douglas
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    Port Douglas

    This morning meet the shuttle bus in the hotel lobby & transfer to Marina Mirage for your full day exploration of the Great Barrier Reef.

    'Quicksilver' is the pioneering vessel on the reef with its own private pontoon for easy access to the reef throughout the day. The vessel & platform can take up to 300 guests and has undercover tables and seating, changing rooms and freshwater showers, buffet servery, underwater marine observatory, semi-submersible vessels, snorkelling platforms, specially designed introductory dive areas and a carpeted sundeck. The vessel alongside also provides air-conditioned comfort, a licensed bar as well as toilets and changing room facilities.

    You will cruise to Agincourt Reef lying within the World Heritage Great Barrier Reef Marine Park. This reef is renowned for its water clarity and coral formations. The Agincourt Reef lagoon is ideal for swimming and snorkelling and is amongst the best in the world. The facilities of Quicksilver allow you to experience the wonderment of the Great Barrier Reef whichever way you choose, wet or dry.

    You will return to the mainland in the late afternoon where your shuttle coach awaits to return to the hotels.

    Overnight: Sea Temple Port Douglas
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 8

    Port Douglas

    In well appointed luxury vehicle, traverse the breathtaking Coral Sea coastline en route to majestic Cooya Beach. You will join Yaba Yabaju of the Kuku Yalanji people, the traditional owners of the land, for an interactive cultural experience. Meander through these primordial habitats with your indigenous guide as you are initiated into the ancient stories and history of the area. Gather food; throw spears and experience traditional hunting in the mudflats, mangroves and beach habitats.

    This morning's experience will have you back at your villa by lunchtime, perhaps to then enjoy a traditional Aussie BBQ lunch and a swim in your private pool or at the famous, pristine Four Mile Beach

    Overnight: Sea Temple Port Douglas
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    Port Douglas - Sydney

    After breakfast this morning you will check out of the Sea Temple & meet your guide on the hotel lobby for your morning touring, before heading to the airport for your flight to Sydney.

    Journey by private vehicle to Kuranda to experience the famous Scenic rail and the quaint Kuranda township & Butterfly Sanctuary. Take off from Freshwater Station by rail, up into the rainforest canopy. This winding ride travels past waterfalls and fertile farmlands. The views over the coastal plains to the Coral Sea are stunning. After arriving at Kuranda stroll through the unique village & visit the Butterfly Sanctuary. The Australian Butterfly Sanctuary is the largest butterfly flight aviary in Australia and home to over 1500 magnificent tropical butterflies. Situated in Kuranda Village this unique attraction allows visitors to interact with these beautiful and usually elusive creatures.

    From here travel onto Cairns airport for your flight to Sydney. On arrival into Sydney in the early evening you will be met by your guide and transferred by private vehicle to your hotel.

    Four Seasons Sydney is superbly situated right on Sydney Harbour in the city's historic Rocks area. The Four Seasons Sydney Hotel has wonderful views of the Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge. It is only a short walk from Sydney's major shopping and business districts.

    Overnight: Four Seasons
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 10

    Sydney

    After breakfast this morning meet your guide in the hotel lobby for your full day exploring Sydney together. Start with a short walk to Circular Quay where you will take the 'locals' ferry across the famous Taronga Zoo. Here enjoy an exclusive, behind the scenes tour of Taronga Zoo's unique Australian animal collection designed especially for the inquisitive traveller. You will be met on arrival by your own private Zookeeper who will guide you through the zoo - gaining your insight as well as some enclosure access. It is an informative introduction to Australia's unusual wildlife and includes excellent photographic opportunities with kangaroos, wallabies, birds, wombats and echidnas as well as over 200 hundred animals in the 'platypus pools' exhibit.

    From here you will be met by your driver & your guide will escort you to a local waterfront restaurant for lunch. Then your afternoon adventure begins!

    This afternoon you will be met at the famous Bondi Beach by a 'true blue' Aussie surf lifesaver and learn to be one! Arriving at Bondi, you will be fitted out with a wetsuit or UV rash vest appropriate to the season, sunscreen and a soft, lightweight Malibu board - perfect for beginners. Your belongings will be securely stored. Your Aussie surf lifesaver will fill you in on surf awareness and safety and teach to read the 'rips, tides & sets'. After warm-ups and stretches on the sand you'll learn correct paddling techniques and how to get up into the standing position. Once you are comfortable with these basics it's time to hit the water, just the little waves to start with! After some great laughs and a lot of fun in the sun it's back to the base for an outdoor shower and use of the changing rooms. Afterwards your guide will escort your back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Four Seasons
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 11

    Sydney - Home

    After breakfast this morning you will be transferred to the International airport for your return journey.

    Meal Plan: B

The best of Australia - A gold program

  • Day 3

    Melbourne - Adelaide - Kangaroo Island

    This morning meet your driver in the hotel lobby for your private vehicle transfer to the domestic airport for your flight to Adelaide. On arrival into Adelaide you will be met by your guide who will escort you to the Regional Air Express check in counter for your flight to Kangaroo Island. Your guide will also assist with any luggage storage you may choose to do at this time.

    IMPORTANT NOTE: for the journey to Kangaroo Island there is a luggage limit of one suitcase of checked luggage, (maximum of 15 kilos), & 7kg hand luggage per person.

    International guests: Excess baggage can be stored, if you are travelling with Rex in both directions, in a secured facility for a fee of $5.50 per bag at Adelaide Airport, (arrange at Rex Check In) - Unlimited hire until return. Luggage can also be left with concierge at your Adelaide hotel.

    On arrival onto Kangaroo Island - a flight of 35 minutes - you will be met by your Lodge representative & transferred to your home for the next 3 nights.

    Southern Ocean Lodge Southern Ocean Lodge is Kangaroo Island's only world-class wilderness lodge. Located at Hanson Bay and commanding spectacular ocean, beach and wilderness views, the 21-suite escape offers the ultimate fusion of nature and luxury. Featuring an environmentally sensitive design, eco chic interiors, premium lodge facilities, personal service, contemporary dining and a signature spa retreat, guests can discover the magnificent coastal scenery and abundant wildlife for which the Island is renowned in impeccable style. With exceptional guided experiences and adventures included in the rate, all guests receive a personalised experiential touring itinerary of the Island's unique natural attractions - the complete Kangaroo Island experience! All touring, gourmet meals, beverages & daily replenished mini bar is included in your stay - where you are invited to make yourself at home in the Lodge.

    Overnight: Southern Ocean Lodge
    Meal Plan: B,L,D

    Kangaroo Island - Alice Springs - Ayers Rock

    After breakfast this morning you will be transferred to Kingscote airport for your flight via Adelaide & Alice Springs to Ayers Rock. On arrival into Ayers Rock you will be met by your representative & transferred to your accommodation.

    The giant red monolith has come to represent Australia itself. The massive sandstone rock - the summit of a buried mountain - generates an almost mystical power. 'The Rock' is 8.8 kilometres around the base, 3.6 kilometres long, 2.4 kilometres wide and towers 348 metres above the surrounding plain. The Anangu people believe they are the direct descendents of the ancestral spirits who gave them the laws and rules that they live by. They believe they are responsible for the protection of the lands and ask visitors to respect their wishes not to climb the rock. Its significant power can be felt particularly in the early morning or at sunset as the Rock undergoes an extraordinary transformation of colour.

    This afternoon, you will enjoy a private tour of The Olgas (Kata Tjuta). This word means (many heads) and is a collection of 36 sandstone domes that in ancient times may have been a single formation bigger than Uluru itself. Walking through the valleys and gorges is a haunting experience in this spiritual place.

    This evening enjoy the 'Sounds of Silence' Dinner. Travel out to a private dining setting in the desert. As the sun dips behind the many domes of Kata Tjuta (Olgas) and Uluru (Ayers Rock) sip champagne while listening to the haunting sounds of the didgeridoo. After a gourmet Barbeque dinner the lanterns will be dimmed. Hear the sounds of the great Outback while an astronomer takes you on a tour of the southern night skies. View the stars through an onsite telescope before returning to the hotel. The resort provides return coach transfers.

    Sails In The Desert provides comfort and luxury right in the heart of Central Australia. Relax in spacious, airy and exquisitely furnished rooms, enjoy a cocktail in the award winning park-like grounds, or sit back and relax under the magnificent white sails that gives the hotel its name.

  • Day 14

    Sydney

    After breakfast this morning meet your guide in the hotel lobby for your morning sightseeing tour of the harbour city. Today's tour begins at Sydney's historic Rocks area known for its narrow cobbled streets and convict buildings. Continue past the historic Mint and Hyde Park Barracks and stop at Mrs Macquarie's Chair for wonderful views of the Harbour, Opera House and Harbour Bridge. From here journey on by private vehicle to the Sydney Opera House for a private, behind-the-scene's tour of this Australian icon.

    50 years ago a young Danish architect named Jorn Utzon dreaming of Bennelong Point, designed a masterpiece which pushed the boundaries of creativity and science - and put Sydney on the map forever. Step inside the world's most recognizable sails for a one hour tour and experience the miracle of architecture and engineering. A work of art that came into existence against impossible odds - to become one of the busiest performing arts centres in the world! Working with a new enriching interactive and audio visual component, where images are projected onto the fabric of the building - our guides will take you on an emotional journey, engrossing you in a story to rival any opera plot with its dramatic twists and turns.

    You'll visit one of the major venues - where there are live performances every day. Some 1500 performances, in every genre you can imagine, are staged each year to audiences of around one million.

    From here your guide will escort you along Circular Quay to Campbell's Cove pontoon where your vessel awaits. This afternoon you will be orientated with Sydney from the most enviable vantage point - on the water! Cruising on Sydney Harbour is a quintessential Australian experience, and today you will experience 'Calypso' - a 52 foot motor cruiser with a minimum of two and a maximum of ten guests onboard.

    Whilst cruising this iconic harbour you will have the opportunity to swim, fish or kayak. However most choose to sit back with a glass of Australian beer or wine & take in the amazing sights - the Harbour Bridge, the Opera House, Luna Park, Fort Denison and the waterfront homes of the rich & famous. A barbeque lunch is served on board, while anchored in a secluded cove. And as the afternoon sun comes in we suggest making your way to the bow and settling in the sunbeds for the perfect views of the harbour city as you come back in to moor. Your 4 hour experience on the water ends at Campbell's Cove pontoon, just a short walk from your hotel.
