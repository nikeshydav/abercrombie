Travel to UK & Ireland

Great Britain travellers prefer starting from the capital of United Kingdom-London which is also a global financial centre. An iconic landmark city, London has a wide array of stores, bars, restaurants and hotels to welcome its guests. The countryside of Southern England has a thorough gentle approach laden with hills, fenlands and country lanes that mingle with the historic cities of Oxford, Cambridge and Bath. The southern coast to Cornwall has picturesque landscape with little figurines of towns, beaches and resorts.

Travel further into UK to the Northern England Wales to understand their historic mood. National parks and amazing coastlines are just some attractions. Lake District is the largest park which plays a game of lagged mountains interspersed with lakes and villages. Wales is called the 'land of castles' with one of the most varied scenic-view of Europe visible.

Holidays in UK are not complete without a look at the mountainous country of Scotland. Bagpipes and kits in the criss-cross of mountains and Central Lowlands give rise to Edinburgh, Scotland's capital city. It is one of the most dramatically-located capital cities that house an international Arts Festival every summer. Towards the north, the Highlands and Islands form Europe's last great wildernesses. The Scottish clans pride themselves on having magnificent peaks, windswept moorlands and lonely sea inlets.

Ireland is also called the 'Emerald Isle'. Vacation tours to Ireland are a hit because of the mild damp climate with high incidence of rainfalls that gives it the green cover, attracting many travellers. Stroll in the pastoral countryside and visit the castles and villages on the way. Enjoy natural beauty in Connemara or Kerry. Indulge in dairy products and ride on horse backs while being in a thriving dairy industry. As Dublin is the capital of Ireland, you must see its Georgian architecture and its happening nightlife scene.
