Travel To Bhutan


Bhutan, with its lofty frontiers shrouded up in mystery, did not have a name for many centuries. The 18th century Tibetan chronicles refer to it as 'Hidden Holy Land,' 'The Southern Valley of Medicinal Herbs,' and 'The Lotus Garden of the Gods.' However, local Bhutanese people still prefer calling it the 'Land of the Thunder Dragon.'

Bhutan holidays and trekking experiences provide a taste of a unique way of life that is protected from the influences of the 21st century. While Bhutan, undoubtedly, is one of the smallest countries in the world, yet its rich culture and diversity is profound. Its early history talks of Buddhist folklore and mythology featuring remarkable and magical beings possessed with supernatural powers. With many years of habitation, this country offers a treasure trove of archaeological riches, including many ancient and ornate temples and fortresses.

Como and Aman hotels have opened up Bhutan's holidays to the luxury traveler by building new properties in the entire town of Paro, which lies in the center of the rich, fertile Paro valley. Wide and verdant, spend time here if you love nature and the stillness and silence it brings with itself. Or, visit the Black and White Temples in the Haa Valley only recently opened to tourists.

The capital city of Thimpu lies on the banks of the Thimpu Chhu River and is dramatically landscaped with steep and abrupt hillsides, scattered with houses, mostly abandoned, the walls slowly crumbling and falling to pieces. The only world capital without traffic lights, Thimpu, despite recent developments, retains its charming, medieval feel thanks to its brightly painted and elaborately decorated facades, ensuring that holidays to this beautiful country etch an everlasting impression on the minds of the travelers.

Bhutan offers some of the best trekking opportunities in the world. There are many day treks through the Paro Valley. Visit the Black and White Temples in the Haa Valley recently opened to tourists. Whilst the trekking is at altitude, the visitor will be rewarded with beautiful remote temples and incredible views of the Himalayas.

A country of surprises, Bhutan is not just another place where monks in claret clothing roam about. Visit the 'Last Shangri-La,' and experience an alternative way to live life.
