Suggested Madikwe & Waterberg accommodation

  • Ant's Hill

    Built on the edge of a gorge, this fabulous bush home offers breathtaking views across the Waterberg. Set amongst boulders on the edge of a cliff, the infinity swimming pool cascades over the lip of the gorge. Accommodating a maximum of 12 guests, the atmosphere is friendly and intimate.

    Spacious rooms benefit from huge folding doors and the thatched lodge roof blends into the bush. Adorned with exotic furniture and brightly coloured African fabrics, Ant's Hill is both sophisticated and comfortable. The draped four-poster beds are seven foot wide and each bathroom is spectacular, offering sunken baths and waterfall showers. Great pride is taken in their sumptuous home cooking, dining outside whenever possible under shady trees.

    Ant's Nest

    Situated in a natural amphitheatre, the original homestead with its wide verandas has a classic African atmosphere. The luxurious en suite rooms accommodate a maximum of 12 guests. Nestled in landscaped gardens, the expansive swimming pool blends naturally into the surroundings.

    Ant's Nest is perfect for small groups, honeymoon couples or families with children of all ages. Sumptuous cuisine, served in a variety of secluded and alternative places, bush breakfasts, lunches under the shade of a sheltering tree and dinner under the stars are all part of the daily routine. Enjoy fabulous horse riding safaris, guided bush walks or game drives.

    Etali Safari Lodge

    Set deep in the heart of the Madikwe Game Reserve, Etali is most definitely a grown-ups paradise in the bush. Intimate and exclusive, it is a place of relaxation amongst nature where body and soul are rejuvenated by people who understand luxury.

    Nestled in sweet-thorn acacia trees, eight thatched suites are privately positioned, each with whirlpools set on open decks and with indoor and outdoor showers. Complementing the bush experience, a lavish Wellness Centre offers specialist treatments with qualified therapists. Again set under thatch, there is no better way to relax than to enjoy a 'Warthog Hour' - mud bath, or vitality body wrap, with only the sounds of elephants at the waterhole or weaverbirds tapping on the window to disturb you. If you are feeling more energetic then there is a full gym, swimming pool and then the aroma filled steam room to cleanse the pores. Aside from relaxation and rejuvenation in the wellness centre, Etali can offer thrilling experiences in the bush.

    Sunrise and sunset game drives are led by expert guides. Watch out for leopard, endangered black rhino, elusive wild dog and some of the area's 300 recorded species of bird life. Get closer to nature with ranger-led game walks, learn interesting facts such as why the top of 'Castles of Clay', termite mounds always face northwest, while the temperature inside the mound remains a constant 37 degrees fahrenheit. Creative cuisine fuses the influences of Africa and Asia and telescopes are on hand to make the most of the African night sky.

    Jaci's Safari Lodge & Tree Lodge

    Two exceptional child-friendly, privately owned lodges are set deep in the Madikwe Game Reserve. Tree lodge, consisting of eight spacious tree houses are set six metres above the ground and linked by raised wooden walkways. Safari Lodge benefits from seven suites, the safari cottage and exclusive two bedroom Nare Suite.

    Children of all ages are welcome at Jaci's properties. Under seven year olds may go on a specially developed 'Jungle Drive' - lasting up to one hour and can learn and interact with nature in a fun and safe environment. Fire cooked, starlit meals ensure Jaci's is perfect for romantic safaris and families alike.

    Makanyane Safari Lodge

    Designed by a team of award winning architects, the key focus was for the lodge to blend in with nature - this has been achieved with great success. Makanyane lies hidden in the lush river-side forest of the Madikwe Game Reserve. Situated on 1,800 hectares of private ground within Madikwe's 75,000 hectares, the lodge affords spectacular views over the tranquil Marico River.

    The main lodge building, set on the edge of a ravine with views down to the waterhole has a wonderful thatched roof supported by ancient leadwood trees. A great place to relax, the lounge has two enormous rock fireplaces and opens out onto a huge wooden deck, again with spectacular views of the bush and a well stocked bar. Eight suites are linked to the main lodge via shaded winding pathways. Glass walled suites allow uninterrupted views of the river and surrounding bush, as do the sizeable private sundecks. Lavish bathrooms benefit from free standing baths, heated floors and indoor and outdoor showers.

    Guided game drives and walks through some of the reserves 75,000 hectares are sure to unveil a vast array of incredible wildlife. Alternatively sit and observe from one of Makanyane's two bush hides. For the more adventurous experience the star bed, set in the bush on a secure elevated deck. Tucked away in the forest, a spa and gym are always on hand to offer tired travellers a little pampering, or to work up an appetite for a sumptuous dinner. Priding itself on fine cuisine, executive chef, Mauritz Greeff, and his excellent team blend European and African flavours, using only the freshest of produce.

    Makweti Safari Lodge

    Located in the heart of the beautiful Welgevonden Private Reserve in South Africa, rolling mountains, steep gorges, rocky outcrops, flowing rivers and open plains provide a magnificent game area that is fast growing in popularity.

    The main lodge, built into natural rock formations in the craggy Waterberg Mountains, is a dramatic architectural synthesis which commands spectacular views of the Makweti Gorge and undulating landscape beyond. Makweti Safari Lodge accommodates just ten guests. Indulge your senses in the secluded suites, individually decorated with antique treasures, Victorian bathtubs and log fires for the cooler winter months. Explore 33,000 hectares of prime game-land, in open safari vehicle or on foot.

    Marataba

    This well-equipped game lodge is the ultimate glamorous retreat. With just twelve exclusive tented suites it is a perfect place to escape from the world and enjoy the panoramic view from your own private sun deck.

    The stunning design of the lodge blends perfectly into its natural setting with the clever use of wood, steel and other natural products. Each of the suites have en suite bathrooms with large stand alone baths, indoor and outdoor showers. Gourmet cuisine with fresh African flavours is served either in the elegantly appointed dining room or outdoors on the dining veranda or, alternatively, under a glorious African sky in the boma.

    Morukuru Lodge

    A beautifully appointed bush villa, set in the wilds of the Madikwe Game Reserve on the banks of the Marico River, Morukuru blends chic stylish décor with warm African hospitality. Three double suites and a children's bedroom that can accommodate four make this a perfect family retreat.

    An experienced and dedicated team are on hand to look after your every need. Your personal chef will cater for any specific dietary requirement whilst the guides tailor the day's safari activities to suit you. The pace of life can be as serene, or hectic as you like as the whole property is yours to enjoy.

    Royal Madikwe Luxury Safari Residence

    At the heart of the reserve, Royal Madikwe is a luxury lodge catering to discerning families and groups of friends. Fabulous facilities and superb game viewing are only one half of the story though, for Royal Madikwe donates twenty percent of all commercial profit into local community projects.

    An intimate retreat the property can accommodate up to ten guests on an exclusive use basis only. Accommodation is in six rooms: four luxury doubles with four-poster beds, one luxury twin and a spacious family suite consisting of a twin and double room. All of the rooms have fabulous en-suite bathrooms, air-conditioning, indoor and outdoor showers, elevated sun decks and private plunge pools.

    Tuningi Safari Lodge

    Tuningi Safari Lodge is one of the few five star lodges that welcome children. Accommodating just 16 guests, the exclusive bush villas have been designed to blend the interiors with the bush. An impressive infinity pool overlooks a busy watering hole.

    As night sets in, lanterns and candles are lit and the mood goes from bush to baroque. As you return from your game drive you will experience a bush extravaganza dinner, prepared by Tuningi's Executive Chef, in the boma with a blazing log fire to keep you warm. For added privacy and exclusivity, Little Tuningi - an exclusive use, self contained lodge offers a maximum six guests unsurpassed luxury.
