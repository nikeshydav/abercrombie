Suggested The Coast Accommodation

  • Alfajiri Villas

    One of the finest private retreats on the East African coast, Alfajiri overlooks the Indian Ocean in all its glory, ensuring both privacy and inspirational views.

    Three butler-attended villas provide a host of accommodation options, from bright and airy Caribbean style bedrooms to a romantic turret suite. Large verandas with comfortable hand-carved sofas dominate the front of the properties, where an infinity pool sits on the cliff edge, merging into the sea and horizon. The Mediterranean influenced cuisine is as impeccable as the service. For those with small children and a desire to relax, two English-speaking ayahs (nannies) are on hand around the clock.

    Funzi Keys

    Tucked away on an unspoilt island in the Indian Ocean, yet just 15 minutes by air from Mombasa, this is a scene of beautiful seclusion and natural landscape.

    Surrounded by water with glorious views of the African sunset, nine of the 17 individually designed, roomy cottages are positioned along the high-water line and blend unobtrusively with the idyllic beachfront setting. Life can be utterly relaxing or more energetic - it's your choice. Watch the sun go down in total privacy from the Jacuzzi with its spectacular sea view. Or perhaps take to the water for some sailing, fishing, scuba-diving or an unforgettable glimpse of kingfishers and dolphins.

    Read more

    Hemingways Resort

    A luxurious tropical retreat lying on one of the world's finest beaches, Hemingways is a 74-room international resort with an ambience of relaxed sophistication and attentive service.

    All stylishly furnished, air-conditioned en suite rooms are extremely comfortable with gorgeous ocean views, are within yards of the beach and benefit from 24-hour room service. The cuisine is based on local, freshly grown and caught ingredients, with African, Arabian and Eastern influences. Opt for a safari adventure or visit a national reserve, play some golf, deep sea fish or take a sundowner cruise on an Arab dhow. Or just laze pool-side. Every day will be one to remember.

    Kiwayu Safari Village

    Set in the remote Dodori National Reserve, 35 miles north of Lamu, Kiwayu is beautifully located for maximum comfort and relaxation. Here you'll discover an unspoilt stretch of wild, uninhabited coastline - ideal for watersports, snorkelling off coral reefs, deep sea and creek fishing

    excursions by dhow to the island and even dolphin spotting. On land, the 18 spacious bandas (cottages) are designed to blend into the surrounding dunes, built with great imagination in the Swahili tradition with palm thatch and woven flooring. From your private veranda, soak up panoramic views over the bay. All you'll hear is the lapping of the sea, the wind in the palms and birdsong.

    Manda Bay

    Manda Bay is a small private lodge on a secluded peninsular of the island, surrounded by magnificent coral gardens, empty beaches and indigenous woodland.

    Guests are assured of total privacy and serenity, with just 16 spacious en suite cottages lining the shoreline complete with shaded verandas overlooking the calm waters of the bay. The cuisine embraces all the rich flavours not only of the sea but also Swahili culture and European tastes. Along with the rich marine life, you'll encounter pristine costal bush where game drives reveal a surprising number of animals and birds. Opportunities abound for pleasure on the water and interacting with the local culture.

    Mombasa Serena Beach Hotel

    Styled to recall a 13th century Swahili town, complete with winding lanes, carved balconies, fountained courts and bustling market squares, this hotel provides an oasis of tranquillity while satisfying sun-worshipping guests looking for watersports, world-class cuisine and vibrant nightlife.

    The 166 luxurious air-conditioned bedrooms and suites ensure the highest standards of modern hospitality. Many overlook the ribbon of palm-fringed powder-white sand and clear blue waters, where all manner of activities take place. Three restaurants offer you excellent fare. You can feast under the stars, experience traditional Swahili evenings, catch live bands, dance past the midnight hour - the sense of escapism lasts your entire visit.

    Peponi Hotel, Lamu

    A perfect combination of beach hotel and coastal hideaway, Peponi Hotel is small and intimate and is still run by the original family who opened the hotel in 1967. Situated at the quiet end of Lamu Island, near the village of Shela just 24 rooms are divided into standard and superior room categories.

    The rooms are scattered throughout the grounds and all benefit from either ocean or garden views. Each of the rooms are individually decorated although all benefit from ceiling fans, mosquito nets, showers and fresh flowers. Watersports including windsurfing, diving and speedboats are available. There has also been an active Turtle Conservation Project operating at Peponi since 1992.

    The Majlis

    A privately owned resort, The Majlis is set on the idyllic Manda Island in the Lamu archipelago on Kenya's Northern Coast. With 25 room this luxury boutique hotel is filled with the ambience, comfort and intimacy of a private home.

    With a superb beachfront location and breathtaking views over the Ras Kitau bay and Shela village, it presents the perfect setting and ideal positing from which to explore the region.

    The Majlis blends seamlessly with its island surroundings, making use of the areas natural resources and traditions including hand-carved timber and white coral blocks. Each of the hotel's air conditioned rooms in unique in character and style, and features traditionally high beamed ceilings and terraces with spectacular views of the beach, landscaped gardens or mangrove forests. Tastefully designed and decorated to reflect the heritage of East Africa, The Majlis houses an exquisite private collection of art mixing local traditional influences with a contemporary twist.

    The Red Pepper House

    THE HOTEL
    If relaxation and indulgence are what you are looking for then The Red Pepper House, located on Lamu Island is somewhere to escape to. Lamu Island is a world heritage site situated within an archipelago off the coast of Kenya and a stone's throw from the historical town of Lamu.

    Lamu has managed to stay unspoilt and untouched by tourism and has retained all the charm and character built up over the centuries.

    &

    The Red Pepper House is an exquisite boutique beach hotel built with traditional Swahili architecture, giving you the feeling that you are in an old Swahili village but surrounded by pure luxury and provides a combination of real African culture and laid-back beach life. The hotel lies between an acacia forest and red pepper trees. Each of the five Nyumbas (the Swahili name for home), are spacious with en suite bathroom, and living room leading to a private beach. Guests have their own private butler who is on hand to bring refreshing cocktails and delicious food to the Nyumbas when required.

    NEED TO KNOW

    • Visit Lamu town to absorb some of the Swahili culture, craft markets and ruins of the city of Takwa
    • Take a stroll through the 'Forest of The Red Pepper Tree' where you may encounter a paradise flycatcher and other wildlife and fauna
    • Take a speedboat or dhow trip to the reefs of Kinyika or Manda Toto to snorkel and view turtles, dolphins and the infinite variety of fish
    • If you are looking for an exciting adventure then spend the morning deep sea fishing
    • Take a trip on a dhow around Lamu Island and enjoy a glass of champagne under the stars
