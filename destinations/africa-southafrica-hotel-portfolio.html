Suggested Cape Town accommodation

  • Cape Cadogan Boutique Hotel

    The Georgian and Victorian buildings of the Cape Cadogan hotel date back to the beginning of the 19th century and it is perfectly located in the centre of Cape Town.

    Twelve luxuriously appointed en suite bedrooms are decorated with an eclectic mix of contemporary and antique furniture using dramatic fabrics to maximum effect. The hotel benefits from six standard rooms and six suites, a library, lounge with open fireplace, plunge pool and beautiful dining room overlooking the gardens. The hotel has as its presidential suite the Owners Villa, serviced by the main property it offers an open plan lounge, dining area, kitchen and bedroom with open plan bathroom.

    Cape Grace Hotel

    Nestled on a private quay between the working harbour of Cape Town's bustling Victoria & Alfred Waterfront and the serenity of an international yacht marina, the recently refashioned Cape Grace is a welcome and gracious sanctuary of traditional sophistication whilst emanating progressive style and comfort.

    The luxurious space within the 121 well-appointed rooms and suites, diversity of amenities and the unsurpassed standards in service that is both proficient and personal ensures Cape Grace is a favourite for many returning guests. A new addition to the hotel is "Spirit of the Cape" a privately owned luxury yacht available for the exclusive use of Cape Grace guests. She is a 56ft luxury motor yacht berthed in the yacht marina in front of the Bascule Whisky Wine & Cocktail Bar. The hotel's restaurant onewaterfront is an exploratory fusion of culinary indulgence strongly influenced by the Cape's West Coast and the rustic vibrancy of local Malay cuisine. Revitalisation of body and mind can be achieved at the African-inspired spa located on the top floor. Other facilities include: library, heated outdoor pool and in-room workout facilities. Children are always welcome at Cape Grace.

    Ellerman House

    This historic landmark hotel is situated in Bantry Bay. It is within walking distance of Clifton Beach, ten minutes by car from the City Bowl and Victoria & Alfred Waterfront.

    Once the stately residence of a shipping magnate, the building has been meticulously restored to provide nine elegant rooms and two superior suites, including the famous Ellerman Suite. Together with the gracious living room, fine art collection, sweeping verandas and patios, a carefully-landscaped garden and large pool, allows guests to relax in the atmosphere of a country house with ocean frontage.

    Ellerman Villa

    A striking, modern property concealed in the gardens adjacent to the main house. A true juxtaposition against the classic feel of the original property, the Villa is a five bedroom, exclusive use property for those seeking...

    the ultimate in clean-cut, 21st century design. The cool, contemporary furniture, minimalist layout, angular infinity pool and decadent Swarovski chandelier are all the height of cutting-edge design. The expansive living area is open plan and flows outdoors. The massive glass windows peel backcompletely, opening up the lounge, dining room and study to the magnificent views of the ocean. Equipped with its own kitchen, butler and spa treatment rooms, the Villa is the epitome of extravagant Cape properties.

    Kensington Place

    Kensington Place is a small exclusive hotel set on the foothills of Table Mountain in Higgovale, one of Cape Town's most prestigious residential areas. It offers breathtaking views of Table Bay, the harbour, city skyline and mountains.

    Each of the eight guest rooms interiors have an unmistakable contemporary feel with eclectic African touches throughout. Kensington Place is the perfect setting for those who wish to experience the city as the locals do, from an elegant area that is just a ten-minute drive from the city centre and beaches, with historical sites and vineyards within easy reach.

    The rooms all have spacious sitting areas, air conditioning, I-Pod docking stations, laptop with complimentary wireless internet access, Skype phone, ceiling fans and balconies. DVD players and satellite TV provide in-room entertainment. Breakfast is served all day, either on your private terrace or in the lounge. 24-hour room service is available to suit any requirement. There are private gardens, a terrace and swimming pool to fill the lazy days of summer. Membership to a nearby Virgin Active gym is also included.

    Molori Clifton

    Molori Clifton evokes the feeling of a luxurious home with each suite designed in its owned style, characterised by distinct furnishing ad state of the art amenities and unique items sourced from all over the world.

    The main villa The Clifton is spread out over three levels, with 6 ensuite suites one being a Presidential Suite, the lower level of The Clifton features a modern spa offering a variety of spa services and a steam room that accommodates 12 guests, a state of the art gym and a cutting edge media room.

    Mount Nelson

    The Mount Nelson Hotel is an oasis of luxury, privacy and tranquillity. This opulent Orient Express property is located in the heart of the historical city centre and just ten minutes drive from the Victoria & Alfred Waterfront.

    Accommodation
    Each of the 57 suites and 144 spacious bedrooms are individually furnished. For increased seclusion, four cottages in Sydenham Terrace have been meticulously restored and are set in private gardens with a swimming pool.

    No stay is at the Mount Nelson is complete without indulging in the famous afternoon tea, best enjoyed in the elegant lounge or on the terrace. The two restaurants offer informal, alfresco health-focused lunches and elegant dinners, while the stylish and lively Planet Bar offers an impressive selection of wine, champagne and cocktails. For those wishing to explore, complimentary transfers are available within a 10km radius of the hotel. Last year's addition of the Labrisa Spa, offering eight treatment rooms of which four are double are perfect for romantic relaxation treatments. The floodlit tennis courts and two heated swimming pools are set against the mighty backdrop of Table Mountain.

    O On Kloof Boutique Hotel

    O on Kloof is a chic boutique hotel, nestled in Bantry Bay. Its convenient location ensures good access to beaches, galleries, restaurants and the bustling Victoria & Alfred Waterfront.

    Recently refashioned, the property is elegant, light and airy with plenty of space to relax in the library or simply sit on the terrace enjoying an evening sundowner. Eight suites have been tastefully and individually decorated. Each is equipped with air-conditioning, DVD players, bar fridge and satellite television. Suites generally have either ocean views or face towards Lion's Head. Suites one and five are ideal for honeymooners as they come complete with a Jacuzzi and private deck.

    O on Kloof offers warm hospitality, superior service and all the modern conveniences including WiFi internet access. For the energetic there is a large heated indoor swimming pool located next to the gym. Enjoy a drink at the Juice Bar or perhaps relax on the sun deck. The friendly management at O on Kloof are always on hand to offer advice on the latest eateries and trendy hangouts. The private terrace of suite number five benefits from magical orange sunsets over the Atlantic Ocean. O is the perfect base to explore Cape Town.

    One & Only Cape Town

    One&Only Cape Town is an oasis of elegance amidst the vibrant playground of one of the world's most cosmopolitan cities. With stunning views of Table Mountain and Signal Hill, the chic island design and contemporary African flair of this urban resort finds a natural home in the heart of Cape Town's fashionable Victoria & Alfred Waterfront.

    The resort's seven-storey Marina Tower features 91 spacious rooms and suites - its crescent-shaped architecture created so all guestrooms and suites enjoy the majesty of Table Mountain. Two lushly landscaped islands at the centre of the waterfront features 40 Island Suites and a tranquil spa. Central to exciting nightlife and entertainment, One&Only Cape Town also serves as a gateway to the uniquely rare adventures of South Africa such as wildlife safaris, wine country excursions, world-class golf and endless water play along pristine beaches.

    Clean lines, contemporary art and dark African woods accentuate the 131 spacious, air-conditioned rooms and suites. Oversized baths are complete with roomy rain shower, private water closet and dual marble vanity. Guests enjoy 24-hour butler service.

    Redcliffe House

    The Taj Cape Town

    The Taj Cape Town is set in the heart of historic Cape Town. The hotel itself was originally the home of the South African Reserve Bank and Temple Chambers and later the Board of Executors. An extensive refurbishment has brought in modern day luxuries yet still keeping the charm and heritage of the building.

    The Mint restaurant is an intimate three-level restaurant with an amazing open-plan show kitchen. The floor to ceiling glass fronted wine cellar adds to the magnificent ambiance of the restaurant. Curries at the Bombay Brassiere are not to be missed or sip a glass of champagne while eating seafood tapas at Twankey Seafood, Champagne and Oyster Bar.

    The Twelve Apostles Hotel and Spa

    The Twelve Apostles Hotel and Spa stands at the edge of the Twelve Apostles Mountain range with breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean.

    This 5-star boutique hotel has only 70 rooms made up of deluxe guest suites or suites - the only hard choice to make is weather you look out onto the ocean or over the majestic mountain range. The Azure Restaurant is famed for its cuisine, service and hospitality. Well worth a try is the famous fynbos menu. At sunset a visit to the Leopard Bar is a must for a sundowner or should you wish something lighter an afternoon tea by the sea. The hotel has two heated swimming pools, one overlooking the Atlantic Ocean and the other built into the rocks overlooking the mountain range.

    Tintswalo Atlantic

    Situated within the unspoilt wilderness of Cape Town's mountain reserve, just off the world famous Chapmans Peak Drive. Tintswalo Atlantic is a stunning new boutique hotel perched on seaside boulders and surrounded by ethereal milkwood trees.

    This chic boutique destination comprises 10 Luxury Suites and a Presidential Suite, with breathtaking views of both the Bay and renowned Sentinel landmark. Predominantly a wood and glass structure, the hotel's understated design blends into the surrounding fynbos environment. The suites are authentically decorated to emulate the experiences and colours of the various well-known islands of the world. The air-conditioned suites are quite literally, situated on the edge of the Atlantic Ocean. Each room has its own unique few of the ocean and the majestic Sentinel across the bay. The suites promise supreme comfort and privacy with spacious en-suite bathrooms, all with his and her basins. Meaning 'the intangible feeling of love, gratitude and peace bestowed upon someone offering you a meaningful and worthy gift' in the native Shangaan language, it does truly live up to its name.

    Victoria & Alfred Hotel

    The luxurious Victoria & Alfred Hotel was converted from a North Quay warehouse in 1990 although the original building dates back to 1904. It occupies a premium location on the Pierhead in the Victoria & Alfred Waterfront with ocean and Table Mountain views.

    In total 94 luxury en suite bedrooms are equipped with air-conditioning, flat-screen satellite television, wireless internet access and numerous other amenities. Blessed with striking views, 26 superior loft bedrooms are situated on the third floor and make the perfect base of a discerning A&K traveller. Views are either to the majestic Table Mountain, the piazza or the working harbour of the Waterfront. Service at the Victoria & Alfred is friendly and efficient, but never intrusive. The minimalist OYO Restaurant and Cocktail bar offers fine dining and is complemented with a range of wines from the walk-in cellar. Transfers can be arranged to a nearby health club, although most people are happy to burn off their calories by wandering through the Waterfront watching the local buskers and street entertainers.
