Travel To Antigua


A beach for every day of the year. This is what they say they have in Antigua! Fascinating all for whom the word 'vacation' signifies lengthy, gorgeous stretches of sand, Antigua provides the ultimate escape with its rich colonial heritage, classy resorts, leisure activities, intriguing culture and gourmet delights. You would need more than a two or three week luxury Antigua holiday to try out all the gorgeous 365 beaches. Once you set out to explore this wonderful island, you find that it is much more than just sun, azure-blue waters and 365 gorgeous pink and white beaches.

Warm trade winds and an endless wall of coral reefs make Antigua an unparalleled holiday destination for sun lovers and water-sports enthusiasts. Located at the heart of the Leeward Islands in the Eastern Caribbean, Antigua is not just about beaches though getting less than 45 inches of rainfall each year, it is the sunniest of all islands in the Caribbean. The capital city of St John's is lusciously peppered with fishing markets with candy-colored buildings vying for attention. Nelson's dockyard at English Harbor is a delightful mix with its assortment of colorful shops and restaurants. It was a major Royal Navy station in the Napoleonic Wars and adds an intriguingly familiar appeal to an island of simple but undeniable charm.

A haven for water lovers, you can try every water sport imaginable in this beautiful island. The coasts here do full justice to those looking for an absolute beach experience complete with sunbathing, surging, frothy waves and a beautiful landscape to marvel at. The Stanford Antigua Sailing Week at the end of April sees sailing enthusiasts coming over from every corner of the world. At the end of July and early August, the whole island turns into a kaleidoscope of color and festivities while celebrating a carnival spread across eleven days of festivities.

Watching the sun set and the sky turning a blazing orange at Shirley Heights is an ideal way to end the day in Antigua. Not only providing an incredible lookout into the sea, Shirley Heights, on Sundays, become the place for locals and visitors to congregate and dance to local steel bands, while sampling local cuisine and finally washing it down with some Cavalier, the local rum used in drinks across the island.

Life is truly a beach at this wonderful, lively and abuzz-with-life island. Make your holidays in Antigua a memorable one with Abercrombie and Kent as we map out the perfect Caribbean holiday whether you're looking for adventure, romance, relaxation or family fun.
