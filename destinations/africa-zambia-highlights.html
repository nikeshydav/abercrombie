Zambia Highlights & Sightseeing

  • South Luangwa National Park

    Luangwa Valley's most popular and accessible national park, the South Luangwa National Park pioneered the now famous 'Walking safaris' which still is one of the best ways to experience the pristine wilderness first hand.

    Profusely teeming with over 1000 species of mammals, 420 species of birds, reptiles, amphibians and insects, a safari in South Luangwa National Park emulates traditional adventures in one of Africa's finest wildlife sanctuaries.

    The Lower Zambezi National Park

    Raw, untainted and relatively new to tourism, the Lower Zambezi National Park covers 4,092 square kilometers and stretches along the banks of the Zambezi River, lying opposite to Mana Pools National Park. Overlooked for many years, it today, is the most treasured national park in Zambia with a beautiful flood plain alongside, speckled vibrant flora and flanked by a steep escarpment on the northern side covered with thick woodland. The river edge is dotted with a thick riverine fringe and mopane forest while the hills in the milieu are covered in broadleaf woodland.

    The best wildlife watching happens on the flood plain and along the river itself, so a boat ride is a major feature throwing in oodles of fun. Spotting herds of as many as 100 elephants swim across the river could be an added treat and a highlight of your trip!

    Livingstone - Victoria Falls

    The present capital of the Southern Province of Zambia, Livingstone or Maramba is a historic colonial city and a tourism centre for the ionic Victoria Falls. When an awestruck David Livingstone first saw Victoria Falls in 1855, he wrote, "On sights as beautiful as this, angels in their flight must have gazed."

    The falls are known as the Mosi-oa-Tunya in the Kololo language, meaning 'The Smoke that Thunders.' A mile-long curtain of furiously surging water, the falls are a spectacular sight of awe inspiring beauty on the Zambezi River bordering Zambia and Zimbabwe. Over 1 mile wide (1.7 km) and 355 feet (108 m) high, the majestic falls sends over 500 million liters (19 million cubic feet) of water thunderously plummeting over the edge into the Zambezi River! This incredible amount of water generates a huge amount of spray which shoots 1000 feet up the sky and can be seen from 30 miles away, hence the name Mosi-oa-Tunya. One special vantage point lies across the Knife edge bridge which allows visitors to have the finest views of the Eastern Cataract and the Main Falls as well as the Boiling Pot where the river turns and heads down the Batoka Gorge. The Falls Bridge and the Lookout Tree are other vantage points offering beautiful, sweeping views.

    Sightseeing in Zambia is incomplete without getting soaked in these majestic falls! A must-see if you are planning a trip to South Africa, you can watch the gushing waters face-on and get to enjoy the full force of the sudsy spray, roaring noise and spectacular rainbows. Truly, a sight to behold.

    Kafue National Park

    Found in the center of Western Zambia, the Kafue National Park is the oldest and largest of Zambia's national parks covering a massive 22,500 square kilometers. Despite its large size and close proximity to the Livingstone Falls, it remains largely little-known and highly unexplored.

    Housing the varied diversity of wildlife than possibly any other national park in Africa, the Kafue National park, in recent years, has seen a small though well-managed growth in the number of safari tours and camps that operate here.

    The Kafue, possible holds the greatest diversity of wildlife enjoyed by any national park in Africa. Teamed as one of the best places in Africa for a leopard encounter, the plain is also renowned for frequent sightings of the lion, the cheetah and other big cats. The Busanga lion prides are a must-see.
