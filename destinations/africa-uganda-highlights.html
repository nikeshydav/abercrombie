Uganda Highlights & Sightseeing

  • Bwindi Impenetrable National Park

    Impenetrable by its name, this National Park lives true to its acronym with a towering mountain range of Virunga protecting what lies in the core heart of this UNESCO world heritage site. Thick and dense rain forests guard the wonders of nature- waterfalls, lakes and abundance of wildlife in South-western Uganda.

    Sightseeing in Uganda can start from Bwindi which has a wide number of animals that are exclusively found here. One amongst them is the majestic mountain gorilla which is one of the rarest animals on the planet. In estimation, about 65% of the world's present population of mountain gorillas that come to about 460 are found here. One encounter with them will leave you bedazzled as they have the most brown and expressive eyes that speak. It will be remembered as Africa's wildest wildlife-encounter.

    Bwindi is also famous for its oldest inhabitants, the Batwa Pygmies of the Great Lakes region of Central Africa. Their first recordings of existence date back to 4000 years ago. Having a rich and deep culture, this hunter gatherers clan are a sight to watch. Any effort to conserve their habitat and culture is a great step towards helping save Bwindi.

    Jinja

    Jinja Town needs no introduction as it the home of the famous and mighty river Nile which is undoubtedly the world's longest river. It waters Uganda, Sudan and Egypt before flowing out to the Mediterranean Sea.

    Having such a huge water source, it has now become home to the best kind of white water rafting making Jinja an ideal tourist place in Uganda. Imagine rafting down the Famed Bujagali falls for that adrenaline kick.

    MURCHISON FALLS NATIONAL PARK

    The Murchison Falls National Park make up for Uganda's largest National Park. Located in the Northwest, it is named after the famed Murchison Falls where Nile River's water oozes out of a narrow gorge of just 6 meters before making a pool of 43m below.

    It makes for the most powerful force of water anywhere in the world. The park plays a host to all 'Big5' in Uganda barring any other competitor. It also helps population of Uganda Kob, Rothschild's giraffe and Patas monkey and other animals and birds to flourish.

    Rwenzori Mountains National Park

    The Rwenzori Mountains National Park is a World Heritage Site that is located in the South-western part of Uganda. More than half of the Rwenzori Mountain Range is covered by this park. The range is often called the Ptolemy's Mountains of the Moon popularly.

    This range of mountains has three mountains primarily- Albert, Alexandra and Margherita which is Africa's third highest peak. This mountain range has very steep and rugged mountains giving over twenty five peaks above 4500m to be trekked. What transverse in between is nothing short of Africa's most beautiful alpine areas with glaciers, waterfalls and lakes. An ensemble caretaker, this park protects some endangered species of flora and fauna. Unusual flora includes giant heathers, groundsels and lobelias which are characterised as Africa's 'botanic big game'. With over 177 species of birds and 70 of mammals, it's a great place to visit.

    Queen Elizabeth National Park

    From savannahs to marshlands, wetlands to lowland forest; Queen Elizabeth National park is Uganda's most visited parks. 250 square kilometres of lake water keeps the park damp and wet.

    Offering a great habitat for both birds and animals alike; Lakes George and Edward, The Kazinga Channels, Ishasha River and a series of smaller crater lakes attract major wildlife variety. In the southern end of the park is Ishasha which is famous for its tree-climbing lions, giving such a rare sight possible for travellers.

    Kibale Forest National Park

    Bordering Queen Elizabeth national park and 35kms south of Fort Portal is the sight of famous Kibale Forest National Park. It is western Uganda which boasts of housing the largest population of forest elephants which can rarely be sighted.

    The primate density in Kibale is the highest in Uganda with red-tailed money, blue monkey, olive baboon, chimpanzee, colobus (black, white and red) and white cheeked mangabey found in abundance here. Some other mammals are bushbuck, red and blue duiker, Uganda kob, Scaly-tailed flying squirrel, tree pangolin, buffalo, waterbuck and hippo as well as a large number of birds.
