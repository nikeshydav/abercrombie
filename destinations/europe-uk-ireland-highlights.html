UK & Ireland Highlights & Sightseeing

  • London

    Capital of United Kingdom-London serves as a starting point for all tours in England. Endowed with natural and man-made wealth, London has a number of tourist attractions. Royal Palaces, world-class museums and fashionable boutiques puts the city on a world map. Visit the monuments in St.Paul's Cathedral and Westminster Abbey, Crown Jewels in the Tower of London, National Gallery and the British Museum. You can celebrate culture by spending an evening at the West End Theatre or watching a play at Shakespeare's Globe. Also, you must visit the famous Harrods department store.

    Tours to London also include the nearby sights. Explore Thames on a boat ride enroute to Maritime Greenwich where the meridian runs through the Royal Observatory. On the opposite side are Kew Gardens, Hampton Court Palace and the Windsor Castle. From here Oxford and Cambridge universities are not too far off. Near Oxford city, you have the Blenheim Palace which was the birthplace of Sir Winston Churchill and also his burial.

    Southern England

    In Southern England, Canterbury was where the first Christians made their first base. It's also home to England's mother church. The White Cliffs of Dover are a rare delight, visible from France too. Moving southwards, try the luxurious resorts including Brighton which reminds all of the flamboyant past of the Royal Pavilion.
    Explore the residences of Arundel castle and Goodwood and remind yourself of the old glorious days of victory with a look at 'HMS Victory', Nelson's ship-a flagship of the Royal Navy in Portsmouth. Go ferrying to the serene Isle of Wight where at Osborne, Queen Victoria's private home is located.

    Sprinkled with villages and quite meadows are the Cotswold Hills. In the north lie Warwick Castle and Shakespeare's Stratford-upon-Avon with its imposing buildings. Get a taste of Roman legacy at Bath, originally a Roman city with Georgian streets. The ancient monument of Stonehenge is within distance and till today its mystery remains uncovered while the Salisbury Cathedral next to it is another great marvel. A kaleidoscopic image of the Southwest England paints the imagination of any artist. Home of clotted cream, Cornish pastries, coastline, fishing inlets, warm climate and wilderness of the ponies that roam free on wild Exmoor and Dartmoor can best describe a day's scenario. The Eden project gives life to many non-native plants that are planted and grown in a controlled environment here.

    Northern England

    Inspiring many artists and writers like William Wordsworth and Beatrix Potter, Northern England has an upland charm. Lake District, Yorkshire Dales (immortalised by James Herriot) are just a few gems. Lined with historical buildings, York is famous for York Minster, largest medieval church in England. Try the ancient city of Chester which reminds of half-timbered stores and unplastered walls.

    Birthplace of Beatles, Liverpool was the centre of the world's maritime trade. There is little left of the Hadrian's Wall which was built by the Romans. Have a look at the Alnwick castle (Hogward's Academy for Harry Potter fans) and Durham Cathedral. Some great country houses are Castle Howard and Chatsworth.

    Wales

    Cardiff in ales was once world's busiest coal port but it has re-invented itself as modern capital city now. View the Cardiff Castle and National Museum of Wales to understand the industrial wealth and that time. See the coal mines in Caerphilly Castle ruins and go southwards to tread the Tintern Abbey and St. David's Cathedral.

    North Wales is surrounded with the mountains of Snowdonia that houses some of the best medieval castles in Europe. Re-live the conflicts between England and Wales by having a look at the turrets of Caernarfon and Conwy. Thomas Telford, an engineer gave the area its roads, buildings, bridges, canals and other important means of infrastructure so as to open the gates of industrialisation. He gave the region its first miniature steam railways to carry slate from the quarries for export. As an example of urban planning, Portmeirion exudes a charming grace with Utopian Manners in place.

    Edinburgh & Southern Scotland

    Edinburgh is dominated by Scotland's most famous and visited castles. It gives a great impetus for travelling. To see old charm start from the Old Town extending from the Castle of Holyrood that serves as the home to royal government of Scotland. Go new with a stroll in the Georgian streets while observing a Royal Yacht Britannia, stationed at Leith docks.

    Play a mystery lover by seeing the ornate Rosslyn Chapel that featured in the movie 'The Da Vinci Code' and the exquisite country homes like Abbotsford built by novelist Sir Walter Scott. A yesteryear world's centre of shipbuilding, Glasglow boasts of art nouveau designs by Charles Rennie Mackintosh. Poet Robbie Burns' fans can see his home and the Culzean Castle in the Clyde Coast. Enjoy the scenic Trossachs and Loch Lomond while browsing through the royal Palaces like Stirling Castle, Scone Palace and Glamis Castle. Of all golf courses in the region, St. Andrews has a spiritual connect.

    The Scottish Islands

    The Scottish Islands complement the country by boasting of fine scenery towards the Atlantic coast and the Hebridean Islands form. Out of these, Skye dominates with Cuillin Hills and Dunvegan Castle.

    Tiny Iona is good with attracting pilgrims and Orkney and Shetland Isles off the coast are haven for Vikings.

    Dublin & Southern Ireland

    Ireland's sightseeing starts from its capital city Dublin with mesmerising architectural wonders and happening nightlife. Divided into three- Georgian Dublin, houses Trinity College and Illuminated Book of Kells; Old Dublin with Dublin Castle and St. Patrick's Cathedral; and North Dublin with its grand O'Connell Street. Presidential Palace in Phoenix Park and a sip of Ireland's favoured drink at Guinness Storehouse is a must. Reaching the province of Leinster, experience the art of Wicklow Mountains, Curragh's heathland and pastoral valleys where horses are bred. See the architectural wonders of Newgrange, ruins at Glendalough and Clonmacnoise and country homes at Russborough and Powerscourt.

    In Southwest Ireland is Munster where Rock of Cashel and the Shannon give the pastoral lands a high. Coastal vistas of the Ring of Kerry, Dingle and the Cliffs of Moher are some of them. Blarney and Bunratty castles are icons while the Muckross House give a perfect view of the Lakes of Killarney.

    In the Northwest is Connaught where the wind plays a mind sweeping game and houses Ashford Castle, gateway to the wild landscapes of Connemara. Spot the Galway and oysters and marble wares. Also visit the Aran Islands where Gaelic is spoken.
