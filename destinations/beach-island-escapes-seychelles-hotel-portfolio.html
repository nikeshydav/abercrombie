    DESROCHES ISLAND

    Located on Desroches Island, this property is the perfect island getaway offering a selection of accommodation; 20 Beach suites, 23 Beach villas, 8 Beach Retreats.

    There are lots of different dining options on the island, from in villa dinning, to a private picnic on the beach, the main restaurant Le VeLoutier offers an eclectic mix of creole and international cuisine while the Ghost Crab Grill & Pizzeria offers the perfect light lunch.     BIRD ISLAND LODGE

    Bird Island Lodge is all about simplicity and the environment that you are in, there are no telephones and no phones immerse yourself in the beauty of this unspoilt unique island. Conservation plays an important role on the island and the conservation workers like Robbie will guide you through the island enlightening your mind on the different species of birds, turtle and the fauna of the island. If you would like to help out while staying on the island with any of the conservation projects this is also possible.

    The cuisine on the island is simple and fresh combining the fresh fish caught daily, with the organic fruit and vegetables as well as hand reared pork from the small farm, fresh tasty food at its best. The turtle laying season is from mid-October to mid-January and the laying season from mid-December to mid-March. Bird Island can claim the highest number of first time records for any single island of the Seychelles. With 112 species recorded in total, they also became the first island to record 100 non-breeding migratory birds. Sooty Terns hover, in their tens of thousands, above their island sanctuary in March and April, before beginning to land in the colony at the northern end of the island from the end of April. Bird Island offers a truly unique experience that is not for everyone, but a very special place that is waiting to be discovered.

    A must is to see Esmeralda the giant Aldabran tortoise weighing in at 304 kilos, and with an estimated age of over 200 years, she is in the Guinness Book of Records as the World's largest living, free roaming tortoise.
