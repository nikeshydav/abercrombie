Suggested Luang Prabang Accommodation

  • AMANTAKA

    Set on a large garden estate, Amantaka is housed in graceful French colonial buildings in the UNESCO protected town of Luang Prabang. Airy and elegant throughout, the décor and furnishings reflect the town's French colonial history.

    This former provincial hospital is centrally located making it perfectly placed to introduce visitors to the wonders of Luang Prabang. A two minute walk and you are on the main street with a choice of boutiques, bakeries and restaurants, as well as close to the banks of the Mekong River and Luang Prabang's popular night market.

    Amantaka offers 24 suites ranging in size from 70 to 120 square metres each. These include eight Suites, four Pool Suites, eight Khan Pool Suites, two Mekong Pool Suites and two Amantaka Pool Suites, all with spacious interior and exterior living areas.

    LA RESIDENCE PHOU VAO

    The only hotel located on Phou Vao Hill - the Hill of the Kites - affords glorious panoramic views of the golden dome of the Vat Chamsi Stupa and the sacred mountain of Mount Phou Si. Each elegantly appointed room commands a gorgeous vista as you soak in the views from your private balcony or terrace.

    Swim in the freshwater infinity pool, indulge in traditional Laotian Spa treatments and sample authentic cuisine at the best restaurant in Luang Prabang.After exploring the famed UNESCO World Heritage designated town, experience a magnificent '500 candles dinner' or relax with an exotic cocktail under a starlit sky in the open-air Champa Bar.

    THREE NAGAS

    In the heart of Luang Prabang, 3 Nagas has been created from three UNESCO World Heritage buildings restored in 2003 and is steeped in Lao tradition while offering modern comfort and contemporary elegance.

    With just 15 bedrooms and suites in two separate buildings, the hotel offers a real sense of intimacy yet has two restaurants; Les 3 Nagas offers fine Loa cuisine with a touch of French flair, while the open-air Mango Tree serves a fusion of Asian and Western flavours. The beautiful gardens edge onto the Nam Khan River, offering glimpses of the serene Lao lifestyle.
