Suggested Masai Mara accommodation.

  • Bateleur Camp

    Overlooking the endless plains of the Masai Mara National Reserve and offering travelers an idyllic view of the African landscape, the Baleur Camp offers dramatic game viewing and exceptional standards of service and luxury accommodation and is close to where 'Out of Africa' was filmed.

    Accommodation is in the form of nine different suites. Gleaming wooden floors and en-suite bathrooms with stone wall showers lend a classic elegance while reflecting the ambience of the 1920's and 30's. Each tent is furnished with its own deck overlooking the game laden plains and a personal butler ensures every need is looked after. After a stunning adventure in a four-wheel drive, take a refreshing swim in the tree shaded lap pool and enjoy your dinner in a fairytale like ambiance by dining in candlelight bush banquets on the riverbank.

    Cottars 1920's Safari Camp

    Romance, adventure and elegance are not just words when it comes to the Cottars 1920's Safari Retreat. A camp that perfectly recreates the classic, bygone era, it is associated with Charles Cottar, Kenya's most renowned hunters as well as one of the first cinematographers to capture wildlife images. Situated on a 22,000 acre concession in the Masai Mara, bordering the Serengeti and Loliondo reserves, this camp will provide you with the highest standard of personalized service and professional guiding.

    Keeping with the family tradition, Calvin Cottar, the owner, is a safari guide of international repute. Tales of hunting adventures and heroic deeds often form a part of evening campfires.

    Get ready to experience the ultimate in personalized service and guiding.

    • Unwind and relax with a complimentary massage, manicure and pedicure.
    • Cool off in the swimming pool.
    • Take a bushwalk with an experienced guide.

    In the Know:

    The Cottar family has been operating luxury tented safaris since 1919 and has been a part of the Mara community for decades.

    Elephant Pepper Camp

    Take a step back in time with modern day comforts at the Elephant Pepper Camp. Situated on the northern edge of the Masai Mara Reserve, this eight-tent encampment is tucked away in prime wildlife area and surrounded by plentiful game.

    On-suite facilities like dressing rooms and safari showers are also provided. Treat yourself to delectable international and local culinary treats created by our chefs as they set out to pamper your palate. Our ever assistive and welcoming staff will leave no stone unturned to make your stay a pleasant and a memorable one. Unwinding around the campfire after a candlelight dinner under the starlit sky marks the perfect end to a day filled with lasting memories.

    Il Moran

    Widely regarded as a premier camp in the Masai Mara, the II Moran is a part of the governor's camp and is perfect for those seeking to revel in a bit of luxury. Ten lavish tents line the winding banks of the Mara River and are perfect for close game viewing. Victorian baths and king-sized beds impart a luxurious and comfortable feel to these tents.

    For a unique experience in the wilderness, take on an early morning Hot Air Balloon safari. A walking safari and a Lake Victoria Day Trip will give you learnings about the intricate and magnificent ways of this equally magnificent region.

    • Knowledgeable driver -guides offering un-paralleled information on game drives.
    • Masai Cultural Visits available - perfect way to learn about the tradition and history of the Masai Mara.

    In season, watch the Great Wildbeest Migration crossing the nearby Mara River.

    IN THE KNOW
    Take part in the once-in-a-lifetime hot air balloon safari over the Masai Mara Reserve while you are at the IL Moran.

    Kicheche Bush Camp

    Comfortably nestled in the Olare Orok, a prime wildlife area, the Kicheche Bush Camp is a small and intimate tented bush camp accommodating a maximum of twelve guests. Completely unfenced, it forms the ideal base for incomparable game viewing.

    Customized four-wheel drive vehicles and escorted walks in the savannah make for the best game views whereas local guides' instincts and experience provides superb opportunities to photograph fascinating wild animals in their natural habitat. Daily schedules are flexible and can be attuned to as per your plans to ensure relaxation. Treat your taste buds to a wide variety of African and International cuisine and freshen your palate with freshly baked bread and pastries.

    Kicheche Mara Camp

    The Kicheche Mara Camp lies nestled among wild olive groves and croton trees on the Northern Mara plains. Located in a prime game viewing area, game viewing is exceptional and starts directly from the camp. The Masai Mara hosts one of earth's greatest shows with the annual wildebeest migration passing through the park annually.

    Hosting no more than 22 guests as any time in its 11 tents, the tents are all furnished with an en-suite bathroom with flush loos, warm showers and low wattage lighting supplied 24 hours a day. Many of the tents overlook the plains and hills and lie on secluded areas while some are woven closely together, ideal if you are traveling as a family or with friends. Nyati, the camp lounge offers comfortable seating area as well as a library. Meals are al fresco whenever possible and are a mixture of African and International cuisine.

    Early morning and late afternoon game drives are taken in open sided vehicles, which are the perfect platform to watch and photograph wildlife without encroaching upon their space. The Mara can also be explored on foot through a walking safari. However, a visit to the local village and school is recommended without which your stay would not be complete.

    Kichwa Tembo

    Located in the remote, north western area of the Masai Mara reserve, the Kichwa Tembo Camp is perhaps Kenya's best loved safari camps and offers quintessential game viewing. Located at the meeting point of a riverine forest and the vast, sweeping plains of the Masai Mara, the camp consists of 40 Hemingway-style safari tents with en-suite bathrooms. Enjoy your meals in the indoor and outdoor dining areas open to warm breezes coming off the Mara or under the myriad stars looking out into the African plains.

    Morning and afternoon safaris are conducted in 4x4 vehicles where bird watching and lectures by a Maasai naturalist help you to understand the region better. You also have the liberty to enjoy night drives or walking trails along the Oloololo escarpment and a visit to a local Maasai village as an optional extra. Afternoons are best spent unwinding by the swimming pool bordered with an ever-changing view.

    Little Governors Camp

    Enjoy the panorama of animals and birds frequenting the watering hole that lies in front of the Little Governor's Camp. Accommodation is intimate and comfortable with tasteful interiors in tune with the safari tradition. Each of the 17 spacious tent safaris are furnished with their own private verandahs and lit up by lantern or candlelight.

    Unfenced and in close proximity to the Mara River, the camp makes sure you have the closest and the best game views. Do not be surprised if a herd of elephants stroll past your dinner table or a family of warthogs wander by as you sit around the campfire recounting the day's events. Enveloped by beauty and serenity, this exquisite, romantic setting is ideal for those who are romantic at heart yet enjoy a bit of adventure.

    Mara Plains Camp

    Set right in the heart of the predator plains, the Mara Plains Camp is located on the northern border of the Masai Mara Game Reserve in the Olare Orok Conservancy.

    There are two lounges, one with a small library and a writing area, a tastefully done up dining room and a splendid chandelier. Interiors are tastefully done up in earthy tones to ensure a relaxed feel after an adventurous day in the wilds. One of the smallest and most personal camps in the whole region, accommodation here is in seven large three-sided-open rooms with decks overlooking the sweeping Savannahs and the Ntiakatek River.

    • Hire a Canon camera with a 100 - 400mm image stabilized lens
    • Enjoy night drives in the Olare Orok Conservancy
    • Visit the nearby Masai village and live the Masai way of life.

    IN THE KNOW:
    A dinner under the starlit night sky is one thing you surely can't miss out on.

    Naibor Camp

    A striking combination of contemporary style and comfort, the Naibor Camp was originally designed by Anthony Russel. Tucked away at a grove in the riverine woodland on the banks of the Talek River, the camp offers spacious luxury tents set in the shade of acacia trees. The main dining tent overlooks the Talek River, often frequented by animals for a drink. Forming a luxurious base from which to explore the Mara, the camp takes you up, close and personal to all major wildebeest crossing sites over the Mara River.

    • Relax, unwind and relish the beautiful, natural setting.
    • Travel to the nearby villages.
    • Rejuvenate those tired muscles with relaxing massages in the Naibor Spa.
    • Embark on a hot air balloon ride for the ultimate experience.

    IN THE KNOW
    Naibor helps nearby communities in preserving wildlife as well as the environment and also provides them with a source of income.

    Rekero Tented Camp

    Located in one of the prime sites in the Masai Mara Reserve, the Rekero Tented Camp boasts of the best guides in the Masai Mara. Sitting on the picturesque bends of the Talek River, the camp is tucked among tall, deciduous trees and is designed in such a way that it complements rather than competes with its artistic surroundings. Each of the seven luxurious private safari tents offer en-suite bathroom, solar lanterns and a terrace.

    Frenzy of predator activity, spectacular river crossings and wild animals make for common sights all around. Expertly guided game drives and walks vie with cultural visits to a Masai village that takes your breath away. Enjoy delicious 'farmhouse' meals cooked with the freshest of produce.

    Sanctuary Olonana

    A beautiful setting for sighting the 'Big Five' - lion, leopard, buffalo, elephant and rhino, Sanctuary Olonana is an award winning tented camp on the foot of the famous Siria Escarpment. Cultural experiences combine with tastefully appointed luxury accommodation. Fourteen spacious and tastefully appointed tents with en-suite bathrooms and river view verandahs make for a delightful sight. Personalized service and an intimate and relaxed atmosphere make for a memorable stay.

    Guided tours in four-wheel open drives will surely set your pulse racing, yet the pace of the safari can be as active or as leisurely as you desire.

    Serian Tented Camp

    A stylish tented camp, the Serian (meaning calm/serene) Serengati Camp offers the intimacy of just eight marquees in the main camp and four others in an even more exclusive setting across the bubbling Mara River.

    Undoubtedly jewels in the crown, each of the eight en-suite marquees are tastefully furnished. A combination of Mediterranean and Coastal cuisine with a distinct African flavor will pamper your taste buds. Allow our experienced guides will transport you to the heart of this amazingly beautiful country. Game drives, walking safaris, village visits, meals in the bush and sundowners are other availabilities. Come and explore the 'wild' side of life with us.
