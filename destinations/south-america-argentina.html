Travel to Argentina


Argentina, whose name in Latin means "silver," is the eighth largest country in the world. It is renowned for its European elegance combined with native Latin American charms. Buenos Aires, the capital of Argentina, is regarded as "Paris of South America," offers charming architecture, sumptuous cuisine, a vivacious nightlife, and chic shopping outlets.

Elegant boulevards reverberating with the romantic sounds of the Tango, open air cafes spilling into wide avenues, the best of museums, the most dramatic of theatres, delightful art galleries and chic designer boutiques, there is no shortage of Argentinean tourist attractions. Begin your Argentina holiday in Buenos Aires, make time for witness a proper Tango show in its full glory and explore the magnificent architecture - including Eva Peron's grave in the Recoleta cemetery before visiting the old port, now brightly-painted and buzzing with the city's creative flair. A visit to Buenos Aires is incomplete without a tour to La Bocca, where slender alleyways meander through brightly coloured houses to the central Bocca Stadium.

A stunning panorama of rugged mountains and sparkling lakes inhabited by a diverse variety of birds greets you as you head towards the North West side of Argentina. It is also home to the Socompa, the Llullaillaco and the Tridente Volcanoes, which are all currently inactive. The spectacular backdrop is that of the Andean Mountain Range, lying to the East.

Continue your Argentina tour towards the Brazilian border. Thundering in a myriad of waterfalls through a primeval jungle region, lie the Iguazu Falls.

Adventure lovers are in for a thrill at Patagonia, a geographic region containing the southernmost portion of South America. Located in Argentina and Chile, it comprises the Andes Mountains to the west which provide a natural border whose foothills are perfect for hiking and exploring on horseback.

Another gem that you cannot miss on your Argentina tour is the Lake District. Outdoor enthusiasts from the world over travel here to seek extreme outdoor adventures and serene escapes. The diverse variety of natural attractions and activities in addition to its striking aesthetic appeal make the Lake District a very popular destination.

Argentina is also a gastronomy haven, with its globally celebrated Argentinean steaks and Malbec wine. Conclude your Argentina holiday in the City of Mendoza, known as "the land of sunshine and wine."
