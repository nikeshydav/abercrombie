AN INDULGENT TOUR OF PARIS

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 5 days
    A five day VIP trip to Paris.
    Highlights: Paris, Versailles

  • Day 1

    PARIS

    Upon arrival at Paris airport you will be welcomed to France by your local Guardian Angel as you exit customs into the arrivals hall, and transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in procedures.

    A lunch reservation will be made for you at the Jules Verne restaurant (lunch payable directly). A private elevator will transport you to the inner sanctum whose cushy black carpet, black walls, and black-crossbarred chairs set off by gleaming, crisp white tablecloths were designed by Slavik to make the diners feel part of the tower itself. We will try to reserve a table near the window to allow you to experience the magnificent view to the full. Classic French cuisine with chic and professional service is offered in this restaurant.

    This afternoon enjoy a special visit to the Opera de Paris which has been housed in Garnier Palace since its completion by architect Garnier in 1875 following the orders of Napoleon III. The beauty of the Opera Garnier, its marble Grand Staircase; the gold, the mosaics and the magnificent painted ceiling of the Grand Foyer; the immense crystal chandelier (weighing 8 tons) and Marc Chagall's ceiling of the Italian-style Auditorium are renowned all over the world. Your privately guided visit will take you through the famous public spaces - an hour and a half of insider anecdotes and the history tailing the artistic and social highlights of the era. A glorious visit not to be missed! After your tour you will be transferred back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel Plaza Athenee

  • Day 2

    PARIS

    Today you will be met at your hotel by a Paris restaurant critic and food expert, who will spend the morning with you pointing you to some of the best food suppliers in Paris during your 3 hour walking tour. During your tour there are a number of different possibilities according to your interests. For example you might visit a world-renowned boulangerie where you will meet the baker and learn about the French art of breadbaking or you may have a glimpse at the life of a dedicated chocolatier who trained with the very best.

    In the afternoon you will enjoy a half day (up to 5 hours) sightseeing tour to Versailles with your private vehicle and English-speaking guide. You will drive out to Versailles to admire the huge palace of the great Sun King Louis XIV, visiting the State Apartments and decorative gardens.

    This evening you will be privately transferred to Hotel Ritz to the School Ritz Escoffier to start your private Practical Class in Cuisine Followed by a Dinner. You will have the opportunity to prepare a starter, main course and a dessert and at the end of the class, a convivial meal will be shared by you all. A glass of champagne or non-alcoholic drink will be served after the class and all participants will receive personalized certificates and a recipe book in French & English. After your tour you will be privately transferred back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel Plaza Athenee
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 3

    PARIS

    This morning meet your private vehicle and English-speaking guide for a 4 hours Impressionists sightseeing tour. Visit of the recently re-opened Musèe de l' Orangerie, which houses Monet's magnificent "Water Lily" paintings. Afterwards go up to the hill of Montmartre where many painters lived and worked and where, today, artists still gather.

    This afternoon you will have the opportunity to learn firsthand about refined table manners in a most exclusive setting. You will start with a private lesson in French Table Etiquette travelling by private vehicle to the Parisian apartments of a High society socialite (a Princess) who has educated generations on formal Protocol. Her apartments are aristocratically furnished with family portraits.

    In the early evening you will leave Paris for a dinner with your evening's hosts - noble descendants of Colbert, the powerful Minister of Louis XIV. They will welcome you into their historic 18th Century Château du Saussay that still belongs to the family and will give you a personal tour of their ancestral home (which includes magnificent Colbert library) and its landscaped park. The candlelit dinner will take place in the formal dinning room where you will have the opportunity to get to know the Barons while experimenting refined table manners, gastronomic cuisine prepared for you by a renowned Parisian Chef and the elegant 18th Century setting of the Château which was the film location for "Dangerous Liasons" staring Glenn Close and John Malkovich. After dinner you will be privately transferred back to Paris.

    Overnight: Hotel Plaza Athenee
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 4

    PARIS

    You will be joined by a personal shopper and a private vehicle with driver today for a full day shopping excursion through the most famous Paris stores. Some of the stores you may visit are Christian Lacroix, Jean Paul Gaultier, Maria Luisa (multibrand concept store: Manolo Blahnik, Martin Margiela, Alexander McQueen, John Galliano), Stella Cadente, Cacharel, Jey, Lancel Champs Elysees, Mauboussin, Daum and many more! You will also receive a discount on purchases in some of the stores visited and a VIP welcome with champagne.

    During the afternoon a diamond expert will welcome you into the exclusive, state of the art surroundings of Korloff Paris, located on the famous Rue de la Paix. Whilst enjoying champagne and canapés listen to the Korloff Saga - this history of the world famous "Korloff Noir" - the biggest black diamond in the world at 88 carats - and learn about the diamond cutting process, including the only recently developed Korloff cut which achieves a total of 65 facets. After your excursion you will be transferred back to your hotel.

    This evening you will be privately transferred with your English-speaking driver from your hotel in the center to Paris Port Henri IV to enjoy a public 3 hour dinner cruise on a yacht called Don Juan II. The City of Light will surround you and you will feel the intimate and warm atmosphere of a true luxurious yacht. The wines and dishes composing a magnificent meal are presented on your exquisitely-laid table. The Don Juan II recalls the boats of the great pleasure craft era, blending modernism and charm. It features a spacious bar and reception room, soundproofing and air conditioning, non-smoking area, copper fittings and precious woods, uniformed seamen and open-air and covered decks. Two Michelin star chef Jean-Pierre Vigato will prepare the gourmet meal, accompanied by fine wines (payable directly), in an exquisite setting. You will be transported to another era, on the Seine, in a unique environment, for an unforgettable dinner cruise. Vigato's cuisine is modern, inventive and light. He combines a refined version of traditional "bourgeois" cooking with his imaginative style. After the cruise you will be privately transferred back to your hotel.

    Overnight: Hotel Plaza Athenee
    Meal Plan: B,D

  • Day 5

    PARIS

    This morning meet your private vehicle and English-speaking guide (for a 6 hour sightseeing tour) to visit the St. Ouen 'Flea Market', the largest antiques market in the world, with more than 3000 stalls. A wide selection of goods is on offer: furniture, paintings, jewelry, chandeliers, trinkets, porcelains, objets d'art and decorations. The St. Ouen antique dealers will do their best to find the object you've been searching for to adorn your home or to complete the collection. Return to Paris to spend the afternoon at the Louvre des Antiquaires, a stylish mall devoted primarily to antiques, with 250 different shops. It is the major centre of attraction for the whole French art and antiques world. The complex provides bars and restaurants, and exhibitions are held on the 1st floor.

    In the late afternoon you will be privately transferred to Paris airport with the assistance of your local Representative.

    Overnight: Hotel Plaza Athenee
    Meal Plan: B

A TASTE OF PROVENCE

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 8 days
    This itinerary is perfect for those who want to experience the Provencal culture and life with all its pleasures.

    Highlights: Avignon, StRemy, Arles, Luberon, Gordes, Cavaillon, Mane en Provence, Gorges du Verdon, Aix en Provence

  • Day 1

    AVIGNON

    On arrival in France, at either Marseilles airport or Avignon TGV station, you will be met by a representative, shown to your waiting vehicle and transferred to your hotel, just outside Avignon.

    The rest of the day is at leisure.

    You will be confirmed for three nights at your hotel.

    Overnight: Le Mirande
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 2

    AVIGNON/ CHATEAUNEUF DU PAPE/AVIGNON

    Today, transfer to Avignon city center and meet your English-speaking local guide for a half-day walking tour (3hrs) in the magnificent papal stronghold of Avignon. Visit the Clock Square before entering the huge Palais des Papes, where seven exiled popes lived between 1309 and 1377 and where the main rooms are decorated with some excellent 14th century frescoes.

    In the afternoon, travel north to Chateauneuf du Pape with your driver-guide.

    The town is renowned for its fabulous wines and is set in the hills overlooking its famous vineyards. There are now 35 Chateauneuf du Pape domains strictly controlled by the government. Visit a famous Domaine and enjoy a wine tasting before being driven back to your hotel. If time allows, you may like to enjoy a stroll in L'Isle sur Sorgue, famous for its antique shops and have time to browse for that perfect souvenir of your trip.

    Overnight: Le Mirande
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    AVIGNON/ LES BAUX/ST REMY/AVIGNON

    Today, meet your local English-speaking driver-guide and private vehicle for a full-day (8 hours) sightseeing tour to Les Baux-de-Provence, Saint-Rémy-de-Provence and Arles. Spend time in the medieval village citadel of Les Baux, which sits high above the surrounding countryside. You will also be able to visit St-Rémy-de-Provence with its Roman antiquities.

    OPTIONAL SIGHTSEEING: Private Tour of Avignon and surrounding areas in a Citroen 2CV

    Discover Provence in a Citroen 2CV with your own private English-speaking driver-guide. Experience the small winding streets of Avignon in a traditional French car and receive a unique view of Provencal daily life.

    Overnight: Le Mirande
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    AVIGNON/LUBÉRON/MANE EN PROVENCE

    The Lubéron, inspiration for the book "A Year in Provence", is a region of rolling hills and verdant nature dotted with churches and rural communities. Your driver-guide will take you to the great 12th century Cistercian Abbey of Sénanque and to the enchanting village of Gordes before heading towards upper Provence. Stop en route in Cavaillon for a 2hr Melon Discovery Tour (available June to September only - this is a public tour). The tour includes a visit to a Melon Farm where full explanations behind the famous Melon de Cavaillon will be given. Tasting of the local delicacies is a must before heading on to the Luberon and upper Provence.

    Later this afternoon settle into the Couvent de Minimes, a former Convent that has been transformed into a hotel nestling below the Alps mountainside.

    You will be confirmed for three nights at Le Couvent des Minimes.

    Overnight: Le Couvent des Minimes
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    MANE EN PROVENCE

    Today, with your driver-guide you will travel further into the lesser-known upper region of Provence, and in Forcalquier visit an old distillery that makes the signature aperitif of Provence - pastis, where you will enjoy a tasting.

    Later today design the rest of your day by taking up one of these options:

    OPTIONAL SIGHTSEEING #1: Private painting lesson
    A private painting lesson where you get to create your own masterpiece which your representative will mail home to await your return.

    OPTIONAL SIGHTSEEING #2: Visit to the French National Observatory

    Tonight you drive to the local French National Observatory to admire privately the bright stars of the night, considered to be the clearest in all of France.

    Overnight: Le Couvent des Minimes
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    MANE/MOUSTIERS STE MARIE/GORGES DU VERDON/MANE

    This morning your driver-guide will take you to the factory of L'Occitane for a private visit of this most famous of Provence's bathroom and toiletry products, and continue to the charming village of Moustiers Ste Marie wedged in between two rocky bluffs and famous for its "faïence" potteries. Afterwards, drive the spectacular road above and along the awesome Gorges of Verdon, considered one of the great natural wonders of France, and indeed of all Europe, with breathtaking views of the river cascading along its course thousands of feet below. Later this afternoon, return to the delightful Couvent des Minimes.

    Overnight: Le Couvent des Minimes
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 7

    MANE EN PROVENCE - AIX EN PROVENCE

    This morning, transfer to Aix, with a stop en route to visit Mont Sainte Victoire, a local landmark made famous by Cezanne and the renowned Chateau de Vauvenargues, Picasso's final home and resting place. Public visits to Picasso's burial site will be possible for the first time since his death and this will be only between the month of May and September of this year.

    After your arrival at your hotel and once you have settled in, your local English-speaking guide will meet you for a half day (3 hour) walking tour of Aix, once the capital of Provence. Enjoy the bustling Cours Mirabeau, the heart of the town, and a visit to Cézanne's studio. It was here that Cézanne painted some of his most well-known works - there is also a striking collection of simple objects which once featured in his still life paintings.

    You will be confirmed for one night at your hotel.

    Overnight: Villa Gallici
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    DEPART AIX EN PROVENCE

    Today you will be privately transferred to Marseilles airport or Aix train station.

    Meal Plan: B

Arts & Culture Tour of Nice, Avignon & Lyon

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 9 days

    Enjoy an enriching experience with the arts and culture of Nice, Avignon and Lyon in a nine-day trip.

    Highlights: Nice, St Paul de Vence, Avignon, Arles, Les Baux, Palais des Papes, Lyon, Beaujolais

  • Day 1

    ARRIVE NICE

    Upon arrival at the Nice airport you will be welcomed to France by your local representative and transferred to your hotel where you will be assisted with check-in procedures.

    You will be confirmed for three nights at the Hotel La Pérouse.

    Overnight: La Pérouse

  • Day 2

    NICE

    Meet your qualified English-speaking guide for a half-day walking tour of Nice's stunning old town today. In marked contrast to the rest of Nice, the ancient old town dates from Venetian times and was developed around the harbor. It is a labyrinth of cobbled streets and vast plazas such as the Cours Saleya with its colorful open air flower market in the mornings. Ascend up to the castle hill by elevator to enjoy fantastic views across the Baie des Anges.

    Overnight: La Pérouse
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    NICE - ST. PAUL DE VENCE - NICE

    Today, meet your local English-speaking guide and private vehicle for a further half day (4 hours) to the beautiful village of St Paul de Vence. Perfectly preserved within its medieval ramparts, the village has been a mecca for artists since the 19th century. A pétanque specialist, native to Saint Paul de Vence, will initiate you into this Provencal game on the village's legendary sandy bowling pitch. Located just outside the village is the Fondation Maeght, world renowned for its collection of 20th century paintings, sculptures and ceramics.

    You will then be transferred back to your hotel where the remainder of the day will be at your leisure.

    Overnight: La Pérouse
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 4

    NICE - AVIGNON

    Today you will be met at your hotel and privately transferred with your representative to the Nice railway station to begin your rail journey. Here you will be shown to your pre-reserved 1st class seating. Upon arrival at the Avignon railway station, you will be met from the door of the train and privately transferred to your hotel.

    You will be confirmed for three nights at the Le Prieuré Hotel.

    Overnight: Le Prieuré Hotel
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    AVIGNON - ARLES - LES BAUX - AVIGNON

    Today you travel to the former Roman city of Arles with a visit to the Musée de l'Arles Antique and learn from the curator about all the aspects of Arles in its heyday of the Roman Empire. Seven superb Roman floor mosaics can be viewed from an elevated platform. You then wander the very streets which Van Gogh would have known when he lived and worked in Arles, enjoying a coffee in the cafe which he used for his painting 'Starry Night'. Other highlights of your visit to Arles will include its Romanesque cathedral and Roman amphitheatre. Driving through the countryside which Van Gogh painted, you will have time to visit the medieval citadel of Les Baux with its narrow cobbled streets lined with arts and crafts shops. Afterwards you visit the asylum of St Paul to which Van Gogh was admitted. There is time to sit and contemplate in the beautiful cloisters and chapel and to stand at the exact spot where Vincent painted his many pictures.

    Overnight: Le Prieuré
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    AVIGNON

    After breakfast today, you will depart for Hotel la Mirande in the heart of Avignon. The hotel is reputed for its cookery classes and it is here that you will spend the morning in the company of their top chefs learning the art of Provencal cuisine. This hotel was originally the private residence of one of the leading families of Avignon for over 200 years. Its 18th-century interiors have now been meticulously restored with magnificent antiques and a fine collection of old master paintings and china. The restaurant, an impressive room with 15th-century wood-beamed ceilings, features classic French cuisine, opening onto a delightful and secluded garden with a terrace for outdoor dining. The cookery course is subject to the scheduling of English-language courses and enough participants for the course to run. After lunch, you will take a walking tour with your local English-speaking guide around the ancient city of Avignon, wrapped in a crenellated wall punctuated by Gothic slit windows and visit the Palais des Papes. In the 14th-century, this luxurious palace used to be the Capital of the Christian world. It witnessed nine popes in succession and its bold architecture and rich paintings still remain as signs of this glorious past.

    Overnight: Le Prieuré
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 7

    AVIGNON - LYON

    Today you will be met at your hotel and privately transferred with your representative to the Avignon railway station to begin your rail journey. Here you will be shown to your pre-reserved 1st class seating. Upon arrival at the Lyon railway station you will be met from the door of the train and privately transferred to your hotel.

    Later, take a walking tour (3 hours) through 'Vieux Lyon' which has narrow cobblestone streets, 17th and 18th century mansions, small museums and the City's Cathedral. Above Vieux Lyon are the remains of Roman theatres and the Basilique de Notre-Dame, visible from all over the city. Also visit Les Halles de Lyon, the famous covered market where you will see the renowned Charcuterie Sibilin described as a 'temple of sausages' and the cheese-merchants Elenore Maréchal and Renée Richard. Here too is Rolle, the foie-gras specialist. There are a handful of stalls selling fish, catches from the Mediterranean and the Atlantic. There are restaurant counters where you will be able to slurp oysters and chilled glasses of white wine (payable upon consumption).

    You will be confirmed for two nights at the Cour des Loges Hotel.

    Overnight: Château de Bagnols
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 8

    LYON - BEAUJOLAIS - LYON

    This morning you will travel North of Lyon along the Saone River and visit the vineyards of Beaujolais, an ideal area for wine tasting, offering delicious wine in a glorious countryside.

    Lunch is at leisure on your own arrangements in the village of Juliénas, famous for 'Coq au Vin'. Later this afternoon, you will visit Beaujeu and enjoy wine tasting. Visit the Moulin-à-Vent, a 17th century windmill and enjoy a tasting of a 'cru' from the region. Return to Lyon from here.

    Overnight: Chàteau de Bagnols
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 9

    DEPART LYON

    Today you will be privately transferred to Lyon Airport.

    Meal Plan: B

Rediscovering The Burgundy Wine Region

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 6 days

    A bespoke tour celebrating and exploring this fascinating wine region in France.

    Highlights: Paris, Chablis, Vezelay, La Bussière Sur Ouche, Dijon, Beaune and Rochepot

  • Day 1

    PARIS - CHABLIS - VEZELAY

    Upon arrival at the Paris airport you will be welcomed to France in the arrivals hall by your English- speaking wine expert guide, who will accompany you throughout your tour of Burgundy.

    On your way to Burgundy stop in Chablis, the most northerly and insular of 'Burgundian Wine regions'; here the Chardonnay grape and the clay soil produce delicate wines with considerable finesse, there are seven different Grand Cru vineyards in the Chablis appellation. It is here that you will be able to enjoy tasting of the distinct wines of the region at Domaine William Févre. The Domain is set within 47 hectares of skillfully harvested vineyards in the heart of Chablis. Afterwards continue deeper into the countryside of Burgundy, stopping to walk in the valley of the River Serein, before arriving this evening at the foot of the 'holy' hill of Vézelay to check into your celebrated hotel, Hotel de l'Espérance.

    This evening a reservation will be made for you at your hotel to sample the cooking of Marc Mènaud, the owner of the hotel and one of the founders of la nouvelle cuisine française. Marc Mènaud was born in the village and knows all the flavors of the region and you may like to have a local Vezelay wine to accompany your meal.

    Overnight: Hotel de L'Espèrance

  • Day 2

    VEZELAY

    Today your private vehicle with driver and an English-speaking wine expert guide will be at your disposal for a half day. You may like to visit the beautiful setting of Vézelay and the magnificence of its glorious Basilica. Follow the footsteps of medieval pilgrims with a visit to the 'sacred hill' of Vézelay. Best known for its mighty Basilica of Ste-Madeleine, where St Bernard preached the Second Crusade in 1146, and whose carved capitals are one of the greatest Romanesque treasures of Europe.

    Early this afternoon enjoy a private cooking class with Marc Ménaud, sous chef. You will be able to create some of Marc's original recipes yourself followed by a feast of your creations.

    The reminder of the afternoon is at your leisure.

    Overnight: Hotel de L'Espérance
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 3

    VEZELAY - PULIGNY MONTRACHET - LA BUSSIÈRE SUR OUCHE

    Today your private vehicle with driver and an English-speaking wine expert guide will be at your disposal for a full day transfer to your hotel in the beautiful location of la Bussière Sur Ouche.

    Later morning you will stop in a wine workshop (not available on Sundays) and for a lunch at Olivier Leflaive's estate. In 1984 Olivier Leflaive, with help from his uncle Vincent Leflaive, set up his own wine nègociant business, which he continues to manage to this day. With the help of winemaker Jean-Marc Boillot, Olivier Leflaive produced wines of quality from the outset. In 1988 Franck Grux took over as winemaker and continues in this role. Over the two decades quality has continued to improve, and Olivier Leflaive has established a reputation as a small nègociant of high quality. Leflaive has expanded with the purchase of several vineyards, building up a range of domaine wines alongside the nègoce bottlings. Olivier Leflaive owns vines in the Premier Cru vineyards of Les Chaumèes, Les Vergers and Abbaye de Morgeot (all Chassagne Montrachet), and Les Poruzots (Meursault). As well as running the family business producing wines from his estate, he is renowned for his Table-d'Hôtes, where you will be invited today for lunch and a wine tasting.

    Continue on your journey to the Chateau de la Bussière, which is superbly located behind the Cote d'Or.

    Table reservation will be made this evening for you to enjoy dinner at your hotel's gastronomic restaurant.

    You will be confirmed for three nights at the Abbaye de La Bussière.

    Overnight: Abbaye de la Bussière
    Meal Plan: B,L

  • Day 4

    DIJON

    This morning you will meet your driver and an English-speaking wine expert guide for a full day (8 hour) sightseeing tour. This morning travel to Dijon. On arrival in Dijon, the historical capital of Burgundy, enjoy a walking tour of the Old Town, with its splendid half-timber houses. Burgundians are truly obsessed with good wine and food and there is nowhere better to experience this than the local market (Tuesday & Friday, on Thursday the market is only inside the Halles), where you can sample the wonderful local delicacies and produce. Continue to the impressive Fine Arts Museum, housed within the Palais des Ducs, to admire its collection of Flemish masters and treasures from the reign of the former Dukes of Burgundy.

    This afternoon you will be treated to a visit to the Chateau du Clos de Vougeot that lies at the heart of one of the largest of all the Grand Cru vineyards. The chateau is particularly famous for its historic wine-making barn. Vougeot is also 'the home of the celebrated Chevaliers du Tastevin'. Time permitting, you may also like to visit Le Gevrey Chambertin vineyard, which is known as the cru of the Côte de Nuits. At the end of the afternoon you will be transferred back to your hotel.

    This evening we would be happy to make dinner reservation at L'Ecluse de la Charme restaurant in Saint-Victor-sur-Ouche.

    Overnight: Abbaye de la Bussière
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 5

    BEAUNE - MEURSAULT - ROCHEPOT

    This morning you will meet your driver and an English-speaking wine expert guide for a full day (8 hour) sightseeing tour to Beaune & Rochepot.

    Beaune is world famous as the capital of the Côte D'Or wine industry. Located in the heart of Burgundy, its numerous medieval buildings with their colourful roofs are a reminder of a wealthy past. Start with a visit to the medieval Hospices founded in 1443 and lavishly decorated with a multi coloured Flemish roof and famous triptych painting of the Last Judgement by Rogier van der Weyden. You will then drive out into the countryside of the Cote de Beaune that surrounds the town. The southern part of the Cote d'Or, the Cote de Beaune is home to some of the world's finest dry white wines produced from the Chardonnay grape, and also well-known red wines from Pinot Noir. You will be taken to visit one of the noted domaines in Meursault, a village that is famed for its production of white wine. You will have the opportunity to taste the typical wines from the village as well.

    This afternoon travel to the nearby Chateau de la Rochepot, a splendid fortress-castle offering a fascinating trip through the history of medieval times. Return to your hotel at the end of the day.

    Later today enjoy a private hot air balloon flight (subject to good weather conditions) over the châteaux, villages and vineyards of Burgundy. This breathtaking experience, taking place at sun set, will provide you with an unforgettable memory, seeing some of the places you have visited during the past few days from the sky. (Hot air balloon flight - is also available in the morning - at sun rise.)

    This evening we suggest you enjoy dinner at the restaurant L'Eire du Temps in Barbirey-sur-Ouche

    Overnight: Abbaye de la Bussière
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 6

    PARIS

    Depart your hotel for your onward journey home.

SWITZERLAND AND NORTHERN ITALY

Programme Overview

  • Duration: 10 days

    A 10-day itinerary showcasing the beautiful highlights of Switzerland and Northern Italy.

    Highlights: Geneva, Lausanne, Zermatt, Chillon, Lugano, Lake Maggiore, St. Moritz, Lake Como, Interlaken, Bern, Lucerne.

  • Day 1

    Arrive GENEVA

    Upon arrival at Geneva Airport you are met by your local representative and accompanied to your hotel in Lausanne, where you are assisted with check-in.

    Overnight: Beau Rivage Palace

  • Day 2

    LAUSANNE

    TToday you will enjoy a three hour city tour by private vehicle with your English-speaking guide. Your tour will include the Old Town. The Gothic Cathedral of Notre Dame, an architectural treasure, is Switzerland's largest church and probably its finest. The Hotel de Ville is the seat of municipal and communal councils, and the painted medieval Fontaine de la Justice draws people to lounge on its heavy rim.

    Overnight: Beau Rivage Palace
    Meal Plan: B

  • Day 3

    LAUSANNE - CHILLON - ZERMATT
