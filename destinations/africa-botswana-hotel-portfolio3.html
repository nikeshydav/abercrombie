Suggested Selinda Reserve Accommodation

  • Selinda Camp

    Selinda Camp is located on an island on the banks of the Selinda Spillway near to where the Kwando, Savuti, and Linyanti Rivers converge. Selinda Camp is made up of 9 luxury tented under thatch, one of which is a 2 bed roomed family tent all of which look out onto the panoramic views of the floodplains.

    Each tent has a unique egg shaped stone bath and it furnished with beautifully appointed furniture and various features in each of these luxury tents have been collected from areas around Africa. The main dinner and lounge area is built on raised decks under thatched roofing and offer breathing taking views over the flood plains and savannas. Adjacent to the main public areas housed above the temperature-controlled wine cellar is a photo and art gallery which features the work of world renowned and award winning filmmakers and photographers Dereck and Beverly Joubert and a small swimming pool provides welcome relief in the heat of the day.

    Custom built Land Cruisers with individual bucket seats and a removable canvas roof are the perfect way to explore this diverse and interesting reserve. You may opt to spend time in one of the hides which overlook waterholes and flood plains. For a chance to get to know the reserve a bit more why not go on a walking safari which are led by licensed armed guides. Canoeing, fishing and boating activities are also available.

    Selinda Canoe Trail - 3 nights

    In May 2009 the Selinda Spillway joined the Okavango Delta for the first time in over thirty years, as it did again in May 2010. This has given the Selinda Reserve an opportunity to offer one of the rarest experiences in Africa, a three night/four day canoe trail along the Selinda Spillway to the Linyanti in north eastern Botswana.

    You will travel 45kms along the Spillway over your three night epic journey in Canadian canoes. The trip is run at a leisurely pace with stops along the way to discover wildlife from your canoe or on foot. During the evenings you will camp in dome tents on one of the many islands along the way. Your hosts will set up your camps and you will be served with wholesome meals around the campfire. The canoes trails run from late May to early October and have a guaranteed weekly departure with a maximum of 8 guests.

    Zarafa Camp

    Set on the fringes of the Zibadanja Lagoon on the 320,000 acre Selinda Reserve, Zarafa Camp evokes an atmosphere of an 1920's safari with all the modern touches expected of today's living. Each of the four "marquis" style guest tents has its own private plunge pool, indoor and outdoor shower, large beautiful copper bath, central fireplace, and large bedroom and lounge areas.

    A unique touch to each tent is they come with their own Canon 50D digital camera, 28 to 135 and 100 to 400 Canon lenses, backpack, tripod and digital memory cards for guest to use during the course of their stay and on departure the camp will download your images onto CD to take home with you. Zarafa Camp pride themselves on being one of the greenest luxury camps in Africa. All guest tents and the main lounge and dining areas are crafted only from recycled hardwoods and canvas. All the electricity in the camp comes from its solar farm with its system of batteries and inverters and vehicles run on a mixture of 85% recycled cooking oil.

    Activities at Zarafa include day and night drives which are done in custom built Land Cruisers with 7 individual buckets seats and removable canvas roof. A walking safari is a great way to explore the smaller life on the Selinda Reserve, you can take a short walk around camp or a more in-depth full morning walk which will be led by licensed armed guides. For those who want something a bit more relaxing fishing in the lagoons are also an option which can be arranged.
